• jhillikā f. (-kā)
    1 A cricket.
    2 The light of sunshine, strong light, splendour.
    3 Dirt taken off the body in rubbing it with perfumes.
    4 A cloth or rag used for applying colour, unguents, &c.
    5 A cricket's cry.
    6 Membrane, thin skin, parchment.
    E. jhilla for cilla imitative sound, expressing the cricket's chirp, and ṅīṣ being added jhillī, and kan again added jhillikā; also with the penultimate long jhillīkā.
