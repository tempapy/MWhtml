• kairava m. (-baḥ)
    1 A rogue, a cheat.
    2 An enemy. f. (-vī) Moonlight. n. (-vaṃ)
    1 The white esculent water-lily.
    2 Fraud, trick.
    E. kerava here said to mean, a goose, from ke in water, and ru to sound, with ap affix; again, aṇ affix, liked by the geese; or kirava a cheat, &c. or kairava a lotus, and aṇ affix, implying identity or resemblance. [249]
