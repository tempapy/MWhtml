• vakavratin m. (-tī) A false devotee, a religious hypocrite, or one who assumes the appearance of devotion or meditation, for interested purposes.
    E. vaka a crane, vrata observance, and ini aff.; compared to the crane, who stands by a pool demure and sly, and apparently absorbed in contemplation, till he sees a fish on which to dart; also with ṭhak, aff. vakavratika, and derived from vaka, and cara who goes, vakacara or vakavratacara m. (-raḥ) &c. see vaiḍālavratika.
