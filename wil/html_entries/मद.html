• mada [636] r. 1st cl. (mādayate) (i, ir) madi r. 1st cl. (mandati), and (ī, ir) madī r. 4th cl. (mādyati) 1 To be glad, to be satisfied or content, to rejoice. 2 To be proud. (i) madi r. 1st cl. (mandati) 1 To praise. 2 To sleep, to doze, to be lazy or sluggish. 3 To be stupid, to be an idiot or fool. 4 To be elegant. 5 To go or approach. mada r. 10th cl. (mādayate) To cause or justify satisfaction or contentment. madī r. 1st cl. causal form (mādayati) 1 To delight, to make glad, to exhilarate. 2 To madden or intoxicate, literally or figuratively. 2 To be poor or distressed. 4 To be proud.
    With ni prefixed, (nimādayati) To articulate.
  • mada [636] m. (-daḥ) 1 Joy, pleasure, delight. 2 The juice that flows from an elephant's temples when in rut. 3 Spirituous or vinous liquor. 4 Inebriety, intoxication. 5 Pride, arrogance. 6 Insanity, madness. 7 Semen virile. 8 Musk. 9 A male river. 10 Passion, desire. f. (-dī) 1 A drinking vessel. 2 An instrument for making furrows. E. mada to be pleased, &c., aff. ap or ac.
