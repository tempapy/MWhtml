• vṛṣabha [803] m. (-bhaḥ) 1 A bull. 2 (In composition,) Pre-eminent, excellent. 3 The orifice of the ear. 4 An elephant's ear. 5 A drug; also Ṛṣabha, described as a root resembling the horn of a bull, brought from the Himālaya mountains, of cooling and tonic properties, and serviceable in catarrh and consumption. 6 The first of the Jaina pontiffs of the present era. f. (-bhī) 1 A widow. 2 Cowach. E. vṛṣa to sprinkle, Uṇādi aff. abhac.
