• nārāca m. (-traḥ)
    1 An iron arrow. (E. nāra men, āṅ before cama to eat or consume, affix ḍa; destroying hosts of men.)
    2 A bad or cloudy day.
    3 A species of the Dhṛti metre. (E. nāra water, ācama to spit, affix ḍa.) f. (-trī) A goldsmith's scales, a fine or assay balance.
    E. nārāca an iron arrow, and ṅīṣ diminutive affix; also with kan added in the fem. form, nārācikā f. (-kā) [462]
