• rama(au, u) auramu [696] r. 1st cl. (ramate) To sport or play; with some prefixes this root forms an active verb, as with āṅ, upa, and vi; āramati to rest, to repose, &c. with upa the form is optional, if the sense is intransitive, as (uparamati or -te) To stop or cease.
  • rama [696] mfn. (-maḥ-mā-maṃ) 1 Dear, beloved. 2 Pleasing, delighting, charming. m. (-maḥ) 1 A husband, a lover. 2 The deity of love, KĀMA. 3 A sort of tree called a red variety of the Aśoka, (Jonesia asoca.) f. (-mā) 1 A name of LAKṢMĪ. 2 A wife, a mistress. E. rama to sport, aff. ac.
