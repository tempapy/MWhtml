• kaliṅga m. (-ṅgaḥ)
    1 The febrifuge nut plant, (Caesalpinia bonduccella.)
    2 The fork-tailed shrike, (Lanius forficatus, Lath.)
    2 The name of a country; the name Kaliṅga is applied in the Purāṇas to several places, but it especially signifies a district on the Coromandel coast, extending from below Cuttack to the vicinity of Madras, (in this sense, like some other names of countries, it is usually confined to the plural number, (-ṅgāḥ) confounding the place with the people inhabiting it. nf. (-ṅgaṃ-ṅgā) A medicinal seed, that of Echites antidysenterica. f. (-ṅgā)
    1 A woman of a good shape.
    2 A plant, commonly Teöri, (Convolvulus turpethum:) see trivṛt.
    E. kali strife, &c. ga from gama to go, khac aff. [202]
