• gulma m. (-lmaḥ)
    1 The division of an army, a body of troops, consisting of nine platoons, or nine elephants, nine chariots, twenty-seven horse, and forty-five foot.
    2 The spleen.
    3 A shrub, a bush.
    4 A clump of grass, &c.
    5 A fort, an intrenchment.
    6 Disciplining an army, keeping it in a posture of defence.
    7 A wharf or stairs, a Ghat.
    8 A disease; according to some, a chronic enlargement of the spleen, but as variously situated it is any glandular enlargement in the abdomen, as that of the mesenteric gland, &c. so as to be perceived externally. f. (-lmī)
    1 A tent.
    2 Emblic myrobalan; also āmalakī.
    3 Small cardamoms: see elā.
    4 A cluster or clump of trees, a multitude of thickets.
    5 A kind of plant, commonly Kurkavali.
    E. guḍa to surround, mak affix, ḍa changed to la.
