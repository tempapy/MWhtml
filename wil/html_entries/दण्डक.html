• daṇḍaka mn. (-kaḥ-kaṃ) A sort of metre, the stanza of which exceeds twenty-seven syllables, and may extend to 200. f. (-kā) The peninsula of India, from between the Narmadā and Godāvarī rivers to the south, the whole of which, in the days of RĀMA, was a large forest.
    E. daṇḍa a staff, &c. and kan aff. [396]
