• durdhara mfn. (-raḥ-rā-raṃ) Difficult to be sustained or borne, troublesome, unbearable.
    2 Irresistible, difficult to be restrained. m. (-raḥ)
    1 A division of hell.
    2 A kind of drug, commonly Ṛṣabha.
    3 The name of an Asura or Titan.
    E. dur bad, ill, dhara having, possessing; it is also written durddhaira. [416]
