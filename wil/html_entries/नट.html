• naṭa r. 1st and 10th cls. (naṭati nāṭayati)
    1 To dance, to dance as an actor, to act.
    2 To drop or fall.
    3 To shake, to move slightly.
    4 To injure.
    5 To shine. [451]
  • naṭa m. (-ṭaḥ)
    1 A dancer, a mime, an actor, a Nut, &c. in modern times: in caste, the son of a degraded Kṣatriya by a woman of the second caste.
    4 A sort of reed, (Arundo tibialis, or latterly called A. karka, Roxburgh's Catalogue;) also naḍa and nala.
    5 A subordinate mode of music. f. (-ṭā) A sort of shrub, (Cæsalpinia bonduccella.) (-ṭī)
    1 A medicinal plant: see nalī.
    2 A whore.
    3 A Nach girl, a dancer, an actress.
    4 Red arsenick: see nalī.
    5 A Rāgiṇī.
    E. naṭa to dance, affix ac, and ṭāp or ṅīṣ; otherwise, naśa to destroy, Uṇādi affix ḍaṭa, &c. [451]
