• sairindhra [944] m. (-ndhraḥ) A menial servant, one employed to dress and decorate the person and perform all servile offices; also who may be employed in the chase, snaring animals, &c. the offspring of a Dasyu by an Ayogava female. f. (-ndhrī) 1 An independant female artisan working in another person's house. 2 A female attendant in the women's apartments. 3 A woman of one of the mixed and degraded classes. 4 DRAUPADĪ, the wife of the Pāṇḍu princes, and who upon the temporary servitude of her husbands, became workwoman in the house of the Rāja of Virāṭa. E. sīra a plough, dhṛ to hold or take, aff. ka, and muk augment, aṇ added, and the penultimate vowel changed optionally to i; whence also sairandhrī, or svaira independent, dhṛ to hold or be, aff. kan, fem. aff. ṅīṣ, and form irr.
