• śaka r. 4th cl. (śakyati-te) r. 5th cl. (ira ḷ) iraśaktṛ (śaknoti)
    1 To bear patiently, to endure, to be patient.
    2 To be able to effect, to be competent or powerful. (i) śaki r. 1st cl. (śaṅkate)
    1 To think probable, to suspect.
    2 To fear, to apprehend.
    With āṅ, to dread. [823]
  • śaka m. (-kaḥ)
    1 A sovereign, any prince who gives his name to an era, especially applied to Śālivāhana.
    2 A particular caste, the followers or descendants of ŚAKA, or Sālivāhana.
    3 A country, or m. plu. (-kāḥ) The inhabitants, the Sacœ, the Scythians.
    4 An era: see śāka.
    E. śaka to be able, aff. ac. [823]
