• medhya mfn. (-dhyaḥ-dhyā-dhyaṃ)
    1 Pure, purified.
    2 Fit for a sacrifice or oblation. f. (-dhyā) A kind of orris root, described as the red sort.
    3 A sort of pigment; see rocanā. m. (-dhyaḥ)
    1 Khayar, (Mimosa catechu.)
    2 Barley.
    3 A goat.
    E. medha to associate, aff. ṇyat, or medha a sacrifice, and yat aff. [674]
