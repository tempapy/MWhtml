• avatāra n. (-raṃ)
    1 Descent, especially of a deity from heaven; the appearance of any deity upon earth, but more particularly the incarnations of VIṢṆU in ten principal forms which are well known, viz.
    1 The fish.
    2 The tortoise.
    3 The boar.
    4 The man-lion.
    5 The dwarf.
    6 and
    7 The two RĀMAS.
    8 KṚṢṆA.
    9 BUDDHA. And
    10 KALKI. A pious or distinguished person, in the language of respect or flattery, is also called an Avatāra, a descent or incarnation of the deity.
    2 A Tīrtha or sacred place.
    3 Translation, translating.
    3 Descent.
    4 Crossing.
    E. ava before, tṝ to cross, affix ghañ. [078]
