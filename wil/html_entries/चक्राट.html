• cakrāṭa m. (-ṭaḥ)
    1 A juggler, a snake-catcher, one who exhibits snakes and pretends to cure their bites.
    2 A knave, a cheat, a rogue.
    3 A gold coin or certain weight of gold, a Dīnāra.
    E. cakra a number of villages, and aṭa who goes, i. e. a vagabond, &c. [313]
