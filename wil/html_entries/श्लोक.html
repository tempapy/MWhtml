• śloka [866] (ṛ) ślokṛ r. 1st cl. (ślokate) 1 To put together, to compose, as verses. 2 To be put together or composed.
  • śloka [866] m. (-kaḥ) 1 A verse, a stanza. 2 Fame, celebrity. E. śloka to compose, (as verses,) aff. ac; according to the Rāmāyaṇa, irr. derived from śoka sorrow: the first verse ever composed, having been the involuntary expression of VĀLMĪKI'S grief at beholding a bird killed by a fowler.
