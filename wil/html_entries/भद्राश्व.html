• bhadrāśva n. (-śvaṃ) One of the four Mahā Dvīpas, into which the known world is divided, according to some systems; or according to another system, one of the nine Khāṇḍas, or smaller divisions of the continent; in either case it is the east division. m. (-śvaḥ) One of the nine sons of AGNĪDHRA, to whom the division of the earth called Bhadrāśva was assigned, and from whom it was so named.
    E. bhadra auspicious, aśva a horse. [612]
