• pūrṇapāca n. (-caṃ)
    1 A full cup or vessel.
    2 A vessel filled with clothes or ornaments, which are scrambled for by the guests and relations at a festival.
    3 A vessel full of rice, presented at a sacrifice to the superintending and officiating priests; the Pūrṇa Pātra is properly a measure of 256 handfuls of rice; it may also be composed of as much as will satisfy one great eater.
    E. pūrṇa full, and pāca a vessel. [549]
