• tīrtha mn. (-rthaḥ-rthaṃ)
    1 Sacred science, or any of the branches of knowledge esteemed holy.
    2 A holy place, any place of pilgrimage, as Benares, &c. but especially particular spots along the course of sacred streams, as the Ganges, &c. and in the vicinity of some piece of water or sacred springs.
    3 Any piece of water.
    4 A Ghāt or stairs of a landing place.
    5 A sacred preceptor, a Guru.
    6 Sacrifice.
    7 An Avatāra or descent of a deity.
    8 A school of philosophy, a Darśana, a sect.
    9 A counsellor, an adviser.
    10 An expedient, a means of success.
    11 The menses.
    12 Pudendum muliebre.
    13 A vessel.
    14 A royal vessel.
    15 A Brahman.
    16 Ascertainment of disease.
    17 Fire.
    18 A part of the hand sacred to any deity, as between the thumb and finger to the manes, the root of the thumb to BRAHMA, &c.
    E. tṝ to pass over, thak Uṇādi affix; by which persons are extricated from sin, difficulty, &c.
