• rājasūya [702] n. (-yaṃ) 1 A sacrifice performed only by a universal monarch, attended by his tributary princes; as in the case of YUDHIṢṬHIRA, and others. 2 A lotus. 3 A mountain. 4 A sort of rice. E. rāja a king, (by him,) and ṣū to be produced, to be effected, aff. kyap; or rāja the moon, for the moon plant, of which the acid juice is drank at the ceremony, and su to bring forth, the same aff., deriv. irr.
