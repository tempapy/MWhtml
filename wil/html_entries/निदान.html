• nidāna n. (-naṃ)
    1 A first cause, a primary or remote cause.
    2 Disappearance, cessation or removal of a first cause.
    3 Purification, purity, correctness.
    4 Asking for the recompense or objects of austere devotion.
    5 End, cessation.
    6 A rope for tying a calf.
    7 Ascertaining the causes of disease, study of symptoms with a view to trace the remote or proximate causes; hence this word is the name of a division of all works on medicine.
    E. ni always or certainly, dā to give, affix lyuṭ; by which all effects are produced.
