• ṣa The thirty-first consonant of the Nāgarī alphabet, and second sibilant, corresponding to Sh, pronounced in some schools Kh: this letter is usually the first element of the roots, the derivatives of which commence with sa.
  • ṣa mfn. (ṣaḥ-ṣā-ṣaṃ) Best, excellent. m. (ṣaḥ)
    1 A learned man, a teacher.
    2 A nipple.
    3 Loss, destruction.
    4 Rest, remainder.
    5 Loss of knowledge previously acquired.
    6 Eternal happiness, final emancipation.
    7 Heaven, paradise.
    8 Sleep.
    9 End, term. n. (-ṣaṃ)
    1 The embryo.
    2 Patience, endurance.
    E. ṣo to destroy, &c., aff. ka or ḍa and the consonant unchanged, &c.
