• kapila mfn. (-laḥ-lā-laṃ) Tawny. m. (-laḥ)
    1 KAPILA, a celebrated Muni or saint, the founder of the Sāṃkhya system of philosophy: the son of KĀRDDAMA by DEVAHŪTI, and according to some, an avatāra of VIṢṆU.
    2 A title of AGNI the deity of fire.
    3 A dog.
    5 Tawny, (the colour.) f. (-lā)
    1 The female elephant of the south-east.
    2 A kind of Sisu, or timber tree so called: see śiṃśapā.
    3 A sort of perfume: see reṇukā.
    4 The aloe plant.
    5 A fabulous cow, celebrated in the Purāṇas.
    5 The common leech.
    E. kabha to desire, ilac Uṇādi affix, and pa substituted for ma; or kapi a monkey, and la to take, &c. being of such a colour.
