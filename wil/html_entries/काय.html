• kāya mn. (-yaḥ-yaṃ) The body. n. (-yaṃ)
    1 Part of the hand sacred to the creator; the root of the little finger.
    2 Clarified butter or any oblation to BRAHMĀ. m. (-yaḥ)
    1 One of the eight modes of marriage, the same as prājāpatya.
    2 Assemblage, collection.
    3 A butt or mark, an object to be hit or attained.
    4 Natural temperament of any thing or being.
    5 A house, a habitation.
    6 Principal, capital.
    E. ka a name of BRAHMĀ, and aṇ deriv. affix, i is added to ka, and aya is substituted for the short vowel; proceeding from or relating to BRAHMĀ, &c.
