• pañcakoṣā m. plu. (-ṣāḥ) The five sheathes supposed to invest the soul, or the Annamaya koṣa that supported by food the gross form; the Prāṇamaya koṣa, the organs of action; the Manomaya koṣa, the organs of perception, with the manas or mind; the Vijñānamaya, the same with the Buddhi or intellect; and the Ānandamaya, consisting of the elements of identity and divine wisdom.
    E. pañca, and koṣa a sheath. [492]
