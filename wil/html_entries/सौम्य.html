• saumya mfn. (-myaḥ-myā or mī-myaṃ)
    1 Handsome, pleasing.
    2 Mild, gentle, placid.
    3 Sacred or belonging to SOMA or the moon. m. (-myaḥ)
    1 BUDDHA, regent of MERCURY, and son of SOMA or the moon.
    2 One of the nine Khaṇḍas or divisions of the known continent.
    3 A term applied to a pupil when addressing instruction to one.
    4 (In anatomy,) The blood before it receives the red particles, the serum. m. plu. (-myāḥ) Five stars in Orion's head. f. (-myā)
    1 A shrub, (Hedysarum gangeticum.)
    2 Heart-pea.
    3 The Abrus precatorious.
    4 The moon-plant, (Sarcostema acida.)
    5 Zedoary, (Curcuma zerumbet.)
    6 Arabian jasmine.
    E. soma the moon, ṇyat or yat aff., or dyaṇ aff., when the word takes ṅīp in the feminine form. [947]
