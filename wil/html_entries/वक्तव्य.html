• vaktavya mfn. (-vyaḥ-vyā-vyaṃ)
    1 Fit or proper to be said or spoken, to be spoken or uttered.
    2 Vile, bad, reprehensible.
    3 Subject, dependant.
    4 Low, base. n. (-vyaṃ)
    1 A rule, a dictum, a sentence.
    2 Speech.
    E. vaca to speak, and tavya aff., of the future participle. [728]
