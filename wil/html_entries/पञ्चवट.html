• pañcavaṭa [494] m. (-ṭaḥ) The Brahminical thread as worn across the breast; also pañcāvaṭa. f. (-ṭī) 1 Five trees, the Indian and holy fig, the Bel, Grislea tomentosa, and Asoka, placed or planted in several quarters; or east, west, north, south and south-east. 2 Part of the great southern forest where the Godāvarī rises. E. pañca, and vaṭa the Indian fig.
