• tāta mfn. (-taḥ-tā-taṃ) Venerable, reverend, respectable. m. (-taḥ)
    1 A father.
    2 A term of affection addressed to any person, but especially to a junior or inferior, as he tāta oh! child, &c.
    E. tana to extend, (his race or fame,) kta affix, and the vowel made long. [371]
