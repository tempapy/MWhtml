• yamaka mfn. (-kaḥ-kā-kaṃ) Twin, fellow, one of a pair or twins. m. (-kaḥ) A religious obligation or observance. n. (-kaṃ)
    1 A species of alliteration, the repetition of similar words or syllables either in a similar or a dissimilar sense in various parts of a stanza, beginning, middle, or end of each hemistich, or pervading the whole verse: ten varieties are enumerated.
    2 A couple of like articles; (in medicine,) serum and marrow: (in diet,) oil and ghee, &c.
    E. kan added to the preceding. [681]
