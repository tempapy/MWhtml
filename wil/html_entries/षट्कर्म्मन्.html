• ṣaṭkarmman m. (-rmmā)
    1 A Brahman.
    2 An adept, (in the Tantras, &c.) n. (-rmma or pl. rmmāṇi)
    1 The six acts proper for a Brahman, (collectively,) or teaching the Vedas, holy study, offering sacrifices, conducting them for others, giving, accepting gifts.
    2 Six acts allowable to a Brahman for subsistence, or gleaning, accepting gifts, asking alms, agriculture, trade, and tending cattle, or according to some lending money at interest.
    3 The six acts taught in the Tantras, or killing, infatuating, enthralling, expelling, exciting animosity, and the stopping or privation of any faculty.
    E. ṣaṣ six, karmman act or duty.
