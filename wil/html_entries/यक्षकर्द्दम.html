• yakṣakarddama m. (-maḥ) Perfumed paste, used especially for anointing the body, and consisting of camphor, agallochum, musk and Kakkola; or the three first, with saffron and Sandal in equal proportions.
    E. yakṣa the demigod, and karddama clay; a perfume of which these beings are supposed to be fond. [677]
