• nātha [460] (ṛ) nāthṛ r. 1st cl. (nāthati-te) 1 To ask or beg. 2 To bless. 3 To destroy. 4 To have wealth or power. 5 To be deceased.
  • nātha [460] m. (-thaḥ) 1 A master, a lord. 2 A name of ŚIVA, especially in the form of a Liṅga; it is usually compounded with some word relating to the legendary history of the Liṅga, as Somanātha, the Liṅga a set up by SOMA, &c. 3 A name adopted by a class of Yogis, as GORAKHNĀTH, &c. 4 A rope passed through the nose of a draft ox. E. nātha to ask, (from whom,) and ac aff.
