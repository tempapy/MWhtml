• vāṇa m. (-ṇaḥ)
    1 An arrow.
    2 A name of fire.
    3 The name of a sovereign considered also as an Asura or infernal being, the son of BĀLĪ, and destroyed by VIṢṆU.
    4 The udder of a cow.
    5 Alone, solitary.
    6 A pipe, a fife, a flute. mf. (-ṇaḥ-ṇā)
    1 Blue Barleria.
    2 The root or feathered part of an arrow.
    E. vaṇa or baṇa to sound or go, aff. ghañ; whence this word and other derivatives are sometimes also written with an initial ba. [748]
