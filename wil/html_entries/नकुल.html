• nakula [450] mfn. (-laḥ-lā-laṃ) Disgraced, rejected from a family or race. m. (-laḥ) 1 The Bengal mungoose, (Viverra ichneumon.) 2 A proper name, NAKULA, the fourth of the five Pāṇḍu princes. 3 A son. 4 A name of ŚIVA. f. (-lī) 1 Silk cotton tree. 2 Indian spikenard. 3 A sort of perfume: see śaṅkhinī. 4 Saffron. 5 The letter ha as used in the Tantras. E. na not, kula race or heap, fem. affix ṅīṣ.
