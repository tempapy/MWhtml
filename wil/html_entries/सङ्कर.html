• saṅkara [879] m. (-raḥ) 1 Mixing, blending, confounding. 2 A mixed caste or race, one proceeding from the promiscuous intercourse of the four tribes in the first instance, and again from their commerce with the descendants of such a connexion, or the indiscriminate cohabitation of those descendants amongst one another: most Hindus of the present age are of one of the many branches of this race, the highest of which is impure and inferior to the Śūdra: under this term two kinds of men are included according to the Rāmāyaṇa; the one denominated yonisaṅkara, mixed or degraded by birth; and ācārasaṅkara degraded by conduct. 3 Dust, sweepings. 4 The crackling of flame. E. sam before kṝ to scatter, or kṛ to make, aff. ap.
