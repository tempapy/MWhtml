• trivarga [391] m. (-rgaḥ) 1 Three human objects or pursuits, as love, duty, and wealth. 2 Three conditions of a king or state; prosperity, evenness, and decay; or loss, gain, equality, &c. 3 The three qualities of nature, purity, blindness, and depravity. 4 The three myrobalans. 5 The three spices: see trikaṭu. 6 Propriety, good behaviour. E. tri three, and varga a class; a class or aggregate of three.
