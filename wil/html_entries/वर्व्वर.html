• varvvara [739] mfn. subst. (-raḥ-rā or -rī-raṃ) A sort of basil, (Ocymum pilosum, Rox.) m. (-raḥ) 1 A low man, one of a degraded tribe or occupation, an out-caste, a barbarians. 2 A region, a country, the country inhabited by barbarians. 3 Woolly or curly hair, as the hair of an African. 4 A shrub, (Shiponanthus Indica.) 5 A sort of worm. 6 The noise or clash of weapons. 7 A mode of dancing. f. (-rā-rī) 1 A sort of potherb. 2 A small bee. 3 The Baya or Indian grosbeak, (Loxia philippensis.) n. (-raṃ) 1 Vermilion. 2 Gum myrrh. 3 A sort of Sandal wood. E. vṛ to choose, Uṇādi aff. karakṣ, or the same root, with ac aff., in the reiterative form; it is also derived from barba to go, and is variously written barbbara, varśara, varvvara, and varvvara, &c.
