• māya m. (-yaḥ)
    1 A mime, a juggler.
    2 An Asura or evil spirit. f. (-yā)
    1 Fraud, trick, deceit, illusion.
    2 A female juggler.
    3 Understanding, human intellect.
    4 Compassion.
    5 Wickedness, villainy, villainous deception.
    6 A name of LAKṢMĪ.
    7 Trick in negotiation, political fraud, diplomacy.
    8 Philosophical illusion, idealism, unreality of all worldly existence; personified in mythology as a female, the consort of BRAHMĀ or GOD, and the immediate and active cause of creation.
    9 The mother of BUDDHA.
    E. mā to measure, Uṇādi aff. ya; the medium through which all things are seen, and by which they are estimated.
