• maṅgalya [631] mfn. (-lyaḥ-lyā-lyaṃ) 1 Auspicious, propitious, conferring happiness, prosperity, &c. 2 Beautiful, pleasing, agreeable. 3 Pure, pious. m. (-lyaḥ) 1 A plant, commonly Trāyamāṇa. 2 The holy fig tree, (Ficus religiosa.) 3 The Vilva, (Ægle marmelos.) 4 A sort of pulse, (Cicer lens.) f. (-lyā) 1 A fragrant sort of Agallochum or aloe wood. 2 A kind of Mimosa, (M. Suma, Rox.) 3 A herb, commonly Gojihva, (Hieracium, or according to Rox.'s Catalogue, a herb of the same class, Elephantopus scaber.) 4 A sort of grass, (Andropogon aciculatum.) 5 A sort of fennel, (Anethum sowa.) 6 A sort of perfume, commonly Rochana. 7 A kind of Orris root, (Acorus calamus.) n. (-lyaṃ) 1 Sour curds. 2 Sandal. 3 Red lead. 4 Gold. E. maṅgala prosperous, and yat aff.
