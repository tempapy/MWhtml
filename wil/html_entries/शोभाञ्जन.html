• śobhāñjana m. (-naḥ) A tree, commonly Sajinā or Sahajnā, (Hyperanthera morunga;) the legumes, blossoms, and leaves are esculent, and the root of the young tree is used as a substitute for horse-radish: in medicine the root is used as a rubefacient externally, as a stimulant internally, and the expressed oil of the seeds is employed to relieve arthritic pains, &c.
    E. śobhā beauty, añja to pervade or anoint, and lyuṭ or yuc aff; also śaubhāñjana and sobhāñjana, &c.
