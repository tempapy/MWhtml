• plakṣa r. 1st cl. (plakṣati-te) To eat. [593]
  • plakṣa m. (-kṣaḥ)
    1 Waved leaf fig tree, (Ficus infectoria.)
    2 Another tree, (Hibiscus populneoides.)
    3 The holy fig, (Ficus religiosa.)
    4 One of the seven Dvīpas or continents, into which the world is divided.
    5 A private or back door.
    E. pluṣa to burn, Uṇādi aff. sa, and a substituted for the penultimate vowel. [593]
