• śākha (ṛ) śākhṛ r. 1st cl. (śākhate) To pervade.
  • śākha m. (-khaḥ) A plant, (Galedupa arborea.) f. (-khā)
    1 A branch, the branch of a tree.
    2 A branch or subdivision of the Vedas, consisting of the several Saṃhitās or collections of prayers in each Veda, as received in different schools, modified more or less either in the arrangement of the whole text, or in particular portions of it.
    3 An arm.
    4 A sect, a faction, a party.
    5 Any subdivision.
    6 Any part of an animal not endowed with sensibility, as a horn, &c.
    E. śākha to pervade, aff. ac.
