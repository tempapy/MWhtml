• kācalavaṇa n. (-ṇaṃ) Black salt, a medicinal salt, prepared by calcining fossile salt and the fruit of the emblic myrobalan together. It consists chiefly or muriate of soda, with a small quantity of iron, lime and sulphur, and is a tonic aperient.
    E. kāca alkaline ashes, and lavaṇa salt.
