• vitarddhi [766] f. (-rddhiḥ-rddhī) 1 A quadrangular building, or sort of covered terrace of wood in the centre of the court-yard of a temple or palace. 2 A quadrangular resting place made of wood, a seat, a bench. 3 A place in a court-yard for sitting in or standing under. 4 A floor on four posts in the midst of a quadrangular house. 5 A temporary stage or shed in the middle of a court-yard, for performing sacrifices, representing plays, &c. 6 A terrace furnished with pillars, a verandah, a balcony. 7 A covered temporary terrace on which idols are sometimes placed and worshipped. E. vi privative, tarda to injure, aff. in; also ṅīṣ optionally added, and with kan added in the fem. form vitarddhikā.
