• pārvvaṇa m. (-ṇaḥ)
    1 A sort of deer.
    2 The general funeral ceremony to be offered to all the manes at the Parva, or conjunction of the sun and moon, at which double oblations are offered; three cakes to the father, paternal grandfather, and great grandfather; and three to the maternal grandfather, his father, and grandfather; and the crumbs of each set to the remoter ancestors in each line.
    E. parvva a knot, &c. aff. aṇ. [530]
