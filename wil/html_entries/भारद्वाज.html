• bhāradvāja [617] m. (-jaḥ) 1 A name of DROṆA, the military preceptor of the Kurus, and Pāṇḍus. 2 One of the seven Ṛṣis. 3 A name of the Muni AGASTYA. 4 The son of VṚHASPATI. 5 A skylark. n. (-jaṃ) A bone. f. (-jī) Wild cotton. E. bharadvāja a lark, and aṇ pleonastick or derivative aff.; in the latter case it refers to a legend of the Ṛṣi BHARADVĀJA having been nursed by a lark, in consequence of being abandoned at his birth by his natural parents.
