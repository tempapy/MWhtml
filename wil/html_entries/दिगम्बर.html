• digambara [407] mfn. (-raḥ-rā-rī-raṃ) 1 Naked, unclad, unclothed. 2 Dressed in, or enveloped by the atmosphere, clouds, &c. m. (-raḥ) 1 A Bauddha mendicant, wearing coloured clothes, or going naked. 2 Any mendicant not wearing clothes. 3 Darkness. 4 A name of ŚIVA, (from his being naked.) 5 A Jain of one great division, which represents the images of their saints, either naked or plainly attired. f. (-rī) A name of DURGĀ. E. dik inflection of diś space, ambara vestment; whose only garment is the atmosphere.
