• hariścandra [970] m. (-ndraḥ) A sovereign, the twenty-eighth of the solar dynasty in the second age, celebrated for his piety and liberality, and who on those accounts was elevated, together with his subjects, to heaven: having been insidiously induced by NĀRADA, to relate his actions with unbecoming pride, he descended from Svarga, a stage at each sentence, till stopping in time, and doing homage to the gods, he was fixed with his capital in mid-air. E. hari VIṢṆU, and candra the moon.
