• brāhmaṇa m. (-ṇaḥ) A man of the first Hindu tribe, a Brahman. n. (-ṇaṃ)
    1 An assemblage of Brahmans, a council, a conclave.
    2 A portion of the Vedas, that which comprises the precepts that inculcate religious duties, the maxims which explain those precepts, and the arguments which relate to theology; it is, however, now often blended with other portions. f. (-ṇī)
    1 The wife of a Brahman, or a woman of the Brahmanical tribe.
    2 A wasp.
    3 A small ant.
    4 A potherb, (Ruta graveolens.)
    5 A shrub, (Siphonanthus Indica.)
    6 A sort of grass, (Trigonella corniculata.)
    E. brahman the Veda, &c. aṇ aff., and the final syllable of the original word retained.
