• plava mfn. (-vaḥ-vā-vaṃ) Who or what goes by leaps or jumps, leaping, jumping, a tumbler. m. (-vaḥ)
    1 Jumping, leaping, plunging, going by leaps or plunges.
    2 Swimming, floating, diving.
    3 A raft, a float.
    4 A frog.
    5 A monkey.
    6 A sheep.
    7 A diver, or bird so called, (Pelicanus fusicollis.)
    8 A sort of duck.
    9 A man of a low or degraded tribe.
    10 Waved-leaf fig tree, (Ficus infectoria.)
    11 The continuous protracted accent, the lengthened sound of vowels in poetry or the Vedas.
    12 Protracting a sentence through several stanzas.
    13 A declivity or shelving ground.
    14 A piece of water.
    15 A sort of basket or snare of basket work for catching fish.
    16 An enemy.
    17 Sending, directing. n. (-vaṃ)
    1 A sort of grass, (Cyperus rotundus.)
    2 Fragrant grass in general, or another sort.
    E. plu to go, aff. ap.
