• śakvara m. (-raḥ) A bull, an ox. f. (-rī)
    1 A finger.
    2 A river.
    3 A zone, a girdle.
    4 A form of metre, a stanza of four lines of fourteen syllables each, and comprising many varieties according to the different feet of which the lines are composed.
    E. śaka to be able or strong, kvarap aff., or vanip aff. with ra augment, and the final rejected: the feminine form is also derived from śakvan with ṅīp affix, and ra augment, and the word is also read śakkara and śakkarī. [825]
