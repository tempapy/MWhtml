• mūrddhābhiṣikta m. (-ktaḥ)
    1 A man of the second or military class, the Kṣatriya or soldier.
    2 A king, a prince.
    3 A chief minister, a counsellor of state.
    4 The name of a mixed class, sprung from a Brahman, and female of the second or Kṣatriya tribe; the principal of the mixed races, and soldier by profession.
    E. mūrddha the head, and abhiṣikta sprinkled, anointed; kings being consecrated by having poured on their heads, while seated on a throne prepared for the purpose, water from some sacred stream, mixed with honey, clarified butter, and spirituous liquor, as well as two sorts of grass and the sprouts of corn: the term applies to the Kṣatriya as identified with the king, the duties of royalty, belonging especially, to the military caste.
