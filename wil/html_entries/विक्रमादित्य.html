• vikramāditya m. (-tyaḥ) The name of a celebrated prince, the sovereign of Ougein, and reputed founder of an æra still in use amongst the Hindus, commencing 56 years before the Christian æra; there are however many princes of this name, and it has been applied to Rāja BHOJA, to ŚĀLIVĀHANA, and PṚTHVĪ Rāja, as well as to five or six others; the name also occurs variously written, as VIKRAMA, ĀDITYA, VIKRAMASENA, VIKRAMASIṂHA, VIKRAMĀRKA, &c.
    E. vikrama power, and āditya the sun. [760]
