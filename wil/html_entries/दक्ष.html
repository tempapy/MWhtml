• dakṣa r. 1st cl. (dakṣate)
    1 To grow or increase.
    2 To dispatch, to expedite, to do quickly.
    3 To go, to move.
    4 To hurt or kill. [394]
  • dakṣa mfn. (-kṣaḥ-kṣā-kṣaṃ) Clever, able, dexterous. m. (-kṣaḥ)
    1 A Brahmādika; according to one legend, son of BRAHMĀ, born from the thumb of his right hand, for the purpose of peopling the world: according to another, he is the son of the Munis, called Prācetasas. DAKṢA had sixty daughters, of whom twenty-seven are the nymphs who form the lunar asterisms, and wives of the moon; one of his daughters also was SATI or DURGĀ, the wife of ŚIVA; and seventeen were married to KAŚYAPA, and were the mothers of all created beings. DAKṢA was on one occasion decapitated by ŚIVA: see dakṣādhvaradhvaṃsakṛt; he is sometimes regarded as an Avatāra of BRAHMĀ himself.
    2 A Muni and legislator, who flourished in the beginning of the Tretā Yuga.
    3 A kind of tree.
    4 The bull of ŚIVA.
    5 A cock.
    6 A scholar, a Paṇḍita.
    7 A dexterous or clever man.
    8 ŚIVA.
    9 A name of Agni of fire.
    10 A general lover, one attached to many mistresses. f. (-kṣā) The earth.
    E. dakṣa to grow or increase, affix ac. [394]
