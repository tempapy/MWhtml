• gaura mfn. (-raḥ-rā-rī-raṃ)
    1 White.
    2 Yellow.
    3 Pale red: (or it may be used as a noun substantive mas. to imply either of these colours.)
    4 Clean, cleansed, pure. m. (-raḥ)
    1 White mustard.
    2 The moon.
    3 A tree, (Grislea tomentosa:) see dhava.
    4 A name of CAITANYA. n. (-raṃ)
    1 The filament of a lotus.
    2 Gold.
    3 Saffron. f. (-rā or rī)
    1 A name of the goddess PĀRVATĪ. (-rī)
    2 A young girl, eight years old.
    3 Any young girl prior to menstruation, a maid, a virgin.
    4 The name of a river.
    5 The wife of the deity VARUṆA.
    6 The earth.
    7 Turmeric.
    8 A yellow die called Gorocanā.
    9 A plant: see rocanī.
    10 A plant bearing a fragrant seed: see priyaṅgu.
    11 One of the female energies or Śaktis of the Bauddhas.
    12 A white kind of Dūrbbā or bent grass.
    13 Arabian jasmin.
    14 Sacred basil or Tulasi.
    15 One of the Rāgiṇīs.
    E. guḍa to sound, and ran Uṇādi affix, the deriv. is irregular; also gurī to endeavour, ghañ affix, and derivative irr.; upon whom or what the mind exerts itself; again, gaura white, pure, &c. affix ṅīṣ, that which is white or brilliant, &c. which applies to the virgin, &c.
