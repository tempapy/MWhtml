• puroḍāśa m. (-śaḥ)
    1 Ghee or clarified butter as offered in oblations to fire, with cakes of ground barley meal, that have been well steeped in it.
    2 The orts, or leavings of any substance, used in an oblation to fire.
    3 A sort of flat ladle or spoon, used for placing the cakes in the sacrificial fire.
    4 The juice of the acid asclepias as drank at certain sacrifices.
    5 A Mantra, or prayer recited in offering oblations to fire.
    E. puras first, diśa to shew, aff. ac, deriv. irr.; the first ceremony on all sacred occasions. [544]
