• dhanvantari [438] m. (-riḥ) 1 The physician of the gods, who was produced at the churning of the ocean. 2 A celebrated physician; also kāśirāja being the same as the preceding in another existence: he appears to have been the founder of the Hindu medical school. 3 The sun. 4 A name of ŚIVA. 5 One of the nine gems of the court of VIKRAMĀDITYA.
