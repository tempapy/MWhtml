• kuṇḍa m. (-ṇḍaḥ) A son born in adultery, the son of a woman by another man than her husband, while the husband is alive. n. (-ṇḍaṃ)
    1 A hole in the ground for receiving and preserving consecrated fire.
    2 A pool, a well, a spring or basin of water, especially consecrated to some holy purpose or person.
    2 A measure. mf. (-ṇḍaḥ-ṇḍī) A pitcher. f. (-ṇḍī) A student's water-pot, made of a hollow cocoanut, &c.
    E. kuḍi to preserve, ḍa aff. [228]
