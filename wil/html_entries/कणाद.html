• kaṇāda n. (-daḥ)
    1 The name of a Muni, also called Kaśyapa: the author of the Vaiśeṣika philosophy.
    2 A goldsmith.
    E. kaṇa a little, and ada to eat, referring to the abstemiousness of the saint; or ādā to take, alluding to the proverbial dishonesty of the artificer.
