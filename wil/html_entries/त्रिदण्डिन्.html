• tridaṇḍin [389] m. (-ṇḍī) 1 A wandering devotee, one who carries three long bambu staves in his right hand. 2 The religious man who has obtained a command over his words, thoughts, and actions, or mind, body, and speech. E. tri three, and daṇḍa a punishment, a staff, &c. ini aff.
