• ātharvvaṇa m. (-ṇaḥ)
    1 A family priest.
    2 A Brahman versed in the Atharvan Veda. n. (-ṇaṃ)
    1 A collection of prayers, &c. delivered by ATHARVAN, a sage.
    2 A private apartment in which, after a sacrifice, the sacrificer receives absolution from the officiating Brahman.
    E. atharvvaṇa a Veda, and aṇ aff.
