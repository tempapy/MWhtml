• ṭa The 11th consonant of the Nāgarī alphabet, and first of the 3rd class or cerebrals. It corresponds in sound to the letter T pronounced far back in the mouth, or as in true, and is expressed in the roman character by the accented letter T. [359]
  • ṭa m. (-ṭaḥ)
    1 Sound, or a particular sound, like the twang of a bow strong.
    2 A dwarf.
    3 A quarter, a fourth. n. (-ṭaṃ) A hollowed cocoanut. f. (ṭā)
    1 The earth.
    2 An oath, confirming an assertion by ordeal, &c.
    E. ṭaka to bind, &c. affix ḍa. [359]
