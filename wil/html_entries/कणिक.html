• kaṇika [183] m. (-kaḥ) 1 A small particle, as a crystal, an eye of corn, &c. 2 An enemy. 3 Whirling round a set of lamps at sacrificial ceremonies. 4 The heart of wheat, commonly Suji, sharps or semoule. f. (-kā) 1 An atom, a small particle. 2 Small, minute: see kaṇa. 3 A plant, (Premna spinosa:) see gaṇikārikā. 4 A kind of rice. E. kaṇa small, vun affix, and iṭ inserted, fem. affix ṭāp.
