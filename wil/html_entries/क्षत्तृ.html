• kṣattṛ mfn. (-ttā-ttrī-ttṛ) Occupied, engaged in work. m. (-ttā)
    1 A charioteer, a coachman.
    2 A doorkeeper, a porter.
    3 The son of a Śūdra by a Kṣatriya woman, whose employment is catching animals that live in holes.
    4 The son of a female slave.
    5 A name of BRAHMĀ.
    6 The son of a Śūdra man and Vaiśya woman.
    E. kṣada a Sautra root, to screen or defend, to join or mix, Uṇādi affix tṛc. [261]
