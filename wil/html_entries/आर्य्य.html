• āryya mfn. (-ryyaḥ-ryyā-ryyaṃ)
    1 Of a good family.
    2 Respectable, venerable.
    3 Apposite, proper.
    4 To be sought or obtained. m. (-ryyaḥ)
    1 A master, an owner.
    2 A name of BUDDHA.
    3 A friend. f. (-ryyāṃ)
    1 A name of PĀRVATĪ.
    2 A kind of metre, measured by feet, each foot being equal to four short syllables, or two long and two short; there are seven and a half feet in each verse of the couplet, equal to thirty and to twenty-seven syllabic instants: the regular form consists of alternate long and short verses, but it admits of eighty principal variations.
    E. ṛ to go, ṇyat affix; also aryya.
