• vāna mfn. subst. (-naḥ-nā-naṃ) Dry fruit. adj. Dry, dried. n. (-naṃ)
    1 Living.
    2 Going, moving.
    3 A mat of straw.
    4 A heavy sea, the rolling of water from wind, &c. hence the high tide in the Indian rivers, commonly called the Bore.
    5 A hole in the wall of a house.
    6 A perfume, a fragrance. mfn. (-naḥ-nī-naṃ) Relating to a wood, a house, &c.: see vana. n. (-naṃ) A number or multitude of woods.
    E. vai to dry or wither, aff. kta; form irr.: in other of its applications vana to sound, aff. ghañ; or vana a wood, water, a house, &c., and aṇ referential aff.
