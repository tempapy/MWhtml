• kālapuruṣa m. (-ṣaḥ)
    1 One of YAMA'S attendants or ministers.
    2 An astrological figure of a man, with the positions of the constellations, &c. inscribed on different parts of the body for the purpose of predicting his fortunes; also kālanara, &c.
    E. kāla black, and puruṣa a man. [217]
