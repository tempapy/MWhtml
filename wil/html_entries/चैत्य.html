• caitya [331] m. (-tyaḥ) A sacred tree, a religious fig tree, &c. growing in a village or near it, and held in veneration by the villagers. n. (-tyaṃ) 1 A place of sacrifice or religious worship, an altar, a shed kept for sacrifices, &c. 2 A religious building peculiar to the Jainas, a Jaina temple. 3 A Jaina or Bauddha image, &c. 4 A monument, a tombstone, a column, &c. erected in memory of some deceased person, or on the site of a funeral pile. 5 A pile of stones forming a landmark or boundary. E. ci to collect, deriv. irr.; or cita to consider, affix kyap, citya, and aṇ aff.
