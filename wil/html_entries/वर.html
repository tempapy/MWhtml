• vara r. 10th cl. (varayati) To desire, to obtain, to covet or ask.
  • vara mfn. (-raḥ-rā-raṃ)
    1 Best, excellent.
    2 Eldest. m. (-raḥ)
    1 A boon, a blessing, especially in the gift of a Brahman or deity.
    2 Engaging, selecting, appointing.
    3 Requesting, soliciting, especially prevailing on a person by intreaty to undertake any business.
    4 Surrounding, encompassing.
    5 A husband, a bridegroom.
    6 A son-in-law.
    7 A catamite.
    8 A drug: see guggula.
    9 Wish, desire.
    10 A sparrow. n. adv. (varaṃ) or ind. (varama) Better, preferable. n. (-raṃ) Saffron. f. (-rā)
    1 The three myrobalans.
    2 A sort of perfume, commonly Reṇuka.
    3 A plant, (Cissampelos hexandra.) f. (-rī)
    1 Asparagus racemosus.
    2 A name of CHĀYĀ, wife of the sun.
    E. vṛ to select, to cover, &c., aff. ap or ac; it is also derived from vṛ, and is then written with an initial consonant: see vara; also vara to desire, aff. ac.
