• brahmavadya [608] n. (-dyaṃ) Knowledge or declaration of BRAHMĀ, the knowledge of the universal permeation of one spirit, as taught by the Vedānta system of philosophy. E. BRAHMĀ or the spiritual deity, vada to speak or declare, aff. kyap; also with the aff. yat, and the semivowel changed, brahmodya n. (-dyaṃ.)
