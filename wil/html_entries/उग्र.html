• ugra mfn. (-graḥ-grā-graṃ)
    1 Angry, wrathful.
    2 Hot, sharp, pungent, acrid.
    3 Formidable, terrible.
    4 Cruel, fierce, savage. m. (-graḥ)
    1 A name of ŚIVA.
    2 A name of a mixed tribe, from a Kṣatriya father and Śūdra mother: the employment of this caste, according to MANU, is the killing or catching of animals that live in holes, as a snake-catcher, &c. according to the Tantras, the Ugra is an encomiast or bard.
    3 A tree, (Morunga hyperanthera, &c.)
    4 A class of asterisms, Maghā, Bharaṇi, Pūrvaphalgunī, Pūrvāṣāḍhā, Pūrvabhadrapada.
    5 A name of Malabar. f. (-grā)
    1 Orris root.
    2 A medicinal plant.
    3 An Ugra woman. n. (-graṃ) Wrath, anger.
    E. uca to accumulate, ran Uṇādi affix, ca is changed to ga. [137]
