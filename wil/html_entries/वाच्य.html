• vācya [747] mfn. (-cyaḥ-cyā-cyaṃ) 1 Vile, bad. 2 Vile, contemptible, low, outcast. 3 Fit or proper to be spoken or said. 4 Attributive, adjective, to be predicated of any thing. 5 Declinable as an adjective, taking the three genders. n. (-cyaṃ) 1 Blame, reviling. 2 A predicate, that which may be said of any thing. 3 (In grammar,) The voice or mode of a verb: karttṛvācyaṃ the active voice, karmmaṇivācyaṃ the passive voice. E. vaca to speak, aff. ṇyat.
