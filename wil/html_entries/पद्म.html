• padma [500] mn. (-dmaḥ-dmaṃ) 1 A lotus, (Nelumbium speciosum;) it is often confounded with the water lily, (Nymphæa.) 2 A form of array. 3 One of KUVERA'S treasures or gems. 4 A large number, ten billions. 5 Coloured marks on the face and trunk of an elephant. 6 A drug, also termed Padmakāṣṭha. 7 Lead. 8 A name given to the six Cakras of the body, or to the mystical faculties present in them. m. (-dmaḥ) 1 A Nāga or serpent of the lower regions. 2 One of the twelve Cakravartīs or paramount princes of the Jainas. 3 One of the nine persons termed Śukla Balas by the Jainas. 4 A name of RĀMA. 5 The personified treasure of KUVERA, as worshipped by the Tāntrikas. f. (-dmā) 1 A name of LAKṢMĪ. 2 A plant, (Hibiscus mutabilis.) 3 A shrub, (Siphonanthus Indica.) 4 A female serpent or Nāga, the goddess MANASĀ, and wife to the sage JARATKĀRU. 5 The mother of the twentieth Jina or Jaina saint. 6 The flower of the Carthamus or safflower. E. pada to go, (on the water, &c.) Uṇādi aff. mam.
