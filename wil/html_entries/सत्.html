• sat mfn. (-san-satī-sat)
    1 True.
    2 Good, virtuous.
    3 Being, existing.
    4 Excellent, best.
    5 Venerable, respectable.
    6 Wise, learned.
    7 Firm, steady.
    1 Right, proper. f. (-satī)
    1 A virtuous wife; in ordinary use applied especially to the wife who burns herself with her husband's corpse.
    2 The goddess UMĀ.
    3 A fragrant earth, commonly Surat earth.
    4 A species of the Pratiṣṭhā metre. n. (sat) The true God, the always present and all-pervading spirit. ind. (sat) In composition, a particle of reverence or respect, implying, good, right, fit, &c. as satkriyā virtue, doing what is right, &c.
    E. asa to be, aff. of the participle of the present tense śatṛ.
