• meda(ṛ)medṛ r. 1st cl. (medati-te) To understand, to kill or injure. [673]
  • meda m. (-daḥ)
    1 Adeps, fat, the supposed proper seat of which is the abdomen.
    2 One of the outcast tribes. f. (-dā) A drug, described as a sort of root resembling ginger, brought from the Morung district; it is one of the eight principal medicaments, and is said to be of cooling and emollient properties, and of particular use in fever and consumption.
    E. mida to be greasy or unctuous, aff. ghañ; also medas. [673]
