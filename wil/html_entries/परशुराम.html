• paraśurāma m. (-maḥ) A hero and demigod; the first of the three RĀMAS, and the sixth Avatāra or descent of the deity VIṢṆU; who appeared in the world as the son of the saint JAMADAGNI, for the purpose of repressing the tyranny and punishing the violence of the Kṣatriya, or military tribe; PARAŚURĀMA appears to typify the tribe of Brahmans and their contests with the Kṣetriyas.
    E. paraśu an axe, and rāma who delights in; also with kan added, paraśurāmaka; and with the first member, parśu, parśurāma, q. v.
