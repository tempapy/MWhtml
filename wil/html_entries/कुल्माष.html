• kulmāṣa mn. (-ṣaḥ-ṣaṃ) Sour gruel prepared by the spontaneous fermentation of the water of boiled rice. n. (-ṣaṃ)
    1 Half ripe barley.
    2 Forced rice.
    3 A sort of Phaseolus: see rājamāṣa.
    4 A species of Dolichos, (D. biflorus.) see yāvaka.
    5 A disease. m. (-ṣaḥ) A dish consisting of half boiled rice, with pulse, &c. commonly Khicri.
    E. kula a class or race, maṣa or masa to hurt or kill, aṇ affix; hence the word is also written kulmāsa. [235]
