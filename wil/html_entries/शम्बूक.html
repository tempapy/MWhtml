• śambūka mf. (-kaḥ-kā) A bivalve shell. m. (-kaḥ)
    1 A conch shell.
    2 A snail.
    3 A Śūdra, who had become a devotee in the time of RĀMACANDRA, and was slain by him.
    4 The edge of the frontal protuberance of the elephant.
    5 The name of a Daitya.
    E. śama to be tranquil, ūkan aff., and vuk augment; also with the vowel short, śambuka, and the final consonant then rejected, śambu; it is also read, śambukka, and with the first vowel long, śāmbūka, &c. [830]
