• sarvvatobhadra mfn. (-draḥ-drā-draṃ) Every where auspicious. mn. (-draḥ-draṃ) A temple or palace of a square form, with an entrance opposed to each point of the compass. m. (-draḥ)
    1 The Nimb tree, (Melia azadirachta.)
    2 The carriage of VIṢṆU.
    3 A bambu.
    4 A form of military array.
    5 A square mystical diagram, to be painted on the cloth which on particular occasions covers a sort of altar erected to VIṢṆU.
    6 A kind of charade, in which the same word answers several questions.
    7 A whimsical form of verse, so contrived that the same meanings and words occur, whether the line be read backwards or forwards, or in several other directions. f. (-drā)
    1 A tree, (Gmelina arborea.)
    2 An actress or the wife of an actor or dancer, &c.
    3 A sort of yam, (Dioscorea.)
    E. sarvvatas on every side, bhadra auspicious.
