• vedi f. (-diḥ-dī)
    1 Ground for placing the vessels used at an oblation, or for binding the victim, or lighting the sacrificial fire; it is more or less raised and of various shapes; an altar, &c.
    2 A quadrangular spot in the court-yard of a temple or palace, usually furnished with a raised floor or seat, and covered with a roof supported by pillars: see vitardi.
    3 A seal ring. m. (-diḥ) A Paṇḍita, a teacher. f. (-diḥ) A name of SARASVATĪ, the wife of BRAHMĀ.
    E. vida to know, &c. Uṇādi aff. in, and ṅīṣ optionally added in the fem. form.
