  • kheṭa mfn. (-ṭaḥ-ṭā-ṭaṃ)
    1 Vile, bad, low.
    2 Armed, having a weapon or weapons. mn. (-ṭaḥ-ṭaṃ) Hunting, the chase; also ākheṭa. m. (-ṭaḥ)
    1 A shield.
    2 A village, the residence of peasants or farmers.
    3 A small town, half the Pura or town.
    4 The club of BALARĀMA.
    5 Phlegm, the phlegmatic or watery humour.
    6 The ascending node or RĀHU.
    7 A horse. n. (-ṭaṃ) Grass.
    E. khiṭa to terrify, &c. affix ghañ. [274]
