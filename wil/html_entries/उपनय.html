• upanaya m. (-yaḥ) The initiation of the three first classes; investiture with a peculiar thread or cord worn over the left shoulder, and under the right: the cord of the Brahman is of cotton, Munja, or Kusa grass; of the Kṣatriya, of Śaṇa or flax; and of the Vaiśya, of wool: the youths should be invested, respectively, from eight to sixteen, from eleven to twenty-two, and from twelve to twenty-four years of age. n. (-naṃ)
    1 Attaining, gaining.
    2 Bringing to.
    E. upa over, ṇīñ to obtain, ac affix; also with ghañ affix upanāya, and with lyuṭ affix upanayana. [155]
