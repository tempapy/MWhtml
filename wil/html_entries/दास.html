• dāsa [407] (ṛ) dāsṛ r. 1st cl. (dāsati-te) To give; also dāśa r. 5th cl. (dāsnoti) To hurt, to injure, to wound or kill: this and dāśa, in the last sense, are restricted by some authorities to the Vedas.
  • dāsa [407] m. (-saḥ) 1 A fisherman. 2 A servant, a slave. 3 A Śūdra or man of the fourth tribe. 4 A Śūdra affix or appellation. 5 A person to whom it is proper to make gifts. 6 A sage, one to whom the proper nature of the soul is known. f. (-sī) 1 A female servant or slave. 2 The wife of a slave, a fisherman or Śūdra. 3 A harlot. 4 A plant, a sort of Barleria. E. dāsa to give, affix ghañ, to whom wages, &c. are given; also dāśa; otherwise, daśa or daṃśa to bite, &c. Uṇādi affix, ṭan, and the short vowel and nasal changed to ā.
