• tala r. 1st and 10th cls. (talati tālayati)
    1 To be full or complete.
    2 To fix, to found.
    3 To establish.
  • tala n. (-laṃ)
    1 Essential nature, (in composition especially, as mahītalaṃ the earth itself, the very earth.)
    2 Depth, bottom, lowness, inferiority of position, the place under or underneath.
    3 A wood, a forest.
    4 A hole, a pit, a chasm.
    5 Cause, origin, motive, the root or seed of events.
    6 Sole of the foot.
    7 A slap with the hand. nf. (-laṃ-lā) A leathern fence worn by archers on the left arm. m. (-laḥ)
    1 The palmyra tree.
    2 The palm with extended fingers.
    3 The hilt or handle of a sword, &c.
    4 Pressing the strings of a lute with the left hand.
    3 The stand or support of any thing, that which is under or below it.
    6 The fore arm.
    7 A span.
    E. tala to fix, affix ac.
