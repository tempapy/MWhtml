• śramaṇa [861] mfn. (-ṇaḥ-ṇā-ṇaṃ) 1 Following a low or degraded business or profession. 2 Base, vile, bad. m. (-ṇaḥ) 1 A religious character, an ascetic, a Yati, one devoted to meditation for the purpose of obtaining final emancipation from existence, probably the Sarmanes of the Greek writers. 2 A beggar, the religious mendicant. f. (-ṇā or ṇī) 1 Indian spikenard, (Valeriana Jatamansi.) 2 Bengal madder, (Rubia manjith.) 3 A handsome woman. 4 A woman of low caste or business. 5 A female mendicant. E. śrama to be wearied, aff. yuc.
