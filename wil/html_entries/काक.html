• kāka m. (-kaḥ)
    1 A crow.
    2 A plant, (Ardisia solanacea?)
    3 A lame man, a cripple, one whose legs are wanting or useless.
    4 One of the divisions of the world or Dvīpas.
    5 A measure.
    6 Washing the head, bathing by dipping the head only into the water.
    7 A sectarial mark, the Tilaka?
    8 Shameless, arrogant. n. (-kaṃ)
    1 Modus coeundi.
    2 A multitude or assemblage of crows. f. (-kā) An appellation of several plants, as,
    1 The common Bakapuṣpa: see vaka,
    2 Kakjhangi, (Leea hirta;) also in this sense kākī;
    3 A medicinal plant, vulg. Kākoli;
    4 The Ratti or Gunja, (Abrus precatorius;)
    5 Opposite leaved fig tree: see uḍumbara;
    6 A potherb, (Solanum Indicum, &c.) see kākamācī.
    E. kai to sound, Uṇādi affix kan; or ka for ku bad, ill, and aka to go, &c.
