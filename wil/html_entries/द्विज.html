• dvija [432] mfn. (-jaḥ-jā-jaṃ) 1 Twice born. 2 Oviparous. m. (-jaḥ) 1 A man of either of the three first classes, a Brahman, a Kṣatriya or a Vaiśya whose investiture with the characteristick string, at years of puberty, constitutes, religiously and metaphorically, their second birth. 2 Any oviparous animal, as a bird, a snake, a fish, &c. first born in the shell, and in the second instance produced from it. 3 A tooth. 4 Coriander seed. f. (-jā) 1 A sort of perfume; also called Reṇukā. 2 A plant, (Siphonanthus Indicus.) 3 Kundur or gum olibanum. E. dvi two, and ja born.
