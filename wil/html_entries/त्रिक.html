• trika [388] n. (-kaṃ) 1 The lower part of the spine. 2 The aggregate of three, a trine, a triad. 3 The three mystical words to be pronounced in daily prayer. 4 A place where three roads meet. 5 Three spices, three myrobalans, &c. collectively: see trikaṭu, triphala and the like. m. (-kaḥ) A plant: see gokṣuraka. f. (-kā) 1 A triangular frame or bar across the mouth of a well over which passes the rope of the bucket, or to which one end of it is tied to guard against, its slipping. 2 A wooden frame at the mouth of a well, or the upper part of the well. 3 A frame at the bottom of a well, on which the masonry rests. 4 The cover or lid of a well. E. tri three, and kan aff.
