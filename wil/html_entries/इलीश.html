• ilīśa m. (-śaḥ) A fish, commonly the Hilsa or Sable, (Clupea alosa.)
    E. ila to go, (in the water,) and īśa chief, principal. The fish being one of high flavour, and very abundant at certain seasons, it is generally eaten both by the natives of Bengal, and by Europeans residing there. [133]
