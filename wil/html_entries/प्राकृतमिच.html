• prākṛtamica m. (-traḥ) A natural friend or ally; as applied to a sovereign, it means one whose states are removed by an intervening kingdom from those to which it should be friendly, the prākṛtāri or natural enemy is the chief of the intervening state, and the prākṛtodāsīnaḥ or natural neutral is the outer one of the four.
    E. prākṛta natural, and mica a friend. [584]
