• kāśa (ṛ) kāśṛ r. 1st cl. (kāśate) also (u, ṛ) (ukāśṛ) r. 4th cl. (kāśyate)
    1 To shine.
    With nir prefixed, To expel,
    2 To hide or obscure;
    with pra, To make or become manifest. [219]
  • kāśa m. (-śaḥ)
    1 Cough, catarrh: see kāsa.
    2 Sneezing. n. (-śaṃ)
    1 A species of grass, (Saccharum spontaneum.)
    2 Irritation in the throat. f. (-śī) Kāśi or Benares, the holy city so called. (-śī or -śā) Saccharum spontaneum, as above.
    E. kaśa to sound, in the causal form, ac affix; or kāśa to shine, ac, and ṅīṣ fem. aff. [219]
