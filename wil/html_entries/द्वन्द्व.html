• dvandva n. (-ndvaṃ)
    1 A pair, a brace.
    2 A couple of animals, or male and female.
    3 Union of the sexes or coupling.
    4 Strife, dispute.
    5 A secret. m. (-ndvaḥ)
    1 A form of grammatical combination uniting two or more words in the same case, properly separated by a conjunction, as rāmalakṣmaṇau RĀMA and LAKṢMAṆA; pāṇipādaṃ hand and foot.
    2 A sign of the zodiac, (Gemini.)
    3 A species of disease, a complication of two disorders, or compound affection of two humours.
    E. dvi two, reduplicate, deriv. irr. [431]
