• sagara mfn. (-raḥ-rā-raṃ) Poisonous. m. (-raḥ) A sovereign of Ayodhyā, the father of ASAMAÑJA by KEŚINĪ, and sixty thousand sons by SUMATI: the latter being turned into a heap of ashes by the sage KAPILA, GARUḌA instructed the king to perform their funeral ceremonies with the waters of Gaṅgā, to be brought from heaven for that purpose; this was finally accomplished by the devotion of BHAGĪRATHA, the great-grandson of ASAMAÑJA, who having led the river to the sea, denominated it Sāgara, in honor of this his ancestor.
    E. sa for saha with, gara poison; being born together with a poison given to his mother by the other wife of his father. [879]
