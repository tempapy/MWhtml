• tapa (au) tapau r. 1st cl. (tapati-te) r. 4th cl. (tapyati-te) and r. 10th cl. (tāpayati-te)
    1 To heat or be hot, to burn; (hence figuratively,) to suffer mental or bodily pain.
    2 To have pre-eminent or superhuman power: the deponent form only is used when the root with the prefix ut or vi occurs intransitively, or governs as its object part of the body of the agent, as pāṇiṃ vitapate warms the hand, (uttapate vitapate) shines, burns, &c. The root takes the passive form also, when implying religious meditation, as tapyate tapastāpasaḥ the devotee revolves religious thoughts.
    With anu prefixed, (anutapate) To repent, to regret.
    With pari or sam (paritapati santapati,)
    1 To bear or inflict pain, anguish, heat, distress, &c.
    2 To be sorrowful, to repent. [365]
  • tapa m. (-paḥ)
    1 The hot season, summer.
    2 Heat, warmth.
    3 The sun.
    E. tapa to heat, affix ac. [366]
