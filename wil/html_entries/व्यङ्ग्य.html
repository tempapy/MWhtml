• vyaṅgya m. (-ṅgyaḥ)
    1 Figurative or poetical speech, elliptical language or that which conveys something more than the simple meaning of the words.
    2 Sarcasm, the covert but intelligible expression of suspicion or contempt.
    E. vi before añca to make clear or manifest, ghañ aff., and yat added. [813]
