• viśvāmica m. (-caḥ) A Muni, the son of GĀDHI, originally of the military order, but who became by long and painful austerities a Brahmarṣi, in which character he appears in the Rāmāyaṇa, as the early preceptor and counsellor of RĀMA.
    E. viśva all, mica the friend, and the vowel of the prefix made long in this sense; if attributively used, the compound is viśvamica. [789]
