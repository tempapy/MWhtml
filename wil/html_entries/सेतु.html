• setu m. (-tuḥ)
    1 A mound, a bank, a causeway, an elevated piece of ground separating fields, and serving, during their inundation in the rains, for the passage of travellers, &c.
    2 A bridge.
    3 A pass, a defile, a road practised in mountainous countries, and places of difficult access.
    4 A tree, (Tapia cratæva.)
    E. ṣi to bind, tun Uṇādi aff. [943]
