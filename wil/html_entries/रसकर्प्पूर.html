• rasakarppūra n. (-raḥ) A white sublimate or a muriate of mercury, made in close vessels with sulphur, mercury, and common salt; the crystals formed in the first operation are sublimed a second time, which seems to be the only essential difference between this and the preparations called Rasapuṣpa; ut infra.
    E. rasa mercury, and karppūra camphor; from its white colour. [697]
