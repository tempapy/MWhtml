• dūradarśin [419] mfn. (-rśī-rśinī-rśi) Long-sighted, far-seeing, (literally or metaphorically.) m. (-rśī) 1 A Paṇḍita, a learned man, a teacher. 2 A prophet, a seer. 3 A vulture. E. dūra far, and darśin who sees, from dṛśa to see, with ṇini affix; also with kvip affix dūradṛś.
