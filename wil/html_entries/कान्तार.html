• kāntāra mn. (-raḥ-raṃ)
    1 A bad or difficult road.
    2 A wood, a forest.
    3 A hole, a cavity. m. (-raḥ)
    1 A red variety of the sugar cane.
    2 A bamboo.
    3 Mountain ebony. n. (-raṃ)
    1 A symptom or symptomatic disease.
    2 A lotus. f. (-rī) A sort of sugar cane.
    E. kān for kañcit any one, here meaning no one, tṛ to go, in the causal form, ac affix; allowing none to pass; or ka pleasure, &c. anta end, and ṛ to go, aṇ aff. [209]
