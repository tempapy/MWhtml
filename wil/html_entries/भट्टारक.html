• bhaṭṭāraka [611] mfn. (-kaḥ-kā-kaṃ) Venerable, respectable, entitled to reverence or to homage. m. (-kaḥ) 1 A sage, a Muni or saint. 2 A deity. 3 (In theatrical language) A king. 4 The sun. 5 A term especially applied to a Bauddha teacher. E. bhaṭa to nourish, aff. kvip, tṛ to pass, aff. ṇvul, ta changed to ṭa; otherwise with ṭala to be confused, the same aff., and la changed to ṣa; a cherisher and punisher, a distributor of rewards and punishments; again, bhaṭṭa said here to mean, authority, ṛ to go to or obtain, aṇ aff., kan added.
