• brahman [607] m. (-hyā) 1 BRAHMĀ, the first deity of the Hindu triad, and the operative creator of the world. 2 A Brahman. 3 The superintending or presiding priest at a sacrifice. 4 One of the astronomical Yogas. 5 One of the principal servants of the Jinas. n. (-hma) 1 The divine cause, and essence of the world, from which all created things are supposed to emanate, and to which they return; the unknown God. 2 The practice of austere devotion. 3 The Vedas or scripture. 4 Holy knowledge. E. vṛha to increase, (mankind,) aff. manin and the initial letter changed to ba.
