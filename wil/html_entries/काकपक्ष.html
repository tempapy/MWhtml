• kākapakṣa [206] m. (-kṣaḥ) The side locks of the head of hair; three or five on each side left when the head is first shaved, and which may be allowed to remain, especially in persons of the military cast. E. kāka and pakṣa a feather, a wing, a crow's wing; the word is also used as an epithet of a warrior, a name of RĀMA, &c.
