• ujjayanī f. (-nī) Ujjayanī or Oujein, a city so called in Mālava, formerly the capital of VIKRAMĀDITYA. It is one of the seven sacred cities of the Hindus, and the first meridian of their geographers: the modern Oujein is about a mile south of the ancient city.
    E. ut much, jayanī victorious or place of victory; from the military character of its people; also avantī and ujjayinī. [139]
