• śiva mfn. (-vaḥ-vā-vaṃ) Prosperous, happy. m. (-vaḥ)
    1 The deity ŚIVA, the most formidable of the Hindu triad, the destroyer of creation: the adoration of which he is the object, is of a more gloomy nature in general than that of the rest, and he is the particular god of the Tāntrikas or followers of the books called Tantras.
    2 Final emancipation from existence, eternal happiness.
    3 A pillar or post to which cattle are tied.
    4 An auspicious planetary conjunction.
    5 Scripture, the Vedas.
    6 A drug, commonly Elavāluka.
    7 A perfume, Puṇḍarīya.
    8 Bdellium.
    9 One of the astronomical periods termed Yogas.
    10 Quicksilver.
    11 The penis.
    12 The phallic emblem of ŚIVA. n. (-vaṃ)
    1 Happiness, pleasure.
    2 Auspiciousness, well being.
    3 Water.
    4 Sea or fossile salt.
    5 Borax. f. (-vā)
    1 The goddess DURGĀ, the wife of ŚIVA.
    2 The Śami tree, (Mimosa suma, Rox.)
    3 Yellow myrobalan, (Terminalia chebula.)
    4 Emblic myrobalan.
    5 A shakal.
    6 The mother of the twenty-second Jina.
    E. śī to sleep, Uṇādi aff. van, on or in whom or which, the universe reposes.
