• avadāha n. (-haṃ) The root of a fragrant grass, (Andropogon muricatum).
    E. aba reverse, and dāha heat, burning, heat-preventing, from its being used in Tātis or screens, placed before doors, &c. and kept constantly wet: the air passing through these, blows into a room cool and refreshing. [078]
