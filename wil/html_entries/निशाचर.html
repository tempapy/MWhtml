• niśācara [479] mfn. (-raḥ-rā-rī-raṃ) Nocturnal, night-walking, what goes or moves about by night. m. (-raḥ) 1 A Rākṣasa, a fiend, an imp or goblin. 2 A Shakāl. 3 An owl. 4 A snake. 5 The ruddy goose. 6 A ghost, an evil spirit. 7 A thief. f. (-rī) 1 A woman who goes to an assignation, a harlot, a whore. 2 A she devil, a female fiend. 3 A sort of perfume: see keśinī. E. niśā night, and car who goes.
