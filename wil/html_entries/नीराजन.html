• nīrājana nf. (-naṃ-nā)
    1 Lustration of arms; a military and religious ceremony held on the 19th of Aśvin, and by kings or generals before taking the field; it is still observed by some Hindū states, under the name of Desahara.
    2 Waving lights before an idol; but it may also be performed with a lotus, clean cloth, or the leaves of various sacred plants; prostration is also considered as the fifth kind of this sort of adoration.
    E. nir completely, rāja to shine, aff. yuc; or nīra water, and ajana going, sprinkling the arms, horses, &c. with water. [484]
