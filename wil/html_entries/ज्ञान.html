• jñāna n. (-naṃ)
    1 Knowledge in general.
    2 Knowledge of a specific and religious kind, that which is derived from meditation, and the study of philosophy, which teaches man the divine nature and origin of his immaterial part, with the unreality of corporal enjoyments or worldly forms, and which, separating him during life from terrestrial objects, secures him, after death, a final emancipation from existence, and reunion with the universal spirit.
    E. jñā to know, aff. lyuṭ. [355]
