• sīmantonnayana n. (-naṃ) One of the Saṃskāras, or a purificatory and sacrificial ceremony, observed by women in the fourth, sixth, or eighth month of their pregnancy.
    E. sīmanta partition of the hair, and unnayana arranging; this forming an essential part of the rite. [927]
