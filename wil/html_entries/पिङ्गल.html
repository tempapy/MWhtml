• piṅgala mfn. (-laḥ-lā-laṃ) Of a tawny colour, brown, yellowish. m. (-laḥ)
    1 Tawny, (the colour,) a dull brown, or yellow.
    2 An attendant upon the sun.
    3 The sun.
    4 A fabulous being in the form of a Nāga, or serpent of the lower regions, to whom a treatise on prosody is ascribed; he is hence considered as a Muni, or inspired and divine personage.
    5 A name of ŚIVA.
    6 One of KUVERA'S divine treasures.
    7 A monkey.
    8 Fire.
    9 An ichneumon.
    10 A sort of poison.
    11 A small owl.
    12 The 51st year of the Hindū cycle. n. (-laṃ)
    1 Brass.
    2 Yellow orpiment. f. (-lā)
    1 The female elephant of the south quarter.
    2 A pious whore, commemorated in the Bhāgavata.
    3 A particular vessel of the body, the right of three canals which run from the os-coccygis to the head, and which are the chief passages of breath and air, according to the anatomy of the Yoga school of philosophy.
    4 An astrological house or period.
    5 The Sisu tree, (Dalbergia Sisu.)
    6 Heartpea.
    E. piji to colour, Uṇādi aff. alac; or piṅgala of a tawny hue, fem. aff. ṭāp.
