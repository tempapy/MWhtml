• nāgara mfn. (-raḥ-rī-raṃ)
    1 Clever, sharp, knowing, (as a buck, a blood, a wag, &c.)
    2 Town-born or bred, city, citizen.
    3 Nameless.
    4 Bad, vile. n. (-raṃ)
    1 Dry ginger.
    2 A sort of grass, (Cyperus pertenuis.)
    3 A form of writing, the Devanāgarī alphabet. m. (-raḥ)
    1 A husband's brother.
    9 An orange.
    2 A lecturer.
    4 Denial of knowledge.
    5 Fatigue.
    6 Desire of final beatitude. f. (-rī)
    1 A sort of Euphorbin.
    2 A clever or intriguing woman.
    E. nagara a city, aṇ aff. [459]
