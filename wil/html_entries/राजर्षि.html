• rājarṣi [701] m. (-rṣiḥ) A kind of saint; that holy and superhuman personage which a king or man of the military class may become by the practice of religious austerities; seven classes of Ṛṣis are enumerated, the Devarṣi, Brahmarṣi, Maharṣi, Paramarṣi, Rājarṣi, Kāṇḍarṣi, and Śrutarṣi: the order is variously given, but the Rājarṣi is inferior to the four preceding ones, and the two last mean the inspired saints and supposed authors of the Hindu scriptures. E. rāja king, and ṛṣi a saint.
