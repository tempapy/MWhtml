• kāśyapi m. (-piḥ)
    1 A name of ARUṆA.
    2 A name of GARUḌA, the bird of VIṢṆU.
    E. kaśyapa the saint, and iñ affix of descent, being both sons of KAŚYAPA. f. (-pī) The earth.
    E. as before; alluding to a legend from the Purāṇas, in which PARAŚURĀMA, after the destruction of the Kṣatriya race, and performance of an Aśvamedha, presented the sovereignty of the world to his Guru, KAŚYAPA. [219]
