• yudhiṣṭhira m. (-raḥ) The elder of the five Pāṇḍava princes, and leader in the great war, between them and the Kurus, in the beginning of the fourth age; the nominal son of PĀṆḌU, whom he succeeded in the sovereignty of India, but according to the legend, begotten on Kunti by the deity YAMA.
    E. yudhi in war or battle, and ṣṭhira for sthira firm; it is also sometimes read yudghasthira. [687]
