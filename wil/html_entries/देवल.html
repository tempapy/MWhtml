• devala m. (-laḥ)
    1 An attendant upon an idol; a Brahman of an inferior order, who subsists upon the offerings made to the images which he attends, and who conducts the ceremonies of all sorts of people for hire.
    2 A Muni; according to some authorities, a name of NĀRADA; to others, of a different saint, who is also a legislator, the son of ASITA and also named AṢṬAVAKRA.
    3 A husband's brother: see devara.
    4 A holy or virtuous man.
    E. deva a deity, lā to bring, and ka aff. [423]
