• agnimāruti [007] m. (-tiḥ) A name of the saint, AGASTYA. E. agni fire, i. e. the fire of the stomach, the digestive faculty, and māruti a name of HANUMĀN; because the saint could digest as well as the monkey: the epithet alludes to a story of his devouring and digesting VĀTĀPI, a demon, who had assumed the form of a sheep.
