• cāndrāyaṇa m. (-ṇaḥ) A religious or expiatory observance, regulated by the moon's age; diminishing the daily consumption of food every day, by one mouthful, for the dark half of the month beginning with fifteen at the full moon, until it is reduced to one, at the new moon, and then increasing it in like manner during the fortnight of the moon's increase: there are other forms of this penance.
    E. candra the moon, and phak aff.
