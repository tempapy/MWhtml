• kuṇḍakīṭa m. (-ṭaḥ)
    1 A follower of the Cārvāka doctrine, or the tenets of those sects who deny the authority of the Vedas.
    2 The son of a woman of the Brahminical tribe born in adultery, particularly with a man of an inferior caste.
    3 A keeper of concubines, a man who has female slaves.
    E. kuṇḍa a pool, &c. and kīṭa a worm, a reptile.
