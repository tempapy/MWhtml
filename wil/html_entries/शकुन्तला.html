• śakuntalā f. (-lā) The daughter of MENAKĀ, the courtezan of Svarga, by the sage VIŚVĀMITRA, brought up by KAṆVA the hermit, and afterwards married to DUṢYANTA, by whom she became the mother of BHARATA, the sovereign of all India.
    E. śakunta a bird, lā to get, aff. ka, and ṭāp, being born in a solitary forest, and protected by the birds till found by KAṆVA. [824]
