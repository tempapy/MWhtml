• caṇḍāla m. (-laḥ) An outcaste, a Cāṇḍāla, the generic name for a man of the lowest of the mixed tribes, born from a Śūdra father and Brahman mother.
    E. caṇḍa angry, and ala able, or caṇḍa to be angry, and ālañ Uṇādi affix; proper to execute all vile or barbarous acts; also cāṇḍāla.
