• tāṇḍava mn. (-vaḥ-vaṃ)
    1 Dancing, especially with violent gesticulation, and particularly applied to the frantic dance of the god ŚIVA, and his votaries.
    2 A sort of grass, (Saccharum procerum.) m. (-baḥ) In prosody, a tribrach or foot of three short syllables.
    E. taṇḍu the name of the Muni, by whom the style of dancing was first taught, affix aṇ; or taḍi to beat, and ava aff.
