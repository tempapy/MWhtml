• niyama m. (-maḥ)
    1 Agreement, contract, engagement, assent, promise.
    2 Any religious observance voluntarily practised, as fasting, watching, pilgrimage, praying, &c. voluntary penance, meritorious or supererogatory piety.
    3 A religious observance or obligation in general.
    4 Certainty, ascertainment.
    5 Rule, precept.
    6 Usage, practice.
    7 Common places in poetry, conventional expression, as notice of the Birch in describing the Himālaya, of the Sandal tree in describing the Malaya mountain, the whiteness of vesture, blackness of hair, the cry of the peacock in the rains, of the Koïl in spring, &c.
    E. ni before yama to refrain, affix ac. [470]
