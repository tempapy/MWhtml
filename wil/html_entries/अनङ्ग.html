• anaṅga mfn. (-ṅgaḥ-ṅgā-ṅgaṃ) Bodiless, incorporeal. m. (-hṛ) A name of KĀMA, the Hindu deity of love. n. (-ṅgaṃ)
    1 Heaven, aether or the atmosphere.
    2 The mind or faculty of reasoning.
    E. an neg. and aṅga body; as applicable to KĀMA, it alludes to his having been reduced to ashes, by the eye of ŚIVA, for having disturbed his devotions, and rendered him enamoured of PĀRVATĪ. [025]
