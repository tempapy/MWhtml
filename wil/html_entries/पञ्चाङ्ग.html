• pañcāṅga mfn. (-ṅgaḥ-ṅgī-ṅgaṃ) Having five limbs or members, five parts or subdivisions, consisting of five things, &c. n. (-ṅgaṃ)
    1 Any aggregate of five parts or five things.
    3 Five modes of devotion or silent prayer, burnt offering, libations, bathing idols, and feeding Brahmans.
    3 An almanac describing solar days, lunar days, and the periods of asterisms, Yogas and Karaṇas. m. (-ṅgaḥ)
    1 A tortoise.
    2 A horse: see pañcabhadra.
    3 Reverence by extending the hands, bending the knees and head, and in speech and look.
    E. pañca five, aṅga body. [494]
