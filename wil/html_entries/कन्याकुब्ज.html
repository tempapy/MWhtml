• kanyākubja m. (-bjaḥ) Kanyākubja or Kinnoge, an ancient city of great note, in the north of Hindustan, known to classical geography as Canogyza: the name also applies to its dependencies or the surrounding district; also kānyakubja.
    E. kanyā a girl, and kubja round-shouldered, crooked; the etymology alludes to a legend relating to the one hundred daughters of KUŚANĀBHA the king of this city, who were all rendered crooked by Vāyu, for non-compliance with his licentious desires. [187]
