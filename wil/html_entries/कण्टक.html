• kaṇṭaka [183] mn. (-kaḥ-kaṃ) 1 A thorn. 2 A paltry foe. 3 A fish bone. 4 Horripilation, or the erection of the hair of the body. 5 Any annoyance or source of vexation. 6 A term in the Nyāya philosophy, implying refutation of argument, detection of error, &c. m. (-kaḥ) 1 A workshop, a manufactory. 2 Fault, defect. 3 The point of a pin or needle. 4 A fish or marine monster, the symbol of KĀMADEVA: see makara. 5 A bamboo. E. kaṭi to divide, vun aff.
