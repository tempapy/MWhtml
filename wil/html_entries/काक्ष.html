• kākṣa r. 1st cl. (i) kākṣi (kāṃkṣati) To desire; it is most frequently used with āṅ prefixed, in the same sense. [207]
  • kākṣa mfn. (-kṣaḥ-kṣā-kṣaṃ) Frowning, looking scornfully or in displeasure. m. (-kṣaḥ) A glance, a wink or leer. n. (-kṣaṃ) A frown, a look of displeasure.
    E. kā for ku diminutive, or depreciative, and akṣi the eye. f. (-kṣī)
    1 A sort of trefoil, (Cytisus cajan.)
    2 A perfume, a fragrant kind of earth.
    E. kakṣa a creeping plant, aṇ and ṅīp affs. [207]
