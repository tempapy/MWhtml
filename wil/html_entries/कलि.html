• kali m. (-liḥ)
    1 The fourth age of the world, according to the Hindus; the iron age, or that of vice: the commencement of the Kali-yug or age, is placed 3,101 years anterior to the Christian aera, so that in A. D. 1830, the Kali year is 4,931, the number of its years is 432,000, at the expiration of which, the world is to be destroyed.
    2 The age personified.
    3 War, battle.
    4 Strife, dissension.
    5 An arrow.
    6 Beleric myrobalan. f. (-liḥ or lī) An unblown flower: see kalikā.
    E. kala to count or reckon, in Uṇādi aff. [202]
