• daṇḍa [396] r. 10th cl. (daṇḍayati) 1 To punish, especially by fine. 2 To fine, to amerce.
  • daṇḍa [396] mn. (-ṇḍaḥ-ṇḍaṃ) A stick, a staff. m. (-ṇḍaḥ) 1 Punishment, castigation, amercement, imprisonment or putting to death. 2 A name of YAMA, regent of the dead. 3 An army. 4 A form of array, a line or column of troops. 5 A long measure, a pole of four cubits. 6 A churning stick. 7 A corner, an angle. 8 A horse. 2 A companion or attendant of the sun. 10 A stem or stick of a tree. 11 Subduing, subjecting. 12 Pride, arrogance. 13 A measure of time, a Daṇḍa, (1/60)th part of the day and night, or twenty-four minutes. 14 Standing upright or erect, being like a staff. 15 A son of IKṢVĀKU. E. dama to tame, and ḍa Uṇādi aff. or daṇḍa to punish, affix ac.
