• kāma [210] m. (-maḥ) 1 KĀMA the Hindu Cupid or deity of love. 2 Wish, desire. 3 BALARĀMA the brother of KṚṢṆA. 4 The mango tree. f. (-mā) Love, desire. n. (-maṃ) 1 Semen virile. 2 An object of desire. 3 Willingly, voluntarily. 4 A particle of assent or agreement: see kāmam, ind. (in composition,) Following one's own desire, as kāmavāda speaking what one lists; kāmabhakṣa eating whatever one likes; kāmacāra doing whatever one chooses; in law, a child previous to the imposition of the characteristic cord is all these. E. kama to desire, ghañ aff.
