• nāndī f. (-ndī)
    1 Eulogium of a king, or praise of a deity, recited in benedictory verses at the commencement of a religious ceremony, or the opening of a drama; it most usually signifies the kind of blessing pronounced as a prologue to a Nāṭaka or play.
    2 Prosperity, thriving, increase.
    E. nadi to be happy, in the causal form, affix in, and ṅīṣ; delighting the gods, &c. or procuring happiness for mankind.
