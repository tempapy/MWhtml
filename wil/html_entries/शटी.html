• śaṭī f. (-ṭiḥ or -ṭī) Zedoary, (Curcuma zerumbet,) otherwise considered as a synonym of the Ambahaldi, or Curcuma Amhaldi, the mango-smelling ginger, so termed because the fresh root possesses the smell of a green mango.
    E. śaṭa to divide (remove disease), aff. ina, and ṅīṣa optionally added. [826]
