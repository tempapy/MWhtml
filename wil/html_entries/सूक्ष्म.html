• sūkṣma mfn. (-kṣmaḥ-kṣmā-kṣmaṃ)
    1 Little, small.
    2 Minute, atomic.
    3 Fine, delicate.
    4 Ingenious, subtle. m. (-kṣmaḥ)
    1 An atom.
    2 Clearingnut plant, (Strychnos potatorum.) n. (-hyaṃ)
    1 The Supreme Soul, the subtle and invisible spirit that pervades all existence.
    2 Fraud, cheating.
    3 Fine thread, &c.
    4 A figure of rhetoric, the delicate or subtle expression of act or intention.
    5 Subtlety, ingenuity, craft. f. (-kṣmā)
    1 A kind of jasmine, (Jasminum auriculatum.)
    2 Small cardamoms.
    3 A perfume, consisting of a substance in small grains: see Elavāluka.
    E. sūc to inform or make known, sman Uṇādi aff. [939]
