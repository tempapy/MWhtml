• karṇa r. 10th cl. (karṇayati) To pierce or bore: with the prefix āṅ, samā, (sam and āṅ) or upa, to hear or listen.
  • karṇa m. (-rṇaḥ)
    1 The ear.
    2 KARṆA a prince, sovereign of Aṅgadeśa, and elder brother, by the mother's side, to the Pāṇḍu princes, being the son of SŪRYA by KUNTĪ, before her marriage with PĀṆḌU.
    3 The helm or rudder of a vessel.
    4 (In prosody,) A spondee, a foot of two long syllables.
    5 (In geometry,) The hypothenuse of a triangle, or the diagonal of a tetragon.
    6 A plant, (Cassia fistula.)
    7 A kind of swallow wort, (Colotropis gigantea.)
    E. karṇa to hear, ghañ affix; or kṛ do, &c. na Uṇādi aff.
