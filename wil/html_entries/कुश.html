• kuśa (ira) kuśira r. 4th cl. (kuśyati)
    1 To embrace, to enfold.
    2 To take out or from. (iḥ) kuśi r. 1st and 10th cl. (kuṃśati kuṃśayati) To shine: see kusa.
  • kuśa mfn. (-śaḥ-śā-śaṃ)
    1 Wicked, depraved.
    2 Mad, inebriate. mn. (-śaḥ-śa) A species of grass used in many solemn and religious observances, hence called sacrificial grass, (Poa cynosuroides.) m. (-śaḥ)
    1 The yoke of a plough.
    2 A proper name, one of the sons of RĀMA.
    3 One of the great Dvīpas or divisions of the universe, surrounded by the sea of spirituous liquor. f. (-śā)
    1 A rope.
    2 A plant, commonly Maükāṭha. n. (-śaṃ) Water. (-śī)
    1 A ploughshare.
    2 Wrought iron. mf. (-śaḥ-śī) A bridle, a horse's head rope.
    E. ku the earth, śīṅ to sleep or rest, and ḍa affix, or ku bad, and śo to destroy, again kuśa to embrace or to enfold, and ka aff.
