• kṣetraja mfn. (-jaḥ-jā-jaṃ)
    1 Produced in a field, (as corn, &c.)
    2 Born of the body, &c. m. (-jaḥ) A son, the offspring of the wife by a kinsman or person duly appointed to procreate issue to the husband.
    E. kṣetra wife, and ja born, born of the wife only; this is one of the twelve kinds of issue acknowledged by the old Hindu law. [267]
