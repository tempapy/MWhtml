• ḍāmara m. (-raḥ)
    1 Affray, conflict without weapons, &c. see ḍamara.
    2 Rout, uproar, the bustle and the confusion of festivity or strife.
    3 Any surprising thing, sight or occurrence.
    4 A name common to six Tantras, as the Śivaḍāmara, Yogaḍāmara, Durgāḍāmara, &c.
    E. ḍamara q. v. and aṇ aff. [360]
