• nārada [462] m. (-daḥ) A name of NĀRADA, the son of BRAHMĀ, and one of the ten divine Munis or Ṛṣis; he is a friend of KṚṢṆA, a celebrated legislator, and inventor of the Vīṇā or lute. E. nāra men, and dā to give, (instruction,) ḍa affix; or nāra water, and da who gives; offering sacrifices to the manes.
