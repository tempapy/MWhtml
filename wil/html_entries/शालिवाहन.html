• śālivāhana m. (-naḥ) A sovereign of India, whose capital was Pratiṣṭhāna in the Dekhin, and institutor of the era now called Śakā, beginning 76 or 78 years after CRIST.
    E. śāli from śāla the Śāl tree, or a proper name, that of a Yakṣa transformed to a lion, on which the prince rode when an infant, and vāhana vehicle.
