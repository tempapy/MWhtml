• āśrama m. (-maḥ)
    1 A religious order, of which there are four kinds referable to the different periods of life, 1st, that of the student or Brahmacārī; 2d, that of the householder or Gṛhastha; 3d, that of the anchorite or Vānaprastha; and 4th, that of the beggar or Bhikṣu: see brahmacārī, &c.
    2 A college, a school.
    3 A hermitage, the abode of retired saints or sages.
    4 A wood or thicket.
    E. āṅ before śrama to perform religious austerities, affix ghañ. [124]
