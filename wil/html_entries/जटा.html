• jaṭā [338] f. (-ṭā) 1 The hair matted as worn by the god ŚIVA, and by ascetics; the long hairs occasionally clotted together, and brought over the head so as to project like a horn from the forehead, at other times allowed to fall carelessly over the back and shoulders. 2 The root of a tree, a fibrous root. 3 Spikenard: see jaṭāmāṃsī. 4 A plant, (Asparagus racemosa.) 5 Cowach. 6 Hedysarum, various species. E. jaṭa to entangle, ac affix; or jana to be produced, ṭan Uṇādi affix, and the radical final rejected.
