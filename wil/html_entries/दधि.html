• dadhi mfn. (-dhiḥ-dhiḥ-dhi) Holding, having, who or what has or possesses. n. (-dhi)
    1 Milk curdled or coagulated spontaneously by heat, or by the addition of buttermilk; it is an article of food of general use, and in high estimation amongst the Hindus, and is considered, medicinally, as the remedy or preventative of most disorders.
    2 Resin, turpentine; (all the synonyms are common to these two senses.)
    3 A house, an abode.
    E. dhā to have, kin affix, and the root reiterated. [398]
