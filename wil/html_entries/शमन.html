• śamana n. (-naṃ)
    1 Killing animals for sacrifice, immolation.
    2 Mental tranquillity, calmness, indifference.
    3 Abuse, malediction.
    4 Hurt, injury.
    5 Chewing. m. (-naḥ)
    1 YAMA, the ruler of Tartarus.
    2 An antelope. f. (-nī) Night.
    E. śama to be tranquil, aff. lyuṭ or yuc. [830]
