• vandin m. (-ndī)
    1 A panegyrist, a bard, a herald, a servant whose duty it is to proclaim the titles of a great man as he passes along, or a poet, who sings the praises of a prince in his presence, or accompanies an army, to chaunt martial songs: considered as one of a distinct tribe, he is the son of a Kṣatriya by a Śūdra female.
    2 A praiser, a flatterer.
    E. vadi to praise, aff. in. [734]
