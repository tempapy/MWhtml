• viśva mfn. (-śvaḥ-śvā-śvaṃ) All, entire, whole, universal. n. (-śvaṃ) The world, the universe. m. (-śvaḥ) A deity of a particular class in which ten are enumerated; their names are VASU, SATYA, KRATU, DAKṢA, KĀLA, KĀMA, DHṚTI, KURU, PURURĀVAN, and MADRAVA: They are worshipped particularly at the funeral obsequies in honor of deceased progenitors in general, and receive an oblation of clarified butter at the daily and domestic Śrāddha. nf. (-śvaṃ-śvā) Dry ginger. f. (-śvā) A tree, the bark of which is said to be used in dying red, commonly Atis, (Betula.)
    E. viśa to enter, to pervade, Uṇādi aff. kan.
