• vedānta m. (-ntaḥ) The theological part of the Vedas; considered collectively it is contained in the many numerous passages or chapters of the Vedas termed Upaniṣads, which inculcate an abstract and speculative monotheistical worship, and these have been further explained and illustrated by later writers: the founder of the school is VYĀSA, and subsequently ŚAṂKARĀCĀRYA is its most eminent teacher.
    E. veda the Vedas, and anta end, sum, scope or substance.
