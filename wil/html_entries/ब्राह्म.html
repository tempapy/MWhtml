• brāhma [609] mfn. (-hmaḥ-hmī-hmaṃ) Relating or belonging to BRAHMĀ, the Vedas, &c. n. (-hmaṃ) 1 The part of the hand under the root of the thumb. 2 A mode of marriage, the presentation of the bride elegantly adorned, by the father, to the bridegroom whom he has invited. m. (-hmaḥ) NĀRADA the sage, and son of BRAHMĀ. f. (-hmī) 1 One of the eight divine mothers of created beings, or the personified energies of the gods; the Śakti or energy of BRAHMĀ. 2 The goddess of speech SARASVATĪ. 3 The moon plant, (Asclepias acida.) 4 A sort of potherb, (Ruta graveolens.) 5 A shrub, (Siphonanthus Indica.) 6 A sort of fish. 7 The asterism Rohiṇī. 8 A she frog. 9 A yellow coloured ant. 10 The wife of a Brahman. E. brahma BRAHMĀ, &c. aṇ aff.
