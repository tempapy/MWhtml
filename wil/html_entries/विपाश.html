• vipāśa [775] mfn. (-śaḥ-śā-śaṃ) Unnoosed, unfettered, freed from a noose or tie. f. (-śā) 1 The Vipāśā or the Beyāh river in the Pañjāb. E. vi privative, and pāś or pāśā a noose, from paśa to bind, causal form, with ac and ṭāp or kvip aff.; hence also vipāś; the name applies to the river as having destroyed the cord which the Muni VAŚIṢṬHA had tied round his neck, when about to hang himself through grief for the death of his sons slain by VIŚVĀMITRA.
