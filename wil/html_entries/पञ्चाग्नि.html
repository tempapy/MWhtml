• pañcāgni n. (-gni)
    1 A collection of five fires, amidst which a devotee performs penance during the summer season; or four fires lighted severally to the north, south, east and west, and the sun over head.
    2 Five mystic fires, supposed to be present in the body. m. (-gniḥ)
    1 A householder who maintains five fires, or the domestic one, and one for warming visitors in addition to the other three.
    2 One who is acquainted with the doctrine of the five mystic fires.
    E. pañca five, and agni fire.
