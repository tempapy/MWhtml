• ḍamara [360] mf. (-raḥ-rā) 1 An affray, a conflict without weapons. 2 Terrifying an enemy by shouts and gestures. 3 Petty or predatory warfare, war carried on by detachments or by villages, one in which kings are not engaged. 4 Rout, riot. n. (-raṃ) Flight, running away through fear. E. ḍam imitative sound, and ara what occasions, from ṛ to go, affix ap.
