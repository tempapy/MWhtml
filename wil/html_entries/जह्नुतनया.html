• jahnutanayā f. (-yā) The Ganges.
    E. jahnu, and tanayā the daughter; the river in its course disturbed the devotions of the saint JAHNU, upon which he drank up its waters; having released them at the intercession of BHAGĪRATHA, he is thus considered as the parent of the stream: see jāhnavī; also jahnusutā, &c. [346]
