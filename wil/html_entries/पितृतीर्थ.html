• pitṛtīrtha n. (-rthaṃ)
    1 Gaya, the city so called, where the performance of funeral sacrifices is peculiarly efficacious and meritorious.
    2 The part between the forefinger and thumb, sacred to the manes.
    E. pitṛ a progenitor, and tīrtha a place of pilgrimage. [535]
