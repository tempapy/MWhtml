• kāca r. 1st cl. (i) kāci (kācate)
    1 To shine.
    2 To bind.
  • kāca m. (-caḥ)
    1 Alkaline ashes, any salt of potash or soda in a glassy or crystalline state.
    2 Crystal, quartz or glass, considered as a natural production, and used as a jewel or ornament.
    3 A loop, a swinging shelf, a string so contrived as to hold or support burthens, &c. A disease of the eyes, affection of the optic nerve or gutta serena. n. (-caṃ)
    1 Alkaline salt, black salt.
    2 Wax.
    E. kaca to shine or bind, ghañ aff.
