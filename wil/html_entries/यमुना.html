• yamunā f. (-nā) The YAMUNĀ or Jumnā river, which rises on the south side of the Himāla range, at a short distance to the north-west of the source of the Ganges, and which after a course of about 378 miles falls into that river immediately below Allahabad; in mythology, the personified river is considered as the daughter of SŪRYA, and sister of YAMA.
    E. yama to stop, (at the Ganges,) and unan Uṇādi aff. [681]
