• ācāryya [106] m. (-ryyaḥ) 1 A spiritual guide or teacher; he who invests the student with the sacrificial thread, and instructs him in the Vedas, the law of sacrifice, and religious mysteries. 2 A name of DROṆA, the teacher of the Pāṇḍus. f. (-ryyā) A spiritual preceptress. f. (-ryyānī) The wife of an Ācārya or holy teacher. E. āṅ, cara to go, ṇyat affix, in the fem. ṭāp or ṅīp with ānuka inserted.
