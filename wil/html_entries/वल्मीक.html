• valmīka mn. (-kaḥ-kaṃ) A hillock, ground thrown up by moles, &c., but especially the large accumulations of soil, sometimes made by the white ant. m. (-kaḥ)
    1 The poet VĀLMĪKI.
    2 Elephantiasis, or swellings of the neck, chest, hands and feet, which break and discharge ichor in many places.
    E. bala to cover, or bala to live, kīkac Uṇādi aff., and mum augment; in the latter case this word and the corresponding ones are written balbhīka, &c. also valmika, valmīki. [742]
