• tamas n. (-maḥ)
    1 The third of the qualities incident to the state of humanity, the Tama guṇa, or property of darkness, whence proceed folly, ignorance, mental blindness, worldly delusion, &c.
    2 Darkness, gloom.
    3 Sin.
    4 Sorrow, grief. mn. (-māḥ-maḥ) Rāhu or the personified ascending node: see rāhu.
    E. tama to be disturbed, and asac Uṇādi affix; that property by which the mind is troubled, the world perplexed, &c. also tamam and tama. [367]
