• jñā r. 9th cl. (jānāti jānīte) To know, to understand.
    With anu prefixed,
    1 To assent or conform to.
    2 To acknowledge.
    With apa, To conceal.
    With ava, To disrespect, to despise.
    With upa, To deny knowledge of any thing.
    With pari, To ascertain.
    With pra, To know well.
    With prati or sam, To assent, to admit, to agree, to promise.
    With prati and abhi, To recognise.
    With vi, To discriminate, to know.
    With sam, To recollect or possess the power of knowing. (In combination, it usually takes the deponent form, as anujānīte, apajānīte, &c.) r. 10th cl. (jñāpayati) To command, to direct; āṅ is usually prefixed in this sense. r. 1st cl. but occurring in the form of the causal, (jñapayati)
    1 To slay.
    2 To gratify or please.
    3 To exhibit.
    4 To sharpen.
    5 To praise. In these senses the root is, in general, combined with a prefix.
