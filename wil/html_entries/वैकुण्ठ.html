• vaikuṇṭha m. (-ṇṭhaḥ)
    1 A name of VIṢṆU.
    2 INDRA. n. (-ṇṭhaṃ)
    1 The paradise or world of Vaikuṇṭha or VIṢṆU; its site is variously described, either as in the northern ocean, or on the eastern peak of mount Meru.
    2 Talc.
    E. vikuṇṭhā the wife of ŚUBHRA, and mother of VIṢṆU, in one form, aṇ patronymic aff.: other etymologies occur, as vi privative, &c. and kuṇṭha said to mean destruction, he who is indestructible; or vi before the same, explained knowledge or abode, being omniscient or omnipresent, &c. again vi various, and kuṇṭha illusion, aff. aṇ.
