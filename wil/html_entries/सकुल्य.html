• sakulya m. (-lyaḥ)
    1 A kinsman, one of the same family name and common origin: see sagotra.
    2 A distant kinsman or relation; one who shares a divided oblation, as the grandson's grandson or other descendant as far as three degrees from him, or the offspring of the grandfather's grandfather or other remoter ancestor.
    E. sa same, kulya of family: some authorities extend the name of Sakulya to the tenth descendant.
