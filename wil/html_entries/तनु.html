• tanu mfn. (-nuḥ-nuḥ or nvī or nūḥ-nu)
    1 Small, minute.
    2 Delicate, fine, but with interstices.
    3 Thin, slender, emaciated.
    4 Little. f. (-nuḥ-nūḥ)
    1 The body.
    2 The skin. f. (nuḥ)
    1 A woman.
    2 Point of conjunction, or sun's entrance into a new sign. f. (-nvī)
    1 A delicate or slender woman.
    2 A stanza of four lines, and twenty-four syllables in each: a variety of the Saṃkṛti metre.
    E. tana to stretch or spread, Uṇādi affix u; also tanū and tanus. [364]
