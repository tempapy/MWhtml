• om ind.
    1 The mystic name of the deity, prefacing all the prayers and most of the writings of the Hindus.
    E. a a name of VIṢṆU, u ŚIVA, and ma of BRAHMA; it therefore implies the Indian triad, and expresses the three in one.
    2 A particle of command or injunction,
    3 Of assent, (verily, amen.)
    4 Of auspiciousness.
    5 Of removal, (away, hence,) and,
    6 It is an inceptive particle.
    E. ava to go, to preserve, &c. man Uṇādi affix, the radical being converted into o.
