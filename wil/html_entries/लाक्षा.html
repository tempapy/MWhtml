• lākṣā [718] f. (-kṣā) Lac, a red dye, or an insect which is analogous to the cochineal insect, and like it forms when dried and prepared, a dye of a red colour; the nest is formed of a resinous substance which is used as sealing wax, and is usually termed Shel lac. E. lakṣa to mark or stain, aff. a, and aṇ added, the vowel made long: or lakṣa a hundred thousand, and aṇ aff.; made by a multitude of the insects.
