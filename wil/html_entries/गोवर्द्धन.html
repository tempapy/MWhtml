• govarddhana m. (-naḥ) A celebrated hill in Vindrāvan or the country about Mathura; it was upheld by KṚṢṆA upon one finger, to shelter the cowherds from a storm excited by INDRA, as a test of the former's divinity.
    E. go a cow, and varddhana encreasing; pasturing cattle. [301]
