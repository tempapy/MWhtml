• śavara m. (-raḥ)
    1 A barbarian, one inhabiting the mountainous districts of India, and wearing the feathers of the peacock, &c. as decorations.
    2 Water.
    3 A name of ŚIVA.
    4 The hand.
    5 A Śāstra, a sacred treatise.
    E. śava a corpse, rā to take, aff. ka and ṭāp. [834]
