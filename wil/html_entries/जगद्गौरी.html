• jagadgaurī f. (-rī) A name of MANASĀDEVĪ, the deified wife of the sage JARATKĀRU, and sister to the Nāgas or serpent race.
    E. jagat the world, and gaurī the goddess GAURĪ; a terrestrial GAURĪ; her relationship to the snake breed is supposed to give her an influence over them, which is implored frequently by the Hindus.
