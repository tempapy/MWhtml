• nakṣatra n. (-traṃ)
    1 A star in general.
    2 An asterism in the moon's path or lunar mansion, of which twenty-eight, distinct in name, figure, and number of stars, are enumerated; the Paurānic and popular enumeration of those constellations is twenty-seven; Abhijit, the twenty-seventh, being considered as formed of portions of the two contiguous asterisms, and not distinct from them.
    2 A pearl.
    E. ṇakṣa to go, Uṇādi affix atran or kṣada Sautra root, to injure, or kṣara to drop, or kṣī to waste or decay, with the negative prefix, and ṣṭran affix; deriv. irr. [450]
