• cirajīvin mfn. (-vī-vinī-vi) Long-lived. m. (-vī)
    1 A name of VIṢṆU.
    2 A crow.
    3 The Jivaka: see the preceding.
    4 An epithet applied especially to eight chiefs or saints, viz. MĀRKAṆḌEYA, AŚVATTHĀMA, BĀLĪ, VYĀSA, HANUMĀN, VIBHĪṢAṆA, KṚPA, and PARAŚURĀMA.
    E. cira for ciram long, and jīvin living or lived; also cirañjīvin. [327]
