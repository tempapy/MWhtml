• ḍa The third letter of the cerebral class of consonants, having the sound of D, pronounced far back in the head: it is often confounded in pronounciation with a hard R, or L, sounded as in the Northumbrian bur; it is written in the Roman character D.
  • ḍa m. (ḍaḥ)
    1 A name of ŚIVA.
    2 Fear.
    3 Sound.
    4 Submarine fire.
    5 A kind of drum or tabor. f. (ḍā)
    1 A kind of female imp or goblin.
    2 A basket, &c. carried by a sling: see ḍalaka.
    E. ḍapa to collect, affix ḍa.
