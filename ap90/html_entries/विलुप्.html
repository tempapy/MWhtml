• vilup 6 P.
      1 To break off, pull out, cut off.
      2 To seize, plunder, rob, carry off.
      3 To mar, spoil, impair.
      4 (a) To destroy, ruin, cause to disappear; priyamatyaṃtaviluptadarśanaṃ Ku. 4. 2 ‘for ever lost to view’. (b) To eat up; kravyādbhiraṃgalatikā niyataṃ viluptā U. 3. 28, Mk. 1. 9.
      5 To wipe or rub off. --Pass. To be destroyed or lost, to perish, disappear; jarāviluptamānāvamānaciṃtaḥ Dk. [0991-b]
