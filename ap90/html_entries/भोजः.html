• bhojaḥ [bhuj-ac] 1 N. of a celebrated king of Mālvā (or Dhārā); (supposed to have flourished about the end of the tenth or the beginning of the eleventh century, and to have been a great patron of Sanskrit learning; he is also supposed to have been the author of several learned works, such as sarasvatīkaṃṭhābharaṇa &c.). --2 N. of a country. --3 N. of a king of the Vidarbhas; bhojena dūto raghave visṛṣṭaḥ R. 5. 39; 7. 18, 29, 35. --jāḥ (m. pl.) N. of a people. --Comp. --adhipaḥ an epithet of {1} Kaṃsa. {2} Karṇa. --iṃdraḥ a king of the Bhojas. --kaṭaṃ N. of a town founded by Rukmin. --devaḥ, --rājaḥ king Bhoja; see (1) above. --patiḥ {1} king Bhoja. {2} an epithet of Kaṃsa.
