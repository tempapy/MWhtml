ājyaṃ [ājyate, ā-aṃj-kyap]
    • 1 Clarified butter, ghee; ājyadhūmodgamena Ś. 1. 15; (it is often distinguished from ghṛta; sapiṃviṃlīnamājyaṃ syād ghanībhūtaṃ ghṛtaṃ bhavet).
    • 2 (In a wider sense) Oil, milk &c. used instead of clarified butter; ghṛtaṃ vā yadi vā tailaṃ payo vā dadhi yāvakaṃ . ājyasyāne niyuktānāmājyaśabdo vidhīyate ...
    • 3 N. of a sort of chant or religious service (sāmastomabhedaḥ); ājimīyustasmādājyānāmājyatvaṃ Nir.
    • 4 Turpentine.
      Comp. a hymn to be recited by a Sāmavedin.
      a. drinking ghee. (--pāḥ pl.) a class of Manes who are the sons of Pulastya and the ancestors of the Vaiśya order; pulastyasyājyapāḥ putrāḥ Mb.; Ms. 3. 197-8.
      a vessel or dish to hold clarified butter.
      m. {1} an epithet of Agni (the consumer of clarified butter). {2} a god, deity.
      ‘sea of clarified butter’, one of the seven seas. [0231-a]
