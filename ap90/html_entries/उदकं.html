udakaṃ [uṃd-ṇvul ni° nalopaśca Uṇ. 2. 39] Water; anītvā paṃkatāṃ dhūlimudakaṃ nāvatiṣṭhate Śi. 2. 34. udakaṃ dā, -pradā or kṛ To offer a libation of water to a dead person; udakaṃ upaspṛś to touch certain parts of the body with water, bathe; [cf. Gr. hudor; L. unda ‘a wave’].
      Comp. margin of water, bank, shore; odakāṃtātsnigdho jano'nugaṃtavya iti śrūyate Ś. 4. --arthin a. desirous of water, thirsty.
      a. chiefly consisting of water.
      a reservoir, a cistern, well.
      a water-jar.
      dropsy.
      a. dropsical.
      rice boiled with water.
      , --kriyā, --dānaṃ presentation of (a libation) of water to dead ancestors or the manes; vṛkodarasyodakakriyāṃ kuru Ve. 6; Y. 3. 4. --kuṃbhaḥ a water-jar.
      a kind of vow.
      entering water, bathing.
      , parvataḥ a mountain abounding in streams of water.
      drinking water; Pt. 1. --caṃdraḥ a kind of magic.
      a. giver of water. (--daḥ) {1} a giver of water to the manes. {2} an heir, near kinsman.
      = °karman q. v. --dharaḥ a cloud.
      a kind of ordeal. --pūrvakaṃ ind. preceded by the pouring of water, by pouring water on the hand as preparatory to or confirmatory of a gift or promise.
      a. watery, like water.
      a yoke for carrying water.
      water or moist soil.
      a water-pitcher (Ved.).
      a kind of peeled grain.
      a sort of diabetes (passing watery urine).
      a. suffering from this disease.
      {1} a thunder-shower. {2} thunderbolt in the form of shower; Śi. 8. 39. --śākaṃ any aquatic herb.
      f. sprinkling holy or consecrated water over a sick person to allay fever; cf. śāṃtyudakaṃ.
      a. bathed, purified by ablutions.
      ground rice moistened with water. --sparśaḥ {1} touching different parts of the body with water. {2} touching water preparatory to or in confirmation of an oath, gift, or promise.
      a water-carrier. [0300-c]
