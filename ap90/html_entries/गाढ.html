• gāḍha See under gāha. [0456-c]
    • gāḍha p. p. [gāh-kta]
      1 Dived or plunged into, bathed in, deeply entered.
      2 Frequently plunged into, resorted to, thickly crowded or inhabited; tapasvigāḍhāṃ tamasāṃ prāpa nadīṃ turaṃgameṇa R. 9. 72.
      3 Closely pressed together, tightly drawn, fast, close, tight; gāḍhāṃgadairbāhubhiḥ R. 16. 60; gāḍhāliṃgana Amaru. 36, a close embrace; Ch. P. 6.
      4 Thick, dense.
      5 Deep, impervious.
      6 Strong, vehement excessive, intense; gāḍhanidrāmavāpa fell fast asleep; 20 °udvega excessively tor-mented; U. 3. 31; Māl. 9. 12; gāḍhotkaṃṭhālalitalulitairaṃgakaistāmyatīti Māl. 1. 15; Me. 83; prāptagāḍhaprakaṃpā Ś. Til. 12; Amaru. 72; so also gāḍhataptena taptaṃ Me. 102. --ḍhaṃ ind. Closely, fast, much, excessively, heavily, vehemently, powerfully. Comp. --aṃgada a. having closely fitting armlets.
    • --āliṃganaṃ a close embrace.
    • --karṇaḥ an attentive ear.
    • --muṣṭi a. close-fisted, avaricious, miserly. (--ṣṭiḥ) a sword. [0458-b]
