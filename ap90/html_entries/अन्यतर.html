anyatara a. (declined like a pronoun) One of two (persons or things), either of the two (with gen.); tayormunikumārayoranyataraḥ K. 151; saṃtaḥ parīkṣyānyataradbhajaṃte M. 1. 2 the one or the other; yuvayoranyatarāgacchatu Ś. 3; Ms. 2. 111.; 9. 171; other, different; anyatara-anyatara the one-the other; anyatarasyāṃ (loc. of °rā) either way, in both ways, optionally; frequently used by Pāṇini in his Sūtras in the sense of vā or vibhāṣā; hṛkroranyatarasyāṃ, ātmanepadeṣvanyatarasyāṃ &c. &c.
