bhaṭṭaḥ [bhaṭ-tan]
      1 A lord, master (used as a title of respect in addressing princes.).
      2 A title used with the names of learned Brāhmaṇas; bhaṭṭagopālasya pautraḥ Māl. 1; so kumārilabhaṭṭaḥ &c.
      3 Any learned man or philosopher.
      4 A kind of mixed caste, whose occupation is that of bards or panegyrists; kṣatriyādvipakanyāyāṃ bhaṭṭo jāto'nuvācakaḥ.
      5 A bard, panegyrist.
    Comp.
      --ācāryaḥ {1} a title given to a learned man or any celebrated teacher. {2} a great doctor.
      --prayāgaḥ = prayāga q. v. [0807-c]
