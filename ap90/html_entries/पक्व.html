• pakva a. [pac-kta tasya vaḥ]
      1 Cooked, roasted, boiled; as in pakvānna.
      2 Digested.
      3 Baked, burned, annealed (opp. āma); prakveṣṭakānāmākarṣaṇaṃ Mk. 3.
      4 Mature, ripe; pakvabiṃbādharoṣṭhī Me. 82.
      5 Fully developed, come to perfection, perfect, matured; as in pakvadhī.
      6 Experienced, shrewd.
      7 Ripe (as a boil), ready to suppurate.
      8 Grey (as hair).
      9 Perished, decaying, on the eve of destruction, ripe to meet one's doom.
    • --kvaṃ Cooked food. Comp. --atisāraḥ chronic dysentery.
    • --annaṃ cooked or dressed food.
    • --ādhānaṃ, --āśayaḥ the stomach, abdomen.
    • --iṣṭakā a baked brick.
    • --iṣṭakācitaṃ a building constructed with baked bricks.
    • --kṛt a. {1} cooking. {2} maturing. (--m.) the Nimba tree.
    • --keśa a. greyhaired.
    • --rasaḥ wine or any spirituous liquor.
    • --vāri n. the water of boiled rice (kāṃjika), sour rice-gruel. [0655-c]
