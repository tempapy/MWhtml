• takṣakaḥ [takṣ ṇvul] 1 A carpenter, wood-cutter (whether by caste or profession). --2 The chief actor in the prelude of a drama (i. e. the sūtradhāra). --3 N. of the architect of the gods. --4 N. of one of the principal Nāgas or serpents of the Pātāla, son of Kaśyapa and Kadru; (saved at the intercession of the sage Āstīka from being burnt down in the serpent-sacrifice performed by king Janamejaya, in which many others of his race were burnt down to ashes). [0525-a]
