• adhyāpakaḥ [adhi-i-ṇic-ṇvul] A teacher, preceptor, instructor; especially of the Vedas; vyākaraṇa°, nyāya° professor or teacher of grammar, logic &c. bhṛtaka° a hired teacher, mercenary teacher; °uditaḥ styled a professor. According to Viṣṇu Smṛti an adhyāpaka is of {2} kinds: he is either an Ācārya i. e. one who invests a boy with the sacred thread and initiates him into the Vedas, or he is an Upādhyāya i. e. one who teaches for livelihood (vṛttyarthaṃ) See Ms. 2. 140-141 and the two words.
