hemakūṭa The ‘golden-peaked’ mountain, one of the ranges of mountains which divide the known continent into nine Varṣas (varṣapavarta); it is generally supposed to be situated north of the Himālaya --or between the Meru and the Himālaya-forming with it the boundaries of the Kimpuruṣavarsha or abode of Kinnaras, cf K. 136. Kālidāsa speaks of it as ‘having plunged into the eastern and western oceans and emitting golden fluid’; see Ś. 7. [1196-b]
