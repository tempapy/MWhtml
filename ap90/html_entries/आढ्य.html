āḍhya a. [ā-dhyai-ka-pṛṣo° Tv.]
    • 1 Rich, wealthy; āḍhyo' bhijanavānasmi konyosti sadṛśo mayā Bg. 16. 15; Pt. 5. 8; Ms. 8. 169.
    • 2 (a) Rich in, abounding in, possessing abundantly, with instr. or as the last member of comp.; satya° Pt. 3. 9 very truthful; vaṃśasaṃpallāvaṇyāḍhyāya Dk. 18; evamādiguṇairāḍhyaḥ Vet.; samudramiva ratnāḍhyaṃ Rām. (b) Mixed with, watered with; gaṃdhāḍhya; sraja uttamagaṃdhāḍhyāḥ Mb.; mūtrāḍhyaiḥ karaṃjaphalasarṣapaiḥ Suśr.
    • 3 Abundant, copious.
      Comp. a. striving to get wealth.
      a. [āḍhyakule bhavaḥ kha] Descended from a rich family.
      a. (rī f.) [bhūtapūrva āḍhyaḥ caraṭ] once opulent.
      [āḍhyo vāto yatra] a convulsive or rheumatic palsy of the loins. [0232-a]
