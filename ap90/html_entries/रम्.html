• ram {c1c} A. (ramate, but Paras. when preceded by vi, ā, pari and upa; reme, araṃsta, araṃsīt Par. raṃsyate, raṃtuṃ, rata,) 1 To be pleased or delighted, rejoice, be gratified; rahasi ramate Māl. 3. 2, Ms. 2. 223; U. 2. 18. --2 To rejoice at, be pleased with, take delight in, be fond of (with instr. or loc.) lolāpāṃgairyadi na ramase locanairvaṃcito'si Me. 27.; vyajeṣṭa ṣaḍvargamaraṃsta nītau Bk. 1. 2. --3 To play, sport, dally, amuse oneself with; rājapriyāḥ kairaviṇyo ramaṃte madhupaiḥ saha Bv. 1. 126 (where the next meaning is also hinted); Bk. 6. 15, 67. --4 To have sexual intercourse with; sā tatputreṇa saha ramate H. 3. --5 To remain, stay, pause. --6 To take rest, remain quiet. --7 To gladden, delight. --Caus. (ramayati-te) To please, delight, satisfy. --Desid. (riraṃsate) To wish to sport &c. Śi. 15. 88. [0904-c]
