ṛbhu a. Ved.
      1 Skilful, clever, prudent (as an epithet of Indra, Agni and Ādityas).
      2 Handy (as a weapon).
      3 Shining far.
      --bhuḥ
      1 A deity, divinity; a god (dwelling in heaven).
      2 The god who is worshipped by the gods.
      3 A class of the attendants of gods.
      4 An artist, smith, especially a coach-builder (rathakāra).
      5 N. of three semi-divine beings called Ṛbhu, Vibhvan and Vāja, sons of Sudhanvan, a descendant of Aṅgiras, who were so called from the name of the eldest son. [Through their performance of good works they obtained divinity, exercised superhuman powers, and became entitled to worship. They are supposed to dwell in the Solar sphere, and are the artists who formed the horses of Indra, the carriage of the Aśvins, and the miraculous cow of Bṛhaspati. They made their aged parents young, and constructed four cups at a sacrifice from the one cup of Tvaṣṭṛ, who as the proper artificer of the gods, was in this respect their rival. They appear generally as accompanying Indra at the evening sacrifices. -- M. W.]. [0350-b]
