• praveśakaḥ ‘The introducer,’ an interlude acted by inferior characters (such as servants, buffoon &c.) for the purpose of acquainting the audience with events not represented on the stage, but a knowledge of which is essential for the proper understanding of what follows; (like the Viṣkambhaka it connects the story of the drama and the subdivisions of the plot, by briefly referring to what has occurred in the intervals of the acts, or what is likely to happen at the end; it never occurs at the beginning of the first act or at the end of the last). S. D. thus defines it: --praveśako' nudāttoktyā nīcapātraprayojitaḥ . aṃkadvayāṃtarvijñeyaḥ śeṣaṃ viṣkaṃbhake yathā .. 308; see viṣkaṃbhaka. [0760-c]
