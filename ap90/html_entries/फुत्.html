phu(phū)t ind. An onomatopoetic word generally used in composition with kṛ. and imitative of the sound made by blowing into liquids &c. sometimes it expresses disregard or contempt; (phu phū) tkṛ
      1 to blow into (a liquid); bālaḥ pāyasadagdho dadhyapi phūtkṛtya bhakṣayati H. 4. 103.
      2 To scream aloud, cry, shriek.
    Comp.
      --karaḥ fire.
      --kāraḥ, --kṛtaṃ, --kṛtiḥ f. {1} blowing into. {2} hissing, whizzing. {3} the hiss of a serpent. {4} sobbing. {5} screaming, a loud shriek, yell.
      --kṛta a. {1} blown into &c. {2} blown up (as a bubble.) {3} screamed aloud. (--taṃ) {1} the sound of a wind-instrument. {2} a loud cry, shriek, scream. [0785-b]
