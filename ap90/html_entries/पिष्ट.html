• piṣṭa p. p. [piṣ-kta] 1 Ground, powdered, crushed; Bv. 1. 12. --2 Rubbed together, squeezed or clasped (as the hands). --3 Kneaded. --ṣṭaṃ 1 Anything ground, a ground substance. --2 Flour, meal; piṣṭaṃ pinaṣṭi ‘he grinds flour’; i. e. does a useless work or a profitless repetition. --3 Lead. --Comp. --udakaṃ water mixed with flour. --pacana a pan for parching flour, a boiler &c. --paśuḥ an effigy of a beast made with flour. --pākabhṛt m. a boiler --pācakaṃ a boiler. --piṃḍaḥ a cake or ball of flour. --pūraḥ see ghṛtapūra--peṣaḥ, --peṣaṇaṃ ‘grinding flour’; i. e. doing any useless work, a vain or profitless repetition. °nyāyaḥ see under nyāya. --mehaḥ a variety of diabetes. --vartiḥ a kind of small ball made of the flour of barley, pulse or rice. --saurabhaḥ (pounded) sandal wood.
