• ajñāna a. [na. ba.] Ignorant, unwise. --naṃ [na. ta.] Ignorance, unconsciousness; especially, spiritual ignorance (avidyā) which makes one consider his self as distinct from the Supreme spirit and the material world as a reality. According to the Vedāntins, ajñāna is not merely a negative principle (jñānasya abhāvaḥ), but a distinct positive principle; oft. identified with māyā, prakṛti &c. See avidyā also. In compounds ajñāna may be translated by ‘unawares,’ ‘inadvertently,’ ‘unconsciously’; °ācarita, °uccārita &c. °nataḥ, -°nena, °nāt unawares, inadvertently, unconsciously, unwillingly; °taḥ svacaritaṃ nṛpatiḥ śaśaṃsa R. 9. 77 committed unintentionally or unconsciously.
