• ākāraḥ [ā-kṛ-ghañ] 1 Form, shape, figure; dvidhā° of two forms or sorts; Pt. 3. 37. --2 Aspect, appearance, mien, countenance; ākārasadṛśaprajñaḥ R. 1. 15, 16. 7; Ś. 1. --3 (Particularly) expression of the face, as giving a clue to one's inward thoughts or mental disposition; tasya saṃvṛtamaṃtrasya gūḍhākāreṃgitasya ca R. 1. 20; Pt. 1; bhavānapi saṃvṛtākāramāstāṃ V. 2; Ś. 7; Ki. 1. 14; sākāro nispṛhaḥ Pt. 3. 88 giving no clue to his inward thoughts, reserved; K. 233; Mv. 6; Ms. 7. 63, 8. 25-6. --4 Hint, sign, token. --5 Identity, oneness. --6 Recognition of identity (in Sān. phil.). --7 The letter ā. --Comp. --guptiḥ, --gopanaṃ, --gūhanaṃ dissimulation, suppressing all outward manifestation of the internal feelings.
