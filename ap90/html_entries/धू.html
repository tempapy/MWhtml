• dhū {c6c} P., {c1c} {c5c} {c9c} {c10c} U. (dhuvati, dhavati-te, dhunoti, dhūnute, dhūnāti, dhunīte, dhūnayatite, dhūta- dhūna) 1 To shake, agitate, cause to move or tremble; dhunvaṃti pakṣapavanairna nabho balākāḥ Rs. 3. 12; dhunvan kalpadrumakisalayāni Me. 62; Ku. 7. 49; R. 4. 67; Bk. 5. 101, 9. 7; 10. 22. --2 To shake off, remove, throw off; srajamapi śirasyaṃdhaḥ kṣiptāṃ dhunotyahiśaṃkayā Ś. 7. 24. --3 To blow away, destroy. --4 To kindle, excite, fan (as fire); vāyunā dhūyamāno hi vanaṃ dahati pāvakaḥ Mb.; pavanadhūtaḥ…agniḥ Rs. 1. 26. --5 To treat roughly, hurt, injure; mā na dhāvīrariṃ raṇe Bk. 9. 50; 15. 61. --6 To shake off from oneself, free oneself from; (sevakāḥ) ārohaṃti śanaiḥ paścāddhunvaṃtamapi pārthivaṃ Pt. 1. 36. --7 To strive against, resist, oppose. (The following stanza from Kavirahasya illustrates the root in its different conjugations: --dhūnoti caṃpakavanāni dhunotyaśokaṃ cūtaṃ dhunāti dhuvati sphuṭitātimuktaṃ . vāyurvidhūnayati caṃpakapuṣpareṇūn yatkānane dhavati caṃdanamaṃjarīśca ..).
