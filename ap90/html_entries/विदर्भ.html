• vidarbha The modern Berār, a great kingdom in ancient times lying to the north of Kuntala and extending from the banks of the Kṛṣṇā to about the banks of the Narmadā. On account of its great size, the country was also called ‘Mahārāṣṭra’; cf. B. R. 10. 74. Kuṇḍinapura, also called Vidarbhā, was its ancient capital, which probably stands for the modern Beder. The river Varadā (Warda) divided Vidarbha into two parts, Amarāvatī being the capital of the northern, and Pratiṣṭhāna of the southern, part. [1195-b]
