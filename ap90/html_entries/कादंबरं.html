• kādaṃbaraṃ A spirituous liquor distilled from the flowers of the Kadamba tree; niṣevya madhu mādhavāḥ sarasamatra kādaṃbaraṃ Śi. 4. 66., --rī 1 A spirituous liquor distilled from the flowers of the kadaṃba tree. --2 Spirituous liquor or wine in general; kādaṃbarīsākṣikaṃ prathamasauhṛdamiṣyate Ś. 6; or kādaṃbarīmadavighūrṇitalocanasya yuktaṃ hi lāṃgalabhṛtaḥ patanaṃ pṛthivyām Udb.; °rasabhareṇa K. 240. --3 The fluid issuing from the temples of a rutting elephant. --4 An epithet of Sarasvatī, the goddess of learning. --5 A female cuckoo. --6 The rain-water collected into clefts or hollow places. --7 A female bird (sārikā).
