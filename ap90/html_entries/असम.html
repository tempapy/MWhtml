asama a.
    • 1 Uneven, odd (as a number); asamaśīlāḥ khalu mṛgāḥ Bv. 1. 2 mean, contemptible.
    • 2 Unequal (in space, number or dignity); asamaiḥ samīyamānaḥ Pt. 1. 74.
    • 3 Unequalled, matchless, unsurpassed; samavatārasamairasamaistaṭaiḥ Ki. 5. 7; vādyaviśeṣāṇāmasamaḥ śrotā K. 12; Ms. 10. 73.
    • 4 Uneven, not level (as ground).
      N. of Buddha.
      Comp. ‘having an odd number of arrows,’ epithets of Cupid who has five arrows. --nayana, --netra, --locana a. ‘having an odd number of eyes’, epithets of Śiva, who has three eyes.
      a metre in which the ganas are not the same in all lines. [0211-a]
