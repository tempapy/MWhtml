• piśācaḥ [piśitamācāmati, ā + cam bā° ḍa pṛṣo°] A fiend, goblin, devil, spirit, malevolent being; nanvāśvāsitaḥ piśācopi bhojanena V. 2; Ms. 1. 37; 12. 44. --Comp. --ālayaḥ phosphorescence. --druḥ a kind of tree. --bādhā --saṃcāraḥ demoniacal possession. --bhāṣā ‘the language of devils’, a gibberish or corruption of Sanskrit, one of the lowest Prākṛta dialects used in plays. --sabhaṃ {1} an assemblage of fiends. {2} pandemonium, the hall of their assembly.
