• arātiḥ [na rāti dadāti sukhaṃ, rā-ka, na. ta.] 1 An enemy, foe; deśaḥ soyamarātiśoṇitajalairyasminhradāḥ pūritāḥ Ve. 3. 31; (in the Veda) non-offering (of sacrifices), stinginess, hardness, malignity, malevolence, failure or adversity; malignity personified; evil spirit whose aim it was to defeat the good intentions and disturb the happiness of man (used in f.). --2 The number six. --3 The sixth position (in astronomy). --Comp. --dūṣaṇa, --dūṣi, --ha a. Ved. destroying adversities or enemies. --bhaṃgaḥ destruction of enemies. [0160-c]
