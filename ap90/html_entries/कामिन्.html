• kāmin a. (nī f.) [kam-ṇini] 1 Lustful. --2 Desirous. --3 Loving, fond. --m. 1 A lover, a lustful person (paying particular attention to ladies); tvayā caṃdramasā cātisaṃdhīyate kamijanasārthaḥ Ś. 3; tvāṃ kāmino madanadūtimudāharaṃti V. 4. 11; Amaru. 2; M. 3. 14. --2 A uxorious husband. --3 The ruddy goose or cakravāka bird. --4 A sparrow. --5 An epithet of Śiva. --6 The moon. --7 A pigeon. --8 The Supreme being. --nī 1 A loving, affectionate, or fond woman; Ms. 8. 112. --2 A lovely or beautiful woman; udayati hi śaśāṃkaḥ kāminīgaṃḍapāṃḍuḥ Mk. 1. 57; keṣāṃ naiṣā kathaya kavitākāminī kautukāya P. R. 1. 22. --3 A woman (in general); mṛgayā jahāra catureva kāminī R. 9. 69; Me. 63; Rs. 1. 28. --4 A timid woman. --5 Spirituous liquor. [0395-b]
