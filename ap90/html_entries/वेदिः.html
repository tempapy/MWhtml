• vediḥ [vid-in] A learned man, sage, Paṇḍita.
    • --diḥ, -dī f.
      1 An altar especially one prepared for a sacrifice; amī vediṃ paritaḥ kḷptadhiṣṇyāḥ (vahrayaḥ) Ś. 4. 7.
      2 An altar of a particular shape, the middle points of which come very close to each other; madhyena sā vedivilagnamadhyā Ku. 1. 39; (some propose to take vedi in this passage as meaning ‘a sealring’).
      3 A quadrangular spot in the court-yard of a temple or palace.
      4 A seal-ring.
      5 N. of Sarasvatī.
      6 A tract or region.
    Comp.
    • --īśaḥ {1} N. of Agni. {2} of Brahman.
    • --jā an epithet of Draupadi who was born from the midst of the sacrificial altar of king Drupada. [1011-a]
