• chaṃda a. [chaṃd-ac] 1 Pleasing, fascinating, inviting, alluring. --2 Private, solitary, secret. --3 Praising. --daḥ 1 Wish, desire, fancy, liking, will; vijñāpyatāṃ devī yaste chaṃda iti V. 3 just as you like; Pt. 1. 69. --2 Free will, one's own choice, whim, free or wilful conduct; ṣaṣṭhe kāle tvamapi divasasyātmanaśchaṃ davartī V. 2. 1; Gīt. 1; Y. 2. 195; svacchaṃdaṃ according to one's free will, independently. --3 (Hence) subjection, control. --4 Meaning, intention, purport. --5 Poison. --6 Appearance, look, shape. --7 Pleasure, delight. --Comp. --anuvṛttaṃ, --ttiḥ indulgence of whims, humouring, compliance.
