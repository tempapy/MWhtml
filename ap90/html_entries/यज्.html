• yaj {c1c} U. (yajati-te, iyāja, īje; ayākṣīt ayaṣṭa, yakṣyati-te, yaṣṭaṃ, iṣṭa; pass. ijyate; desid. yiyakṣati-te) 1 To sacrifice, worship with sacrifices (often with instr. of words meaning ‘a sacrifice’); yajeta rājā kratubhiḥ Ms. 7. 79; 5. 53, 6. 36, 11. 40; Bk. 14. 90; so aśvamedheneje, pākayajñeneje &c. --2 To make an oblation to (with acc. of the deity and instr. of the means of sacrifice or oblation); paśunā rudraṃ yajate Sk.; yastilairyajataṃ pitṝn Mb., Ms. 8. 105, 11. 119. --3 To worship, adore, honour, revere. --4 To consecrate, dedicate. --5 To give, bestow. --Caus. (yājayati-te) 1 To cause to sacrifice. --2 To assist at a sacrifice. --3 To perform the office of the sacrificing priest. [0883-b]
