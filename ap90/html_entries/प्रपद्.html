• prapad {c4c} A. 1 To enter upon, set forward, set foot in. --2 (a) To go to or towards, approach, resort or attain to, reach; tāṃ janmane śailavadhūṃ prapede Ku. 1. 21; (kṣitīśaṃ) kautsaḥ prapede varataṃtuśiṣyaḥ R. 5. 1; Bk. 4. 1; Ki. 1. 9; 11. 16; R. 8. 11. (b) To take shelter or refuge with, flee to for safety, submit; śaraṇārthamanyāṃ kathaṃ prapatsye tvayi dīpyamāne R. 14. 64. --3 To go or come to a particular state, arrive at, attain to, arrive at or be in a particular condition; reṇuḥ prapede pathi paṃkabhāvaṃ R. 16. 30; muhūrtakarṇotpalatāṃ prapede Ku. 7. 81; bālyātparaṃ sātha vayaḥ prapede Ku. 1. 31; 5. 24; īdṛśīmavasthāṃ prapannosmi Ś. 5: ṛṣinikarauriti saṃśayaḥ prapede Bv. 4. 33; Amaru. 27. --4 To get, find, secure, obtain, attain to; partake of share in; sahakāra na prapede madhupena bhavatsamaṃ jagati Bv. 1. 21; kāṃtaṃ vapurvyomacaraṃ prapede R. 5. 51. --5 To behave or act towards, deal with; kiṃ prapadyate vaidarbhaḥ M. 1 ‘what does he propose to do’; paśyāmo mayi kiṃ prapadyate Amaru. 20. --6 To admit, allow, agree or consent to; Y. 2. 40. --7 To draw near, come on, approach (as time &c.). --8 To be going on, to proceed. --9 To take effect, thrive, prosper. --10 To throw oneself down, fall down (at another's feet). --11 Ved. To attack, assault. [0751-a]
