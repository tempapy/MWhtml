• danuḥ f. N. of one of the daughters of Dakṣa given in marriage to Kaśyapa and mother of the Dānavas. -- m. N. of a monster, son of Śrī, cursed by Indra and killed by Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa. He had a headless trunk, and hence called danukabaṃdha. --Comp. --jaḥ, --putraḥ, --saṃbhavaḥ, --dūnuḥ a demon. °ariḥ, --dviṣ m. a god. [0556-b]
