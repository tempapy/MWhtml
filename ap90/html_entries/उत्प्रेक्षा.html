• utprekṣā 1 Conjecture, guess. --2 Disregarding, carelessness, indifference. --3 (In Rhet.) A figure of speech, ‘Poetical fancy,’ which consists in supposing upameya and upamāna as similar to each other in some respects and in indicating, expressly or by implication, a probability of their identity based on such similarity; it is the imagining of one object under the character of another; saṃbhāvanamathotprekṣā prakṛtasya pareṇa yat K. P. 10; e. g. liṃpatīva tamoṃgāni vaṣaṃtīvāṃjanaṃ nabhaḥ Mk. 1. 34; sthitaḥ pṛthivyā iva mānadaṃḍaḥ Ku. 1. 1. It is usually expressed by iva, or by words like manye, śaṃke, dhruvaṃ, prāyaḥ, nūnaṃ &c. (see Kāv. 2. 234); cf. S. D. 686-692 and R. G. under utprekṣā also. --4 A parable. --5 An ironical comparison. [0298-b]
