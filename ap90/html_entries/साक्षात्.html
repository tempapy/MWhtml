• sākṣāt ind. 1 In the presence of, before the very eyes, visibly, openly, evidently. --2 In person, actually, in bodily form; sākṣāt priyāmupagatāmapahāya pūrvaṃ Ś. 6. 15; 1. 6. --3 Directly. In comp. it is often translated by ‘incarnate’; sākṣādyamaḥ; or by ‘open, direct’; tatsākṣātpratiṣedhaḥ kopāya Māl. 1. 11. ( sākṣātkṛ {1} to see with one's own eyes, realize personally. {2} to have an intuitive perception or manifestation of; sākṣātkṛtadharmāṇa ṝṣayaḥ U. 7). --Comp. --karaṇaṃ {1} causing to be visibly present, {2} making evident to the senses. {3} intuitive perception. --kāraḥ perception, apprehension, knowledge.
