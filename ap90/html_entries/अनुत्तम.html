• anuttama a. [na uttamo yasmāt] 1 Than which there is nothing better, having no superior or better, unsurpassed, the very best or highest, incomparably or pre-eminently the best; sarvadravyeṣu vidyaiva dravyamāhuranuttamaṃ H. Pr. 4; kāṃkṣan gatimanuttamāṃ Ms. 2. 242; Y. 1. 87; adastvayā nunnamanuttamaṃ tamaḥ Śi. 1. 27 all-pervading; Bg. 7. 18; Ms. 2. 9; 5. 158; 8. 81. --2 Not the best. --3 (In gram.) Not used in the uttama or first, person. --maḥ N. of Śiva or Viṣṇu. --Comp. --aṃbhas, --aṃbhasikaṃ a term in Sāṃkhya philosophy, said to mean ‘indifference to and abstinence from sensual enjoyment, as fatiguing or involving injury to external objects.’ [0070-c]
