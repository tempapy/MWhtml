• śaṃk {c1c} A. (śaṃkate, śaṃkita) 1 To doubt, be uncertain, hesitate, be doubtful; śaṃke jīvati vā na vā Rām. --2 To dread, fear, be afraid (with abl.); nāśaṃkiṣṭa vivasvataḥ Bk. 15. 39; aśakitebhyaḥ śaketa śaṃkitebhyaśca sarvataḥ Subhāṣ. --3 To suspect, mistrust, distrust; svairdoṣairbhavati hi śaṃkito manuṣyaḥ Mk. 4. 2. --4 To think, believe, fancy, imagine, think probable, suspect, fear; tvayyāsanne nayanamuparispaṃdi śaṃke mṛgākṣyāḥ Me. 95; nāhaṃ punastathā tvāyi yathā hi māṃ śaṃkase bhīru V. 3. 14; Bk. 3. 26, N. 22. 42. --5 To start an objection, raise a doubt or objection (about); atredaṃ śaṃkyate (often used in controversial language); na ca brahmaṇaḥ pramāṇāṃtaragamyatvaṃ śaṃkituṃ śakyaṃ Sarva S. --Caus. To frighten, terrify. [1029-b]
