vat a.
      1 An affix added to nouns to show ‘possession’; dhanavat possessed of wealth; rūpavat beautiful; so bhagavat, bhāsvat &c. (the words so formed being adjectives).
      2 Added to the base of the past passive participle vat turns it into a past active participle; ityuktavaṃtaṃ janakātmajāyāṃ R. 14. 43. --ind. An affix added to nouns or adjectives to denote ‘likeness’ or ‘resemblance’ and may be translated by ‘like’, ‘as’; ātmavatsarvabhūtāni yaḥ paśyati sa paṃḍitaḥ. [0940-c]
