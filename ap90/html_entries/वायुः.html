• vāyuḥ [vā-uṇ yuka ca Uṇ. 1. 1] 1 Air, wind; vāyurvidhunayati caṃpakapuṣpareṇūn K.R.; ākāśāttu vikurvāṇātsarvagaṃdhavahaḥ śuciḥ . balavāñjāyate vāyuḥ sa vai sparśaguṇo mataḥ .. Ms. 1. 76. (There are seven courses of wind one above the other: --āvahaḥ pravahaścaiva saṃvahaścodvahastathā . vivahākhyaḥ parivahaḥ parāvaha iti kramāt). --2 The god of wind, the deity supposed to preside over wind, (who is the regent of the north-west quarter.). --3 A lifewind or vital air, of which five kinds are enumerated: --prāṇa, apāna, samāna, vyāna, and udāna. --4 Morbid affection or vitiation of the windy humour. --Comp. --āspadaṃ the sky, atmosphere. --ketuḥ dust. --koṇaḥ the north-west. --gaṃḍaḥ flatulence (caused by indigestion). --gati a. swift as wind, very fleet. --gulmaḥ {1} a hurricane, storm. {2} a whirlpool. --gocaraḥ the range of the wind. --grasta a. {1} affected by wind, flatulent. {2} gouty. --jātaḥ, --tanayaḥ, naṃdanaḥ, --putraḥ, --sutaḥ, --sūnuḥ epithets of Hanumat or Bhīma. --dāruḥ a cloud. --nighna a. affected by wind, crazy, mad, frantic. --nivṛttiḥ f. {1} a lull, calm. {2} cure of windy distempers (such as gout &c.). --purāṇaṃ N. of one of the 18 Purāṇas. --phalaṃ {1} hail. {2} the rain-bow. --bhakṣaḥ, --bhakṣaṇaḥ, --bhuj m. {1} one who feeds only on air, as an ascetic. {2} a snake; cf. pavanāśana. --roṣā night. --rugṇa a. broken down by wind; R. 9. 63. --vartman m., n. the sky, atmosphere. --vāhaḥ smoke. --vāhinī a vein, an artery, a vessel of the body. --vega, sama a. swift as wind. --sakhaḥ, --sakhiḥ m. fire.
