pārśva a. Near, proximate.
      --rśvaḥ --rśvaṃ [parśūnāṃ samūhaḥ]
      1 The part of the body below the arm-pit, the region of the ribs; śayane saṃniṣaṇṇaikapārśvā Me. 89.
      2 The side, flank (in general) (of animate or inanimate objects); piṭharaṃ kvathadatimātraṃ nijapārśvāneva dahatitarāṃ Pt. 1. 324.
      3 Vicinity.
      4 Ved. A curved knife.
      --rśvaḥ An epithet of Jina.
      --rśvaṃ
      1 A multitude of ribs.
      2 A fraudulent expedient, a dishonourable means.
      3 The extremity of the fore-axle of a wheel. (pārśvaṃ is used adverbially in the sense of ‘near to,’ ‘by the side of,’ ‘towards’; Ś. 7. 8; so pārśvāt ‘from the side of, away, from’; pārśve ‘near’, ‘at hand’, ‘at the side’,; na me dūrekiṃcitkṣaṇamapi na pārśve rathajavāt Ś. 1. 9, Bh. 3. 37).
