• śakya pot. p. 1 Possible, practicable, capable of being done or effected (usually with an inf.); śa kyo vārayituṃ jalena hutabhuk Bh. 2. 11, R. 2. 49, 54. --2 Fit to be effected. --3 Easy to be effected. --4 Directly conveyed or expressed (as the meaning of a word); śakyo'rtho'bhidhayā jñeyaḥ S. D. 10. --5 Potential. (The form śakyaṃ is some times used as a predicative word with an inf. in a passive sense, the real object of the infinitive being in the nom. case; evaṃ hi praṇaya vatī sā śakyamupekṣituṃ kupitā M. 3. 22; śakyaṃ…aviralamāliṃgitu pavanaḥ Ś. 3. 7, vibhūtayaḥ śakyamava ptumūrjitāḥ Subhāṣ.; Bg. 18. 11.). --Comp. --arthaḥ the meaning directly expressed. --pratīkāra a. remediable.
