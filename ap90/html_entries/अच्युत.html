• acyuta a. [na. ta. svarūpasāmarthyāt na cyutaḥ cyavate vā-kālasāmānye kartarikta] 1 Not fallen, firm, fixed; not giving way, solid; garuḍamūrtiriva acyutasthitiramaṇīyā K. 52 (acyuta meaning ‘Vishṇu’ and ‘firm’, ‘fixed’); °kṣit having solid ground. --2 Imperishable, permanent; °ruṣ inveterate enmity. --3 [na cyotati kṣarati; cyut-ka. na. ta.] Not melting away or perishing, not leaking or dripping. --taḥ 1 N. of Viṣṇu; of the Almighty being; yasmānna cyutapūrvohamacyutastena karmaṇā Bhāg. gacchāmyacyutadarśanena K. P. 5. (where a° also means ‘one who is firm, does not yield to passions’). --2 N. of a plant, Morinda Tinctoria. --3 A sort of poetical composition containing 12 cantos. --Comp. --agrajaḥ [ṣa. ta.] N. of Balarāma or Indra. --aṃgajaḥ, --putraḥ, ātmajaḥ N. of Cupid, son of Kṛṣṇa and Rukmiṇī. --āvāsaḥ, --vāsaḥ the sacred fig-tree. --jaḥ [paṃ. ta.] a class of Jaina deities said to have been produced from Viṣṇu --sthalaṃ N. of a place in the Punjab.
