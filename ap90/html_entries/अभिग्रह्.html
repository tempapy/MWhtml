abhigrah (Ved. grabh) 9 U.
      1 To take or seize, catch, catch hold of, seize forcibly, attack; ripuṇābhigṛhya Dk. 5, 98, 103.
      2 To accept, take.
      3 To receive, (as a guest).
      4 To fold, lay or bring together (as the hands).
      5 To set, show or bring forth (as blossom, fruit &c.). --Caus. To catch or surprise one in the very act, to let oneself be so caught; aryavaryasya kasyacid gṛhe corayitvā rūpābhigrahito baddhaḥ Dk. 96. [0128-a]
