• caṃḍāla a. [caṃḍ-ālac]
      1 Wicked or cruel in deeds, of black deeds ( krūrakarman); cf. karmacāḍāla.
    • --laḥ A general name for the lowest and most despised of the mixed castes originating from a Śūdra father and a Brāhmaṇa mother.
      2 A man of this caste, an outcast, caṃḍālaḥ kimayaṃ dvija tirathavā Bh. 3. 56; Ms. 5. 131; 10. 12, 16; 11. 176. Comp. --vallakī the lute of a Cāṇḍāla, a common or vulgar lute. [0482-b]
