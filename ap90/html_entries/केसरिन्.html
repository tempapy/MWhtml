• kesa(śa)rin m. [keśa-sa-ra-ini] 1 A lion; anuhuṃkurute ghanadhvaniṃ na hi gomāyurutāni kesarī Śi. 16. 25; dhanurdharaḥ kesariṇaṃ dadarśa R. 2. 29; Ś. 7. 3. --2 The best, excellent, or most pro minent of a class (at the end ofcomp.); cf. kuṃjara, siṃha &c. --3 A horse. --4 The citron plant. --5 The Punnāga tree. --6 N. of the father of Hanūmat. --Comp. --sutaḥ an epithet of Hanūmat.
