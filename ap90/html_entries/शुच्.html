śuc
      I. 1 P. (śocati)
      1 To be sorry, grieve for, bewall, mourn; arodīdrāvaṇo'śocīnmohaṃ cāśiśriyatparaṃ Bk. 15. 71; 21. 6; Bg. 16. 5.
      2 To regret, repent.
      II. 4 U. ( śucyati-te)
      1 To be sorry or afflicted.
      2 To be wet.
      3 To shine.
      4 To be pure or clean.
      5 To decay; become fetid.
      6 To brighten, illuminate.
      7 To burn, consume. [1049-c]
      śuc, śucā f. [śuc-kvip ṭāp vā] Grief, sorrow, affliction, distress; vikalakaraṇaḥ pāṃḍucchāyaḥ śucā paridurbalaḥ U. 3. 22; kāmaṃ jīvati me nātha iti sā vijahau śucaṃ R. 12. 75, 8. 72; Me. 88, Ś. 4. 18. [1049-c]
