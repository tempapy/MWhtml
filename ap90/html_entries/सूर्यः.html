• sūryaḥ [sarati ākāśe sūryaḥ, yadvā suvati karmaṇi lokaṃ prerayati; cf. Sk. on P. III. 1. 114] 1 The sun; sūrye tapatyāvaraṇāya ṭṭaṣṭeḥ kalpeta lokasya kathaṃ tamisrā R. 5. 13. [In mythology, the sun is regarded as a son of Kaśyapa and Aditi; cf. Ś. 7. 20. He is represented as moving in a chariot drawn by seven horses, with Aruṇa for his charioteer. He is also represented as all-seeing, the constant beholder of the good and bad deeds of mortals. Saṃjñā (or Chāyā or Aśvinī) was his principal wife, by whom he had Yama and Yamunā, the two Aśvins and Saturn. He is also described as having been the father of Manu Vaivasvata, the founder of the solar race of kings,]. --2 The tree called Arka. --3 The number ‘twelve’ (derived from the twelve forms of the sun). --4 The swallow-wort. --5 N. of Śiva. --Comp. --apāyaḥ sunset; Me. 80. --ardhyaṃ the presentation of an offering to the sun. --aśman m. the sun-stone. --aśvaḥ a horse of the sun. --astaṃ sunset. --ātapaḥ heat or glare of the sun, sunshine. --ālokaḥ sunshine. --āvartaḥ a kind of sun-flower. --āhva a. named after the sun.
