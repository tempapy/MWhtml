• jīvana a. (nī f.) [jīv bhāve lyuṭ] Enlivening, giving life. --naḥ 1 A living being. --2 Wind. --3 A son. --4 The Supreme Being. --naṃ 1 Life, existence; (fig. also); tvamāse mama bhūṣaṇaṃ tvamasi mama jīvanaṃ Gīt. 10. --2 The principle of life, vital energy; Bg. 7. 9. --3 Water; bījānāṃ prabhava namo'stu jīvanāya Ki. 18. 39; or jīvanaṃ jīvanaṃ (life) haṃti prāṇān haṃti samīraṇaḥ Udb. --4 Livelihood, profession, means of existence (fig. also); Ms. 11. 77; viduṣāṃ jīvanaṃ mūrkhaḥ H. 3. 33. --5 Butter made from milk one day old. --6 Marrow. --7 Enlivening, making alive. --Comp. --aṃtaḥ death. --āghātaṃ poison. --āvāsaḥ {1} ‘residing in water’, epithet of Varuṇa, the regent of water. {2} the body. --upāyaḥ livelihood. --oṣadhaṃ {1} elixir of life. {2} a lifegiving medicine.
