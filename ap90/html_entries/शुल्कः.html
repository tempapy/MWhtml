śulkaḥ, --lakaṃ [śulkyate atisṛjyate karmaṇi ghañ]
      1 A toll, tax, customs, duty; particularly levied at ferries, passes, roads &c. kaḥ sudhīḥ saṃtyajedrāṃḍaṃ śulkasyaivātisādhvasāt H. 3. 125; Ms. 8. 159; Y. 2. 47.
      2 Gain, profit.
      3 Money advanced to ratify a bargain.
      4 Purchase-price (of a girl); money given to the parents of a bride; pīḍivo duṃhitṛśulkasaṃsthayā R. 11. 38; na kanyāyā pitā vidvān gṛhṇīyācchulkamaṇvapi Ms. 3. 51, 8. 204, 9. 93, 98.
      5 A nuptial present.
      6 Marriage settlement or dowry.
      7 Present given by the bride-groom to his bride.
    Comp.
      --khaṃḍanaṃ defrauding (government) of its due revenue.
      --grāhaka, --grāhin m. a toll-collector.
      --daḥ {1} the giver of a nuptial present. {2} an affianced suitor.
      --śālā --sthānaṃ a toll-station, customhouse. [1051-b]
