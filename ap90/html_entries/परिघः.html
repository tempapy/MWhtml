• parighaḥ 1 An iron (or wooden) beam or bar used for locking or shutting a gate (argala)); ekaḥ kṛtsnāṃ nagaraparighaprāṃśubāhurbhunakti Ś. 2. 15; R. 16. 84; Śi. 19. 32; M. 5. 2. --2 (Hence) A bar barrier, hindrance, obstacle; bhārgavasya sukṛto'pi so' bhavatsvargamārgaparigho duratyayaḥ R. 11. 88. --3 A stick or club studded or tipped with iron; R. 12. 73. --4 An iron club in general. --5 A water-jar, pitcher. --6 A glass-pitcher. --7 A house, dwelling. --8 Killing, destroying. --9 Striking, a stroke or blow. --10 A child which assumes a peculiar cross position in birth. --11 A line of clouds crossing the sun at sunrise or sunset. --12 The gate of a palace, town or house. --ghau (m. dual) Two birds flying on each side of a traveller (regarded as an omen).
