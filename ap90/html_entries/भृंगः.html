bhṛṃgaḥ [bhṛ-gan kit nuṭa ca Uṇ. 1. 122]
      1 A large black bee; Bv. 1. 5; R. 8. 53.
      2 A kind of wasp.
      3 A kind of bird.
      4 A libertine, dissolute or lecherous man; cf. bhramara.
      5 A golden vase or jar.
      6 The forktailed shrike.
      --gaṃ Talc.
      --gī
      1 The female of the large black bee; bhṛṃgīva puṣpaṃ puruṣaṃ strī vāṃcchati navaṃ navam.
      2 A poisonous plant (ativiṣa).
    Comp.
      --adhipaḥ the queen of bees. --abhīṣṭaḥ the mango tree.
      --ānaṃdā the Yūthikā creeper.
      --āvalī a flight of bees.
      --jaṃ {1} aloe-wood. {2} talc. (--jā) the plant bhārgī.
      --parṇikā small cardamoms.
      --priyā the Mādhavī creeper.
      --rāj m. {1} a kind of large bee. {2} N. of a shrub.
      --riṭiḥ, --rīṭiḥ N. of one of the attendants of Śiva (said to be very deformed).
      --rolaḥ a kind of wasp.
      --vallabhaḥ a species of Kadamba. [0824-c]
