• aṃguliḥ --lī --riḥ --rī f. [aṃga-uli Uṇ. 4. 2] 1 A finger (the names of the {5} fingers are aṃguṣṭha thumb, tarjanī forefinger, madhyamā middle finger, anāmikā ring-finger, and kaniṣṭhā or kaniṣṭhikā the little finger); a toe (of the foot); ekaviṃśoyaṃ puruṣaḥ daśa hastyā aṃgulayo daśa pādyā ātmaikaviṃśaḥ Ait. Br. --2 The thumb, great toe. --3 The tip of an elephant's trunk. --4 The measure aṃgula. --5 N. of the tree gajakarṇikā. --6 Penis (?). --Comp. --toraṇaṃ [aṃguleḥ toraṇamiva kṛtaṃ] a mark on the forehead of the form of the half moon made with sandal &c. --traṃ, --trāṇaṃ [aṃguliṃ trāyate, aṃgulistrāyate anena trai- ka.] a finger-protector (a contrivance like a thimble used by archers to protect the thumb or finger from being injured by the bow-string). --paṃcakaṃ the five fingers collectively. --mudrā, --mudrikā a seal-ring. --moṭanaṃ, --sphoṭanaṃ [aṃgulyormoṭanaṃ mardanaṃ sphoṭanaṃ tāḍanaṃ vā yatra] snapping or cracking the fingers (Mar. cuṭakī). --saṃgā [aṃgulau saṃgo yasyāḥ sā] sticking to the fingers; °gā yavāgūḥ : °gā gāḥ sādayati P. VIII. 3. 80 Sk. (aṃgulisaṃlepakārakaṃ yavāgūdravyaṃ Tv.). (--gaḥ) contact of the fingers; act of fingering. --saṃjñā [tṛ. ta.] a sign made by the finger; mukhārpitaikāṃgulisaṃjñayaiva Ku. 3. 41. --saṃdeśaḥ making signs with fingers; cracking or snapping the fingers as a sign. --saṃbhūta a. [sa. ta.] produced from or on the finger. (--taḥ) a finger-nail. [0021-a]
