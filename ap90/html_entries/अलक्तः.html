alaktaḥ --ktakaḥ [na rakto'smāt, rasya latvaṃ, svārthe kan Tv.] The red resin of certain trees, red lac or sap (formerly used by women to dye certain parts of their body, particularly the soles of the feet and lip); (daṃtavāsasā) cirojjhitālaktakapāṭalena Ku. 5. 34, 68; 7. 58; biṃbādharālaktakaḥ M. 3. 5; alaktakāṃkāṃ padavīṃ tatāna R. 7. 7; striyo hṛtārthāḥ puruṣaṃ nirarthaṃ niṣpīḍitālaktakavattyajaṃti Mk. 4. 15.
      Comp. red lac, juice; alaktarasaraktābhāvalaktarasavarjitau . adyāpi caraṇau tasyāḥ padmakośasamaprabhau Rām. --rāgaḥ the red colour of alakta. [0171-c]
