• kṣāra a. [kṣar-jvalā bā° ṇa]
      1 Corrosive, caustic, acid, pungent, saline.
      2 Flowing, oozing.
    • --raḥ
      1 Juice, essence.
      2 Treacle, molasses.
      3 Any corrosive or acid substance; kṣate kṣāramivāsahyaṃ jātaṃ tasyaiva darśanaṃ U. 4. 7; kṣāraṃ kṣate prakṣipan Mk. 5. 18; (kṣāraṃ kṣate kṣip &c. has become proverbial, and means ‘to aggravate the pain which is already unbearable,’ ‘to make bad worse’, ‘to add insult to injury’).
      4 Glass.
      5 Salt.
      6 Ashes.
      7 A rogue, cheat.
    • --raṃ
      1 Black salt.
      2 Water.
    Comp.
    • --acchaṃ sea-salt.
    • --aṃjanaṃ an alkaline unguent.
    • --aṃbu n. an alkaline fluid.
    • --udaḥ, --udakaḥ, --udadhiḥ, --samudraḥ the salt ocean.
    • --kardamaḥ {1} a pool of saline mud. {2} N. of a hell.
    • --tailaṃ oil cooked with alkaline ingredients.
    • --trayaṃ, --tritayaṃ natron, salt-petre and borax.
    • --nadī a river of alkaline water in hell.
    • --bhūmiḥ f., --mṛttikā saline soil; kimāścaryaṃ kṣārabhūmau prāṇadā yamadūtikā Udb. --melakaḥ an alkaline substance.
    • --rasaḥ a saline flavour.
    • --śreṣṭhaṃ alkaline earth. [0435-c]
