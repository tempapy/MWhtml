• tātaḥ [tan-kta dīrghaśca Uṇ. 3. 90] 1 {1} A father; mṛṣyaṃtu lavasya bāliśatāṃ tātapādāḥ U. 6; hā tāteti kraṃditamākarṇya viṣaṇṇaḥ R. 9. 75. --2 A term of affection, endearment or pity, applied to any person, but usually to inferiors or juniors, pupils, children &c. tāta caṃdrāpīḍa K. 106; Māl. 6. 16; rakṣasā bhakṣitastāta tava tāto vanāṃtare Mb. --3 A term of respect applied to elders or other venerable personages; hepitā hi bahavo nareśvarāstena tāta dhanuṣā dhanurbhṛtaḥ R. 11. 40; tasmānmucye yathā tāta saṃvidhātuṃ tathārhasi 1. 72. --4 Any person for whom one feels pity. --Comp. --gu a. {1} agreeable to a father. {2} paternal. (--guḥ) a paternal uncle. --tulyaḥ a paternal uncle, or the most respectable of a man's male relations. [0534-b]
