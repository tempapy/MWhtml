• kākuḥ f. 1 Change of the voice under different emotions, such as fear, grief, anger; bhinnakaṃṭhadhvanirdhīraiḥ kākurityabhidhīyate S. D.; alīkakākukaraṇakuśalatāṃ K. 222. (Hence) --2 A word of negation used in such a manner that it implies the contrary (affirmative), as in questions of appeal, (in such cases the intended meaning is suggested by a change of the voice), cf. Pt. 1. 146. --3 Muttering, murmuring. --4 Tongue. --5 Stress, emphasis. [0391-b]
