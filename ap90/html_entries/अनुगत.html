anugata p. p.
      1 (Used passively) (a) Followed (lit. & fig.), attended; anugatamalivṛṃdairgaṃḍabhittīrvihāya R. 12. 102; M. 3. 9; Ms. 11. 71. (b) Full of, filled with; dāridryeṇābhibhūtena tvatsnehānugatena ca Mk. 4. 5; varṣadharakalamūkānugatena parijanena K. 159 consisting or made up of; ciṃtānugatasarvātman Mb. (c) Covered, as by a dress hanging behind; śivamivānugataṃ gajacarmaṇā Ki. 5. 2 (paścādvyāptaṃ). (d) Extinguished &c. (e) Acquired, obtained.
      2 (Used actively) (a) Following, obeying, observing; svamatamanugataḥ Mu. 5. 19; vibhavānugatā bhāryā Mk. 3. 28; digvijayaprasaṃgenānugato bhūmimimāṃ K. 191 come to; Ms. 9. 267; K. 166; Mu. 6. 5; H. 2. 56; R. 15. 9. (b) Corresponding or tallying with, adapted or answering to, in harmony with; sūtreṇānugataṃ bhavati Ś. B.; pādanyāso layamanugataḥ M. 2. 9 in accompaniment to the musical time; mṛdaṃga °taḥ saṃgītamadhuraḥ Ratn. 1; °vīṇaiḥ Śi. 11. 10. (c) Adequate or suitable to, fit for; prastāvānugataṃ pṛṣṭaḥ Pt. 5. (d) Imitating; parituṣṭo'smi yatpitaramanugato vatsaḥ M. 5 I am glad that the boy takes after his father.
      --taṃ Moderate time in music.
    Comp.
      --artha a. having a corresponding or easily discoverable sense. [0068-c]
