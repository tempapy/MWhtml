• agādha a. [gādh-pratiṣṭhāyāṃ ghañ na. ba.] Unfathomable, very deep, bottomless; agādhasalilātsamudrāt H. 1. 52; (fig.) profound, sound, very deep, °satva Mk. 1. 3, R. 6. 21; so °jñānaṃ, °buddhi; great; as °bhayaṃ; yasya jñānadayāsiṃdhoragādhasyānaghā guṇāḥ Ak. unfathomable, incomprehensible, inscrutable. --dhaḥ --dhaṃ a deep hole or chasm. --dhaḥ N. of one of the 5 fires at the svāhākāra [cf. Gr. agathos]. --Comp. --jalaḥ [agādhaṃ jalaṃ yatra] a deep pool or pond, deep lake. [0010-b]
