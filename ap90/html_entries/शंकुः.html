• śaṃkuḥ [śaṃk-uṇ Uṇ. 1. 36] 1 A dart, spear, spike, javelin, dagger; oft. at the end of comp.; śokaśaṃkuḥ ‘the dart of grief’, i. e. sharp or poignant grief; U. 3. 35; R. 8. 93. --2 A stake, pillar, post, pale. --3 A nail, pin, peg; R. 12. 95. --4 The sharp head or point of an arrow, barb. --5 The trunk (of a lopped tree), stump, pollard. --6 The pin of a dial. --7 A measure of twelve fingers. --8 A measuring-rod --9 The sine of altitude (in astr.). --10 Ten billions. --11 The fibres of a leaf. --12 An ant-hill. --13 The penis. --14 The skate-fish. --15 A demon. --16 Poison. --17 Sin; crime. --18 An aquatic animal, particularly, a goose. --19 N. of Śiva. --20 The sāla tree. --21 A kind of perfume (nakhī). --22 N. of Kāma, the god of love. --Comp. --karṇa a. spikecared. (--rṇaḥ) an ass. --jīvā (in astr.) the gnomon sine. --taruḥ, --vṛkṣaḥ the Sāla tree. [1029-c]
