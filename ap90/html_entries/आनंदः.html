ānaṃdaḥ [ānaṃd --ghañ]
    • 1 Happiness, joy, delight, pleasure; ānaṃdaṃ brahmaṇo vidvānna bibheti kadācana supreme bliss or felicity; ānaṃda evāsya vijñānamātmānaṃdātmāno haivaṃ sarve devāḥ Śat. Br.
    • 2 God, Supreme spirit (brahma) (said to be n. also in this sense).
    • 3 N. of the forty-eighth year of the cycle of Jupiter.
    • 4 N. of Śiva.
    • 5 N. of Viṣṇu.
    • 6 N. of Balarāma (according to Jaina doctrines).
    • 7 N. of a cousin and follower and favourite disciple of Buddha Śākyamuni, compiler of the Sūtras.
    • 8 A variety of the Daṇḍaka metre.
      N. of two plants.
    • 1 Wine, liquor.
    • 2 A kind of house. Comp. --arṇavaḥ the delight of Brahma.
      N. of Kāśī. --giriḥ, --jñānaḥ, --jñānagiriḥ N. of a celebrated annotator on Śankarāchārya.
      a. caused by joy (as tears).
      a. exhilarating, delighting.
      [ānaṃdo datto yena] the membrum virile.
      [ ānaṃdajanako paṭaḥ] a bridal garment.
      a. delighted supremely, full of bliss. (--rṇaḥ) the Supreme spirit.
      semen.
      a. causing both joy and fear. (--vaḥ) N. of Śiva.
      , --rī f. ‘wave of enjoyment,’ title of a small hymn by Śaṅkarācārya addressed to Pārvatī. [0241-a]
