sevya a. [sev-ṇyat]
      1 To be served or waited upon.
      2 To be used or employed
      3 To be enjoyed.
      4 To be taken care of or guarded.
      --vyaḥ
      1 A master (opp. sevaka); bhayaṃ tāvat sevyādabhiniviśate sevakajanaṃ Mu. 5. 12; Pt. 1. 48.
      2 The Aśvattha tree.
      --vyaṃ A kind of root.
    Comp.
      --sevakau m. dual. master and servant. [1138-b]
