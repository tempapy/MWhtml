• sa ind. A prefix substituted for saha or sam, sama, tulya, or sadṛśa and eka or samāna, and compounded with nouns to form adjectives and adverbs in the sense of (a) with, together with, along with, accompanied by, having, possessed of; saputra, sabhārya, satṛṣṇa, sadhana, saroṣaṃ, sakopaṃ, sahari &c. (b) similar, like; sadharman ‘of a similar nature’; so sajāti, savarṇa; (c) same; sodara, sapakṣa, sapiṃḍa, sanābhi &c. --m. 1 A snake. --2 Air, wind. --3 A bird. --4 short name for the musical note ṣaḍja q. v. --5 N. of Śiva. --6 Of Viṣṇu. --7 (In prosody) A foot consisting of two short syllables followed by a long one. --sā The goddess Lakshmī. --saṃ 1 Knowledge. --2 Meditation. --3 A carriage-road. --4 A fence, an enclosure. [1066-1]
