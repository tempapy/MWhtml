• kheṭa a. [khe aṭati, aṭ ac; khiṭ-ac vā] Having a weapon, armed. --ṭaḥ 1 A village, small town or hamlet. --2 Phlegm. --3 The club of Balarāma. --4 A horse. --ṭaḥ, --ṭaṃ 1 Hunting, chase. --2 A shield. --ṭaṃ 1 Grass. --2 Hide, skin. (N. B. At the end of comp. kheṭa expresses ‘defectiveness’ or ‘deterioration’, & may be rendered by ‘miserable’, ‘low’, ‘vile’, ‘wretched’ &c. nagarakheṭaṃ a miserable town.) [0445-b]
