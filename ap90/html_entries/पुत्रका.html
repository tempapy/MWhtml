putrakā, --putrikā
      1 A daughter.
      2 A doll, puppet.
      3 A daughter appointed to raise male issue for a father who has no sons; aputra'nena vidhinā sutāṃ kurvīta putrikāṃ . yadapatyaṃ bhavedasyāṃ tanmama syātsvadhākaram ...
      4 The cotton or down of the tamarisk.
      5 (At the end of comp.) Anything little or small of its kind; as in asiputrikā, khaṅgaputrikā &c.
    Comp.
      --putraḥ, --sutaḥ {1} a daughter's son who by agreement becomes the son of her father; see Ms. 9. 127. {2} a daughter who, being regarded as a son, returns to her father's house; (putrikaiva putraḥ; athavā putrikaiva sutaḥ putrikāsutaḥ so'pyaurasasama eva Mit. on Y. 2. 128). {3} a grandson --prasūḥ a mother of daughters.
      --bhartṛ m. ‘a daughter's husband,’ a son-in-law. [0708-a]
