• cira a. [ci-rak] Long, lasting a long time, existing from a long time, old; ciravirahaḥ cirakālaḥ ciramitraṃ &c.
    • --raṃ A long time. Note.--The singular of any of the oblique cases of cira may be used adverbially in the sense of ‘long’, ‘for a long time,’ ‘after a long time,’ ‘long since,’ ‘at last,’ ‘finally;’ na ciraṃ parvate vaset Ms. 4. 60; tataḥ prajānāṃ ciramātmanā dhṛtāṃ R. 3. 35, 62; Amaru. 79; kiyaccireṇāryaputraḥ pratipattiṃ dāsyati Ś. 6; R. 5. 64; prītāsmi te saumya cirāya jīva R. 14. 59; Ku. 5. 47; Amaru. 3; cirātsutasparśarasajñatāṃ yayau R. 3. 26; 11. 63, 12. 87; cirasya vācyaṃ na gataḥ prajāpatiḥ Ś. 5. 15; cire kuryāt Śat. Br.
