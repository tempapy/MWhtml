• utpat {c1c} P. 1 (a) To fly or jump up; maṃkṣūdapāti paritaḥ paṭalairalīnāṃ Śi. 5. 37, 3. 77; oft. with acc. or dat. of place; pituḥ padaṃ madhyamamutpataṃtī V. 1. 19; utpatodaṅmukhaḥ khaṃ Me. 14; Bk. 5. 30, 6. 89; Ku. 6. 36; K. 46, 132; V. 4; svargāyotpatitā bhavet V. 4. 2. (b) To go or rush towards; R. 9. 63. (c) To start up, emerge into view; mātaṃgacakraiḥ sahasotpatadbhiḥ R. 13. 11. --2 To rebound (as a ball); pātitopi karāghātairutpatatyeva kaṃdukaḥ Bh. 2. 85. --3 To rise, be produced or originated; niṣpeṣotpatitānalaṃ R. 4. 77; rasāttasmādvarastriya utpetuḥ Rām.; Rs. 1. 26. --4 To be born (as from the womb).
