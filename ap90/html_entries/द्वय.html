dvaya a. (yī f.) Two-fold, double, of two kinds or sorts; anupekṣaṇe dvayī gatiḥ Mu. 3; Bh. 2. 104 v. l.; sometimes used in pl. also; see Śi. 3. 57. --yaṃ
      1 Pair, couple, brace; (usually at the end of comp.); dvitayena dvayameva saṃgataṃ R. 8. 6; 1. 19; 3. 8; 4. 4.
      2 Two fold nature, duplicity.
      3 Untruthfulness.
      4 (In gram.) The masculine and feminine gender.
      --yī A pair, couple.
    Comp.
      --atiga a. one whose mind is freed from the influence of the two bad qualities rajas and tamas, a saint or a virtuous man.
      --ātmaka a. of a two-fold nature.
      --vādin a. doubletongued, insincere.
      --hīna a. of the neuter gender. [0587-a]
