• kadā ind. When, at what time; kadā gamiṣyasi-eṣa gacchāmi; kadā kathayiṣyasi &c. when connected with a following api it means ‘now and then’, ‘at times’, ‘sometimes’, ‘at some time’: na kadāpi never; with a following cana it means ‘at some time’, ‘one day’, ‘at one time or another’, ‘once’; ānaṃdaṃ brahmaṇo vidvānna bibheti kadācana; Ms. 2. 54, 144, 3. 25, 101; with a following cit it means ‘at one time’, ‘once upon a time’, ‘at some time or other’; atha kadācit once upon a time; R. 2. 37, 12. 21; nākṣaiḥ krīḍetkadācittu Ms. 4. 74, 65, 169; kadācit-kadācit ‘now-now’; kadācit kānanaṃ jagāhe kadācit kamalavaneṣu reme K. 58 et seq. [cf. L. quando]. [0375-a]
