• ācāryaḥ [ā-car-ṇyat P. VI. 2. 36] 1 A teacher or preceptor (in general). --2 A spiritual guide or preceptor, holy teacher (one who invests a boy with the sacred thread, instructs him in the Vedas &c.); upanīya tu yaḥ śiṣyaṃ vedamadhyāpayet dvijaḥ . sakalpaṃ sarahasyaṃ ca tamācāryaṃ pracakṣate Ms. 2. 140, see adhyāpaka also. --3 One who propounds a particular doctrine. --4 (When affixed to proper names) Learned, venerable (somewhat like the English Dr.). --5 An adviser or preceptor at a sacrifice &c. --6 An epithet of Droṇa. --ryā A female preceptor, a spiritual preceptress. --Comp. --upāsanaṃ waiting upon or serving the spiritual preceptor. --karaṇaṃ acting as a teacher; P. I. 3. 36. --devaḥ one whose preceptor is his god. --bhogīna a. [ācāryabhogāya hitaṃ kha] to be enjoyed by, fit for the use of, a preceptor; delighting the preceptor. --miśra a. venerable, honourable.
