uśanas m. [vaś-kanasi saṃpra° Uṇ. 4. 238] (Nom. sing. uśanā; Voc. sing. uśanan, uśana, uśanaḥ) N. of Śukra, regent of the planet Venus, son of Bhṛgu and preceptor of the Asuras. In the Vedas he has the epithet Kāvya given to him, probably because he was noted for his wisdom; cf. Bg. 10. 37; kavīnāmuśanā kaviḥ. He is also known as a writer on civil and religious law (Y. 1. 4), and as an authority on civil polity; śāstramuśanasā praṇītaṃ Pt. 5; adhyāpitasyośanasāpi nītiṃ Ku. 3. 6. [0343-b]
