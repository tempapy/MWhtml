śākhā
      1 A branch (as of a tree); āvajrya śākhāḥ R. 16. 19.
      2 An arm.
      3 A party, section, faction.
      4 A part of subdivision of a work.
      5 A school, branch, sect.
      6 A part or division of an animal.
      7 A school or traditional recension of the Veda, the traditional text followed by a school; as in śākalaśākhā, āśvalāyanaśākhā, bāṣkalaśākhā &c.
    Comp.
      --caṃkramaṇaṃ leaping from branch to branch’, irregular study.
      --caṃdranyāyaḥ see under nyāya.
      --nagaraṃ, --puraṃ a suburb.
      --pittaḥ inflammation of the extremities of the body, e. g. hands, shoulders &c.
      --bhṛt m. a tree. --bhedaḥ difference of (Vedic) school.
      --mṛgaḥ {1} a monkey, an ape. {2} a squirrel.
      --raṃḍaḥ ‘a traitor to his Śākhā’, a Brāhmaṇa who has changed his own school of the Vedas.
      --rathyā a branch-road.
      --vilīna a. sitting on branches (as a bird).
      --śiphā a root growing from a branch (as of the fig-tree). [1039-a]
