• dhur f. (Nom. sing. dhūḥ) 1 A yoke (lit.); na gardabhā vājidhuraṃ vahaṃti Mk. 4. 17; atrasnubhiryuktadhuraṃ turaṃgaiḥ R. 14. 47. --2 (a) That part of a yoke which rests on the shoulder. (dhuri kṛ ‘to place at the head or in front of’; Ś. 7. 4). --Comp. --gata (dhūrgata) a. {1} standing on the pole of a chariot. {2} standing at the head, chief, head, foremost. --jaṭiḥ (dhūrjaṭiḥ) an epithet of Śiva. --dhara (dhūrdhara, also dhuraṃdhara) a. {1} bearing the yoke. {2} fit to be harnessed. {3} laden with good qualities or important duties. {4} chief, head, foremost, pre-eminent; kuladhuraṃdharo bhava V. 5. (--raḥ) {1} a beast of burden. {2} a man of business. {3} a chief, head, leader. {4} an epithet of Śiva. --vaha (dhūrvaha) a. {1} carrying or bearing a burden. {2} managing affairs.
