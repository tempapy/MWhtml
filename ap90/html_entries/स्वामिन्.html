svāmin a. (nī f.) [sva-astyarthe mini dīrghaḥ] Possessing proprietory rights. --m.
      1 A proprietor, an owner.
      2 A lord, master; raghusvāminaḥ saccaritraṃ Vikr. 18. 107.
      3 A sovereign, king, monarch.
      4 A husband.
      5 A spiritual preceptor.
      6 A learned Brāhmaṇa, an ascetic or religious man of the highest order; (in this sense usually added to proper names).
      7 An epithet of Kārtikeya.
      8 Of Viṣṇu.
      9 Of Śiva.
      10 Of the sage Vātsyāyana.
      11 Of Garuḍa.
    Comp.
      --upakārakaḥ a horse.
      --kāryaṃ the business of a king or master.
      --jaṃghin m. N. of Paraśurāma.
      --pāla m. du. the owner and the keeper (of cattle); Ms. 8. 5. --bhāvaḥ the state of a lord or owner, ownership.
      --mūla a. originating in or derived from a master or lord.
      --vātsalyaṃ affection for the husband or lord.
      --sadbhāvaḥ {1} existence of a master or owner. {2} goodness of a master or lord.
      --sevā {1} the service of a master. {2} respect for a husband. [1162-b]
