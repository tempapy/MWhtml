ha ind. An emphatic particle used to lay stress on the preceding word and translateable by ‘verily’, ‘indeed’, ‘certainly’, &c. but it is often used expletively without any particular signification, especially in Vedic literature; tasya ha śataṃ jāyā babhūvuḥ; tasya ha parvatanāradau gṛha ūṣatuḥ &c. Ait. Br. It is sometimes used as a vocative particle and rarely of disdain or laughter.
