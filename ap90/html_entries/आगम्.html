• āgam {c1c} P. 1 To come, come near, approach. --2 To arrive at, attain, reach; fall into (a particular state or condition); ānṛṇyamāgam to become free from debt; so viśvāsaṃ, dhyānaṃ &c. --3 To have recourse to. --Caus. (--gamayati) 1 To cause to come or draw near. --2 To lead towards, take, convey; āgamitāpi vidūraṃ Gīt. 12. --3 To announce the arrival of; rājānamāgamayati = rājāgamanamācaṣṭe Sk. --4 To ascertain, inform oneself about; prajñāmevāgamayati yaḥ prājñebhyaḥ sa paṃḍitaḥ Vop. --5 To learn, acquire, study; samyagāgamitā vidyā prabodhavinayāviva R. 10. 71; tadapyāgamitaṃ mayā Mb.; āgamaya daṃḍanītiṃ kulavidyāṃ Dk. 155; Mv. 5; Śi. 9. 79. --6 (Atm.) To wait for, have patience; āgamayasva tāvat = kṣamasva Sk.; āgamayate kālaṃ Vop. [0226-a]
