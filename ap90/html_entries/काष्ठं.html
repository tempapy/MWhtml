kāṣṭhaṃ [kāś-kthan Uṇ. 2. 2]
      1 A piece of wood, especially one used as fuel; Ms. 4. 49, 241; 5. 69.
      2 Wood or timber, a piece or log of wood in general; yathā kāṣṭhaṃ ca kāṣṭhaṃ ca sameyātāṃ mahodadhau H. 4. 69; Ms. 4. 49.
      3 A stick; Y. 2. 218.
      4 An instrument for measuring length.
    Comp.
      --agāraḥ, --raṃ a wooden house or enclosure.
      --aṃbuvāhinī a wooden bucket.
      --ka dalī the wild plantain.
      --kīṭaḥ a small insect found in decayed wood.
      --kuṭaḥ, --kūṭaḥ a wood-pecker; Pt. 1. 332 (a worm generally found in wood).
      --kuddālaḥ a kind of wooden shovel used for baling water out of a boat or for scraping and cleaning its bottom.
      --takṣ m., --takṣakaḥ a carpenter.
      --taṃtuḥ a small worm found in timber.
      --dāruḥ the Indian pine tree; also called devadāru.
      --druḥ the Palāśa tree.
      --puttalikā a wooden statue or image.
      --pradānaṃ piling up wood, forming a funeral pile.
      --bhārikaḥ a wood-carrier.
      --maṭhī a funeral pile.
      --mallaḥ a bier, a wooden frame on which dead bodies are carried. --lekhakaḥ a small worm found in wood (= kāṣṭhakūṭa).
      --lohin m. a cudgel armed with iron.
      --vāṭaḥ, --ṭaṃ a wall made of wood. [0401-c]
