kāṣṭhaṃ [kāś-kthan Uṇ. 2. 2]
    • 1 A piece of wood, especially one used as fuel; Ms. 4. 49, 241; 5. 69.
    • 2 Wood or timber, a piece or log of wood in general; yathā kāṣṭhaṃ ca kāṣṭhaṃ ca sameyātāṃ mahodadhau H. 4. 69; Ms. 4. 49.
    • 3 A stick; Y. 2. 218.
    • 4 An instrument for measuring length.
      Comp., --raṃ a wooden house or enclosure.
      a wooden bucket.
      dalī the wild plantain.
      a small insect found in decayed wood.
      a wood-pecker; Pt. 1. 332 (a worm generally found in wood).
      a kind of wooden shovel used for baling water out of a boat or for scraping and cleaning its bottom.
      m., --takṣakaḥ a carpenter.
      a small worm found in timber.
      the Indian pine tree; also called devadāru.
      the Palāśa tree.
      a wooden statue or image.
      piling up wood, forming a funeral pile.
      a wood-carrier.
      a funeral pile.
      a bier, a wooden frame on which dead bodies are carried. --lekhakaḥ a small worm found in wood (= kāṣṭhakūṭa).
      m. a cudgel armed with iron.
      a wall made of wood. [0401-c]
