anustaraṇaṃ Strewing or spreading round.
      --ṇī [karaṇe lyuṭ]
      1 A cover, (ācchādanaṃ such as leather); anustaraṇyā vapāmutkhidya śiromukhaṃ pracchādayet Āśval.
      2 A cow; (seyaṃ gauḥ stṛtaṃ dīkṣitamanustṛtatvāddhiṃsitatvāccānustaraṇītyucyate Sāy.); especially the cow sacrificed at the funeral ceremony, which enables the departed spirit to safely cross the river of Hell called vaitariṇī (anutīryate vaitariṇī nadī anayā tṝ-karaṇe lyuṭ pṛṣo-suṭ Tv.).
