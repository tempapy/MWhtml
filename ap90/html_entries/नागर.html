• nāgara a. (rī f.) [nagare bhavaḥ aṇ] 1 Town-born, town-bred. --2 Relating to a town, civic. --3 Spoken in a town. --4 Polite, civil. --5 Clever, sharp. --6 Bad, vile, one who has contracted the vices of a town. --7 Nameless. --raḥ 1 A citizen (paura); Me. 25, Śānti. 4. 19. --2 A husband's brother. --3 A lecturer. --4 An orange. --5 Fatigue; hardship, toil. --6 Desire of final beatitude. --7 A term applied to a prince engaged in war under certain circumstances and also to a planet when in opposition to other planets (in astrol.). --8 Denial of knowledge. --raṃ 1 Dry ginger. --2 A kind of coitus. --rī 1 The character in which Sanskrit is generally written; cf. devanāgarī. --2 A clever, intriguing or shrewd woman; haṃtābhīrīḥ smaratu sa kathaṃ saṃvṛto nāgarībhiḥ Ud. D. 16. --3 The plant snuhī. --Comp. --āhvaṃ dry ginger. [0612-c]
