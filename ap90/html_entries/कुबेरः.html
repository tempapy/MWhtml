• kube(ve)raḥ [kutsitaṃ be(ve)raṃ śarīraṃ yasya saḥ] The god of riches and treasure and the regent of the northern quarter; kuberaguptāṃ diśamuṣṇaraśmau gaṃtuṃ pravṛtte samayaṃ vilaṃghya Ku. 3. 25 (vide Malli. thereon.). [Kubera is the son of Viśravas by Iḍaviḍā and thus the halfbrother of Rāvaṇa. Besides being the lord of riches and regent of the north, he is the king of the Yakṣas and Kinnaras, and a friend of Rudra. His abode is Kailāsa. He is represented as being deformed in body-having three legs, only eight teeth, and a yellow mark in place of one eye]. --Comp. --adriḥ. --acalaḥ an epithet of mountain Kailāsa. --diś f. the north.
