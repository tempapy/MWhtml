veśaḥ [viś-ghañ]
      1 Entrance.
      2 Ingress, access.
      3 A house, dwelling.
      4 A house or residence of prostitutes; taruṇajanasahāyaściṃtyatāṃ veśavāsaḥ Mk. 1. 31.
      5 Dress, apparel (also written veṣa in this sense); mṛga yāveṣadhārī; vinītaveṣeṇa Ś. 1; kṛtaveśe keśave Gīt. 11.
      6 Disguise.
      7 Hire, wages.
      --dānaḥ the sun-flower.
      --dhārin a. disguised. (--m.) {1} a hypocrite, false ascetic. {2} an actor.
      --nārī, --vanitā a harlot; Mu. 3. 10. --vāsaḥ the residence of harlots. [1011-c]
