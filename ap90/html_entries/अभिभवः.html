abhibhavaḥ
      1 Defeat, subjugation, subjection, overpowering; itaretarānabhibhavena mṛgāstamupāsate gurumivāṃtasadaḥ Ki. 6. 34 (cf. K. 45 and the Bible “The wolf shall also dwell with the lamb” &c.); 8. 28; sparśānukūlā iva sūryakāṃtāstadanyatejobhibhavādvamaṃti Ś. 2. 7 when assailed, opposed, overpowered by another energy; abhibhavaḥ kuta eva sapatnajaḥ R. 9. 4, 4. 21.
      2 Being overpowered; jarābhibhavavicchāyaṃ K. 346; being attacked or affected, stupefied (by fever &c.); na rogaśāṃtirna cābhibhavaḥ Suśr.
      3 Contempt, disrespect; nirabhibhavasārāḥ parakathāḥ Bh. 2. 64.
      4 Humiliation, mortification (of pride); alabhyaśokābhibhaveyamākṛtiḥ Ku. 5. 43; K. 195.
      5 Predominance, prevalence, rise, spread; adharmābhibhavātkṛṣṇa praduṣyaṃti kulastriyaḥ Bg. 1. 41; Ki. 2. 37. [0134-a]
