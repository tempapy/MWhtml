• kaṭiḥ, --ṭī f. [kaṭ-in]
      1 The hip.
      2 The buttocks (considered by rhetoricians as vulgar and colloquial in these senses; the word kaṭi in kaṭiste harate manaḥ is said to be grāmya.)
      3 An elephant's cheek.
    • --ṭī Long pepper.
    Comp.
    • --kūpaḥ the hollow above the hip, the loins.
    • --taṭaṃ the loins; kaṭītaṭaniveśitaṃ Mk. 1. 27. --traṃ {1} a cloth girt round the loins. {2} a zone, girdle. {3} an ornament of small bells worn round the loins. {4} an armour of the hip or the loins.
    • --deśaḥ the loins. (--ṭi or ṭī) prothaḥ the buttocks.
    • --mālikā a woman's zone or girdle.
    • --rohakaḥ the rider of an elephant (who sits upon the hinder parts of the elephant as distinct from the driver).
    • --śīrṣakaḥ the loins.
    • --śṛṃkhalā a girdle furnished with small bells. --sūtraṃ a zone or waistband. [0369-b]
