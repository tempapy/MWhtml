• mathu(thū)rā [math-u-ū-rac] N. of an ancient town situated on the right bank of the Yamunā, the birth-place of Kṛṣṇa and the scene of his amours and exploits; it is one of the seven sacred cities in India (see avaṃti), and is, to this day, the favourite resort of thousands of devotees. It is said to have been founded by Śatrughna; nirmame nirmamortheṣu mathurāṃ madhurākṛtiḥ R. 15. 28; kaliṃdakanyā mathurāṃ gatā'pi gaṃgormisaṃsaktajaleva bhāti 6. 48.
    Comp.
    • --īśaḥ, --nāthaḥ epithets of Kṛṣṇa. [0835-c]
