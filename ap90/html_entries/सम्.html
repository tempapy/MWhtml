• sam
      I. 1 P. (samati)
      1 To be confused or agitated.
      2 Not to be confused or agitated.
      II. 10 U. (samayati-te) To be agitated. [1092-c]
    • sam ind.
      1 As a prefix to verbs and verbal derivatives it means (a) with, together with, together; as in saṃgam, saṃbhāṣaṇa, saṃdhā, saṃyuj &c. (b) Sometimes it intensifies the meaning of the simple root, and may be translated by ‘very, quite, greatly, thoroughly, very much’; saṃtuṣ, saṃtoṣa, saṃnyas, saṃnyāsa, saṃtāpa &c. (c) It also expresses completeness, perfection, or beauty.
      2 As prefixed to nouns to form comp. it means ‘like, same, similar,’ as in samartha.
      3 Sometimes it means ‘near’, ‘before’; as in samakṣa.
      4 In the Vedas it is sometimes used as a separable preposition (with instr.). [1092-c]
