• kṣīṇa p. p. [kṣi-kta]
      1 Thin, emaciated, waned, become lean, diminished, worn away, expended; bhāryāṃ kṣīṇeṣu vitteṣu (jānīyāt) H. 1. 72; so kṣīṇaḥ. śaśī; kṣīṇe puṇye martyaloke viśaṃti.
      2 Slender, delicate.
      3 Small, little.
      4 Poor, miserable.
      5 Powerless, weak.
      6 Wasted away, decreased, lost, diminished.
      7 Dead, destroyed; akṣīṇabhaktiḥ kṣīṇe'pi naṃde Mu. 2. 21,
      8 Injured, broken, torn.
    Comp.
    • --caṃdraḥ the moon on the wane.
    • --dhana a. reduced to poverty, impoverished.
    • --pāpa a. one who is purified after having suffered the consequences of sin.
    • --puṇya a. one who has enjoyed all his stock of merit, and must work to acquire more in another birth.
    • --madhya a. slenderwaisted.
    • --vāsin a. inhabiting a dilapidated house. (m.) a dove or pigeon.
    • --vikrāṃta a. destitute of courage or prowess.
    • --vṛtti a. deprived of the means of support, out of employ.
    • --śakti, --bala a. weakened in strength, subsided (as a disease); Pt. 1. 235. [0436-c]
