• phalgu a. [phal --u guk ca Uṇ. 1. 18] 1 Pithless, unessential; unsubstantial; sāraṃ tato grāhyamapāsya phalgu Pt. 1. --2 Worthless, useless, unimportant; Śi. 3. 76. --3 Small, minute. --4 Vain, unmeaning. --5 Weak, feeble, flimsy. --6 Untrue. --7 Beautiful, lovely. --lguḥ f. 1 The spring season. --2 The opposite-leaved fig-tree. --3 N. of a river at Gayā. --4 A red powder of wild ginger (Mar. gulāla) thrown by the Hindus over one another at the Holi festival. --Comp. --utsavaḥ the vernal festival, commonly called Holi. --vāṭikā the opposite-leaved fig-tree. [0784-c]
