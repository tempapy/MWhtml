anūcāna a. or --naḥ [anuvac-kānac nipātaḥ P. III. 2. 109; vedasya anuvacanaṃ kṛtavān anūcānaḥ Sk.]
      1 One devoted to study, learned; especially one wellversed in the Vedas with their Aṅgas so as to be able to repeat, read and teach them; yo'nūcānaḥ sa no mahān Ms. 2. 154; 5. 82; Y. 3. 24; idamūcuranūcānāḥ Ku. 6. 15.
      2 Modest, unassuming; humble, well-behaved. [0080-b]
