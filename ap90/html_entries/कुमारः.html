• kumāraḥ [cf. Uṇ. 3. 138] 1 A son, boy; a youth; R. 3. 48. --2 A boy below five. --3 A prince, an heir-apparent (especially in dramas); viproṣitakumāraṃ tadrājyamastamiteśvaraṃ R. 12. 11; kumārasyāyuṣo bāṇaḥ V. 5; upaveṣṭumarhati kumāraḥ Mu. 4 (said by Rākṣasa to Malayaketu). --4 N. of Kārtikeya, the god of war; kumārakalpaṃ suṣuve kumāraṃ R. 5. 36; kumāropi kumāravikramaḥ 3. 55. --5 N. of Agni. --6 A parrot. --Comp. --pālanaḥ {1} one who takes care of children. {2} N. of king Śālivāhana. --bhṛtyā {1} care of young children. {2} care of a woman in pregnancy or confinement, midwifery; R. 3. 12. --vāhin, --vāhanaḥ a peacock. --vrataṃ a vow of eternal celibacy. --sūḥ f. {1} an epithet of Pārvatī. {2} or of the Ganges. (--m.) an epithet of Agni. [0410-b]
