• jval {c1c} P. (jvalati, jvalita) 1 To burn brightly, blaze, glow, shine; jvalati caliteṃdhanogniḥ Ś. 6. 30; Ku. 5. 30. --2 To be burnt up, be consumed or afflicted (as by fire) amṛtamadhuramṛdutaravacanena jvalati na sā malayajapavanena Gīt. 7. --3 To be ardent; jajvāla lokasthitaye sa rājā Bk. 1. 4. --4 To burn (as a wound). --Caus. (jvalayati-te, jvālayati-te, but prajvalayati) 1 To set on fire, light, kindle. --2 To irradiate, illuminate, brighten. [0520-b]
