• parijalpitaṃ A covert indication (as by a servant) of one's own skill, superiority &c. by pointing out the cruelty, deceitfulness and such other faults of his master; Ujjvalamaṇi thus defines it: --prabhornirdayatāśāṭhyacāpalāghupapādanāt . svavicaṇatāvyaktirbhaṃgyā syātparijalpitam .. (Wilson renders the word by ‘the covert reproaches of a mistress neglected or ill-used by her lover’). [0674-a]
