• nirūḍha a. 1 Conventional, become current in popular usage, accepted (as the meaning of a word, as opposed to its yaugika or etymological sense); dyaurna kācidathavāsti nirūḍhā saiva sā calati yatra hi cittaṃ N. 5. 57. --2 Unmarried. --3 Drawn out, purged. --ḍhaḥ 1 Inherence (as of ‘redness’ in the word ‘red’). --2 (In Rhet.) The accepted and popular meaning of a word. --Comp. --lakṣaṇā a lakshaṇā or secondary use of a word which is based not on the vivakshā or particular intention of the speaker, but on its accepted and popular sense. [0631-c]
