• marman n. [mṛ-manin]
      1 (a) A vital part of the body, the vitals, weak or tender point (of the body); tathavai tīvro hṛdi śokaśaṃkurmarmāṇi kṛṃtannapi kiṃ na soḍhaḥ U. 3. 35; Y. 1. 153; 16; svahṛdayamarmaṇi varma karoti Gīt. 4. (b) Any vital member or organ.
      2 Any weak or vulnerable point, a defect, failing.
      3 The core, quick.
      4 Any joint (of a limb).
      5 The secret or hidden meaning, the pith or essence (of anything); kāvyamarbhaprakāśikā ṭīkā; natvā gaṃgādharaṃ marmaprakāśaṃ tanute guruṃ --tāgeśabhaṭṭa.
      6 A secret, a mystery.
      7 Truth.
    Comp.

    • --āvidh, --upaghātin a. piercing the vitals (of the heart); Mv. 3. 10. --kīlaḥ a husband.
    • --ga a. piercing to the quick, very acute, poignant.
    • --ghna a. piercing the vitals, excessively painful.
    • --caraṃ the heart.

    • --traṃ a coat of mail.
