karabhaḥ [kṛ-abhac Uṇ. 3. 122; kare bhāti, bhā-ka Tv.]
      1 The back of the hand from the wrist to the root of the fingers; metacarpus; as in karabhorūḥ R. 6. 83; see karabhorū below.
      2 The trunk of an elephant.
      3 A young elephant.
      4 A young camel; Śi. 5. 3.
      5 A camel in general.
      6 A kind of perfume.
      --bhī A shecamel.
    Comp.
      --ūrūḥ f. a lady whose thighs resemble the back of the fore-arm; aṃke nidhāya karabhoru yathāsukhaṃ te Ś. 3. 21; Śi. 10. 69; Amaru. 69; or (according to another explanation), whose thighs resemble the trunk of an elephant; cf. Ku. 1. 36. [0380-b]
