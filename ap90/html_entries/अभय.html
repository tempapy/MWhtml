abhaya a. [na. ba.] Free from fear or danger, secure, safe; vairāgyamevābhayam Bh. 3. 35. --yaḥ [na bhayaṃ yasmāt]
    • 1 An epithet of the Supreme Being, or knowledge concerning that being.
    • 2 N. of Śiva.
    • 3 One devoid of all worldly possessions.
    • 4 One who fearlessly executes scriptural commandments.
    • 5 N. of a Yoga (conjuncture or time) favourable to a march or expedition.
    • 1 N. of a plant (harītakī Mar. hiraḍā).
    • 2 A form of the goddess Durgā.
    • 1 Absence or removal of fear.
    • 2 Security, safety, protection from fear or danger; mayā tasyābhayaṃ dattaṃ Pt. 1; abhayasya hi yo dātā Ms. 8. 303; Ś. 2. 16.
    • 3 N. of a sacrificial hymn.
    • 4 The root of a fragrant grass (vīraṇamūlaṃ, uśīraṃ).
      Comp. a. {1} not terrific, mild. {2} giving safety.
      m. one dwelling on the mountain of safety, N. of a class of Kātyāyana's pupils.
      {1} proclamation of assurance or safety. {2} a military or war-drum.
      a. giving a guarantee or promise of safety; bhayeṣvabhayadaḥ Rām.; °pradaḥ Ms. 4. 232. (--daḥ) an Arhat of the Jainas; N. of Viṣṇu.
      giving a promise, assurance, or guarantee of safety or protection (from danger); sarvapradāneṣvabhayapradānaṃ (pradhānaṃ) Pt. 1. 290; Ms. 4. 247. --patraṃ a written document or paper granting assurance of safety; cf. the modern ‘safe-conduct’.
      a variety of mudrā in Tantra literature.
      asking for protection; °aṃjaliḥ R. 11. 78. --vacanaṃ, --vāc f. an assurance or promise of safety.
      a. Ved. giving safety. [0126-a]
