avyaya a. [nāsti vyayo yasya]
    • 1 (a) Not liable to change, imperishable, undecaying, immutable; vedāvināśinaṃ nityaṃ ya enamajamavyayaṃ Bg. 2. 21; vināśamavyayasyāsya na kaścitkartumarhati 17, 4. 1, 6, 13; 7. 24-5; 15. 5, 17; Ms. 1. 18, 19, 57; 2. 81; R. 8. 24. (b) Eternal, everlasting; aśvatthaṃ prāhuravyayaṃ Bg. 15. 1; akīrtiṃ kathayiṣyaṃti te'vyayāṃ 2. 34.
    • 2 Unexpended, unwasted.
    • 3 Economical.
    • 4 Giving imperishable fruit.
    • 1 N. of Viṣṇu.
    • 2 N of Śiva.
    • 1 (In the Vedānta) A member or corporeal part of an organized body.
    • 2 Brahma.
    • 3 (In gram.) An indeclinable particle &c. sadṛśaṃ triṣu liṃgeṣu sarvāsu ca vibhaktiṣu . vacaneṣu ca sarveṣu yanna vyeti tadavyayam ... Comp. --ātman of an imperishable or eternal nature. (--tmā) the soul or spirit.
      the class of indeclinable words. [0198-c]
