kāliyaḥ N. of a tremendously large serpent who dwelt at the bottom of the Yamunā (which was a ground forbidden to Garuḍa, the enemy of serpents, owing to the curse of the sage Saubhari). He was crushed to death by Kṛṣṇa when he was but a boy; R. 6. 49; Śi. 17. 69. --yāḥ (pl.) The family of black serpents; Śi. 19. 28
      Comp. epithets of Kṛṣṇa. [0400-c]
