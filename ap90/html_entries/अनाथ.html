anātha a. [na. ba.] Helpless, poor, forlorn, parentless, orphan (as a child); widowed (as a wife); having no master or natural protector, without a protector in general; nāthavaṃtastvayā lokāstvamanāthā vipatsyase U. 1. 43; R. 12. 12. --thaṃ Ved. Helplessness.
      Comp. ‘giver of food to the poor,’ N. of a merchant in whose garden Buddha Gautama used to instruct his pupils.
      a poor-house. [0061-b]
