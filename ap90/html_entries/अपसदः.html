• apasadaḥ [apakṛṣṭo nīca iva sīdati, sad-ac]
      1 An outcast, a low man; usually at the end of comp. in the sense of ‘vile,’ ‘wretched’ ‘accursed’; kāpālika° Māl. 5; re re kṣatriyāpasadāḥ Ve. 3; nara°, brāhmaṇa° &c.
      2 N. for the children of six degrading connections, i. e. of men of the first three castes with women of the castes inferior to their own; viprasya triṣu varṇeṣu nṛpatervarṇayordvayoḥ . vaiśyasya varṇe caikasmin ṣaḍete'pasadāḥ smṛtāḥ .. Ms. 10. 10. [0113-b]
