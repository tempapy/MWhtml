alakaḥ [al-kvun, alati bhūṣayati mukhaṃ]
    • 1 A curl, lock of hair, hair in general; lalāṭikācaṃdanadhūsarālakā Ku. 5. 55; aspṛṣṭālakaveṣṭanau R. 1. 42; 4. 54; alakabhaṃgatāṃ gataḥ K. 4; alake bālakuṃdānuviddhaṃ Me. 65 (the word is n. also, as appears from a quotation of Malli.: svabhāvavakrāṇyalakāni tāsāṃ).
    • 2 Curls on the fore-head.
    • 3 Saffron besmeared on the body.
    • 4 A mad dog (for alarka).
    • 1 A girl from eight to ten years of age.
    • 2 N. of the capital of Kubera (situated on a peak of the Himālaya above the peak of Meru, inhabited also by Śiva), and of the lord of the Yakṣas; alakāmativāhyaiva Ku. 6. 37; vibhāti yasyāṃ lalitālakāyāṃ manoharā vaiśravaṇasya lakṣmīḥ Bv. 2. 10; gaṃtavyā te vasatiralakā nāma yakṣeśvarāṇāṃ Me. 7.
      Comp., --patiḥ, --īśvaraḥ ‘lord of Alakā’, N. of Kubera; atyajīvadamarālakeśvarau R. 19. 15. --aṃtaḥ the end of a curl or ringlet; Śi. 4. 9; Me. 8. --naṃdā {1} N. of the Ganges, or a river falling into it. {2} a girl from eight to ten years of age.
      N. of the capital of Kubera.
      N. of a tree ( pītasāla).
      f. rows of curls; Śi. 6. 3. [0171-c]
