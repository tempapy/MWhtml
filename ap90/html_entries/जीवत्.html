• jīvat a. (ntī f.) Living, alive.
    Comp.
    • --tokā a woman whose children are living.
    • --patiḥ f., --patnī f. a woman whose husband is living.
    • --pitṛkaḥ one whose father is alive.
    • --mukta a. ‘liberated while living’, a man who, being purified by a true knowledge of the Supreme spirit, is freed from the future birth and all ceremonial rites while yet living.
    • --muktiḥ f. final liberation in the present state of life.
    • --mṛta a. ‘dead while alive,’ one who, though alive, is as good as dead and useless to the world (said of a mad man or one whose character is lost). [0515-c]
