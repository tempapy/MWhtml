bilaṃ
      1 A hole, cavity, burrow; khanannākhubilaṃ siṃhaḥ… prāpnoti nakhabhaṃgaṃ hi Pt. 3. 17; R. 12. 5.
      2 A gap, pit, chasm.
      3 An aperture, opening, outlet.
      4 A cave, hollow.
      5 The hollow of a dish.
      6 The vagina.
      --laḥ
      1 N. of uccaiḥśravas, the horse of Indra.
      2 A sort of cane.
    Comp.
      --okas m. any animal that lives in holes.
      --kārin m. a mouse.
      --yoni a. of the breed of Bila; yatrāśvā bilayonayaḥ Ku. 6. 39. --vāsaḥ a pole-cat.
      --vāsin (also bilevāsin) m. a snake.
      --śāyin m. any animal living in burrows. [0797-b]
