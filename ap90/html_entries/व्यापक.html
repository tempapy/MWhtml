• vyāpaka a. (pikā f.)
      1 Pervading, comprehensive, diffusive, widely spread, extending over the whole of anything; tiryagūrdhvamadhastācca vyāpako mahimā hareḥ Ku. 6. 71.
      2 (In law) Comprehending all the points of an argument.
      3 Invariably concomitant.
      4 That which is more extensive than the vyāpya; e. g. in the instance manuṣyo martyaḥ; martyaṃ is vyāpaka as it includes manuṣya, and is more extensive than it.
    • --kaḥ An attribute which is invariably concomitant or inherent.
    • --kaṃ An invariably concomitant or inherent property. [1022-c]
