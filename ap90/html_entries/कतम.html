katama pron. a. [kim-ḍatam] (°mat n.) Who or which of many; api jñāyate katamena digbhāgena gataḥ sa jālma iti V. 1; atha katamaṃ punarṛtumadhikṛtya gāsyāmi Ś. 1; katame te guṇāstatraṃ yānudāharaṃtyāryamiśrāḥ Māl. 1; G. L. 22; (sometimes it is used merely as a strengthened substitute for kim). When followed by ca and preceded by yatama it means ‘any whosoever’, ‘whatsoever’. In negative sentences katama with cana or api means ‘not even one’, ‘none at all’ It also means ‘best or excessively good-looking’. [0373-a]
