uragaḥ (gī f.) [urasā gacchati, uras-gam-ḍa; salopaśca P. III. 2. 48 Vārt.]
    • 1 A serpent, snake; aṃgulīvoragakṣatā R. 1. 28, 12. 5, 91.
    • 2 A Nāga or semi-divine serpent usually represented in mythology with a human face; devagaṃdharvamānuṣoragarākṣasān Nala. 1. 28; Ms. 3. 196.
    • 3 Lead.
      N. of a city; R. 6. 59. --gī A female snake.
      Comp., --aśanaḥ, --śatruḥ {1} N. of Garuḍa (enemy of snakes). {2} a pea-cock.
      a kind of spade (serpentshaped hoe).
      N. of Vāsuki or Śeṣa.
      a. having a serpent for a wedding-ring.
      N. of Śiva (decked with serpents).
      a kind of sandal-wood.
      the abode of the Nāgas, i. e. Pātāla. [0340-b]
