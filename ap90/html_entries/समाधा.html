• samādhā {c3c} U. 1 To place or put together, join, unite. --2 To place, put, put or place upon, apply to; padaṃ mūrdhni samādhatte kesarī mattadaṃtinaḥ Pt. 1. 327. --3 To install, place on the throne; R. 17. 8. --4 To compose, collect (as the mind); manaḥ samādhāya nivṛttaśokaḥ Rām.; na śaśāka samādhātuṃ mano madanavepitaṃ Bhāg. --5 To concentrate, fix or apply intently upon (as the eye, mind &c.); Bg. 12. 9; Bh. 3. 48. --6 To satisfy, clear or solve (a doubt), answer an objection; iti samādhatte (in commentaries). --7 To repair, redress, set right, remove; na te śakyāḥ samādhātuṃ H. 3. 37; utpannāmāpadaṃ yastu samādhatte sa buddhimān 4. 7. --8 To think over; Bk. 12. 6. --9 To entrust, commit to, deliver over. --10 To produce, effect, accomplish. --11 To place a burden, load. --12 To assume, take upon oneself. --13 To conceive (in the womb). --14 To establish.
