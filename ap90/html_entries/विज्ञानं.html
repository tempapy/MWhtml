• vijñānaṃ 1 Knowledge, wisdom, intelligence, understanding; Pt. 1. 24; 5. 3; vijñānamayaḥ kośaḥ ‘the sheath of intelligence’ (the first of the five sheaths of the soul). --2 Discrimination, discernment. --3 Skill, proficiency; prayogavijñānaṃ Ś. 1. 2. --4 Worldly or profane knowledge, knowledge derived from worldly experience (opp. jñāna which is ‘knowledge of Brahma or Supreme Spirit’); jñānaṃ tehaṃ savijñānamidaṃ vakṣyāmyaśeṣataḥ Bg. 7. 2, 3. 41; (the whole of the 7th Adhyāya of Bg. explains jñāna and vijñāna). --5 Business, employment. --6 Music. --7 Knowledge of the fourteen lores. --Comp. --īśvaraḥ N. of the author of the Mitākṣarā, a commentary on Yājñavalkya's Smṛti. --pādaḥ N. of Vyāsa. --mātṛkaḥ an epithet of Buddha. --vādaḥ the theory of knowledge, the doctrine taught by Buddha.
