• jīmūtaḥ [jayā ti nabhaḥ, jīyate anilena jīvanasyodakasya mūtaṃ baṃdho yatra, jīvanaṃ jalaṃ mūtaṃ baddhaṃ anena, jāvanaṃ muṃcatīti vā pṛṣo° Tv. cf. Uṇ. 3. 91]
      1 A cloud; jīmūtena svakuśalamayīṃ hārayiṣyan pravṛttiṃ Me. 4.
      2 A mountain.
      3 A nourisher, sustainer.
      4 An epithet of Indra.
    Comp.
    • --kūṭaḥ a mountain.
    • --ketuḥ an epithet of Śiva.
    • --vāhanaḥ {1} N. of Indra. {2} N. of a king of the Vidyādharas, hero of the play called Nāgānanda; (mentioned also in kathāsaritsāgara). [He was the son of Jīmūtaketu and renowned for his benevolent and charitable disposition. When his father's kingdom was invaded by his kinsmen, he scorned the idea of fighting with them and induced his father to leave it to those who sought for it and to repair with him to the Malaya mountain to lead a holy life. It is related that there he one day took the place of a young serpent who was, by virtue of an agreement, to be offered to Garuḍa as his daily meal, and induced, by his generous and touching behaviour, the enemy of serpents to give up his practice of devouring them. The story is very pathetically told in the play].
    • --vāhin smoke. [0514-c]
