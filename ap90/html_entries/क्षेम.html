• kṣema a. [kṣi-man Uṇ. 1. 138] 1 Conferring happiness, ease or comfort, good, beneficial, well; dhārtarāṣṭrā raṇe hanyustanme kṣemataraṃ bhavet Bg. 1. 46. --2 Prosperous, at ease, comfortable, --3 Secure, happy. --maḥ, --maṃ 1 Peace, happiness, ease, welfare, wellbeing; vitanvati kṣemamadevamātṛkāścirāya tāsmin kuravaścakāsate Ki. 1. 17; vaiśyaṃ kṣemaṃ samāgamya (pṛcchet) Ms. 2. 127; adhunā sarvajalacarāṇāṃ kṣemaṃ bhaviṣyati Pt. 1. --2 Safety, security; kṣemeṇa vraja bāṃdhavān Mk. 7. 7 safely; Pt. 1. 146. --3 Preserving, protection; R. 15. 6. --4 Keeping what is acquired; cf. yogakṣema. --5 Final beatitude, eternal happiness. --6 Basis, foundation. --7 Residence, resting-place. --8 A star, asterism (nakṣatra). --maḥ A kind of perfume. --mā An epithet of Durgā. --Comp. --kara, --kāra (also kṣemaṃkara) a. propitious, causing peace or security.
