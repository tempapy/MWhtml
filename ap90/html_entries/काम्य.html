kāmya a. [kam-yat]
      1 To be desired, desirable: sudhā viṣṭhā ca kāmyāśanaṃ Śānti. 2. 8.
      2 Optional, performed for some particular object (opp. nitya); aṃte kāmyasya karmaṇaḥ R. 10. 50; Ms. 2. 2; 12. 89; Bg. 18. 2.
      3 Beautiful, lovely, charming, handsome; nāsau na kāmyaḥ R. 6. 30; U. 5. 12. --myā A wish, desire, intention, request; brāhmaṇakāmyā Mk. 3; R. 1. 35; Bg. 10. 1.
    Comp.
      --abhiprāyaḥ a self-interested motive or purpose.
      --karman n. a rite performed for some particular object and with a view to future fruition.
      --gir a. sweet-voiced, having a pleasing voice; Śi. 6. 8. (--f.) an agreeable speech.
      --dānaṃ {1} an acceptable gift. {2} a free-will offering voluntary gift.
      --maraṇaṃ voluntary death, suicide.
      --vṛttaṃ voluntary vow. [0395-c]
