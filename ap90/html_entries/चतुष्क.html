• catuṣka a. [caturavayavaṃ catvāro'vayavā yasya vā kan] 1 Consisting of four. --2 Increased by four; dvikaṃ trikaṃ catuṣkaṃ ca paṃcakaṃ ca śataṃ samaṃ Ms. 8. 142 (i. e. 102, 103, 104, or 105, or interest at the rate of 2 to 5 per cent). --ṣkaṃ 1 The number ‘four.’ --2 A collection of four. --3 A crossway. --4 A quadrangular courtyard. --5 A hall resting on (four) pillars, a hall or saloon in general; Ku. 5. 68, 7. 9. --6 A necklace of four strings. --ṣkī 1 A large foursided pond. --2 A mosquito curtain.
