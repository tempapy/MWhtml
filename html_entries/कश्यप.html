    • (fr. kaśyakaśya + 2. papa) having black teeth Comm. on KātyŚr. x, 2, 35 [265,2]
  • kaśyapakaśyapa m.
    • a tortoise (kacchapakacchapa), VS. xxiv, 37; AitBr.; ŚBr. [265,2]
    • a sort of fish, W. [265,2]
    • a kind of deer (cf. kāśyapakāśyapa), L. [265,2]
    • a class of divine beings associated with Prajāpati, AV.; TS.; VS. [265,2]
  • kaśyapakaśyapa m. pl.
    • a class of semidivine genii connected with or regulating the course of the sun, AV. xiii, 1, 23; TĀr. i, 8; PārGṛ. ii, 9, 13 [265,2]
  • kaśyapakaśyapa m.
    • N. of a mythical Ṛṣi, AitBr.; ŚBr. [265,2]
    • of an ancient sage, VS.; AV. &c., (a descendant of Marīci and author of several hymns of the Ṛg-veda, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; he was husband of Aditi and twelve other daughters of Dakṣa, MBh. i, 2598; Mn. ix, 129; by Aditi he was father of the Ādityas [cf. kāśyapeyakāśyapeya] TS.; ŚBr.; and of Vivasvat, R.; and of Viṣṇu in his vāmanavāmana avatāraavatāra, R.; BhP.; VP.; by his other twelve wives he was father of demons, nāgas, reptiles, birds, and all kinds of living things; from the prominent part ascribed to him in creation he is sometimes called Prajā-pati; he is one of the seven great Ṛṣis and priest of Paraśu-rāma and Rāma-candra; he is supposed by some to be a personification of races inhabiting the Caucasus, the Caspian, Kaśmīr, &c.) [265,2]
    • a patronymic from Kaśyapa, ŚBr. [265,2]
    • the author of a Dharmaśāstra called Kaśyahollara-saṃhitā; the constellation Cancer (cf. Pers. kashaf), VP. [265,2]
  • kaśyapakaśyapa m. pl.
    • the descendants of Kaśyapa, AitBr.; ĀśvŚr. [265,2]
