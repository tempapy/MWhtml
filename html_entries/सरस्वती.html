  • sarasvatīsarasvatī
  • f. (of sarasvatsarasvat q.v. under sarassaras) a region abounding in pools and lakes, MBh. i, 7745 [1182,2]
  • N. of a river (celebrated in RV. and held to be a goddess whose identity is much disputed; most authorities hold that the name Sárasvatīsarasvatī is identical with the Avestan Haraquaiti river in Afghanistan, but that it usually means the Indus in the RV., and only occasionally the small sacred rivers in Madhya-deśamadhyadeśamadhyadeśa [see below]; the river-goddess has seven sisters and is herself sevenfold, she is called the mother of streams, the best of mothers, of rivers, and of goddesses; the Ṛṣiṛṣis always recognize the connection of the goddess with the river, and invoke her to descend from the sky, to bestow vitality, renown, and riches; elsewhere she is described as moving along a golden path and as destroying Vṛtravṛtra &c.; as a goddess she is often connected with other deities e.g. with Pūṣanpūṣan, Indraindra, the Marutmaruts and the Aśvinaśvins ; in the Āprīāprī hymns she forms a triad with the sacrificial goddesses Iḍāiḍā and Bhāratībhāratī; accord. to a myth told in the VS. xix, 12, Sárasvatīsarasvatī through speech [vācāvācā] communicated vigour to Indraindra; in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas she is identified with vācvāc, ‘Speech’, and in later times becomes goddess of eloquence See below), RV. &c. &c. [1182,2]
  • N. of a well-known small river (held very sacred by the Hindūs; identified with the modern Sursooty, and formerly marking with the Dṛṣadvatīdṛṣadvatī one of the boundaries of the region Ārya-deśaāryadeṣaāryadeṣa and of the sacred district called Brahmâvartabrahmāvarta [see, Mn. ii, 17] in RV. vii, 95, 2, this river is represented as flowing into the sea, although later legends make it disappear underground and join the Ganges and Jumnā at Allahābād; See tri-veṇītriveṇītriveṇī, prayāgaprayāga), ib. [1182,3]
  • N. of various rivers (esp. of rivers which in sacredness are equal to Sárasvatīsarasvatī and which are three accord. to AV. vi, 101, and seven accord. to MBh. ix, 2188) [1182,3]
  • any river, Naigh. i, 13 [1182,3]
  • N. of the goddess of eloquence and learning (cf. above; she is opposed to Śrīśrī or Lakṣmīlakṣmī [cf. Vikr. v, 24], and sometimes considered as the daughter and also wife of Brahmābrahmā, the proper wife of that god being rather Sāvitrisāvitri or Gāyatrīgāyatrī; she is also identified with Durgādurgā, or even with the wife of Viṣṇuviṣṇu and of Manumanu, and held to be the daughter of Dakṣadakṣa), Mn.; MBh. &c. [1182,3]
  • speech or the power of speech, eloquence, learning wisdom, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1182,3]
  • a celestial or oracular voice, Kālid.; Kathās.; Rājat. [1182,3]
  • a cow, VS. viii, 43 [1182,3]
  • an excellent woman (= strī-ratnastrīratnastrīratna), L. [1182,3]
  • N. of various plants (Cardiospermum Halicacabum, Egle Marmelos, Ruta Graveolens &c.), L. [1182,3]
  • N. of a two-year-old girl representing Durgādurgā at her festival, L. [1182,3]
  • of a poetess, Cat. [1182,3]
  • of various other women (esp. of the wives of Dadhīcadadhīca, Śaṃkarâcāryaśaṃkarācārya, Maṇḍanamiśramaṇḍanamiśra &c.), ib. [1182,3]
  • of one of the ten mendicant orders traced back to Śaṃkarâcāryaśaṃkarācārya (whose members add the word sarasvatīsarasvatī to their names). [1182,3]
