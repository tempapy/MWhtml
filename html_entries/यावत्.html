• yāvatyāvat
  • mf(atīatī)n. (fr. 3. yaya; correlative of tāvattāvat q.v.) as great, as large, as much, as many, as often, as frequent, as far, as long, as old &c. (or how great &c. = quantus, quot or qualis), RV. &c. &c. (yāvantaḥyāvantaḥ kiyantaḥkiyantaḥ, ‘as many as’ TBr.; yāvadyāvad vāvā yāvadyāvad vāvā, ‘as much as possible’ ŚBr.; yāvatyāvat tāvattāvat, ‘so much as’, in alg. applied to the first unknown quantity [= x] or so much of the unknown as its co-efficient number; in this sense also expressed by the first syllable yāyā cf. IW. 182; itiiti yāvatyāvat in Comms. ‘just so much’, ‘only so’, ‘that is to say’, ‘such is the explanation’) [852,1]
