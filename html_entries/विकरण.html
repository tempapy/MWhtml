• 1. vivivī—karaṇakaraṇa mfn.
    • (for 2. See ib.) deprived of organs of sense (-tvavikaraṇatvavikaraṇatva n.), Bādar. Sch. [950,1]
  • 2. vi-karaṇavikaraṇavikaraṇa m.
    • (for 1. See p. 950, col. 1) ‘producing a change.’ (in gram.) a term for the affix or conjugational characteristic which is placed between the root and terminations, the inserted conjugational affix (according to Pāṇini these affixes are śapśap, śapośapaśap luklukluc, śluślu, śyanśyan, śnuśnu, śaśa, śnamśnam, uuū, śnāśnā, yakyacyaj, and cli [with its substitutes], tāsi, syasya, sipsibh, the first nine of which are added in the Pres., Impf., Imperative, and Potential, and before a Kṛt which contains a mute palatal ś, in the case of Active verbs; yakyacyaj is added in the case of the Karman or Bhāva i.e. Passives or Neuters; cli is added in the Aorist, tāsi in the 1st Future, syasya in the 2nd Future and Conditional, and sipsibh before Leṭ.; lug-vikaraṇalukvikaraṇa, ‘having luklukluc for its Vikaraṇa’ [said of roots of cl. 2.]; ākhyāta-pada-vikaraṇāḥākhyātapada, ‘words which modify the finite verb’ i.e. make it accented) [954,2]
  • vi-karaṇavikaraṇavikaraṇa n.
    • change, modification, Nir. [954,2]
    • a disturbing influence, Sarvad. [954,2]
