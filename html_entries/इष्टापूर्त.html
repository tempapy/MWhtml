• iṣṭā̑pūrta n. ‘filled up or stored up sacrificial rites’, or the merit of sacred rites &c. stored up in heaven, RV.; AV.; VS.; ŚBr.; R. &c. (See Muir v, 293; according to Banerjea's translation of Brahma-sūtras, p.19, iṣṭa means personal piety, pūrta works for the benefit of others). [169,3]
