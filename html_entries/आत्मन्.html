• ātmanātman m.
    • (variously derived fr. ananat, to breathe; atatad, to move; vāvāvauū, to blow; cf. tmantman) the breath, RV. [135,1]
    • the soul, principle of life and sensation, RV.; AV. &c. [135,1]
    • the individual soul, self, abstract individual [e.g. ātmanātman (Ved. loc.) dhatte, or karoti, ‘he places in himself’, makes his own, TS. v; ŚBr.; ātmanāātmanā akarot, ‘he did it himself’ Kād.; ātmanāātmanā vi-√yujviyujviyuj, ‘to lose one's life’ Mn. vii, 46; ātmanātman in the sg. is used as reflexive pronoun for all three persons and all three genders e.g. ātmānaṃ sāsāsa hantihanti, ‘she strikes herself’; putramputra ātmanaḥ spṛṣṭvā nipetatuḥ, ‘they two having touched their son fell down’ R. ii, 64, 28; see also below s.v. ātmanāātmanā] [135,1]
    • essence, nature, character, peculiarity (often ifc. e.g. karmā̑tmankarmātmankarmaātman, &c.), RV. x, 97, 11, &c. [135,1]
    • the person or whole body considered as one and opposed to the separate members of the body, VS.; ŚBr. [135,1]

    • (ifc.) ‘the understanding, intellect, mind’ See naṣṭā̑tmannaṣṭātmannaṣṭaātman, mandā̑° [135,1]

    • effort, L. [135,1]
