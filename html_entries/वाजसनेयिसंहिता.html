• vājasaneyivājasaneyi—saṃhitāsaṃhitā
  • f. ‘the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā or continuous text of the Vājasaneyinvājasaneyins’ (i.e. of the hymns of the White Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda ascribed to the Ṛṣiṛṣi Yājñavalkyayājñavalkya and called śuklaśukla, ‘white’, to distinguish it from the Black or Dark Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda, which was the name given to the Taittirīya-saṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitā [q.v.] of the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda, because in this last, the separation between the Mantramantra and Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa portion is obscured, and the two are blended together; whereas the White Saṃhitāsaṃhitā is clearly distinguished from the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa; it is divided into 40 Adhyāyaadhyāyas with 303 Anuvākaanuvākas, comprising 1975 sections or Kaṇḍikākaṇḍikās: the legend relates that the original Yajusyajus was taught by the Ṛṣiṛṣi Vaiśampāyanavaiśampāyana to his pupil Yājñavalkyayājñavalkya, but the latter having incurred his teacher's anger was made to disgorge all the texts he had learnt, which were then picked up by Vaiśampāyanavaiśampāyana's other disciples in the form of partridges [see taittirīya-saṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitā] Yājñavalkyayājñavalkya then hymned the Sun, who gratified by his homage, appeared in the form of a vājinvājin or horse, and consented to give him fresh Yajusyajus texts, which were not known to his former master; cf. vājinvājin) [938,2]
