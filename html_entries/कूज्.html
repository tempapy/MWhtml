• kūjkūj [Roots]
    • cl. 1. P. kūjati (perf. cukūja, Kum. iii, 32 &c.), to make any inarticulate or monotonous sound, utter a cry (as a bird), coo (as a pigeon), caw (as a crow), warble, moan, groan, utter any indistinct sound, AV. vii, 95, 2; MBh.; R. &c.
    • ‘to fill with monotonous sounds’, &c. See kūjitakūjita;
    • to blow or breathe (the flute), BhP. x, 21, 2. [299,2]
