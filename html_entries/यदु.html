• yaduyadu
  • m. N. of an ancient hero (in the Vedaveda often mentioned together with Turvaśaturvaśa [or Turvasuturvasu] q.v., and described as preserved by Indraindra during an inundation; in epic poetry he is a son of Yayātiyayāti and brother of Purupuru and Turvasuturvasu, Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa being descended from Yaduyadu, and Bharatabharata and Kurukuru from Purupuru; Yaduyadu is also called a son of Vasuvasu, king of Cedicedi, or a son of Hary-aśvaharyaśvaharyaśva), RV.; MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. [845,1]
  • N. of a country on the west of the Jumnā river (about Mathurāmathurā and Vṛndā-vanavṛndāvanavṛndāvana, over which Yaduyadu ruled; according to others the Deccan or Southern peninsula of India), W. [845,1]
  • pl. the people of Yaduyadu or the descendants of king Yaduyadu (cf. yādavayādava), ib. [845,1]
