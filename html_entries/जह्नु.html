• jahnujahnu
  • m. N. of an ancient king and sage (son of Aja-mīḍhaajamīḍhaajamīḍha, of Su-hotrasuhotrasuhotra, of Kurukuru, of Hotrakahotraka; ancestor of the Kuśikakuśikas; the Ganges, when brought down from heaven by Bhagī-rathabhagīrathabhagīratha's austerities, was forced to flow over the earth and to follow him to the ocean and thence to the lower regions in order to water the ashes of Sagarasagara's sons; in its course it inundated the sacrificial ground of Jahnujahnu, who drank up its waters but consented at Bhagī-rathabhagīrathabhagīratha's prayer to discharge them from his ears; hence the river is regarded as his daughter), MBh. i, xii f.; Hariv.; R. i, 44, 35 ff.; BhP. ix [416,3]
  • N. of Viṣṇuviṣṇu, L. [416,3]
  • of a Ṛṣiṛṣi of the 4th Manv-antaramanvantaramanvantara, Hariv. 426 (v.l. janyujanyu) [416,3]
  • of a Himâlayahimālaya cavern (from which the Gaṅgāgaṅgā is bursting forth), Kād. ii, 473; Hcar. iii [416,3]
  • pl. Jahnujahnu's race, AitBr. vii, 18; TāṇḍyaBr. xxi, 12, 2; Pravar. iv, 12. [416,3]
