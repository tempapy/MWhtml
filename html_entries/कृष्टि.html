• kṛṣṭikṛṣṭi f. pl.
    • (once only sg. RV. iv, 42, 1) men, races of men (sometimes with the epithet mānuṣīsmānuṣī [i, 59, 5 and vi, 18, 2] or nāhuṣīsnāhuṣi [vi, 46, 7] or mānavīsmānavī [AV. iii, 24, 3]; cf. carṣaṇicarṣaṇicarṣaṇī; originally the word may have meant cultivated ground, then an inhabited land, next its inhabitants, and lastly any race of men; Indra and Agni have the N. rājārājarāj or patiḥpati kṛṣṭīnāmkṛṣṭi; the term pañcapañca kṛṣṭayaskṛṣṭi, perhaps originally designating the five Āryan tribes of the Yadus, Turvaśas, Druhyus, Anus, and Pūrus, comprehends the whole human race, not only the Āryan tribes), RV.; AV.
