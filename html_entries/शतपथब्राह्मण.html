• śataśata—pathapatha—brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa
  • n. ‘the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa with a hundred paths or sections’ N. of a well-known Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa attached to the Vājasaneyi-saṃhitāvājasaneyisaṃhitāvājasaneyisaṃhitā or White Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda, (like the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā, this Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa is ascribed to the Ṛṣiṛṣi Yājñavalkyayājñavalkya; it is perhaps the most modern of the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas, and is preserved in two Śākhāśākhās or schools, Mādhyaṃdinamādhyaṃdina and Kāṇvakāṇva; the version belonging to the former is best known, and is divided into fourteen Kāṇḍakāṇḍas or books which contain one hundred Adhyāyaadhyāyas or lectures [or according to another arrangement into sixty-eight Prapāṭhakaprapāṭhakas]; the whole work is regarded as the most systematic and interesting of all the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas, and though intended mainly for ritual and sacrificial purposes, is full of curious mythological details and legends; cf. yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda, vijasaneyisaṃhitāvijasaneyisaṃhitā, brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa), IW. 25 &c. [1049,2]
