• ṣaṭ—karman n. the six duties of Brāhmans (viz. adhyayana, ‘studying or repeating the Veda’, adhyāpana, ‘teaching the Veda’, yajana ‘offering sacrifices’, yājana, ‘conducting them for others’, dāna, ‘giving’, and pratigraha, ‘accepting gifts’), ŚāṅkhGṛ.; Mn. &c. (the six daily duties accord. to the later law-books, are, snāna, ‘religious bathing’, saṃdhyājapa, ‘repetition of prayers at the three Saṃdhyās’, brahma-yajñabrahma-yajñabrahmayajña, ‘worship of the Supreme Being by repeating the first words of sacred books’, tarpaṇa, ‘daily oblations of water to the gods, sages, and Pitṛs’, homa, ‘oblations of fuel, rice &c. to fire’, deva-pūjādeva-pūjādevapūjā, ‘worship of the secondary gods either in the domestic sanctuary or in temples’), Parāś.; RTL. 394 [1108,1]
  • six acts any one of which is allowable to a Brāhman householder as a means of subsistence (viz. ṛta, ‘gleaning’, amṛta, ‘unsolicited alms’, mṛta, ‘solicited alms’, karṣaṇa, ‘agriculture’, satyā̑nṛta, ‘commerce or trade’, śva-vṛttiśva-vṛttiśvavṛtti, ‘servitude’, the last being condemned), Mn. iv, 4, 5, 6, 9 [1108,1]
  • six acts belonging to the practice of Yoga (viz. dhautī, vastī, netī, trāṭaka, naulika, kapāla-bhātīkapāla-bhātīkapālabhātī, these consist of suppressions of the breath and self-mortifications of various kinds), Cat. [1108,2]
  • six acts for inflicting various kinds of injury on enemies (viz. śānti, vaśya, stambhana, vidveṣa, uccāṭana, māraṇa, qq.vv.; these acts consist in repeating certain magical spells and texts taught in the Tantras), ib. [1108,2]
  • ṣaṭ—karman m. a performer of the above six acts, a Brāhman who is an adept in the Tantra magical formularies, Mn.; MBh. [1108,2]
