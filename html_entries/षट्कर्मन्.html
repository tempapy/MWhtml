• ṣaṭṣaṭ—karmankarman
  • n. the six duties of Brāhmans (viz. adhyayanaadhyayana, ‘studying or repeating the Vedaveda’, adhyāpanaadhyāpana, ‘teaching the Vedaveda’, yajanayajana ‘offering sacrifices’, yājanayājana, ‘conducting them for others’, dānadāna, ‘giving’, and pratigrahapratigraha, ‘accepting gifts’), ŚāṅkhGṛ.; Mn. &c. (the six daily duties accord. to the later law-books, are, snānasnāna, ‘religious bathing’, saṃdhyājapasaṃdhyājapa, ‘repetition of prayers at the three Saṃdhyāsaṃdhyās’, brahma-yajñabrahmayajñabrahmayajña, ‘worship of the Supreme Being by repeating the first words of sacred books’, tarpaṇatarpaṇa, ‘daily oblations of water to the gods, sages, and Pitṛpitṛs’, homahoma, ‘oblations of fuel, rice &c. to fire’, deva-pūjādevapūjādevapūjā, ‘worship of the secondary gods either in the domestic sanctuary or in temples’), Parāś.; RTL. 394 [1108,1]
  • six acts any one of which is allowable to a Brāhman householder as a means of subsistence (viz. ṛtaṛta, ‘gleaning’, amṛtaamṛta, ‘unsolicited alms’, mṛtamṛta, ‘solicited alms’, karṣaṇakarṣaṇa, ‘agriculture’, satyānṛtasatyānṛta, ‘commerce or trade’, śva-vṛttiśvavṛttiśvavṛtti, ‘servitude’, the last being condemned), Mn. iv, 4, 5, 6, 9 [1108,1]
  • six acts belonging to the practice of Yogayoga (viz. dhautīdhautī, vastīvastī, netīnetī, trāṭakatrāṭaka, naulikanaulika, kapāla-bhātīkapālabhātīkapālabhātī, these consist of suppressions of the breath and self-mortifications of various kinds), Cat. [1108,2]
  • six acts for inflicting various kinds of injury on enemies (viz. śāntiśānti, vaśyavaśya, stambhanastambhana, vidveṣavidveṣa, uccāṭanauccāṭana, māraṇamāraṇa, qq.vv.; these acts consist in repeating certain magical spells and texts taught in the Tantratantras), ib. [1108,2]
  • ṣaṭṣaṭ—karmankarman
  • m. a performer of the above six acts, a Brāhman who is an adept in the Tantratantra magical formularies, Mn.; MBh. [1108,2]
