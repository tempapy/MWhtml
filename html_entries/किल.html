• 1. kilakila m.
    • play, trifling, L. [284,1]
  • 2. kilakila ind.
    • (a particle of asseveration or emphasis) indeed, verily, assuredly, RV.; AV. &c. [284,1]
    • (or of explanation) namely, ŚBr. &c. [284,1]
    • ‘so said’ ‘so reported’, pretendedly, VarBṛS.; Kād. [284,1]
    • (kilakila is preceded by the word on which it lays stress, and occurs very rarely at the beginning of a sentence or verse [R. iv, 14, 14; Pañcat. lxxxix, 4]; according to native lexicographers kilakila may be used in communicating intelligence, and may imply ‘probably’, ‘possibly’, ‘agreement’, ‘dislike’, ‘falsehood’, ‘inaccuracy’, and ‘reason.’) [284,1]
  • 3. kilakila m.
    • N. of a man, Pravar. [284,1]
