• devadeva mf(īīiin)n.
    • (fr. 3. divdiv) heavenly, divine (also said of terrestrial things of high excellence), RV.; AV.; VS.; ŚBr. (superl. m. deva-tamadevatamadevatama, RV. iv, 22, 3 &c. f. devi-tamādevitamādevitamadevitamātama, ii, 41, 16) [492,2]
  • devadeva m.

    • (rarely applied to) evil demons, AV. iii, 15, 5; TS. iii, 5, 4, 1 [492,2]
    • (pl. the gods as the heavenly or shining ones; viśveviśvaviśvāviśvi devāsdevadevā, all the gods, RV. ii, 3, 4 &c., or a partic. class of deities [see under viśvaviśva], often reckoned as 33, either 11 for each of the 3 worlds, RV. i, 139, 11 &c. [cf. deva-patnīdevapatnīdevapatnīpatni below]) [492,3]

    • N. of Indra as the god of the sky and giver of rain, MBh.; R. &c. [492,3]
    • a cloud, L. [492,3]
    • (with Jainas) the 22nd Arhat of the future Ut-sarpiṇī [492,3]
