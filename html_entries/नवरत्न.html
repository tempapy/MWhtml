• navanava—ratnaratna
  • n. 9 precious gems (viz. pearl, ruby, topaz, diamond, emerald, lapis lazuli, coral, sapphire, and Go-medhagomedhagomedha they are supposed to be related to the 9 planets), L. (cf. MWB. 528) [531,2]
  • the 9 jewels (i.e. the 9 men of letters at the court of Vikramâdityavikramāditya, viz. Dhanvantaridhanvantari, Kṣapaṇakakṣapaṇaka, Amara-siṉhaamarasiṃhaamarasiṃha, Śaṅkuśaṅku, Vetāla-bhaṭṭavetālabhaṭṭavetālabhaṭṭa, Ghaṭa-karparaghaṭakarparaghaṭakarpara, Kāli-dāsakālidāsakālidāsa, Vara-rucivararucivararuci, and Varāha-mihiravarāhamihiravarāhamihira), L. [531,2]
  • N. of a collection of 9 stanzas and other works. [531,2]
  • nine gems (for ‘lapis lazuli’ read ‘cat's eye’ [= vaiḍūryavaiḍūrya], and for go-medhagomedhagomedha read go-medagomedagomeda, ‘zircon’ or ‘jacinth’; the 9 gems are sacred to the five planets with the Sun and Moon, Rāhurāhu and Ketuketu). [1329,2]
