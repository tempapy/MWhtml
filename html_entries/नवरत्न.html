• navanavanavā—ratnaratna n.
    • 9 precious gems (viz. pearl, ruby, topaz, diamond, emerald, lapis lazuli, coral, sapphire, and Go-medha they are supposed to be related to the 9 planets), L. (cf. MWB. 528) [531,2]
    • the 9 jewels (i.e. the 9 men of letters at the court of Vikramâditya, viz. Dhanvantari, Kṣapaṇaka, Amara-siṉha, Śaṅku, Vetāla-bhaṭṭa, Ghaṭa-karpara, Kāli-dāsa, Vara-ruci, and Varāha-mihira), L. [531,2]
    • N. of a collection of 9 stanzas and other works. [531,2]
    • nine gems (for ‘lapis lazuli’ read ‘cat's eye’ [= vaiḍūryavaiḍūrya], and for go-medhagomedhagomedha read go-medagomedagomeda, ‘zircon’ or ‘jacinth’; the 9 gems are sacred to the five planets with the Sun and Moon, Rāhu and Ketu). [1329,2]
