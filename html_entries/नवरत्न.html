• nava—ratna n. 9 precious gems (viz. pearl, ruby, topaz, diamond, emerald, lapis lazuli, coral, sapphire, and Go-medhago-medhagomedha they are supposed to be related to the 9 planets), L. (cf. MWB. 528) [531,2]
  • the 9 jewels (i.e. the 9 men of letters at the court of Vikramâditya, viz. Dhanvantari, Kṣapaṇaka, Amara-siṉhaamara-siṃhaamarasiṃha, Śaṅku, Vetāla-bhaṭṭavetāla-bhaṭṭavetālabhaṭṭa, Ghaṭa-karparaghaṭa-karparaghaṭakarpara, Kāli-dāsakāli-dāsakālidāsa, Vara-rucivara-rucivararuci, and Varāha-mihiravarāha-mihiravarāhamihira), L. [531,2]
  • N. of a collection of 9 stanzas and other works. [531,2]
  • nine gems (for ‘lapis lazuli’ read ‘cat's eye’ [= vaiḍūrya], and for go-medhago-medhagomedha read go-medago-medagomeda, ‘zircon’ or ‘jacinth’; the 9 gems are sacred to the five planets with the Sun and Moon, Rāhu and Ketu). [1329,2]
