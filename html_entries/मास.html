• māsamāsa m.
    • (or n., Siddh.) the moon (See pūrṇa-m°pūrṇamāsapūrṇamāsa) [814,3]
    • a month or the 12th part of the Hindū year (there are 4 kinds of months, viz. the solar, saurasaura; the natural, sāvanasāvana; the stellar, nākṣatranākṣatra, and the lunar, cā̑ndracāndraca; the latter, which is the most usual and consists of 30 Tithis, being itself of two kinds as reckoned from the new or full moon cf. IW. 179; for the names of the months See ib. 173 n. 3), RV. &c. &c. (māsammāsamās, for a month; māsammāsamās ekameka, for one month; māsenamāsa, in the course of a month; māsemāsamās, in a month = after the lapse of a month) [814,3]
    • a symbolical N. for the number ‘twelve’ Sūryas. [814,3]
