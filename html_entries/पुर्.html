• 1. purpur f.
    • (√pṝpṝ) only instr. pl. pūrbhis, in abundance, abundantly, RV. v, 66, 4. [634,2]
  • 2. purpur
    • cl. 6. P. purati, to precede, go before, lead, Dhātup. xxviii, 56 (prob. invented to furnish an etymology for puraspuraspura and purāpurāpura below). [634,2]
  • 3. purpur f.
    • (in nom. sg. and before consonants pūrpūr) a rampart, wall, stronghold, fortress, castle, city, town (also of demons), RV. &c. &c. [635,2]
    • the body (considered as the stronghold of the puruṣapuruṣa q.v.), BhP. [635,2]
    • the intellect (= mahatmahatmahad), VP. [635,2]
    • N. of a Daśa-rātra, KātyŚr. [Perhaps fr. √pṝpṝ and orig. identical with 1. purpur; cf. Gk., πόλις] [635,2]
