• katamakatama
  • mfn (°atkatamat). (superlative of 2. kaka; declined as a pronom., Gram. 236), who or which of many? (e.g. katamenakatamena pathāpathā yātāsyātās tete, by which road have they gone?) [246,2]
  • it is often a mere strengthened substitute for kaka, the superlative affix imparting emphasis [246,2]
  • hence it may occasionally be used for ‘who or which of two?’ (e.g. tayoḥtayoḥ katamasmaikatamasmai, to which of these two?) [246,2]
  • it may optionally be compounded with the word to which it refers (e.g. katamaḥkatamaḥ kaṭhaḥkaṭhaḥ, or katama-kaṭhaḥkatamakaṭhaḥkatamakaṭhaḥ, which Kaṭhakaṭha out of many?) [246,2]
  • when followed by caca and preceded yatamayatama an indefinite expression is formed equivalent to ‘any whosoever’, ‘any whatsoever’, &c. (e.g. yatamadyatamad evaeva katamackatamac caca vidyātvidyāt he may know anything whatsoever). In negative sentences katamakatama with canacana or katamakatama with apiapi = not even one, none at all (e.g. nana katamaccanāhaḥkatamaccanāhaḥ, not even on a single day, on no day at all) [246,3]
  • in addition to the above uses katamakatama is said to mean ‘best’, ‘excessively good-looking’ (cf. 3. kaka), RV. &c. [246,3]
