• vopa-devavopadevavopadeva
  • m. N. of the author of the Mugdha-bodhamugdhabodhamugdhabodha grammar (also of the Kavi-kalpadrumakavikalpadrumakavikalpadruma and various other works, including, according to some, the Bhāgavatabhāgavata-Purāṇapurāṇa; he was a son of Keśavakeśava and pupil of Dhanêśvaradhaneśvara, and is said to have flourished about the latter half of the thirteenth century at the court of Hemâdrihemādri, king of Deva-giridevagiridevagiri, now Dowlatābād). [1028,2]
