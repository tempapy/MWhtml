• vopa-devavopa-devavopadeva m. N. of the author of the Mugdha-bodhamugdha-bodhamugdhabodha grammar (also of the Kavi-kalpadrumakavi-kalpadrumakavikalpadruma and various other works, including, according to some, the Bhāgavata-Purāṇa; he was a son of Keśava and pupil of Dhanêśvara, and is said to have flourished about the latter half of the thirteenth century at the court of Hemâdri, king of Deva-girideva-giridevagiri, now Dowlatābād). [1028,2]
