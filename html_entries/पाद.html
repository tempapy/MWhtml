    • the foot or leg of an inanimate object, column, pillar, AV.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [617,2]
    • a wheel, Śiś. xii, 21 [617,2]
    • a foot as a measure (= 12 Aṅgulas), ŚBr.; ŚrS.; MārkP. [617,2]
    • the foot or root of a tree, Var. [617,2]
    • the foot or a hill at the foot of a mountain, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [617,2]
    • the bottom (dṛteh pādātpādātpāda, ‘from the bottom of a bag’ v.l. pātrātpātra), MBh. v, 1047 [617,2]
    • a ray or beam of light (considered as the foot of a heavenly body), ib. [617,2]
    • a quarter, a fourth part (the foot of a quadruped being one out of 4), ŚBr.; Mn.; MBh. &c. (pl. the 4 parts i.e. all things required for [gen.] Suśr.) [617,2]
    • the quadrant (of a circle), Āryabh., Sch. [617,2]
    • a verse or line (as the fourth part of a regular stanza), Br.; ŚrS.; Prāt. &c. [617,2]
    • the caesura of a verse, AgP., the chapter of a book (orig. only of a book or section of a book consisting of 4 parts, as the Adhyāyas of Pāṇini's grammar). [617,2]
