• vi-citravicitravicitra—vīryavīrya
  • m. ‘of marvellous heroism’, N. of a celebrated king of the lunar race (the son of Śāṃtanuśāṃtanu by his wife Satya-vatīsatyavatīsatyavatī, and so half-brother of Bhīṣmabhīṣma; when he died childless, his mother requested Vyāsavyāsa, whom she had borne before her marriage to the sage Parāśaraparāśara, to raise up issue to Vicitra-vīryavicitravīryavicitravīrya; so Vyāsavyāsa married the two widows of his half-brother, Ambikāambikā and Ambālikāambālikā, and by them became the father of Dhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍupāṇḍu; cf. IW. 375; 376), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. [959,1]
