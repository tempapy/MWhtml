• vi-citravi-citravicitra—vīrya m. ‘of marvellous heroism’, N. of a celebrated king of the lunar race (the son of Śāṃtanu by his wife Satya-vatīsatya-vatīsatyavatī, and so half-brother of Bhīṣma; when he died childless, his mother requested Vyāsa, whom she had borne before her marriage to the sage Parāśara, to raise up issue to Vicitra-vīryavicitra-vīryavicitravīrya; so Vyāsa married the two widows of his half-brother, Ambikā and Ambālikā, and by them became the father of Dhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍu; cf. IW. 375; 376), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. [959,1]
