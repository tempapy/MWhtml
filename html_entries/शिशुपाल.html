• śiśuśiśu—pālapāla
  • m. ‘child-protector’, N. of the king of the Cedicedis inhabiting a country in central India, probably the same as Bundelkhand (See cedicedi; he was son of Dama-ghoṣadamaghoṣadamaghoṣa, and is also called Sunīthasunītha; his impiety in opposing the worship of Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa is described in the Sabhā-parvansabhāparvansabhāparvan of the Mahā-bhāratamahābhāratamahābhārata; when Yudhi-ṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhira was about to perform a Rājasūyarājasūya sacrifice, numerous princes attended, and Bhīṣmabhīṣma proposed that especial honour should be paid to Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa, who was also present, but Śiśu-pālaśiśupālaśiśupāla objected, and after denouncing Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa as a contemptible person challenged him to fight, whereupon Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa struck off his head with his discus; the Viṣṇu-Purāṇaviṣṇupurāṇaviṣṇupurāṇa identifies this impious monarch with the demons Hiraṇya-kaśipuhiraṇyakaśipuhiraṇyakaśipu and Rāvaṇarāvaṇa; his death forms the subject of Māghamāgha's celebrated poem called Śiśupāla-vadhaśiśupālavadhaśiśupālavadha) [1076,2]
