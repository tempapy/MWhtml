• yathāyathā a ind.
    • (in Veda also unaccented; fr. 3. yaya, correlative of tathātathā) in which manner or way, according as, as, like (also with cidcid, haha, haha vaivaiuū, ivaiva, ivā̑ṅgaivaaṅgaāṅga, ivaiva haha, evaeva, and followed by correl. tathātathā, tathātathā tathātathā, tadvattadvat, evamevameva, Ved. also evaeva), RV. &c. &c. (yatha͡itatyathayathāetatetad or yatha͡iva͡itatyathayathāevaetatetad, ‘as for that’ ; yathā-tathāyathātathayathātathā or yathāyathā - tenatenata satyenasatya, ‘as surely as’ - ‘so truly’) [841,2]
    • as, for instance, namely (also tadtadtat yathāyathā, ‘as here follows’), Up.; GṛŚrS.; Nir. [841,3]
    • as it is or was (elliptically), BhP. [841,3]
    • that, so that, in order that (with Pot. or Subj., later also with fut. pres., impf. and aor.; in earlier language yathāyathā is often placed after the first word of a sentence; sometimes with ellipsis of syātsyātsyasyād and bhavet), RV. &c. &c. [841,3]
    • that (esp. after verbs of ‘knowing’, ‘believing’, ‘hearing’, ‘doubting’ &c. either with or without itiiti at the end of the sentence), Up.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. [841,3]
    • as soon as, Megh. [841,3]
    • as, because, since (yathā-tathāyathātathayathātathā, ‘as’ - ‘therefore’), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [841,3]
    • as if (with Pot.), Daś.; Śak.; how (= quam, expressing ‘admiration’), Pāṇ. viii, 1, 37 Sch. [841,3]
    • according to what is right, properly, correctly (= yathāvatyathāvat), BhP. (yathāyathā yathā-tathāyathātathayathātathā tathātathā or eva͡ivaevaeva, ‘in whatever manner’, - ‘in that manner’, ‘according as’ or ‘in proportion as’, - ‘so’, ‘by how much the more’ - ‘by so much’, ‘the more’ - ‘the more’; yathāyathā tathātathā, ‘in whatever manner’, ‘in every way’, ‘anyhow’; with nana, ‘in no way’, ‘really not’; yathāyathā kathaṃcit, ‘in any way’, ‘somehow or other’; yatha͡ivayathayathāeva, ‘just as’; tadtadtat yathā̑pināmayathayathāapināma, ‘just as if’). [841,3]
