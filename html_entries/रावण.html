  • rāvaṇarāvaṇa
  • m. N. of the ruler of Laṅkālaṅkā or Ceylon and the famous chief of the Rākṣasarākṣasas or demons whose destruction by Rāmacandrarāmacandra forms the subject of the Rāmāyaṇarāmāyaṇa (as son of Viśravasviśravas he was younger brother of Kuberakubera, but by a different mother, Ilavilailavila being the mother of Kuberakubera, and Keśinīkeśinī of the three other brothers Rāvaṇarāvaṇa Vibhīṣaṇavibhīṣaṇa, and Kumbha-karṇakumbhakarṇakumbhakarṇa; he is one of the worst of the many impersonations of evil common in Hindū mythology; he has ten heads and twenty arms, symbolizing strength; this power was, as usual, acquired by self-inflicted austerities, which had obtained from Brahmābrahmā a boon, in virtue of which Rāvaṇarāvaṇa was invulnerable by gods and divine beings of all kinds, though not by men or a god in human form; as Viṣṇuviṣṇu became incarnate in Rāma-candrarāmacandrarāmacandra to destroy Rāvaṇarāvaṇa, so the other gods produced innumerable monkeys, bears, and various semi-divine animals to do battle with the legions of demons, his subjects, under Kharakhara, Dūṣaṇadūṣaṇa, and his other generals), MBh.; R.; Hariv. &c.
