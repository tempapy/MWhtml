• sāmarthyasāmarthya n.
    • (fr. sam-arthasamarthasamartha) sameness of aim or object or meaning or signification, belonging or agreeing together (in aim, object &c.), adequacy, accordance, fitness, suitableness, Pat.; Hariv.; Suśr. &c. [1205,3]
    • the being entitled to, justification for (loc. or comp.), MBh.; R. &c. [1205,3]
    • ability to or capacity for (inf. dat. loc., or comp.; acc. with √kṛkṛ, ‘to do one's utmost’; with √bhajbhaj, ‘to take pains’, ‘exert one's self’), ib. [1205,3]
    • efficacy, power, strength, force (ātāta, or -tassāmarthyatasta or -yogātsāmarthyayoga, ‘through the force of circumstances’, ‘by reason of.’, ‘in consequence of’, ‘on account of.’ ‘as a matter of course’), ib. [1205,3]
    • the force or function or sense of a word, Kusum. [1205,3]
