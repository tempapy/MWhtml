• śakunaśakuna m.
    • (said to be fr. √śakśakśacśaś, Uṇ. iii, 49) a bird (esp. a large bird or one of good or bad omen), RV. &c. &c. [1046,2]
    • a partic. kind of bird (either = gridhra, a vulture, or = cillacilla, a common kite or Pondicherry eagle), L. [1046,2]
    • a kind of Brāhman (vipra-bhedaviprabheda), MW. [1046,2]
    • a sort of hymn or song (sung at festivals to secure good fortune), W. [1046,2]
    • (with vasiṣṭhasyavasiṣṭha) N. of a Sāman, ĀrṣBr. [1046,2]
    • N. of an Asura, BhP. [1046,2]
    • pl. N. of a people, MBh.; Buddh. [1046,2]
  • śakunaśakuna n.
    • any auspicious object or lucky omen, an omen or prognostic (in general; rarely ‘an inauspicious omen’), Kāv.; Kathās.; Pañcat. [1046,2]
  • śakunaśakuna mfn.
    • indicating good luck, auspicious, MW. [1046,2]
