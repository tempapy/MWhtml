• maryādāmaryādāmaryāda f.
    • (doubtful whether fr. maryāmaryāmarya + dādāda or maryāmaryāmarya + ādaāda [fr. āāa √dādāda]; fancifully said to be fr. maryamarya + adaada, ‘devouring young men’ who are killed in defending boundaries) ‘giving or containing clear marks or signs’, a frontier, limit, boundary, border, bank, shore, mark, end, extreme point, goal (in space and time), RV. &c. &c. (ṣaṇ-māsa-maryādayāṣaṇṣaṭmāsamaryādāmaryāda, within six months, VarBṛS.) [791,3]
    • the bounds or limits of morality and propriety, rule or custom, distinct law or definition, Mn.; MBh. &c. [791,3]
    • a covenant, agreement, bond, contract, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [791,3]
    • continuance in the right way, propriety of conduct, Kāv.; Pañcat. [791,3]
    • N. of a kind of ring used as an amulet, AV. vi, 81, 2 [791,3]
    • N. of the wife of Avācīna (daughter of a king of Vidarbha), MBh. [791,3]
    • of the wife of Devâtithi (daughter of a king of Videha), ib. [791,3]
