• kathkath
    • cl. 10. P. (ep. also Ā.) kathayati (-tekathtatāti) aor. acakathat (Pāṇ. vii, 4, 93; Kāś.) and acīkathat (Vop.),
    • to converse with any one (instr., sometimes with sahasaha), MBh.;
    • to tell, relate, narrate, report, inform, speak about, declare, explain, describe (with acc. of the thing or person spoken about), MBh.; R.; Śak. &c.
    • to announce, show, exhibit, bespeak, betoken, Mn. xi, 114; Śak. 291, 4; Suśr. &c.
    • to order, command, Pañcat. 57, 22;
    • to suppose, state, MBh. iii, 10668; Mn. vii, 157 :
    • Pass. kathyate, to be called, be regarded or considered as, pass for, Pañcat.; Hit. &c.
