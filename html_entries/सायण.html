• sāyaṇasāyaṇa m.
    • (said to be a Drâviḍa word) N. of a learned Brāhman (also called Sāyaṇamādhava and Sāyaṇâcārya; he was son of Māyaṇa, pupil of Viṣṇu Sarva-jña and of Śaṃkarânanda; and flourished under Bukka I of Vijaya-nagara or Vidyā-nagara [A.D. 1350-1379] and his successor Harihara, and died in 1387; of more than a hundred works attributed to him, among which are commentaries on nearly all parts of the Veda, some were carried out by his pupils, and some were written in conjunction with his brother Mādhavâcārya or Vidyâraṇya-svāmin). [1207,3]
