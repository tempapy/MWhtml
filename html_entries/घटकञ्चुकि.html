• ghaṭaghaṭa—kañcukikañcuki
  • n. an immoral rite practised by Tāntrikatāntrikas and Śāktaśāktas (in which the bodices of different women are placed in a receptacle and the men present at the ceremony are allowed to take them out one by one and then cohabit with the woman to whom each bodice belongs), Āgamapr. [375,2]
