• kārttikeyakārttikeya
  • m. N. of a son of Śivaśiva and Pārvatīpārvatī (popularly regarded as god of war, because he leads the Gaṇagaṇas or hosts of Śivaśiva against the demon hosts See RTL. p.213; accord. to one legend he was son of Śivaśiva without the intervention of Pārvatīpārvatī, the generative energy of Śivaśiva being cast into the fire and then received by the Ganges, whence he is sometimes described as son of Agniagni and Gaṅgāgaṅgā; when born he was fostered by the six Kṛttikākṛttikās q.v., and these offering their six breasts to the child he became six-headed; he is also called Kumārakumāra, Skandaskanda, and Subrahmaṇyasubrahmaṇya; his N. Kārttikeyakārttikeya may be derived from his foster mothers or from the month Kārttikakārttika as the best for warfare: in the Mṛcch. and elsewhere he is regarded as presiding over thieves), MBh. &c. [275,3]
