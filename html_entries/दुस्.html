• dus
  • ind. a prefix to nouns and rarely to verbs or adverbs (Pāṇ. ii, 1, 6; 2, 18, Vārtt. 2, Pat.; iii, 3, 126 &c.) implying evil, bad, difficult, hard [488,1]
  • badly, hardly; slight, inferior &c. (opp. to su), often = Engl. in- or un- [488,2]
  • dus
  • [cf. √2. duz; Zd. dush-; Gk. δυσ-; Goth. tuz-; O.H.G. zur.] [488,2]
  • It becomes dur (q.v.) before vowels and soft consonants; dU (q.v.) before r and sometimes before d, D, n, which become q, Q, R [488,2]
  • remains unchanged before t, T (in older language however zw, zW) [488,2]
  • becomes duz (q.v.), rarely duH before k, K; p, P [488,2]
  • duS (q.v.) before c, C [488,2]
  • duH (q.v.), rarely duS, duz, dus, before S, z, s. [488,2]
