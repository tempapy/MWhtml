• dusdusdu ind.
    • a prefix to nouns and rarely to verbs or adverbs (Pāṇ. ii, 1, 6; 2, 18, Vārtt. 2, Pat.; iii, 3, 126 &c.) implying evil, bad, difficult, hard [488,1]
    • badly, hardly; slight, inferior &c. (opp. to sususū), often = Engl. in- or un- [488,2]
  • dusdusdu
    • [cf. √2. duṣduṣ; Zd. dush-; Gk. δυσ-; Goth. tuz-; O.H.G. zur.] [488,2]
    • It becomes durdur (q.v.) before vowels and soft consonants; dūdūdu (q.v.) before r and sometimes before d, dh, n, which become ḍ, ḍh, ṇ [488,2]
    • remains unchanged before t, th (in older language however ṣṭ, ṣṭh) [488,2]
    • becomes duṣduṣ (q.v.), rarely duḥduḥdusdudur before k, kh; p, ph [488,2]
    • duśduś (q.v.) before c, ch [488,2]
    • duḥduḥdusdudur (q.v.), rarely duśduś, duṣduṣ, dusdusdu, before ś, ṣ, s. [488,2]
