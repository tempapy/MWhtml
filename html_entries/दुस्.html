• dusdus
  • ind. a prefix to nouns and rarely to verbs or adverbs (Pāṇ. ii, 1, 6; 2, 18, Vārtt. 2, Pat.; iii, 3, 126 &c.) implying evil, bad, difficult, hard [488,1]
  • badly, hardly; slight, inferior &c. (opp. to susu), often = Engl. in- or un- [488,2]
  • dusdus
  • [cf. √2. duṣduṣ; Zd. dush-; Gk. δυσ-; Goth. tuz-; O.H.G. zur.] [488,2]
  • It becomes durdur (q.v.) before vowels and soft consonants; dūdū (q.v.) before rr and sometimes before dd, dhdh, nn, which become ḍḍ, ḍhḍh, ṇṇ [488,2]
  • remains unchanged before tt, thth (in older language however ṣṭṣṭ, ṣṭhṣṭh) [488,2]
  • becomes duṣduṣ (q.v.), rarely duḥduḥ before kk, khkh; pp, phph [488,2]
  • duśduś (q.v.) before cc, chch [488,2]
  • duḥduḥ (q.v.), rarely duśduś, duṣduṣ, dusdus, before śś, ṣṣ, ss. [488,2]
