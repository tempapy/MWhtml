• aṭaṭajaṭhaḍ [Roots]
    • cl. 1. Ā. P. aṭati, °te, āṭaāṭa, aṭiṣyati, āṭīt, aṭitum, to roam, wander about (sometimes with acc.; frequently used of religious mendicants) :
    • Intens. aṭāṭyate, to roam or wander about zealously or habitually, especially as a religious mendicant:
    • Desid. aṭiṭiṣati, to be desirous of roaming. [11,2]
