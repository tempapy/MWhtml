• laghulaghulaghū mf(vīvīvi, or uuū)n.
    • (a later form of raghuraghu, q.v.) light, quick, swift, active, prompt, nimble, Mn.; MBh. &c. (also said of a partic. mode of flying peculiar to birds, Pañcat.; applied to the Nakṣatras Hasta, Aśvinī, and Puṣya, VarBṛS.) [893,3]
    • light, easy, not heavy or difficult, AV. &c. &c. [893,3]
    • light in the stomach, easily digested, Suśr. [893,3]
    • easy in mind, light-hearted, Hariv.; Kālid. [893,3]
    • causing easiness or relief, Sāṃkhyak. [893,3]
    • well, in good health, L. [893,3]
    • unimpeded, without attendance or a retinue, MBh. iii, 8449 [893,3]
    • short (in time, as a suppression of the breath), MārkP. [893,3]
    • (in prosody) short or light (as a vowel or syllable, opp. to gurugurugurū) [893,3]
    • (in gram.) easily pronounced or articulated (said of the pronunciation of vava, as opp. to madhyamamadhyama and gurugurugurū) [893,3]
    • small, minute, slight, little, insignificant, ŚBr. &c. &c. [893,3]
    • weak, feeble, wretched, humble, mean, low (said of persons), Mn.; MBh. &c. [893,3]
    • young, younger (See -bhrātṛlaghubhrātṛlaghubhrātṛ) [893,3]
    • clean, pure (See -vāsaslaghuvāsaslaghuvāsasvāsavās) [893,3]
    • soft, gentle (as sound), Kathās.; BhP. [893,3]
    • pleasing, agreeable, handsome, beautiful, MBh.; Kālid. [893,3]
