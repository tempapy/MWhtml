• mahāmahā—bhāratabhārata
  • m. or n. (with or scil. āhavaāhava, yuddhayuddha or any word signifying ‘battle’) the great war of the Bharatabharatas, MBh.; Hariv. [798,2]
  • mahāmahā—bhāratabhārata
  • n. (with or scil. ākhyānaākhyāna), ‘great narrative of the war of the Bharatabharatas’, N. of the great epic poem in about 215, 000 lines describing the acts and contests of the sons of the two brothers Dhṛtarāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍupāṇḍu, descendants of Bharatabharata, who were of the lunar line of kings reigning in the neighbourhood of Hastinā-purahastināpurahastināpura (the poem consists of 18 books with a supplement called Hari-vaṉśaharivaṃśaharivaṃśa, the whole being attributed to the sage Vyāsavyāsa), ĀśvGṛ.; MBh. &c. (IW. 370 &c.) [798,2]
