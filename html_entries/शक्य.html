• śakyaśakya mf(āāa)n.
    • able, possible, practicable, capable of being (with inf. in pass. sense e.g. nana sāsāsa śakyāśakya netum balātbalātbala, ‘she cannot be conducted by force’; tantantat mayāmayāmayamāma śakyamśakya pratipattum, ‘that is able to be acquired by me’; the form śakyamśakya may also be used with a nom. case which is in a different gender or number e.g. śakyaṃśakya śva-māṃsā̑dibhirśvamāṃsādinmāṃsaādiādin apiapiap kṣutkṣut pratihantum, ‘hunger can be appeased even by dog's flesh &c.’; cf. Vām. v, 2, 25), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1045,1]
    • to be conquered or subdued, liable to be compelled to (inf.), MBh. [1045,1]
    • explicit, direct, literal (as the meaning of a word or sentence, opp. to lakṣyalakṣya and vyaṅgyavyaṅgya), Alaṃkāraś. [1045,1]
