• śakyaśakya
  • mf(āā)n. able, possible, practicable, capable of being (with inf. in pass. sense e.g. nana sāsā śakyāśakyā netumnetum balātbalāt, ‘she cannot be conducted by force’; tantan mayāmayā śakyamśakyam pratipattumpratipattum, ‘that is able to be acquired by me’; the form śakyamśakyam may also be used with a nom. case which is in a different gender or number e.g. śakyaṃśakyaṃ śva-māṃsādibhirśvamāṃsādibhirśvamāṃsādibhir apiapi kṣutkṣut pratihantumpratihantum, ‘hunger can be appeased even by dog's flesh &c.’; cf. Vām. v, 2, 25), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1045,1]
  • to be conquered or subdued, liable to be compelled to (inf.), MBh. [1045,1]
  • explicit, direct, literal (as the meaning of a word or sentence, opp. to lakṣyalakṣya and vyaṅgyavyaṅgya), Alaṃkāraś. [1045,1]
