• liṅgaliṅga—śarīraśarīra n.
    • the subtle body which accompanies the individual spirit or soul in all its transmigrations and is not destroyed by death (it is also called sūkṣma-ś°sūkṣmaśarīrasūkṣmaśarīra q.v., and since it is the sign and accompaniment of individuality it can never perish till the individualized soul is finally merged in the Universal), Kap.; Vedântas. (IW. 53 n. 2). [902,1]
