• śṛṅgāraśṛṅgāra m.
    • (prob. connected with śṛṅgaśṛṅga as vṛndāravṛndāra with vṛndavṛnda) love (as ‘the horned’ or ‘the strong one’ ?), sexual passion or desire or enjoyment, Kāv.; Rājat. &c. [1087,3]
    • (in rhet.) the erotic sentiment (one of the 8 or 10 Rasas q.v.; it has Viṣṇu for its tutelary deity and black for its colour; accord. to most authorities it is of two kinds, viz. sambhogasambhoga, ‘mutual enjoyment’, and vipralambhavipralambha, ‘deception, disappointment’, to which by some is added as third a-yogaayogaayoga, ‘separation’), Bhar.; Daśar.; Sāh. &c. [1087,3]
    • a dress suitable for amorous purposes, elegant dress, fine garments, finery, Kāv.; Pañcat.; Kathās. &c. [1087,3]
    • the ornaments on an elephant (esp. red marks on its head and trunk), L. (cf. -dhārinśṛṅgāradhārinśṛṅgāradhārin) [1087,3]
    • any mark, MW. [1087,3]
    • (also with bhaṭṭabhaṭṭa) N. of various persons (esp. of a poet), Rājat.; Cat. [1087,3]
  • śṛṅgāraśṛṅgāra n.
