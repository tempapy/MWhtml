• candracandra—vaṃśavaṃśa
  • m. the lunar race of kings (2nd great line of royal dynasties, the progenitor of which was Somasoma the Moon, child of the Ṛṣiṛṣi Atriatri and father of Budhabudha [Mercury cf. candra-jacandrajacandraja]; the latter married Iḻāiłā, daughter of the solar king Ikṣvākuikṣvāku, and had by her a son, Ailaaila or Purūravaspurūravas; this last had a son by Urvaśīurvaśī, named Āyusāyus, from whom came Nahuṣanahuṣa, father of Yayātiyayāti; the latter had two sons, Purupuru and Yaduyadu, from whom proceeded the two branches of the lunar line; in that of Yaduyadu was born Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa and Bala-rāmabalarāmabalarāma; in that of Purupuru came Duṣyantaduṣyanta, hero of the Śakuntalāśakuntalā and father of the great Bharatabharata; 9th from Bharatabharata came Kurukuru, and 14th from him Śāntanuśāntanu, who had a son Vicitra-vīryavicitravīryavicitravīrya and a step-son Vyāsavyāsa; the latter married the two widows of his half-brother, and had by them Dhṛtarāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍupāṇḍu, the wars of whose sons form the subject of the MBh.) (cf. sūrya-v°sūryavaṃśasūryavaṃśa.) [387,2]
