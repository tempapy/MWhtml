• nāmannāman n.
    • (prob. neither fr. √jñājñājña nor fr. √mnāmnā [cf. Uṇ. iv, 150]; ifc. f. either = m. or °mnī) a characteristic mark or sign, form, nature, kind, manner, RV.; VS.; AV. [536,2]
    • name, appellation, RV. &c. &c. [536,2]
    • personal name (as opp. to gotregotragotrā, family name; cf. nama-gotranamagotra above), Kāś. on Pāṇ. viii, 2, 23 (often ifc. = named, called e.g. viṣṇu-śarma-nāmāviṣṇuśarmanāma paṇḍitaḥpaṇḍita, a sage named ViṣṇuŚarma) [536,2]
    • merely the name (as opp. to reality; cf. nāma-dhārakanāmadhārakanāmadhāraka, -mātranāmanmātra, -śeṣanāmanśeṣa &c.), a noun (as opp. to a verb), Nir.; Prāt. [536,2]
    • substance, essence (in the Mīmāṉsā phil. opp. to guṇaguṇa, accidental quality) [536,2]
    • a good or great name, renown, fame (only ifc.; cf. śva-śvanāman, sumantu-sumantunāmansumantunāman) [536,2]
    • water, Naigh. i, 11 [536,2]
