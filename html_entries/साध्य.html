    • (pl.) ‘they that are to be propitiated’, N. of a class of celestial beings (belonging to the gaṇa-devatāgaṇadevatāgaṇadevatā q.v., sometimes mentioned in the Veda [see, RV. x, 90, 16]; in the ŚBr. their world is said to be above the sphere of the gods; according to Yāska [Nir. xii, 41] their locality is the Bhuvarloka or middle region between the earth and sun; in Mn. i, 22, the Sādhyas are described as created after the gods with natures exquisitely refined, and in iii, 195, as children of the Soma-sads, sons of Virāj; in the Purāṇas they are sons of Sādhyā, and their number is variously twelve or seventeen; in the later mythology they seem to be superseded by the Siddhas See siddhasiddha; and their names are Manas, Mantṛ, Prâṇa, Nara, Pāna, Vinirbhaya, Naya, Daṉśa, Nārāyaṇa, Vṛṣa, Prabhu), RV. &c. &c.
