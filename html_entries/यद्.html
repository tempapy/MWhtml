• yadyad
    • (nom. and acc. sg. n. and base in comp. of 3. yaya), who, which, what, whichever, whatever, that, RV. &c. &c. (with correlatives tadtad, tyadtyad, etadetad, idamidamid, adasadasadaad, tadtadtat etadetad, etadetad tyadtyad, idaṃidamid tadtad, tadtadtat idamidamid, tādṛśatādṛśa, īdṛśaīdṛśa, īdṛśīdṛś, etāvadetāvad, by which it is oftener followed than preceded; or the correl. is dropped, e.g. yasyasyaī tutu nā̑rabhatenanā karmakarma kṣipramkṣipramkṣipra bhavatibhavatībhavat nirdravyaḥ, ‘[he] indeed who does not begin work soon becomes poor’, R.; or the rel. is dropped, e.g. andhakamandhaka bhartāraṃ nana tyajet sāsāsasas mahā-satīmahāsatīmahāsatīsatisat, ‘she who does not desert a blind husband is a very faithful wife’, Vet. yadyad is often repeated to express ‘whoever’, ‘whatever’, ‘whichever’, e.g. yoyuyasyaī yaḥyasyaī, ‘whatever man’; yāyāyaiīyāsyas yāyāyaiī, ‘whatever woman’; yoyuyasyaī yajyajyat jayatijayatijayat tasyata tattat, ‘whatever he wins [in war] belongs to him’, Mn. vii, 96; yadyadyat yadyadyat vadati tadtad tadtad bhavatibhavatībhavat, ‘whatever he says is true’, or the two relatives may be separated by hihihī, and are followed by the doubled or single correl. tadtad, e.g. upyate yadyad dhidhidhī yadyad bījambīja tattat tadtadtat evaeva prarohati, ‘whatever seed is sown, that even comes forth’, Mn. ix, 40; similar indefinite meanings are expressed by the relative joined with tadtad, e.g. yasmaiya tasmaita, ‘to any one whatever’, esp. in yadvāyadvāyadu tadvātadvā, ‘anything whatever’; or by yaḥyasyaī with kaśca, kaścanakaścana, kaścit, or [in later language, not in Manu] ko'pi, e.g. yaḥyasyaī kaścit, ‘whosoever’; yāniyayānī kānika caca mitrāṇimitra, ‘any friends whatsoever’; yenayenaya kenā̑pykenaapiapīapāpiāpīāp upā̑yenaupāyaupaupāayaāya, ‘by any means whatsoever’. yadyad is joined with tvadtvad to express generalization, e.g. śūdrāṃsśūdra tvadtvadtvat yāṃsyayat tvadtvad, ‘either the Śūdras or anybody else’, ŚBr.; or immediately followed by a pers. pron. on which it lays emphasis, e.g. yoyuyasyaī 'hamahaṃahamahaah, ‘I that very person who’; yasyasyaī tvaṃtvaṃtva kathaṃkathaṃkathamkath vettha, ‘how do you know?’, ŚBr.; it is also used in the sense of ‘si quis’, e.g. striyaṃstrī spṛśed yaḥyasyaī, ‘should any one touch a woman’. yadyad is also used without the copula, e.g. andhoandhuandhasandhaandh jaḍaḥjaḍa pīṭha-sarpīpīṭhasarpinpīṭhasarpīsarpisarpin saptatyāsaptati sthaviraśca yaḥ, ‘a blind man, an idiot, a cripple, and a man seventy years old’, Mn. viii, 394; sometimes there is a change of construction in such cases, e.g. yeyayā caca mānuṣāḥ for mānuṣāṃś-caca, Mn. x, 86; the nom. sg. n. yadyad is then often used without regard to gender or number and may be translated by ‘as regards’, ‘as for’, e.g. kṣatraṃkṣatra vāvāvauūvaivāsvas etadetadetat vanaspatīnāṃvanaspati yanyanyat nyag-rodhaḥnyagrodhanyagnyakrodhaḥrodhasrodha, ‘as for the Nyag-rodha, it is certainly the prince among trees’, AitBr.; or by ‘that is to say’, ‘to wit’ e.g. tatotatotatastatatat devādevādeva etaṃeta vajraṃvajra dadṛśur yadyadyat apaḥapasapaap, ‘the gods then saw this thunderbolt, to wit, the water’, ŚBr. yadyad as an adv. conjunction generally = ‘that’, esp. after verbs of saying, thinking &c., often introducing an oratio directa with or without itiitiit; itiitiit yadyad, at the end of a sentence = ‘thinking that’, ‘under the impression that’ e.g. Ratnāv. ii, 2/8. yadyad also = ‘so that’, ‘in order that’, ‘wherefore’, ‘whence’, ‘as’, ‘in as much as’, ‘since’, ‘because’ [the correlative being tadtad, ‘therefore’], ‘when’, ‘if’, RV. &c. &c. adhaadha yadyad, ‘even if’, ‘although’, RV. yadyadyat apiapiapīap id., Megh. yadyadyat uuū - evamevameva, ‘as - so’, ŚvetUp.; yadyadyat utauta, ‘that’, Bālar.; ‘that is to say’, ‘scilicet’, Kāraṇḍ.; Divyāv.; yatyat kilakila, ‘that’, Prasannar.; yacyacyat caca, ‘if’, ‘that is to say’, Car.; yacyacyat ca-yaccayac caca, ‘both - and’, Divyāv.; ‘that’ [accord. to Pāṇ. iii, 3, 148 after expressions of ‘impossibility’, ‘disbelief’, ‘hope’, ‘disregard’, ‘reproach’ and ‘wonder’]; yadyadyat vāvāvauū, ‘or else’, ‘whether’, Kāv.; Rājat.; [yadyadyat vāvāvauū, ‘or else’, is very often in commentators]; ‘however’, Bālar.; yadyadyat vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū, ‘if-or if’, Bhag.; yadyadyat bhūyasābhūyasābhūyas, ‘for the most part’, Divyāv.; yatyat satyamsatyamsatya, ‘certainly’, ‘indeed’, ‘of course’, Mṛcch.; Ratnāv.; yanyanyat nununū, with 1st pers., ‘what if I’, ‘let me’, Divyāv.)
