• yadyad
  • (nom. and acc. sg. n. and base in comp. of 3. yaya), who, which, what, whichever, whatever, that, RV. &c. &c. (with correlatives tadtad, tyadtyad, etadetad, idamidam, adasadas, tadtad etadetad, etadetad tyadtyad, idaṃidaṃ tadtad, tadtad idamidam, tādṛśatādṛśa, īdṛśaīdṛśa, īdṛśīdṛś, etāvadetāvad, by which it is oftener followed than preceded; or the correl. is dropped e.g. yasyas tutu nā̑rabhatenārabhate karmakarma kṣipramkṣipram bhavatibhavati nirdravyaḥnirdravyaḥ, ‘[he] indeed who does not begin work soon becomes poor’ R.; or the rel. is dropped e.g. andhakamandhakam bhartāraṃbhartāraṃ nana tyajettyajet sāsā mahā-satīmahāsatīmahāsatī, ‘she who does not desert a blind husband is a very faithful wife’ Vet. yadyad is often repeated to express ‘whoever’, ‘whatever’, ‘whichever’, e.g. yoyo yaḥyaḥ, ‘whatever man’; yāyā yāyā, ‘whatever woman’; yoyo yajyaj jayatijayati tasyatasya tattat, ‘whatever he wins [in war] belongs to him’ Mn. vii, 96; yadyad yadyad vadativadati tadtad tadtad bhavatibhavati, ‘whatever he says is true’, or the two relatives may be separated by hihi, and are followed by the doubled or single correl. tadtad e.g. upyateupyate yadyad dhidhi yadyad bījambījam tattat tadtad evaeva prarohatiprarohati, ‘whatever seed is sown, that even comes forth’ Mn. ix, 40; similar indefinite meanings are expressed by the relative joined with tadtad e.g. yasmaiyasmai tasmaitasmai, ‘to any one whatever’, esp. in yadvāyadvā tadvātadvā, ‘anything whatever’; or by yaḥyaḥ with kaścakaśca, kaścanakaścana, kaścitkaścit, or [in later language, not in Manumanu] ko'pikopi e.g. yaḥyaḥ kaścitkaścit, ‘whosoever’; yāniyāni kānikāni caca mitrāṇimitrāṇi, ‘any friends whatsoever’; yenayena kenā̑pykenāpy upā̑yenaupāyena, ‘by any means whatsoever.’ yadyad is joined with tvadtvad to express generalization e.g. śūdrāṃsśūdrāṃs tvadtvad yāṃsyāṃs tvadtvad, ‘either the Śūdraśūdras or anybody else’ ŚBr.; or immediately followed by a pers. pron. on which it lays emphasis e.g. yoyo 'hamham, ‘I that very person who’; yasyas tvaṃtvaṃ kathaṃkathaṃ vetthavettha, ‘how do you know?’ ŚBr.; it is also used in the sense of ‘si quis’ e.g. striyaṃstriyaṃ spṛśedspṛśed yaḥyaḥ, ‘should any one touch a woman.’ yadyad is also used without the copula e.g. andhoandho jaḍaḥjaḍaḥ pīṭha-sarpīpīṭhasarpīpīṭhasarpī saptatyāsaptatyā sthaviraścasthaviraśca yaḥyaḥ, ‘a blind man, an idiot, a cripple, and a man seventy years old’ Mn. viii, 394; sometimes there is a change of construction in such cases e.g. yeye caca mānuṣāḥmānuṣāḥ for mānuṣāṃś-camānuṣāṃścamānuṣāṃśca, Mn. x, 86; the nom. sg. n. yadyad is then often used without regard to gender or number and may be translated by ‘as regards’, ‘as for’, e.g. kṣatraṃkṣatraṃ vāvā etadetad vanaspatīnāṃvanaspatīnāṃ yanyan nyag-rodhaḥnyagrodhaḥnyagrodhaḥ, ‘as for the Nyag-rodhanyagrodhanyagrodha, it is certainly the prince among trees’ AitBr.; or by ‘that is to say’, ‘to wit’ e.g. tatotato devādevā etaṃetaṃ vajraṃvajraṃ dadṛśurdadṛśur yadyad apaḥapaḥ, ‘the gods then saw this thunderbolt, to wit, the water’ ŚBr. yadyad as an adv. conjunction generally = ‘that’, esp. after verbs of saying, thinking &c., often introducing an oratio directa with or without itiiti; itiiti yadyad, at the end of a sentence = ‘thinking that’, ‘under the impression that’ e.g. Ratnâv. ii, 2/8. yadyad also = ‘so that’, ‘in order that’, ‘wherefore’, ‘whence’, ‘as’, ‘in as much as’, ‘since’, ‘because’ [the correlative being tadtad, ‘therefore’], ‘when’, ‘if’ RV. &c. &c.; adhaadha yadyad, ‘even if’, ‘although’ RV. yadyad apiapi id., Megh. yadyad uu - evamevam, ‘as - so’ ŚvetUp.; yadyad utauta, ‘that’ Bālar.; ‘that is to say’, ‘scilicet’ Kāraṇḍ.; Divyâv.; yatyat kilakila, ‘that’ Prasannar.; yacyac caca, ‘if’, ‘that is to say’ Car.; yacyac ca-yaccayaccayac caca, ‘both - and’ Divyâv.; ‘that’ [accord. to Pāṇ. iii, 3, 148 after expressions of ‘impossibility’, ‘disbelief’, ‘hope’, ‘disregard’, ‘reproach’ and, ‘wonder’]; yadyad vāvā, ‘or else’, ‘whether’ Kāv.; Rājat.; [yadyad vāvā, ‘or else’, is very often in commentators]; ‘however’ Bālar.; yadyad vāvā - yadiyadi vāvā, ‘if-or if’ Bhag.; yadyad bhūyasābhūyasā, ‘for the most part’ Divyâv.; yatyat satyamsatyam, ‘certainly’, ‘indeed’, ‘of course’ Mṛcch.; Ratnâv.; yanyan nunu, with 1st pers., ‘what if I’, ‘let me’ Divyâv.)
