• katara mfn. (as, ā, at). (comparative of 2. ka; declined as a pronom., Gram. 236), who or which of two? whether of two? Analogously to katama above katara may occasionally be used to express ‘who or which of many?’ (e.g. katarasyāṃ diśi, in which quarter?), and may optionally be compounded with the word to which it refers (e.g. kataraḥ kaṭhaḥ or katara-kaṭhaḥkatara-kaṭhaḥkatarakaṭhaḥ) [246,3]
  • in negative sentences katara with cana = neither of the two (e.g. na kataraś-canakataraś-canakataraścana jigye), neither of the two was conquered, RV. &c.; [246,3]
  • katara [cf. Zd. katāra; Gk. πότερος, κότερος; Goth. hvathar; Eng. whether; Lat. uter; Old Germ. huedar; Slav. kotoryi.] [246,3]
