• katara/
  • mfn. (as, A, at). (comparative of 2. ka; declined as a pronom., Gram. 236), who or which of two? whether of two? Analogously to katama above katara may occasionally be used to express ‘who or which of many?’ (e.g. katarasyAM diSi, in which quarter?), and may optionally be compounded with the word to which it refers (e.g. kataraH kaWaH or katara-kaWaH) [246,3]
  • in negative sentences katara with cana = neither of the two (e.g. na kataraS-cana jigye), neither of the two was conquered, RV. &c.; [246,3]
