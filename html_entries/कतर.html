• katarakatara mfn.
    • (asasa, āāa, atatad). (comparative of 2. kaka; declined as a pronom., Gram. 236), who or which of two? whether of two? Analogously to katamakatama above katarakatara may occasionally be used to express ‘who or which of many?’ (e.g. katarasyāṃkatara diśidiś, in which quarter?), and may optionally be compounded with the word to which it refers (e.g. kataraḥkatara kaṭhaḥkaṭha or katara-kaṭhaḥkatarakaṭha) [246,3]
    • in negative sentences katarakatara with canacana = neither of the two (e.g. nana kataraś-canacana jigye), neither of the two was conquered, RV. &c. [246,3]
