• katarakatara
  • mfn. (asas, āā, atat). (comparative of 2. kaka; declined as a pronom., Gram. 236), who or which of two? whether of two? Analogously to katamakatama above katarakatara may occasionally be used to express ‘who or which of many?’ (e.g. katarasyāṃkatarasyāṃ diśidiśi, in which quarter?), and may optionally be compounded with the word to which it refers (e.g. kataraḥkataraḥ kaṭhaḥkaṭhaḥ or katara-kaṭhaḥkatarakaṭhaḥkatarakaṭhaḥ) [246,3]
  • in negative sentences katarakatara with canacana = neither of the two (e.g. nana kataraś-canakataraścanakataraścana jigyejigye), neither of the two was conquered, RV. &c.; [246,3]
