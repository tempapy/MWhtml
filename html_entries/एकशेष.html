• eka—śeṣa a m. the only remainder, Naiṣ.; Veṇīs.; Kathās. [229,3]
  • ‘the remaining of one’, (in Gr.) a term denoting that of two or more stems (alike in form and followed by the same termination) only one remains (e.g. the pl. vṛkṣās is the only remainder of vṛkṣas + vṛkṣas + vṛkṣas + ...), Pāṇ. i, 2, 64 ff. [229,3]
  • eka—śeṣa b mfn. of which only one is left, MBh. [1323,1]
