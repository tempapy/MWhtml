  • kiM—nara
  • m. ‘what sort of man?’ a mythical being with a human figure and the head of a horse (or with a horse's body and the head of a man, Śiś. iv, 38; originally perhaps a kind of monkey cf. vA-nara; in later times (like the Naranaras) reckoned among the Gandharvagandharvas or celestial choristers, and celebrated as musicians; also attached to the service of Kuberakubera; (with Jains) one of the eight orders of the Vyantaravyantaras), Mn.; MBh. &c. [283,1]

  • of Naranara (a son of Vibhīṣaṇavibhīṣaṇa), Rājat. i, 197 [283,1]
  • of the attendant of the fifteenth Arhatarhat of the present Avasarpiṇīavasarpiṇī, Jain. [283,1]
