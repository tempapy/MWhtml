  • kiṃkiṃkimki—naranara b m.
    • ‘what sort of man?’ a mythical being with a human figure and the head of a horse (or with a horse's body and the head of a man, Śiś. iv, 38; originally perhaps a kind of monkey cf. vā-naravānaravāvāsnara; in later times (like the Naras) reckoned among the Gandharvas or celestial choristers, and celebrated as musicians; also attached to the service of Kubera; (with Jains) one of the eight orders of the Vyantaras), Mn.; MBh. &c. [283,1]
    • N. of a prince, VP. [283,1]
    • of Nara (a son of Vibhīṣaṇa), Rājat. i, 197 [283,1]
    • of the attendant of the fifteenth Arhat of the present Avasarpiṇī, Jain. [283,1]
    • N. of a locality g. takṣaśilā̑ditakṣaśilatakṣaśilāadādiādīādin [283,1]
