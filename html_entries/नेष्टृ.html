• neṣṭṛneṣṭṛ
  • m. (prob. fr. √nīnī aor. stem neṣneṣ; but cf. Pāṇ. iii, 2, 135, Vārtt. 2 &c.) one of the chief officiating priests at a Somasoma sacrifice, he who leads forward the wife of the sacrificer and prepares the Surāsurā (Tvaṣṭṛtvaṣṭṛ so called, RV. i, 15, 3), RV.; Br.; ŚrS. &c. [569,3]
