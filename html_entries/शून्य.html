• śūnyaśūnya a mf(āāa)n.
    • empty, void (with vājinvājin = ‘a riderless horse’; with rājyarājya = ‘a kingless kingdom’), hollow, barren, desolate, deserted, Br. &c. &c. [1085,2]
    • empty i.e. vacant (as a look or stare), absent, absentminded, having no certain object or aim, distracted, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1085,2]
    • empty i.e. possessing nothing, wholly destitute, MBh.; Kathās. [1085,2]
    • wholly alone or solitary, having no friends or companions, R.; BhP. [1085,2]
    • void of, free from, destitute of (instr. or comp.), wanting, lacking, Kāv.; Kathās.; Pur.; Sarvad. non-existent, absent, missing, Kāv.; Pañcat. [1085,2]
    • vain, idle, unreal, nonsensical, R.; Rājat.; Sarvad. [1085,2]
    • void of results, ineffectual (a-śūnyaṃ-√kṛakṛ, ‘to effect’, ‘accomplish’), Śak.; Ratnâv. [1085,2]
    • free from sensitiveness or sensation (said of the skin), insensible, Bhpr. [1085,2]
    • bare, naked, MW. [1085,2]
    • guileless, innocent, ib. [1085,2]
    • indifferent, ib. [1085,2]
  • śūnyaśūnya n.
    • a void, vacuum, empty or deserted place, desert (śūnyeśūnyaśūnyā, in a lonely place), MBh.; R. &c. [1085,2]
    • (in phil.) vacuity, nonentity, absolute non-existence (esp. with Buddhists), IW. 83 n. 3; 105, n.4; MWB. 7 n. 1; 142 [1085,2]
    • N. of Brahma, MW. [1085,2]
    • (in arithm.) nought, a cypher, VarBṛS.; Gaṇit. (cf. IW. 183) [1085,2]
    • space, heaven, atmosphere, L. [1085,2]
    • a partic. phenomenon in the sky, L. [1085,2]
    • an earring (See next). [cf. Gk. κενός, κενεός; Aeol. κέννος.] [1085,2]
