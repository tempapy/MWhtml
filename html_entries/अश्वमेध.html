• a/Sva—meDa/
  • m. the horse-sacrifice (a celebrated ceremony, the antiquity of which reaches back to the Vedic period; the hymns, RV. i, 162 and 163 [= VS. xxii seqq.], referring to it, are however of comparatively late origin; in later times its efficacy was so exaggerated, that a hundred such sacrifices entitled the sacrificer to displace Indraindra from the dominion of Svargasvarga; kings who engaged in it spent enormous sums in gifts to the Brāhmans; it is said that the horse was sometimes not immolated, but kept bound during the ceremony), VS. xviii, 22; TS.; Ragh. &c., (cf. arkASvameDa/) [115,2]
  • a/Sva—meDa
  • (a/Sva-) m. N. of a descendant of Bharatabharata, RV. v, 27, 4-6. [115,2]
