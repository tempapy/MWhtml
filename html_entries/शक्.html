• śakśakśacśaś [Roots]
    • cl. 5. P. (Dhātup. xxvii, 15) śaknoti (pf. śaśāka, śekuḥśeku, RV. &c. &c. aor. aśakat, AV. &c. [Ved. also Pot. śakeyam and śakyāmśakya; Impv. śagdhi, śaktamśakta]; fut. śaktāśakta, or śakitāśakita Gr. ; śakṣyati, °teśaktaśakti, Br. &c. śakiṣyate, °teśaktaśakti Gr.; inf. -śaktaveśakśaktu, RV.; śaktumśaktu or śakitum Gr.),
    • to be strong or powerful, be able to or capable of or competent for (with acc. dat. or loc., rarely acc. of a verbal noun, or with an inf. in amama or tumtu; or with pr.p.; e.g. with grahaṇāyagrahaṇa or grahaṇegrahaṇagrahaṇi, ‘to be able to seize’; vadha-nirṇekamvadhanirṇekavadhanirṇeka a-śaknuvanaśaknuvata, ‘unable to atone for slaughter’; śakema vājino yamamyama, ‘may we be able to guide horses’; vī̑kṣitumvivī nana śaknoti, ‘he is not able to see’; pūrayan nana śaknoti, ‘he is not able to fill’), RV. &c. &c. (in these meanings ep. also śakyati, °teśaktaśakti, with inf. in tuṃ cf. Dhātup. xxvi, 78);
    • to be strong or exert one's self for another (dat.), aid, help, assist, RV. vii, 67, 5; 68, 8 &c.
    • to help to (dat. of thing), ib. ii, 2, 12; iv, 21, 10 &c. :
    • Pass. śakyateśakyatā (ep. also °tiśaktiśaktin), to be overcome or subdued, succumb, MBh.;
    • to yield, give way, ib.;
    • to be compelled or caused by any one (instr.) to (inf.), ib.;
    • to be able or capable or possible or practicable (with an inf. in pass. sense, e.g. tattat kartuṃkartu śakyateśakyatā, ‘that can be done’; sometimes with pass. p., e.g. nana śakyateśakyatā vāryamāṇaḥ, ‘he cannot be restrained’; or used impers., with or with out instr., e.g. yadiyadiyad [tvayātva] śakyateśakyatā, ‘if it can be done by thee’, ‘if it is possible’), Mn.; MBh. &c. :
    • Caus. śākayati (aor. aśīśakat), Gr.:
