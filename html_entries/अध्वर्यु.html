• aDvaryu/
  • m. one who institutes an Adhvaraadhvara any officiating priest [24,1]
  • a priest of a particular class (as distinguished from the hotf, the udgAtf, and the brahman classes. The Adhvaryuadhvaryu priests ‘had to measure the ground, to build the altar, to prepare the sacrificial vessels, to fetch wood and water, to light the fire, to bring the animal and immolate it’; whilst engaged in these duties, they had to repeat the hymns of the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda, hence that Vedaveda itself is also called Adhvaryuadhvaryu) [24,1]
  • pl. (aDvaryavas) the adherents of the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda [24,1]
  • aDvaryu/
  • f. (us) the wife of an Adhvaryuadhvaryu priest, Pāṇ. iv, 1, 66 Sch. [24,1]
