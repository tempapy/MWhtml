• paśupaśu—patipati
  • m. ‘lord of animals’ (or ‘lord of a servant named Paśupaśu’ or ‘lord of the soul’ RTL. 89), N. of the later Rudrarudra-Śivaśiva or of a similar deity (often associated in the Vedaveda with Bhavabhava, Śarvaśarva, Ugraugra, Rudrarudra, Mahā-devamahādevamahādeva, Īśānaīśāna and others who together with Bhīmabhīma are in later times regarded as manifestations of Rudrarudra), AV. &c. &c. [611,3]
  • of Agniagni.; TS.; ŚBr. [611,3]
  • of Śivaśiva, MBh. &c. (according to one legend every deity acknowledged himself to be a mere paśupaśu or animal when entreating Śivaśiva to destroy the Asuraasura Tri-puratripuratripura) [611,3]
  • of a lexicographer [611,3]
  • of a Scholiast &c. [611,3]
