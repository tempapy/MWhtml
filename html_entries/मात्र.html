  • mātramātra n.
    • an element, elementary matter, BhP. [804,2]
    • (ifc.) measure, quantity, sum, size, duration, measure of any kind (whether of height, depth, breadth, length, distance, time or number e.g. aṅgula-mātramaṅgulamātra, a finger's breadth, Pañcat.; artha-mātramarthamātraarthamātra, a certain sum of money, ib.; krośa-mātrekrośamātramātrā, at the distance of a Kos, Hit. ; māsa-mātremāsamātramātrā, in a month, Lāṭy.; śata-mātramśatamātra, a hundred in number, Kathās.) [804,2]
    • the full or simple measure of anything, the whole or totality, the one thing and no more, often = nothing but, entirely, only (e.g. rāja-mātramrājamātrarājamātra, the whole class of kings, ŚāṅkhŚr.; bhaya-m°bhayamātra, all that may be called danger, any danger, VarBṛS.; rati-m°ratimātra, nothing but sensuality, Mn.; śabda-mātreṇaśabdamātraśabdamātra, only by a sound, Śak.) [804,3]
