• 1. preṣpreṣ
    • cl. 1. Ā. preṣate, to go, move, Dhātup. xvi, 18 (v.l. hreṣhreṣ). [712,3]
  • 2. prȇṣpreṣpraprāiṣīṣ
    • (pra-√iṣpraiṣ) P. Ā. prȇṣyatipraprā, °tetatā (Ved. inf. prȇṣepreṣapreṣpraprāiṣaiṣiiṣīṣaīṣāīṣ, Pāṇ. iii, 4, 9 Sch.; ind.p. pra͡iṣampraiṣampraiṣapraprāeṣaaiṣa s.v.),
    • to drive on, urge, impel, send forth, RV.; MBh.;
    • to invite, summon, call upon (another priest to commence a recitation or a ceremony [acc.] e.g. sāmasāma prȇṣyatipraprā, ‘he calls upon to commence the recitation of a Sāman’; esp. Impv. prȇṣyapreṣyapraprāiṣya, ‘call upon to recite or offer [acc. or gen.] to [dat.]’), ŚBr.; KātyŚr. (cf. Pāṇ. ii, 3, 61; viii, 2, 91) :
    • Caus. prȇṣayatipraprā, to hurl, fling, cast, throw, MBh.; R.; Bhaṭṭ.;
    • to turn or direct the eyes, Śak. ii, 2 (v.l. prȇrayantyā̑praprāaā);
    • to send forth, dismiss, dispatch, MBh.; Kāv. &c.
    • to send into exile, banish, R.; Kathās.;
    • to send word, send a message to a person (gen.), R. [712,3]
  • 3. prȇṣpreṣpraprāiṣīṣ f.
