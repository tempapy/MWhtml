• parvata mfn. (fr. parvan cf. Pāṇ. v, 2, 122, Vārtt. 10, Pat.) knotty, rugged (said of mountains), RV.; AV. (according to, ĀpŚr. Sch. = parutka, parva-vatparva-vatparvavat) [609,1]
  • parvata m. a mountain, mountain-range, height, hill, rock (often personified; ifc. f(ā). ), RV. &c. &c. [609,1]
  • parvata m. an artificial mound or heap (of grain, salt, silver, gold &c. presented to Brāhmans cf. -dānaparvata-dānadāna) [609,1]
  • the number 7 (from the 7 principal mountain-ranges), Sūryas. [609,1]
  • a fragment of rock, a stone (adrayaḥ parvatāḥ, the stones for pressing Soma), RV. [609,1]
  • a (mountain-like) cloud, ib. (cf. Naigh. i, 10) [609,1]
  • a tree, L. [609,1]
  • a species of pot-herb, L. [609,1]
  • a species of fish (Silurus Pabda), L. [609,1]
  • N. of a Vasu, Hariv. [609,1]
  • of a Ṛṣi (associated with Nārada and messenger of the gods, supposed author of RV. viii, 12; ix, 104, 105, where he has the patr. Kāṇva and Kāśyapa), MBh.; Kathās. [609,1]
  • of a son of Paurṇamāsa (son of Marīci and Sambhūti), MārkP. [609,1]
  • of a minister of king Purū-ravaspurū-ravaspurūravas, Vikr. [609,1]
  • of a monkey, R. [609,1]
  • of one of the 10 religious orders founded by Śaṃkarâcārya's pupils (whose members add the word parvata to their names), W. [609,1]
