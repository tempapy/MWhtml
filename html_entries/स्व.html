• sva 1 mf(ā)n. own, one's own, my own, thy own, his own, her own, our own, their own &c. (referring to all three persons accord. to context, often ibc., but generally declinable like the pronominal sarva e.g. svasmai dat. svasmāt abl. [optionally in abl. loc. sing. nom. pl. e.g. taṃ svād āsyād asṛjat, ‘he created him from his own mouth’ Mn. i, 94]; and always like śiva when used substantively [see below]; sometimes used loosely for ‘my’, ‘thy’, ‘his’, ‘our’ [e.g. rājā bhrātaraṃ sva-gṛhamsva-gṛhamsvagṛham prȇṣayām-āsapreṣayām-āsapreṣayāmāsa, ‘the king sent his brother to his (i.e. the brother's) house’]; in the oblique cases it is used as a reflexive pronoun = ātman, e.g. svaṃ dūṣayati, ‘he defiles himself’; svaṃ nindanti, ‘they blame themselves’), RV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
  • sva m. one's self, the Ego, the human soul, W. [1275,1]
  • N. of Viṣṇu, MBh. [1275,1]
  • a man of one's own people or tribe, a kinsman, relative, relation, friend (svāḥ, ‘one's own relations’, ‘one's own people’), AV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
  • sva n. (ifc. f(ā). ) one's self, the Ego (e.g. svaṃ ca brahma ca, ‘the Ego and Brahman’) [1275,1]
  • one's own goods, property, wealth, riches (in this sense said to be also m.), RV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
  • the second astrological mansion, VarBṛS. [1275,1]
  • (in alg.) plus or the affirmative quantity, W. (N.B. in the following comp. o° own stands for one's own.) [1275,1]
  • sva [cf. Gk. ἕ, ὅ, σφός; Lat. se, sovos, suus; Goth. sik; Germ. sich &c.] [1275,1]
  • sva Nom. P. svati (pf. svām-āsasvām-āsasvāmāsa) = sva ivā̑carati, he acts like himself or his kindred, Vop. xxi, 7. [1278,1]
