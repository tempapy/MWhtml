• svasva 1 mf(āāa)n.
    • own, one's own, my own, thy own, his own, her own, our own, their own &c. (referring to all three persons accord. to context, often ibc., but generally declinable like the pronominal sarvasarva e.g. svasmaisva dat. svasmātsva abl. [optionally in abl. loc. sing. nom. pl. e.g. taṃtamta svādsvādsva āsyādāsya asṛjat, ‘he created him from his own mouth’ Mn. i, 94]; and always like śivaśiva when used substantively [see below]; sometimes used loosely for ‘my’, ‘thy’, ‘his’, ‘our’ [e.g. rājārājarāj bhrātaraṃ sva-gṛhamsvagṛhasvagṛhagṛh prȇṣayām-āsapraprāāsa, ‘the king sent his brother to his (i.e. the brother's) house’]; in the oblique cases it is used as a reflexive pronoun = ātmanātman, e.g. svaṃsva dūṣayatidūṣayat, ‘he defiles himself’; svaṃsva nindanti, ‘they blame themselves’), RV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
  • svasva m.
    • one's self, the Ego, the human soul, W. [1275,1]
    • N. of Viṣṇu, MBh. [1275,1]
    • a man of one's own people or tribe, a kinsman, relative, relation, friend (svāḥsvāssvasvāsusū, ‘one's own relations’, ‘one's own people’), AV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
  • svasva n.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ) one's self, the Ego (e.g. svaṃsva caca brahmabrahma caca, ‘the Ego and Brahman’) [1275,1]
    • one's own goods, property, wealth, riches (in this sense said to be also m.), RV. &c. &c. [1275,1]
    • the second astrological mansion, VarBṛS. [1275,1]
    • (in alg.) plus or the affirmative quantity, W. (N.B. in the following comp. o° own stands for one's own.) [1275,1]
