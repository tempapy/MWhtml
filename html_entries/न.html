• 1. nana
    • the dental nasal (found at the beginning of words and before or after dental consonants as well as between vowels; subject to conversion into ṇaṇa, Pāṇ. viii, 4, 1-39). [523,1]
  • 2. nana ind.
    • not, no, nor, neither, RV. (nānāna, x, 34, 8) &c. &c. (as well in simple negation as in wishing, requesting and commanding, except in prohibition before an Impv. or an augmentless aor. [cf. 1. māmāma]; in successive sentences or clauses either simply repeated e.g. Mn. iv, 34; or strengthened by another particle, esp. at the second place or further on in the sentence e.g. by uuū [cf. nȏnonunanāuū], utauta, apiapiap, cā̑picāpincaapiapāpiāpī, vāvāvauū, vā̑pivāpivāpīvāpinvavāapiapāpiāpī or athaatha vāvāvauū, RV. i, 170, 1; 151, 9; Nal. iii, 24, &c. it may even be replaced by caca, vāvāvauū, apiapiap caca, apiapiap vāvāvauū, &c. alone, as, Mn. ii, 98; Nal. i, 14, &c. often joined with other particles, beside those mentioned above esp. with a following tutu, tvtu evaeva, tvtu evaeva tutu, cȇdcedcaid q.v., khalukhalu q.v., haha [cf. g. cā̑dicādicaadādiādin and, Pāṇ. viii, 1, 31] &c. before round or collective numbers and after any numeral in the instr. or abl. it expresses deficiency e.g. ekayāekāeka nana viṃśativiṃśati, not 20 by 1 i.e. 19 ŚBr.; pañcabhir nana catvāri śatāni, 395 ib.; with another nana or an aa priv. it generally forms a strong affirmation [cf. Vām. v, 1, 9] e.g. nȇyaṃneyananāiyamī nana vakṣyati, she will most certainly declare, Śak. iii, 9; nā̑daṇḍyonanāadaṇḍya 'sti, he must certainly be punished, Mn. viii, 335; it may also, like aa, form compounds, Vām. v, 2, 13 [cf. below]) [523,1]
    • that not, lest, for fear lest (with Pot.), MBh.; R.; Daś. &c. [523,1]
    • like, as, as it were (only in Veda and later artificial language, e.g. gaurogaura nana tṛṣitaḥtṛṣita pibapiba, drink like [lit. ‘not’ i.e. ‘although not being’] a thirsty deer; in this sense it does not coalesce metrically with a following vowel). [523,1]
