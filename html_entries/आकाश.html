• ā-kāśaākāśaākāśa m.
    • (Ved.) or (later) n. (ifc. f(āāa). ) a free or open space, vacuity, AitBr.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [127,1]
    • the ether, sky or atmosphere, Naigh.; ŚBr.; Mn. &c. [127,1]
  • ā-kāśaākāśaākāśa n.
    • (in philos.) the subtle and ethereal fluid (supposed to fill and pervade the universe and to be the peculiar vehicle of life and of sound), Vedāntas. &c. [127,1]
    • Brahma (as identical with ether), L. [127,1]
    • = ākāśa-bhāṣitaākāśabhāṣitaākāśabhāṣita below Comm. on Śak. [127,1]
