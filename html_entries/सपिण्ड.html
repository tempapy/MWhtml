• sasa—piṇḍapiṇḍa
  • &c. See s.v. [1148,2]
  • sa-piṇḍasapiṇḍasapiṇḍa
  • m. ‘having the same Piṇḍapiṇḍa’, a kinsman connected by the offering of the Piṇḍapiṇḍa (q.v.) to certain deceased ancestors at the Śrāddhaśrāddha (q.v.; the kinship is through six generations in an ascending and descending line, or through a man's father, father's father, father's grandfather; mother, mother's father, mother's grandfather; son, son's son, son's grandson; daughter, daughter's son &c.; and also includes father's mother, father's grandmother &c., also father's brothers and sisters, mother's brothers and sisters, and several others), GṛŚrS.; Gaut.; Mn. v, 60; MBh. &c. (RTL. 285; 286 IW. 248; 266). [1149,1]
