• ghagha
  • the 4th consonant of the Sanskṛt alphabet (aspirate of the preceding). [375,1]
  • ghagha
  • ind. (used to lay stress on a word) at least, surely, verily, indeed, especially (= Gk. γε), RV.; AV. v, 13, 10 & 11; vi, 1, 3. In the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā the final vowel is generally lengthened (ghāghā cf. Pāṇ. vi, 3, 133) [375,1]
  • as a rule it is preceded by other particles (utauta, utouto, utauta vāvā, cidcid, nana, vāvā) or by a pronoun or a preposition [375,1]
  • it is also found between ivaiva and idid [375,1]
  • or between ivaiva and idid ahaaha, or between vāvā and idid [375,1]
  • sometimes it occurs in the clause which depends on a conditional or relative sentence (e.g. āā ghāghā gamadgamad yadiyadi śravatśravat, ‘he will surely come when he hears’ RV. i, 30, 8), i, 161, 8; viii, 46, 4. [375,1]
  • ghagha
  • mfn. (√hanhan) ifc. ‘striking, killing’ cf. jīva-jīvajīvagha, tāḍa-tāḍatāḍagha, pāṇi-pāṇipāṇigha, rāja-rājarājagha, &c. (cf. also parighaparigha) [375,1]
  • ghagha
  • m. a rattling or gurgling or tinkling sound, L. [375,1]
  • a bell, L. [375,1]
