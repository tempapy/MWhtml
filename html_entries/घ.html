• Ga
  • the 4th consonant of the Sanskṛt alphabet (aspirate of the preceding). [375,1]
  • Ga
  • ind. (used to lay stress on a word) at least, surely, verily, indeed, especially (= Gk. γε), RV.; AV. v, 13, 10 & 11; vi, 1, 3. In the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā the final vowel is generally lengthened (GA cf. Pāṇ. vi, 3, 133) [375,1]
  • as a rule it is preceded by other particles (uta/, uto/, uta/ vA, cid, na/, vA) or by a pronoun or a preposition [375,1]
  • it is also found between iva and i/d [375,1]
  • or between iva and i/d aha, or between vA and i/d [375,1]
  • sometimes it occurs in the clause which depends on a conditional or relative sentence (e.g. A/ GA gamad ya/di Sra/vat, ‘he will surely come when he hears’ RV. i, 30, 8), i, 161, 8; viii, 46, 4. [375,1]
