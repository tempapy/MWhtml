• 1. ghagha
    • the 4th consonant of the Sanskṛt alphabet (aspirate of the preceding). [375,1]
  • 2. ghagha ind.
    • (used to lay stress on a word) at least, surely, verily, indeed, especially (= Gk. γε), RV.; AV. v, 13, 10 & 11; vi, 1, 3. In the Saṃhitā the final vowel is generally lengthened (ghāghāgha cf. Pāṇ. vi, 3, 133) [375,1]
    • as a rule it is preceded by other particles (utauta, uto, utauta vāvāvauū, cidcid, nana, vāvāvauū) or by a pronoun or a preposition [375,1]
    • it is also found between ivaiva and idid [375,1]
    • or between ivaiva and ididit ahaaha, or between vāvāvauū and idid [375,1]
    • sometimes it occurs in the clause which depends on a conditional or relative sentence (e.g. āāaās ghāghāgha gamad yadiyadiyad śravat, ‘he will surely come when he hears’ RV. i, 30, 8), i, 161, 8; viii, 46, 4. [375,1]
  • 3. ghagha mfn.
    • (√hanhan) ifc. ‘striking, killing’ cf. jīva-jīvagha, tāḍa-tāḍaghatāḍagha, pāṇi-pāṇighapāṇigha, rāja-rājagharājagha, &c. (cf. also parighaparigha) [375,1]
  • 4. ghagha m.
    • a rattling or gurgling or tinkling sound, L. [375,1]
    • a bell, L. [375,1]
