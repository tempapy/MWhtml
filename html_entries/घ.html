• gha the 4th consonant of the Sanskṛt alphabet (aspirate of the preceding). [375,1]
  • gha ind. (used to lay stress on a word) at least, surely, verily, indeed, especially (= Gk. γε), RV.; AV. v, 13, 10 & 11; vi, 1, 3. In the Saṃhitā the final vowel is generally lengthened (ghā cf. Pāṇ. vi, 3, 133) [375,1]
  • as a rule it is preceded by other particles (uta, uto, uta vā, cid, na, vā) or by a pronoun or a preposition [375,1]
  • it is also found between iva and id [375,1]
  • or between iva and id aha, or between vā and id [375,1]
  • sometimes it occurs in the clause which depends on a conditional or relative sentence (e.g. ā ghā gamad yadi śravat, ‘he will surely come when he hears’ RV. i, 30, 8), i, 161, 8; viii, 46, 4. [375,1]
  • gha mfn. (√han) ifc. ‘striking, killing’ cf. jīva-jīvajīva-gha, tāḍa-tāḍatāḍa-gha, pāṇi-pāṇipāṇi-gha, rāja-rājarāja-gha, &c. (cf. also parigha) [375,1]
  • gha m. a rattling or gurgling or tinkling sound, L. [375,1]
  • a bell, L. [375,1]
