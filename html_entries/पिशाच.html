• piśācapiśāca
  • m. (ifc. f(āā). ) N. of a class of demons (possibly so called either from their fondness for flesh [piśapiśa for piśitapiśita] or from their yellowish appearance; they were perhaps originally a personification of the ignis fatuus; they are mentioned in the Vedaveda along with Asuraasuras and Rākṣasarākṣasas See also, Mn. xii, 44; in later times they are the children of Krodhākrodhā cf. IW. 276) [628,2]
  • a fiend, ogre, demon, imp, malevolent or devilish being, AV. &c. &c. (ifc. ‘a devil of a -’ Kād.) [628,2]
  • N. of a Rakṣasrakṣas, R. [628,2]
