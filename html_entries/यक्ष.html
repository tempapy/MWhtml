• yakṣayakṣa
  • n. a living supernatural being, spiritual apparition, ghost, spirit, RV.; AV.; VS.; Br.; GṛŚrS. (accord. to some native Comms. = yajñayajña, pujāpujā, pūjitapūjita &c.) [838,2]
  • yakṣayakṣa
  • m. N. of a class of semi-divine beings (attendants of Kuberakubera, exceptionally also of Viṣṇuviṣṇu; described as sons of Pulastyapulastya, of Pulahapulaha, of Kaśyapakaśyapa, of Khasākhasā or Krodhākrodhā; also as produced from the feet of Brahmābrahmā; though generally regarded as beings of a benevolent and inoffensive disposition, like the Yakṣayakṣa in Kālidāsakālidāsa's Megha-dūtameghadūtameghadūta, they are occasionally classed with Piśācapiśācas and other malignant spirits, and sometimes said to cause demoniacal possession; as to their position in the Buddhist system See MWB. 206, 218), Up.; GṛS.; Mn.; MBh. &c. [838,2]
  • (with Jainajainas) a subdivision of the Vyantaravyantaras [838,2]
  • N. of Kuberakubera, VarYogay. [838,2]
  • of a Munimuni, R. [838,2]
  • of a son of Śvaphalkaśvaphalka, VP. [838,2]
  • of Indraindra's palace, L. [838,2]
  • a dog, L. [838,2]
