• yakṣa n. a living supernatural being, spiritual apparition, ghost, spirit, RV.; AV.; VS.; Br.; GṛŚrS. (accord. to some native Comms. = yajña, pujā, pūjita &c.) [838,2]
  • yakṣa m. N. of a class of semi-divine beings (attendants of Kubera, exceptionally also of Viṣṇu; described as sons of Pulastya, of Pulaha, of Kaśyapa, of Khasā or Krodhā; also as produced from the feet of Brahmā; though generally regarded as beings of a benevolent and inoffensive disposition, like the Yakṣa in Kālidāsa's Megha-dūtamegha-dūtameghadūta, they are occasionally classed with Piśācas and other malignant spirits, and sometimes said to cause demoniacal possession; as to their position in the Buddhist system See MWB. 206, 218), Up.; GṛS.; Mn.; MBh. &c. [838,2]
  • (with Jainas) a subdivision of the Vyantaras [838,2]
  • N. of Kubera, VarYogay. [838,2]

  • of a son of Śvaphalka, VP. [838,2]
  • of Indra's palace, L. [838,2]
  • a dog, L. [838,2]
