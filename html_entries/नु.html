• 1. nununū ind.
    • (in, RV. also nūnūnu; esp. at the beginning of a verse, where often = nununū + uuū), now, still, just, at once [567,1]
    • so now, now then, RV.; AV.; Br.; Up. [567,1]
    • indeed, certainly, surely, RV. &c. &c. [567,1]
    • cf. Pāṇ. iii, 2, 121 Sch. (often connected with other particles, esp. with negatives e.g. nahinahi nununū, ‘by no means’, nakirnakis nununū, ‘no one or nothing at all’, māmāmamās nununū, ‘in order that surely not’ ; often also ghagha nununū, haha nununū, ininit nununū, nununū kamkamka &c. [nūnūnu citcitcid, either ‘for ever, evermore; at once, forthwith’ or, ‘never, never more’; so also nūnūnu alone, RV. vii, 100, 1]; with relat. = -cunque or -soever; sometimes it lays stress upon a preceding word, esp. an interr. pronoun or particle, and is then often connected with khalukhalu, RV. &c. &c. it is also employed in questions, esp. in sentences of two or more clauses [cf. Pāṇ. viii, 2, 98; Kāś.] where nununū is either always repeated [Śak. vi, 9] or omitted in the first place [ib. i, 8] or in the second place and further replaced by svidsvid, yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū &c., and strengthened by vāvāvauū, athaatha vāvāvauū &c.)
