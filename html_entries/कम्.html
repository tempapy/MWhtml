• 1. kamkamka ind.
    • (Gk. κεν) well (opposed to a-kamakaakamka, ‘ill’), TS.; ŚBr. &c. [251,3]
    • a particle placed after the word to which it belongs with an affirmative sense, ‘yes’, ‘well’ (but this sense is generally so weak that Indian grammarians are perhaps right in enumerating kamkamka among the expletives, Nir.; it is often found attached to a dat. case, giving to that case a stronger meaning, and is generally placed at the end of the Pāda, e.g. ajījana oṣadhīroṣadhioṣadhī bhojanāyabhojana kamkamka, thou didst create the plants for actual food, RV. v, 83, 10), RV.; AV.; TS. v [251,3]
    • kamkamka is also used as an enclitic with the particles nununū, sususū, and hihihī (but is treated in the Pada-pāṭha as a separate word; in this connection kamkamka has no accent but once, AV. vi, 110, 1), RV.; AV. [251,3]
    • a particle of interrogation (like kadkad and kimkim), RV. x, 52, 3 [251,3]
    • (sometimes, like kimkim and kadkad, at the beginning of compounds) marking the strange or unusual character of anything or expressing reproach, L. [251,3]
    • head, L. [252,1]
    • food, Nir. [252,1]
    • water, Nir.; Nigh. [252,1]
    • happiness, bliss, L. [252,1]
