• kam ind. (Gk. κεν) well (opposed to a-kama-kamakam, ‘ill’), TS.; ŚBr. &c. [251,3]
  • a particle placed after the word to which it belongs with an affirmative sense, ‘yes’, ‘well’ (but this sense is generally so weak that Indian grammarians are perhaps right in enumerating kam among the expletives, Nir.; it is often found attached to a dat. case, giving to that case a stronger meaning, and is generally placed at the end of the Pāda, e.g. ajījana oṣadhīr bhojanāya kam, thou didst create the plants for actual food, RV. v, 83, 10), RV.; AV.; TS. v [251,3]
  • kam is also used as an enclitic with the particles nu, su, and hi (but is treated in the Pada-pāṭhapada-pāṭhapadapāṭha as a separate word; in this connection kam has no accent but once, AV. vi, 110, 1), RV.; AV. [251,3]
  • a particle of interrogation (like kad and kim), RV. x, 52, 3 [251,3]
  • (sometimes, like kim and kad, at the beginning of compounds) marking the strange or unusual character of anything or expressing reproach, L. [251,3]
