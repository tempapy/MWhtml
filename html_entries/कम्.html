• kamkam
  • ind. (Gk. κεν) well (opposed to a-kamakamakam, ‘ill’), TS.; ŚBr. &c. [251,3]
  • a particle placed after the word to which it belongs with an affirmative sense, ‘yes’, ‘well’ (but this sense is generally so weak that Indian grammarians are perhaps right in enumerating kamkam among the expletives, Nir.; it is often found attached to a dat. case, giving to that case a stronger meaning, and is generally placed at the end of the Pādapāda, e.g. ajījanaajījana oṣadhīroṣadhīr bhojanāyabhojanāya kamkam, thou didst create the plants for actual food, RV. v, 83, 10), RV.; AV.; TS. v [251,3]
  • kamkam is also used as an enclitic with the particles nunu, susu, and hihi (but is treated in the Pada-pāṭhapadapāṭhapadapāṭha as a separate word; in this connection kamkam has no accent but once, AV. vi, 110, 1), RV.; AV. [251,3]
  • a particle of interrogation (like kadkad and kimkim), RV. x, 52, 3 [251,3]
  • (sometimes, like kimkim and kadkad, at the beginning of compounds) marking the strange or unusual character of anything or expressing reproach, L. [251,3]
