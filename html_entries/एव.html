• 1. evaeva ind.
    • (in the Saṃhitā also evāeva) (√iiī, Uṇ. i, 152; fr. pronom. base eeaāi, BRD., probably connected with 2. evaeva), so, just so, exactly so (in the sense of the later evamevameva), RV.; AV. [232,2]
    • indeed, truly, really (often at the beginning of a verse in conjunction with other particles, as idid, hihihī), RV. [232,2]
    • (in its most frequent use of strengthening the idea expressed by any word, evaeva must be variously rendered by such adverbs as) just, exactly, very, same, only, even, alone, merely, immediately on, still, already, &c. (e.g. tvam evaeva yantā nā̑nyonanāanyaanī 'sti pṛthivyāmpṛthivipṛthivī, thou alone art a charioteer, no other is on earth, i.e. thou art the best charioteer, MBh. iii, 2825; tāvatīmtāvat evaeva rātrimrātri, just so long as a night; evamevameva evaeva or tatha͡ivatathāeva, exactly so, in this manner only; in the same manner as above; tena͡ivatenaeva mantreṇamantra, with the same Mantra as above; apaḥapasapaap spṛṣṭva͡ivaeva, by merely touching water; tāntatat evaeva, these very persons; nana cirādcirādcirātcira evaeva, in no long time at all; japyena͡ivaeva, by sole repetition; abhuktva͡ivaeva, even without having eaten; itiiti vadann evaeva, at the very moment of saying so; sasa jīvann evaeva, he while still living, &c.), RV. &c., MBh. &c. [232,2]
    • (sometimes, esp. in connection with other adverbs, evaeva is a mere expletive without any exact meaning and not translatable e.g. tvtu evaeva, ca͡ivacaeva, evaeva caca, &c. according to native authorities evaeva implies emphasis, affirmation, detraction, diminution, command, restrainment); [232,2]
