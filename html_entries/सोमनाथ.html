• somasoma—nāthanātha
  • m. N. of various scholars, Cat.; Col. [1250,1]
  • somasoma—nāthanātha
  • n. N. of a celebrated Liṅgaliṅga of Śivaśiva and of the place where it was set up by the god Somasoma (in the town described below; it was one of the 12 great Liṅgaliṅga temples of India held in especial veneration [cf. IW. 322 n. 1], and was so famed for its splendour and wealth that it attracted the celebrated Mahmūd of Ghaznī, A.D. 1024, who, under pretext of destroying its idols, carried off its treasures along with its renowned gates), Vcar.; Vop.; Cat.; Col. &c. [1250,1]
