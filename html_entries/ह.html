• 1. haha
    • the thirty-third and last consonant of the Nāgarī alphabet (in Pāṇini's system belonging to the guttural class, and usually pronounced like the English h in hard; it is not an original letter, but is mostly derived from an older gh, rarely from dh or bh). [1286,1]

  • 3. haha ind.
    • (prob. orig. identical with 2. ghagha, and used as a particle for emphasizing a preceding word, esp. if it begins a sentence closely connected with another; very frequent in the Brāhmaṇas and Sūtras, and often translatable by) indeed, assuredly, verily, of course, then &c. (often with other particles e.g. with tvtu evaeva, uuū, smasma, vaivaiuū &c. nana haha, ‘not indeed’; also with interrogatives and relatives e.g. yadyadyat dhadha, ‘when indeed’; kadkadkat dhadha, ‘what then?’ sometimes with impf. or pf. [cf. Pāṇ. iii, 2, 116]; in later language very commonly used as a mere expletive, esp. at the end of a verse), RV.; &c. [1286,1]
