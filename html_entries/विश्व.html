• viśvaviśva mf(āāa)n.
    • (prob. fr. √1. viśviś, to pervade cf. Uṇ. i, 151; declined as a pron. like sarvasarva, by which it is superseded in the Brāhmaṇas and later language) all, every, every one [992,2]
    • whole, entire, universal, RV. &c. &c. [992,2]
    • all-pervading or all-containing, omnipresent (applied to Viṣṇu-Kṛṣṇa, the soul, intellect &c.), Up.; MBh. &c. [992,2]
  • viśvaviśva m.
    • (in phil.) the intellectual faculty or (accord. to some) the faculty which perceives individuality or the individual underlying the gross body (sthūla-śarīra-vyaṣṭy-upahitasthūlaśarīraupahita), Vedântas. [992,2]
    • N. of a class of gods cf. below [992,2]
    • N. of the number ‘thirteen’ Gol. [992,2]
    • of a class of deceased ancestors, MārkP. [992,2]
    • of a king, MBh. [992,2]
    • of a well-known dictionary = viśva-prakāśaviśvaprakāśaviśvaprakāśa [992,2]
    • pl. (viśveviśvaviśvāviśvi, with or scil. devāsdevadevā cf. viśve-devaviśvedevadeva, p.995) ‘all the gods collectively’ or the ‘All-gods’ (a partic. class of gods, forming one of the 9 Gaṇas enumerated under gaṇadevatāgaṇadevatā q.v.; accord. to the Viṣṇu and other Purāṇas they were sons of Viśvā, daughter of Dakṣa, and their names are as follow, 1. Vasu, 2. Satya, 3. Kratu, 4. Dakṣa, 5. Kāla, 6. Kāma, 7. Dhṛti, 8. Kuru, 9. Purū-ravas, 10. Mādravas [?]; two others are added by some, viz. 11. Rocaka or Locana, 12. Dhvani [or Dhūri; or this may make 13] : they are particularly worshipped at Śrāddhas and at the Vaiśvadeva ceremony [RTL. 416]; moreover accord. to Manu [iii, 90, 121], offerings should be made to them daily - these privileges having been bestowed on them by Brahmā and the Pitṛs, as a reward for severe austerities they had performed on the Himâlaya: sometimes it is difficult to decide whether the expression viśveviśvaviśvāviśvi devāḥ refers to all the gods or to the particular troop of deities described above), RV. &c. &c. [992,2]
