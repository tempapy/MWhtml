• sāṃnāyya n. (fr. saṃ-√nīsaṃ-√nīsaṃ√nī) any, substance mixed with clarified butter &c. and offered as a burnt offering or oblation, (esp.) a partic. offering of the Agni-hotṛagni-hotṛagnihotṛs (said to consist of milk taken from a cow on the evening of the new moon, mixed on the next day with other milk and offered with clarified butter), TS.; Br.; KātyŚr. [1203,3]
