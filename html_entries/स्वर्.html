• 1. svarsvar
    • (= √sursur) cl. 10. P. svarayati, to find fault, blame, censure, Dhātup. xxxv, 11. [1281,1]
  • 2. svarsvar
    • (prob. = a lost √sursur; cf. √svṛsvṛ) cl. 1. P. svarati, Caus. svarayati, to shine. [1281,1]
  • 4. svarsvar ind.
    • (in Yajur-veda also suvarsuvar) (used in Veda as nom. acc. loc., or gen., in Naiṣ. vi, 99 also as abl.; from the weak base sūrsūr the RV. forms the gen. sūrassūra and the dat. sūresūrasūri [iv, 3, 8]), the sun, sunshine, light, lustre, RV.; AV.; VS. [1281,1]
    • bright space or sky, heaven (as distinguished from divdiv, which is regarded as the vault above it; often ‘heaven’ as a paradise and as the abode of the gods and the Blest, in AV. also of the Asuras; svaḥsvaḥsvasūsvar prayātaḥprayāta, ‘gone to heaven’ i.e. ‘departed this life’), RV. &c. &c. [1281,2]
    • the space above the sun or between the sun and the polar star, the region of the planets and constellations (regarded as the 3rd of the 7 worlds [see lokaloka] and the 3rd of the three Vyāhṛtis [i.e. bhurbhurbhu bhuvaḥbhuvasbhuvabhū svaḥsvaḥsvasūsvar]; svarsvar is pronounced after omom and before the Gāyatrī by every Brāhman on beginning his daily prayers), Mn.; MBh. &c. [1281,2]
    • water, Naigh. i, 12 [1281,2]
    • N. of Śiva, MBh. [1281,2]
  • svarsvar
    • [cf. Gk. ἠέλιος, ἤλιος; Lat. sol; Lith. sáule; Goth. sauil; Angl.Sax. sôl.] [1281,2]
