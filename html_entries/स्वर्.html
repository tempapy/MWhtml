  • svarsvar
  • (prob. = a lost √sursur; cf. √svṛsvṛ) cl. 1. P. svaratisvarati, Caus. svarayatisvarayati, to shine. [1281,1]
  • svarsvar
  • ind. (in Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda also suvarsuvar) (used in Vedaveda as nom. acc. loc., or gen., in Naiṣ. vi, 99 also as abl.; from the weak base sūrsūr the RV. forms the gen. sūrassūras and the dat. sūresūre [iv, 3, 8]), the sun, sunshine, light, lustre, RV.; AV.; VS. [1281,1]
  • bright space or sky, heaven (as distinguished from divdiv, which is regarded as the vault above it; often ‘heaven’ as a paradise and as the abode of the gods and the Blest, in AV. also of the Asuraasuras; svaḥsvaḥ prayātaḥprayātaḥ, ‘gone to heaven’ i.e. ‘departed this life’), RV. &c. &c. [1281,2]
  • the space above the sun or between the sun and the polar star, the region of the planets and constellations (regarded as the 3rd of the 7 worlds [see lokaloka] and the 3rd of the three Vyāhṛtivyāhṛtis [i.e. bhurbhur bhuvaḥbhuvaḥ svaḥsvaḥ]; svarsvar is pronounced after omom and before the Gāyatrīgāyatrī by every Brāhman on beginning his daily prayers), Mn.; MBh. &c. [1281,2]
  • water, Naigh. i, 12 [1281,2]
  • N. of Śivaśiva, MBh. [1281,2]
