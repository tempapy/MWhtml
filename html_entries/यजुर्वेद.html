• yajuryajur—vedaveda
  • m. ‘the sacrificial Vedaveda’, the collective body of sacred Mantramantras or texts which constitute the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda (these Mantramantras, though often consisting of the prose Yajusyajus, are frequently identical with the Mantramantras of the Ṛg-vedaṛgvedaṛgveda, the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda being only a sort of sacrificial prayer-book for the Adhvaryuadhvaryu priests formed out of the Ṛg-vedaṛgvedaṛgveda, which had to be dissected and rearranged with additional texts for sacrificial purposes; the most characteristic feature of the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda is its division into two distinct collections of texts, the Taittirīya-saṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitātaittirīyasaṃhitā and the Vājasaneyi-saṃhitāvājasaneyisaṃhitāvājasaneyisaṃhitā q.v.; the former of which is also called Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa i.e. ‘Black’, because in it the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā and Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa portions are confused; and the latter Śuklaśukla i.e. ‘White’, because in this, which is thought the more recent of the two recensions, the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā is cleared from confusion with its Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa and is as it were white or orderly; the order of sacrifices, however, of both recensions is similar, two of the principal being the Darśa-pūrṇa-māsadarśapūrṇamāsadarśapūrṇamāsa or sacrifice to be performed at new and full moon, and the Aśva-medhaaśvamedhaaśvamedha or horse-sacrifice; cf. IW. 6; 245 n. 2), Br.; GṛŚrS.; Mn. &c. [839,2]
