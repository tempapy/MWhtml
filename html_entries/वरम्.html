• varamvaram
  • ind. (amam) (g. svar-ādisvarādisvarādi) preferably, rather, better (also = preferable, sometimes with abl. which in Vedaveda is often followed by āā e.g. agnibhyoagnibhyo varamvaram, ‘better than fires’ RV.; sakhibhyasakhibhya āā varamvaram, ‘better than companions’ ib.; exceptionally with acc. e.g. śiṣyaiḥśiṣyaiḥ śata-hutānśatahutānśatahutān homānhomān, ekaḥekaḥ putra-hutoputrahutoputrahuto varamvaram, ‘better one sacrifice offered by a son than a hundred offered by disciples’ ṢaḍvBr.), RV. &c. &c. [922,1]
  • varamvaram
  • ind. it is better that, it would be best if (with pres. e.g. varaṃvaraṃ gacchāmigacchāmi, ‘it is better that I go’; or with Impv. e.g. varaṃvaraṃ nayenaye sthāpyatāmsthāpyatām, ‘it would be better if he were initiated into our plan’ Kathās.; or without any verb e.g. varaṃvaraṃ siṃhātsiṃhāt, ‘better [death caused] by a lion’ Pañcat.; sometimes with Pot., e.g. varaṃvaraṃ tattat kuryātkuryāt, ‘better that he should do that’ Kām.), Kāv.; Kathās. &c. [922,1]
  • it is better than, rather than (in these senses varamvaram is followed by nana, nana caca, nana tutu, nana punaḥpunaḥ, tadtad apiapi nana or tathāpitathāpi nana, with nom. e.g. varaṃvaraṃ mṛtyurmṛtyur nacākīrtiḥnacākīrtiḥ, ‘better death than [lit. ‘and not’] infamy’; exceptionally with instr. e.g. varamvaram ekoeko guṇīguṇī putroputro nana caca mūrkha-śatairmūrkhaśatairmūrkhaśatair apiapi, ‘better one virtuous son than hundreds of fools’ Hit.; nana hi-varamhivaramhivaram, ‘by no mean - but rather’), Mn.; MBh. &c.
