• varamvaramvara ind.
    • (amama) (g. svar-ādisvarādiādīādin) preferably, rather, better (also = preferable, sometimes with abl. which in Veda is often followed by āāa e.g. agnibhyoagni varamvaramvara, ‘better than fires’ RV.; sakhibhyasakhi āāaāsas varamvaramvara, ‘better than companions’ ib.; exceptionally with acc. e.g. śiṣyaiḥśiṣya śata-hutānśatahutaśatahuta homānhoma, ekaḥeka putra-hutoputra varamvaramvara, ‘better one sacrifice offered by a son than a hundred offered by disciples’ ṢaḍvBr.), RV. &c. &c. [922,1]
  • varamvaramvara ind.
    • it is better that, it would be best if (with pres. e.g. varaṃvaramvara gacchāmi, ‘it is better that I go’; or with Impv. e.g. varaṃvaramvara nayenayani sthāpyatām, ‘it would be better if he were initiated into our plan’ Kathās.; or without any verb e.g. varaṃvaramvara siṃhātsiṃha, ‘better [death caused] by a lion’ Pañcat.; sometimes with Pot., e.g. varaṃvaramvara tattat kuryāt, ‘better that he should do that’ Kām.), Kāv.; Kathās. &c. [922,1]
    • it is better than, rather than (in these senses varamvaramvara is followed by nana, nana caca, nana tutu, nana punaḥpunaḥ, tadtadtat apiapiapīap nana or tathā̑pitathāapiapīapāpiāpīāp nana, with nom. e.g. varaṃvaramvara mṛtyurmṛtyu nacā̑kīrtiḥakīrti, ‘better death than [lit. ‘and not’] infamy’; exceptionally with instr. e.g. varamvaramvara ekoeka guṇīguṇīguṇiguṇin putro nana caca mūrkha-śatairmūrkha apiapiapīap, ‘better one virtuous son than hundreds of fools’ Hit.; nana hi-varamhivaramvara, ‘by no mean - but rather’), Mn.; MBh. &c. [922,1]
