• varam ind. (am) (g. svar-ādisvar-ādisvarādi) preferably, rather, better (also = preferable, sometimes with abl. which in Veda is often followed by ā e.g. agnibhyo varam, ‘better than fires’ RV.; sakhibhya ā varam, ‘better than companions’ ib.; exceptionally with acc. e.g. śiṣyaiḥ śata-hutānśata-hutānśatahutān homān, ekaḥ putra-hutoputra-hutoputrahuto varam, ‘better one sacrifice offered by a son than a hundred offered by disciples’ ṢaḍvBr.), RV. &c. &c. [922,1]
  • varam ind. it is better that, it would be best if (with pres. e.g. varaṃ gacchāmi, ‘it is better that I go’; or with Impv. e.g. varaṃ naye sthāpyatām, ‘it would be better if he were initiated into our plan’ Kathās.; or without any verb e.g. varaṃ siṃhāt, ‘better [death caused] by a lion’ Pañcat.; sometimes with Pot., e.g. varaṃ tat kuryāt, ‘better that he should do that’ Kām.), Kāv.; Kathās. &c. [922,1]
  • it is better than, rather than (in these senses varam is followed by na, na ca, na tu, na punaḥ, tad api na or tathā̑pi na, with nom. e.g. varaṃ mṛtyur nacā̑kīrtiḥ, ‘better death than [lit. ‘and not’] infamy’; exceptionally with instr. e.g. varam eko guṇī putro na ca mūrkha-śatairmūrkha-śatairmūrkhaśatair api, ‘better one virtuous son than hundreds of fools’ Hit.; na hi-varamhi-varamhivaram, ‘by no mean - but rather’), Mn.; MBh. &c. [922,1]
