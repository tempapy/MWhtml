• mithmith [Roots]
    • cl. 1. Ā. P. (cf. Dhātup. xxi, 7) methati, °tetatā (pr. p.f. mithatī, RV.; pf. mimetha, ib.; ind.p. mithitvā, BhP.),
    • to unite, pair, couple, meet (as friend or antagonist), alternate, engage in altercation;
    • (Ā.) to dash together, RV. i, 113, 3 (accord. to Dhātup. also ‘to understand’ or ‘to kill’). [816,3]
