• droṇadroṇa n.
    • (fr. 4. drudrudrū) a wooden vessel, bucket, trough &c. a Soma vessel ([cf. Zd. draona]), RV.; MBh. &c. (ifc. f(āāa). , Hcat.) [502,3]
  • droṇadroṇa mn.
    • a measure of capacity (= 4 Āḍhakas = 16 Puṣkalas = 128 Kuñcis = 1024 Muṣṭis, or = 200 Palas = 1/20 Kumbha, or = 1/16 Khārī= 4 Āḍhakas, or = 2 Āḍhakas = 1/2 Śūrpa= 64 Śeras, or = 32 Śeras), Mn.; Yājñ.; MBh.; Suśr. &c. [502,3]
    • a measure for measuring fields (as much land as is sown with a Dróṇa of corn), Col. [502,3]
  • droṇadroṇa n.
    • an altar shaped like a trough, Śulbas. iii, 216 [502,3]
  • droṇadroṇa m.
    • a lake or large piece of water of 400 poles length, L. [502,3]
    • a kind of cloud (from which the rain streams forth as from a bucket), L. (cf. -meghadroṇameghadroṇamegha and -vṛṣṭidroṇavṛṣṭidroṇavṛṣṭi below) [503,1]
    • a raven or crow, L. (cf. -kākadroṇakākadroṇakāka) [503,1]
    • a scorpion (cf. druṇadruṇa) [503,1]
    • a kind of plant (prob. Leucas Linifolia), L. [503,1]
    • N. of one of the 8 Vasus (husband of Abhimati and father of Harṣa, Śoka, Bhaya &c.), BhP. [503,1]
    • of a Brāhman said to have been generated by Bharad-vāja in a bucket (the military preceptor of both the Kuru and Paṇḍu princes; afterwards king of a part of Pañcāla and general of the Kurus, the husband of Kṛpī and father of Aśvatthāman), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. &c. [503,1]
    • of one of the 4 sons of Mandapāla and Jaritā (born as birds), MBh. i, 8345 &c. (as author of RV. x, 142, 3, 4, with the patr. Śārṅga) [503,1]
    • of a Brāhman, Pañc. [503,1]
