• raṭraṭ [Roots]
  • cl. 1. P. (Dhātup. ix, 10) raṭatiraṭati (pf. rarāṭararāṭa; fut. raṭitāraṭitā &c. Gr.),
    to howl, shout, roar, yell, cry, Kāv.; Var.; Kathās.;
    to crash (as an axe), Prab.;
    to ring (as a bell), Mālatīm.;
    to lament, wail, HPariś.;
    to proclaim aloud, Kṛṣṇaj. :
    Caus. raṭayatiraṭayati (aor. arīraṭatarīraṭat), to howl, shout &c., Daś. :
    Intens. rāraṭītirāraṭīti, to scream aloud, roar, yell, caw &c., R.; KāśīKh.; Bhojapr. [863,3]
