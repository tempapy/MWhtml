• idamidamid 1
    • ayamayamaya, iyamiyamī, idamidamid (fr. idid, Uṇ. iv, 156; g. sarvā̑disarvādisarvaadādiādin, Pāṇ. i, 1, 27; Vop.; a kind of neut. of the pronom. base 3. iiī with amama [cf. Lat. is, ea, id, and idem]; the regular forms are partly derived from the pronom. base aa; See Gr. 224; the Veda exhibits various irregular formations e.g. fr. pronom. base aa, an inst. enāenā, ayāayāayaāa [used in general adverbially], and gen. loc. du. ayosaā, and perhaps also avos, in RV. vi, 67, 11; vii, 67, 4; x, 132, 5 [BRD.]; fr. the base imaima, a gen. sing. imasya, only, RV.; the RV. has in a few instances the irregular accentuation asmaia, v, 39, 5, &c. asyaa, iv, 15, 5, &c. ābhisāa, vi, 25, 2, &c. : the forms derived fr. aa are used enclitically if they take the place of the third personal pronoun, do not stand at the beginning of a verse or period, and have no peculiar stress laid upon them), this, this here, referring to something near the speaker [165,2]
    • known, present [165,3]
    • (opposed to adasadasadaad e.g. ayaṃayamaya lokaḥloka or idaṃidamid viśvamviśva or idaṃidamid sarvamsarvamsarva, this earthly world, this universe; ayamayamaya agniḥagni, this fire which burns on the earth; but asāvasauasiasu agniḥagni, that fire in the sky, i.e. the lightning: so also idamidamid or iyamiyamī alone sometimes signifies ‘this earth’; ime smaḥsma, here we are.) idamidamid often refers to something immediately following, whereas etadetad points to what precedes (e.g. śrutva͡itadetadetat idamidamid ūcuḥ, having heard that they said this). idamidamid occurs connected with yadyad, tadtad, etadetad, kimkim, and a personal pronoun, partly to point out anything more distinctly and emphatically, partly pleonastically (e.g. tadtadtat idaṃidamid vākyamvākya, this speech here following ; sososusassa 'yaṃayamaya vidūṣakaḥvidūṣaka, this Vidūṣaka here). [165,2]
