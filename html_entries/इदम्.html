• idamidam
  • ayamayam, iyamiyam, idamidam (fr. idid, Uṇ. iv, 156; g. sarvādisarvādi, Pāṇ. i, 1, 27; Vop.; a kind of neut. of the pronom. base 3. ii with amam [cf. Lat. is, ea, id, and idem]; the regular forms are partly derived from the pronom. base aa; See Gr. 224; the Vedaveda exhibits various irregular formations e.g. fr. pronom. base aa, an inst. enāenā, ayāayā [used in general adverbially], and gen. loc. du. ayosayos, and perhaps also avosavos, in RV. vi, 67, 11; vii, 67, 4; x, 132, 5 [BRD.]; fr. the base imaima, a gen. sing. imasyaimasya, only, RV.; the RV. has in a few instances the irregular accentuation asmaiasmai, v, 39, 5, &c.; asyaasya, iv, 15, 5, &c.; ābhisābhis, vi, 25, 2, &c. : the forms derived fr. aa are used enclitically if they take the place of the third personal pronoun, do not stand at the beginning of a verse or period, and have no peculiar stress laid upon them), this, this here, referring to something near the speaker [165,2]
  • known, present [165,3]
  • (opposed to adasadas e.g. ayaṃayaṃ lokaḥlokaḥ or idaṃidaṃ viśvamviśvam or idaṃidaṃ sarvamsarvam, this earthly world, this universe; ayamayam agniḥagniḥ, this fire which burns on the earth; but asāvasāv agniḥagniḥ, that fire in the sky, i.e. the lightning: so also idamidam or iyamiyam alone sometimes signifies ‘this earth’; imeime smaḥsmaḥ, here we are.) idamidam often refers to something immediately following, whereas etadetad points to what precedes (e.g. śrutvaitadśrutvaitad idamidam ūcuḥūcuḥ, having heard that they said this). idamidam occurs connected with yadyad, tadtad, etadetad, kimkim, and a personal pronoun, partly to point out anything more distinctly and emphatically, partly pleonastically (e.g. tadtad idaṃidaṃ vākyamvākyam, this speech here following ; soso 'yaṃyaṃ vidūṣakaḥvidūṣakaḥ, this Vidūṣakavidūṣaka here). [165,2]
