• kāpotakāpota mf(īīi)n.
    • (fr. kapotakapota, Pāṇ. iv, 3, 135, Sch., 154 Sch.), belonging to or coming from a pigeon, MBh.; BhP.; Pat. [271,3]
    • of the colour of a pigeon, of a dull white colour, grey, VarBṛS. [271,3]
  • kāpotakāpota m.
    • natron, fossil alkali, L. [271,3]
    • (also) a Vānaprastha who has collected food (like a pigeon) for a year, L. [1324,2]
    • antimony, a collyrium or application for the eyes, W. [271,3]
  • kāpotakāpota n.
    • a flock of pigeons Comm. on Pāṇ. iv, 2, 44 [271,3]
    • antimony, L. [271,3]
    • N. of two Sāmans, ĀrṣBr. [271,3]
