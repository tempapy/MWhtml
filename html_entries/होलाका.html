  • holākāholākā
  • f. (perhaps from a cry or shout or sound in singing), the spring festival at the approach of the vernal equinox (commonly called Hūlī or Holīholī, and said to be dedicated to Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa and the Gopīgopīs; it is celebrated during the ten days preceding the full moon of the month Phālgunaphālguna, when people sprinkle red powder in sport and light fires; in some parts of India the Holīholī festival corresponds to or immediately precedes the Dolā-yātrādolāyātrādolāyātrā q.v.), RTL. 430.
