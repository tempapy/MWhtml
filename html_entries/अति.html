• ati ind. ([probably neut. of an obsolete adj. atin, passing, going, beyond; See √at, and cf. Old Germ. anti, unti, inti, unde, indi, &c.; Eng. and; Germ. und; Gk. ἔτι, ἀντί, Lat. ante; Lith. ant; Arm. ti; Zd. aiti]). [12,2]
  • As a prefix to verbs and their derivatives, expresses beyond, over, and, if not standing by itself, leaves the accent on the verb or its derivative; as, ati-kramati-kramatikram (√kram), to overstep, Ved. Inf. ati-krameati-krameatikrame, (fit) to be walked on, to be passed, RV. i, 105, 16, ati-kramaṇaati-kramaṇaatikramaṇa n. See s.v. When prefixed to nouns, not derived from verbs, it expresses beyond, surpassing, as, ati-kaśaati-kaśaatikaśa, past the whip, ati-mānuṣaati-mānuṣaatimānuṣa, superhuman, &c. See s.v. [12,2]
  • As a separable adverb or preposition (with acc.), Ved. beyond (with gen.) over, at the top of RV.; AV. [12,2]
  • is often prefixed to nouns and adjectives, and rarely to verbs, in the sense excessive, extraordinary, intense; [12,2]
  • excessively, too [12,2]
  • exceedingly, very [12,2]
  • in such compounds the accent is generally on ati. [12,2]
