• guptagupta mfn.
    • protected, guarded, preserved, AV. &c. [359,1]
    • hidden, concealed, kept secret, secret, Bhartṛ.; Pañcat.; Kathās. &c. (with daṇḍadaṇḍa, a secret fine, fine secretly imposed or exacted, Hit.; cf. gūḍha-d°gūḍha) [359,1]
    • = saṃgatasaṃgata (? joined, combined), W. [359,1]
  • guptagupta m.
    • (Pāṇ. vi, 1, 205, Kāś.) N. of several men belonging to the Vaiśya caste (PārGṛ. i, 17; cf. RTL. p. 358), especially of the founder of the renowned Gupta dynasty in which the names of the sovereigns generally end in guptagupta (cf. candra-candraguptacandragupta, samudra-samudraguptasamudragupta, skanda-skandaguptaskandagupta; guptagupta is also often found ifc. in names of the Vaiśya class) [359,1]
  • guptagupta m.
    • (also) the era named after the Gupta dynasty (beginning, A.D. 319; hence the Gupta year 165 corresponds to, A.D. 484-85; in later times the years are called valabhī-saṃvatsaṃvat from the rulers of Valabhi, and the era is spoken of as the Gupta-Valabhi era). [1326,2]
