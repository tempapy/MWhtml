• somasoma
  • m. (fr. √3. susu) juice, extract, (esp.) the juice of the Somasoma plant, (also) the Somasoma plant itself (said to be the climbing plant Sarcostema Viminalis or Asclepias Acida, the stalks [aṃśuaṃśu] of which were pressed between stones [adriadri] by the priests, then sprinkled with water, and purified in a strainer [pavitrapavitra]; whence the acid juice trinkled into jars [kalaśakalaśa] or larger vessels [droṇadroṇa]; after which it was mixed with clarified butter, flour &c., made to ferment, and then offered in libations to the gods [in this respect corresponding with the ritual of the Iranian Avesta] or was drunk by the Brāhmans, by both of whom its exhilarating effect was supposed to be prized; it was collected by moonlight on certain mountains [in, RV. x, 34, 1, the mountain Mūja-vatmūjavatmūjavat is mentioned]; it is sometimes described as having been brought from the sky by a falcon [śyenaśyena] and guarded by the Gandharvagandharvas; it is personified as one of the most important of Vedic gods, to whose praise all the 114 hymns of the 9th book of the RV. besides 6 in other books and the whole, SV. are dedicated; in post-Vedic mythology and even in a few of the latest hymns of the RV. [although not in the whole of the 9th book] as well as sometimes in the AV. and in the Br., Somasoma is identified with the moon [as the receptacle of the other beverage of the gods called Amṛtaamṛta, or as the lord of plants cf. induindu, oṣadhi-patioṣadhipatioṣadhipati] and with the god of the moon, as well as with Viṣṇuviṣṇu, Śivaśiva, Yamayama, and Kuberakubera; he is called rājanrājan, and appears among the 8 Vasuvasus and the 8 Loka-pālalokapālalokapālas [Mn. v, 96], and is the reputed author of RV. x, 124, 1, 5-9, of a law-book &c.; cf. below), RV. &c. &c.
