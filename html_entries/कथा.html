• 1. kathākathākath f.
    • (for 2. See col. 3) conversation, speech, talking together, ĀśvGṛ.; MBh.; Mn. &c. [247,1]
    • talk, mention [247,1]
    • (kākāka kathākathākath [with gen. or more commonly with loc. and sometimes with pratipratipratī], what should one say of? how should one speak of? e.g. ekoeka 'pi kṛcchrād varteta bhūyasāṃ tutu katha͡ivakathāeva kākāka, even one person would live with difficulty, what should one say of many? i.e. how much more many? Kathās. iv, 123; kākāka kathākathākath bāṇa-saṃdhānebāṇasaṃdhānabāṇasaṃdhāna, what mention of fitting the arrow? i.e. what necessity for fitting the arrow? Śak. 53 a) [247,1]
    • story, tale, fable, MBh.; R.; Hit. &c. [247,1]
    • a feigned story, tale (as one, of the species of poetical composition), Sāh. 567; Kāvyād. [247,1]
    • Story (personified), Kathās. [247,1]
    • (in log.) discussion, disputation, Sarvad. [247,1]
  • 2. kathākathākath ind.
    • (for 1. See col. 1) (Ved. for kathamkathamkathkathaṃ, Pāṇ. v, 3, 26) how? whence? why? RV.; AV. viii, 1, 16; TS. &c. [247,3]
    • (yathāyathāyatha kathākathākath caca, in any way whatsoever, ŚBr. iv) [247,3]
    • sometimes merely a particle of interrogation (e.g. kathākathākath śṛṇoti … indraḥindra, does Indra hear? RV. iv, 23, 3; kathā-kathākathākathākath, whether-or? TS. ii, 6, 1, 7). [247,3]
