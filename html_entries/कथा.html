• 1. kathākathā f.
    • (for 2. See col.3) conversation, speech, talking together, ĀśvGṛ.; MBh.; Mn. &c. [247,1]
    • talk, mention [247,1]
    • (kākāka kathākathā [with gen. or more commonly with loc. and sometimes with pratiprati], what should one say of? how should one speak of? e.g. ekoeka 'pi kṛcchrādkṛcchrātkṛcchra varteta bhūyasāṃbhūyasābhūyas tutu katha͡ivakathāeva kākāka, even one person would live with difficulty, what should one say of many? i.e. how much more many? Kathās. iv, 123; kākāka kathākathā bāṇa-saṃdhānebāṇasaṃdhānabāṇasaṃdhāna, what mention of fitting the arrow? i.e. what necessity for fitting the arrow? Śak. 53 a) [247,1]
    • story, tale, fable, MBh.; R.; Hit. &c. [247,1]
    • a feigned story, tale (as one, of the species of poetical composition), Sāh. 567; Kāvyâd. [247,1]
    • Story (personified), Kathās. [247,1]
    • (in log.) discussion, disputation, Sarvad. [247,1]
  • 2. kathākathā ind.
    • (for 1. See col.1) (Ved. for kathamkatham, Pāṇ. v, 3, 26) how? whence? why? RV.; AV. viii, 1, 16; TS. &c. [247,3]
    • (yathāyathā kathākathā caca, in any way whatsoever, ŚBr. iv) [247,3]
    • sometimes merely a particle of interrogation (e.g. kathākathā śṛṇoti... indraḥindra, does Indra hear? RV. iv, 23, 3; kathā-kathākathākathā, whether-or? TS. ii, 6, 1, 7). [247,3]
