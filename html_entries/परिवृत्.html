• pari-√vṛtparivṛtparivṛt
  • Ā. -vartatevartatevartate (ep. also °tiparivṛti; ind.p. -vṛtyavṛtyavṛtya, or parī-vartamparīvartamparīvartam),
    to turn round, revolve, move in a circle or to and fro, roll or wheel or wander about, circumambulate (acc.), RV. &c. &c.;
    (with hṛdihṛdi or hṛdayehṛdaye) to run in a person's mind, MBh.;
    to return, go or come back to (acc.), ib.;
    to be reborn in (loc.), VP.;
    (also with anyathāanyathā) to change, turn out different, MBh.; Kāv.;
    to abide, stay, remain, Mn.; MBh. &c.;
    to act, proceed, behave, R. :
    Caus. -vartayativartayativartayati, to cause to turn or move round or back or to and fro, MBh.; Kāv. &c.;
    (Ā.) to roll or bring near (Subj. -vartayātevartayātevartayāte), RV. v, 37, 3;
    to overthrow, upset (a carriage), Hariv.;
    to invert, put in a reverse order, Mṛcch. v, 9/10 (read -vartyavartyavartya for -vṛtyavṛtyavṛtya);
    to change, barter, exchange, MBh.; Hariv. &c.;
    to renew (an agreement), Mn. viii, 154 &c.;
    to understand or explain wrongly (words &c.), MBh.; Kāv.;
    to turn topsy-turvy i.e. search thoroughly, R.;
    to destroy, annihilate, ib.;
    to straiten, contract, Car.;
    (Ā.) to cause one's self to be turned round (in having one's head shaved all round), TBr.; ŚBr. (cf. -vartanavartanavartana) :
    Intens. -vavarttivavarttivavartti, to turn (intrans.) continually, RV. i, 164, 11. [601,2]
