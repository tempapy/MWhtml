• hitȏpadeśahitopadeśahitahitāupadeśa m.
    • friendly advice, salutary instruction, MBh.; Pañcat. &c. [1298,3]
    • N. of a popular collection of fables intermixed with didactic sentences and moral precepts (compiled by Nārāyaṇa, and supposed to be narrated by a Brahman named Viṣṇu-śarman to some young princes; it is chiefly founded on the Pañca-tantra q.v.) [1298,3]
    • N. of two works on medicine. [1298,3]
