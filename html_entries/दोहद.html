• dohadadohada
  • m. (also n., L.; probably Prākṛt for. daurhṛdadaurhṛda lit. sickness of heart, nausea) the longing of a pregnant woman for partic. objects (fig. said of plants which at budding time long to be touched by the foot or by the mouth [Ragh. xix, 12] of a lovely woman) [499,1]
  • any morbid desire or wish for (loc. or comp. f(āā). ), Yājñ.; R.; Kālid.; Pañc.; Kathās. &c. [499,1]
  • pregnancy [499,1]
  • accord. to Luders fr. duhalīduhalī = dvihṛddvihṛd, ‘pregnant’. [1329,1]
  • a kind of fragrant substance used as manure, Naiṣ. i, 82 Sch. [499,1]
