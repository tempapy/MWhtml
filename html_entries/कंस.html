  • kaṃsakaṃsa
  • m. N. of a king of Mathurāmathurā (son of Ugra-senaugrasenaugrasena and cousin of the Devakīdevakī who was mother of Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa [Ugra-senaugrasenaugrasena being brother of Devakadevaka, who was father of Devakīdevakī]; he is usually called the uncle, but was really a cousin of Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa, and became his implacable enemy because it had been prophesied to Kaṉsakaṃsa that he would be killed by a child of Devakīdevakī; as the foe of the deity he is identified with the Asuraasura Kālanemikālanemi; and, as he was ultimately slain by Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa, the latter receives epithets like kaṃsa-jitkaṃsajitkaṃsajit, conqueror of Kaṉsakaṃsa, &c.), MBh.; VP.; BhP. &c. [241,1]
