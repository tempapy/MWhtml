• yājñayājña—valkya m.
    • (yāyāyaiī, fr. yajñavalkayajñavalka) N. of an ancient sage (frequently quoted as an authority in the ŚBr.; the first reputed teacher of the Vājasaneyi-saṃhitā or White Yajur-veda, revealed to him by the Sun; he is also the supposed author of a celebrated code of laws, which is only second in importance to that of Manu, and with its well-known commentary, the Mitākṣarā, is the leading authority of the Mithilā school; cf. IW. 291), ŚBr. &c. &c. [850,3]
    • pl. the family of Yajur-veda, Hariv. [850,3]
  • yājñayājña—valkya mfn.
    • relating to or derived by Yajur-veda [850,3]
  • yājñayājña—valkya n.
    • N. of an Upaniṣad [850,3]
