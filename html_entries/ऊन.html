• ūnaūna mfn.
    • (√avav, Uṇ. iii, 2; ? cf. Zd. ūnaūna), wanting, deficient, defective, short of the right quantity, less than the right number, not sufficient [221,2]
    • less (in number, size, or degree), minus, fewer, smaller, inferior, AV. x, 8, 15; 44; xii, 1, 61; TS.; ŚBr.; Mn.; Ragh. &c. [221,2]
    • less than (with abl. e.g. lakṣādlakṣa ūnaūna, less than a Lakṣa, Kathās. liii, 10; or ifc. e.g. tad-ūnatadtatūna, inferior to that one, Mn. ix, 123), less by (with instr. e.g. dvābhyām ūnaūna, less by two, ŚBr. xi; or ifc. e.g. alpȏnaalponaalpaūna, less by a little, a little less, Mn.; pañcȏnapañcaūna, less by five &c.) [221,2]
    • less by one (prefixed to decimals from twenty up to one hundred, e.g. ūna-viṃśaūnaviṃśa = ekȏna-viṃśaekonaekaekāūnaviṃśa, the twentieth minus one, the nineteenth). [221,2]
