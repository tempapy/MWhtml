• 1. kavyakavya mfn.
    • (= kavikavi, Kāś. on Pāṇ. v, 4, 30) wise, RV. x, 15, 9 (at, VS. xxii, 2 read kavyāḥkavyakavi) [264,3]
    • a sacrificer, sacrificial priest, RV. ix, 91, 2 [264,3]
    • N. of a class of deities associated with Aṅgiras and Ṛkvan ([Gmn.; a class of manes]), RV. x, 14, 3; AV. [264,3]
    • N. of one of the seven sages of the fourth Manv-antara, Hariv. [264,3]
  • kavyakavya n.
    • (generally in connection with havyahavya See havya-kavyahavyakavyahavyakavya) ‘what must be offered to the wise’, an oblation of food to deceased ancestors, MBh.; Mn. [264,3]
  • 2. kavyakavya
    • Nom. P. kavyatikavyat, to be wise, Pāṇ. vii, 4, 39. [265,1]
