• aurvaaurva
  • m. a descendant of Ūrvaūrva, N. of a Ṛṣiṛṣi, RV. viii, 102, 4; TS. vii; AitBr.; MBh. &c. [239,3]
  • (in later mythology he is called Aurvaaurva Bhārgavabhārgava as son of Cyavanacyavana and grandson of Bhṛgubhṛgu; he is the subject of a legend told in MBh. i, 6802; there it is said that the sons of Kṛtavīryakṛtavīrya, wishing to destroy the descendants of Bhṛgubhṛgu in order to recover the wealth left them by their father, slew even the children in the womb; one of the women of the family of Bhṛgubhṛgu, in order to preserve her embryo, secreted it in her thigh [ūruūru], whence the child at its birth was named Aurvaaurva; on beholding whom, the sons of Kṛtavīryakṛtavīrya were struck with blindness, and from whose wrath proceeded a flame that threatened to destroy the world, had not Aurvaaurva at the persuasion of the Bhārgavabhārgavas cast it into the ocean, where it remained concealed, and having the face of a horse; Aurvaaurva was afterwards preceptor to Sagarasagara and gave him the Āgneyâstramāgneyāstram, with which he conquered the barbarians who invaded his possessions; cf. vaḍavā-mukhavaḍavāmukhavaḍavāmukha, vaḍavāgnivaḍavāgni) [239,3]
