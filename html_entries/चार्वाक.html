• cārvākacārvāka m.
    • (for °ru-v°ru = cārvāccārvāc s.v. cārucāru) N. of a Rākṣasa (friend of Duryodhana, who took the shape of a mendicant Brāhman, when Yudhiṣṭhira entered Hāstina-pura in triumph, and reviled him, but was soon detected and killed by the real Brāhmans), MBh. i, 349; ix, 3619; xii, 1414 [394,1]
    • N. of a materialistic philosopher (whose doctrines are embodied in the Bārhaspatya-sūtras), Vedântas.; Śīl.; Rājat. iv, 345; Prab.; Madhus. [394,1]
    • a follower of Cārvāka, Sarvad. [394,1]
  • cārvākacārvāka mfn.
    • composed by Cārvāka, Prab. ii, 18/19 Sch. [394,1]
