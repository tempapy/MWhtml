• cārvākacārvāka
  • m. (for °ru-v°cāruvākacāruvāka = cārvāccārvāc s.v. cārucāru) N. of a Rākṣasarākṣasa (friend of Duryodhanaduryodhana, who took the shape of a mendicant Brāhman, when Yudhiṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhira entered Hāstina-purahāstinapurahāstinapura in triumph, and reviled him, but was soon detected and killed by the real Brāhmans), MBh. i, 349; ix, 3619; xii, 1414 [394,1]
  • N. of a materialistic philosopher (whose doctrines are embodied in the Bārhaspatyabārhaspatya-sūtrasūtras), Vedântas.; Śīl.; Rājat. iv, 345; Prab.; Madhus. [394,1]
  • a follower of Cārvākacārvāka, Sarvad. [394,1]
