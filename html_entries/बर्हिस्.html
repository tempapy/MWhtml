• barhisbarhisbarhi n.
    • (rarely m.) ‘that which is plucked up’, sacrificial grass, a bed or layer of Kuśa grass (usually strewed over the sacrificial ground and esp. over the Vedi, to serve as a sacred surface on which to present the oblations, and as a seat for the gods and for the sacrificers), RV. &c. &c. [722,2]
  • barhisbarhisbarhi n.
    • Sacrificial Grass personified (and enumerated among the Prayāja and Anuyāja deities), RV.; Br. [722,2]
    • sacrifice, RV.; BhP. [722,2]
    • ether, L. [722,2]
    • water, L. [722,2]
    • a kind of perfume, L. [722,2]
  • barhisbarhisbarhi m.
    • fire, light, splendour, L. [722,2]
    • Plumbago Zeylanica, L. [722,2]
    • N. of a man, MaitrUp. [722,2]
    • of a son of Bṛhad-rāja, BhP. [722,2]
    • pl. the descendants of Barhis, Saṃskārak. [722,2]
