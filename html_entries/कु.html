• 1. kukukū
    • a pronom. base appearing in kutaskutaskuta, kutrakutra, kuvidkuvid, kuhakuha, kvakva, and as a prefix implying deterioration, depreciation, deficiency, want, littleness, hindrance, reproach, contempt, guilt [285,3]
    • originally perhaps kukukū signified ‘how (strange!)’ [285,3]
    • as a separate word kukukū occurs only in the lengthened form 3. kūkūku q.v. [285,3]
  • 2. kukukū f.
    • the earth, Āryabh.; VarBṛS.; VarBṛ.; BhP. vi, 1, 42 [286,3]
    • the ground or base of a triangle or other plane figure, Comm. on Āryabh. [286,3]
    • the number ‘one.’ [286,3]
  • 3. kukukū
    • See √1. kūkūku. [286,3]
