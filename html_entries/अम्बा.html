• ambāambā
  • f. (Ved. voc. ambeambe [VS.] or ambaamba [RV.], in later Sanskrit ambaamba only, sometimes a mere interjection, ĀśvŚr.), a mother, good woman (as a title of respect) [83,2]
  • N. of a plant [83,2]
  • N. of Durgādurgā (the wife of Śivaśiva) [83,2]
  • N. of an Apsarasapsaras, L. [83,2]
  • N. of a daughter of a king of Kāśīkāśī, MBh. [83,2]
  • N. of one of the seven Kṛttikākṛttikās, TS.; Kāṭh.; TBr. [83,2]
  • a term in astrol. (to denote the fourth condition which results from the conjunction of planets?). In the South Indian languages, ambāambā is corrupted into ammāammā, and is often affixed to the names of goddesses, and females in general [83,2]
  • ambāambā
  • [Germ. Amme, a nurse; Old Germ. amma, Them. ammôn, ammûn] [83,2]
