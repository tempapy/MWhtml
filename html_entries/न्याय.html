• ny-āyanyāyanyaninīāyaa m.
    • (fr. 4. nī̑nīnininīiīin) that into which a thing goes back i.e. an original type, standard, method, rule, (esp.) a general or universal rule, model, axiom, system, plan, manner, right or fit manner or way, fitness, propriety, TS.; Br.; Mn.; MBh. &c. (nyāyenanyāyenanyāya ind. and °yātnya ind. either ‘in the right manner, regularly, duly’, or ifc. ‘after the manner of, by way of’) [572,2]
    • a lawsuit, legal proceeding, judicial sentence, judgement, Mṛcch.; Pañc. [572,2]
    • a logical or syllogistic argument or inference (consisting of a combination of enthymeme and syllogism, and so having, according to the Naiyāyikas 5 members, viz. pratijñāpratijñāpratijña, hetuhetu, udāharaṇaudāharaṇa, upanayaupanaya, nigamananigamana, or according to the Vedāntins 3 members) [572,2]
    • a system of philosophy delivered by Gotama or Gautama (it is one of the six darśanasdarśana, q.v., and is perhaps so called, because it ‘goes into’ all subjects physical and metaphysical according to the above syllogistic method treated of in one division of the system; its branch is called Vaiśeṣika) [572,2]
    • likeness, analogy, a popular maxim or apposite illustration (cf. kākā̑kṣi-kākākṣikākakākāakṣiākṣinyāyanya, ghuṇā̑kṣara-ghuṇākṣaranyāyaghuṇākṣaraghuṇaakṣaranyāyanya, daṇḍā̑pūpadaṇḍāpūpadaṇḍadaṇḍāapūpa. &c.) [572,2]
