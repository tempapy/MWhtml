• ny-āyanyāyanyāya ⮩ nyāyena nyāyāt
  • m. (fr. 4. nī̑nī) that into which a thing goes back i.e. an original type, standard, method, rule, (esp.) a general or universal rule, model, axiom, system, plan, manner, right or fit manner or way, fitness, propriety, TS.; Br.; Mn.; MBh. &c. (nyāyenanyāyena ind. and °yātnyāyāt ind. either ‘in the right manner, regularly, duly’, or ifc. ‘after the manner of, by way of’) [572,2]
  • a lawsuit, legal proceeding, judicial sentence, judgement, Mṛcch.; Pañc. [572,2]
  • a logical or syllogistic argument or inference (consisting of a combination of enthymeme and syllogism, and so having, according to the Naiyāyikanaiyāyikas 5 members, viz. pratijñāpratijñā, hetuhetu, udāharaṇaudāharaṇa, upanayaupanaya, nigamananigamana, or according to the Vedāntinvedāntins 3 members) [572,2]
  • a system of philosophy delivered by Gotamagotama or Gautamagautama (it is one of the six darśanasdarśanas q.v., and is perhaps so called, because it ‘goes into’ all subjects physical and metaphysical according to the above syllogistic method treated of in one division of the system; its branch is called Vaiśeṣikavaiśeṣika) [572,2]
  • likeness, analogy, a popular maxim or apposite illustration (cf. kākā̑kṣi-kākākṣikākākṣi-nyāya, ghuṇā̑kṣara-ghuṇākṣaraghuṇākṣara-nyāya, daṇḍā̑pūpadaṇḍāpūpa. &c.) [572,2]
