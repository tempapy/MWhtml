• 1. ṣaṣa
    • the second of the three sibilants (it belongs to the cerebral class, and is sometimes substituted for s, and more rarely for ś, and occasionally interchangeable with kh; in sound it corresponds to sh in the English word shun; many roots which begin with s are written in the Dhātu-pāṭha with ṣ, prob. to show that their initial s is liable to be cerebralized after certain prepositions).
