• saṃ-s-saṃsamsaṃskṛ√1. kṛkṛ
    • (cf. saṃ-kṛsaṃkṛsaṃsamkṛ; upa-s-kṛupaskṛupakṛ and pari-ṣ-kṛpariṣkṛparikṛ) P. Ā. -skarotisaṃskṛ, -skurutesaṃskṛ (impf. sam-askurvatasam, TS.; pf. saṃ-caskārasaṃsam, Nir.; aor. sam-askṛtasam; Prec. saṃ-skriyātsaṃsam, saṃ-skṛṣīṣṭasaṃsam; fut. saṃ-skariṣyatisaṃsam, MBh.; inf. saṃ-skaritumsaṃsam, Divyāv.; ind.p. saṃ-skṛtyasaṃsam, ŚBr.; Pāṇ. vi, 1, 137),
    • to put together, form well, join together, compose, RV. &c. &c.
    • (Ā.) to accumulate (pāpānipāpa, ‘to add evil to evil’), Mṛcch. ix, 4;
    • to prepare, make ready, dress, cook (food), MBh.; R. &c.
    • to form or arrange according to sacred precept, consecrate, hallow (in various ways; cf. saṃ-skārasaṃskārasaṃsam), Mn.; MBh. &c.
    • to adorn, embellish, refine, elaborate, make perfect, (esp.) form language according to strict rules (cf. saṃ-skṛtasaṃskṛtasaṃsam), Sarvad.;
    • to correct (astronomically), Sūryas. :
    • Pass. saṃ-skriyatesaṃsam, to be put together or arranged or prepared or consecrated or refined, Mn.; MBh. &c.:
    • Caus. saṃskārayati, to cause to (be) put together &c.
    • to cause to be consecrated, MBh. :
    • Desid. saṃ-ciṣkīrṣatisaṃsam, Vop. :
