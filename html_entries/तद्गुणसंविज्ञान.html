• tadtad—guṇaguṇa—saṃvijñānasaṃvijñāna
  • n. (a Bahuvrīhibahuvrīhi compound) in which the qualities implied are perceived along with the thing itself (e.g. dīrghakarṇadīrghakarṇa, ‘long-ear’; opposed to a-aa-tadguṇasaṃvijñāna e.g. dṛṣṭasāgaradṛṣṭasāgara, ‘one who has seen the ocean’), Bādar. i, 1, 2 Sch.; Sarvad. [434,2]
