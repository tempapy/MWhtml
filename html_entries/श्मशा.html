• śmaśā f. (prob. connected with aśman) the elevated ridge or edge of a trench or ditch or channel for water or of a vessel, RV. x, 105, 1 (but in ŚBr. the m. pl. śmaśāḥ is said to mean those deceased ancestors who consume or eat the oblations [?], and a comp. śmaśā̑nna is formed to explain śmaśāna) [1094,1]
