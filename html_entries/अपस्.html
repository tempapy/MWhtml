• 1. apasapasapaap n.
    • (fr. 1. apapab), work, action, especially sacred act, sacrificial act, RV.[Lat. opus.] [53,1]
  • 2. apasapasapaap mfn.
    • active, skilful in any art, RV. [53,1]
  • apasapasapaap f. pl.
    • (asas) N. of the hands and fingers (when employed in kindling the sacred fire and in performing the sacrifices), RV. [53,1]
    • of the three goddesses of sacred speech, RV.; VS. [53,1]
    • of the active or running waters, RV.; AV. [53,1]
  • 3. apasapasapaap mfn.
    • (fr. 2. apapab), watery. (So some passages of the Ṛg-veda [i, 95, 4, &c.] may (according to, NBD. and others) be translated where the word is applied to the running waters See 2. apasapasapaap at end and apas-tamaapastamaapastama.) [53,1]
