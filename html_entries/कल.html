• kalakala mf(āāa)n.
    • (etym. doubtful) indistinct, dumb, BṛĀrUp.; ChUp. [260,1]
    • (ifc., bāṣpabāṣpa, or aśruaśru preceding) indistinct or inarticulate (on account of tears), MBh.; R. &c. [260,2]
    • low, soft (as a tone), emitting a soft tone, melodious (as a voice or throat), R.; BhP.; Vikr. &c. [260,2]
    • a kind of faulty pronunciation of vowels, Pat. [260,2]
    • weak, crude, undigested, L. [260,2]
  • kalakala m.

    • (in poetry) time equal to four Mātras or instants, W. [260,2]
  • kalakala m. pl.
    • a class of manes, MBh. [260,2]
  • kalakala n.
