• pratīpapratīpa mf(āāa)n.
    • (fr. pratipratipratī + apap; cf. anūpaanūpa, dvīpadvīpa, samīpasamīpa), ‘against the stream’, ‘against the grain’, going in an opposite direction, meeting, encountering, adverse, contrary, opposite, reverse, MBh.; R.; Ragh. &c. [674,1]
    • inverted, out of order, Suśr.; Var. [674,1]
    • displeasing, disagreeable, Mn.; MBh.; R.; Hariv. [674,1]
    • resisting, refractory, cross, obstinate [674,1]
    • impeding, hindering, BhP.; MBh.; R. &c. backward, retrograde [674,1]
    • turned away, averted, W. [674,1]
  • pratīpapratīpa m.
    • an adversary, opponent, BhP. [674,1]
    • N. of a prince, the father of Śāṃtanu and grandfather of Bhīṣma, AV.; MBh.; Hariv. &c. [674,1]
  • pratīpapratīpa n.
    • (in rhet.) inverse comparison (e.g. ‘the lotus resembles thine eyes’, instead of the usual comparison ‘thine eyes resemble the lotus’; 5 forms are enumerated), Kuval.; Pratāp.; Sāh.; Kpr. [674,1]
    • N. of a gram. wk. [674,1]
  • pratīpapratīpa n.
    • against, RV. &c. &c. [674,1]

    • in inverted order, Mn. [674,1]
    • refractorily (with √gamgamga, to resist, Śak.; with abhy-upa-√gamabhyupagamabhiabhīupaupeupasgamga, to go against, oppose, R.) [674,1]
