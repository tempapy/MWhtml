• ācāryaācārya
  • m. ‘knowing or teaching the ācāraācāra or rules’, a spiritual guide or teacher (especially one who invests the student with the sacrificial thread, and instructs him in the Vedavedas, in the law of sacrifice and religious mysteries [Mn. ii, 140; 171]), AV.; ŚBr. &c. [131,3]
  • a N. of Droṇadroṇa (the teacher of the Pāṇḍavapāṇḍavas), Bhag. i, 2 [131,3]
  • (also) the son of an outcast Vaiśyavaiśya and a Vaiśyāvaiśyā who was before a Brāhman's wife, Mn. x, 23 (accord. to some, read cāryacārya) [1319,1]
