• 1. ahaaha ind.
    • (as a particle implying ascertainment, affirmation, certainty, &c.) surely, certainly, RV.; AV.; ŚBr. [124,1]
    • (as explaining, defining) namely, ŚBr. [124,1]
    • (as admitting, limiting, &c.) it is true, I grant, granted, indeed, at least, ŚBr. ([For the rules of accentuation necessitated in a phrase by the particle ahaaha cf. Pāṇ. viii, 1, 24 seqq.]) [124,1]
  • 2. ahaaha n.
    • (only Ved.; nom. pl. ahāaha, RV.; AV.; gen. pl. ahānāmaha, RV. viii, 22, 13) = aharahar q.v., a day [124,1]
    • often ifc. m(ahaaha). (e.g. dvādaśā̑hadvādaśāhadvādaśaahaāha, try-ahatryahatrytriaha, ṣaḍ-ahaṣaḍahaṣaḍṣaṭaha, &c.) or n. (e.g. puṇyā̑hapuṇyāhapuṇyapuṇyāahaāha, bhadrā̑habhadrāhabhadrabhadrāahaāha, and sudinā̑hasudināhasudinaahaāha) [124,1]
    • See also ahnaahna s.v. [124,1]
    • 1 (also) a particle answering to haha in a preceding sentence (ha-ahahaaha = μὲν --δέ), Gaṇar. [1318,3]
