• hastinā-purahastināpurahastināpura
  • n. (less correctly hastina-p°hastinapurahastinapura or hastinīhastinī.) N. of a city founded by king Hastinhastin q.v. (it was situated about fifty-seven miles north-east of the modern Delhi on the banks of an old channel of the Ganges, and was the capital of the kings of the Lunar line, as Ayodhyāayodhyā was of the Solar dynasty; hence it forms a central scene of action in the Mahābhāratamahābhārata ; here Yudhi-ṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhira was crowned after a triumphal progress through the streets of the city; See MBh. xii, 1386-1410 : other names for this celebrated town are gajā̑hvayagajāhvaya, nāga-sā̑hvayanāgasāhvayanāgasāhvaya, nāgā̑hvanāgāhva, hāctinahāctina), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. &c. [1295,3]
