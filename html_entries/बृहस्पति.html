• bṛhas-patibṛhaspatibṛhaspati
  • m. (also written vṛh°-p°vṛhaspatipativṛhaspatipati; fr. 3. bṛhbṛh + patipati; cf. brahmaṇas-patibrahmaṇaspatibrahmaṇaspati) ‘lord of prayer or devotion’ N. of a deity (in whom Piety and Religion are personified; he is the chief offerer of prayers and sacrifices, and therefore represented as the type of the priestly order, and the Purohitapurohita of the gods with whom he intercedes for men; in later times he is the god of wisdom and eloquence, to whom various works are ascribed; he is also regarded as son of Aṅgirasaṅgiras, husband of Tārātārā and father of Kacakaca, and sometimes identified with Vyāsavyāsa; in astronomy he is the regent of Jupiter and often identified with that planet), RV. &c., &c. (cf. RTL. 215) [737,1]
  • N. of a prince (great-grandson of Aśokaaśoka), Buddh. [737,1]
  • of a king of Kaśmīrakaśmīra, Rājat. [737,1]
  • of the author of a law-book, IW. 203; 302 [737,1]
  • of a philosopher, ib. 120 [737,1]
  • of other authors (also with miśramiśra and ācāryaācārya cf. above), Cat. [737,1]
  • (with āṅgirasaāṅgirasa cf. above) N. of the author of RV. x, 71; 72; Anukr. [737,1]
