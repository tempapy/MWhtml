• upa-vedaupa-vedaupaveda m. ‘secondary knowledge’, N. of a class of writings subordinate or appended to the four Vedas (viz. the āyur-vedaāyur-vedaāyurveda, or science of medicine, to the Ṛg-veda; the dhanurveda or science of archery, to the Yajur-vedayajur-vedayajurveda; the gāndharva-vedagāndharva-vedagāndharvaveda or science of music, to the Sāmaveda; and the śastra-śāstraśastra-śāstraśastraśāstra or science of arms, to the Atharva-veda; this is according to the Caraṇavyūha, but, Suśr. and the Bhpr. make the Āyur-vedaāyur-vedaāyurveda belong to the Atharva-veda; according to others, the sthāpatya-vedasthāpatya-vedasthāpatyaveda or science of architecture, and śilpa-śāstraśilpa-śāstraśilpaśāstra or knowledge of arts, are reckoned as the fourth Upa-vedaupa-vedaupaveda).
