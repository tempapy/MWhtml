• upa-vedaupavedaupaveda
  • m. ‘secondary knowledge’, N. of a class of writings subordinate or appended to the four Vedavedas (viz. the āyur-vedaāyurvedaāyurveda, or science of medicine, to the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda; the dhanurvedadhanurveda or science of archery, to the Yajur-vedayajurvedayajurveda; the gāndharva-vedagāndharvavedagāndharvaveda or science of music, to the Sāmavedasāmaveda; and the śastra-śāstraśastraśāstraśastraśāstra or science of arms, to the Atharvaatharva-vedaveda; this is according to the Caraṇavyūhacaraṇavyūha, but, Suśr. and the Bhpr. make the Āyur-vedaāyurvedaāyurveda belong to the Atharvaatharva-vedaveda; according to others, the sthāpatya-vedasthāpatyavedasthāpatyaveda or science of architecture, and śilpa-śāstraśilpaśāstraśilpaśāstra or knowledge of arts, are reckoned as the fourth Upa-vedaupavedaupaveda).
