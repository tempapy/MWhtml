• upa-vedaupavedaupaveda m.
    • ‘secondary knowledge’, N. of a class of writings subordinate or appended to the four Vedas (viz. the āyur-vedaāyurvedaāyurāyusveda, or science of medicine, to the Ṛg-veda; the dhanurvedadhanurveda or science of archery, to the Yajur-veda; the gāndharva-vedagāndharvavedagāndharvaveda or science of music, to the Sāmaveda; and the śastra-śāstraśastraśāstraśastraśāstra or science of arms, to the Atharva-veda; this is according to the Caraṇavyūha, but, Suśr. and the Bhpr. make the Āyur-veda belong to the Atharva-veda; according to others, the sthāpatya-vedasthāpatyavedasthāpatyaveda or science of architecture, and śilpa-śāstraśilpaśāstraśilpaśāstra or knowledge of arts, are reckoned as the fourth Upa-veda).
