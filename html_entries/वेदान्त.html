  • vedā̑ntavedāsrssrsnta
  • m. end of the Vedaveda (= ‘complete knowledge of the Vedaveda’ cf. vedā̑nta-gavedāsrssrsntagavedāsrssrsntaga), TĀr.; MBh. [1017,1]
  • N. of the second and most important part of the Mīmāṉsāmīmāṃsā or third of the three great divisions of Hindū philosophy (called Vedântavedānta either as teaching the ultimate scope of the Vedaveda or simply as explained in the Upaniṣadupaniṣads which come at the end of the Vedaveda; this system, although belonging to the Mīmāṉsāmīmāṃsā [q.v.] and sometimes called Uttara-mīmāṉsāuttaramīmāṃsāuttaramīmāṃsā, ‘examination of the later portion or jñāna-kāṇḍajñānakāṇḍajñānakāṇḍa [q.v.] of the Vedaveda’, is really the one sole orthodox exponent of the pantheistic creed of the Hindūs of the present day - a creed which underlies all the polytheism and multiform mythology of the people; its chief doctrine [as expounded by Śaṃkaraśaṃkara] is that of Advaitaadvaita i.e. that nothing really exists but the One Self or Soul of the Universe called Brahmanbrahman [neut.] or Paramâtmanparamātman, and that the Jīvâtmanjīvātman or individual human soul and indeed all the phenomena of nature are really identical with the Paramâtmanparamātman, and that their existence is only the result of Ajñānaajñāna [otherwise called Avidyāavidyā] or an assumed ignorance on the part of that one universal Soul which is described as both Creator and Creation; Actor and Act; Existence, Knowledge and Joy, and as devoid of the three qualities [see guṇaguṇa]; the liberation of the human soul, its deliverance from transmigrations, and re-union with the Paramâtmanparamātman, with which it is really identified, is only to be effected by a removal of that ignorance through a proper understanding of the Vedântavedānta; this system is also called Brahma-mīmāṉsābrahmamīmāṃsābrahmamīmāṃsā and Śārīrakamīmāṉsāśārīrakamīmāṃsā, ‘inquiring into Spirit or embodied Spirit’; the founder of the school is said to have been Vyāsavyāsa, also called Bādarāyaṇabādarāyaṇa, and its most eminent teacher was Śaṃkarâcāryaśaṃkarācārya), Up.; MBh. &c. [1017,1]
