• harihari—dvāradvāra
  • n. ‘Viṣṇuviṣṇu's gate’, N. of a celebrated town and sacred bathing-place (commonly called Hardvār, where the Ganges finally leaves the mountains for the plains of Hindūstān, whence it is sometimes called Gaṅgadvāra; it is called ‘Harihari's gate’, as leading to Vaikuṇṭhavaikuṇṭha or Viṣṇuviṣṇu's heaven), Rudray.; Buddh. [1290,1]
