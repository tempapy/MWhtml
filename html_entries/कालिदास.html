• kāli-dāsakālidāsadāsa m.
    • (fr. kālīkālīkālin, the goddess Durgā, and dāsadāsa, a slave, the final of kālīkālīkālin being shortened; cf. Pāṇ. vi, 3, 63), N. of a celebrated poet (author of the Śakuntalā, Vikramôrvaśī, Mālavikâgnimitra, Megha-dūta, and Raghu-vaṉśa; described as one of the nine gems of Vikramâditya's court, and variously placed in the first, second, third, and middle of the sixth century A.D.; the name is, however, applied to several persons, especially to two others who may have written the Nalôdaya and Śruta-bodha [hence the N. is used to denote the number, ‘three’], and seems, in some measure, to have been used as an honorary title). [279,3]
