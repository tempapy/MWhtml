• muṇḍakȏpaniṣadmuṇḍakopaniṣadmuṇḍakaupaniṣad f.
    • N. of a well-known Upaniṣad of the Atharva-veda (called also Ātharvaṇôpaniṣad and said to take its former name from the word muṇḍamuṇḍa, because every one who comprehends its sacred doctrine is ‘shorn’, i.e. liberated from all error, a similar idea being probably involved in the name of the Kṣurikopaniṣad or ‘Razor Upaniṣad’; cf. IW. 35, 39 &c.) [822,1]
