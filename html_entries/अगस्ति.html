• agasti m. (according to, Uṇ. iv, 179 fr. 2.a-gaa-gaaga, a mountain, and asti, thrower, √2. as). N. of a Ṛṣi (author of several Vedic hymns; said to have been the son of both Mitra and Varuṇa by Urvaśī; to have been born in a water-jar; to have been of short stature; to have swallowed the ocean, and compelled the Vindhya mountains to prostrate themselves before him; to have conquered and civilized the South; to have written on medicine, &c.) [4,3]
  • the star Canopus (of which Agastya is the regent, said to be the ‘cleanser of water’, because of turbid waters becoming clean at its rising, Ragh. xiii, 36) [4,3]
  • Agasti Grandiflora, Suśr. ([also -druagasti-drudru f., L.]) ⮩ agastidru [4,3]
