• arharh [Roots]
    • cl. 1. P. arhati, rarely Ā. arhate ([MBh. iii, 1580; R.]), (p. arkat [see below]; Ved. Inf. arhase [RV. x 77, 1]; perf. 3. pl. ānarhuḥ, Pāṇ. vi, 1, 39 Sch., but Ved. ānṛhuḥ [cf. ānṛcuḥ, √arcarc] Pāṇ. vi, 1, 36)
    • to deserve, merit, be worthy of, to have a claim to, be entitled to (acc.), to be allowed to do anything (Inf.);
    • to be obliged or required to do anything (acc.);
    • to be worth, counterbalance, to be able;
    • (arhasi, 2. sg. with an Inf. is often used as a softened form of Imper.; e.g. dātumdātu arhasi, ‘be pleased to give’; śrotumśrotu arhasi, ‘deign to listen’, for śṛṇu) :
    • Caus. (Opt. arhayet, Mn. iii, 3 and 119; aor., ārjihat, Bhaṭṭ.)
    • to honour. Desid. arjihiṣati ([cf. Gk. ἄρχω]). [93,3]
