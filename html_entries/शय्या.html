• śayyā f. (ifc. f(ā). ) a bed, couch, sofa (acc. with saṃ-√viśsaṃ-√viśsaṃ√viś or adhi-ṣṭhāadhi-ṣṭhāadhiṣṭhā [√sthā], ‘to go to bed or to rest’; with Caus. of ā-√ruhā-√ruhā√ruh, ‘to take [a woman] to bed, have sexual intercourse with’ [acc.]; śayyāyām ā-rūḍhaā-rūḍhaārūḍha mfn. ‘gone to bed, lying in bed’), ṢaḍvBr. &c. &c. [1056,1]
  • lying, reposing, sleeping, KātyŚr.; Mn.; MBh. &c. [1056,1]
  • resort, refuge (See comp.) [1056,1]
  • stringing together (esp. of words = gumphana or śabdagumpha), rhetorical composition or a partic. rhetor. figure, L. (‘couch’ and ‘rhetor. composition’ Kād., Introd., v. 8). [1056,1]
