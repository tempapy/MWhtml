  • na-nunanunanunū b ind.
    • (2. nana + nununū) not, not at all, never, RV.; AV.; (interr.) not? is it not? = nonne, AV.; ŚBr. &c. &c. (hence often =) certainly, surely, indeed, no doubt (esp. in questions amounting to an affirmation e.g. nanvnanu ahaṃahaṃahamahaah tetatāti priyaḥ, am I not your friend i.e. certainly I am your friend, Daś. [so also nana caca, there can be no doubt, Pāṇ. 2-3, 11; 29; Kāś.], or to a request e.g. nanunanu gacchāmi bhoḥbhoḥ, surely I may go, Pāṇ. viii, 1, 43; Kāś., and even as a responsive particle e.g. akārṣīḥ kaṭamnanu karomi bhoḥbhoḥ, indeed I have made it, ib.; with another interr. or an Impv. = pray, please e.g. nanunanu kokoku bhavānbhavabhavat, pray who are you? Mṛcch. x, 48/49; nanū̑cyatāmnanu, please tell, ib. 52/53; in argument often as an inceptive particle implying doubt or objection, ‘now it may be said, well, but then’ &c., esp. in nanvnanu astuastu, or nanunanu māmāma bhūt-tathā̑pitathāapiapīapāpiāpīāp, well, be it so or not so-nevertheless) [526,2]
    • a vocat. particle implying kindness or reproach or perplexity &c., L. [526,2]
