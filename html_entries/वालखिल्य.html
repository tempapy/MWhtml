• vālakhilyavālakhilya
  • n. (also written bāl°bālya, of doubtful derivation) N. of a collection of 11 (accord. to some only 6 or 8) hymns of the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda (commonly inserted after viii, 48, but numbered separately as a supplement by some editors; they are also called vālakhilyāḥvālakhilyāḥ, with or scil. mantrāḥmantrāḥ, or ṛcaḥṛcaḥ, and daśatīdaśatī vālakhilyakāvālakhilyakā), Br.; ŚrS. &c. [946,2]
  • (°lyavālakhilya) pl. N. of a class of Ṛṣiṛṣis of the size of a thumb (sixty thousand were produced from Brahmābrahmā's body and surround the chariot of the sun), TĀr.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. [946,2]
