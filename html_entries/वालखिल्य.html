• vālakhilyavālakhilya n.
    • (also written bāl°bālakhilya, of doubtful derivation) N. of a collection of 11 (accord. to some only 6 or 8) hymns of the Ṛg-veda (commonly inserted after viii, 48, but numbered separately as a supplement by some editors; they are also called vālakhilyāḥvālakhilyavālakhilyā, with or scil. mantrāḥmantra, or ṛcaḥṛcaṛc, and daśatīdaśatidaśat vālakhilyakā), Br.; ŚrS. &c. [946,2]
    • (°lyavālakhilya) pl. N. of a class of Ṛṣis of the size of a thumb (sixty thousand were produced from Brahmā's body and surround the chariot of the sun), TĀr.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. [946,2]
