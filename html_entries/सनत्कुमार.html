• sanatsanat—kumārakumāra
  • m. ‘always a youth’ or ‘son of Brahmābrahmā’, N. of one of the four or seven sons of Brahmābrahmā (cf. sanakasanaka; he is said to be the oldest of the progenitors of mankind [= vaidhātravaidhātra q.v.], and sometimes identified with Skandaskanda and Pradyumnapradyumna, he is also the supposed author of an Upa-purāṇaupapurāṇaupapurāṇa and other works; with Jainajainas he is one of the 12 Sārvabhaumasārvabhaumas or Cakravartincakravartins [emperors of India]; the N. of Sanat-kumārasanatkumārasanatkumāra is sometimes given to any great saint who retains youthful purity), ChUp.; MBh.; Hariv. &c.
