• viṭṭhalaviṭṭhala
  • m. (also written viṭhalaviṭhala, viṭhṭhalaviṭhṭhala, and viḍhḍhalaviḍhḍhala) N. of a god worshipped at Pandharpur in the Deccan (he is commonly called Viṭho-bāviṭhobāviṭhobā, and stated to be an incarnation of Viṣṇuviṣṇu or Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa himself, who is believed to have visited this city and infused a large portion of his essence into a Brāhman named Puṇḍarīkapuṇḍarīka or Puṇḍalīkapuṇḍalīka, who had gained a great reputation for filial piety; his images represent him standing on a brick [cf. 2. viḍviḍ] with his arms akimbo), RTL. 263 [961,3]
  • (also with ācāryaācārya, dīkṣitadīkṣita, bhaṭṭabhaṭṭa &c.) N. of various authors and teachers (esp. of a grammarian, disparaged by Bhaṭṭojibhaṭṭoji, and of a son of Vallabhâcāryavallabhācārya and successor to his chair, also called Viṭṭhala-dīkṣitaviṭṭhaladīkṣitaviṭṭhaladīkṣita or Viṭṭhala-nāthaviṭṭhalanāthaviṭṭhalanātha or Viṭṭhalêśaviṭṭhaleśa or Viṭṭhalêśvaraviṭṭhaleśvara, said to have been born in 1515), Cat. [961,3]
