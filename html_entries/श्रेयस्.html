• śreyasśreyas mfn.
    • (either compar. of śrīśrīśri, or rather accord. to native authorities of śrī-matśrīmatśrīmat or praśasyapraśasya; cf. Gk. κρείων) more splendid or beautiful, more excellent or distinguished, superior, preferable, better, better than (with abl. or with nana See below), RV. &c. &c. [1102,2]
    • most excellent, best, MBh. iii, 1256 [1102,2]
    • propitious, well disposed to (gen.), ib. i, 3020 [1102,2]
    • auspicious, fortunate, conducive to welfare or prosperity, Kāv.; Hit.; MārkP. [1102,2]
  • śreyasśreyas m.
    • (in astron.) N. of the second Muhūrta [1102,2]
    • of the third month (accord. to a partic. reckoning) [1102,2]
    • (with Jainas) N. of the 11th Arhat of the present Avasarpiṇī, L. [1102,2]
  • śreyasśreyas n.
    • (asasa) the better state, the better fortune or condition (sometimes used when the subject of a sentence would seem to require the masc. form), AV.; TS.; Br.; Kauś. [1102,2]
    • good (as opp. to ‘evil’), welfare, bliss, fortune, happiness, KaṭhUp.; MBh. &c. [1102,2]
    • the bliss of final emancipation, felicity (See śreyaḥ-pariśrāmaśreyaḥpariśrāmaśreyaḥśreyaspariśrāma, col. 3) [1102,2]
  • śreyasśreyas ind.
    • better, rather, rather than (used like varamvaramvara [q.v.] with nana; e.g. śreyośreyośreyas mṛtaṃmṛtamṛt nana jīvitamjīvita, ‘better is death and not life’ or ‘rather than life’, or ‘death is better than life’), MBh.; R. &c. [1102,2]
    • = dharmadharma, L. [1102,2]
    • N. of a Sāman, ĀrṣBr. [1102,2]
