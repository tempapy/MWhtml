• 1. krīkrī [Roots]
    • cl. 9. Ā. P. krīṇāti, krīṇīte (fut. p. kreṣyat, Lāṭy.; ind.p. krīvā, AV.; Mn.),
    • to buy, purchase (with instr. of the price, and abl. or gen. of the person from whom anything is bought e.g. kakakokukas imam indraṃindra daśabhir dhenubhirdhenu mamamama krīṇāti, who will buy this Indra of me for ten cows? RV. iv, 24, 10; yamyamya mātā-pitrormātā antikātantikātantika [or sakāśātsakāśa] krīṇīyāt whom he may buy from his father and mother, Mn. ix, 174; krīṇīṣva tadtadtat daśabhiḥ suvarṇaih, buy that for ten suvarṇas) :
    • Caus. P. krāpayati, Pāṇ. vi, 1, 48; [321,2]
