• 1. katikatikat
    • (fr. 2. kaka declined in pl. only, Gram. 227 aa; all the cases except the nom. voc. and acc. taking terminations, whereas the correlative itiitiit has become fixed as an indeclinable adverb), how many? quot? several (e.g. katikatikat devāḥ, how many gods? katikatikat vyāpādayati katikatikat vāvāvauū tāḍayati, some he kills and some he strikes). In the sense of ‘several’, ‘some’, katikatikat is generally followed by cidcid or apiapiapīap (e.g. katicid ahāniaha, for several or some days) [246,3]
    • it may be used as an adverb with cidcid in the sense of ‘oftentimes’, ‘much’, ‘in many ways’ (e.g. katicit stutaḥstutastut, much or often praised), RV. &c. [246,3]
  • katikatikat
    • [cf. Zd. caiti; Gk. πόστος; Lat. quot; cf. Sk. tatitatitat and Lat. tot.] [246,3]
  • 2. katikatikat
    • (for 1. See above) m. N. of a sage (son of Viśvā-mitra and ancestor of Kātyāyana), Hariv. [246,3]
