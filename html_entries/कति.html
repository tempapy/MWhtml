• katikati
  • (fr. 2. kaka declined in pl. only, Gram. 227 aa; all the cases except the nom. voc. and acc. taking terminations, whereas the correlative itiiti has become fixed as an indeclinable adverb), how many? quot? several (e.g. katikati devāḥdevāḥ, how many gods? katikati vyāpādayativyāpādayati katikati vāvā tāḍayatitāḍayati, some he kills and some he strikes). In the sense of ‘several’, ‘some’, katikati is generally followed by cidcid or apiapi (e.g. katicidkaticid ahāniahāni, for several or some days) [246,3]
  • it may be used as an adverb with cidcid in the sense of ‘oftentimes’, ‘much’, ‘in many ways’ (e.g. katicitkaticit stutaḥstutaḥ, much or often praised), RV. &c.; [246,3]
