• veṇīveṇī—saṃhārasaṃhāra
  • m. ‘binding up of the braided hair’ N. of a well-known drama by Bhaṭṭa-nārāyaṇabhaṭṭanārāyaṇabhaṭṭanārāyaṇa (who probably lived in the 9th century; its subject is taken from an incident narrated in the 2nd and 8th books of the Mahā-bhāratamahābhāratamahābhārata, in which is described how Yudhiṣṭhirayudhiṣṭhira gambled away all his possessions, including Draupadīdraupadī, and how Duḥ-śāsanaduḥśāsanaduḥśāsana then insulted Draupadīdraupadī by loosening her braided hair and dragging her away by her dishevelled locks, and how Bhīmabhīma, who witnessed the insult, swore that he would one day kill Duḥ-śāsanaduḥśāsanaduḥśāsana and drink his blood; this threat he fulfilled, and Draupadīdraupadī's hair was then bound up again; cf. MBh. ii, 2229-2235, viii, 4235). [1014,2]
