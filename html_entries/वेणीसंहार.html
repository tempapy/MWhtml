• veṇī—saṃhāra m. ‘binding up of the braided hair’ N. of a well-known drama by Bhaṭṭa-nārāyaṇabhaṭṭa-nārāyaṇabhaṭṭanārāyaṇa (who probably lived in the 9th century; its subject is taken from an incident narrated in the 2nd and 8th books of the Mahā-bhāratamahā-bhāratamahābhārata, in which is described how Yudhiṣṭhira gambled away all his possessions, including Draupadī, and how Duḥ-śāsanaduḥ-śāsanaduḥśāsana then insulted Draupadī by loosening her braided hair and dragging her away by her dishevelled locks, and how Bhīma, who witnessed the insult, swore that he would one day kill Duḥ-śāsanaduḥ-śāsanaduḥśāsana and drink his blood; this threat he fulfilled, and Draupadī's hair was then bound up again; cf. MBh. ii, 2229-2235, viii, 4235). [1014,2]
