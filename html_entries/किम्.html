• kimkim a ind.
    • (fr. 1. ki, originally nom. and acc. sg. n. of 2. kaka q.v.), what? how? whence? wherefore? why? [282,2]
  • kimkim b
    • is much used as a particle of interrogation like the Lat. num, an, sometimes translatable by ‘whether?’ but oftener serving only like a note of interrogation to mark a question (e.g. kiṃkiṃkim vyādhāvyādhāvyādha vanevanevanavanāvani 'smina saṃcaranti, ‘do hunters roam about in this wood?’ In an interrogation the verb, if uncompounded with a preposition, generally retains its accent after kimkim, Pāṇ. viii, 1, 44). To this sense may be referred the kimkim expressing inferiority, deficiency, &c. at the beginning of compounds (e.g. kiṃ-rājankiṃrājankiṃkimrājan, what sort of king? i.e. a bad king, Pāṇ. ii, 1, 64; v, 4, 70) [282,3]
    • also the kimkim prefixed to verbs with a similar meaning (e.g. kim-adhī̑tekimadhītaadhītiadhiadhīitaitiīti, he reads badly, Pāṇ. viii, 1, 44,Kāś.) kimkim—utauta, or kimkim—uta-vāutavāū or kimkim—athavāathavā—utauta, whether—or—or, R.; Śak.; Bhartṛ. &c. (cf. utauta.) [282,3]
