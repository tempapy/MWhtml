• ambū-kṛtaambūkṛtakṛta mfn.
    • (ambūambu used onomatopoetically to denote by trying to utter mb the effect caused by shutting the lips on pronouncing a vowel), pronounced indistinctly (so that the words remain too much in the mouth) [84,1]
    • [in later writers derived fr. ambuambu, water] sputtered, accompanied with saliva, Pat.; Lāṭy. (an-anambūkṛta, neg.) [84,1]
  • ambū-kṛtaambūkṛtakṛta n.
    • a peculiar indistinct pronunciation of the vowels, RPrāt.; Pat. [84,1]
    • roaring (of beasts) accompanied with emission of saliva, Uttarar.; Mālatīm. [84,1]
