• tattat—puruṣapuruṣa m.
    • the original or supreme spirit (one of the 5 forms of Īśvara [also °ṣa-vaktraṣavaktra] Sarvad. vii), Kāṭh. xvii, 1; TĀr., x, 1, 5 f.; LiṅgaP. i, 13 [433,2]
    • the servant of him, KātyŚr. vii, 1, 8 [433,2]
    • N. of a Kalpa period, MatsyaP. liii, 41 [433,2]
    • a class of compounds (formed like the word tat-puruṣatatpuruṣatatpuruṣa, ‘his servant’) in which the last member is qualified by the first without losing (as the last member of Bahu-vrīhi compounds) its grammatical independence (whether as noun or adj. or p.) [433,2]
    • two subdivisions of these compounds are called Karma-dhāraya and Dvi-gu (qq.vv.) [433,2]
