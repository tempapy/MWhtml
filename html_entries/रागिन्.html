• rāginrāgin mf(iṇī)n.
    • (fr. √rañjrañj, and rāgarāga) coloured, having a partic. colour (applied to a kind of Amaurosis or blindness when it affects the second membrane of the eye, as opp. to a-rāginarāginaārāgin, which affects the first), Suśr. [872,2]
    • colouring, dyeing, L. [872,2]
    • red, of a red colour, Pañcat.; Kathās. [872,2]
    • impassioned, affectionate, enamoured, passionately fond of or attached to or hankering after (loc. or comp.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [872,2]
    • gladdening, delighting, Mālatīm. [872,2]
  • rāginrāgin m.
    • a painter, L. [872,2]
    • a lover, libertine, ib. [872,2]
    • a sort of grain, L. [872,2]
