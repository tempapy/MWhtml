• madhva m. N. of the founder of a sect of Vaiṣṇavas in the south of India (he was a Kanarese Brāhman otherwise called Ānanda-tīrthaānanda-tīrthaānandatīrtha, Bhagavat-pāda or Madhu, said to have been born about 1200; his doctrine is commonly called Dvaita, ‘Duality’, in opposition to the A-dvaitaa-dvaitaadvaita, ‘Nonduality’, of the great Vedāntist Śaṃkarâcārya, and his sect are called Mādhvas), RTL. 130 &c. [782,3]
