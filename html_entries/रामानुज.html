• rāmā̑nujarāmānujarāmarāmāanuja m.
    • ‘younger brother of Rāma’ (this title would be applicable to Kṛṣṇa as born after Bala-rāma of the same father) [878,1]
    • N. of a celebrated Vaiṣṇava reformer (founder of a particular Vedantic school which taught the doctrine of viśiṣṭā̑dvaitaviśiṣṭādvaitaviśiṣṭaadvaita or qualified non-duality i.e. that the human spirit is separate and different from the one Supreme Spirit though dependent on it and ultimately to be united with it; he lived at Kāñcīpuram and Śrī-raṅgam in the South of India, in the 12th century, and is believed by his followers to have been an incarnation of Śeṣa; he is also called Rāmânujâcārya and Yati-rāja; n. or jamjamjajap matammatamat, Rāmânuja's doctrine), RTL. 119, 448 &c. [878,1]
