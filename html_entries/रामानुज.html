• rāmānujarāmānuja
  • m. ‘younger brother of Rāmarāma’ (this title would be applicable to Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa as born after Bala-rāmabalarāmabalarāma of the same father) [878,1]
  • N. of a celebrated Vaiṣṇavavaiṣṇava reformer (founder of a particular Vedantic school which taught the doctrine of viśiṣṭādvaitaviśiṣṭādvaita or qualified non-duality i.e. that the human spirit is separate and different from the one Supreme Spirit though dependent on it and ultimately to be united with it; he lived at Kāñcīpuram and Śrī-raṅgam in the South of India, in the 12th century, and is believed by his followers to have been an incarnation of Śeṣaśeṣa; he is also called Rāmânujâcāryarāmānujācārya and Yati-rājayatirājayatirāja; n. or jamjam matammatam, Rāmânujarāmānuja's doctrine), RTL. 119, 448 &c. [878,1]
  • (with dīkṣitadīkṣita) N. of another author, Cat. [878,1]
