• śakuntalāśakuntalā
  • f. (said to be fr. śakuntaśakunta) N. of a daughter of the Apsarasapsaras Menakāmenakā by Viśvāmitraviśvāmitra (she was supposed to have been born and left in a forest, where she was protected by birds till found by the sage Kaṇvakaṇva, who took her to his hermitage and reared her as his daughter; she was there seen by king Duṣyantaduṣyanta, when on a hunting expedition, and married by him, and became the mother of Bharatabharata, sovereign of all India; the story of Duṣyantaduṣyanta's accidental meeting with Śakuntalāśakuntalā, their marriage, separation, his repudiation of her through temporary loss of memory caused by a curse, his subsequent recognition of her by means of a ring which was lost but afterwards recovered, forms the subject of Kālidāsakālidāsa's celebrated drama called Abhijñāna-śakuntalāabhijñānaśakuntalāabhijñānaśakuntalā q.v.). [1046,3]
