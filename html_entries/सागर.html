• sāgarasāgara m.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ; fr. 2. sa-garasagarasagara) the ocean (said to have been named so by Bhagīratha after his son Sagara [see 2. sa-garasagarasagara, p. 1125]; another legend asserts that the bed of the ocean was dug by the sons of Sagara; 3 or 4 or 7 oceans are reckoned, cf. 1. sam-udrasamudrasamudra; sāgarasyasāgara phenaḥ = samudraph°), Mn.; MBh. &c. [1198,2]
    • an ocean (as expressing any vast body or inexhaustible mass; often ifc. cf. guṇa-śoka-guṇaśokasāgara, saṃsāra-s°saṃsārasāgarasaṃsārasāgara) [1198,2]
    • a symbolical expression for the number, ‘four’ (like other words signifying ‘ocean’), Gaṇit. [1198,2]
    • a partic. high number (= 10 Padmas), Pur. [1198,2]
    • a sort of deer, L. [1198,2]
    • N. of a serpent-demon, Kāraṇḍ. [1198,2]
    • (with Jainas) of the third Arhat of the past Utsarpiṇī, L. [1198,2]
    • of one of the 10 orders of mendicants traced back to disciples of Śaṃkarācārya, Cat. [1198,2]
    • of various persons, HPariś. [1198,2]
    • of two authors and of a wk. on Dharma, Cat. [1198,2]
    • of a place, ib. [1198,2]
    • (pl.) the sons of Sagara, MBh.; R. [1198,2]
  • sāgarasāgara n.
    • N. of a town, Buddh. [1198,2]
  • sāgarasāgara mf(īīiin)n.
    • relating to the sea, marine, Hariv. [1198,2]
