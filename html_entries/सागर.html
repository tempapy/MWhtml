• sāgara m. (ifc. f(ā). ; fr. 2. sa-garasa-garasagara) the ocean (said to have been named so by Bhagīratha after his son Sagara [see 2. sa-garasa-garasagara, p.1125]; another legend asserts that the bed of the ocean was dug by the sons of Sagara; 3 or 4 or 7 oceans are reckoned cf. 1, sam-udrasam-udrasamudra; sāgarasya phenaḥ = samudraph°), Mn.; MBh. &c. [1198,2]
  • an ocean (as expressing any vast body or inexhaustible mass; often ifc. cf. guṇa-śoka-guṇaśokaguṇa-śoka-sāgara, saṃsāra-s°saṃsāra-sāgarasaṃsārasāgara) [1198,2]
  • a symbolical expression for the number, ‘four’ (like other words signifying ‘ocean’), Gaṇit. [1198,2]
  • a partic. high number (= 10 Padmas), Pur. [1198,2]
  • a sort of deer, L. [1198,2]
  • N. of a serpent-demon, Kāraṇḍ. [1198,2]
  • (with Jainas) of the third Arhat of the past Utsarpiṇī, L. [1198,2]
  • of one of the 10 orders of mendicants traced back to disciples of Śaṃkarâcārya, Cat. [1198,2]
  • of various persons, HPariś. [1198,2]
  • of two authors and of a wk. on Dharma, Cat. [1198,2]
  • of a place, ib. [1198,2]
  • (pl.) the sons of Sagara, MBh.; R. [1198,2]
  • sāgara n. N. of a town, Buddh. [1198,2]
  • sāgara mf(ī)n. relating to the sea, marine, Hariv. [1198,2]
