• ut-thāut-thāutthā (ud-√sthāud-√sthāud√sthā Pāṇ. viii, 4, 61; cf. ut-tambhut-tambhuttambh, col.1) P. Ā. (but not Ā. in the sense of, ‘rising, standing up’ Pāṇ. i, 3, 24) -tiṣṭhatiutthā-tiṣṭhatitiṣṭhati, -teutthā-tete (pf. -tasthauutthā-tasthautasthau aor. -asthātutthā-asthātasthāt &c.) to stand up, spring up, rise, raise one's self, set out, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; Ragh.; Śak.; Bhag. &c.;
    to rise (from the dead), BhP.;
    to rise (from any occupation), leave off;
    to finish, AitBr.; ŚBr.; TāṇḍyaBr. &c.;
    to come forth, arise, appear, become visible, result;
    to spring, originate from, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; TS.; MBh.; Kathās. &c.;
    to come in (as revenues), Śak.;
    to rise (for the performance of any action);
    to be active or brave;
    to make efforts, take pains with, strive for;
    to excel, MBh.; R. &c.:
    Caus. -thāpayatiutthā-thāpayatithāpayati (aor. 1. sg. ud-atiṣṭhipamud-atiṣṭhipamudatiṣṭhipam, AV. vii, 95, 2) to cause to stand up, raise, rouse, start, AV.; AitBr.; TBr.; ŚBr.; MBh.; Daś.; BhP. &c.;
    to set up, lift up, erect, Gobh.; MBh.; R.; Hit. &c.;
    to get out, Hit.;
    to drive out, send out, push out, AitBr. : Kathās.; BhP.;
    to excite;
    to produce, Ragh.; Sāh.;
    to arouse, awaken, raise to life, make alive, animate;
    to stir up, agitate, ŚBr.; KauṣUp.; Hariv.; R.; Kathās. &c.:
    Desid. -tiṣṭhāsatiutthā-tiṣṭhāsatitiṣṭhāsati, to wish or intend to stand up, ŚBr. xi, 1, 6, 5;
    to intend to leave off (a sacrifice), Nyāyam. [179,3]
