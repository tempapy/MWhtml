[The word śrīśrī is frequently used as an honorific prefix (= ‘sacred’, ‘holy’) to the names of deities (e.g. Śri-Durgāśrīdurgāśrīdurgā, Śrī-Rāmaśrīrāmaśrīrāma), and may be repeated two, three, or even four times to express excessive veneration. (e.g. Śrī-śrī-Durgāśrīśrīdurgāśrīśrīdurgā &c.); it is also used as a respectful title (like ‘Reverend’) to the names of eminent persons as well as of celebrated works and sacred objects (e.g. Śrī-Jayadevaśrījayadevaśrījayadeva, ŚrīBhāgavataśrībhāgavata), and is often placed at the beginning or back of letters, manuscripts, important documents &c.; also before the words caraṇacaraṇa and pādapāda ‘feet’, and even the end of personal names.] [1098,3]
