  • kāmamkāmamkāma b ind.
    • (acc. of kāmakāma g. svarā̑disvarasvarāadādiādin, not in Kāś.) according to wish or desire, according to inclination, agreeably to desire, at will, freely, willingly, RV.; TS.; AitBr.; ŚBr.; ChUp.; MBh.; R. &c. [273,1]
    • with pleasure, readily, gladly, MBh. iii, 298; Ragh. [273,1]
    • (as a particle of assent) well, very well, granted, admitted that, indeed, really, surely, MBh. iii, 17195; R. v, 24, 4; Śak.; Bhartṛ. [273,1]
    • well and good, in any case, at any rate, MBh. iii, 310, 19; R. iv, 9, 105; v, 53, 11; Śak.; Dhūrtas. [273,1]
    • (with nana, ‘in no case’ R. iii, 56, 17) [273,1]
    • granted that, in spite of that, notwithstanding, R. iv, 16, 50; Pañcat. &c. [273,1]
    • though, although, supposing that (usually with Impv.), R. vi, 95, 49 and 56; Ragh. ii, 43; Śāntiś. (kāmaṃ-nakāmamna or nana tutu or nana caca, rather than e.g. kāmamkāmamkāma āāaās maraṇātmaraṇa tiṣṭhed gṛhegṛhagṛh kanyākanyākanyakanī - nananāninonunas enāmenā prayacchet tutu guṇa-hīnāyaguṇahīnaguṇahīna, ‘rather should a girl stay at home till her death, than that he should give her to one void of excellent qualities’ Mn. ix, 89; the negative sentence with nana or natu or nana caca may also precede, or its place may be taken by an interrogative sentence e.g. kāmaṃkāmamkāma nayatu māṃmāmmāma devaḥdeva kimkim ardhenā̑tmano hihihī mememamā, ‘rather let the god take me, what is the use to me of half my existence?’ BhP. vii, 2, 54 ; kāmaṃkāmamkāma - tutu or kiṃkiṃkim tutu or caca or punarpunar or athā̑piathāpiathaathāapiapāpiāpī or tathā̑pitathāapiapāpiāpī, well, indeed, surely, truly, granted, though - however, notwithstanding, nevertheless e.g. kāmaṃkāmamkāma tvayā parityaktāparityakta gamiṣyāmi - imaṃ tutu bālaṃbāla saṃtyaktuṃ, nā̑rhasinanā, ‘granted that forsaken by thee I shall go - this child however thou must not forsake’ MBh. i, 3059; or the disjunctive particles may be left out, R.; Ragh. ii, 43; Śāntiś.; yady-api-kāmaṃyadiapikāmamkāma tathā̑pitathāapiapāpiāpī, though - nevertheless, Prab.)
