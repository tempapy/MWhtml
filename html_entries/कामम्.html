  • kāmamkāmamkāma b ind.
    • (acc. of kāmakāma g. svarā̑disvarasvarāadādiādīādin, not in Kāś.) according to wish or desire, according to inclination, agreeably to desire, at will, freely, willingly, RV.; TS.; AitBr.; ŚBr.; ChUp.; MBh.; R. &c. [273,1]
    • with pleasure, readily, gladly, MBh. iii, 298; Ragh. [273,1]
    • (as a particle of assent) well, very well, granted, admitted that, indeed, really, surely, MBh. iii, 17195; R. v, 24, 4; Śak.; Bhartṛ. [273,1]
    • well and good, in any case, at any rate, MBh. iii, 310, 19; R. iv, 9, 105; v, 53, 11; Śak.; Dhūrtas. [273,1]
    • (with nana, ‘in no case’, R. iii, 56, 17) [273,1]
    • granted that, in spite of that, notwithstanding, R. iv, 16, 50; Pañcat. &c. [273,1]
    • though, although, supposing that (usually with Impv.), R. vi, 95, 49 and 56; Ragh. ii, 43; Śāntiś. (kāmaṃ-nakāmamna or nana tutu or nana caca, rather than, e.g. kāmamkāmamkāma āāaāsas maraṇātmaraṇa tiṣṭhed gṛhegṛhagṛhigṛh kanyākanyākanyakanī - nananāninonunas enāmenāena prayacchet tutu guṇa-hīnāyaguṇahīnaguṇahīna, ‘rather should a girl stay at home till her death, than that he should give her to one void of excellent qualities’, Mn. ix, 89; the negative sentence with nana or natu or nana caca may also precede, or its place may be taken by an interrogative sentence, e.g. kāmaṃkāmamkāma nayatu māṃ devaḥdeva kimkimki ardhenā̑tmano hihihī mememamāmi, ‘rather let the god take me, what is the use to me of half my existence?’, BhP. vii, 2, 54 ; kāmaṃ - tutu or kiṃkiṃkimki tutu or caca or punarpunar or athā̑piathāpiathaathāapiapīapāpiāpīāp or tathā̑pitathāapiapīapāpiāpīāp, well, indeed, surely, truly, granted, though - however, notwithstanding, nevertheless, e.g. kāmaṃkāmamkāma tvayātva parityaktāparityakta gamiṣyāmi - imaṃima tutu bālaṃ saṃtyaktuṃ, nā̑rhasinanā, ‘granted that forsaken by thee I shall go - this child however thou must not forsake’, MBh. i, 3059; or the disjunctive particles may be left out, R.; Ragh. ii, 43; Śāntiś.; yady-api-kāmaṃyadiapi tathā̑pitathāapiapīapāpiāpīāp, though - nevertheless, Prab.)
