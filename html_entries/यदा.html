• yadāyadāyadayad a ind.
    • (fr. 3. yaya) when, at what time, whenever (generally followed by the correlatives tadātadātad, tatastatastatatat, tarhitarhi, in Veda also by ātāta, ādāta īt, athaatha, adhaadha and tadtad), RV. &c. &c. (yadāyadāyadayad yadāyadāyadayad, followed by tadātadātad or tadātadātad tadātadātad, ‘as often as - so often’, ‘whenever’; yadāyadāyadayad - tadātadātad id., with repeated verbs, e.g. Hit. i, 197; yada͡iva-tada͡ivayadayadāevatadāeva, ‘when indeed - then indeed’, Śak. yadāyadāyadayad prabhṛtiprabhṛti - tadātadātad prabhṛtiprabhṛti, ‘from whatever time - from that time forward’, R.; yada͡ivayadayadāeva khalukhalu - tadātadātad prabhrity evaeva, ‘as soon as - thence forward’, Śak.; yadāyadāyadayad kadākadākadakad caca, ‘as often as’, ‘whenever’, RV.; yadāyadāyadayad kadākadākadakad citcit, ‘at any time’, Kauś.; yadāyadāyadayad tadātadātad, ‘always’, Naiṣ.; the copula after yadāyadāyadayad is often dropped, esp. after a participle, e.g. yadāyadāyadayad kṣayaṃ gataṃgatagat sarvamsarvamsarva, ‘when all had gone to ruin’, R. yadāyadāyadayad is sometimes joined with other relatives used indefinitely, e.g. yoyuyasyaī 'tti yasyayasyaya yadāyadāyadayad māṃsammāṃsamāṃs, ‘when any one eats the flesh of any one’, Hit.)
