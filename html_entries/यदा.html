• yadāyadā
  • ind. (fr. 3. yaya) when, at what time, whenever (generally followed by the correlatives tadātadā, tatastatas, tarhitarhi, in Vedaveda also by ātāt, ādād ītīt, athaatha, adhaadha and tadtad), RV. &c. &c. (yadāyadā yadāyadā, followed by tadātadā or tadātadā tadātadā, ‘as often as - so often’, ‘whenever’; yadāyadā - tadātadā id., with repeated verbs e.g. Hit. i, 197; yadaiva-tadaivayadaivatadaivayadaivatadaiva, ‘when indeed - then indeed’ Śak. yadāyadā prabhṛtiprabhṛti - tadātadā prabhṛtiprabhṛti, ‘from whatever time - from that time forward’ R.; yadaivayadaiva khalukhalu - tadātadā prabhrityprabhrity evaeva, ‘as soon as - thence forward’ Śak.; yadāyadā kadākadā caca, ‘as often as’, ‘whenever’ RV.; yadāyadā kadākadā citcit, ‘at any time’ Kauś.; yadāyadā tadātadā, ‘always’ Naiṣ.; the copula after yadāyadā is often dropped, esp. after a participle, e.g. yadāyadā kṣayaṃkṣayaṃ gataṃgataṃ sarvamsarvam, ‘when all had gone to ruin’ R. yadāyadā is sometimes joined with other relatives used indefinitely, e.g. yoyo 'ttitti yasyayasya yadāyadā māṃsammāṃsam, ‘when any one eats the flesh of any one’ Hit.)
