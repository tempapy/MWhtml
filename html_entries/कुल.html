• kula n. (ifc. f(ā). ) a herd, troop, flock, assemblage, multitude, number, &c. (of quadrupeds, birds, insects, &c., or of inanimate objects e.g. go-kulago-kulagokula, a herd of cows, R. &c.; mahiṣīk°, a herd of female buffaloes, Ṛtus.; ali-k°ali-k°alikula, a swarm of bees, Śiś.; Gīt. &c.; alaka-k°alaka-k°alakakula, a multitude of curls, BhP.) [294,2]
  • a race, family, community, tribe, caste, set, company (e.g. brāhmaṇa-k°brāhmaṇa-k°brāhmaṇakula, the caste of the Brāhmans, BhP.; padātīnāṃ kula, infantry, Rājat. v, 247) [294,2]
  • (ifc. with a gen. sg.) a lot, gang (e.g. caurasya-k°caurasya-k°caurasyakula, a gang of thieves), Pāṇ. vi, 3, 21; Kāś. [294,2]
  • the residence of a family, seat of a community, inhabited country (as much ground as can be ploughed by two ploughs each drawn by six bulls Comm. on Mn. vii, 119) [294,3]
  • a house, abode, MBh. [294,3]
  • a noble or eminent family or race, Mn.; MBh. &c. [294,3]
  • high station (in comp. ‘chief, principal’ cf. kula-girikula-girikulagiri, &c.) [294,3]
  • the body, L. [294,3]
  • the front, forepart, W. [294,3]
  • a blue stone, L. [294,3]
  • (with Śāktas) N. of Śakti and of the rites observed in her worship (cf. kaula) [294,3]
  • = kula-nakṣatrakula-nakṣatrakulanakṣatra q.v., Tantras. [294,3]
  • kula m. the chief of a corporation or guild, L. [294,3]
  • = kula-vārakula-vārakulavāra q.v., Tantras. [294,3]
  • N. of a man, R. vii, 43, 2 [294,3]
