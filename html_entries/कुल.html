• kulakula n.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ) a herd, troop, flock, assemblage, multitude, number, &c. (of quadrupeds, birds, insects, &c., or of inanimate objects e.g. go-kulagokulagokula, a herd of cows, R. &c. mahiṣīk°, a herd of female buffaloes, Ṛtus.; ali-k°ali, a swarm of bees, Śiś.; Gīt. &c. alaka-k°alaka, a multitude of curls, BhP.) [294,2]
    • a race, family, community, tribe, caste, set, company (e.g. brāhmaṇa-k°brāhmaṇa, the caste of the Brāhmans, BhP.; padātīnāṃpadāti kulakula, infantry, Rājat. v, 247) [294,2]
    • (ifc. with a gen. sg.) a lot, gang (e.g. caurasya-k°, a gang of thieves), Pāṇ. vi, 3, 21; Kāś. [294,2]
    • the residence of a family, seat of a community, inhabited country (as much ground as can be ploughed by two ploughs each drawn by six bulls Comm. on Mn. vii, 119) [294,3]
    • a house, abode, MBh. [294,3]
    • a noble or eminent family or race, Mn.; MBh. &c. [294,3]
    • high station (in comp. ‘chief, principal’ cf. kula-girikulagirikulagiri, &c.) [294,3]
    • the body, L. [294,3]
    • the front, forepart, W. [294,3]
    • a blue stone, L. [294,3]
    • (with Śāktas) N. of Śakti and of the rites observed in her worship (cf. kaulakaula) [294,3]
    • = kula-nakṣatrakulanakṣatrakulanakṣatra q.v., Tantras. [294,3]
  • kulakula m.
    • the chief of a corporation or guild, L. [294,3]
    • = kula-vārakulavārakulavāra q.v., Tantras. [294,3]
    • N. of a man, R. vii, 43, 2 [294,3]
