• 1. aa
    • the first letter of the alphabet [1,1]
    • the first short vowel inherent in consonants. [1,1]
  • 2. aa
    • (pragṛhyapragṛhya q.v.), a vocative particle ([aa anantaananta, O Viṣṇu]), T. [1,1]
    • interjection of pity, Ah! [1,1]
  • 3. aa
    • (before a vowel ananat, exc. a-ṛṇinaṛṇinaeoasṛṇin), a prefix corresponding to Gk. ἀ, ἀν, Lat. in, Goth. and Germ. un, Eng. in or un, and having a negative or privative or contrary sense (an-ekaanekaaneka not one; an-antaanantaananta endless; a-satasatasadasat not good; a-paśyatapaśyatapaśyat not seeing) [1,1]
    • rarely prefixed to Inf. (a-svaptuma not to sleep, TāṇḍyaBr.) and even to forms of the finite verb (a-spṛhayantia they do not desire, BhP.; Śiś.) and to pronouns (a-saḥasaasa not he, Śiś.; a-tadatadatad not that, BhP.) [1,1]
    • occasionally denoting comparison (a-brāhmaṇaabrāhmaṇaabrāhmaṇa like a Brahman, T.) [1,1]
    • sometimes disparagement (a-yajñaayajñaayajña a miserable sacrifice) [1,1]
    • sometimes diminutiveness (cf. a-karṇaakarṇaakarṇa, an-udarāanudaraanudara) [1,1]
    • rarely an expletive (cf. a-kupyaakupyaakupya, a-pūpaapūpaapūpa). According to, Pāṇ. vi, 2, 161, the accent may be optionally either on the first or last syllable in certain compounds formed with aa (as a-tīkṣṇaatīkṣṇaatīkṣṇa or a-tīkṣṇaatīkṣṇaatīkṣṇa, a-śuciaśuciaśuciśucīśucinśuc or a-śuciaśuciaśuciśucīśucinśuc, an-annaanannaananna or an-annaanannaananna) [1,1]
    • the same applies to stems ending in tṛtṛ accentuated on the first syllable before aa is prefixed [1,1]
    • cf. also a-tūrtaatūrtaatūrta and a-tūrtaatūrtaatūrta, a-bhinnaabhinnaabhinna and a-bhinnaabhinnaabhinna, &c. [1,1]
  • aa 4
    • the base of some pronouns and pronom. forms, in asyaa, atraatra, &c. [1,1]
  • 5. aa
    • the augment prefixed to the root in the formation of the imperfect, aorist, and conditional tenses (in the Veda often wanting, as in Homer, the fact being that originally the augment was only prefixed in principal sentences where it was accentuated, whilst it was dropped in subordinate sentences where the root-vowel took the accent). [1,1]
  • 6. aa m.
    • N. of Viṣṇu, L. (especially as the first of the three sounds in the sacred syllable omom). [1,1]
