• a the first letter of the alphabet [1,1]
  • the first short vowel inherent in consonants. [1,1]
  • a (pragṛhya q.v.), a vocative particle ([a ananta, O Viṣṇu]), T. [1,1]
  • interjection of pity, Ah! [1,1]
  • a (before a vowel an, exc. a-ṛṇina-ṛṇinaṛṇin), a prefix corresponding to Gk. ἀ, ἀν, Lat. in, Goth. and Germ. un, Eng. in or un, and having a negative or privative or contrary sense (an-ekaan-ekaaneka not one; an-antaan-antaananta endless; a-sata-satasat not good; a-paśyata-paśyatapaśyat not seeing) [1,1]
  • rarely prefixed to Inf. (a-svaptuma-svaptumasvaptum not to sleep, TāṇḍyaBr.) and even to forms of the finite verb (a-spṛhayantia-spṛhayantiaspṛhayanti they do not desire, BhP.; Śiś.) and to pronouns (a-saḥa-saḥasaḥ not he, Śiś.; a-tada-tadatad not that, BhP.) [1,1]
  • occasionally denoting comparison (a-brāhmaṇaa-brāhmaṇaabrāhmaṇa like a Brahman, T.) [1,1]
  • sometimes disparagement (a-yajñaa-yajñaayajña a miserable sacrifice) [1,1]
  • sometimes diminutiveness (cf. a-karṇaa-karṇaakarṇa, an-udarāan-udarāanudarā) [1,1]
  • rarely an expletive (cf. a-kupyaa-kupyaakupya, a-pūpaa-pūpaapūpa). According to, Pāṇ. vi, 2, 161, the accent may be optionally either on the first or last syllable in certain compounds formed with a (as a-tīkṣṇaa-tīkṣṇaatīkṣṇa or a-tīkṣṇaa-tīkṣṇaatīkṣṇa, a-śucia-śuciaśuci or a-śucia-śuciaśuci, an-annaan-annaananna or an-annaan-annaananna) [1,1]
  • the same applies to stems ending in tṛ accentuated on the first syllable before a is prefixed [1,1]
  • cf. also a-tūrtaa-tūrtaatūrta and a-tūrtaa-tūrtaatūrta, a-bhinnaa-bhinnaabhinna and a-bhinnaa-bhinnaabhinna, &c. [1,1]
  • a 4 the base of some pronouns and pronom. forms, in asya, atra, &c. [1,1]
  • a the augment prefixed to the root in the formation of the imperfect, aorist, and conditional tenses (in the Veda often wanting, as in Homer, the fact being that originally the augment was only prefixed in principal sentences where it was accentuated, whilst it was dropped in subordinate sentences where the root-vowel took the accent). [1,1]
  • a m. N. of Viṣṇu, L. (especially as the first of the three sounds in the sacred syllable om). [1,1]
