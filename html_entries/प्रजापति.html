• prajāprajāpraja—patipatipat
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) m. ‘lord of creatures’, N. of Savitṛ, Soma, Agni, Indra &c., RV.; AV. [658,2]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) a divinity presiding over procreation, protector of life, ib.; VS.; Mn.; Suśr.; BhP. [658,2]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) lord of creatures, creator, RV. &c. &c. (N. of a supreme god above or among the Vedic deities [RV. (only x, 21, 10); AV.; VS.; Br.] but in later times also applied to Viṣṇu, Śiva, Time personified, the sun, fire, &c., and to various progenitors, esp. to the 10 lords of created beings first created by Brahmā, viz. Marīci, Atri, Aṅgiras, Pulastya, Pulaka, Kratu, Vasiṣṭha, Pracetas or Dakṣa, Bhṛgu, Nārada [Mn. i, 34; cf. IW. 206 n. 1], of whom some authorities count only the first 7, others the last 3) [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) a father, L. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) a king, prince, L. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) a son-in-law, L. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) the planet Mars, a partic. star, δ Aurigae, Sūryas. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) (in astrol.) = 2. kāla-narakālanarakālanara q.v. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) a species of insect, L. [658,3]
    • (°jā-jāprajāpatiprajāpatī) N. of sev. men and authors, Cat. [658,3]
