• yadiyadiyad ind.
    • (in Veda also yadīyadiyad, sometimes yadiyadiyad citcit, yadiyadiyad haha vaivaiuū, yadī̑tyadiitididh, yadyyadiyad uuū, yadyyadiyad uuū vaivaiuū) if, in case that, RV. &c. &c. In the earlier language yadiyadiyad may be joined with Indic. Subj. or Leṭ Pot., or Fut., the consequent clause of the conditional sentence being generally without any particle. In the later language yadiyadiyad may be joined with Pres. (followed in consequent clause by another Pres., e.g. yadiyadiyad jīvatijīvat bhadrāṇibhadra paśyatipaśyat, ‘if he lives he beholds prosperity’, or by fut. or by Impv. or by Pot. or by no verb) ; or it may be joined with Pot. (e.g. yadiyadiyad rājārājarāj daṇḍaṃdaṇḍa nana praṇayet, ‘if the king were not to inflict punishment’, followed by another Pot. or by Cond. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by fut. or by no verb); or it may be joined with fut. (e.g. yadiyadiyad nana kariṣyantikariṣyat tattat, ‘if they will not do that’, followed by another fut. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by no verb); or it may be joined with Cond. (e.g. yadyyadiyad anujñāmanujñā adāsyatadāsyat, ‘if he should give permission’, followed by another Cond. or by Pot. or by aor.); or it may be joined with aor. (e.g. yadiyadiyad prajā-patirprajā nana vapurvapur arsrākṣīt, ‘if the Creator had not created the body’, followed by Cond. or by Pot. or by pf.); or it may be joined with Impv. or even with pf. (e.g. yadyyadiyad āhaāha, ‘if he had said’). There may be other constructions, and in the consequent clauses some one of the following may be used: athaatha, atraatra, tadtad, tenatenata, tatastatastatatat, tataḥtatastatatat paramparampara, tadātadātad, tarhitarhi, tadānīmtadānīmtadānīṃ. Observe that yadiyadiyad may sometimes = ‘as sure as’ (esp. in asseverations, followed by Impv. with or without tathātathā or tenatenata or followed by Pot. with tadtad), MBh.; Kāv. &c. or it may = ‘whether’ (followed by Pres. or Pot. or no verb, e.g. yadi-nayadina vāvāvauū, ‘whether-or not’, and sometimes kimkimkikiṃ is added), ib.; or it may = ‘that’ (after verbs of ‘not believing’ or ‘doubting’, with Pres. or Pot., e.g. nā̑śaṃsenanāāśaṃsaāśaṃsāāśaṃs yadiyadiyad jīvantijīvantijīvantījīvat, ‘I do not expect that they are alive’ cf. Pāṇ. iii, 3, 147, Sch.); or if placed after duṣkaraṃ or kathaṃkathaṃkathamkath cidcid it may = ‘hardly’, ‘scarcely’, MBh.; R.; or it may = ‘if perchance’, ‘perhaps’ (with Pot. with or without itiitiit, or with fut. or pres.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. The following are other combinations: yadiyadiyad tāvattāvat, ‘how would it be if’ (with Pres. or Impv.) [844,3]
    • yadiyadiyad nāmanāma, ‘if ever’ [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad cȇtcetcaitididh (cȇtcetcedcaitididh being added redundantly) = ‘if’ (e.g. yadiyadiyad cȇtcetcait syātsyātsyasyād, ‘if it should be’) [845,1]
    • purāpurāpurapuras yadiyadiyad = ‘before’ (e.g. purāpurāpurapuras yadiyadiyad paśyāmi, ‘before that I see’) [845,1]
