• yadiyadi
  • ind. (in Vedaveda also yadīyadī, sometimes yadiyadi citcit, yadiyadi haha vaivai, yadī̑tyadīt, yadyyady uu, yadyyady uu vaivai) if, in case that, RV. &c. &c. In the earlier language yadiyadi may be joined with Indic. Subj. or Leṭleṭ Pot., or Fut., the consequent clause of the conditional sentence being generally without any particle. In the later language yadiyadi may be joined with Pres. (followed in consequent clause by another Pres. e.g. yadiyadi jīvatijīvati bhadrāṇibhadrāṇi paśyatipaśyati, ‘if he lives he beholds prosperity’, or by fut. or by Impv. or by Pot. or by no verb) ; or it may be joined with Pot. (e.g. yadiyadi rājārājā daṇḍaṃdaṇḍaṃ nana praṇayetpraṇayet, ‘if the king were not to inflict punishment’, followed by another Pot. or by Cond. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by fut. or by no verb); or it may be joined with fut. (e.g. yadiyadi nana kariṣyantikariṣyanti tattat, ‘if they will not do that’, followed by another fut. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by no verb); or it may be joined with Cond. (e.g. yadyyady anujñāmanujñām adāsyatadāsyat, ‘if he should give permission’, followed by another Cond. or by Pot. or by aor.); or it may be joined with aor. (e.g. yadiyadi prajā-patirprajāpatirprajāpatir nana vapurvapur arsrākṣītarsrākṣīt, ‘if the Creator had not created the body’, followed by Cond. or by Pot. or by pf.); or it may be joined with Impv. or even with pf. (e.g. yadyyady āhaāha, ‘if he had said’). There may be other constructions, and in the consequent clauses some one of the following may be used: athaatha, atraatra, tadtad, tenatena, tatastatas, tataḥtataḥ paramparam, tadātadā, tarhitarhi, tadānīmtadānīm. Observe that yadiyadi may sometimes = ‘as sure as’ (esp. in asseverations, followed by Impv. with or without tathātathā or tenatena or followed by Pot. with tadtad), MBh.; Kāv. &c.; or it may = ‘whether’ (followed by Pres. or Pot. or no verb e.g. yadi-nayadinayadina vāvā, ‘whether-or not’, and sometimes kimkim is added), ib.; or it may = ‘that’ (after verbs of ‘not believing’ or ‘doubting’, with Pres. or Pot. e.g. nā̑śaṃsenāśaṃse yadiyadi jīvantijīvanti, ‘I do not expect that they are alive’ cf. Pāṇ. iii, 3, 147 Sch.); or if placed after duṣkaraṃduṣkaraṃ or kathaṃkathaṃ cidcid it may = ‘hardly’, ‘scarcely’ MBh.; R.; or it may = ‘if perchance’, ‘perhaps’ (with Pot. with or without itiiti, or with fut. or pres.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. The following are other combinations: yadiyadi tāvattāvat, ‘how would it be if’ (with Pres. or Impv.) [844,3]
  • yadiyadi nāmanāma, ‘if ever’ [845,1]
  • yadiyadi cȇtcet (cȇtcet being added redundantly) = ‘if’ (e.g. yadiyadi cȇtcet syātsyāt, ‘if it should be’) [845,1]
  • purāpurā yadiyadi = ‘before’ (e.g. purāpurā yadiyadi paśyāmipaśyāmi, ‘before that I see’) [845,1]
  • yadyyady apiapi (rarely apiapi yadiyadi), ‘even if’, ‘although’ (followed by tathā̑pitathāpi or tadtad apiapi or sometimes by no particle in the correlative clause) [845,1]
  • yadiyadi - yadiyadi ca-yadycayadycayady apiapi, ‘if - and if - if also’ [845,1]
  • yadiyadi - yadi-vāyadivāyadivā, or yadiyadi vāvā - yadiyadi vāvā, or yadiyadi vāvā - yadiyadi, or yadiyadi vāvā - vāvā, or vāvā - yadiyadi vāvā, or yadyad vāvā - yadiyadi vāvā, ‘if - or if’, ‘whether - or’ [845,1]
  • yadiyadi vāvā - nana vāvā, ‘whether - or not’ [845,1]
  • vāvā - yadiyadi vāvā - yadiyadi vā-tathā̑pivātathāpivātathāpi, ‘whether - or - or - yet’ [845,1]
  • vāvā yadiyadi = ‘or if’, ‘or rather’ [845,1]
  • yadiyadi vāvā id. or = ‘yet’, ‘however.’ [845,1]
