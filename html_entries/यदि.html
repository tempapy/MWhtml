• yadiyadiyad ind.
    • (in Veda also yadīyadiyad, sometimes yadiyadiyad citcitcid, yadiyadiyad haha vaivaiuū, yadī̑tyadiitididh, yadyyadiyad uuū, yadyyadiyad uuū vaivaiuū) if, in case that, RV. &c. &c. In the earlier language yadiyadiyad may be joined with Indic. Subj. or Leṭ Pot., or Fut., the consequent clause of the conditional sentence being generally without any particle. In the later language yadiyadiyad may be joined with Pres. (followed in consequent clause by another Pres. e.g. yadiyadiyad jīvatijīvat bhadrāṇibhadra paśyatipaśyat, ‘if he lives he beholds prosperity’, or by fut. or by Impv. or by Pot. or by no verb) ; or it may be joined with Pot. (e.g. yadiyadiyad rājārājarāj daṇḍaṃdaṇḍa nana praṇayet, ‘if the king were not to inflict punishment’, followed by another Pot. or by Cond. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by fut. or by no verb); or it may be joined with fut. (e.g. yadiyadiyad nana kariṣyanti tattattad, ‘if they will not do that’, followed by another fut. or by Pres. or by Impv. or by no verb); or it may be joined with Cond. (e.g. yadyyadiyad anujñāmanujñā adāsyatadāsyat, ‘if he should give permission’, followed by another Cond. or by Pot. or by aor.); or it may be joined with aor. (e.g. yadiyadiyad prajā-patirprajāpatiprajāpati nana vapurvapurvapusvapu arsrākṣīt, ‘if the Creator had not created the body’, followed by Cond. or by Pot. or by pf.); or it may be joined with Impv. or even with pf. (e.g. yadyyadiyad āhaāha, ‘if he had said’). There may be other constructions, and in the consequent clauses some one of the following may be used: athaatha, atraatra, tadtad, tenatenata, tatastatastatatat, tataḥtatastatatat paramparampara, tadātadātad, tarhitarhi, tadānīmtadānīm. Observe that yadiyadiyad may sometimes = ‘as sure as’ (esp. in asseverations, followed by Impv. with or without tathātathā or tenatenata or followed by Pot. with tadtad), MBh.; Kāv. &c. or it may = ‘whether’ (followed by Pres. or Pot. or no verb e.g. yadi-nayadina vāvāvauū, ‘whether-or not’, and sometimes kimkim is added), ib.; or it may = ‘that’ (after verbs of ‘not believing’ or ‘doubting’, with Pres. or Pot. e.g. nā̑śaṃsenanāāśaṃsaāśaṃsā yadiyadiyad jīvantijīvantijīvantījīvat, ‘I do not expect that they are alive’ cf. Pāṇ. iii, 3, 147 Sch.); or if placed after duṣkaraṃduṣkara or kathaṃkathaṃkatham cidcid it may = ‘hardly’, ‘scarcely’ MBh.; R.; or it may = ‘if perchance’, ‘perhaps’ (with Pot. with or without itiiti, or with fut. or pres.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. The following are other combinations: yadiyadiyad tāvattāvattāvad, ‘how would it be if’ (with Pres. or Impv.) [844,3]
    • yadiyadiyad nāmanāma, ‘if ever’ [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad cȇtcetcedcaitididh (cȇtcetcedcaitididh being added redundantly) = ‘if’ (e.g. yadiyadiyad cȇtcetcait syātsyātsyasyād, ‘if it should be’) [845,1]
    • purāpurāpurapuras yadiyadiyad = ‘before’ (e.g. purāpurāpurapuras yadiyadiyad paśyāmi, ‘before that I see’) [845,1]
    • yadyyadiyad apiapiap (rarely apiapiap yadiyadiyad), ‘even if’, ‘although’ (followed by tathā̑pitathāapiapāpiāpī or tadtadtat apiapiap or sometimes by no particle in the correlative clause) [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad - yadiyadiyad ca-yadycayadiyad apiapiap, ‘if - and if - if also’ [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad - yadi-vāyadivāvauū, or yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū, or yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad, or yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū - vāvāvauū, or vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū, or yadyadyat vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū, ‘if - or if’, ‘whether - or’ [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū - nana vāvāvauū, ‘whether - or not’ [845,1]
    • vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū - yadiyadiyad vā-tathā̑pivātathāapiapāpiāpī, ‘whether - or - or - yet’ [845,1]
    • vāvāvauūvās yadiyadiyad = ‘or if’, ‘or rather’ [845,1]
    • yadiyadiyad vāvāvauū id. or = ‘yet’, ‘however.’ [845,1]
