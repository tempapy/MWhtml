• 1. uuū
    • the fifth letter and third short vowel of the alphabet, pronounced as the u in full. [171,3]
  • 2. uuū ind.
    • an interjection of compassion, anger, L. [171,3]
    • a particle implying assent, calling, command, L. [171,3]
  • 3. uuū ind.
    • an enclitic copula used frequently in the Vedas; [171,3]
    • (as a particle implying restriction and antithesis, generally after pronominals, prepositions, particles, and before nununū and sususū, equivalent to) and, also, further; on the other hand (especially in connexion with a relative e.g. yayayāyuyasī uuū, he on the contrary who &c.) [171,3]
    • This particle may serve to give emphasis, like idid and evaeva, especially after prepositions or demonstrative pronouns, in conjunction with nununū, vaivaiuū, hihihī, cidcid, &c. (e.g. ayamayamaya uuū vāmvāmvāvauū purutamo... johavīti [RV. iii, 62, 2], this very person [your worshipper] invokes you &c.) It is especially used in the figure of speech called Anaphora, and particularly when the pronouns are repeated (e.g. tamtamta uuū stuṣa indramindra tamtamta gṛṇīṣe [RV. ii, 20, 4], him I praise, Indra, him I sing). It may be used in drawing a conclusion, like the English ‘now’ (e.g. tadtadtat uuū tathātathā nana kuryāt [ŚBr. v, 2, 2, 3], that now he should not do in such a manner), and is frequently found in interrogative sentences (e.g. kakakākikokukas uuū tactac ciketa [RV. i, 164, 48], who, I ask, should know that?) [171,3]
    • Pāṇini calls this particle uñ to distinguish it from the interrogative uuū. In the Pada-pāṭha it is written ūmūmūūṃ. [171,3]
    • In the classical language uuū occurs only after athaatha, nana, and kimkimkikiṃ, with a slight modification of the sense, and often only as an expletive (See kimkimkikiṃ); [171,3]
    • uuū — uuū or uuū — utauta, on the one hand - on the other hand; partly — partly; as well — as. [171,3]
  • 4. uuū
    • cl. 5. P. unoti (See vy-uvyuvyvivīuū, RV. v, 31, 1) : cl. 2. Ā. (1. sg. uveuveū, RV. x, 86, 7) : cl. 1. Ā. avateavataavat, Dhātup.;
    • to call to, hail;
    • to roar, bellow (See also ȏtaotaaāutaūta = ā-utaāaiāsuta). [171,3]
  • 5. uuū m.
    • N. of Śiva [171,3]
    • also of Brahman, L. [171,3]
