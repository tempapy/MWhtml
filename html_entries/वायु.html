  • m. (fr. √2. vāvā) wind, air (as one of the 5 elements; in MBh. 7 winds are reckoned), RV. &c. &c. [942,2]
  • the god of the wind (often associated with Indraindra in the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda, as Vātavāta [q.v.] with Parjanyaparjanya, but although of equal rank with Indraindra, not occupying so prominent a position; in the Puruṣasûktapuruṣasūkta he is said to have sprung form the breath of Puruṣapuruṣa, and elsewhere is described as the son-in-law of Tvaṣṭṛtvaṣṭṛ; be is said to move in a shining car drawn by a pair of red or purple horses or by several teams consisting of ninety-nine or a hundred or even a thousand horses [cf. ni-yutniyutniyut]; he is often made to occupy the same chariot with Indraindra, and in conjunction with him honoured with the first draught of the Somasoma libation; he is rarely connected with the Marutmaruts, although in i, 134, 4, he is said to have begotten them from the rivers of heaven; he is regent of the Nakṣatranakṣatra Svātisvāti and north-west quarter See loka-pālalokapālalokapāla), ib.
