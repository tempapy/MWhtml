• vāyu m. (fr. √2. vā) wind, air (as one of the 5 elements; in MBh. 7 winds are reckoned), RV. &c. &c. [942,2]
  • the god of the wind (often associated with Indra in the Ṛg-veda, as Vāta [q.v.] with Parjanya, but although of equal rank with Indra, not occupying so prominent a position; in the Puruṣasûkta he is said to have sprung form the breath of Puruṣa, and elsewhere is described as the son-in-law of Tvaṣṭṛ; be is said to move in a shining car drawn by a pair of red or purple horses or by several teams consisting of ninety-nine or a hundred or even a thousand horses [cf. ni-yutni-yutniyut]; he is often made to occupy the same chariot with Indra, and in conjunction with him honoured with the first draught of the Soma libation; he is rarely connected with the Maruts, although in i, 134, 4, he is said to have begotten them from the rivers of heaven; he is regent of the Nakṣatra Svāti and north-west quarter See loka-pālaloka-pālalokapāla), ib. [942,2]
  • breathing, breath, VPrāt; ĪśUp. [942,2]
  • the wind of the body, a vital air (of which 5 are reckoned, viz. prā̑ṇa, apā̑na, samāna, udāna, and vyāna; or nāga, kūrma, kṛkara, devadatta, and dhanaṃ-jayadhanaṃ-jayadhanaṃjaya), Hariv.; Sāṃkhyak.; Vedântas. [942,2]
  • (in medicine) the windy humour or any morbid affection of it, Suśr. [942,2]
  • the wind as a kind of demon producing madness, Kād.; Vcar. (cf. -grastavāyu-grastagrasta) [942,2]
  • (in astron.) N. of the fourth Muhūrta [942,2]
  • a mystical N. of the letter ya, Up. [942,2]
  • N. of a Vasu, Hariv. [942,2]
  • of a Daitya, ib. [942,2]
  • of a king of the Gandharvas, VP. [942,2]
  • of a Marut, R. [942,2]
  • pl. the Maruts, Kathās.; MārkP. [942,2]
  • vāyu mfn. (fr. √vai) tired, languid, RV. vii, 91, 1. [943,1]
  • vāyu mfn. (fr. √vī) desirous, covetous, greedy (for food, applied to calves), TS. [943,1]
  • desirable, desired by the appetite, RV. [943,1]
