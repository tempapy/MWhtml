• lopalopa
  • m. breaking, hurting, injury, destruction, interruption, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [904,3]
  • neglect, violation, transgression (of a vow or duty), Mn.; Yājñ. &c. [904,3]
  • robbing, plundering, MBh. [904,3]
  • want, deficiency, absence, disappearance, ŚrS.; Ragh. [904,3]
  • (in gram.) dropping, elision (generally as distinguished from the terms luplup, śluślu, lukluk, which are only applicable to affixes; when lopalopa of an affix takes place, a blank is substituted, which exerts the same influence on the base as the affix itself, but when either lukluk or luplup or śluślu of an affix is enjoined, then the affix is not only dropped but it is also inoperative on the base; thus in the 1st pl. of katikati, where jasjas is said to be elided by lukluk, the change of the final of the base to Guṇaguṇa does not take place i.e. both the affix and its effect on the base are abolished; moreover, lopalopa refers only to the last letter of an affix, whereas by lukluk &c. the dropping of the whole affix is implied), Nir.; Prāt.; Pāṇ. &c. [904,3]
