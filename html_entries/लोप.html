• lopalopa a m.
    • breaking, hurting, injury, destruction, interruption, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [904,3]
    • neglect, violation, transgression (of a vow or duty), Mn.; Yājñ. &c. [904,3]
    • robbing, plundering, MBh. [904,3]
    • want, deficiency, absence, disappearance, ŚrS.; Ragh. [904,3]
    • (in gram.) dropping, elision (generally as distinguished from the terms lupluplubh, śluślu, luklukluc, which are only applicable to affixes; when lopalopa of an affix takes place, a blank is substituted, which exerts the same influence on the base as the affix itself, but when either luklukluc or lupluplubh or śluślu of an affix is enjoined, then the affix is not only dropped but it is also inoperative on the base; thus in the 1st pl. of katikati, where jasjasja is said to be elided by luklukluc, the change of the final of the base to Guṇa does not take place i.e. both the affix and its effect on the base are abolished; moreover, lopalopa refers only to the last letter of an affix, whereas by luklukluc &c. the dropping of the whole affix is implied), Nir.; Prāt.; Pāṇ. &c. [904,3]
