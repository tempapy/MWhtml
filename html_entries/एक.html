• eka mfn. (√i, Uṇ. iii, 43, probably fr. a base e; cf. Zd. ae-va; Gk. οἰ-ν-ός, οἶος; Goth. ai-n-s; also Lat. aequu-s; g. sarvā̑di, Pāṇ. i, 1, 27; See Gr. 200), one (eko'pi, or ekaś-canaekaś-canaekaścana, with na preceding or following, no one, nobody; the words ekayā na or ekān na are used before decade numerals to lessen them by one e.g. ekān na triṃśat, twenty-nine), RV. &c. [227,3]
  • (with and without eva) alone, solitary, single, happening only once, that one only (frequently ifc.; cf. dharma͡ika-rakṣadharmaika-rakṣadharmaikarakṣa, &c.), RV. &c. [227,3]
  • the same, one and the same, identical, ŚBr. v; KātyŚr.; Mn. &c. [227,3]
  • one of two or many (eka - eka, eka - dvitīya, the one - the other; esp. pl. eke, some, eke - apare some - others, &c.), ŚBr.; KātyŚr.; MBh.; Hit. &c. [227,3]
  • (eka repeated twice, either as a compound [cf. eka͡ika] or uncompounded, may have the sense ‘one and one’, ‘one by one’ RV. i, 20, 7; 123, 8; v, 52, 17; R.; BhP. &c.) [227,3]
  • single of its kind, unique, singular, chief, pre-eminent, excellent, Ragh.; Kathās.; Kum. &c. [227,3]
  • sincere, truthful, MW. [227,3]
  • little, small, L. [227,3]
  • (sometimes used as an indefinite article), a, an, R.; Śak.; Vet. &c. (the fem. of eka before a Taddhita suffix and as first member of a compound is eka not ekā, Pāṇ. vi, 3, 62) [227,3]
  • eka m. N. of a teacher, Āp. [227,3]
  • of a son of Raya, BhP. [227,3]
  • eka n. unity, a unit (ifc.), Hcat. [227,3]
