• gaReSa
  • m. (= °Ra-nATa) N. of the god of wisdom and of obstacles (son of Śivaśiva and Pārvatīpārvatī, or according to one legend of Pārvatīpārvatī alone; though Gaṇêśagaṇeśa causes obstacles he also removes them; hence he is invoked at the commencement of all undertakings and at the opening of all compositions with the words namo gaReSAya viGneSvarAya; he is represented as a short fat man with a protuberant belly, frequently riding on a rat or attended by one, and to denote his sagacity has the head of an elephant, which however has only one tusk; the appellation Gaṇêśagaṇeśa, with other similar compounds, alludes to his office as chief of the various classes of subordinate gods, who are regarded as Śivaśiva's attendants; cf. RTL. pp. 48, 62, 79, 392, 440; he is said to have written down the MBh. as dictated by Vyāsavyāsa, MBh. i, 74 ff.; persons possessed, by Gaṇêśagaṇeśa are referred to, Yājñ. i, 270 ff.) [343,2]

  • = gaRa-puMgava, VarBṛ. xiii, 8 [343,2]
  • m. pl. (= vidyeSa or °Svara) a class of Siddhasiddhas (with Śaivaśaivas), Hcat. i, 11, 857 ff [343,2]
  • N. of a renowned astronomer of the 16th century [343,2]
  • of a son of Rāma-devarāmadevarāmadeva (author of a Comm. on Nalôd.) [343,3]

  • m. (with Jainajainas) a disciple who is put in charge of a few others. [1326,1]
