• bālabāla mf(āāa)n.
    • (cf. vālavāla) young, childish, infantine, not full-grown or developed (of persons and things), GṛS.; Up.; Mn.; MBh. &c. [728,3]
    • newly risen, early (as the sun or its rays), Ragh. [728,3]
    • new or waxing (as the moon), ib.; Kum. [728,3]
    • puerile, ignorant, simple, foolish, Mn.; Hariv.; Kāv. [728,3]
    • pure (as an animal fit for sacrifice), L. [728,3]
  • bālabāla m.
    • a child, boy (esp. one under 5 years), Mn.; MBh. &c. [728,3]
    • (in law) a minor (minors are classified as kumārakumāra or boys under 5 years of age, śiśuśiśu under 8, pogaṇḍapogaṇḍa from the 5th to the end of the 9th or till the 16th year, and kiśorakiśora from the 10th to the 16th year) [728,3]
    • a fool, simpleton, Mn.; Pañcat. [728,3]
    • any young animal, L. [728,3]
    • a colt, foal, L. [728,3]
    • a five years old elephant, L. [728,3]
    • Cyprinus Denticulatus or Rohita, L. [728,3]
    • N. of a Rakṣas, VP. [728,3]
    • of a prince, Rājat. [728,3]
  • bālabāla n.
    • Andropogon Muricatus, L. [728,3]
    • heat, L. [728,3]
