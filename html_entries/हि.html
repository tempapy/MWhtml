  • 2. hihihī ind.
    • (used as a particle [cf. haha and ghagha] and usually denoting) for, because, on account of (never standing first in a sentence, but generally after the first word and used enclitically, sometimes after pronouns; e.g. sarvosarva hihihī pṛtanāpṛtanāpṛtana jigīṣati, ‘for everybody wishes to win battles’; bhavānbhavabhavat hihihī pramāṇampramāṇa, ‘for your honour is the authority’; tathātathā hihihī, ‘for example’, ‘accordingly’; nana hihihī or nahinahinah, ‘for not’, ‘not at all’), RV. &c. &c. [1297,3]
    • just, pray, do (with an Impv. or Pot. emphatically; sometimes with Indic., e.g. paśyāmo hihihī, ‘we will just see’), ib. [1297,3]
    • indeed, assuredly, surely, of course, certainly (hihihī vaivaiuū, ‘most assuredly’; hi-tuhitu or hi-punarhipunar, ‘indeed-but’; often a mere expletive, esp. to avoid a hiatus, sometimes repeated in the same sentence; hihihī is also said to be an interjection of ‘envy’, ‘contempt’, ‘hurry’ &c.), ib.
