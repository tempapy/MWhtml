• anv-āhāryaanvāhāryaanuāhārya m.
    • a gift, consisting of food prepared with rice, presented to the Ṛtvij priest at the Darśapūrṇamāsa ceremonies, TS. &c. [47,2]
  • anv-āhāryaanvāhāryaanuāhārya
    • (amama or akamaka), n. the monthly Śrāddha (q.v.) held on the day of new moon (according to, Mn. iii, 123 it should be of meat eaten after the presentation of a Piṇḍa or ball of rice). [47,2]
