• nīvinīvinīvī or nīvīnīvīnīvi, f.
    • (prob. fr. nininī + √vyevyevya) a piece of cloth wrapped round the waist (esp. as worn by women; but cf. Vām. i, 3, 5), a kind of skirt or petticoat, VS.; AV.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [567,1]
    • a band or cord for tying together folded Kuśa-grass in making offerings at the funeral rites of a Śūdra, L. [567,1]
    • a hostage, L. [567,1]
    • capital, principal stock, Daś. [567,1]
