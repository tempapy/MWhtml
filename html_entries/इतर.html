• i-taraitaraitara mf(āāa)n.
    • (the neuter is adad in classical Sanskṛt, but amama [adad, ŚBr.] in Ved. Pāṇ. vii, 1, 25, 26; comparative form of pronom. base 3. iiī; cf. Lat. iterum; Hib. iter), the other (of two), another [164,3]
    • pl. the rest [164,3]
    • (with abl.) different from, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; MBh.; R.; Mn.; Ragh.; Hit. &c. [164,3]
    • low, vile, Kād. [164,3]
    • expelled, rejected, L. [164,3]
  • i-taraitaraitara
    • itaraitara, itaraitara, the one - the other, this - that. (itaraitara connected antithetically with a preceding word often signifies the contrary idea e.g. vijayāyavijaya itarāya vāvāvauū [MBh.], to victory or defeat; so in Dvandva compounds, sukhȇtareṣusukhetarasukhasukhā [ŚvetUp.], in happiness and distress; it sometimes, however, forms a Tat-puruṣa compound with another word to express the one idea implied in the contrary of that word, e.g. dakṣiṇȇtaradakṣiṇetaradakṣiṇadakṣiṇāitara, the left hand.)
