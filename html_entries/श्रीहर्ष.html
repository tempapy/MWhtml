• śrīśrī—harṣaharṣa m.
    • N. of various authors &c. (esp. of a celebrated king and poet or patron of poets, also called Śrīharṣa-kavi or Śrī-harṣa-deva, who lived probably in the first half of the seventh century A.D. and is the supposed author of three plays, viz. Nāgânanda, Priya-darśikā, and Ratnâvalī). [1100,2]
