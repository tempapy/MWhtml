• mahāmahāmahamah—kāvyakāvya n.
    • a great or classical poem (applied as a distinguishing title to 6 chief artificial poems, viz. the Raghu-vaṉśa, Kumārasambhava and Megha-dūta by Kālidāsa, the Śiśupāla-vadha by Māgha, the Kirātârjunīya by Bhāravi and the Naiṣadha-carita by Śrī-harṣa; accord. to some the Bhaṭṭi-kāvya is also a Mahā-kāvya), Kāvyâd.; Pratāp. (IW. 452). [795,1]
