• sam-ā-√dhāsamādhāsamāaiāsdhādha a
    • P. Ā. -dadhātisamādhā, -dhattesamādhā, to place or put or hold or fix together, ŚBr.; MBh. &c.
    • to compose, set right, repair, put in order, arrange, redress, restore, MBh.; Hit.;
    • to put to, add, put on (esp. fuel on the fire), AV.; ŚBr.; R.;
    • to kindle, stir (fire), ŚBr.;
    • to place, set, lay, fix, direct, settle, adjust (with astramastra, ‘to adjust an arrow’; with garbhamgarbha, ‘to impregnate’; with savituḥ, ‘to lay in the sun’; with dṛṣṭimdṛṣṭi, cittamcitta, cetascetascet, matimmati, manasmanasmana, ‘to direct or fix the eyes or mind upon [loc.]’; with matimmati, ‘to settle in one's mind, resolve’, ‘make up one's mind’ [followed by itiitiit]; with ātmānam, or manasmanasmana, ‘to collect the thoughts or concentrate the mind in meditation &c.’; without an acc. - ‘to be absorbed in meditation or prayer’), RV. &c. &c.
    • to impose upon (loc.), MBh.;
    • to entrust or commit to (loc.), ib.;
    • to establish in (loc.), Ragh.;
    • to effect, cause, produce, Hariv.;
    • (in the following meanings only Ā.) to take to or upon one's self. conceive (in the womb), put on (a garment or dress), assume (a shape), undergo (effort), show, exhibit, display, MBh.; Kāv. &c.
    • to devote one's self entirely to, give one's self up to (acc.), R.; Hariv. &c. (once in Bhaṭṭ. P.);
    • to lay down as settled, settle, establish, prove, declare, Kap. Sch.;
    • to admit, grant, Kull. on Mn. viii, 54 :
    • Desid. -dhitsatisamādhā, to wish to put together, desire to collect the thoughts, MBh. xii, 9586. [1159,3]
  • sam-ādhāsamādhāsamādhā b m.
    • (only L.) putting together, adjusting, settling, reconciling, clearing up difficulties &c. [1159,3]
    • completion, accomplishment. [1159,3]
