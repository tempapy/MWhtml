  • itiiti
  • ind. (fr. pronominal base 3. ii), in this manner, thus (in its original signification itiiti refers to something that has been said or thought, or lays stress on what precedes; in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas it is often equivalent to ‘as you know’, reminding the hearer or reader of certain customs, conditions, &c. supposed to be known to him). In quotations of every kind itiiti means that the preceding words are the very words which some person has or might have spoken, and placed thus at the end of a speech it serves the purpose of inverted commas (ityity uktvāuktvā, having so said; itiiti kṛtvākṛtvā, having so considered, having so decided). It may often have reference merely to what is passing in the mind e.g. bālobālo 'pipi nāvamantavyonāvamantavyo manuṣyamanuṣya itiiti bhūmipaḥbhūmipaḥ, a king, though a child, is not to be despised, saying to one's self, ‘he is a mortal’, (Gr. 928.) In dram. itiiti tathātathā karotikaroti means ‘after these words he acts thus.’
    Sometimes itiiti is used to include under one head a number of separate objects aggregated together (e.g. ijyādhyayanadānāniijyādhyayanadānāni tapaḥtapaḥ satyaṃsatyaṃ kṣamākṣamā damaḥdamaḥ . alobhaalobha itiiti mārgomārgo 'yamyam, ‘sacrificing, studying, liberality, penance, truth, patience, self-restraint, absence of desire’, this course of conduct, &c.)
    itiiti is sometimes followed by evamevam, ivaiva, or a demonstrative pronoun pleonastically (e.g. tāmtām brūyādbrūyād bhavatītybhavatīty evamevam, her he may call ‘lady’, thus).
    itiiti may form an adverbial compound with the name of an author (e.g. iti-pāṇiniitipāṇiniitipāṇini, thus according to Pāṇinipāṇini). It may also express the act of calling attention (lo! behold!) It may have some other significations e.g. something additional (as in ityādiityādi, et caetera), order, arrangement specific or distinctive, and identity. It is used by native commentators after quoting a rule to express ‘according to such a rule’ (e.g. anudāttaṅitaanudāttaṅita ityity ātmanepadamātmanepadam bhavatibhavati, according to the rule of Pāṇinipāṇini, i, 3, 12, the Ātmane-padaātmanepadaātmanepada takes place).
    kimkim itiiti = kimkim, wherefore, why? (In the Śatapatha-brāhmaṇaśatapathabrāhmaṇaśatapathabrāhmaṇa titi occurs for itiiti; cf. Prākṛt ti and tti.) [165,1]
  • ‘and so forth’ (itiiti ceticeti caca, ‘thus and thus’, ‘in this and that manner’), MBh. [1320,3]
