  • 2. itiitiit ind.
    • (fr. pronominal base 3. iiīin), in this manner, thus (in its original signification itiitiit refers to something that has been said or thought, or lays stress on what precedes; in the Brāhmaṇas it is often equivalent to ‘as you know’, reminding the hearer or reader of certain customs, conditions, &c. supposed to be known to him). In quotations of every kind itiitiit means that the preceding words are the very words which some person has or might have spoken, and placed thus at the end of a speech it serves the purpose of inverted commas (ityitiit uktvāuktvā, having so said; itiitiit kṛtvākṛtvā, having so considered, having so decided). It may often have reference merely to what is passing in the mind e.g. bālobālubāla 'pi nā̑vamantavyonanāavamantavya manuṣyamanuṣyamanuṣī itiitiit bhūmipaḥ, a king, though a child, is not to be despised, saying to one's self, ‘he is a mortal’, (Gr. 928.) In dram. itiitiit tathātathā karoti means ‘after these words he acts thus.’
    • Sometimes itiitiit is used to include under one head a number of separate objects aggregated together (e.g. ijyā̑dhyayanadānāniijyaijyā tapaḥtapaḥ satyaṃsatyamsatya kṣamākṣamākṣama damaḥ . alobhaalobha itiitiit mārgo 'yamayamaya, ‘sacrificing, studying, liberality, penance, truth, patience, self-restraint, absence of desire’, this course of conduct, &c.)
    • itiitiit is sometimes followed by evamevameva, ivaiva, or a demonstrative pronoun pleonastically (e.g. tāmtāta brūyād bhavatī̑tybhavatīitiitīti evamevameva, her he may call ‘lady’, thus).
    • itiitiit may form an adverbial compound with the name of an author (e.g. iti-pāṇiniitipāṇiniitipāṇinipāṇipāṇin, thus according to Pāṇini). It may also express the act of calling attention (lo! behold!) It may have some other significations e.g. something additional (as in ityādiityādi, et caetera), order, arrangement specific or distinctive, and identity. It is used by native commentators after quoting a rule to express ‘according to such a rule’ (e.g. anudāttaṅita ityitiit ātmanepadamātmanepada bhavatibhavatībhavat, according to the rule of Pāṇini, i, 3, 12, the Ātmane-pada takes place).
    • kimkimki itiitiit = kimkimkikiṃ, wherefore, why? (In the Śatapatha-brāhmaṇa titi occurs for itiitiit; cf. Prākṛt ti and tti.) [165,1]
    • ‘and so forth’ (itiitiit cȇticetīcetcaitiitīti caca, ‘thus and thus’, ‘in this and that manner’), MBh. [1320,3]
