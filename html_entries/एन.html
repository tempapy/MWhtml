• enaenaa 1
    • a pronom. base (used for certain cases of the 3rd personal pronoun, thus in the acc. sing. du. pl. [enamena, enāmenāena, enad, &c.], inst. sing. [enenaena, enayāenāena] gen. loc. du. [enayosenaenā, Ved. enos]; the other cases are formed fr. the pronom. base aa See under idamidamid), he, she, it [232,1]
    • this, that, (this pronoun is enclitic and cannot begin a sentence; it is generally used alone, so that enamena puruṣampuruṣa, ‘that man’, would be very unusual if not incorrect. Grammarians assert that the substitution of enamena &c. for imam or etameta &c. takes place when something is referred to which has already been mentioned in a previous part of the sentence; See Gr. 223 and 836) [232,1]
