• mukharamukhara mf(āāa)n.
    • (fr. mukhamukha; cf. Pān v 2 107, Vārtt. 1, Pat.) talkative, garrulous, loquacious (said also of birds and bees), Kāv.; Kathās. [820,2]
    • noisy, tinkling (as an anklet &c.), Mṛcch.; Kālid. [820,2]
    • sound resonant or eloquent with, expressive of (comp.), Kāv.; Kathās. [820,2]
    • scurrilous, Kāv.; Rājat. [820,2]
    • foul-mouthed, scurrilous speaking harshly or abusively, L. [820,2]
  • mukharamukhara m.
    • a crow, L. [820,2]
    • a conch shell, L. [820,2]
    • a leader, principal, chief, Hit. [820,2]
    • N. of a Nāga, MBh. [820,2]
    • of a rogue, Cat. [820,2]
