• aharahar
  • n. (the weak cases come fr. ahanahan q.v., the middle ones fr. ahasahas [see below] or in RV. also fr. ahanahan, q.v.) a day, RV. &c. [124,3]
  • a sacrificial or festival day, portion of a sacrifice appointed for one day's performance, AitBr. &c. (often ifc., as dvādaśā̑hadvādaśāha, &c. See s.v. 2. ahaaha) [124,3]
  • day personified as one of the eight Vasuvasus, MBh. i, 2582 seqq. [124,3]
  • N. of an Āṅgirasaāṅgirasa, KāṭhAnukr. [124,3]
  • of a Tīrthatīrtha, MBh. iii, 6070 [124,3]
  • (ahanīahanī) nom. du. day and night, RV.; AV. xiii, 2,3 (cf. ahaśahaś caca kṛṣṇamkṛṣṇam aharahar arjunamarjunam caca, ‘the black and the white day’ i.e. night and day, RV. vi, 9, 1) [124,3]
