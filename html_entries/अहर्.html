• aharahar n.
    • (the weak cases come fr. ahanahan, q.v., the middle ones fr. ahasahasahaah [see below] or in RV. also fr. ahanahan, q.v.) a day, RV. &c. [124,3]
    • a sacrificial or festival day, portion of a sacrifice appointed for one day's performance, AitBr. &c. (often ifc., as dvādaśā̑hadvādaśāhadvādaśaahaāha, &c. See s.v. 2. ahaaha) [124,3]
    • day personified as one of the eight Vasus, MBh. i, 2582 seqq. [124,3]
    • N. of an Āṅgirasa, KāṭhAnukr. [124,3]
    • of a Tīrtha, MBh. iii, 6070 [124,3]
    • (ahanī) nom. du. day and night, RV.; AV. xiii, 2, 3 (cf. ahaśahaśahasahaahahar caca kṛṣṇamkṛṣṇa aharahar arjunamarjuna caca, ‘the black and the white day’ i.e. night and day, RV. vi, 9, 1) [124,3]
