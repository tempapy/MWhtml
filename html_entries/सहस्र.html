  • sahasrasahasra b n.
    • (rarely) m. (perhaps fr. 7, sasa + hasrahasra = Gk. χίλιοι for χεσλοι; cf. Pers. hazār) a thousand (with the counted object in the same case sg. or pl. e.g. sahasreṇasahasra bāhunābāhu, ‘with a thousand arms’ Hariv.; sahasraṃsahasramsahasra bhiṣajaḥbhiṣajabhiṣaj, ‘a thousand drugs’ RV.; or in the gen. e.g. dvedvadvādvi sahasresahasra suvarṇasyasuvarṇa, ‘two thousand pieces of gold’ Rājat.; catvāri sahasrāṇisahasra varṣāṇāmvarṣavarṣā, ‘four thousand years’ Mn.; sometimes in comp., either ibc. e.g. yuga-sahasramyugasahasramsahasra, ‘a thousand ages’ Mn., or ifc. e.g. sahasrā̑śvenasahasrāśvasahasraaśvaāśva, ‘with a thousand horses’; sahasramsahasramsahasra may also be used as an ind. e.g. sahasramsahasramsahasra ṛṣibhiḥṛṣi, ‘with a thousand Ṛṣis’ RV.; with other numerals it is used thus, ekā̑dhikaṃekaekāadhikamadhika sahasramsahasramsahasra, or eka-sahasramekasahasraekasahasramsahasra, ‘a thousand one’, 1001; dvyadhikaṃdvyadhika s°sahasra, ‘a thousand two’, 1002; ekādaśā̑dhikamekādaśaadhikamadhika s°sahasram or ekādaśaṃekādaśa s°sahasra or ekādaśa-s°ekādaśasahasra, ‘a thousand eleven’ or ‘a thousand having eleven’, 1011; viṃśaty-adhikaṃviṃśatyviṃśatiadhikamadhika s°sahasra or vimaṃ s°sahasra, ‘a thousand twenty’, 1020; dvedvadvādvi sahasresahasra or dvi-sahacramdvi, ‘two thousand’; trīṇitri sahasrāṇisahasra or tri-sahasramtrisahasramsahasra, ‘three thousand’ &c.), RV. &c. &c. [1195,2]
    • a thousand cows or gifts (= sahasraṃsahasramsahasra gavyamgavya &c., used to express wealth; sahasraṃsahasramsahasra śatā̑śvamśatāśvaśataśatāaśvaāśva, ‘a thousand cows and a hundred horses’ ŚāṅkhŚr.), RV.; VS.; ŚBr. (in later language often = ‘1000 Paṇas’, e.g. Mn. viii, 120; 336 &c.) [1195,2]
    • any very large number (in, Naigh. iii, 1 among the bahu-nāmānibahunāma; cf. sahasra-kiraṇasahasrakiraṇasahasrakiraṇa &c. below) [1195,2]
  • sahasrasahasra mf(īīiin)n.
    • a thousandth or the thousandth (= sahasra-tamasahasratamasahasratama which is the better form; cf. Pāṇ. v, 2, 57). [1195,2]
