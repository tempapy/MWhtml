• alamalam
  • ind. (later form of aramaram q.v.), enough, sufficient, adequate, equal to, competent, able. (alamalam may govern a dat. [jīvitavaijīvitavai (Ved. Inf. dat.) alamalam, AV. vi, 109, 1, or alaṃalaṃ jīvanāyajīvanāya, Mn. xi, 76, &c., sufficient for living] or Inf. [Pāṇ. ii, 4, 66; alaṃalaṃ vijñātumvijñātum ‘able to conceive’ Nir. ii, 3] or instr. [Pāṇ. ii, 3, 27; Siddh.; alaṃalaṃ śaṅkayāśaṅkayā, enough i.e. away with fear!] or gen. [alaṃalaṃ prajāyāḥprajāyāḥ, capable of obtaining progeny, PBr.] or may be used with the fut. [alaṃalaṃ haniṣyatihaniṣyati, he will be able to kill, Pāṇ. iii, 3, 154 Sch.] or with an ind. [Pāṇ. iii, 4, 18; alaṃalaṃ bhuktvābhuktvā, enough of eating i.e. do not eat more, alaṃalaṃ vicāryavicārya, enough of consideration].) [94,2]
