• alamalamala ind.
    • (later form of aramaramara q.v.), enough, sufficient, adequate, equal to, competent, able. (alamalamala may govern a dat. [jīvitavai (Ved. Inf. dat.) alamalamala, AV. vi, 109, 1, or alaṃalaṃalamala jīvanāyajīvanāyajīvana, Mn. xi, 76, &c., sufficient for living] or Inf. [Pāṇ. ii, 4, 66; alaṃalaṃalamala vijñātum ‘able to conceive’ Nir. ii, 3] or instr. [Pāṇ. ii, 3, 27; Siddh.; alaṃalaṃalamala śaṅkayāśaṅkā, enough i.e. away with fear!] or gen. [alaṃalaṃalamala prajāyāḥ, capable of obtaining progeny, PBr.] or may be used with the fut. [alaṃalaṃalamala haniṣyati, he will be able to kill, Pāṇ. iii, 3, 154 Sch.] or with an ind. [Pāṇ. iii, 4, 18; alaṃalaṃalamala bhuktvābhuktvā, enough of eating i.e. do not eat more, alaṃalaṃalamala vicāryavicārya, enough of consideration].) [94,2]
