• vāsavavāsava—dattādattādatta f.
    • N. of various women (esp. of the wife of Udayana, king of Vatsa and daughter of king Caṇḍa-mahā-sena of Ujjayinī [Kathās.] or of king Pradyota [Ratnâv.], to whom she offered herself after having been betrothed by her father to Saṃjaya [Mālatīm.]; and of the heroine of Subandhu's novel, who is represented to have been betrothed by her father to Puṣpa-ketu, but carried off by Kandarpa-ketu) [948,1]
    • the story of Vāsava-dattā, Pāṇ. iv, 3, 87, Vārtt. 1, Pat. (esp. as narrated in Subandhu's tale) [948,1]
