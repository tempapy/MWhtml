• vi-jñānavijñānavijñāna n.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ) the act of distinguishing or discerning, understanding, comprehending, recognizing, intelligence, knowledge, AV. &c. &c. [961,2]
    • skill, proficiency, art, Uttamac. [961,2]
    • science, doctrine, Suśr. [961,2]
    • worldly or profane knowledge (opp. to jñānajñāna, ‘knowledge of the true nature of God’), Mn.; MBh. &c. [961,2]
    • the faculty of discernment or of right judgement, MBh.; R. &c. [961,2]
    • the organ of knowledge (= manasmanasmana), BhP. [961,2]
    • (ifc.) the understanding of (a particular meaning), regarding as, Kāś. on Pāṇ. ii, 3, 17; 66 &c. [961,2]
    • (with Buddhists) consciousness or thought-faculty (one of the 5 constituent elements or Skandhas, also considered as one of the 6 elements or Dhātus, and as one of the 12 links of the chain of causation), Dharmas. 22; 42; 58 (cf. MWB. 102; 109) [961,2]
