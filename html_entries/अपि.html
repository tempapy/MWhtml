• apiapiap
    • or sometimes pi (See pi-dṛbhpidṛbhpidṛbh, pi-dhāpidhāpidhādha, pi-nahpinahpinah), expresses placing near or over, uniting to, annexing, reaching to, proximity, &c. [55,1]
  • apiapiap
    • [cf. Gk. ἐπί Zd. api; Germ. and Eng. prefix be] [55,1]
    • in later Sanskṛt its place seems frequently supplied by abhiabhiabhī. [55,1]
    • (As a separable adv.) and, also, moreover, besides, assuredly, surely [55,1]
    • suppose that, perhaps, Jātakam. [1314,2]
    • apiapiap apiapiap or api-caapica, as well as [55,1]
    • nana vā̑pivāpivāpīvāpinvavāapiapāpiāpī or nana apivā or nana nacā̑piapiapāpiāpī, neither, nor, cā̑picāpincaapiapāpiāpī, (and at the beginning of a sentence) api-caapica, moreover. [55,1]
    • is often used to express emphasis, in the sense of even, also, very [55,1]
    • e.g. anyad apiapiap, also another, something more [55,1]
    • adyā̑piadyāpiadyaapiapāpiāpī, this very day, even now [55,1]
    • tathā̑pitathāapiapāpiāpī, even thus, notwithstanding [55,1]
    • yadyyadiyad apiapiap, even if, although [55,1]
    • yadyapi tathā̑pitathāapiapāpiāpī, although, nevertheless [55,1]
    • nana kadācid apiapiap, never at any time: sometimes in the sense of but, only, at least e.g. muhūrtammuhūrta apiapiap, only a moment. [55,1]
    • may be affixed to an interrogative to make it indefinite, e.g. kokokukaska 'pi, any one [55,1]
    • kutrā̑pikutraapiapāpiāpī, anywhere. [55,1]
    • imparts to numerals the notion of totality e.g. caturṇām apiapiap varṇānāmvarṇavarṇā, of all the four castes. [55,1]
    • may be interrogative at the beginning of a sentence. [55,1]
    • may strengthen the original force of the Potential, or may soften the Imperative, like the English ‘be pleased to’ [55,1]
    • sometimes it is a mere expletive. [55,1]
    • apiapiap tutu but, but yet. [55,1]
