• api or sometimes pi (See pi-dṛbhpi-dṛbhpidṛbh, pi-dhāpi-dhāpidhā, pi-nahpi-nahpinah), expresses placing near or over, uniting to, annexing, reaching to, proximity, &c. [55,1]
  • api [cf. Gk. ἐπί Zd. api; Germ. and Eng. prefix be] [55,1]
  • in later Sanskṛt its place seems frequently supplied by abhi. [55,1]
  • (As a separable adv.) and, also, moreover, besides, assuredly, surely [55,1]
  • suppose that, perhaps, Jātakam. [1314,2]
  • api api or api-caapi-caapica, as well as [55,1]
  • na vā̑pi or na apivā or na nacā̑pi, neither, nor, cā̑pi, (and at the beginning of a sentence) api-caapi-caapica, moreover. [55,1]
  • is often used to express emphasis, in the sense of even, also, very [55,1]
  • e.g. anyad api, also another, something more [55,1]
  • adyā̑pi, this very day, even now [55,1]
  • tathā̑pi, even thus, notwithstanding [55,1]
  • yady api, even if, although [55,1]
  • yadyapi tathā̑pi, although, nevertheless [55,1]
  • na kadācid api, never at any time: sometimes in the sense of but, only, at least e.g. muhūrtam api, only a moment. [55,1]
  • may be affixed to an interrogative to make it indefinite, e.g. ko 'pi, any one [55,1]
  • kutrā̑pi, anywhere. [55,1]
  • imparts to numerals the notion of totality e.g. caturṇām api varṇānām, of all the four castes. [55,1]
  • may be interrogative at the beginning of a sentence. [55,1]
  • may strengthen the original force of the Potential, or may soften the Imperative, like the English ‘be pleased to’ [55,1]
  • sometimes it is a mere expletive. [55,1]
  • api tu but, but yet. [55,1]
  • (api-apiapi-apiapiapi), as well-as, ib. [1314,2]
