• a/pi
  • or sometimes pi (See pi-dfB, pi-DA, pi-nah), expresses placing near or over, uniting to, annexing, reaching to, proximity, &c. [55,1]
  • api
  • [cf. Gk. ἐπί Zd. api; Germ. and Eng. prefix be] [55,1]
  • in later Sanskṛt its place seems frequently supplied by aBi. [55,1]
  • (As a separable adv.) and, also, moreover, besides, assuredly, surely [55,1]
  • suppose that, perhaps, Jātakam. [1314,2]
  • api api or api-ca, as well as [55,1]
  • na vApi or na apivA or na nacApi, neither, nor, cApi, (and at the beginning of a sentence) api-ca, moreover. [55,1]
  • is often used to express emphasis, in the sense of even, also, very [55,1]
  • e.g. anyad api, also another, something more [55,1]
  • adyApi, this very day, even now [55,1]
  • taTApi, even thus, notwithstanding [55,1]
  • yady api, even if, although [55,1]
  • yadyapi taTApi, although, nevertheless [55,1]
  • na kadAcid api, never at any time: sometimes in the sense of but, only, at least e.g. muhUrtam api, only a moment. [55,1]
  • may be affixed to an interrogative to make it indefinite, e.g. ko 'pi, any one [55,1]
  • kutrApi, anywhere. [55,1]
  • imparts to numerals the notion of totality e.g. caturRAm api varRAnAm, of all the four castes. [55,1]
  • may be interrogative at the beginning of a sentence. [55,1]
  • may strengthen the original force of the Potential, or may soften the Imperative, like the English ‘be pleased to’ [55,1]
  • sometimes it is a mere expletive. [55,1]
  • api tu but, but yet. [55,1]
  • (api-api), as well-as, ib. [1314,2]
