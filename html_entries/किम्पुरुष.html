• kimkimki—puruṣapuruṣa
    • -puruṣakimpuruṣapuruṣa [ŚBr. i] or -puruṣakimpuruṣapuruṣa [ŚBr. vii], m. ‘what sort of a man?’ a mongrel being (according to the Brāhmaṇas an evil being similar to man; perhaps originally a kind of monkey [cf. BhP. xi, 16, 29]; in later times the word is usually identified with kiṃ-narakiṃnarakiṃkimnara, though sometimes applied to other beings in which the figure of a man and that of an animal are combined; these beings are supposed to live on Hema-kūṭa and are regarded as the attendants of Kubera; with Jains the Kimpuruṣas, like the Kiṃnaras, belong to the Vyantaras) [283,2]
    • N. of one of the nine sons of Āgnīdhra (having the Varṣa Kimpuruṣa as his hereditary portion), VP. [283,2]
    • a division of the earth (one of the nine Khaṇḍas or portions into which the earth is divided, and described as the country between the Himâcala and Hema-kūṭa mountains, also called kimpuruṣa-varṣakimpuruṣavarṣa, Kād.), VP.; BhP.; MatsyaP. &c. [283,2]
