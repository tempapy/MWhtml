• kimkim—puruṣapuruṣa
  • -puruṣapuruṣakimpuruṣa [ŚBr. i] or -puruṣapuruṣakimpuruṣa [ŚBr. vii], m. ‘what sort of a man?’ a mongrel being (according to the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas an evil being similar to man; perhaps originally a kind of monkey [cf. BhP. xi, 16, 29]; in later times the word is usually identified with kiṃ-narakiṃnarakiṃnara, though sometimes applied to other beings in which the figure of a man and that of an animal are combined; these beings are supposed to live on Hema-kūṭahemakūṭahemakūṭa and are regarded as the attendants of Kuberakubera; with Jains the Kimpuruṣakimpuruṣas, like the Kiṃnarakiṃnaras, belong to the Vyantaravyantaras) [283,2]
  • N. of one of the nine sons of Āgnīdhraāgnīdhra (having the Varṣavarṣa Kimpuruṣakimpuruṣa as his hereditary portion), VP. [283,2]
  • a division of the earth (one of the nine Khaṇḍakhaṇḍas or portions into which the earth is divided, and described as the country between the Himâcalahimācala and Hema-kūṭahemakūṭahemakūṭa mountains, also called kimpuruṣa-varṣakimpuruṣavarṣakimpuruṣavarṣa, Kād.), VP.; BhP.; MatsyaP. &c. [283,2]
