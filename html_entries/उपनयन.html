• upa-nayanaupanayanaupanayana
  • n. the act of leading to or near, bringing, R.; BhP.; Vikr. [201,2]
  • employment, application, Car. [201,2]
  • introduction (into any science), Prab. [201,2]
  • leading or drawing towards one's self [201,2]
  • that ceremony in which a Guru draws a boy towards himself and initiates him into one of the three twice-born classes (one of the twelve Saṃskārasaṃskāras or purificatory rites [prescribed in the Dharmadharma-sūtrasūtras and explained in the Gṛhyagṛhya-sūtrasūtras] in which the boy is invested with the sacred thread [different for the three castes] and thus endowed with second or spiritual birth and qualified to learn the Vedaveda by heart; a Brāhman is initiated in the eighth year [or seventh according to Hiraṇyakeśinhiraṇyakeśin; or eighth from conception, according to Śāṅkhāyanaśāṅkhāyana &c.], a Kṣatriyakṣatriya in the eleventh, a Vaiśyavaiśya in the twelfth; but the term could be delayed) [201,2]
  • See IW. p.201 RTL. p.360 seqq.; ĀśvGṛ. i, 19-22; ŚāṅkhGṛ. ii, 1-6; PārGṛ. ii, 2-5; Gobh. ii, 10; HirGṛ. i, 1 seqq.; Mn. ii, 36; Yājñ. i, 14. [201,2]
