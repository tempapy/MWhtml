• āyurāyur—vedaveda
  • m. the science of health or medicine (it is classed among sacred sciences, and considered as a supplement of the Atharvaatharva-vedaveda; it contains eight departments: 1. Śalyaśalya or (removal of) any substance which has entered the body (as extraction of darts, of splinters, &c.); 2. Śālākyaśālākya or cure of diseases of the eye or ear &c. by Śalākāśalākās or sharp instruments; 3. Kāya-cikitsākāyacikitsākāyacikitsā or cure of diseases affecting the whole body; 4. Bhūtavidyābhūtavidyā or treatment of mental diseases supposed to be produced by demoniacal influence; 5. Kaumāra-bhṛtyakaumārabhṛtyakaumārabhṛtya or treatment of children ; 6. Agada-tantraagadatantraagadatantra or doctrine of antidotes; 7. Rāsāyana-tantrarāsāyanatantrarāsāyanatantra or doctrine of elixirs; 8. Vājīkaraṇa-tantravājīkaraṇatantravājīkaraṇatantra or doctrine of aphrodisiacs, Suśr.; MBh.; Hariv. &c.) [148,3]
