• haihaya m. N. of a race (said to have been descendants of Yadu; they are described in the Purāṇas as separated into 5 divisions, viz. the Tālajaṅghas, Vīti-hotravīti-hotravītihotras, Āvantyas, Tuṇḍikeras, and Jātas; they are, said to have overrun parts of India along with the Śakas or Scythian tribes), MBh.; R.; Pur. &c. [1305,3]
  • a king of the Haihayas (esp. applied to Arjuna Kārtavīrya, who is said to have had a thousand arms; See kārtavīrya), ib. [1305,3]
  • N. of a son of Sahasrada, Hariv. [1305,3]
  • of a son of Śata-jitśata-jitśatajit, Pur. [1305,3]
  • (?) of a medical author, Cat. [1305,3]
