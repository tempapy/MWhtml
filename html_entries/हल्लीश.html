• hallīśahallīśa m.
    • one of the eighteen Upa-rūpakas or minor dramatic entertainments (described as a piece in one act, consisting chiefly of singing and dancing by one male and 7, 8, or 10 female performers; perhaps a kind of ballet), Sāh. [1293,2]
  • hallīśahallīśa n.
    • a circular dance (performed by women under the direction of a man), Kāvyâd. [1293,2]
