• sūnāsūnāsūna a f.
    • a daughter, L. (for other meanings See sūnāsūnāsūna, p. 1242, col. 3) [1240,1]
  • sūnāsūnāsūna b f.
    • (prob. fr. √sivsiv, ‘to sew’, and connected with sūcisūcisūcīsūcin and sūtrasūtra; cf. sūnasūna, p. 1240, col. 1) a woven wicker-work basket or vessel of any kind, RV.; AV.; GṛŚrS. [1242,3]
    • a place for slaughtering animals, slaughter-house, butchery (wrongly śūnāśūnāśūna; cf. sūdanāsūdana), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1242,3]
    • the sale of flesh or meat, MW. [1242,3]
    • any place or utensil in a house where animals are liable to be accidentally destroyed (See pañca-sūnāpañcasūnāpañcasūnāsūna) [1242,3]
    • a stick fixed to an elephant's hook, L. [1242,3]
    • killing, hurting, injuring, BhP. [1242,3]
    • imminent death, danger of life (sūnāyāmsūnāsūna apiapiap, ‘even in the last extremity’), ib. [1242,3]
    • the uvula or soft palate (in this and the next sense perhaps connected with śūnaśūna), L. [1242,3]
    • inflammation of the glands of the neck (commonly called ‘mumps’), W. (accord. to some also, ‘a zone, girdle’; ‘a ray’; ‘a river’). [1242,3]
