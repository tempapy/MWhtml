• etadetad
  • mfn. (Gr. 223; g. sarvā̑disarvādi, Pāṇ. i, 1, 27) this, this here, here (especially as pointing to what is nearest to the speaker e.g. eṣaeṣa bāṇaḥbāṇaḥ, this arrow here in my hand; eṣaeṣa yātiyāti panthāḥpanthāḥ, here passes the way; eṣaeṣa kālaḥkālaḥ, here i.e. now, is the time; etadetad, this here i.e. this world here below) [231,2]
  • sometimes used to give emphasis to the personal pronouns (e.g. eṣo'hameṣoham, I, this very person here) or with omission of those pronouns (e.g. eṣaeṣa tvāṃtvāṃ svargaṃsvargaṃ nayāminayāmi, I standing here will convey thee to heaven; etauetau praviṣṭaupraviṣṭau svaḥsvaḥ, we two here have entered) [231,2]
  • as the subject of a sentence it agrees in gender and number with the predicate without reference to the noun to be supplied (e.g. etadetad evaeva hihi meme dhanamdhanam, for this [scil. cow] is my only wealth, MBh.) [231,2]
  • but sometimes the neuter sing. remains (e.g. etadetad guruṣuguruṣu vṛttiḥvṛttiḥ, this is the custom among Gurugurus, Mn. ii, 206) [231,2]
  • etadetad generally refers to what precedes, esp. when connected with idamidam, the latter then referring to what follows (e.g. eṣaeṣa vaivai prathamaḥprathamaḥ kalpaḥkalpaḥ . anukalpasanukalpas tvtv ayaṃayaṃ jñeyaḥjñeyaḥ, this before-mentioned is the principal rule, but this following may be considered a secondary rule, Mn. iii, 147) [231,2]
  • it refers also to that which follows, esp. when connected with a relative clause (e.g. eṣaeṣa ca͡ivacaïva gururgurur dharmodharmo yamyam pravakṣyāmypravakṣyāmy ahaṃahaṃ tavatava, this is the important law, which I will proclaim to you, MBh.), RV. &c. &c. [231,2]
  • etadetad
  • ind. in this manner, thus, so, here, at this time, now (e.g. nana vāvā uu etanetan mriyasemriyase, thou dost not die in this manner or by that, RV. i, 162, 21), AV.; VS.; &c.; [231,2]
