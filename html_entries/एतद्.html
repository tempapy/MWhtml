• etadetad mfn.
    • (Gr. 223; g. sarvā̑disarvādisarvaadādiādīādin, Pāṇ. i, 1, 27) this, this here, here (especially as pointing to what is nearest to the speaker, e.g. eṣaeṣa bāṇaḥ, this arrow here in my hand; eṣaeṣa yātiyātiyāt panthāḥ, here passes the way; eṣaeṣa kālaḥ, here i.e. now, is the time; etadetad, this here i.e. this world here below) [231,2]
    • sometimes used to give emphasis to the personal pronouns (e.g. eṣo'ham, I, this very person here) or with omission of those pronouns (e.g. eṣaeṣa tvāṃ svargaṃsvarga nayāmi, I standing here will convey thee to heaven; etauetaeti praviṣṭaupraviṣṭa svaḥsvaḥ, we two here have entered) [231,2]
    • as the subject of a sentence it agrees in gender and number with the predicate without reference to the noun to be supplied (e.g. etadetadetat evaeva hihihī mememamāmi dhanamdhana, for this [scil. cow] is my only wealth, MBh.) [231,2]
    • but sometimes the neuter sing. remains (e.g. etadetadetat guruṣuguru vṛttiḥ, this is the custom among Gurus, Mn. ii, 206) [231,2]
    • etadetad generally refers to what precedes, esp. when connected with idamidamid, the latter then referring to what follows (e.g. eṣaeṣa vaivaiuū prathamaḥ kalpaḥ . anukalpasanukalpa tvtu ayaṃayamaya jñeyaḥjñeya, this before-mentioned is the principal rule, but this following may be considered a secondary rule, Mn. iii, 147) [231,2]
    • it refers also to that which follows, esp. when connected with a relative clause (e.g. eṣaeṣa ca͡ivacaeva gurur dharmodharma yamyamya pravakṣyāmy ahaṃahaṃahamahaah tava, this is the important law, which I will proclaim to you, MBh.), RV. &c. &c. [231,2]
  • etadetad ind.
    • in this manner, thus, so, here, at this time, now (e.g. nana vāvāvauū uuū etanetanetat mriyase, thou dost not die in this manner or by that, RV. i, 162, 21), AV.; VS.; &c. [231,2]
