• savyasavya mf(āāa)n.
    • (accord. to Uṇ. iv, 109 fr. √sūsūsu; perhaps for skavya cf. Gk., col.3) left, left hand (amama, enaenaa, āāa, eeaāi, and ibc., ‘on the left’), RV. &c. &c. [1191,2]
    • opposite to left, right, right hand (amama, enaenaa, and ibc., ‘on the right’) [1191,2]
    • south, southern (amama &c., ‘to the south’), Sūryas.; VarBṛS. [1191,2]
    • reverse, contrary, backward, L. [1191,2]
  • savyasavya m.
    • the left arm or hand, RV.; Br.; KātyŚr.; Yājñ. [1191,2]
    • the left foot, KātyŚr.; Gobh. [1191,2]
    • one of the ten ways in which an eclipse takes place, VarBṛS. [1191,2]
    • a fire lighted at a person's death, L. [1191,2]
    • N. of Viṣṇu, L. [1191,2]
    • (savyasavya) N. of a man befriended by the Aśvins, RV. [1191,2]
    • of an Aṅgirasa and author of RV. i, 51-57; Anukr. [1191,2]
  • savyasavya n.
    • (scil. yajñȏpavītayajñopavītayajñaupavīta) the sacred thread worn over the left shoulder (acc. with √kṛkṛ, ‘to put the sacred thread over the left shoulder’), KātyŚr. Sch. [1191,2]
