• prā̑ṇā̑tman m. the spirit which connects the totality of subtle bodies like a thread = sūsrā̑tman (sometimes called hiraṇya-garbhahiraṇya-garbhahiraṇyagarbha), vital or animal soul (the lowest of the 3 souls of a human being ; the other 2 being jīvā̑tman and paramā̑tman), Tarkas. (cf. IW. 114). [705,3]
