iḍāiḍā, f. or (in Ṛgṛg-vedaveda) iłāiłā, (not to be confounded with the inst. case of iḍiḍ above), refreshing draught, refreshment, animation, recreation, comfort, vital spirit, RV.; AV.; AitBr. [164,2]
  • offering, libation (especially a holy libation, offered between the Pra-yāgaprayāgaprayāga and Anu-yāgaanuyāgaanuyāga, and consisting of four preparations of milk, poured into a vessel containing water, and then partially drunk by the priest and sacrificers; personified in the cow, the symbol of feeding, and nourishment), ŚBr. i, 8, 1, 1, &c.; AitBr.; KātyŚr.; Kauś. [164,2]
  • (metaphorically cf. idid), stream or flow of praise and worship (personified as the goddess of sacred speech and action, invoked together with Aditiaditi and other deities, but especially in the Āprīāprī hymns together with Sarasvatīsarasvatī and Mahīmahī or Bhāratībhāratī), RV.; AV.; VS. &c. [164,2]
  • the earth, food, Sāy. [164,3]
  • a cow [164,3]
  • the goddess iḍāiḍā or iłāiłā (daughter of Manumanu or of man thinking on and worshipping the gods; she is the wife of Budhabudha and mother of Purū-ravaspurūravaspurūravas; in another aspect she is called Maitrāvaruṇimaitrāvaruṇi as daughter of Mitramitra-Varuṇavaruṇa, two gods who were objects of the highest and most spiritual devotion) [164,3]
  • N. of Durgādurgā [164,3]
  • of a daughter of Dakṣadakṣa and wife of Kaśyapakaśyapa [164,3]
  • of a wife of Vasudevavasudeva and of the Rudrarudra Ṛta-dhvajaṛtadhvajaṛtadhvaja [164,3]
  • speech, BhP. [164,3]
  • heaven, L. [164,3]
  • earth, MBh. [164,3]
  • a particular artery on the left side of the body [164,3]
  • a tubular vessel (one of the principal channels of the vital spirit, that which is on the right side of the body), L. [164,3]
