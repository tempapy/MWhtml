• 1. vardhanavardhana n.
    • the act of cutting or cutting off (See nābhi-v°nābhivardhananābhivardhana) [926,1]
  • vardhanavardhana mfn.
    • ifc. after a proper N. prob. = ‘town’ (cf. puṇḍra-v°puṇḍravardhanapuṇḍravardhana, and Old Persian vardana). [926,1]
  • 2. vardhanavardhana mf(īīi)n.
    • increasing, growing, thriving, MBh. [926,1]
    • (often ifc.) causing to increase, strengthening, granting prosperity, RV. &c. &c. [926,1]
    • (mostly ifc.) animating, gladdening, exhilarating, MBh.; R.; BhP. [926,1]
  • vardhanavardhana m.
    • a granter of increase, bestower of prosperity, ib. [926,1]
    • a tooth growing over another tooth, Suśr. [926,1]
    • (in music) a kind of measure, Saṃgīt. [926,1]
    • N. of Śiva, MBh. [926,1]
    • of one of Skanda's attendants, ib. [926,1]
    • of a son of Kṛṣṇa and Mitra-vindā, BhP. [926,1]
  • vardhanavardhana a
    • &c. See p. 926, col. 1. [1010,2]
