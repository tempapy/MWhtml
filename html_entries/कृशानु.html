• kṛśānukṛśānu m.
    • (fr. √kṛśkṛś for kṛṣkṛṣ?), ‘bending the bow’, N. applied to a good archer (connected with astṛastṛ, ‘an archer’, though sometimes used alone; Kṛśānu, according to some, is a divine being, in character like Rudra or identified with him; armed with the lightning he defends the ‘heavenly’ Soma from the hawk, who tries to steal and bear it from heaven to earth), RV.; VS. iv, 27; AitBr. iii, 26 [306,1]
    • N. of Agni or fire, VS. v, 32; ŚāṅkhŚr. vi, 12, 3 [306,1]
    • (hence) fire, Suśr.; Ragh.; Kum.; Bhartṛ. [306,1]
    • N. of Viṣṇu, VarBṛS. xliii, 54 [306,1]
    • of a Gandharva [306,1]
    • Plumbago Zeylanica, L. [306,1]
