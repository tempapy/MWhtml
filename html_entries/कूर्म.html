• kūrma m. a tortoise, turtle, VS.; TS. &c. (ifc. f(ā). , MBh. iv, 2016) [300,2]
  • the earth considered as a tortoise swimming on the waters (See -vibhāgakūrma-vibhāgavibhāga) [300,2]
  • (hence) N. of the fourteenth Adhyāya of VarBṛS.; VarYogay. ix, 4 [300,2]
  • a particular figure or intertwining of the fingers (mudrā), Tantras. [300,2]
  • one of the outer winds of the body (causing the closing of the eyes), Vedântas. [300,2]
  • N. of a deity, Rasik. [300,2]
  • of a serpent or Kādraveya king, MBh. i, 2549 [300,2]
  • of a Ṛṣi (son of Gṛtsa-madagṛtsa-madagṛtsamada, author of RV. ii, 27-29), RAnukr. [300,2]
  • Viṣṇu's second incarnation (descent in the form of a tortoise to support the mountain Mandara at the churning of the ocean), NarasP. &c. [300,2]
  • kūrma ([cf. κλέμμυς, χέλυς, χελώνη.]) [300,2]
