• lakṣmīlakṣmī
  • f. (nom. īsīs, rarely īī; also ifc. as mf., but n(ii). ; cf. lakṣmīkalakṣmīka) a mark, sign, token, RV. x, 71, 2; Nir. iv, 10 [892,3]
  • (with or without pāpīpāpī) a bad sign, impending misfortune, AV.; ĀpŚr. [892,3]
  • (but in the older language more usually with puṇyāpuṇyā) a good sign, good fortune, prosperity, success, happiness (also pl.), AV. &c. &c. [892,3]
  • wealth, riches, Kāv.; Rājat. [892,3]
  • beauty, loveliness, grace, charm, splendour, lustre, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [892,3]
  • N. of the goddess of fortune and beauty (frequently in the later mythology identified with Śrīśrī and regarded as the wife of Viṣṇuviṣṇu or Nārāyaṇanārāyaṇa; accord. to R. i, 45, 40-43 she sprang with other precious things from the foam of the ocean when churned by the gods and demons for the recovery of the Amṛtaamṛta q.v.; she appeared with a lotus in her hand, whence she is also called Padmāpadmā; accord. to another legend she appeared at the creation floating over the water on the expanded petals of a lotus-flower, she is also variously regarded as a wife of Sūryasūrya, as a wife of Prajā-patiprajāpatiprajāpati, as a wife of Dharmadharma and mother of Kāmakāma, as sister or mother of Dhātṛdhātṛ and Vidhātṛvidhātṛ, as wife of Dattátreyadattatreya, as one of the 9 Śaktiśaktis of Viṣṇuviṣṇu, as a manifestation of Prakṛtiprakṛti &c., as identified with Dākṣāyaṇīdākṣāyaṇī in Bharatâśramabharatāśrama, and with Sītāsītā, wife of Rāmarāma, and with other women), ib. (cf. RTL. 103; 108 &c.) [892,3]
  • the Good Genius or Fortune of a king personified (and often regarded as a rival of his queen), royal power, dominion, majesty, Kāv.; Kathās.; Rājat. [892,3]
  • a partic. verse or formula, NṛsUp. [892,3]
  • N. of various plants (Hibiscus Mutabilis; Mimosa Suma; turmeric; a white Tulasītulasī; = ṛddhiṛddhi, vṛddhivṛddhi, priyaṅgupriyaṅgu, and phalinīphalinī), L. [892,3]
  • of the eleventh Kalākalā of the moon, Cat. [892,3]
  • of two kinds of metre, Col. [892,3]
  • the wife of a hero, L. [892,3]
  • = dravyadravya, L. [892,3]
  • a pearl, L. [892,3]
  • N. of the wife of king Candra-siṉhacandrasiṃhacandrasiṃha of Mithilāmithilā and patroness of various authors (also called lakhamālakhamā, laṣamālaṣamā, lakhimālakhimā or lachimālachimā), Cat. [892,3]
  • of a poetess, ib. [892,3]
  • of another woman, Śukas. [892,3]
