• lakṣmīlakṣmī f.
    • (nom. īsiī, rarely īīi; also ifc. as mf., but n(iiī). ; cf. lakṣmīkalakṣmīka) a mark, sign, token, RV. x, 71, 2; Nir. iv, 10 [892,3]
    • (with or without pāpīpāpin) a bad sign, impending misfortune, AV.; ĀpŚr. [892,3]
    • (but in the older language more usually with puṇyāpuṇyāpuṇya) a good sign, good fortune, prosperity, success, happiness (also pl.), AV. &c. &c. [892,3]
    • wealth, riches, Kāv.; Rājat. [892,3]
    • beauty, loveliness, grace, charm, splendour, lustre, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [892,3]
    • N. of the goddess of fortune and beauty (frequently in the later mythology identified with Śrī and regarded as the wife of Viṣṇu or Nārāyaṇa; accord. to R. i, 45, 40-43 she sprang with other precious things from the foam of the ocean when churned by the gods and demons for the recovery of the Amṛta q.v.; she appeared with a lotus in her hand, whence she is also called Padmā; accord. to another legend she appeared at the creation floating over the water on the expanded petals of a lotus-flower, she is also variously regarded as a wife of Sūrya, as a wife of Prajā-pati, as a wife of Dharma and mother of Kāma, as sister or mother of Dhātṛ and Vidhātṛ, as wife of Dattátreya, as one of the 9 Śaktis of Viṣṇu, as a manifestation of Prakṛti &c., as identified with Dākṣāyaṇī in Bharatâśrama, and with Sītā, wife of Rāma, and with other women), ib. (cf. RTL. 103; 108 &c.) [892,3]
    • the Good Genius or Fortune of a king personified (and often regarded as a rival of his queen), royal power, dominion, majesty, Kāv.; Kathās.; Rājat. [892,3]
    • a partic. verse or formula, NṛsUp. [892,3]
    • N. of various plants (Hibiscus Mutabilis; Mimosa Suma; turmeric; a white Tulasī; = ṛddhiṛddhi, vṛddhivṛddhi, priyaṅgupriyaṅgu, and phalinīphalinīphaliphalin), L. [892,3]
    • of the eleventh Kalā of the moon, Cat. [892,3]
    • of two kinds of metre, Col. [892,3]
    • the wife of a hero, L. [892,3]
    • = dravyadravya, L. [892,3]
    • a pearl, L. [892,3]
    • N. of the wife of king Candra-siṉha of Mithilā and patroness of various authors (also called lakhamā, laṣamā, lakhimā or lachimālachimā), Cat. [892,3]
    • of a poetess, ib. [892,3]
    • of another woman, Śukas. [892,3]
