• vy-āpyavyāpyavyvivīāpya mfn.
    • permeable, penetrable, capable of being attended by any inherent characteristic, BhP.; Kap. Sch.; Tarkas. [1037,3]
  • vy-āpyavyāpyavyvivīāpya n.
    • that which may be the site or locality of universal pervasion or of an invariably concomitant cause or characteristic (e.g. ‘smoke which is invariably pervaded by fire’), IW. 62 [1037,3]
    • the sign or middle term of an inference, proof, reason, cause (= sādhanasādhana, hetuhetu), L. [1037,3]
    • Costus Speciosus or Arabicus, L. [1037,3]
