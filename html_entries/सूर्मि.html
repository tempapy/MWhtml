sūrmisūrmi or sūrmīsūrmī, f. (rather fr. √sṛsṛ than fr. susu + ūrmiūrmi; also written śūrmiśūrmi and śūrmīśūrmī) a pipe for conveying water, RV.; ŚBr. [1244,3]
  • a kind of tube serving as a candlestick, RV.; TS.; Kāṭh. [1244,3]
  • a metal image, W. [1244,3]
  • a hollow metal column made red-hot for burning criminals (esp. adulterers) to death, Gaut.; Mn. &c. [1244,3]
