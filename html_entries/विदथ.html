• vidathavidatha n.
    • knowledge, wisdom [963,3]
    • (esp.) ‘knowledge given to others’ i.e. instruction, direction, order, arrangement, disposition, rule, command (also pl.), RV.; AV.; VS. (vidathamvidatha ā-√vadāvadāvad, to impart knowledge, give instruction, rule, govern) [963,3]
    • a meeting, assembly (either for deliberating or for the observance of festive or religious rites i.e.) council, community, association, congregation, ib. (also applied to partic. associations or communities of gods, which in RV. viii, 39, 1 &c. are opposed to those of men; in RV. ii, 27, 8; vi, 51, 2 &c. three associations of gods are mentioned) [963,3]
    • a host, army, body of warriors (esp. applied to the Maruts), RV. [963,3]
    • war, fight, ib. [963,3]
    • a sage, scholar, L. [963,3]
    • a saint, devotee, ascetic (= yoginyogin), L. [963,3]
    • = kṛtinkṛtin, L. [963,3]
    • N. of a man, Sāy. on RV. v, 33, 9. [963,3]
    • accord. to some for vidhā̑thavidhavidhāatha fr. √vidhvidh, and orig. = ‘homage, worship, sacrifice’; accord. to others also = ‘house, household, &c.’. [1332,2]
