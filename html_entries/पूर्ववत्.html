• 1. pūrvapūrva—vatvat mfn.
    • having (or relating to) something preceding or antecedent, VPrāt. [644,1]
    • (an argument) in which a conclusion is drawn from a previous cause to in effect, Nyāyad. [644,1]
  • pūrvapūrva—vatvat f.
    • one who has been previously married, Āp. [644,1]
  • 2. pūrvapūrva—vatvat ind.
    • as before, as hitherto, as heretofore, as aforesaid [644,1]
    • according to something previous (applied in the Nyāya to a kind of inference such as inferring from the previous appearance of a cloud that rain will fall), RV.; &c. [644,1]
  • pūrvapūrva—vatvat a n.
    • add, ‘reasoning from cause to effect’ (one of the 3 kinds of anumānaanumāna, cf. śeṣa-vatśeṣavatśeṣavat, p. 1332). [1330,2]
