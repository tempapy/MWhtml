• 1. kārakakāraka mf(ikā)n.
    • (generally ifc.) making, doing, acting, who or what does or produces or creates, MBh. &c. (cf. siṃha-k°siṃhakārakasiṃhakāraka, kṛtsna-k°kṛtsnakārakakṛtsnakāraka, śilpa-kśilpa) [274,2]
    • intending to act or do, Pāṇ. ii, 3, 70 Sch. [274,2]
  • kārakakāraka n.
    • ‘instrumental in bringing about the action denoted by a verb (= kriyā-hetukriyāhetu or -nimittakārakanimitta)’, the notion of a case (but not co-extensive with the term case; there are six such relations accord. to Pāṇ., viz. karmankarman, karaṇakaraṇa, kartṛkartṛ, sampradānasampradāna, apā̑dānaapādānaapaadānaādāna, adhikaraṇaadhikaraṇa, qq.vv. The idea of the genitive case is not considered a kārakakāraka, because it ordinarily expresses the relation of two nouns to each other, but not the relation of a noun and verb). [274,2]
  • 2. kārakakāraka n.
    • hail-water, L. [274,3]
