• kāraka mf(ikā)n. (generally ifc.) making, doing, acting, who or what does or produces or creates, MBh. &c. (cf. siṃha-k°siṃha-kārakasiṃhakāraka, kṛtsna-k°kṛtsna-kārakakṛtsnakāraka, śilpa-kśilpa-kśilpak) [274,2]
  • intending to act or do, Pāṇ. ii, 3, 70 Sch. [274,2]
  • kāraka n. ‘instrumental in bringing about the action denoted by a verb (= kriyā-hetukriyā-hetukriyāhetu or -nimittakāraka-nimittanimitta)’, the notion of a case (but not co-extensive with the term case; there are six such relations accord. to Pāṇ., viz. karman, karaṇa, kartṛ, sampradāna, apā̑dāna, adhikaraṇa, qq.vv. The idea of the genitive case is not considered a kāraka, because it ordinarily expresses the relation of two nouns to each other, but not the relation of a noun and verb). [274,2]
  • kāraka n. hail-water, L. [274,3]
