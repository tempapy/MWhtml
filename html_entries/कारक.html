  • kārakakāraka
  • n. ‘instrumental in bringing about the action denoted by a verb (= kriyā-hetukriyāhetukriyāhetu or -nimittanimittakāraka-nimitta)’, the notion of a case (but not co-extensive with the term case; there are six such relations accord. to Pāṇ., viz. karmankarman, karaṇakaraṇa, kartṛkartṛ, sampradānasampradāna, apā̑dānaapādāna, adhikaraṇaadhikaraṇa, qq.vv. The idea of the genitive case is not considered a kārakakāraka, because it ordinarily expresses the relation of two nouns to each other, but not the relation of a noun and verb). [274,2]
