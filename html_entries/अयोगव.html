ayo—gava and -gU/, see s.v. [85,1]
  • ayogava
  • m. the offspring of a Śūdraśūdra man and Vaiśyavaiśya woman (whose business is carpentry), Mn. x, 32, (since the word occurs only the loc. ayogave and is by both commentaries said to imply the female of this mixed tribe, the original reading is probably, as suggested by, BR. ayoguvi loc. fr. ayogU q.v.)
