• ivaiva
  • ind. (fr. pronominal base 3. ii), like, in the same manner as (in this sense = yathāyathā, and used correlatively to tathātathā) [168,3]
  • as it were, as if (e.g. pathevapatheva, as if on a path) [168,3]
  • in a certain manner, in some measure, a little, perhaps (in qualification or mitigation of a strong assertion) [168,3]
  • nearly, almost, about (e.g. muhūrtammuhūrtam ivaiva, almost an hour) [168,3]
  • so, just so, just, exactly, indeed, very (especially after words which involve some restriction e.g. īṣadīṣad ivaiva, just a little; kiṃcidkiṃcid ivaiva, just a little bit: and after a negation e.g. nana cirādcirād ivaiva, very soon). ivaiva is connected vaguely, and somewhat pleonastically, with an interrogative pronoun or adverb (e.g. kimkim ivaiva, what? kathamkatham ivaiva, how could that possibly be? kvevakveva, where, I should like to know?). In the Padapada texts of the Ṛgṛg, Yajuryajur, and Atharvaatharva-vedaveda, and by native grammarians, ivaiva is considered to be enclitic, and therefore compounded with the word after which it stands, RV.; AV.; &c. [168,3]
