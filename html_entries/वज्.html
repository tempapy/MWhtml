• vajvaj [Roots]
    • (prob. corrupted fr. √vrajvraj) cl. 1. P. vajati (pf. vavāja fut. vajitā &c. Gr.), to go, Dhātup. vii, 78:
    • Caus. or cl. 10. P. vājayati, to prepare the way, to trim or feather an arrow (mārga-mārgavaj, or mārgaṇa-saṃskā̑remārgaṇasaṃskāraarearaarāariāreāraārā), Dhātup. xxxii, 74. Another √vajvaj or uj, ‘to be hard or strong’, may be inferred from ugraugra, ojasojasojaoj, vajravajra, vājavāja (qq.vv.), the last of which gave rise to the Nom. vājayavājaya, q.v. [For cognate words See under ugraugra and ojasojasojaoj.] [913,1]
