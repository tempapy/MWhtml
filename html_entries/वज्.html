• vajvaj [Roots]
  • (prob. corrupted fr. √vrajvraj) cl. 1. P. vajativajati (pf. vavājavavāja fut. vajitāvajitā &c. Gr.), to go, Dhātup. vii, 78:
    Caus. or cl. 10. P. vājayativājayati, to prepare the way, to trim or feather an arrow (mārga-mārgamārgavaj, or mārgaṇa-saṃskā̑remārgaṇasaṃskāremārgaṇasaṃskāre), Dhātup. xxxii, 74. Another √vajvaj or ujuj, ‘to be hard or strong’, may be inferred from ugraugra, ojasojas, vajravajra, vājavāja (qq.vv.), the last of which gave rise to the Nom. vājayavājaya q.v. ([For cognate words See under ugraugra and ojasojas.]) [913,1]
