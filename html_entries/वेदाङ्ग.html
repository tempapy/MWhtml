  • vedā̑ṅgavedāṅga
  • n. ‘a limb (for preserving the body) of the Vedaveda’, N. of certain works or classes of works regarded as auxiliary to and even in some sense as part of the Vedaveda, (six are usually enumerated [and mostly written in the Sūtrasūtra or aphoristic style]; 1. śikṣāśikṣā, ‘the science of proper articulation and pronunciation’, comprising the knowledge of letters, accents, quantity, the use of the organs of pronunciation, and phonetics generally, but especially the laws of euphony peculiar to the Vedaveda [many short treatises and a chapter of the Taittirīya-āraṇyakataittirīyaāraṇyakataittirīyaāraṇyaka are regarded as the representatives of this subject; but other works on Vedic phonetics may be included under it See prātiśākhyaprātiśākhya] : 2. chandaschandas, ‘metre’ [represented by a treatise ascribed to Piṅgala-nāgapiṅgalanāgapiṅgalanāga, which, however, treats of Prākṛt as well as Saṉskṛt metres, and includes only a few of the leading Vedic metres] : 3. vyākaraṇavyākaraṇa, ‘linguistic analysis or grammar’ [represented by Pāṇinipāṇini's celebrated Sūtrasūtras] : 4. niruktanirukta, ‘explanation of difficult Vedic words’ [cf. yāskayāska] : 5. jyotiṣajyotiṣa, ‘astronomy’, or rather the Vedic calendar [represented by a small tract, the object of which is to fix the most auspicious days for sacrifices] : 6. kalpakalpa, ‘ceremonial’, represented by a large number of Sūtrasūtra works [cf. sūtrasūtra] : the first and second of these Vedâṅgavedāṅgas are said to be intended to secure the correct reading or recitation of the Vedaveda, the third and fourth the understanding of it, and the fifth and sixth its proper employment at sacrifices: the Vedâṅgavedāṅgas are alluded to by Manumanu, who calls them, in iii, 184, Pravacanapravacanas, ‘expositions’, a term which is said to be also applied to the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas), IW. 145 &c. [1016,3]
