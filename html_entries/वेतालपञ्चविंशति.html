• vetāla—pañca-viṃśatipañca-viṃśatipañcaviṃśati or vetāla—pañca-viṃśa°tikāpañca-viṃśa°tikāpañcaviṃśa°tikā, f. a collection of 25 tales or fables told by a Vetāla or demon to king Vikramâditya (of which there are 5 recensions extant, one by Kṣemêndra in his Bṛhat-kathā-mañjarībṛhat-kathā-mañjarībṛhatkathāmañjarī, one by Soma-devasoma-devasomadeva in the Kathā-sarit-sāgarakathā-sarit-sāgarakathāsaritsāgara, and the other three by Jambhala-dattajambhala-dattajambhaladatta, Vallabha, and Śiva-dāsaśiva-dāsaśivadāsa ; versions of these popular tales exist in Hindī, Tamil and Telugu, and almost every Hindū vernacular).
