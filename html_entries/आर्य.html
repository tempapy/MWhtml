• āryaārya
  • m. (fr. aryaarya, √ṛṛ), a respectable or honourable or faithful man, an inhabitant of Āryāvartaāryāvarta [152,2]
  • one who is faithful to the religion of his country [152,2]
  • N. of the race which immigrated from Central Asia into Āryāvartaāryāvarta (opposed to an-āryaanāryaanārya, dasyudasyu, dāsadāsa) [152,2]
  • in later times N. of the first three castes (opposed to śūdraśūdra), RV.; AV.; VS.; MBh.; Yājñ.; Pañcat. &c. [152,2]
  • a man highly esteemed, a respectable, honourable man, Pañcat.; Śak. &c. [152,2]
  • a master, an owner, L. [152,2]
  • a friend, L. [152,2]
  • a Vaiśyavaiśya, L. [152,2]
  • Buddha [152,2]
  • (with Buddhists [Pālipāli ayyoayyo, or ariyoariyo]) a man who has thought on the four chief truths of Buddhism (See next col.) and lives accordingly, a Buddhist priest [152,2]
  • a son of Manumanu Sāvarṇasāvarṇa, Hariv. [152,2]
