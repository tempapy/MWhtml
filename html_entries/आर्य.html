• āryaārya m.
    • (fr. aryaarya, √ṛṛ), a respectable or honourable or faithful man, an inhabitant of Āryāvarta [152,2]
    • one who is faithful to the religion of his country [152,2]
    • N. of the race which immigrated from Central Asia into Āryāvarta (opposed to an-āryaanāryaanārya, dasyudasyu, dāsadāsa) [152,2]
    • in later times N. of the first three castes (opposed to śūdraśūdra), RV.; AV.; VS.; MBh.; Yājñ.; Pañcat. &c. [152,2]
    • a man highly esteemed, a respectable, honourable man, Pañcat.; Śak. &c. [152,2]
    • a master, an owner, L. [152,2]
    • a friend, L. [152,2]
    • a Vaiśya, L. [152,2]
    • Buddha [152,2]
    • (with Buddhists [Pāli ayyo, or ariyo]) a man who has thought on the four chief truths of Buddhism (See next col.) and lives accordingly, a Buddhist priest [152,2]
    • a son of Manu Sāvarṇa, Hariv. [152,2]
  • āryaārya mf(āāa and ārīārī)n.

    • behaving like an Āryan, worthy of one, honourable, respectable, noble, R.; Mn.; Śak. &c. [152,2]
    • of a good family [152,2]
    • excellent [152,2]
    • wise [152,2]
