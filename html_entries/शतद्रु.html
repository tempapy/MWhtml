• śataśata—drudrudrū f.
    • ‘flowing in a hundred (or numerous) branches’, N. of a river now called the Sutlej (it is the most easterly of the five rivers of the Pañjāb, and rises in a lake [prob. Mānasa Sarovar] on the Himâlaya mountains; flowing in a southwesterly direction for 550 miles, it unites with the Vipāśā or Beas south-east of Amritsar [see vipāśvipāś], afterwards joining the Chenāb and filling into the Indus below Multan; it is also called śutu-driśutudrī, śutu-drudrudrū, śita-druśitadruśitadrudrū &c.), MBh.; Hariv.; R. &c. [1049,2]
    • N. of the Ganges, MW. [1049,2]
