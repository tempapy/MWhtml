• śata—dru f. ‘flowing in a hundred (or numerous) branches’, N. of a river now called the Sutlej (it is the most easterly of the five rivers of the Pañjāb, and rises in a lake [prob. Mānasa Sarovar] on the Himâlaya mountains; flowing in a southwesterly direction for 550 miles, it unites with the Vipāśā or Beas south-east of Amritsar [see vipāś], afterwards joining the Chenāb and filling into the Indus below Multan; it is also called śutu-driśutu-driśutudri, śutu-druśutu-druśutudru, śita-druśita-druśitadru &c.), MBh.; Hariv.; R. &c.
