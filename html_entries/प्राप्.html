• prāpprāp
  • (pra-√āppraāppraāp) P. Ā. prāpnotiprāpnoti (irreg. Pot. prāpeyamprāpeyam), to attain to;
    reach, arrive at, meet with, find, AV. &c. &c.;
    to obtain, receive (also as a husband or wife), MBh.; Kāv. &c.;
    to incur (a fine), Mn. viii, 225;
    to suffer (capital punishment), ib. 364;
    (with diśaḥdiśaḥ) to flee in all directions, Bhaṭṭ.;
    to extend, stretch, reach to (Ā.), Pāṇ. v, 2, 8;
    to be present or at hand, AV.;
    (in gram.) to pass or be changed into (acc.), Siddh. : to result (from a rule), be in force, obtain (also Pass.), Kāś. :
    Caus. prāpayatiprāpayati, °teprāpte (ind.p. prāpayyaprāpayya, or prāpyaprāpya, Pāṇ. vi, 4, 57 Sch.), to cause to reach or attain (2 acc.), advance, promote, further (P. ChUp.; MBh. &c.; Ā. TBr.; MBh.);
    to lead or bring to (dat.), VP.;
    to impart, communicate, announce, relate, Mn.; MBh. &c.;
    to meet with, obtain, R. :
    Desid. prepsatiprepsati, to try to attain, strive to reach, ŚBr. [707,2]
