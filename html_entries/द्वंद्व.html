• dvaṃdvadvaṃdva n.
    • (the repeated nom. of dvadva) pair, couple, male and female, TS.; Br.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. (°amdvaṃdvamdvaṃdva ind., °dvedvaṃdvedvaṃdva ind., or °dvenadvaṃdvenadvaṃdva ind. by two, face to face, secretly) [503,2]
    • a pair of opposites (e.g. heat and cold, joy and sorrow &c.), Up.; Mn.; MBh.; R. &c. [503,2]
    • strife, quarrel, contest, fight (esp. between two persons, a duel), MBh.; R.; Hit. [503,2]
    • stronghold, fortress, L. [503,2]
  • dvaṃdvadvaṃdva m.
    • (scil. samāsasamāsa; rarely n.) a copulative compound (or any compound in which the members if uncompounded would be in the same case and connected by the conjunction, ‘and’ cf. devatā-devatādvaṃdvadevatādvaṃdva, nakṣatra-nakṣatradvaṃdvanakṣatradvaṃdva), Pāṇ. ii, 2, 29; 4, 2 [503,2]
  • dvaṃdvadvaṃdva m.
    • N. of sev. Ekāhas, KātyŚr. [503,2]
    • the sign of the zodiac Gemini, Gol. [503,2]
    • (in music) a kind of measure [503,2]
    • a species of disease, a complication of two disorders, a compound affection of two humours, L. [503,2]
