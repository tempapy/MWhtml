• yuvatiyuvati
  • f. a girl, young woman, any young female animal, RV. &c. &c. (in, RV. applied to Uṣasuṣas, Night and Morning, Heaven and Earth &c. with śaryāśaryā, prob. ‘an arrow just shot off’; but cf. yuvāyuvā; ifc. as f. for yuvanyuvan, a youth e.g. sa-bāla-vṛddhayuvatiḥsabālavṛddhayuvatiḥsabālavṛddhayuvatiḥ purīpurī, a town with boys, old and young men, Hariv.) [855,3]
