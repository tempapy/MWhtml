• 1. sasa
    • the last of the three sibilants (it belongs to the dental class and in sound corresponds to s in sin). [1111,2]
  • 2. sasa
    • (in prosody) an anapest (˘ ˘ ¯) [1111,2]
  • 3. sasa
    • (in music) an abbreviated term for ṣaḍ-jaṣaḍjaṣaḍṣaṭja (See p. 1109, col. 2). [1111,2]
  • 4. sasa m.
    • (only L.) a snake [1111,2]
    • air, wind [1111,2]
    • a bird [1111,2]
    • N. of Viṣṇu or Śiva [1111,2]
  • sasa n.
    • knowledge [1111,2]
    • meditation [1111,2]
    • a carriage road [1111,2]
    • a fence. [1111,2]
  • 5. sasa mfn.
    • (fr. √sansansat) procuring, bestowing (only ifc.; cf. palu-ṣaṣa and priya-sapriyasapriyasa). [1111,2]
  • sasa 6
    • the actual base for the nom. case of the 3rd pers. pron. tadtad q.v. (occurring only in the nom. sg. mf. [sasa or sassassa, sāsāsa], and in the Ved. loc. [sasminsa, RV. i, 152, 6; i, 174, 4; x, 95, 11]; the final s of the nom. m. is dropped before all consonants [except before p in RV. v, 2, 4, and before t in RV. viii, 33, 16] and appears only at the end of a sentence in the form of Visarga; sasa occasionally blends with another vowel [as in saī̑ṣaḥiṣaiṣīṣaīṣ]; and it is often for emphasis connected with another pron. as with ahamahamaha, tvam, eṣaeṣa, ayamayamaya &c. [e.g. sososusassa 'ham, sasa tvam, ‘I (or thou) that very person’; cf. under tadtad, p.434], the verb then following in the 1st and 2nd pers. even if ahamahamaha or tvam be omitted [e.g. sasa tvātu pṛcchāmi ‘I that very person ask you’ BṛĀrUp.; sasa vaivaiuū nononunasna brūhi ‘do thou tell us’ ŚBr.]; similarly, to denote emphasis, with bhavānbhavabhavat [e.g. sasa bhavānbhavabhavat vijayāyavijaya pratiṣṭhatām, ‘let your Highness set out for victory’ Śak.]; it sometimes [and frequently in the Brāhmaṇas] stands as the first word of a sentence preceding a rel. pronoun or adv. such as yaya, yadyad, yadiyadiyad, yathāyathā, cȇdcedcaid; in this position sasa may be used pleonastically or as a kind of ind., even where another gender or number is required [e.g. sasa yadiyadiyad sthāvarāsthāvarāsthāvara āpoāpoāpasāpa bhananti, ‘if those waters are stagnant’ ŚBr.]; in the Sāṃkhya sasa, like eṣaeṣa, kaka, and yaya, is used to denote Puruṣa, ‘the Universal Soul’), RV. &c. &c. [1111,2]

  • 7. sasa ind.
    • (connected with sahasaha, samsamsa, samasama, and occasionally in BhP. standing for sahasaha with instr.) an inseparable prefix expressing ‘junction’, ‘conjunction’, ‘possession’ (as opp. to aa priv.), ‘similarity’, ‘equality’ [1111,2]

[1111,2]
