• sāmba m. (also written śāmba) N. of a son of Kṛṣṇa and Jāmbavatī (in consequence of the curse of some holy sages who had been deceived by a female disguise which he had assumed, he was condemned to produce offspring in the shape of a terrific iron club for the destruction of the race of Vṛṣṇi and Andhaka; he is said to have been instructed by Nārada in the worship of the sun, and by Vyāsa in the ritual of the Magi), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. [1207,1]
  • (also with śāstrin) N. of various authors and teachers, Cat. [1207,1]
  • sāmba n. = -purāṇasāmba-purāṇapurāṇa, ib. [1207,1]
  • sā̑mba mfn. attended by Ambā (q.v.), KāśīKh. [1207,1]
