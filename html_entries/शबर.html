• śabaraśabara mfn.
    • - (also written śavaraśavara; cf. śabalaśabala below) variegated, brindled, L. [1052,2]
    • relating or belonging to a Śabara (prob. for śābaraśābara), MBh. [1052,2]
  • śabaraśabara m.
    • N. of a wild mountaineer tribe in the Deccan (in later language applied to any savage or barbarian = kirātakirāta, pulindapulinda, bhillabhilla; accord. to L. ‘the son of a Śūdra and a Bhillī’), AitBr.; MBh. &c. [1052,2]
    • a kind of Lodhra or Lodh tree, L. (cf. comp.) [1052,2]
    • N. of Śiva, L. [1052,2]
    • (with kāṣivata) N. of the author of RV. x, 169; Anukr. [1052,2]
    • of a poet, Cat. [1052,2]
    • of a Buddhist, ib. [1052,2]
    • = śabara-svāminśabarasvāminśabarasvāmin (in śabara-bhāṣyaśabarabhāṣyaśabarabhāṣya q.v.) [1052,2]
    • = hastahasta and śāstra-viśeṣaśāstraviśeṣa, L. [1052,2]
  • śabaraśabara n.
    • water, L. (prob. w.r. for śambaraśambara). [1052,2]
