• venven [Roots]
  • (in, Dhātup. xxi, 13 v.l. for veṇveṇ q.v.) cl. 1. P. venativenati, to care or long for, be anxious, yearn for, RV.; ŚBr.;
    to tend outwards (said of the vital air), AitBr.;
    to be homesick, TBr.;
    to be envious or jealous, RV. (accord. to Naigh. ii, 6 and 14 also ‘to go’ and ‘to worship’). [1018,2]
