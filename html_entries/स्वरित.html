  • svarita m. n. the Svarita accent (a kind of mixed tone, produced by a combination of high and low tone, and therefore named in Pāṇ. i, 2, 31 sam-āhārasam-āhārasamāhāra, the high and low tones being called ud-āttaud-āttaudātta, ‘raised’ or ‘acute’, and an-udāttaan-udāttaanudātta, ‘low’ or ‘grave’; the Svarita corresponds to the Greek circumflex and is of four kinds, viz. kṣaipra [as in vy-āptavy-āptavyāpta for vi-āptavi-āptaviāpta], jātya [as in kva for kua], praśliṣṭa [as in divīva for divi iva], or abhinihita [as in te 'bruvan for te abruvan]; it is marked in RV. by a small upright stroke above a syllable; and when produced by an udātta immediately preceding is sometimes called ‘a dependent Svarita’, and, when it properly belongs to a word, an ‘independent Svarita’), ĀśvŚr.; Prāt.; Pāṇ.
