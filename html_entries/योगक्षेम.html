• yogayoga—kṣemakṣema
  • m. sg. and pl. (in later language also m. du. and n. sg.) the security or secure possession of what has been acquired, the keeping safe of property, welfare, prosperity, substance, livelihood, RV. &c. &c. (generally explained as a Dvaṃdvadvaṃdva meaning ‘acquisition and preservation of property’ cf. kṣema-yogakṣemayogakṣemayoga; °maṃ-√vahyogakṣemaṃvahyogakṣemaṃvah with dat. = to procure any one a livelihood, support, maintain, Śak.) [856,3]
  • the charge for securing property (from accidents), insurance, Mn. vii, 127 [856,3]
  • property destined for pious uses and sacrifices, Gaut. xxviii, 46; Mn. ix, 219 (others ‘the means of securing protection, i.e. councillors, family priests and the like’) [856,3]
