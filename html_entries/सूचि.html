sūcisūci or sūcīsūcī, f. (prob. to be connected with sūtrasūtra, syūtasyūta &c. fr. √sivsiv, ‘to sew’ cf. sūkṣmasūkṣma; in R. once sūcināsūcinā instr.), a needle or any sharp-pointed instrument (e.g. ‘a needle used in surgery’, ‘a magnet’ &c.), RV. &c. &c. [1241,1]
  • the sharp point or tip of anything or any pointed object, Kāv.; Car.; BhP. [1241,1]
  • a rail or balustrade, Divyâv. [1241,1]
  • a small door-bolt, L. [1241,1]
  • ‘sharp file or column’, a kind of military array (accord. to Kull. on Mn. vii, 187, ‘placing the sharpest and most active soldiers in front’), Mn.; MBh.; Kām. [1241,1]
  • an index, table of contents (in books printed in India; cf. -pattrapattrasūcipattra below) [1241,1]
  • a triangle formed by the sides of a trapezium produced till they meet, Col. [1241,1]
  • a cone, pyramid, ib. [1241,1]
  • (in astron.) the earth's disc in computing eclipses (or ‘the corrected diameter of the earth’), Sūryas. [1241,1]
  • gesticulation, dramatic action, L. [1241,1]
  • a kind of coitus, L. [1241,1]
  • sight, seeing (= dṛṣṭidṛṣṭi), L. [1241,1]
  • sūcisūci
  • m. (only sūcisūci) the son of Niṣādaniṣāda and a Vaiśyāvaiśyā, L. [1241,1]
  • a maker of winnowing baskets &c. (cf. sūnāsūnā), L. [1241,1]
