• vrātyavrātya m.
    • a man of the mendicant or vagrant class, a tramp, out-caste, low or vile person (either a man who has lost caste through non-observance of the ten principal Saṃskāras, or a man of a partic. low caste descended from a Śūdra and a Kṣatriyā; accord. to some ‘the illegitimate son of a Kṣatriya who knows the habits and intentions of soldiers’; in AV. xv, 8, 1; 9, 1, the Rājanyas and even the Brāhmans are said to have sprung from the Vrātya who is identified with the Supreme Being, prob. in glorification of religious mendicancy; accord. to ĀpŚr. vrātyavrātya is used in addressing a guest), AV. &c. &c. [1043,2]
