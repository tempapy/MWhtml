• vrātyavrātya
  • m. a man of the mendicant or vagrant class, a tramp, out-caste, low or vile person (either a man who has lost caste through non-observance of the ten principal Saṃskārasaṃskāras, or a man of a partic. low caste descended from a Śūdraśūdra and a Kṣatriyākṣatriyā; accord. to some ‘the illegitimate son of a Kṣatriyakṣatriya who knows the habits and intentions of soldiers’; in AV. xv, 8, 1; 9, 1, the Rājanyarājanyas and even the Brāhmans are said to have sprung from the Vrātyavrātya who is identified with the Supreme Being, prob. in glorification of religious mendicancy; accord. to ĀpŚr. vrātyavrātya is used in addressing a guest), AV. &c. &c.
