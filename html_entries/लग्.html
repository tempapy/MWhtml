• lag (cf. √lakṣ, lakṣa &c.) cl. 1. P. (Dhātup. xix, 24) lagati (accord. to Nir. iv, 10 also lagyati; pf. lalāga Gr.; aor. alagīt, ib.; fut. lagitā, ib.; lagiṣyati, Pañcat.; ind.p. lagitvā, -lagyalag-lagyalagya, Kāv.),
    to adhere, stick, cling or attach one's self to (loc.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. (with hṛdi and gen., ‘to penetrate to a person's heart’ Kathās.);
    to take effect upon (loc.), Śiś.;
    to meet, come in contact, cut (as lines), Gol. Sch.;
    to follow closely, ensue or happen immediately, Kathās.;
    to pass away (as time), Pañcat. :
    Caus. or cl. 10. (Dhātup. xxxiii, 63) lāgayati, ‘to taste’ or ‘to obtain’ (āsvādane, or āsādane). ([In Hindi this root often means ‘to begin.’]) [893,2]
