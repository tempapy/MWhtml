• laglag [Roots]
  • (cf. √lakṣlakṣ, lakṣalakṣa &c.) cl. 1. P. (Dhātup. xix, 24) lagatilagati (accord. to Nir. iv, 10 also lagyatilagyati; pf. lalāgalalāga Gr.; aor. alagītalagīt, ib.; fut. lagitālagitā, ib.; lagiṣyatilagiṣyati, Pañcat.; ind.p. lagitvālagitvā, -lagyalagyalagya, Kāv.),
    to adhere, stick, cling or attach one's self to (loc.), MBh.; Kāv. &c. (with hṛdihṛdi and gen., ‘to penetrate to a person's heart’ Kathās.);
    to take effect upon (loc.), Śiś.;
    to meet, come in contact, cut (as lines), Gol. Sch.;
    to follow closely, ensue or happen immediately, Kathās.;
    to pass away (as time), Pañcat. :
    Caus. or cl. 10. (Dhātup. xxxiii, 63) lāgayatilāgayati, ‘to taste’ or ‘to obtain’ (āsvādaneāsvādane, or āsādaneāsādane). ([In Hindi this root often means ‘to begin.’]) [893,2]
