• smṛtismṛti
  • f. remembrance, reminiscence, thinking of or upon (loc. or comp.), calling to mind (smṛtimsmṛtim apiapi tete nana yāntiyānti, ‘they are not even thought of’), memory, TĀr.; ChUp.; MBh. &c. [1272,3]
  • memory as one of the Vyabhicāri-bhāvavyabhicāribhāvavyabhicāribhāvas (q.v.), Daśar. [1272,3]
  • Memory (personified either as the daughter of Dakṣadakṣa and wife of Aṅgirasaṅgiras or as the daughter of Dharmadharma and Medhāmedhā), Hariv.; Pur. [1272,3]
  • the whole body of sacred tradition or what is remembered by human teachers (in contradistinction to śrutiśruti or what is directly heard or revealed to the Ṛṣiṛṣis; in its widest acceptation this use of the term Smṛtismṛti includes the 6 Vedâṅgavedāṅgas, the Sūtrasūtras both śrautaśrauta and gṛhyagṛhya, the law-books of Manumanu &c. [see next]; the Itihāsaitihāsas [e.g. the Mahābhāratamahābhārata and Rāmâyaṇarāmāyaṇa], the Purāṇapurāṇas and the Nītiśāstranītiśāstras; itiiti smṛteḥsmṛteḥ, ‘accord. to such and such a traditional precept or legal text’), IW. 144, 145 [1272,3]
  • the whole body of codes of law as handed down memoriter or by tradition (esp. the codes of Manumanu, Yājñavalkyayājñavalkya and the 16 succeeding inspired lawgivers, viz. Atriatri, Viṣṇuviṣṇu, Hārītahārīta, Uśanasuśanas or Śukraśukra, Aṅgirasaṅgiras, Yamayama, Āpastambaāpastamba, Saṃvartasaṃvarta, Kātyāyanakātyāyana, Bṛhas-patibṛhaspatibṛhaspati, Parāśaraparāśara, Vyāsavyāsa, Śaṅkhaśaṅkha, Likhîtalikhīta, Dakṣadakṣa and Gautamagautama; all these lawgivers being held to be inspired and to have based their precepts on the Vedaveda; cf. IW. 203), GṛŚrS.; Mn.; Yājñ. &c. [1272,3]
  • symbolical N. for the number 18 (fr. the 18 lawgivers above) [1272,3]
  • a kind of metre, L. [1272,3]
  • N. of the letter gg, Up. [1272,3]
  • desire, wish, Pañcat. iii, 258 (v.l. for spṛhāspṛhā). [1272,3]
