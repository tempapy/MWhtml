• sāmasāma—vedaveda
  • m. ‘Vedaveda of chants’, N. of one of the three principal Vedavedas (See vedaveda) [1205,2]
  • it contains a number of verses or stanzas nearly all of which [except about 78] occur in the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda and which, modified in various ways, are chanted, mostly, by the Udgātṛudgātṛ priests at Somasoma sacrifices [1205,2]
  • the Saṃhitāsaṃhitā of the Sāma-vedasāmavedasāmaveda consists of two parts [1205,2]
  • the first, called Ārcikaārcika [or Pūrvârcikapūrvārcika or Chando-granthachandogranthachandograntha], contains 585 verses disjoined from their proper sequence in the Ṛg-vedaṛgvedaṛgveda and arranged in 59 Daśatidaśatis or decades, which again are subdivided into Prapāṭhakaprapāṭhakas and Ardha-prapāṭhakaardhaprapāṭhakaardhaprapāṭhakas [1205,2]
  • the second, called Uttarârcikauttarārcika or Uttarā-granthauttarāgranthauttarāgrantha, contains 1225 verses, also chiefly from the Ṛk-saṃhitāṛksaṃhitāṛksaṃhitā, but less disjointed than in the first part, and arranged in nine Prapāṭhakaprapāṭhakas with Ardha-prapāṭhakaardhaprapāṭhakaardhaprapāṭhakas, mostly, however, grouped in triplets [1205,2]
  • the directions for the formation of Sāmansāmans or chants out of these verses are carefully laid down in the Gānagānas or manuals for chanting, two of which, viz. the Geya-gānageyagānageyagāna and Āraṇya-gānaāraṇyagānaāraṇyagāna, are a directory for the Ārcikaārcika portion, and two, viz. Ūha-gānaūhagānaūhagāna and Ūhya-gānaūhyagānaūhyagāna, for the Uttarârcikāuttarārcikā [1205,2]
  • in Mn. i, 23 the Sāma-vedasāmavedasāmaveda is described as drawn forth from the sun [1205,2]
  • in iv, 124 it is described as having a special reference to the Pitṛpitṛs or deceased ancestors, and its sound is therefore said to possess a kind of impurity, whereas the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda has the gods for his objects and the Yajurvedayajurveda men [1205,2]
  • the Sāma-vedasāmavedasāmaveda is said to possess 8 Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas [see brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇa] Br.; ŚāṅkhŚr. &c. ([IW. 25]) [1205,2]
