• hṛdhṛd
  • n. (optionally substituted for hṛdayahṛdaya in the weak cases i.e. in all except the first five inflexions; thought to be connected with śradśrad q.v.) the heart (as the seat of feelings and emotions), soul, mind (as seat of thought and intellectual operations; hṛdyhṛdy avedinavedin, ‘having no capacity of knowledge in the heart or mind’, said of animals), breast, chest, stomach, interior (also in older language, ‘interior of the body’), RV.; &c. [1302,2]
