• evamevameva ind.
    • (fr. pronom. base eeaāi, BRD.; probably connected with 1. evaeva), thus, in this way, in such a manner, such, (it is not found in the oldest hymns of the Veda, where its place is taken by 1. evaeva, but occurs in later hymns and in the Brāhmaṇas, especially in connection with √vidvid, ‘to know’, and its derivatives [e.g. yayayāyuyasī evaṃevameva vedaveda, he who knows so; cf. evaṃ-videvaṃvidevamvid, col.3]; in classical Sanskṛt evamevameva occurs very frequently, especially in connection with the roots vacvac, ‘to speak’, and śruśru, ‘to hear’, and refers to what precedes as well as to what follows [e.g. evamevameva uktvāuktvā, having so said ; evamevameva eva͡itatevaetatetad, this is so; evamevameva astuastu or evamevameva bhavatu, be it so, I assent; astyasti evamevameva, it is so; yadyyadiyad evamevameva, if this be so; kimkim evamevameva, how so? what is the meaning of it? what does this refer to? ma͡ivammamāevameva, not so! evamevameva - yathāyathā or yathāyathā - evamevameva, so - as]), Mn.; Śak. &c. [232,2]
    • (it is also often used like an adjective [e.g. evaṃevameva tetatā vacanevacana rataḥrata, rejoicing in such words of thine; where evamevameva = evaṃ-vidheevaṃvidhaevamvidhavidhāvidhividh]), MBh.; Śak. &c. [232,3]
    • sometimes evamevameva is merely an expletive [232,3]
    • according to lexicographers evamevameva may imply likeness (so) [232,3]
    • sameness of manner (thus) [232,3]
    • assent (yes, verily) [232,3]
    • affirmation (certainly, indeed, assuredly) [232,3]
    • command (thus, &c.) [232,3]
    • and be used as an expletive. [232,3]
