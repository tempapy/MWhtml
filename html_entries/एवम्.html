• evamevam
  • ind. (fr. pronom. base ee, BRD.; probably connected with 1. evaeva), thus, in this way, in such a manner, such, (it is not found in the oldest hymns of the Vedaveda, where its place is taken by 1. evaeva, but occurs in later hymns and in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas, especially in connection with √vidvid, ‘to know’, and its derivatives [e.g. yaya evaṃevaṃ vedaveda, he who knows so; cf. evaṃ-videvaṃvidevaṃvid, col.3]; in classical Sanskṛt evamevam occurs very frequently, especially in connection with the roots vacvac, ‘to speak’, and śruśru, ‘to hear’, and refers to what precedes as well as to what follows [e.g. evamevam uktvāuktvā, having so said ; evamevam evaitatevaitat, this is so; evamevam astuastu or evamevam bhavatubhavatu, be it so, I assent; astyasty evamevam, it is so; yadyyady evamevam, if this be so; kimkim evamevam, how so? what is the meaning of it? what does this refer to? maivammaivam, not so! evamevam - yathāyathā or yathāyathā - evamevam, so - as]), Mn.; Śak. &c. [232,2]
  • (it is also often used like an adjective [e.g. evaṃevaṃ tete vacanevacane rataḥrataḥ, rejoicing in such words of thine; where evamevam = evaṃ-vidheevaṃvidheevaṃvidhe]), MBh.; Śak. &c. [232,3]
  • sometimes evamevam is merely an expletive [232,3]
  • according to lexicographers evamevam may imply likeness (so) [232,3]
  • sameness of manner (thus) [232,3]
  • assent (yes, verily) [232,3]
  • affirmation (certainly, indeed, assuredly) [232,3]
  • command (thus, &c.) [232,3]
  • and be used as an expletive. [232,3]
