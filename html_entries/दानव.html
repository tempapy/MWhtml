• dānava m. (fr. 2. dānu) a class of demons often identified with the Daityas or Asuras and held to be implacable enemies of the gods or Devas, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; Mn.; MBh. &c. (described as children of Danu and Kaśyapa, sometimes reckoned as 40 in number, MBh. i, 252; sometimes as 100 &c.) [474,3]
  • dānava mf(ī)n. belonging to the Danu, MBh.; R. &c. [474,3]
