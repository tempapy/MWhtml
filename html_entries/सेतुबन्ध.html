• setusetu—bandhabandha
  • m. the forming of a causeway or bridge, a dam or bridge (esp. the ridge of rocks extending from Rāmêśvararāmeśvara on the Southeastern coast of India to Ceylon, and supposed to have been formed by Hanumathanumat as a bridge for the passage of Rāmarāma's army), MBh.; R. &c. [1246,2]
  • N. of various works (esp. of the 13th ch. of the Bhaṭṭi-kāvyabhaṭṭikāvyabhaṭṭikāvya and of a Prākṛt poem on the history of Rāmarāma, also called rāma-seturāmaseturāmasetu, or rāvaṇa-vaharāvaṇavaharāvaṇavaha, attributed to Pravarasenapravarasena and sometimes to Kālidāsakālidāsa). [1246,2]
