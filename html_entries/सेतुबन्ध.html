• setu—bandha m. the forming of a causeway or bridge, a dam or bridge (esp. the ridge of rocks extending from Rāmêśvara on the Southeastern coast of India to Ceylon, and supposed to have been formed by Hanumat as a bridge for the passage of Rāma's army), MBh.; R. &c. [1246,2]
  • N. of various works (esp. of the 13th ch. of the Bhaṭṭi-kāvyabhaṭṭi-kāvyabhaṭṭikāvya and of a Prākṛt poem on the history of Rāma, also called rāma-seturāma-seturāmasetu, or rāvaṇa-vaharāvaṇa-vaharāvaṇavaha, attributed to Pravarasena and sometimes to Kālidāsa). [1246,2]
