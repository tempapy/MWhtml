• kuberakubera m.
    • or in later Sanskṛt kuverakuvera (originally) N. of a chief of the evil beings or spirits or darkness having the N. Vaiśravaṇa, AV. viii, 10, 28; ŚBr. &c. [291,3]
    • (afterwards) the god of riches and treasure (regent of the northern quarter which is hence called kubera-guptākuberaguptā diśdiś, Kum. iii, 25), Mn.; MBh. &c. [291,3]
    • (he is regarded as the son of Viśravas by Iḍaviḍā [BhP.], the chief of the Yakṣas, and a friend of Rudra [Hariv. 13131]; he is represented as having three legs and only eight teeth; with Jainas he is the attendant of the nineteenth Arhat of the present Avasarpiṇī) [291,3]
    • N. of a prince of Devarāṣṭra [291,3]
    • of the great-grandfather of Bāṇa-bhaṭṭa (author of the Kādambarī) [291,3]
    • of the author of the Datta-candrikā [291,3]
    • the tree Cedrela Toona [291,3]
  • kuberakubera mfn.
    • deformed, monstrous, L. [291,3]
    • slow, lazy, L. [291,3]
