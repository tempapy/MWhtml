• 1. kadkad [Roots]
    • cl. 1. Ā. kadate, cakāda (R. ed. Gorresio vi, 65, 23; but, R. ed. Bomb. vi, 86, 24 reads cakāracakāra),
    • to be confused, suffer mentally;
    • to grieve;
    • to confound;
    • to kill or hurt;
    • to call;
    • to cry or shed tears, Dhātup. xix, 10. [247,3]
  • 2. kadkad ind.
    • (originally the neuter form of the interrogative pronoun kaka), a particle of interrogation (= Lat. nonne, num), RV. [247,3]
    • anything wrong or bad, BhP. vii, 5, 28 (cf. below) [247,3]
    • = sukhasukha, Nigh. [247,3]
    • kadkad is used, like kimkim, with the particles canacana and cidcid, ‘sometimes, now and then’ [247,3]
    • kac-canakackatcana with the negation nana, ‘in no way or manner’ RV. [247,3]
    • kac-cidkaccidkackatcid is also used, like the simple kadkad, as a particle of interrogation (e.g. kaccidkaccid dṛṣṭādṛṣṭa tvayā rājanrājan damayantīdamayantī, was Damayantī seen by thee, O king?), MBh., or kaccidkaccid may be translated by ‘I hope that’ [247,3]
