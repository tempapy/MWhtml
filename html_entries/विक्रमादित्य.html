  • vikramādityavikramāditya
  • m. ‘valour-sun’, N. of a celebrated Hindū king (of Ujjayinīujjayinī and supposed founder of the [Mālavamālava-] Vikramavikrama era [cf. saṃvatsaṃvat], which begins 58 B.C. [but subtract 57-56 from an expired year of the Vikramavikrama era to convert it into A.D.]; he is said to have driven out the Śakaśakas and to have reigned over almost the whole of Northern India; he is represented as a great patron of literature; nine celebrated men are said to have flourished at his court [see nava-ratnanavaratnanavaratna], and innumerable legends are related of him all teeming with exaggerations; according to some he fell in a battle with his rival Śāli-vāhanaśālivāhanaśālivāhana, king of the south country or Deccan, and the legendary date given for his death is Kali-yugakaliyugakaliyuga 3044 [which really is the epoch-year of the Vikramavikrama era]; there are, however, other kings called Vikramâdityavikramāditya, and the name has been applied to king Bhojabhoja and even to Śāli-vāhanaśālivāhanaśālivāhana), Inscr.; Kathās.; Vet. &c. [955,3]
