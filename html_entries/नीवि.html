• nīnī—vivi
  • See nī-vinīvinīvi. [565,2]nīvinīvi or nīvīnīvī, f. (prob. fr. nini + √vyevye) a piece of cloth wrapped round the waist (esp. as worn by women; but cf. Vām. i, 3, 5), a kind of skirt or petticoat, VS.; AV.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [567,1]
  • a band or cord for tying together folded Kuśakuśa-grass in making offerings at the funeral rites of a Śūdraśūdra, L. [567,1]
  • a hostage, L. [567,1]
  • capital, principal stock, Daś. [567,1]
