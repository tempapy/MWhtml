    • the tusk of an elephant, R.; Kām. [1087,1]
    • the top or summit of a mountain, a peak, crag, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1087,1]
    • the summit of a building, pinnacle, turret, ib. [1087,2]
    • any peak or projection or lofty object, elevation, point, end, extremity, AV.; Kum.; Gīt. [1087,2]
    • a cusp or horn of the moon, R.; Hariv.; VarBṛS. [1087,2]
    • highest point, acme, height or perfection of anything, Hariv. 6424 [1087,2]
    • the horn as a symbol of self reliance or strength or haughtiness, Ragh. [1087,2]
    • the rising of desire, excess of love or passion (cf. śṛṅgāraśṛṅgāra), Sāh. [1087,2]
    • a partic. military array in the form of a horn or crescent, MBh. vi, 2413 [1087,2]

    • a mark, token, sign, L. [1087,2]
    • = śaśaśṛṅgaśaśaśṛṅga, ‘hare's horn’, anything impossible or extra ordinary, Kusum. [1087,2]
