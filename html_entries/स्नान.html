• snānasnāna n.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ) bathing, washing, ablution, religious or ceremonial lustration (as of an idol &c.), bathing in sacred waters (considered as one of the six daily duties [cf. ṣaṭ-karmanṣaṭkarmanṣaṭkarman] or as an essential part of some ceremonial, esp. the ablutions performed by a Brahma-cārin on becoming a householder cf. snātakasnātaka), GṛŚrS.; Mn.; MBh. &c. (IW. 242; RTL. 394 n. 1 &c.) [1266,3]
    • washing off, removal by washing, cleansing, BhP. [1266,3]
    • anything used in ablution (e.g. water, perfumed powder &c.), Kāv.; VarBṛS.; Vet.; BhP. [1266,3]
