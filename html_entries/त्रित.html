• tritatrita
  • m. ‘third’ (τρίτος), N. of a Vedic deity (associated with the Marutmaruts, Vāyuvāyu, and Indraindra; fighting like the latter with Tritatrita, Vṛtravṛtra, and other demons; called Āptyaāptya [q.v.], ‘water-deity’, and supposed to reside in the remotest regions of the world, whence [RV. viii, 47, 13-15; AV.] the idea of wishing to remove calamity to Tritatrita, and the view of the Tritatritas being the keepers of nectar [RV. vi, 44, 23], similarly, L. [RV. ii, 34, 10; TS. i; TBr. i] the notion of Tritatrita's bestowing long life; also conceived as an inferior deity conquering the demons by order and with the help of Indraindra [RV. ii; viii, 52, 1; x]; fallen into a well he begged aid from the gods [i, 105, 17; x, 8, 7]; as to this last myth, Sāy. on i, 105 relates that 3 Ṛṣiṛṣis, Ekataekata, Dvitadvita, and Tritatrita, parched with thirst, looked about and found a well, and when Tritatrita began to draw water, the other two, desirous of his property, pushed him down and closed up the well with a wheel; shut up there, Tritatrita composed a hymn to the gods, and managed miraculously to prepare the sacrificial Somasoma, that he might drink it himself, or offer it to the deities and so be extricated: this is alluded to in RV. ix, 34, 4 [cf. 32, 2; 38, 2; 102, 2] and described in MBh. ix, 2095; also, Nir. iv, 6 makes him a Ṛṣiṛṣi, and he is the supposed author of RV. i, 105; viii, 36; ix, 33 f. and 102; x, 1-7; in epic legends [MBh. ix, xii f.] Ekataekata, Dvitadvita, and Tritatrita are described as 3 brothers, sons of Gautamagautama or of Prajā-patiprajāpatiprajāpati or Brahmābrahmā; elsewhere Tritatrita is one of the 12 sons of Manumanu Cākṣuṣacākṣuṣa by Naḍvalānaḍvalā, BhP. iv, 13, 16; cf. traitanatraitana; Zd. Thrita; Τρίτων, τριτο-γενής, &c.) [461,3]
  • tritatrita
  • n. triplet of young (three-twin), TS. Sch. [461,3]
