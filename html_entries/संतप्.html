• saṃ-√tapsaṃtapsaṃtap
  • P. -tapatitapatisaṃ-tapati, to heat thoroughly, scorch, parch, dry up, R.; Suśr.; VarBṛS. &c.;
    to feel pain or remorse, Mn.; MBh.;
    to pain by heat, torture, oppress, afflict, harass, RV. &c. &c.:
    Pass. -tapyatetapyatesaṃ-tapyate (ep. also °tisaṃti), to be oppressed or afflicted, suffer pain, undergo penance (3. sg. impers. with gen. of pers.), ŚBr. &c. &c.:
    Caus. -tāpayatitāpayatisaṃ-tāpayati (Pass. -tāpyatetāpyatesaṃ-tāpyate), to cause to be heated, make very hot, burn, inflame, scorch, Kauś.; MBh. &c.;
    to pair by heat, torture, torment (ātmānamātmānam, ‘one's self. i.e. to afflict the body by austerities’), afflict, trouble, distress, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1142,1]
