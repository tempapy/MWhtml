• veṅkaṭaveṅkaṭa
  • m. (Prākṛt for vyaṅkaṭavyaṅkaṭa) N. of a very sacred hill in the Drāviḍadrāviḍa country (in the district of North Arcot, about 80 miles from Madras; it reaches an elevation of about 2,500 feet above the sea-level, and on the summit is the celebrated temple dedicated to Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa or Viṣṇuviṣṇu in his character of ‘Lord of Veṅkaṭaveṅkaṭa’, also called Śrī-patiśrīpatiśrīpati or Tirupati, whence the hill is sometimes popularly known as Tri-patītripatītripatī; it is annually thronged with thousands of pilgrims, RTL. 267), BhP. [1014,1]
  • of a king of Vijaya-nagaravijayanagaravijayanagara (the patron. of Appayaappaya Dīkṣitadīkṣita), Cat. [1014,1]
  • (also with adhvarinadhvarin, ācāryaācārya, kavikavi, bhaṭṭabhaṭṭa, yajvanyajvan, yoginyogin &c.) N. of various authors and teachers, ib. [1014,1]
