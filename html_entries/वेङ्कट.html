• veṅkaṭa m. (Prākṛt for vyaṅkaṭa) N. of a very sacred hill in the Drāviḍa country (in the district of North Arcot, about 80 miles from Madras; it reaches an elevation of about 2,500 feet above the sea-level, and on the summit is the celebrated temple dedicated to Kṛṣṇa or Viṣṇu in his character of ‘Lord of Veṅkaṭa’, also called Śrī-patiśrī-patiśrīpati or Tirupati, whence the hill is sometimes popularly known as Tri-patītri-patītripatī; it is annually thronged with thousands of pilgrims, RTL. 267), BhP. [1014,1]
  • of a king of Vijaya-nagaravijaya-nagaravijayanagara (the patron. of Appaya Dīkṣita), Cat. [1014,1]
  • (also with adhvarin, ācārya, kavi, bhaṭṭa, yajvan, yogin &c.) N. of various authors and teachers, ib. [1014,1]
