• manasmanasmana n.
    • mind (in its widest sense as applied to all the mental powers), intellect, intelligence, understanding, perception, sense, conscience, will, RV. &c. &c. (in phil. the internal organ or antaḥ-karaṇaantaḥkaraṇaantaḥantasantarkaraṇa of perception and cognition, the faculty or instrument through which thoughts enter or by which objects of sense affect the soul, IW. 53; in this sense manasmanasmana is always regarded as distinct from ātmanātman and puruṣapuruṣa, ‘spirit or soul’ and belonging only to the body, like which it is - except in the Nyāya - considered perishable; as to its position in the various systems See for Nyāya and Vaiśeṣika, IW. 63; 67; 76, for Sāṃkhya and Vedânta, ib. 84; 109; 117; in RV. it is sometimes joined with hṛdhṛd or hṛdayahṛdaya, the heart, Mn. vii, 6 with cakṣuscakṣuscakṣu, the eye) [783,3]
    • the spirit or spiritual principle, the breath or living soul which escapes from the body at death (called asuasuasū in animals; cf. above), ib. [783,3]
    • thought, imagination, excogitation, invention, reflection, opinion, intention, inclination, affection, desire, mood, temper, spirit, ib.
