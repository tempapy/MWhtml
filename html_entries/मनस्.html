• manasmanas
  • n. mind (in its widest sense as applied to all the mental powers), intellect, intelligence, understanding, perception, sense, conscience, will, RV. &c. &c. (in phil. the internal organ or antaḥ-karaṇaantaḥkaraṇaantaḥkaraṇa of perception and cognition, the faculty or instrument through which thoughts enter or by which objects of sense affect the soul, IW. 53; in this sense manasmanas is always regarded as distinct from ātmanātman and puruṣapuruṣa, ‘spirit or soul’ and belonging only to the body, like which it is - except in the Nyāyanyāya - considered perishable; as to its position in the various systems See for Nyāyanyāya and Vaiśeṣikavaiśeṣika, IW. 63; 67; 76, for Sāṃkhyasāṃkhya and Vedântavedānta, ib. 84; 109; 117; in RV. it is sometimes joined with hṛdhṛd or hṛdayahṛdaya, the heart, Mn. vii, 6 with cakṣuscakṣus, the eye) [783,3]
  • the spirit or spiritual principle, the breath or living soul which escapes from the body at death (called asuasu in animals; cf. above), ib. [783,3]
