• ava-tāraavatāraavatāra
  • m. (Pāṇ. iii, 3, 120) descent (especially of a deity from heaven), appearance of any deity upon earth (but more particularly the incarnations of Viṣṇuviṣṇu in ten principal forms, viz. the fish tortoise, boar, man lion, dwarf, the two Rāmarāmas, Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa, Buddhabuddha, and Kalkikalki, MBh. xii, 12941 seqq.) [99,1]
  • any new and unexpected appearance, Ragh. iii, 36 & v, 24, &c., (any distinguished person in the language of respect is called an Avatāraavatāra or incarnation of a deity) [99,1]
  • opportunity of catching any one, Buddh. [99,1]
  • a Tīrthatīrtha or sacred place, L. [99,1]
  • translation, L. [99,1]
  • (acc. with √labhlabh, ‘to get an opportunity’), Divyâv. [1317,1]
