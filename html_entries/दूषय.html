• dūṣayadūṣaya
  • Nom. P. °yatidūṣayati (ep. also °tedūṣayate) to corrupt, spoil, contaminate, vitiate (of moral corruption also doṣayatidoṣayati See 2. duṣduṣ), AV.; Mn.; MBh.; Kāv. &c.;
    to dishonour or violate (a woman), Mn. viii, 364; MBh. &c.;
    (astrol.) to cause evil or misfortune, Var.;
    to adulterate, falsify, MBh. xiii, 1683;
    to object, refute, disprove, blame, ib.; Kathās. &c.;
    to retract or break (vācamvācam, one's word), MBh. xii, 7256;
    to find fault with, accuse, MBh.; R.;
    (paras-paramparasparamparasparam, each other), Pañc. 450/451;
    to offend, hurt, injure (gen.), R. ii, 74, 3; MBh. iv, 2228 &c. [489,1]
