• ā-√prīāprīāprī
  • P. (-prīṇātiprīṇātiā-prīṇāti, AitBr. ii, 4; aor. Subj. 2. sg. -piprayaspiprayasā-piprayas, RV. ii, 6, 8)
    to satisfy, conciliate, propitiate, please, RV.; TS.; ŚBr.;
    to address or invoke with the Āprīāprī (See below) verses, AitBr.; ŚBr. :
    Ā. (impf. ā̑prīṇītaāprīṇīta) to amuse one's self, be delighted or pleased, TS.; Lāṭy. [144,2]
  • ā-prīāprīāprī
  • f. gaining one's favour, conciliation, propitiation [144,2]
  • f. pl. (-priyaspriyasā-priyas [AV. xi, 7, 19] and -pryaspryasā-pryas [Nārāyaṇanārāyaṇa]) N. of particular invocations spoken previous to the offering of oblations (according to, ĀśvŚr. iii, 2, 5 seqq. they are different in different schools; e.g. samiddhosamiddho agniragnir, RV. v, 28, 1; in the school of Śunakaśunaka; juṣasvajuṣasva naḥnaḥ, RV. vii, 2, 1; in that of Vasiṣṭhavasiṣṭha; samiddhosamiddho adyaadya, RV. x, 110, 1; in that of others; Nārāyaṇanārāyaṇa on this passage gives ten hymns belonging to different schools; See also, Sāy. on RV. i, 13 [susamiddhosusamiddho nana āā vahavaha, the Āprīāprī-hymn of the school of Kaṇvakaṇva], who enumerates twelve Āprīāprīs and explains that twelve deities are propitiated; those deities are personified objects belonging to the fire-sacrifice, viz. the fuel, the sacred grass, the enclosure, &c., all regarded as different forms of Agniagni; hence the objects are also called Āprīāprīs, or, according to others, the objects are the real Āprīāprīs, whence the hymns received their names), AV.; TS.; ĀśvŚr. &c.
