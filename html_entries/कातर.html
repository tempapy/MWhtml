• kātara mf(ā)n. (etym. doubtful, perhaps from katara, ‘uncertain as to which of the two’ BRD.), cowardly, faint-hearted, timid, despairing, discouraged, disheartened, confused, agitated, perplexed, embarrassed, shrinking, frightened, afraid of (loc. or inf. or in comp.), R.; Mṛcch.; Ragh.; Megh.; Śak.; Pañcat.; Hit. [270,1]
  • kātara m. a kind of large fish (Cyprinus Catla, kātala), L. [270,1]
  • N. of a man (See kātarāyaṇa) [270,1]
  • kātara n. (in sa-kātarasa-kātarasakātara) ‘timidity, despair, agitation.’ [270,1]
