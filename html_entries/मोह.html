• mohamoha m.
    • (√1. muhmuh; ifc. f(āāa). ) loss of consciousness, bewilderment, perplexity, distraction, infatuation, delusion, error, folly, AV. &c. &c. (moham-√brūbrū, to say anything that leads to error; mohaṃ-√yāyāyaiī, to fall into error; mohātmohātmoha ind. through folly or ignorance) [836,1]
    • fainting, stupefaction, a swoon, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [836,1]
    • (in phil.) darkness or delusion of mind (preventing the discernment of truth and leading men to believe in the reality of worldly objects) [836,1]
    • (with Buddhists) ignorance (one of the three roots of vice, Dharmas. 139) [836,1]
    • a magical art employed to bewilder an enemy (= mohanamohana), Cat. [836,1]
    • wonder, amazement, L. [836,1]
    • Infatuation personified (as the offspring of Brahmā), VP. [836,1]
