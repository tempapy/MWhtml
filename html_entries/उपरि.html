• upariupari a ind.
    • (as a separable adverb) above, upon, on, upwards, towards the upper side of (opposed to adhasadhasadha and, nīcānīcānīca e.g. upari-√yāupariyāyaiī, to go upwards; sometimes written with a following word as if compounded with it See below) [205,1]
    • besides, in addition to, further (sahasraṃsahasramsahasra śatāny upariupari cā̑ṣṭaucaaṣṭaaṣṭi, 1000 and 800 in addition) [205,1]
    • afterwards (e.g. upariupari payaḥpayaḥpayas pibet, he should drink milk afterwards) [205,1]
    • uparyupari upariupari, higher and higher [205,1]
    • repeatedly, continuously, RV. &c. (As a separable preposition, with acc. loc., or gen.) over, above, upon, on, at the head of, on the upper side of, beyond (e.g. upariupari śailaṃ-√gamgamga, to go over the mountain; upariupari laṅkāyāṃlaṅkālaṅka samprāptaḥsamprāpta saḥsassa, he arrived over Laṅkā; uparyupari upariupari sarveṣāmsarva atiṣṭhatatiṣṭhat, he stood at the very head of all; ātmānaṃ tasyata upariupari kṣiptvā, having thrown himself upon him) [205,1]
    • in connection with, with reference to, with regard to, towards (with gen. e.g. mamȏparimamaupari vikāritaḥvikārita, changed in feeling with regard to me; putrasyȏpariupari kruddhaḥkruddha, enraged towards his son) [205,1]
    • after (with abl. e.g. muhūrtādmuhūrta upariupari, after a minute; See also tad-uparitadtatupari &c.), RV. &c. [205,1]
