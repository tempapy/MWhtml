• sūryasūrya
  • m. the sun or its deity (in the Vedaveda the name Sūryasūrya is generally distinguished from Savitṛsavitṛ [q.v.], and denotes the most concrete of the solar gods, whose connection with the luminary is always present to the poet's mind ; in Nir. vii, 5 he is regarded as one of the original Vedic triad, his place being in the sky, while that of Agniagni is on the earth, and that of Indraindra is in the atmosphere; ten hymns in the RV. are entirely in praise of Sūryasūrya e.g. i, 50, i, 115 &c., also, AV. xiii, 2; he moves through the sky in a chariot drawn by seven ruddy horses or mares [see saptāśvasaptāśva, haritharit, harid-aśvaharidaśvaharidaśva]; in the later mythology Sūryasūrya is identified with Savitṛsavitṛ as one of the 12 Ādityaādityas or emblems of the Sun in the 12 months of the year, and his seven-horsed chariot is said to be driven by Aruṇaaruṇa or the Dawn as its charioteer, who is represented without legs; the Sun, whether named Sūryasūrya or Vivasvatvivasvat, has several wives See sūryāsūryā below), RV. &c. &c. (cf. IW. 11; 16 &c.; RTL. 341) [1243,1]
  • a symbolical expression for the number ‘twelve’ (in allusion to the sun in the 12 signs of the zodiac), Jyot.; Hcat. [1243,2]
  • the swallow-wort (either Calotropis or Asclepias Gigantea, = arkaarka), L. [1243,2]
  • N. of the son of Balibali, L. [1243,2]
  • of a Dānavadānava, VahniP. [1243,2]
  • of an astronomer (= sūrya-dāsasūryadāsasūryadāsa), Cat. [1243,2]
  • epithet of Śivaśiva, MBh. [1243,2]
