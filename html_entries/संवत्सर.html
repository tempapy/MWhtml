• saṃ-vatsarasaṃvatsarasaṃsamvatsara m.
    • (rarely n.; cf. pari-v°pari) a full year, a year (having 12 [TS.] or 13 [VS.] months or 360 days [ŚBr.; AitBr.; Suśr.]; amama, ‘for a year’; eṇaeṇa ‘after or in course of a year’; eeaāi or asyaa, ‘after or within a year’), RV. &c. &c. [1114,1]
    • a year of the Vikrama era (See above; varṣavarṣa is used for the śakaśaka) the first in a cycle of five or six years, TS.; PārGṛ.; VarBṛS.; BhP. [1114,1]
    • the Year personified (having the new and full moon for eyes and presiding over the seasons), TS.; Pur. [1114,1]
    • N. of Śiva, MBh. [1114,1]
