• saṃ-vatsarasaṃvatsarasaṃvatsara
  • m. (rarely n.; cf. pari-v°parivatsaraparivatsara) a full year, a year (having 12 [TS.] or 13 [VS.] months or 360 days [ŚBr.; AitBr.; Suśr.]; amam, ‘for a year’; eṇaeṇa ‘after or in course of a year’; ee or asyaasya, ‘after or within a year’), RV. &c. &c. [1114,1]
  • a year of the Vikramavikrama era (See above; varṣavarṣa is used for the śakaśaka) the first in a cycle of five or six years, TS.; PārGṛ.; VarBṛS.; BhP. [1114,1]
  • the Year personified (having the new and full moon for eyes and presiding over the seasons), TS.; Pur. [1114,1]
  • N. of Śivaśiva, MBh. [1114,1]
