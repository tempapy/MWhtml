• saṃ-vatsarasaṃ-vatsarasaṃvatsara m. (rarely n.; cf. pari-v°pari-vatsaraparivatsara) a full year, a year (having 12 [TS.] or 13 [VS.] months or 360 days [ŚBr.; AitBr.; Suśr.]; am, ‘for a year’; eṇa ‘after or in course of a year’; e or asya, ‘after or within a year’), RV. &c. &c. [1114,1]
  • a year of the Vikrama era (See above; varṣa is used for the śaka) the first in a cycle of five or six years, TS.; PārGṛ.; VarBṛS.; BhP. [1114,1]
  • the Year personified (having the new and full moon for eyes and presiding over the seasons), TS.; Pur. [1114,1]
  • N. of Śiva, MBh. [1114,1]
