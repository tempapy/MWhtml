• vidura mfn. knowing, wise, intelligent, skilled in (comp.), Uttamac. [963,3]
  • vidura m. a learned or clever man, W. [963,3]
  • an intriguer, ib. [963,3]
  • N. of the younger brother of Dhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra and Pāṇḍu (they were all three sons of Vyāsa, but only the latter two by the two widows of Vicitra-vīryavicitra-vīryavicitravīrya; when Vyāsa wanted a third son, the elder widow sent him one of her slave-girls, dressed in her own clothes, and this girl became the mother of Vidura, who is sometimes called Kṣattṛ, as if he were the son of a Kṣatriya man and Śūdra woman Vidura is described as sarva-buddhimatāṃsarva-buddhimatāṃsarvabuddhimatāṃ varaḥ and is one of the wisest characters in the Mahā-bhāratamahā-bhāratamahābhārata, always ready with good advice both for his nephews, the Pāṇḍavas, and for his brother Dhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛta-rāṣṭradhṛtarāṣṭra), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. (cf. IW. 376; 385 &c.) [963,3]
