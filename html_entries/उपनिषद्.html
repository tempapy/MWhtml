• upa-ni-ṣadupaniṣadupaniṣad
  • (upa-ni-√sadupanisadupanisad) P. (pf. -ni-ṣedusniṣedusupaniniṣedus) to sit down near to;
    to approach, set about, AV. xix, 41, 1; ŚBr.; Kauś. [201,1]
  • upa-niṣadupaniṣadupaniṣad
  • f. (according to some) the sitting down at the feet of another to listen to his words (and hence, secret knowledge given in this manner; but according to native authorities upaniṣadupaniṣad means ‘setting at rest ignorance by revealing the knowledge of the supreme spirit’) [201,1]
  • the mystery which underlies or rests underneath the external system of things (cf. IW. p.35seqq.) [201,1]
  • esoteric doctrine, secret doctrine, mysterious or mystical meaning, words of mystery &c., ŚBr.; ChUp. &c. [201,1]
  • a class of philosophical writings (more than a hundred in number, attached to the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas [but See Īśopaniṣadīśopaniṣad]; their aim is the exposition of the secret meaning of the Vedaveda, and they are regarded as the source of the Vedāntavedānta and Sāṃkhyasāṃkhya philosophies; for the most important of the Upaniṣadupaniṣads See IW. p.37seq.) [201,1]
