• sārvasārva—dhātukadhātuka mfn.
    • (fr. sarva-dhātusarvadhātu) applicable to the whole of a radical term or to the complete form of the verbal base (after the conjugational characteristics or Vikaraṇas are affixed in the four conj. or special tenses; in Veda often confounded with the Ārdhadhātukas q.v.) [1210,1]
  • sārvasārva—dhātukadhātuka n.
    • N. of the verbal terminations of the four conj. tenses (Pr., Impf. Pot., and Impv.), and of all the root affixes (such as śānacśānac and śatriśatri) which have an indicatory ṣ, Pāṇ. i, 2, 4; iii, 4, 113 &c. [1210,1]
