  • 5. sususū ind.
    • (opp. to dusdus and corresponding in sense to Gk. εὖ; perhaps connected with 1. vasuvasu, and, accord. to some, with pron. base sasa, as kukukū with kaka; in Veda also sūsūsu and liable to become ṣuṣuṣū or ṣūṣūṣu and to lengthen a preceding vowel, while a following nana may become ṇaṇa; it may be used as an adj. or adv.), good, excellent, right, virtuous, beautiful, easy, well, rightly, much, greatly, very, any, easily, willingly, quickly (in older language often with other particles; esp. with uuū, = ‘forthwith, immediately’; with mȏmomumamāuū i.e. māmāmamās uuū, = ‘never, by no means’ ; sususū kamkamka often emphatically with an Impv. e.g. tiṣṭhā sususū kamkamkakap maghavanmaghavanmaghavat māmāma parāparāparaparāsparas gāḥgagā, ‘do tarry O Maghavan, go not past’ RV. iii, 53, 2; sususū always qualifies the meaning of a verb and is never used independently at the beginning of a verse; in later language it is rarely a separate word, but is mostly prefixed to substantives, adjectives, adverbs and participles, exceptionally also to an ind.p. e.g. su-baddhvāsu, ‘having well bound’ Mṛcch. x, 50; or even to a finite verb e.g. su-nirvavausu, Śiś. vi, 58), RV. &c. [1219,3]
