  • susu
  • ind. (opp. to dusdus and corresponding in sense to Gk. εὖ; perhaps connected with 1. vasuvasu, and, accord. to some, with pron. base sasa, as kuku with kaka; in Vedaveda also sūsū and liable to become ṣuṣu or ṣūṣū and to lengthen a preceding vowel, while a following nana may become ṇaṇa; it may be used as an adj. or adv.), good, excellent, right, virtuous, beautiful, easy, well, rightly, much, greatly, very, any, easily, willingly, quickly (in older language often with other particles; esp. with uu, = ‘forthwith, immediately’; with mȏmosrssrs i.e. māmā uu, = ‘never, by no means’ ; susu kamkam often emphatically with an Impv. e.g. tiṣṭhātiṣṭhā susu kamkam maghavanmaghavan māmā parāparā gāḥgāḥ, ‘do tarry O Maghavanmaghavan, go not past’ RV. iii, 53, 2; susu always qualifies the meaning of a verb and is never used independently at the beginning of a verse; in later language it is rarely a separate word, but is mostly prefixed to substantives, adjectives, adverbs and participles, exceptionally also to an ind.p. e.g. su-baddhvāsubaddhvāsubaddhvā, ‘having well bound’ Mṛcch. x, 50; or even to a finite verb e.g. su-nirvavausunirvavausunirvavau, Śiś. vi, 58), RV. &c.; [1219,3]
