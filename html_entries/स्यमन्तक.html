• syamantakasyamantaka m.
    • N. of a celebrated jewel (worn by Kṛṣṇa on his wrist [cf. kaustubhakaustubha], described as yielding daily eight loads of gold and preserving from all dangers; it is said to have been given to Satrā-jit [q.v.] by the Sun and transferred by him to his brother Prasena, from whom it was taken by Jāmbavat, and after much contention appropriated by Kṛṣṇa See ViṣṇuP. iv, 13), Hariv.; Pur.; Pañcar. [1273,2]
