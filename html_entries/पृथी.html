• pṛthīpṛthīpṛthipṛth m.
    • (nom. °thīpṛthīpṛthipṛth dat. °thyaipṛthipṛthī or °thayepṛthi gen. °thyāspṛthipṛthī) N. of a mythical personage with the patr. Vainya (said to have been the first anointed sovereign of men, to have ruled also the lower animals, and to have introduced the arts of husbandry into the world; he is enumerated among the Ṛṣis and said to be the author of RV. x, 148), RV.; AV.; Br. (cf. pṛthipṛthipṛthīpṛth, pṛthupṛthu; pārthapārtha). [646,2]
