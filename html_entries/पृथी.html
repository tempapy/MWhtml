• pṛthīpṛthī
  • m. (nom. °thīpṛthī dat. °thyaipṛthyai or °thayepṛthaye gen. °thyāspṛthyās) N. of a mythical personage with the patr. Vainyavainya (said to have been the first anointed sovereign of men, to have ruled also the lower animals, and to have introduced the arts of husbandry into the world; he is enumerated among the Ṛṣiṛṣis and said to be the author of RV. x, 148), RV.; AV.; Br. (cf. pṛthipṛthi, pṛthupṛthu; pārthapārtha). [646,2]
