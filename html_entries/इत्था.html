• itTA/
  • ind. Ved. thus [165,2]
  • (often used in the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda, and sometimes only to lay stress on a following word; therefore by native etymologists [Nir.] considered as a particle of affirmation.) itTA is often connected with words expressing devotion to the gods &c. in the sense of thus, truly, really [165,2]
  • accord. to some also ‘here, hither’, ‘there, thither’, = Prākṛt etTa. [1320,3]
  • especially with DI as an adjective. Hence itTA-DI = such, i.e. true (satyA) or real worship. Similarly, itTA-DI/ mfn. so devout, so pious i.e. very devout [165,2]
  • performing such or true works ([Sāy.]), RV.; AV.; KaṭhUp. [165,2]
