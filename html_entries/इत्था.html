• itthāitthāittha ind.
    • Ved. thus [165,2]
    • (often used in the Ṛg-veda, and sometimes only to lay stress on a following word; therefore by native etymologists [Nir.] considered as a particle of affirmation.) itthāitthāittha is often connected with words expressing devotion to the gods &c. in the sense of thus, truly, really [165,2]
    • accord. to some also ‘here, hither’, ‘there, thither’, = Prākṛt ettha. [1320,3]
    • especially with dhīdhīdhi as an adjective. Hence itthā-dhīitthādhīdhi = such, i.e. true (satyāsatyāsatyasatisatīsat) or real worship. Similarly, itthā-dhīitthādhīdhi mfn. so devout, so pious i.e. very devout [165,2]
    • performing such or true works [Sāy.], RV.; AV.; KaṭhUp. [165,2]
