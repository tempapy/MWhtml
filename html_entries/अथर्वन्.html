• atharvanatharvan
  • m. (said to be fr. an obsolete word atharathar, fire), a priest who has to do with fire and Somasoma [17,3]
  • N. of the priest who is said to have been the first to institute the worship of fire and offer Somasoma and prayers (he is represented as a Prajāpatiprajāpati, as Brahmābrahmā's eldest son, as the first learner and earliest teacher of the Brahma-vidyābrahmavidyābrahmavidyā, as the author of the Atharva-vedaatharvavedaatharvaveda, as identical with Aṅgirasaṅgiras, as the father of Agniagni, &c.) [17,3]
  • N. of Śivaśiva, Vasiṣṭhavasiṣṭha ([Kir. x, 10]), Somasoma, Prāṇaprāṇa [17,3]
  • atharvanatharvan
  • mn. (āā, aa) the fourth or Atharvaatharva-vedaveda (said to have been composed by Atharvanatharvan, and consisting chiefly of formulas and spells intended to counteract diseases and calamities) [17,3]
  • atharvanatharvan
  • m. pl. (atharvāṇasatharvāṇas). descendants of Atharvanatharvan, often coupled with those of Aṅgirasaṅgiras and Bhṛgubhṛgu [17,3]
  • pl. the hymns of the Atharvaatharva-vedaveda. [17,3]
