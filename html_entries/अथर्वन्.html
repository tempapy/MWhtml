• atharvanatharvan m.
    • (said to be fr. an obsolete word athar, fire), a priest who has to do with fire and Soma [17,3]
    • N. of the priest who is said to have been the first to institute the worship of fire and offer Soma and prayers (he is represented as a Prajāpati, as Brahmā's eldest son, as the first learner and earliest teacher of the Brahma-vidyā, as the author of the Atharva-veda, as identical with Aṅgiras, as the father of Agni, &c.) [17,3]
    • N. of Śiva, Vasiṣṭha [Kir. x, 10], Soma, Prāṇa [17,3]
  • atharvanatharvan mn.
    • (āāa, aa) the fourth or Atharva-veda (said to have been composed by Atharvan, and consisting chiefly of formulas and spells intended to counteract diseases and calamities) [17,3]
  • atharvanatharvan m.
    • pl. (atharvāṇasatharvāṇa). descendants of Atharvan, often coupled with those of Aṅgiras and Bhṛgu [17,3]
    • pl. the hymns of the Atharva-veda. [17,3]
