  • (orig. identical with √2. vāvā) cl. 1. P. (Dhātup. xxii, 24) vāyativāyati, to become languid or weary or exhausted, RV. (śoṣaṇeśoṣaṇe, Dhātup.);
    to be deprived of (gen.), RV. viii.47, 6;

  • vaivai
  • ind. a particle of emphasis and affirmation, generally placed after a word and laying stress on it (it is usually translatable by ‘indeed’, ‘truly’, ‘certainly’, ‘verily’, ‘just’ &c.; it is very rare in the RV.; more frequent in the AV., and very common in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas and in works that imitate their style; in the Sūtrasūtras it is less frequent and almost restricted to the combination yadyyady uu vaivai; in Manumanu, MBh. and the Kāvyakāvyas it mostly appears at the end of a line, and as a mere expletive. In RV. it is frequently followed by uu in the combination vāvā uu [both particles are separated, v, 18, 3] ; it is also preceded by uu and various other particles e.g. by idid, ahaaha, utauta; in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas it often follows haha, haha smasma, evaeva; in later language apiapi and tutu. According to some it is also a vocative particle). [1019,3]
