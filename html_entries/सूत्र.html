• sūtrasūtra
  • n. (accord. to g. ardhacā̑diardhacādi also m.; fr. √sivsiv, ‘to sew’, and connected with sūcisūci and sūnāsūnā) a thread, yarn, string, line, cord, wire, AV. &c. &c. [1241,3]
  • a measuring line (cf. -pātapātasūtra-pāta), Hariv.; VarBṛS. &c. [1241,3]
  • the sacred thread or cord worn by the first three classes (cf. yajñȏpavītayajñopavīta), BhP. [1241,3]
  • a girdle, ib. [1241,3]
  • a fibre, Kālid. [1241,3]
  • a line, stroke, MBh.; VarBṛS.; Gol. [1241,3]
  • a sketch, plan, Rājat. [1241,3]
  • that which like a thread runs through or holds together everything, rule, direction, BhP. [1241,3]
  • a short sentence or aphoristic rule, and any work or manual consisting of strings of such rules hanging together like threads (these Sūtrasūtra works form manuals of teaching in ritual, philosophy, grammar &c.: e.g. in ritual there are first the Śrauta-sūtraśrautasūtraśrautasūtras, and among them the Kalpa-sūtrakalpasūtrakalpasūtras, founded directly on Śrutiśruti q.v.; they form a kind of rubric to Vedic ceremonial, giving concise rules for the performance of every kind of sacrifice [IW. 146 &c.]; other kinds of Śrutiśruti works are the Gṛhya-sūtragṛhyasūtragṛhyasūtras and Sāmayācārikasāmayācārika or Dharma-sūtradharmasūtradharmasūtras i.e. ‘rules for domestic ceremonies and conventional customs’, sometimes called collectively Smārta-sūtrasmārtasūtrasmārtasūtras [as founded on smṛtismṛti or ‘tradition’ See smārtasmārta]; these led to the later Dharmaśāstradharmaśāstras or ‘law-books’ [IW. 145]; in philosophy each system has its regular text-book of aphorisms written in Sūtrasūtras by its supposed founder [IW. 60 &c.]; in Vyākaraṇavyākaraṇa or grammar there are the celebrated Sūtrasūtras of Pāṇinipāṇini in eight books, which are the groundwork of a vast grammatical literature; with Buddhists, Pāśupatapāśupatas &c. the term Sūtrasūtra is applied to original text books as opp. to explanatory works; with Jainajainas they form part of the Dṛṣṭivādadṛṣṭivāda), IW. 162 &c. [1241,3]
  • a kind of tree, Divyâv. [1241,3]
