• idid
  • ind. Ved. (probably the neut. form of the pronom. base ii See 3. ii; a particle of affirmation) even, just, only [165,2]
  • indeed, assuredly (especially, in strengthening an antithesis, e.g. yathāyathā vaśantivaśanti devāsdevās tathȇdtathesrssrsd asatasat, as the gods wish it, thus indeed it will be, RV. viii, 28, 4; dipsantadipsanta idid ripavoripavo nā̑hanāsrssrsha debhuḥdebhuḥ, the enemies wishing indeed to hurt were in nowise able to hurt, RV. i, 147, 3). idid is often added to words expressing excess or exclusion (e.g. viśvaviśva itit, every one indeed; śaśvadśaśvad itit, constantly indeed; ekaeka itit, one only). At the beginning of sentences it often adds emphasis to pronouns, prepositions, particles (e.g. tvamtvam itit, thou indeed; yadiyadi itit, if indeed, &c.) idid occurs often in the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda and Atharvaatharva-vedaveda, seldom in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas, and its place is taken in classical Sanskṛt by evaeva and other particles.
