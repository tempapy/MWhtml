• idid ind.
    • Ved. (probably the neut. form of the pronom. base iiī See 3. iiī; a particle of affirmation) even, just, only [165,2]
    • indeed, assuredly (especially, in strengthening an antithesis, e.g. yathāyathā vaśanti devāsdevadevā tathȇdtathāidit asatasatasad, as the gods wish it, thus indeed it will be, RV. viii, 28, 4; dipsanta ididit ripavoripu nā̑hanāhananāahaāha debhuḥ, the enemies wishing indeed to hurt were in nowise able to hurt, RV. i, 147, 3). idid is often added to words expressing excess or exclusion (e.g. viśvaviśvaviśvāviśvas ititididh, every one indeed; śaśvadśaśvat ititididh, constantly indeed; ekaekaekā ititididh, one only). At the beginning of sentences it often adds emphasis to pronouns, prepositions, particles (e.g. tvamtva ititididh, thou indeed; yadiyadiyad ititididh, if indeed, &c.) idid occurs often in the Ṛg-veda and Atharva-veda, seldom in the Brāhmaṇas, and its place is taken in classical Sanskṛt by evaeva and other particles.
