• id ind. Ved. (probably the neut. form of the pronom. base i See 3. i; a particle of affirmation) even, just, only [165,2]
  • indeed, assuredly (especially, in strengthening an antithesis, e.g. yathā vaśanti devās tathȇd asat, as the gods wish it, thus indeed it will be, RV. viii, 28, 4; dipsanta id ripavo nā̑ha debhuḥ, the enemies wishing indeed to hurt were in nowise able to hurt, RV. i, 147, 3). id is often added to words expressing excess or exclusion (e.g. viśva it, every one indeed; śaśvad it, constantly indeed; eka it, one only). At the beginning of sentences it often adds emphasis to pronouns, prepositions, particles (e.g. tvam it, thou indeed; yadi it, if indeed, &c.) id occurs often in the Ṛg-veda and Atharva-veda, seldom in the Brāhmaṇas, and its place is taken in classical Sanskṛt by eva and other particles. [165,2]
