• vindhyavindhya
  • m. (of doubtful derivation) N. of a low range of hills connecting the Northern extremities of the Western and Eastern Ghauts, and separating Hindūstān proper from the Dekhan (the Vindhyavindhya range is reckoned among the seven principal ranges of Bhārata-varṣabhāratavarṣabhāratavarṣa [see kulagirikulagiri, p. 294, col. 3], and according to, Manu ii, 21, forms the Southern limit of Madhya-deśamadhyadeśamadhyadeśa or the middle region; according to a legend related in MBh. iii, 8782 &c., the personified Vindhyavindhya, jealous of Himâlayahimālaya, demanded that the sun should revolve round him in the same way as about Merumeru, which the sun declining to do, the Vindhyavindhya then began to elevate himself that he might bar the progress of both sun and moon; the gods alarmed, asked the aid of the saint Agastyaagastya, who approached the Vindhyavindhya and requested that by bending down he would afford him an easy passage to the South country, begging at the same time that he would retain a low position till his return; this he promised to do, but Agastyaagastya never returned, and the Vindhyavindhya range consequently never attained the elevation of the Himâlayahimālaya), Mn.; MBh. &c. [972,2]
