• śūrpaśūrpa—ṇakhāṇakhā
  • (rarely -ṇakhīṇakhīśūrpa—ṇakhī) f. (wrongly -nakhānakhāśūrpa—nakhā, °khīśūrpaṇakhī; cf. Pāṇ. iv, 1, 58) ‘having fingernails like winnowing fans’ N. of the sister of Rāvaṇarāvaṇa (she fell in love with Rāma-candrarāmacandrarāmacandra and, being rejected by him and insulted by Sītāsītā's laughter, assumed a hideous form and threatened to eat her up, but was beaten off by Lakṣmaṇalakṣmaṇa, who cut off her ears and nose and thus doubly disfigured her; in revenge she incited her brother to carry off Sītāsītā), MBh.; R. [1086,2]
