• śūrpaśūrpa—ṇakhā
    • (rarely -ṇakhīśūrpaṇakhā) f. (wrongly -nakhāśūrpaṇakhānakha, °khī; cf. Pāṇ. iv, 1, 58) ‘having fingernails like winnowing fans’ N. of the sister of Rāvaṇa (she fell in love with Rāma-candra and, being rejected by him and insulted by Sītā's laughter, assumed a hideous form and threatened to eat her up, but was beaten off by Lakṣmaṇa, who cut off her ears and nose and thus doubly disfigured her; in revenge she incited her brother to carry off Sītā), MBh.; R. [1086,2]
