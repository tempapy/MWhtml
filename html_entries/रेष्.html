• 1. reṣreṣ
    • cl. 1. Ā. reṣate (pf. rireṣe &c. Gr.), to howl, roar, yell (as wolves), Dhātup. xvi, 19 (others ‘to neigh’ or, ‘to utter any inarticulate sound’). [888,1]
  • 2. reṣreṣ mfn.
    • (nom. reṭreṭrejreḍ; cf. 2. rejrej) any animal that howls or yells or neighs, howling, neighing, W. [888,1]
