• reṣreṣ
  • cl. 1. Ā. reṣatereṣate (pf. rireṣerireṣe &c. Gr.), to howl, roar, yell (as wolves), Dhātup. xvi, 19 (others ‘to neigh’ or, ‘to utter any inarticulate sound’). [888,1]
  • reṣreṣ
  • mfn. (nom. reṭreṭ; cf. 2. rejrej) any animal that howls or yells or neighs, howling, neighing, W. [888,1]
