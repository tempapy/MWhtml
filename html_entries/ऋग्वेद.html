• ṛgṛg—vedaveda
  • m. ‘Hymn - Vedaveda’ or ‘Vedaveda of praise’, the Ṛgṛg-vedaveda, or most ancient sacred book of the Hindūs (that is, the collective body of sacred verses called Ṛcaṛcas [see below], consisting of 1017 hymns [or with the Vālakhilyavālakhilyas 1028] arranged in eight Aṣṭakaaṣṭakas or in ten Maṇḍalamaṇḍalas; Maṇḍalamaṇḍalas 2-8 contain groups of hymns, each group ascribed to one author or to the members of one family; the ninth book contains the hymns sung at the Somasoma ceremonies; the first and tenth contain hymns of a different character, some comparatively modern, composed by a greater variety of individual authors; in its wider sense the term Ṛgṛg-vedaveda comprehends the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas and the Sūtrasūtra works on the ritual connected with the hymns), AitBr.; ŚBr.; Mn. &c. [225,1]
