• canacana ind.
    • (caca nana, SV.) and not, also not, even not, not even (this particle is placed after the word to which it gives force; a preceding verb is accentuated [Pāṇ. viii, 1, 57]; in Vedic language it is generally, but not always, found without any other neg. particle, whereas in the later language another neg. is usually added e.g. āpaśāpaśāpasāpa canapra minanti vrataṃvrata vāṃvāmvāvauū, ‘not even the waters violate your ordinance’ RV. ii, 24, 12; nā̑hanāhananāahaāha vivyāca pṛthivīpṛthivīpṛthivi cana͡inaṃcanaenaaina, ‘the earth even does not contain him’, iii, 36, 4; in class. Sanskṛt it is only used after the interrogatives kaka, katarakatara, katamakatama, kathamkatham, kadkad, kadākadākadakad, kimkim, kutaskutaskuta, kvakva, making them indefinite), RV.; AV. &c. also, RV. i, 139, 2; vi, 26, 7; viii, 78, 10.
