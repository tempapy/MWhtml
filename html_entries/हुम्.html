humhum or hūmhūm, ind. or humhum an exclamation (of remembrance, doubt, interrogation, assent, anger, reproach, fear &c., not translatable) [1301,2]
  • a mystical syllable used in spells and magical texts or sentences [1301,2]
  • in Vedic ritual used immediately before the singing of the Prastāvaprastāva or prelude as well as during the chanting of the Pratihārapratihāra or response, ŚrS.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1301,2]
