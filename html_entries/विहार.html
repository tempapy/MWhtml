• vi-hāravihāravihāra m.
    • (once in BhP. n.) distribution, transposition (of words), AitBr.; Lāṭy. [1003,3]
    • arrangement or disposition (of the 3 sacred fires; also applied to the fires themselves or the space between them), ŚrS. [1003,3]
    • too great expansion of the organs of speech (consisting in too great lengthening or drawling in pronunciation, opp. to saṃ-hārasaṃhārasaṃsamhāra q.v.), RPrāt. [1003,3]
    • walking for pleasure or amusement, wandering, roaming, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1003,3]
    • sport, play, pastime, diversion, enjoyment, pleasure (‘in’ or ‘with’ comp.; ifc. also = taking delight in), Yājñ.; MBh.; R. &c. [1003,3]
    • a place of recreation, pleasure-ground, MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1003,3]
    • (with Buddhists or Jainas) a monastery or temple (originally a hall where the monks met or walked about; afterwards these halls were used as temples), Lalit.; Mṛcch.; Kathās. &c. (cf. MWB. 68; 81 &c.) [1003,3]
    • consecration for a sacrifice, Āpast. [1003,3]
    • N. of the country of Magadha (called Bihār or Behār from the number of Buddhist monasteries See MWB. 68), Cat. [1003,3]
    • the shoulder, L. [1003,3]
    • a partic. bird (= bindurekaka), L. [1003,3]
    • = vaijayantavaijayanta, L. [1003,3]
