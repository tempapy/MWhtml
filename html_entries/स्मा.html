smasma, smāsmā, ind. smāsmā, (or ṣmaṣma, ṣmāṣmā) a particle perhaps originally equivalent to ‘ever’, ‘always’ [1271,2]
  • and later to ‘indeed’, ‘certainly’, ‘verily’, ‘surely’ (it is often used pleonastically, and in earlier language generally follows a similar particle [esp. haha, nana], or relative, or prep. or verb, while in later language it frequently follows itiiti, nana and nānā [cf. 1. māmā]; it is also joined with a pres. tense or pres. participle to give them a past sense [e.g. praviśantipraviśanti smasma, ‘they entered’]; this use of smasma is also found in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas and is extended to vedaveda and āhaāha cf. Vām. v, 2, 46), RV.; &c. [1271,2]
