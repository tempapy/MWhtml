• smasma, smāsmāsma, ind. smāsmāsma, (or ṣma, ṣmā) a particle perhaps originally equivalent to ‘ever’, ‘always’ [1271,2]
    • and later to ‘indeed’, ‘certainly’, ‘verily’, ‘surely’ (it is often used pleonastically, and in earlier language generally follows a similar particle [esp. haha, nana], or relative, or prep. or verb, while in later language it frequently follows itiitiit, nana and nānāna [cf. 1. māmāma]; it is also joined with a pres. tense or pres. participle to give them a past sense [e.g. praviśanti smasma, ‘they entered’]; this use of smasma is also found in the Brāhmaṇas and is extended to vedaveda and āhaāha cf. Vām. v, 2, 46), RV.; &c. [1271,2]
