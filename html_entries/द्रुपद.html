• drudrudrū—padapada n.
    • a wooden pillar, a post (to which captives are tied), any pillar or column, RV.; VS.; AV. [502,1]
  • drudrudrū—padapada m.
    • N. of a king of the Pañcālas (son of Pṛṣata and father of Dhṛṣṭa-dyumna, of Śikhaṇḍin or Śikhaṇḍinī, and of Kṛṣṇā, the wife of the Pāṇḍu princes, hence called Draupadī), MBh.; Hariv.; Pur. [502,2]
