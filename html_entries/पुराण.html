• purāṇapurāṇa a mf(īīiin or āāa)n.
    • belonging to ancient or olden times, ancient, old (also = withered, worn out, opp. to nūtananūtana, navanava), RV. &c. &c. [635,1]
  • purāṇapurāṇa m.
    • a Karṣa or measure of silver (= 16 Paṇas of cowries), Mn. viii, 136 (also n., L.) [635,1]
  • purāṇapurāṇa m.
    • N. of a Ṛṣi, Kāṭh. [635,1]
  • purāṇapurāṇa m. pl.
    • the ancients, MW. [635,1]
  • purāṇapurāṇa n.
    • a thing or event of the past, an ancient tale or legend, old traditional history, AV. &c. &c. [635,1]
    • N. of a class of sacred works (supposed to have been compiled by the poet Vyāsa and to treat of 5 topics [cf. pañca-lakṣaṇapañcalakṣaṇapañcalakṣaṇa]; the chief Purāṇas are 18, grouped in 3 divisions: viz. 1. Rājasa exalting Brahmā [e.g. the Brahma, Brahmâṇḍa, Brahmavaivarta, Mārkaṇḍeya, Bhaviṣya, Vāmana]; 2. Sāttvika exalting Viṣṇu [e.g. the Viṣṇu, Bhāgavata, Nāradīya, Garuḍa, Padma, Varāha]; 3. Tāmasa exalting Śiva [e.g. the Śiva, Liṅga, Skanda, Agni or in place of it the Vāyu, Matsya, Kūrma]; by some the Padma are divided into 4, and by others into 6 groups; cf. IW. 509 &c.) [635,1]
    • N. of wk. (containing an index of the contents of a number of Padma and some other works.) [635,1]
  • purāṇapurāṇa b
    • purātanapurātana See p.635. [636,1]
