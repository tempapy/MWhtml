• sauptikasauptika—parvanparvan
  • n. N. of the 10th book of the Mahā-bhāratamahābhāratamahābhārata (describing how the three surviving Kurukuru warriors, Aśvatthāmanaśvatthāman, Kṛta-varmankṛtavarmankṛtavarman, and Kṛpakṛpa, after the destruction of their army, attacked the camp of the Pāṇḍavapāṇḍavas by night and murdered them while asleep; the whole Pāṇḍavapāṇḍava army was thus destroyed, except the five Pāṇḍupāṇḍu princes themselves, who, with Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa and Sātyakisātyaki, were stationed at some distance from the camp). [1253,2]
