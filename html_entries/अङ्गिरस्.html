• aṅgirasaṅgiras
  • m. N. of a Ṛṣiṛṣi, author of the hymns of RV. ix, of a code of laws, and of a treatise on astronomy (he is said by some to have been born from Brahmābrahmā's mouth, and to have been the husband of Smṛtismṛti, of Śraddhāśraddhā, of two daughters of Maitreyamaitreya, of several daughters of Dakṣadakṣa, &c.; he is considered as one of the seven Ṛṣiṛṣis of the first Manvantaramanvantara, as a Prajāpatiprajāpati, as a teacher of the Brahmavidyābrahmavidyā, which he had learnt from Satyavāhasatyavāha, a descendant of Bharadvājabharadvāja, &c. Among his sons, the chief is Agniagni, others are Saṃvartasaṃvarta, Utathyautathya, and Bṛhaspatibṛhaspati; among his daughters are mentioned Sinīvālīsinīvālī, Kuhūkuhū, Rākārākā, Anumatianumati, and Akūpārāakūpārā; but the Ṛcaṛcas or Vedic hymns, the manes of Haviṣmathaviṣmat, and mankind itself are styled his offspring. In astronomy he is the planet Jupiter, and a star in Ursa Major) [8,2]
  • N. of Agniagni, MBh. [8,2]
  • (asasasas) descendants of Aṅgirasaṅgiras or of Agniagni (mostly personifications of luminous objects) [8,2]
  • the hymns of the Atharvaatharva-vedaveda, TS. [8,2]
  • priests who by using the magical formulas of those hymns protect the sacrifice against the effects of inauspicious accidents. [8,2]
