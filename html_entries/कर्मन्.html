• karmankarman n.
    • (āāa m., L.), (√kṛkṛ, Uṇ. iv, 144), act, action, performance, business, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [258,2]
    • office, special duty, occupation, obligation (frequently ifc., the first member of the compound being either the person who performs the action [e.g. vaṇik-k°vaṇikkarmanvaṇikkarman] or the person or thing for or towards whom the action is performed [e.g. rāja-k°rājakarmanrājakarman, paśu-k°paśukarmanpaśukarman] or a specification of the action [e.g. śaurya-k°śauryakarmanśauryakarman, prīti-k°prītikarmanprītikarman]), ŚBr.; Mn.; Bhartṛ. &c. [258,2]
    • any religious act or rite (as sacrifice, oblation &c., esp. as originating in the hope of future recompense and as opposed to speculative religion or knowledge of spirit), RV.; AV.; VS.; Ragh. &c. [258,2]
    • work, labour, activity (as opposed to rest, praśāntipraśānti), Hit.; RPrāt. &c. [258,2]
    • physicking, medical attendance, Car. [258,2]
    • action consisting in motion (as the third among the seven categories of the Nyāya philosophy; of these motions there are five, viz. ut-kṣepaṇautkṣepaṇautkṣepaṇa, ava-kṣepaṇaavakṣepaṇaavakṣepaṇa, ā-kuñcanaākuñcanaākuñcana, prasāraṇaprasāraṇa, and gamanagamana, qq.vv.), Bhāṣāp.; Tarkas. [258,2]
    • calculation, Sūryas. [258,2]
    • product, result, effect, Mn. xii, 98; Suśr. [258,2]
    • organ of sense, ŚBr. xiv (or of action See karmȇndriyakarmendriyakarmaindriya) [258,2]
    • (in Gr.) the object (it stands either in the acc. [in active construction], or in the nom. [in passive construction], or in the gen. [in connection with a noun of action]; opposed to kartṛkartṛ the subject), Pāṇ. i, 4, 49 ff. (it is of four kinds, viz. a. nirvartyanirvartya, when anything new is produced, e.g. kaṭaṃkaṭakaṭ karoti, ‘he makes a mat’ ; putraṃputra prasūteprasūtaprasūtāprasūti, ‘she bears a son’; b. vikāryavikārya, when change is implied either of the substance and form, e.g. kāṣṭhaṃkāṣṭhamkāṣṭha bhasmabhasma karoti, ‘he reduces fuel to ashes’; or of the form only, e.g. suvarṇaṃsuvarṇa kuṇḍalaṃkuṇḍala karoti, ‘he fashions gold into an ear-ring’; c. prāpyaprāpya, when any desired object is attained, e.g. grāmaṃgrāma gacchatigacchat, ‘he goes to the village’; candraṃcandra paśyatipaśyat, ‘he sees the moon’; d. anīpsitaanīpsita, when an undesired object is abandoned, e.g. pāpaṃpāpampāpa tyajati, ‘he leaves the wicked’) [258,2]
    • former act as leading to inevitable results, fate (as the certain consequence of acts in a previous life), Pañcat.; Hit.; Buddh., (cf. karma-pākakarmapākakarmapāka and -vipākakarmanvipāka) [258,3]
    • the tenth lunar mansion, VarBṛS. &c. [258,3]
