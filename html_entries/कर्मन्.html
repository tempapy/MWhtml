• karman n. (ā m., L.), (√kṛ, Uṇ. iv, 144), act, action, performance, business, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; MBh. &c. [258,2]
  • office, special duty, occupation, obligation (frequently ifc., the first member of the compound being either the person who performs the action [e.g. vaṇik-k°vaṇik-karmanvaṇikkarman] or the person or thing for or towards whom the action is performed [e.g. rāja-k°rāja-karmanrājakarman, paśu-k°paśu-karmanpaśukarman] or a specification of the action [e.g. śaurya-k°śaurya-karmanśauryakarman, prīti-k°prīti-karmanprītikarman]), ŚBr.; Mn.; Bhartṛ. &c. [258,2]
  • any religious act or rite (as sacrifice, oblation &c., esp. as originating in the hope of future recompense and as opposed to speculative religion or knowledge of spirit), RV.; AV.; VS.; Ragh. &c. [258,2]
  • work, labour, activity (as opposed to rest, praśānti), Hit.; RPrāt. &c. [258,2]
  • physicking, medical attendance, Car. [258,2]
  • action consisting in motion (as the third among the seven categories of the Nyāya philosophy; of these motions there are five, viz. ut-kṣepaṇaut-kṣepaṇautkṣepaṇa, ava-kṣepaṇaava-kṣepaṇaavakṣepaṇa, ā-kuñcanaā-kuñcanaākuñcana, prasāraṇa, and gamana, qq.vv.), Bhāṣāp.; Tarkas. [258,2]
  • calculation, Sūryas. [258,2]
  • product, result, effect, Mn. xii, 98; Suśr. [258,2]
  • organ of sense, ŚBr. xiv (or of action See karmȇndriya) [258,2]
  • (in Gr.) the object (it stands either in the acc. [in active construction], or in the nom. [in passive construction], or in the gen. [in connection with a noun of action]; opposed to kartṛ the subject), Pāṇ. i, 4, 49 ff. (it is of four kinds, viz. a. nirvartya, when anything new is produced e.g. kaṭaṃ karoti, ‘he makes a mat’ ; putraṃ prasūte, ‘she bears a son’; b. vikārya, when change is implied either of the substance and form e.g. kāṣṭhaṃ bhasma karoti, ‘he reduces fuel to ashes’; or of the form only e.g. suvarṇaṃ kuṇḍalaṃ karoti, ‘he fashions gold into an ear-ring’; c. prāpya, when any desired object is attained e.g. grāmaṃ gacchati, ‘he goes to the village’; candraṃ paśyati, ‘he sees the moon’; d. anīpsita, when an undesired object is abandoned e.g. pāpaṃ tyajati, ‘he leaves the wicked’) [258,2]
  • former act as leading to inevitable results, fate (as the certain consequence of acts in a previous life), Pañcat.; Hit.; Buddh., (cf. karma-pākakarma-pākakarmapāka and -vipākakarman-vipākavipāka) [258,3]
  • the tenth lunar mansion, VarBṛS. &c. [258,3]
