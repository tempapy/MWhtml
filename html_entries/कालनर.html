• 1. kālakāla—naranara m.
    • N. of a son of Sabhā-nara (a son of Anu), BhP. ix, 23, 1 (cf. kālā̑nalakālānalakālakālāanalaānala.) [277,2]
  • 2. kālakāla—naranara m.
    • ‘a time-man’ i.e. (in astrol.) the figure of a man's body on the various limbs of which the twelve signs of the zodiac are distributed for the purpose of foretelling future destinies Comm. on VarBṛ. [278,2]
