• riphriph [Roots]
    • cl. 6. P. (Dhātup. xxviii, 23) riphati (p. -rephatriph, ŚāṅkhBr.; pf. rirepha &c. Gr.; ind.p. rephitvā, Pāṇ. i, 2, 23 Sch.),
    • to snarl, AV.;
    • (only L.) to speak or boast (kathanekathana or katthanekatthanakatthanā);
    • to blame;
    • to fight;
    • to give;
    • to hurt, kill (cf. √rimphrimph) :
    • Pass. riphyate, to be murmured or spoken in a guttural or burring manner (like the letter r);
    • to have or take the pronunciation of the letter r, ĀśvŚr. (cf. repharepha). [880,3]
