• matsyā̑vatāramatsyāvatāra
  • m. ‘fish-descent’, N. of the first of the 10 incarnations of Viṣṇuviṣṇu (who became a fish to save the 7th Manumanu from the universal deluge; the conversation between them forms the Matsya-Purāṇamatsyapurāṇamatsyapurāṇa q.v.; in MBh. i. the fish is represented as an incarnation of Brahmābrahmā; cf. IW. 327, 397 &c.) [776,3]
