• uta
  • mfn. (fr. √ve q.v.), sewn, woven. [175,2]
  • uta/
  • ind. and, also, even, or, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; ChUp. &c. [175,2]
  • often used for the sake of emphasis, especially at the end of a line after iti or a verb (e.g. sarva-BUtAni tam pArTa sadA pariBavanty uta, all creatures, O king, certainly always despise him, MBh. iii, 1026), MBh.; Bhag. &c. (As an interrogative particle, generally at the beginning of the second or following part of a double interrogation) or, utrum - an (e.g. kaTam nirRIyate kiM syAn nizkAraRo banDur uta viSvAsa-GAtakaH, how can it be decided whether he be a friend without a motive or a violator of confidence? Hit.), Kum.; Kathās.; Bhartṛ.; Sāh. &c. [175,2]
  • in this sense it may be strengthened by Aho (e.g. kaccit tvam asi mAnuzI utAho surANganA, art thou a mortal woman or divine? Nalanala), or by Aho-svit (e.g. SAlihotraH kiM nu syAd utAhosvid rAjA nalaH, can it be Śālihotraśālihotra or king Nalanala?) Rarely kim is repeated before uta used in this sense (e.g. kim nu svargAt prAptA tasyA rUpeRa kimutAnyAgatA, has she arrived from heaven or has another come in her form? Mṛcch.), Amar.; MBh. &c. (As a particle of wishing, especially at the beginning of a sentence followed by a potential) would that! utinam! (e.g. utADIyIta, would that he would read!) (uta preceded by kim) on the contrary, how much more, how much less (e.g. samarTo 'si sahasram api jetuM kimutEkam, thou art able to conquer even a thousand, how much more one, R.), Śak.; Vikr.; Ragh. &c. (uta preceded by prati) on the contrary, rather (e.g. eza pfzwo 'smABir na jalpati hanti praty-uta pAzAREH, this one questioned by us does not speak, but rather throws stones at us), Kathās.; Pañcat. &c. [175,2]
