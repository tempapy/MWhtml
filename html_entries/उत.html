• 1. utauta mfn.
    • (fr. √vevevavāvi q.v.), sewn, woven. [175,2]
  • 2. utauta ind.
    • and, also, even, or, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; ChUp. &c. [175,2]
    • often used for the sake of emphasis, especially at the end of a line after itiiti or a verb (e.g. sarva-bhūtānisarvabhūtasarvabhūta tamtamta pārthapārtha sadāsadāsadasad paribhavanty utauta, all creatures, O king, certainly always despise him, MBh. iii, 1026), MBh.; Bhag. &c. (As an interrogative particle, generally at the beginning of the second or following part of a double interrogation) or, utrum - an (e.g. kathamkatham nirṇīyate kiṃkiṃkim syānsyasyāt niṣkāraṇoniṣkāraṇa bandhurbandhu utauta viśvāsa-ghātakaḥviśvāsaghātakaviśvāsaghātaka, how can it be decided whether he be a friend without a motive or a violator of confidence? Hit.), Kum.; Kathās.; Bhartṛ.; Sāh. &c. [175,2]
    • in this sense it may be strengthened by āhoāho (e.g. kaccit tvam asiasiasī mānuṣīmānuṣī utā̑houtāhoutaahoahuāho surā̑ṅganāsurāṅganāsurasurāaṅganāaṅgana, art thou a mortal woman or divine? Nala), or by āho-svitāhosvitāhosvid (e.g. śālihotraḥśālihotra kiṃkiṃkim nununū syādsyādsyātsya utā̑hosvidutāhosvidutaāhosvit rājārājarāj nalaḥnala, can it be Śālihotra or king Nala?) Rarely kimkim is repeated before utauta used in this sense (e.g. kimkim nununū svargātsvarga prāptāprāpta tasyāta rūpeṇarūpa kimutā̑nyā̑gatāanyaanyāagataāgata, has she arrived from heaven or has another come in her form? Mṛcch.), Amar.; MBh. &c. (As a particle of wishing, especially at the beginning of a sentence followed by a potential) would that! utinam! (e.g. utā̑dhīyītauta, would that he would read!) (utauta preceded by kimkim) on the contrary, how much more, how much less (e.g. samarthosamartha 'si sahasramsahasramsahasra apiapiap jetuṃ kimuta͡ikamekaaika, thou art able to conquer even a thousand, how much more one, R.), Śak.; Vikr.; Ragh. &c.
