• kṣamakṣama mf(āāa)n.
    • (g. pacā̑dipacaadādiādīādin) patient (said of the earth, perhaps with reference to 2. kṣamkṣamkṣa), AV. xii, 1, 29 [326,2]
    • ifc. (Pāṇ. iii, 2, 1, Vārtt. 8) enduring, suffering, bearing, submissive, resisting, MBh.; Śak.; Kum. v, 40 [326,2]
    • adequate, competent, able, fit for (loc. or inf. or in comp., e.g. vayaṃvayamvaya tyaktuṃ kṣamāḥkṣamakṣamā, ‘we are able to quit’, Śāntiś.), Nal.; R.; Ragh. &c. [326,2]
    • favourable to (gen.), R. ii, 35, 31 [326,2]
    • bearable, tolerable, Śak.; Pañcat. (= Subh.) [326,2]
    • fit, appropriate, becoming, suitable, proper for (gen. dat., loc. inf. or in comp.), MBh. (e.g. kṣamaṃkṣama kauravāṇāmkaurava, ‘proper for the Kauravas’, iii, 252), R. (e.g. nana sasa kṣamaḥ kopayitum, ‘he is not a fit object, for anger’, iv, 32, 20) &c. [326,2]
  • kṣamakṣama m.
    • ‘the patient’, N. of Śiva [326,2]
    • a kind of sparrow, L. [326,2]
  • kṣamakṣama n.
    • propriety fitness, W. [326,3]
