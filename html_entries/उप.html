• upaupa
  • ind. (a preposition or prefix to verbs and nouns, expressing) towards, near to (opposed to apaapa, away), by the side of, with, together with, under, down (e.g. upa-√gamupagamupagam, to go near, undergo; upa-gamanaupagamanaupagamana, approaching; in the Vedaveda the verb has sometimes to be supplied from the context, and sometimes upaupa is placed after the verb to which it belongs, e.g. āyayurāyayur upaupa = upā̑yayuḥupāsrssrsyayuḥ, they approached). (As unconnected with verbs and prefixed to nouns upaupa expresses) direction towards, nearness, contiguity in space, time, number, degree, resemblance, and relationship, but with the idea of subordination and inferiority (e.g. upa-kaniṣṭhikāupakaniṣṭhikāupakaniṣṭhikā, the finger next to the little finger; upa-purāṇamupapurāṇamupapurāṇam, a secondary or subordinate Purāṇapurāṇa; upa-daśaupadaśaupadaśa, nearly ten) [194,3]
  • sometimes forming with the nouns to which it is prefixed compound adverbs (e.g. upa-mūlamupamūlamupamūlam, at the root; upa-pūrva-rātramupapūrvarātramupapūrvarātram, towards the beginning of night; upa-kūpeupakūpeupakūpe, near a well) which lose their adverbial terminations if they are again compounded with nouns (e.g. upakūpa-jalā̑śayaupakūpajalāsrssrsśayaupakūpajalāsrssrsśaya, a reservoir in the neighbourhood of a well) [194,3]
  • prefixed to proper names upaupa may express in classical literature ‘a younger brother’ (e.g. upȇndraupesrssrsndra, ‘the younger brother of Indraindra’), and in Buddhist literature ‘a son.’ (As a separable adverb upaupa rarely expresses) thereto, further, moreover (e.g. tatrȏpatatrosrssrspa brahmabrahma yoyo vedaveda, who further knows the Brahmanbrahman), RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; PārGṛ. (As a separable preposition) near to, towards, in the direction of, under, below (with acc. e.g. upaupa āśāḥāśāḥ, towards the regions) [194,3]
  • near to, at, on, upon [194,3]
  • at the time of, upon, up to, in, above (with loc. e.g. upaupa sānuṣusānuṣu, on the tops of the mountains) [194,3]
  • with, together with, at the same time with, according to (with inst. e.g. upaupa dharmabhiḥdharmabhiḥ, according to the rules of duty), RV.; AV.; ŚBr. upaupa, besides the meanings given above, is said by native authorities to imply disease, extinction; ornament; command; reproof; undertaking; giving; killing; diffusing; wish; power; effort; resemblance, &c.; [195,1]
