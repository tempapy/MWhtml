• upaupa ind.
    • (a preposition or prefix to verbs and nouns, expressing) towards, near to (opposed to apaapa, away), by the side of, with, together with, under, down (e.g. upa-√gamupagamupagaupagamga, to go near, undergo; upa-gamanaupagamanaupagamana, approaching; in the Veda the verb has sometimes to be supplied from the context, and sometimes upaupa is placed after the verb to which it belongs, e.g. āyayur upaupa = upā̑yayuḥupaupā, they approached). (As unconnected with verbs and prefixed to nouns upaupa expresses) direction towards, nearness, contiguity in space, time, number, degree, resemblance, and relationship, but with the idea of subordination and inferiority (e.g. upa-kaniṣṭhikāupakaniṣṭhikāupakaniṣṭhikā, the finger next to the little finger; upa-purāṇamupapurāṇaupapurāṇa, a secondary or subordinate Purāṇa; upa-daśaupadaśaupadaśa, nearly ten) [194,3]
    • sometimes forming with the nouns to which it is prefixed compound adverbs (e.g. upa-mūlamupamūlamupamūla, at the root; upa-pūrva-rātramupapūrvarātramupapūrvarātra, towards the beginning of night; upa-kūpeupakūpeupakūpaupakūpakūp, near a well) which lose their adverbial terminations if they are again compounded with nouns (e.g. upakūpa-jalā̑śayaupakūpajalāśayaupakūpajalāśayajalajalāāśaya, a reservoir in the neighbourhood of a well) [194,3]
    • prefixed to proper names upaupa may express in classical literature ‘a younger brother’ (e.g. upȇndraupendraupaupāindra, ‘the younger brother of Indra’), and in Buddhist literature ‘a son.’ (As a separable adverb upaupa rarely expresses) thereto, further, moreover (e.g. tatrȏpatatraupa brahmabrahma yoyu vedaveda, who further knows the Brahman), RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; PārGṛ. (As a separable preposition) near to, towards, in the direction of, under, below (with acc. e.g. upaupaupeupāupas āśāḥāśāsāśaāśāāśas, towards the regions) [194,3]
    • near to, at, on, upon [194,3]
    • at the time of, upon, up to, in, above (with loc. e.g. upaupa sānuṣusānu, on the tops of the mountains) [194,3]
    • with, together with, at the same time with, according to (with inst. e.g. upaupa dharmabhiḥ, according to the rules of duty), RV.; AV.; ŚBr. upaupa, besides the meanings given above, is said by native authorities to imply disease, extinction; ornament; command; reproof; undertaking; giving; killing; diffusing; wish; power; effort; resemblance, &c. [195,1]
