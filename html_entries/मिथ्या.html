• mithyāmithyāmithi a ind.
    • (contracted from mithūyā) invertedly, contrarily, incorrectly, wrongly, improperly, ŚBr. &c. &c. (with Caus., of √kṛkṛ, to pronounce a word wrongly ‘once’ [P.] or ‘repeatedly’ [Ā.] Pāṇ. i, 3, 71; with pra-√carpracarpracar, to act wrongly, Mn. ix, 284; with pra-√vṛtpravṛtpravṛdhpravṛtvṛdh, to behave improperly, MBh. iii, 2414);
    • falsely, deceitfully, untruly, Mn.; MBh. &c. (often with √brūbrū, vacvac or vadvad, to speak falsely, utter a lie; with √kṛkṛ, to deny, MBh.; to break one's word, with na-√kṛnakṛ, to keep it), R.;
    • with √bhūbhūbhu, to turn out or prove false, MBh.;
    • not in reality, only apparently, Madhus.;
    • to no purpose, fruitlessly, in vain, MaitrUp.; MBh. &c. (ibc. often = false, untrue, sham; Mithyā is personified as the wife of A-dharma, KalkiP.) [817,1]
