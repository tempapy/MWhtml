• aDI
  • (√i), aDy-e/ti, or a/Dy-eti (exceptionally aDIyati, RV. x, 32, 3), to turn the mind towards, observe, understand, RV. and, AV.;
    chiefly Ved. (with gen. [cf. Pāṇ. ii, 3, 52] or acc.) to mind, remember, care for, long for, RV. &c.;
    to know, know by heart, TS.; ŚBr.; Up. &c.;
    to go over, study, MBh. iii, 13689;
    to learn from (a teacher's mouth abl.), MBh. iii, 10713;
    to declare, teach, ŚBr. x; Up. : Ā. aDIte/ or (more rarely) aDIyate (Mn. iv, 125; Pot. 3. pl. aDIyIran, Kauś.; Mn. x, 1)
    to study, learn by heart, read, recite:
    Caus. aDy-Apayati (aor. -Apipat, Pāṇ. ii, 4, 51) to cause to read or study, teach, instruct:
    Caus. Desid. aDy-Apipa-yizati, to be desirous of teaching, Pāṇ. ii, 4, 51 :
    Desid. aDIzizati, to be desirous of studying, Pāṇ. viii, 3, 61 Sch. [22,3]
