  • dhvandhvan
    • [cf. 2. dhvāntadhvānta; Lith. dumju, dumti, to cover, wrap up; Angl.Sax. dvinan, dvan, to be extinguished, vanish; dun, dark-brown, dark.] [522,2]

    • to mean, imply, (esp. Pass. dhvanyate, it is meant, it is implied) :
    • Caus. dhvā̆nayati, Dhātup. (aor. adidhvanat or adadh° Gr.), to cause to sound, make resound (cf. °nayatdhvanayat below);
