  • rāmāyaṇarāmāyaṇa
  • n. (ramā̑yaṇaramāsrssrsyaṇa) N. of Vālmīkivālmīki's celebrated poem, describing the ‘goings’ ([ayanaayana]) of Rāmarāma and Sitāsitā (it contains about 24000 verses in 7 books called Kāṇḍakāṇḍas, viz. 1. Bāla-kāṇḍabālakāṇḍabālakāṇḍa or Ādiādi-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 2. Ayodhyāayodhyā-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 3. Araṇyaaraṇya-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 4. Kiṣ-kindhyākiṣkindhyākiṣkindhyā-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 5. Sundarasundara-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 6. Yuddhayuddha-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; 7. Uttarauttara-kāṇḍakāṇḍa; part of the 1st book and the 7th are thought to be comparatively modern additions; the latter gives the history of Rāmarāma and Sītāsītā after their re-union and installation as king and queen of Ayodhyāayodhyā, afterwards dramatized by Bhava-bhūtibhavabhūtibhavabhūti in the Uttara-rāma-caritrauttararāmacaritrauttararāmacaritra; Rāmarāma's character, as described in the Rāmāyaṇarāmāyaṇa, is that of a perfect man, who bears suffering and self-denial with superhuman patience; the author, Vālmīkivālmīki, was probably a Brāhman connected with the royal family of Ayodhyāayodhyā; and although there are three recensions of the poem, all of them go back to a lost original recension, the ground work of which, contained in books 2-6, in spite of many amplifications and interpolations, may be traced back to one man, and does not like the Mahābhāratamahābhārata, represent the production of different epochs and minds), MBh.; Hariv. &c. (cf. IW. 335). [878,3]
