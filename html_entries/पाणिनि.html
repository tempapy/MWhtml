• pāṇinipāṇini
  • m. (according to, Pāṇ. iv, 1, 95 patr. fr. pāṇinapāṇina) N. of the most eminent of all native Sanskṛt grammarians (he was the author of the aṣṭādhyāyīaṣṭādhyāyī and supposed author of sev. other works, viz. the Dhātu-pāṭhadhātupāṭhadhātupāṭha, Gaṇa-pāthagaṇapāthagaṇapātha, Liṅgânuśāsanaliṅgānuśāsana and Śikṣāśikṣā; he was a Gāndhāragāndhāra and a native of Śalāturaśalātura, situated in the North-West near Attok and Peshawar [see iv, 3, 94 and Śālāturīyaśālāturīya]; he lived after Gautamagautama Buddhabuddha but B.C. and is regarded as an inspired Munimuni; his grandfather's name was Devaladevala and his mother's Dākṣidākṣi [see s.v. and Dākṣeyaananta]) [615,3]
  • of a poet (by some identified with the grammarian). [615,3]
