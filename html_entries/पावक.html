• pāvakapāvaka mf(āāa)n.
    • pure, clear, bright, shining, RV.; VS.; AV. (said of Agni, Sūrya and other gods, of water, day and night &c. according to native Comms. it is mostly = sodhaka, ‘cleansing, purifying’) [623,2]
  • pāvakapāvaka m.
    • N. of a partic. Agni (in the Purāṇas said to be a son of Agni Abhimānin and Svāhā or of Antardhāna and Śikhaṇḍinī), TS.; TBr.; KātyŚr.; Pur. [623,2]
  • pāvakapāvaka m.
    • (ifc. f(āāa). ) fire or the god of fire, Up.; MBh.; Kāv. &c. [623,2]
  • pāvakapāvaka m.
    • N. of the number 3 (like all words for ‘fire’, because fire is of three kinds See agniagni), Sūryas. [623,2]
    • a kind of Ṛṣi, a saint, a person purified by religious abstraction or one who purified from sin, MBh. [623,2]
