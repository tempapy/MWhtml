• śāliśāliśālīśālin—vāhanavāhana m.
    • N. of a celebrated sovereign of India (said to be so called either from having ridden on a Yakṣa called Śāli, or from Śalī for Śāla, the Śāl tree, Śāli-vāhana being represented as borne on a cross made of that or other wood; he was the enemy of Vikramâditya and institutor of the era now called Śaka q.v.; his capital was Pratiṣṭhāna on the Godāvarī), Siṉhâs.; Subh.; Buddh. [1068,1]
