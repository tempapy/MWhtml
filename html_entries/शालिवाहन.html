• śāliśāli—vāhanavāhana
  • m. N. of a celebrated sovereign of India (said to be so called either from having ridden on a Yakṣayakṣa called Śāliśāli, or from Śalīśalī for Śālaśāla, the Śāl tree, Śāli-vāhanaśālivāhanaśālivāhana being represented as borne on a cross made of that or other wood; he was the enemy of Vikramâdityavikramāditya and institutor of the era now called Śakaśaka q.v.; his capital was Pratiṣṭhānapratiṣṭhāna on the Godāvarīgodāvarī), Siṉhâs.; Subh.; Buddh. [1068,1]
