• ānantaryaānantarya
  • n. (fr. an-antaraanantaraanantara, Pāṇ. v, 1, 124), immediate sequence or succession, KātyŚr.; Āp.; Mn. &c. [139,3]
  • proximity, absence of interval, MBh. &c. [139,3]
  • an unpardonable sin (said by Buddhists to be five, viz. ‘matricide’, ‘parricide’, ‘killing an Arhat’, ‘shedding the blood of a Buddhabuddha’, ‘causing divisions among the brotherhood’), Dharmas. 60 [1319,2]
