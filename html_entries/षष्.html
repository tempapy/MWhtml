• ṣaṣṣaṣ mfn. pl.
    • (prob. for orig. ṣakṣ; nom. acc. ṣaṭṣaṭṣaḍ instr. ṣaḍbhisṣaḍ dat. abl. ṣaḍbhyasṣaḍ, gen. ṣaṇṇām loc. ṣaṭsuṣaṭ; in comp. ṣaṣṣaṣ becomes ṣaṭṣaṭṣaḍ before hard letters, ṣaḍṣaḍ before soft, ṣoṣoṣu before d, which is changed into ḍ, and ṇ before nasals) six (with the counted object in apposition or exceptionally in gen. or ifc. e.g. ṣaḍṣaḍṣaṭ ṛtavah, or ṣaḍṣaḍṣaṭ ṛtunām, ‘the six seasons’, ṣaṭsuṣaṭ ṣatsu māseṣumāsa, ‘at periods of six months’ Mn. viii 403 at the end of a Bahuvrīhi compound it is declined like other words ending in ṣ, e. g. priya-ṣaṣaspriyaṣaṣ nom. pl. Pāṇ. vii, 1, 22 Schol.; among the words used as expressions for the number six (esp. in giving dates) are aṅgaaṅga, darśanadarśana, tarkatarka, rasarasa, ṛtuṛtu, vajrakoṇa, kārttikeya-mukhakārttikeyamukha), RV. &c. &c. [1108,1]
    • (in gram.) a technical N. for numerals ending in ṣ and n and words like katikatikat, Pāṇ. i, 1, 24, 25 [1108,1]
