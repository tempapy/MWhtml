• 1. nāmanāma ind.
    • (acc. of nāmannāman) by name i.e. named, called, RV. &c. &c. (also with nāmatasnāmatas and nāmnānāmnā) [536,1]
    • indeed, certainly, really, of course, ib. [536,1]
    • quasi, only in appearance, Jātakam. [536,1]
    • however, nevertheless, ib. [536,1]
    • after an interr. = then, pray e.g. kiṃkiṃkim n°nāma, kathaṃkathaṃkatham n°nāma, kadākadākadakad nevertheless, what then? pray, what? &c., MBh.; Kāv. &c. [536,1]
    • after an Impv. = may it be so, no matter e.g. Śak. v, 8 [536,1]
    • apiapiap n°nāma at the beginning of a sentence = perhaps, I dare say, e.g. apyapiapīap eṣaeṣa nāmanāma phalamphala icchati, this man wants perhaps a reward, Mṛcch. viii, 25 [536,1]
    • with Pot. often = would that e.g. apiapiap nāma͡ivaṃnāmaevameva syātsyātsyasyād, would that it were so, Vikr. v, 19/20 [536,1]
    • opp. to māmāmamās n°nāma with Pot. would that not, I should think not, e.g. māmāmamās nāmanāma akāryaṃakārya kuryāt, I hope he will not do something wrong, Mṛcch. iii, 26. [536,1]
  • 2. nāmanāma
    • in comp. for nāmannāman q.v. (sometimes ifc. as in satya-satyanāmasatyanāma q.v.) [536,1]
