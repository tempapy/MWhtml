• bhagavad—gītā f. pl. (sometimes with upaniṣad; once n(°ta). , BhP.) ‘Kṛṣṇa's song’, N. of a celebrated mystical poem (interpolated in the MBh. where it forms an episode of 18 chapters, from vi, 830-1532, containing a dialogue between Kṛṣṇa and Arjuna, in which the Pantheism of the Vedânta is combined with a tinge of the Sāṃkhya and the later principle of bhakti or devotion to Kṛṣṇa as the Supreme Being; cf. IW. 122 &c.) [744,1]
