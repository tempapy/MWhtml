• rāmarāma—candracandra
  • m. ‘Rāmarāma-moon’, N. of the principal Rāmarāma called Dāśarathidāśarathi, as son of Daśa-rathadaśarathadaśaratha, and Rāghavarāghava, as descended from Raghuraghu (although the affix candracandra seems to connect him with the moon, he is not, like Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa and Balabala-rāmarāmarāmarāma, of the lunar but of the solar race of kings; he forms the 7th Avatāraavatāra of Viṣṇuviṣṇu and is the hero of the Rāmāyaṇarāmāyaṇa, who, to recover his faithful wife Sītāsītā, advanced southwards, killed the demon Rāvaṇarāvaṇa and subjugated his followers the Rākṣasarākṣasas, the poetical representatives of the barbarous aborigines of the south), RāmatUp. (IW. 330; RTL. 110) [877,2]
