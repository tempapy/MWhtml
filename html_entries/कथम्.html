• kathamkatham
  • ind. (fr. 2. kaka), how? in what manner? whence? (e.g. kathamkatham etatetat, how is that? kathamkatham idānīmidānīm, how now? what is now to be done? kathamkatham mārā̑tmakemārāsrssrstmake tvayitvayi viśvāsaḥviśvāsaḥ, how can there be reliance on thee of murderous mind? kathamkatham utsṛjyautsṛjya tvāṃtvāṃ gaccheyamgaccheyam, how can I go away deserting you? kathamkatham buddhvābuddhvā bhaviṣyatibhaviṣyati sāsā, how will she be when she awakes? kathamkatham mṛtyuḥmṛtyuḥ prabhavatiprabhavati vedavidāmvedavidām, whence is it that death has power over those that know the Vedaveda? kathamkatham avagamyateavagamyate, whence is it inferred?); [247,2]
  • sometimes kathamkatham merely introduces an interrogation (e.g. kathamkatham ātmānaṃātmānaṃ nivedayāminivedayāmi kathaṃkathaṃ vā̑tmā̑pahāraṃvāsrssrstmāsrssrspahāraṃ karomikaromi, shall I declare myself or shall I withdraw?) kathamkatham is often found in connection with the particles ivaiva, nāmanāma, nunu, svidsvid, which appear to generalize the interrogation (how possibly? how indeed? &c.) [247,2]
  • with nunu it is sometimes = kimukimu, or kutaskutas (e.g. kathaṃkathaṃ nunu, how much more! nana kathaṃkathaṃ nunu, how much less!) kathamkatham is often connected, like kimkim, with the particles canacana, cidcid, and apiapi, which give an indefinite sense to the interrogative (e.g. kathaṃkathaṃ canacana, in any way, some how; scarcely, with difficulty; nana kathaṃkathaṃ canacana, in no way at all; kathaṃcitkathaṃcit, some how or other, by some means or other, in any way, with some difficulty, scarcely, in a moderate degree, a little; nana kathaṃcitkathaṃcit, not at all, in no way whatever; nana kathaṃcidkathaṃcid nana, in no way not i.e. most decidedly; yathāyathā kathaṃcitkathaṃcit, in any way whatsoever; kathaṃcidkathaṃcid yadiyadi jīvatijīvati, it is with difficulty that he lives; kathamkatham apiapi, some how or other, with some difficulty, scarcely a little; kathamkatham apiapi nana, by no means, not at all), RV. &c. [247,2]
  • according to lexicographers kathamkatham is a particle implying amazement [247,2]
  • surprise [247,2]
  • pleasure [247,2]
  • abuse. [247,2]
