• kathamkathamkathkathaṃ ind.
    • (fr. 2. kaka), how? in what manner? whence? (e.g. kathamkathamkath etatetatetad, how is that? kathamkathamkath idānīmidānīm, how now? what is now to be done? kathamkathamkath mārā̑tmakemārātmakamāraātmaka tvayi viśvāsaḥ, how can there be reliance on thee of murderous mind? kathamkathamkath utsṛjyautsṛjya tvāṃ gaccheyam, how can I go away deserting you? kathamkathamkath buddhvā bhaviṣyatibhaviṣyat sāsāsa, how will she be when she awakes? kathamkathamkath mṛtyuḥmṛtyu prabhavatiprabhavat vedavidāmvedavid, whence is it that death has power over those that know the Veda? kathamkathamkath avagamyate, whence is it inferred?); [247,2]
    • sometimes kathamkathamkathkathaṃ merely introduces an interrogation (e.g. kathamkathamkath ātmānaṃ nivedayāmi kathaṃkathaṃ vā̑tmā̑pahāraṃvavāātmaapahāra karomi, shall I declare myself or shall I withdraw?) kathamkathamkathkathaṃ is often found in connection with the particles ivaiva, nāmanāma, nununū, svidsvid, which appear to generalize the interrogation (how possibly? how indeed? &c.) [247,2]
    • with nununū it is sometimes = kimu, or kutaskutaskutakut (e.g. kathaṃkathaṃkathamkath nununū, how much more! nana kathaṃkathaṃ nununū, how much less!) kathamkathamkathkathaṃ is often connected, like kimkimkikiṃ, with the particles canacana, cidcid, and apiapiapīap, which give an indefinite sense to the interrogative (e.g. kathaṃkathaṃkathamkath canacana, in any way, some how; scarcely, with difficulty; nana kathaṃkathaṃ canacana, in no way at all; kathaṃcit, some how or other, by some means or other, in any way, with some difficulty, scarcely, in a moderate degree, a little; nana kathaṃcit, not at all, in no way whatever; nana kathaṃcid nana, in no way not i.e. most decidedly; yathāyathāyatha kathaṃcit, in any way whatsoever; kathaṃcid yadiyadiyad jīvatijīvat, it is with difficulty that he lives; kathamkathamkath apiapiapīap, some how or other, with some difficulty, scarcely a little; kathamkathamkath apiapiapīap nana, by no means, not at all), RV. &c. [247,2]
    • according to lexicographers kathamkathamkathkathaṃ is a particle implying amazement [247,2]
    • surprise [247,2]
    • pleasure [247,2]
    • abuse. [247,2]
