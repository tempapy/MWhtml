• kaTa/m
  • ind. (fr. 2. ka), how? in what manner? whence? (e.g. kaTam etat, how is that? kaTam idAnIm, how now? what is now to be done? kaTam mArAtmake tvayi viSvAsaH, how can there be reliance on thee of murderous mind? kaTam utsfjya tvAM gacCeyam, how can I go away deserting you? kaTam budDvA Bavizyati sA, how will she be when she awakes? kaTam mftyuH praBavati vedavidAm, whence is it that death has power over those that know the Vedaveda? kaTam avagamyate, whence is it inferred?); [247,2]
  • sometimes kaTam merely introduces an interrogation (e.g. kaTam AtmAnaM nivedayAmi kaTaM vAtmApahAraM karomi, shall I declare myself or shall I withdraw?) kaTam is often found in connection with the particles iva, nAma, nu, svid, which appear to generalize the interrogation (how possibly? how indeed? &c.) [247,2]
  • with nu it is sometimes = kimu, or kutas (e.g. kaTaM nu, how much more! na kaTaM nu, how much less!) kaTam is often connected, like kim, with the particles cana, cid, and api, which give an indefinite sense to the interrogative (e.g. kaTaM cana, in any way, some how; scarcely, with difficulty; na kaTaM cana, in no way at all; kaTaMcit, some how or other, by some means or other, in any way, with some difficulty, scarcely, in a moderate degree, a little; na kaTaMcit, not at all, in no way whatever; na kaTaMcid na, in no way not i.e. most decidedly; yaTA kaTaMcit, in any way whatsoever; kaTaMcid yadi jIvati, it is with difficulty that he lives; kaTam api, some how or other, with some difficulty, scarcely a little; kaTam api na, by no means, not at all), RV. &c. [247,2]
  • according to lexicographers kaTam is a particle implying amazement [247,2]
  • surprise [247,2]
  • pleasure [247,2]
  • abuse. [247,2]
