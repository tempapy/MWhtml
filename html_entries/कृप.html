• kṛpakṛpa
  • m. N. of a man (described as a friend of Indraindra), RV. viii, 3, 12 and 4, 2 [305,1]
  • kṛpakṛpa
  • mf(īī). N. of the son and daughter of the sage Śaradvatśaradvat (who performed severe penance; the jealous Indraindra therefore sent a nymph to tempt him, but without success; however, twin sons were born to the sage in a clump of grass [śara-stambeśarastambeśarastambe], who were found by king Śāntanuśāntanu and out of pity [kṛpākṛpā] taken home and reared; the daughter, Kṛpīkṛpī, married Droṇadroṇa, and had by him a son called Aśvatthāmanaśvatthāman; the son, Kṛpakṛpa, became one of the council at Hastināpurahastināpura, and is sometimes called Gautamagautama, sometimes Śāradvataśāradvata; according to, Hariv. and, VP., Kṛpakṛpa and Kṛpīkṛpī were only distant descendants of Śaradvatśaradvat; according to others, Kṛpakṛpa = Vyāsavyāsa or = a son of Kṛṣṇakṛṣṇa), MBh. &c. [305,1]
