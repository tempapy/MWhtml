• savitṛsavitṛ
  • m. a stimulator, rouser, vivifier (applied to Tvaṣṭṛtvaṣṭṛ), RV. iii, 55, 19; x, 10, 5 [1190,2]
  • N. of a sun-deity (accord. to Naigh. belonging to the atmosphere as well as to heaven; and sometimes in the Vedaveda identified with, at other times distinguished from Sūryasūrya, ‘the Sun’, being conceived of and personified as the divine influence and vivifying power of the sun, while Sūryasūrya is the more concrete conception; accord. to Sāy. the sun before rising is called Savitṛsavitṛ, and after rising till its setting Sūryasūrya; eleven whole hymns of the RV. and parts of others [e.g. i, 35; ii, 38; iii, 62, 10-12 &c.] are devoted to the praise of Savitṛsavitṛ; he has golden hands, arms, hair &c.; he is also reckoned among the Ādityaādityas [q.v.], and is even worshipped as ‘of all creatures’, supporting the world and delivering his votaries from sin; the celebrated verse, RV. iii, 62, 10, called gāyatrīgāyatrī and sāvitrīsāvitrī [qq.vv.] is addressed to him), RV. &c. &c. [1190,2]
  • the orb of the sun (in its ordinary form) or its god (his wife is Pṛśnipṛśni), MBh.; Kāv. &c. [1190,2]
  • N. of one of the 28 Vyāsavyāsas, VP. [1190,2]
  • of Śivaśiva or Indraindra, L. [1190,2]
  • Calotropis Gigantea, L. [1190,2]
