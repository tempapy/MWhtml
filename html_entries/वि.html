  • vivi
  • ind. (prob. for an original dvidvi, meaning ‘in two parts’; and opp. to samsam q.v.) apart, asunder, in different directions, to and fro, about, away, away from, off, without, RV. &c. &c. In RV. it appears also as a prep. with acc. denoting ‘through’ or ‘between’ (with ellipse of the verb, e.g. i, 181, 5; x, 86, 20 &c.) It is esp. used as a prefix to verbs or nouns and other parts of speech derived from verbs, to express ‘division’, ‘distinction’, ‘distribution’, ‘arrangement’, ‘order’, ‘opposition’, or ‘deliberation’ (cf. vi-√bhidvibhidvibhid, -śiṣśiṣvi-śiṣ, -dhādhāvi-dhā, -rudhrudhvi-rudh, -carcarvi-car, with their nominal derivatives) [949,3]
  • sometimes it gives a meaning opposite to the idea contained in the simple root (e.g. √krīkrī, ‘to buy’; vi-√krīvikrīvikrī, ‘to sell’), or it intensifies that idea (e.g. √hiṃshiṃs, ‘to injure’; vi-√hiṃsvihiṃsvihiṃs, ‘to injure severely’). The above 3. vivi may also be used in forming compounds not immediately referable to verbs, in which cases it may express ‘difference’ (cf. 1. vi-lakṣaṇavilakṣaṇavilakṣaṇa), ‘change’ or ‘variety’ (cf. vi-citravicitravicitra), ‘intensity’ (cf. vi-karālavikarālavikarāla), ‘manifoldness’ (cf. vi-vidhavividhavividha), ‘contrariety’ (cf. vi-lomavilomaviloma), ‘deviation from right’ (cf. vi-śīlaviśīlaviśīla), ‘negation’ or ‘privation’ (cf. vi-kacchavikacchavikaccha, being often used like 3. aa, nirnir, and nisnis [qq. vv.], and like the Lat. dis, se, and the English a, dis, in, un &c.) [949,3]
  • in some cases it does not seem to modify the meaning of the simple word at all (cf. vi-jāmivijāmivijāmi, vi-jāmātṛvijāmātṛvijāmātṛ) [949,3]
  • it is also used to form proper names out of other proper names (e.g. vi-kokavikokavikoka, vi-pṛthuvipṛthuvipṛthu, vi-viṃśaviviṃśaviviṃśa). To save space such words are here mostly collected under one article [949,3]
