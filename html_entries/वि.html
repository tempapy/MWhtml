• vi m. (nom. vis or ves acc. vim gen. abl. ves; pl. nom. acc. vayas [acc. vīn, Bhaṭṭ.]; vibhis, vibhyas, vīnām) a bird (also applied to horses, arrows, and the Maruts), RV.; VS.; PañcavBr, (also occurring in later language). [949,3]
  • vi [cf. 1. vayas; Gk. οἰωνός for ὀϝιωνος; Lat. a-vis; accord. to some Germ. Ei; Angl.Sax. aég; Eng. egg.] [949,3]
  • vi n. an artificial word said to be = anna, ŚBr. [949,3]
  • vi ind. (prob. for an original dvi, meaning ‘in two parts’; and opp. to sam q.v.) apart, asunder, in different directions, to and fro, about, away, away from, off, without, RV. &c. &c. In RV. it appears also as a prep. with acc. denoting ‘through’ or ‘between’ (with ellipse of the verb, e.g. i, 181, 5; x, 86, 20 &c.) It is esp. used as a prefix to verbs or nouns and other parts of speech derived from verbs, to express ‘division’, ‘distinction’, ‘distribution’, ‘arrangement’, ‘order’, ‘opposition’, or ‘deliberation’ (cf. vi-√bhidvi-√bhidvi√bhid, -śiṣvi-śiṣśiṣ, -dhāvi-dhādhā, -rudhvi-rudhrudh, -carvi-carcar, with their nominal derivatives) [949,3]
  • sometimes it gives a meaning opposite to the idea contained in the simple root (e.g. √krī, ‘to buy’; vi-√krīvi-√krīvi√krī, ‘to sell’), or it intensifies that idea (e.g. √hiṃs, ‘to injure’; vi-√hiṃsvi-√hiṃsvi√hiṃs, ‘to injure severely’). The above 3. vi may also be used in forming compounds not immediately referable to verbs, in which cases it may express ‘difference’ (cf. 1. vi-lakṣaṇavi-lakṣaṇavilakṣaṇa), ‘change’ or ‘variety’ (cf. vi-citravi-citravicitra), ‘intensity’ (cf. vi-karālavi-karālavikarāla), ‘manifoldness’ (cf. vi-vidhavi-vidhavividha), ‘contrariety’ (cf. vi-lomavi-lomaviloma), ‘deviation from right’ (cf. vi-śīlavi-śīlaviśīla), ‘negation’ or ‘privation’ (cf. vi-kacchavi-kacchavikaccha, being often used like 3. a, nir, and nis [qq. vv.], and like the Lat. dis, se, and the English a, dis, in, un &c.) [949,3]
  • in some cases it does not seem to modify the meaning of the simple word at all (cf. vi-jāmivi-jāmivijāmi, vi-jāmātṛvi-jāmātṛvijāmātṛ) [949,3]
  • it is also used to form proper names out of other proper names (e.g. vi-kokavi-kokavikoka, vi-pṛthuvi-pṛthuvipṛthu, vi-viṃśavi-viṃśaviviṃśa). To save space such words are here mostly collected under one article [949,3]
  • but words having several subordinate compounds will be found s.v. [949,3]
