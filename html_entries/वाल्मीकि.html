• vālmīkivālmīki
  • m. (incorrectly vālmikivālmiki) N. of the celebrated author of the Rāmâyaṇarāmāyaṇa (so called, according to some, because when immersed in thought he allowed himself to be overrun with ants like an anthill; he was no doubt a Brāhman by birth and closely connected with the kings of Ayodhyāayodhyā; he collected the different songs and legendary tales relating to Rāma-candrarāmacandrarāmacandra and welded them into one continuous poem, to which later additions may have been made; he is said to have invented the Ślokaśloka metre, and probably the language and style of Indian epic poetry owe their definite form to him; according to one tradition he began life as a robber, but repenting be took himself to a hermitage on a hill in the district of Banda in Bundelkund, where he eventually received Sītāsītā, the wife of Rāmarāma, when banished by her husband; cf. IW. 314; 315 &c.), MBh.; R. &c. [946,3]
  • of a son of Garuḍagaruḍa, MBh. [946,3]
  • of a grammarian, TPrāt. [946,3]
  • N. of the authors of various works (the Yoga-vāsiṣṭhayogavāsiṣṭhayogavāsiṣṭha, the Adbhuta-rāmâyaṇaadbhutarāmāyaṇaadbhutarāmāyaṇa, and the Gaṅgāṣṭakagaṅgāṣṭaka), Cat. [946,3]
  • (with kavikavi) of the son of Rudra-maṇirudramaṇirudramaṇi Tri-pāṭhintripāṭhintripāṭhin and author of the Ramalêndu-prakāśaramalenduprakāśaramalenduprakāśa, ib. [946,3]
