• vedivedi—jājā
  • f. ‘altar-born’, epithet of Draupadīdraupadī, wife of the Pāṇḍupāṇḍu princes (the fee which Droṇadroṇa required for instructing the Pāṇḍupāṇḍu princes was that they should conquer Drupadadrupada, king of Pañcālapañcāla, who had insulted him; they therefore took him prisoner, and he, burning with resentment, undertook a sacrifice to procure a son who might avenge his defeat; two children were then born to him from the midst of the altar, out of the sacrificial fire, viz. a son Dhṛṣṭa-dyumnadhṛṣṭadyumnadhṛṣṭadyumna, and a daughter Draupadīdraupadī or Kṛṣṇākṛṣṇā, afterwards wife of the Pāṇḍavapāṇḍavas), L. [1017,3]
