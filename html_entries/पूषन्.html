• pūṣanpūṣan
  • m. (the aa not lengthened in the strong cases, but acc. °ṣāṇampūṣāṇam, in MārkP.) N. of a Vedic deity (originally connected with the sun, and therefore the surveyor of all things, and the conductor on journeys and on the way to the next world, often associated with Somasoma or the Moon as protector of the universe; he is, moreover, regarded as the keeper of flocks and herds and bringer of prosperity; in the Brāhmaṇabrāhmaṇas he is represented as having lost his teeth and feeding on a kind of gruel, whence he is called karambhādkarambhād; in later times he is one of the 12 Ādityaādityas and regent of the Nakṣatranakṣatra Revatīrevatī or Pauṣṇapauṣṇa; du. ‘Pūṣanpūṣan and Aryamanaryaman’ VP. Sch.) [645,1]
  • the sun, Kād.; Bālar. [645,1]
  • (?) growth, increase (cf. pūṣa-rātipūṣarātipūṣarāti) [645,1]
  • the earth, L. [645,1]
