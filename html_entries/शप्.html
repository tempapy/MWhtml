• 1. śapśap
    • (in gram.) a technical term used for the Vikaraṇa a (inserted between the root and terminations of the conjugational tenses in verbs of the 1st class; See vi-karaṇavikaraṇavikaraṇa, p. 954). [1052,1]
  • 2. śapśap ind.
    • a prefix implying assent or acceptance (as in śap-karotiśap, he admits or accepts), W. [1052,1]
  • 3. śapśap [Roots]
    • cl. 1. 4. Ā. P. (Dhātup. xxiii, 31; xxvi, 59) śapati, °teśapta or śapyati, °teśapta (the latter only in Bhaṭṭ.; pf. śaśāpa, śepeśepa aor. aśāpsīt, aśapta Gr. [2. pl. śāpta in TS. prob. w.r.]; fut. śaptāśapta, ib.; śapsyati, °teśapta, ib.; śapiṣye, MBh.; inf. śaptum or śapitum, ib.; ind.p. śapitvā, ib.; śaptvā Gr.),
    • to curse (mostly P. with acc.; in AV. v, 30, 3 Ā. with dat.), RV. &c. &c.
    • (P. Ā.) to swear an oath, utter an execration (sometimes with śapathamśapatha or °thān; also with anṛtamanṛta, to swear a false oath), RV. &c. &c.
    • (P. Ā.) to revile, scold, blame (acc., rarely dat.), Yājñ.; Kāv.; Pur.;
    • (Ā.; mc. also P.)
    • to curse one's self (followed by yadiyadiyad, ‘if’, i.e. to promise with an oath, vow or swear, ‘that one will not’ &c. or followed by dat. and rarely acc. of the person to whom and instr. of the object by which one swears; or followed by itiitiit, e.g. varuṇȇtivaruṇavaruṇāitiitīti, ‘to swear by the name of Varuṇa’, VS.), RV. &c. &c.
    • (Ā.) to adjure, supplicate, conjure any one (acc.) by (instr.), R.; Hariv. :
    • Caus. śāpayati (aor. aśīśapat), to adjure, conjure, exorcise (demons), AV.; AitBr.;
    • to cause any one (acc.) to swear by (instr.), Mn. viii, 113 (cf. śāpitaśāpita) :
    • Desid. śiśapsati, °teśapta Gr.:
    • Intens. śāśapyate, śāśapti, or śaṃśapyate, śaṃśapti, ib. [1052,1]
