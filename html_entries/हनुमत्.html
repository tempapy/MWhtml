  • hanu-mathanumathanumat
  • m. ‘having (large) jaws’, N. of a monkey-chief (one of the most celebrated of a host of semi-divine monkey-like beings, who, according to, R. i, 16, were created to become the allies of Rāma-candrarāmacandrarāmacandra in his war with Rāvaṇarāvaṇa; Hanumathanumat was held to be a son of Pavanapavana or Mārutamāruta, ‘the Wind’; and is fabled to have assumed any form at will, wielded rocks, removed mountains, mounted the air, seized the clouds, and rivalled Garuḍagaruḍa in swiftness of flight; according to other legends, Hanumathanumat was son of Śivaśiva; his mother's name was Añjanāañjanā q.v.; in modern times Hanumathanumat is a very common village god in the Dekhan, Central and Upper India cf. RTL. 220), MBh.; R. &c. [1288,1]
