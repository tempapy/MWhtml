  • ind. or (excluded, like the Lat. ve, from the first place in a sentence, and generally immediately following, rarely and only m.c. preceding, the word to which it refers), RV. &c. &c. (often used in disjunctive sentences; vā-vāvāvāvāvā, ‘either’ — ‘or’, ‘on the one side’ — ‘on the other’; nana vāvā — vāvā or nana — vāvā, ‘neither’ — ‘nor’; vāvā na-vānavānavā, ‘either not’ — ‘or’; yadiyadi vā-vāvāvāvāvā, ‘whether’ — ‘or’; in a sentence containing more than two members vāvā is nearly always repeated, although if a negative is in the first clause it need not be so repeated; vāvā is sometimes interchangeable with caca and apiapi, and is frequently combined with other particles, esp. with athaatha, athȏatho, utauta, kimkim, yadyad, yadiyadi q.v. [e.g. athaatha vāvā, ‘or else’]; it is also sometimes used as an expletive) [934,2]
