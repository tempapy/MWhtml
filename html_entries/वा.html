  • 1. vāvāvauū ind.
    • or (excluded, like the Lat. ve, from the first place in a sentence, and generally immediately following, rarely and only m.c. preceding, the word to which it refers), RV. &c. &c. (often used in disjunctive sentences; vā-vāvāvavāvāsvāvauū, ‘either’ — ‘or’, ‘on the one side’ — ‘on the other’; nana vāvāvauū — vāvāvauū or nana — vāvāvauū, ‘neither’ — ‘nor’; vāvāvauūvāsvas na-vānavānavanavāvauū, ‘either not’ — ‘or’; yadiyadiyad vā-vāvāvavāvāvauū, ‘whether’ — ‘or’; in a sentence containing more than two members vāvāvauū is nearly always repeated, although if a negative is in the first clause it need not be so repeated; vāvāvauū is sometimes interchangeable with caca and apiapiapīap, and is frequently combined with other particles, esp. with athaatha, athȏathoathaathāuū, utauta, kimkimkikiṃ, yadyad, yadiyadiyad, q.v. [e.g. athaatha vāvāvauū, ‘or else’]; it is also sometimes used as an expletive) [934,2]
    • either-or not, optionally, KātyŚr.; Mn. &c. (in gram. vāvāvauū is used in a rule to denote its being optional, e.g. Pāṇ. i, 2, 13; 35 &c.) [934,2]
    • as, like (= ivaiva), PārGṛ.; MBh. &c. [934,2]
    • just, even, indeed, very (= evaeva, laying stress on the preceding word), KātyŚr.; Kāv. [934,2]
    • but even if, even supposing (followed by a future), Pañc. v, 36/37 [934,2]
    • however, nevertheless, Bādar.; Bālar. [934,2]
    • (after a rel. or interr.) possibly, perhaps, I dare say, MBh.; Kāv. &c. (e.g. kiṃkiṃkimki vāvāvauū śakuntalȇtyśakuntalāitiitīti asyaa māturmātur ākhyāākhyā, ‘is his mother's name perhaps Śakuntalā?’, Śak. vii, 20/21; kokokukaska vāvāvauū or kekakāki vāvāvauū followed by a negative may in such cases be translated by ‘every one, all’ e.g. kekakāki vāvāvauū nana syuḥ paribhava-padaṃparibhavapadaparibhava niṣphalā̑ram-bha-yatnāḥniṣphalaniṣphalāaramārambha, ‘everybody whose efforts are fruitless is an object of contempt’, Megh. 55).
