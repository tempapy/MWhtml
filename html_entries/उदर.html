• udaraudara n.
    • (√dṝdṝ, Uṇ. v, 19; √ṛṛ, BRD. and, T.), the belly, abdomen, stomach, bowels, RV.; AV.; ŚBr.; Suśr.; MBh.; Kathās. &c. [184,2]
    • the womb, MBh.; VP.; Car. [184,2]
    • a cavity, hollow [184,2]
    • the interior or inside of anything (udareudara, inside, in the interior), Pañcat.; Śak.; Ragh.; Mṛcch. &c. [184,2]
    • enlargement of the abdomen (from dropsy or flatulence), any morbid abdominal affection (as of the liver, spleen &c. eight kinds are enumerated), Suśr. [184,2]
    • the thick part of anything (e.g. of the thumb), Suśr. Comm. on Yājñ. [184,2]
    • slaughter, Naiṣ. [184,2]
