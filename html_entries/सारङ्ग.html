• sāraṅga mf(ī)n. or sāraṅga (sometimes written śār°; either fr. sa-raṅgasa-raṅgasaraṅga, ‘having colour &c.’, or for śarā̑ṅga or sār°, ‘having a dappled body’), of a variegated colour, dappled, spotted (cf. kṛṣṇa-s°kṛṣṇa-s°kṛṣṇasāraṅga, lohita-s°lohita-s°lohitasāraṅga), AV.; Br.; MBh. [1208,3]
  • derived from the antelope called Sāraṅga, L. [1208,3]
  • sāraṅga m. (ifc. f(ā). ) a kind of spotted antelope, Kāv.; Kathās. &c. [1208,3]
  • sāraṅga m. N. of various birds (esp. a kind of Viṣkira or Pratuda [qq. vv.]; a peacock; the Indian cuckoo; the Rāja-haṉsarāja-haṃsarājahaṃsa; the Cātaka &c.), Kāv.; Suśr.; Car. &c. [1208,3]
  • a bee, BhP. [1208,3]
  • a kind of metre, Col. [1208,3]
  • (in music) a partic. Rāga, Saṃgīt. [1208,3]
  • (only L., ‘an elephant; lion; cloud; tree; umbrella; parasol; garment; clothes; hair; lotus; flower; conch-shell; sort of musical instrument; ornament; jewel; gold; a bow; sandal; camphor; the earth; light; night’) [1208,3]
  • N. of Śiva, MBh. [1208,3]
  • of Kāma-devakāma-devakāmadeva, L. [1208,3]
  • of the father of Bhaṭṭa Rāghava, Cat. [1208,3]
  • (with kavi) of a poet, ib. [1208,3]
