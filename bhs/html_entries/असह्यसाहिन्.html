• asahya-sāhin, adj. (only m. as epithet of a Buddha; = Pali asayaha-sāhin, also so used), prob. enduring (or sustaining, accomplishing) what cannot be endured (accom- plished, sc. by others); so the Pali comms. (e.g. on Pv ii.9.22) seem to take it; cf. Senart i n. 571, suggesting also the possibility conquering the unconquerable (but prefer- ring the other): Mv i.255.16; 256.1 °sāhi (n. sg.), of Buddha; in Mv iii.109.19 read buddhasya asahyasāhino (for text °tāyino), with same verse in Pali Therag. 536 (asayhasā°).
