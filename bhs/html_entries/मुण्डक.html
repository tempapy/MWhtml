• muṇḍaka, adj., (1) (= Pali id.; cf. muṇḍā, muṇ- ḍika) shaveling, pejorative ep. of Buddhist monks (with śramaṇaka, q.v. for citations; -ka contemptuous or imprecatory): Divy 13.15; 39.26; 574.2; (2) f. °ikā, ep. of gaṇḍī, gong: Av i.272.1; Feer funereal, for the dead (suggested by context; Feer cites Tib. as mjug med pa = tail-less; could this mean without a cord?).
