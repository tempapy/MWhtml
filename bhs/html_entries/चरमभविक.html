• carama-bhavika, adj. (Pali carimabhava plus ika), living in one's last existence, destined to be reborn no more: said of Bodhisattvas, LV 22.4; 85.11 etc.; Kv 68.23; 75.5; Mmk 324.7; Gv 438.23; (bodhisattvasyaikajātiprati- baddhasya, bound to only one further rebirth) caramabhavi- kasya vā Bbh 229.1; without noun, Mvy 7003; of the barber-disciple Upāli, Mv iii.179.8; said of Rāhula before ordination, Mv iii.263.11 (note in 13, said of the same, etehi eva skandhehi parinirvāpayitavyaṃ); said of śrāvaka- pratyekabuddha-yānika, Gv 141.5; of creatures (sattva) in general, not specifically classified, Divy 1.17; 174.1; 177.20; 264.2, 6; 331.5; 423.14; Av ii.135.8.
