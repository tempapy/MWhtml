• kiṇīkṛta, ppp. (Skt. kiṇa plus kṛ-), made callous, hardened, in fig. sense, of mentality (as in Eng. hard or callous): SP 319.8 (prose), for KN kilīkṛta-saṃjñā(ḥ), read with WT and their Ḱ kiṇī°, their fancy made callous; Laṅk 253.7, read kiṇīkṛta-rūkṣa-cetasāṃ (rākṣasānām iva), having minds calloused and harsh: one ms. kiṇī°, another kiṃnī°; text kiṃkanīkṛta-rukṣa°. Suzuki im- plausibly em. (a-)kiṃkarīkṛta°. [183,1]
