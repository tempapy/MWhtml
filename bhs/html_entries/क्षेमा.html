• Kṣemā, (1) (= Pali Khemā) n. of Śākyamuni's chief female disciple: Mv i.251.21 (agrā…śrāvikā); (2) n. of a daughter of King Prasenajit, who also became a nun in the Buddha's order: Av ii.46.7 ff.; in 50.9 she is, in fact, declared by Buddha to be agrā me…bhikṣu- ṇīnāṃ mama śrāvikāṇāṃ, so that she is actually identified with (1); her story, however (Av no. 79) is wholly different from the story of Khemā in Pali. (Mv gives no story about Kṣemā 1.). [201,2]
