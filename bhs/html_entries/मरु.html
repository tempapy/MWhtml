• maru, m. (in mgs. 1, 2 = Pali id.; cf. marut, maruta), (1) in the sense of Skt. Marut, a class of Vedic gods: indro …maruhi parivṛto Mv iii.267.16 (vs); in this sense rare; (2) very commonly, god, = deva, sura; oftenest in vss, but also in prose, e.g. nara-maru-kanyā- LV 82.15; often, as here, bracketed with nara or a synonym: SP 12.13; 30.9; 208.9; LV 12.13; 80.19 (read ca marusahasrair); 129.19; 370.14; Mv i.71.21; 72.11, 15, etc.; 90.18; 100.9; 113.15; 143.16; 268.15; ii.299.5; 328.5, etc.; Av ii.176.12; passim; (3) a kind of drum (not recorded anywhere): Mv i.259.11; ii.180.8; 410.7; iii.443.13; (4) n. of a future Buddha: Mv ii.355.10, but this is prob. a misprint for Meru, in the same passage iii.279.15.
