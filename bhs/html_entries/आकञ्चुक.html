• ākañcuka, nt. (v.l. aṣṭuñcaka, nt.; so Mironov), Mvy 8996; context indicates reference to something worn at the waist; Tib. rgya caṅ (or rgya lcaṅ), a kind of girdle, Jä., narrow, long money-bag made of net and securely joined to the sash, Das; Chin. belt or sash. The var. aṣṭuñ- caka seems probably a corruption. [086,1]
