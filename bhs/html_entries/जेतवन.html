• Jeta-vana, (normally) nt., rarely m. (= Pali id.), n. of the grove at Śrāvastī where Buddha often dwelt and preached: Mv i.4.13; 27.11; 29.13; 30.11; 31.9; 32.14; 33.8; 73.3; iii.224.11; Mvy 4112, °nam; LV 1.5; 4.14 (here masc., sarvāvantaṃ °vanaṃ, acc.); Divy 1.2 etc.; 80.12; Av i.13.5 etc.; also in vs, the two parts separated, Jeta- sāhvayaṃ vanaṃ LV 7.4 (vs), the grove called Jeta.
