• ? mūdhāma (m.; so text by em.; read rather mu- dhāma or mūḍhāma), acc. to context apparently idle or deluded religious longings, see s.v. 2 āma: ŚsP 485.(14--)15 (na ca bodhisattvo mahāsattvo) mūdhāmaṃ patati, text. But this is an em.; all mss. mūrdhānaṃ, and so in same locution 17, where ed. inconsistently reads with mss. Note to 15 suggests reading mudhāmaṃ and interprets by [Page436-b+ 71] vyartha-dharmābhilāṣaḥ. The ŚsP itself authorizes this interpretation of āma. It gives no hint as to how it read or interpreted the prior member; it might be Skt. and Pali mudhā, as ed. suggests, or mūḍha, deluded. It seems clear from the context that the mss. reading is corrupt and that the true text had a cpd. ending -āmaṃ (acc. ṣg.). [436,1]
