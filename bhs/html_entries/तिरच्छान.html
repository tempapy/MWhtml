• tiracchāna (m.; = Pali id.; cf. Skt. tiraścīna; ā for ī due to some analogy, somehow related to the stem-final of tiraccha, q.v. for other forms), animal (sub-human): °na-cārikāṃ Mv i.27.2, 4, journey to the animals (cf. tiryagyoniṣu 4); °na-yonīyaṃ iii.274.16, see s.v. tiric- chāna; °na-gata (= Pali id.), existing in the state of an animal: °gatāṃ Mv i.17.5 (acc. pl.); °gatāye (v.l. tiric- chāna- q.v.) iii.153.19.
