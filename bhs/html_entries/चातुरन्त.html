• cāturanta (cf. also prec.), nt. (?), the whole world (bounded by the four oceans): °taṃ vijitavān Mvy 6542. In Pali used as adj. f. with words for earth; also m. as applied to a king (so also in Skt., Kauṭ. Arth. Sham.^1 11.10 °to 'pi rājā), ruler of the whole earth, Sn 552 etc.; unrecorded in this gender and mg.; cf. Skt. caturantā, adj. or subst., said of the earth; see next, and s.v. catu- raṅga. [227,1]
