• kañcuka, m., or °kā, f. (doubtless = Skt. kañcuka, also fem. °kī, bodice; coat of armor; covering, sheath; skin of a snake), covering, downy coat (of flowers): Mv i.236.9--10 = 241.3--4 (vss, but defective in meter) tāni ca kara- pramuktā surabhīṇi pañcavarṇo (? °ṛṇe? sc. flowers, strewn over Dīpaṃkara), saṃsthihati puṣpakañcuko bhagavato lokanāthasya; Av ii.68.6 (dārako jāto…) divyasumanaḥkañcukayā (so read for °kañcikayā) prā- vṛtaḥ; 70.3 divyayā ca sumanasāṃ (mss. °syāṃ) kañcukayā prāvṛto; for kañcukā Speyer cites Tib. ral chuṅ, fine hair; Feer, couvert d'un duvet de sumanā (jasmine) divin.
