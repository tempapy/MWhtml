• dvyaṅgula-prajñā, adj. f. (= Pali d(u)vaṅgula- paññā), having (only) two-fingers-intelligence, said of women in derogation: °jñāye strīmātrāye tvaṃ nigṛhīto ti Mv iii.391.19. Acc. to comm. on Therīg. (67.1--5) the word refers to a feminine habit of taking grains of rice between two fingers to see if the rice is cooked enough. [275,2]
