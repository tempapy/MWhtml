• paranirmitavaśavartin (= Pali paranimmitava- savatti-n), n. of the highest class of kāmāvacara gods, see deva; lit. controlling (enjoyments) magically created by others; they rank higher than the nirmāṇarati, who create their own magical enjoyments: LV 46.21; 51.1; 150.4; 266.7; 396.15; 401.10; Mv i.33.3; 212.15; 229.16; 240.5; 263.17; 333.6; ii.16.4; 163.12; 348.18; 360.6; iii.223.11; Mvy 3083; Dharmas 127; Divy 68.13; 367.10; Av i.5.1; Suv 86.10; Sukh 41.2, etc.; sg., of the chief of this class (parallel with Sunirmita etc.), LV 44.10; 59.9; 361.13 (later he is referred to as Vaśavarti-, q.v., in 362.15); he is more regularly known as Vaśavartin, also (in vss) as simply Paranirmita, q.v. The interpretation of the name is correctly given by Childers, and supported by the paraphrase paranirmitodyukta. [319,1]
