• utsadana, nt. (= utsīdana; with mg. 1 cf. Skt. utsīdati; with mg. 2 cf. prec. and an-utsanna), (1) coming to grief, ruin: sthale utsadanam MSV i.49.10, 17 (in 2 above read utsatsyati for utpat°); (2) superfluous abun- dance, excess, in °na-dharmakaṃ (bhaktaṃ) Divy 307.23, 27, of food offered to monks and not fully used by them, of the nature of excess (pw 7.323 zum Wegräumen bestimmt). For Mvy 6781 utsādanam = Tib. dril ba (rolling, in sense of rubbing down, massage; so Skt.), Minayev and Mironov read utsadanam, apparently a faulty reading. [127,1]
