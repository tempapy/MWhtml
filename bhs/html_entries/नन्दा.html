• Nandā, (1) n. of the daughter of a village chief who gave food to the Bodhisattva when he broke his fast [Page290-a+ 71] after his long austerities; otherwise known as Sujātā, q.v.: Divy 392.12 (vs); in 392.9 (prose) associated in this act with Nandabalā (they seem to be regarded as sisters, dual grāmikaduhitryoḥ), q.v.; (2) n. of a lokadhātu: ŚsP 52.18; (3) n. of a rākṣasī: Māy 240.7; 241.13.
