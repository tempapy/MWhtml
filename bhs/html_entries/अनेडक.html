• aneḍaka, anelaka, f. °ikā, adj. (= Pali aneḷaka, °laka, of honey; cf. aneḍa, °la), pure; (a) of Buddha's voice, like aneḍa etc., Mv i.255.21 (vs) anelikāṃ (with girāṃ, in prec. line; misunderstood by Senart); on Mv iii.322.2, Senart anelakāye (of Buddha's speech), see s.v. aneḍa (read prob. anela-kalāye); (b) of honey: aneḍakam (madhu, or a synonym) Mvy 5729; Mv i.339.8; 340.13 (°ko); Av i.187.7; 243.1; Bbh 75.11; Karmav 45.14; anelakam Dbh 6.8; in Mv i.341.7 Senart aneḷakaṃ, but mss. °ḍakaṃ or °lakaṃ (of honey); see also nīḍaka.
