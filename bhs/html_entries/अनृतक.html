• anṛtaka, adj. or subst. m. (= anṛta; in vs, perhaps [Page037-a+ 71] m.c.), lying or a liar: Ud xx.19 satyenānṛtakaṃ jayet, one should conquer a liar (not le mensonge with Chakravarti; Pali alīkavādinaṃ; all the parallel words in the vs should also be taken as personal) by truth. [036,2]
