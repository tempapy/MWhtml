• ūrmi, m. or f. (in this sense AMg. ummi, see Ratnach., but not Skt. ūrmi, Pali ūmi, ummi), crowd, throng (of creatures): Mv i.222.14 = ii.24.22 samantormijātā, form- ing a crowd all around (here of gods); LV 173.13 (vs) naṭaraṅgasamā jagi-r-ūrmi-cuti, like an actor's stage-set is the passing of the crowd (of people) in the world; so if Lefm.'s text is right, but v.l. janmi for r-ūrmi, which (or rather janma) seems supported by Tib. skye.
