• laiśika, adj. or subst. nt. (from leśa), lit. concerning a trifling thing: Mvy 8377, the saṃghāvaśeṣa sin of accusing a monk of a pārājika sin on the basis of some trifling matter but afterwards repenting and confessing; cf. Prāt. 482.1 ff. (2 leśamātraṃ dharmam); Tib. bag tsam pa, trifle. Pali lesa interpreted only as trick, PTSD; but cf. Childers. The corresp. Pali is Vin. iii.168.1 ff. (with lesamattaṃ). [463,2]
