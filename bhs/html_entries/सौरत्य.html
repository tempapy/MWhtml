• sauratya, nt. (= Pali soracca; to sūrata, su°, plus -ya; also written soratya and erroneously saurabhya, q.v.), gentleness, mildness; regularly rendered by Tib. des pa, see sūrata: oftenest closely associated with kṣānti, Mvy 1115; SP 234.8 (all mss. °bhya, ed. em.); 236.9; Śikṣ 183.14; 326.12; KP 153.5 (sor°); Dbh 13.19; 37.11; Dbh.g. 51(77).20; Bbh 20.12; 143.27; Ud xix. 2; with sukhasaṃvāsa, q.v., Bbh 333.6; without either of these words, Mvy 6597; Jm 41.3; Bbh 368.26 (a-sau°); Śikṣ 46.14 (dharma-sau°, the gentleness of, according to, the law; not love for the law with Bendall and Rouse).
