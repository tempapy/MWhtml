• paryavanāha, m. (= Pali pariyonāha, or °naha DN i.246.16 = nīvaraṇa; cf. prec. items), growing over, covering [Page334-b+ 71] (only in bad senses): -timira-paṭala-paryav° SP 77.6, 11 (see paryavanaddha); in Gv 401.10 (because of the even and well-spaced teeth of the mahāpuruṣa, as he eats food: nābhūt…) paryavanāho vā (follows abhiṣyanda, q.v.; precedes abhiṣajjana or atisarjana, qq.v.), app. some pathological condition of the teeth, growing over, covering (with remnants of food ? with tartar, or decay, caries?). One might think of impacted teeth which fail to grow out, except that the whole list of non-existent defects seems modified by asyāhāraṃ paribhuñjānasya. [334,1]
