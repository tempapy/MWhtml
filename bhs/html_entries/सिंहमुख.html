• siṃhamukha, (nt.), lit. lion's mouth, (1) a spout or opening thru which water was conducted into or out of a pond: aṣṭottaraṃ ca °kha-śataṃ yena gandhodakaṃ praviśati tasyāḥ…puṣkiriṇyāḥ (ms., ed. em. puṣka°), aṣṭaśatam eva °khānāṃ yena punar eva tad vāri nirvahati RP 40.15 f.; (2) an instrument of torture (cf. ulkāmukha 1 and Pali rāhumukha, MN i.87.13 with comm. ii.58.28): °khaṃ vā hriyamāṇasya Śikṣ 182.4.
