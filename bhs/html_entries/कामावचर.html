• kāmāvacara, m. (rarely f., see below; = Pali id.; cf. -avàcara), dwelling in the kāma-dhātu or the realm of desire, ep. of a group (regularly six; ṣaṭ-kāmāvacarā devāḥ Dharmas 127; LV 290.2) of classes of gods (for list see s.v. deva): LV 30.5; 59.17 (they are kāmadhātu-stha 59.11); 83.6 (read °cara-deve°); 99.8; 273.16; 290.2; 300.3; 302.5 (here a list of some of their ‘overlords’); 369.12; 413.5; Divy 140.18; 203.11; 327.29; Mv i.159.6; 209.6 = ii.11.13; i.265.18; ii.2.8; iii.223.8; Mvy 6895; Suv 10.3; Mmk [Page177-b+ 71] 69.8; Gv 121.22; Bbh 295.23; Karmav 30.13; °vacarā apsaraso LV 353.9; seemingly used in a broader sense (as also in Pali) to refer to any beings ‘subject to desires’ Mvy 2154.
