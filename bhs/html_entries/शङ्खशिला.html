• śaṅkhaśilā (°śirā, once; = Pali saṅkhasilā, con- jectured PTSD to mean mother-of-pearl, a pure guess), a sort of precious or semi-precious stone, mentioned in rigmarole lists of gems and precious metals, and almost always placed between vaiḍūrya and pravāḍa (°la): LV 276.21 (Tib. makes it two items, duṅ = shell, and man śel = crystal, glass); SP 102.2 (prose; Tib. as in LV); 111.7 (vs; here Tib. only man śel); Mv ii.472.1; Divy 115.3; 229.6; 291.9; Av i.184.5; 201.2, etc.; RP 40.8; Dbh 18.26; spelled °śirā, prob. by error, Gv 164.8.
