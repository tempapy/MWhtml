• prasāda, m. (= Pali pasāda, see Childers; cf. a-pra° and prasanna, also abhipras°), (1) faith (in, with loc., so commonly in Pali): Mvy 6822-24 (no locs.); Mv iii. 434.20 Buddhe prasādasya; °da-jātaḥ (after abhiprasan- naḥ, q.v.) Divy 23.14; 75.20; = śraddhā, AbhidhK. La-VP. viii.158; (2) tranquillity of the four physical elements (dhātu) in sense-organs, as resulting in the sensitivity of the organs and so in sense-perception: Śikṣ 250.14 ff.; cf. 251.4, which by contrast with kṣobha agitation proves what prasāda means. Pali pasāda is so used, see PTSD; for Mrs. Rhys Davids' interpretation see her Dhs. transl. 174. [388,2]
