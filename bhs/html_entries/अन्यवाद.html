• anyavāda- (m. ?; cf. Pali aññavādaka, adj. and n. nt.), talking about something else, shuffling and evading the question (CPD); in anyavāda-viheṭhana, nt., (a monk's) causing annoyance (to the community) by doing this, v.l. in Kyoto ed. of Mvy for 8433, text ājñā-viheṭhanam (so Mironov without v.l.; Tib., Chin. and Jap. violation of commands; for the v.l. the Jap. editor gives the correct interpretation, as above); Prāt 505.1, text anyā-vāda (-viheṭhanāt), which Finot would em. to anya°; Chin ‘si un bhikṣu ennuie les autres en donnant des réponses à côté’ (= anya°). The corresp. Pali, Vin. iv. 36.3, 18, 37 etc., has añña-vādaka, which certainly means what is stated above (the context proves it clearly). Mvy ājñā- (-vāda) is evidently an ignorant and secondary hyper- Sktism, based on a MIndic form of anya-interpreted as = ājñā- (cf. § 2.15). It is, however, very curious that the text of Prāt reads anyā-with long stem-final ā! [042,1]
