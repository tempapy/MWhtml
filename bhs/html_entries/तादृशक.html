• tādṛśaka, (1) adj. (= Pali tādisaka), such, = Skt. tādṛśa: Mv iii.287.10 (prose; correl. with yādṛśaka, q.v.); (2) = BHS tādṛśa, and tāyin, q.v.; applied to a Bodhi- sattva or a Buddha; holy: (kausīdyaprāptas tada yo babhūva…) tvam eva so tādṛśako babhūva SP 28.2 (vs), addressed to the Bodhisattva Maitreya, (he who was indolent at that time…) has become none other than thou, the holy one (misunderstood by Burnouf and Kern); tvam eva so tādṛśako bhaviṣyasi anābhibhūto dvipadānam uttamaḥ SP 69.4 (vs), predicting Buddhahood in the future, thou thyself shalt become a Holy One (a Buddha), unconquered, best of bipeds.
