• aniyata, m., (1) with or sc. dharma (= Pali id., with dhamma), one of the two sorts of possible trans- gressions of monks which are undetermined as to type of offense and consequent punishment, i.e. of which the punishment depends on circumstances (Pali Vin. iii.187- 194; SBE 13.16 f.): dvāv aniyatau (sc. dharmau) Mvy 8382; dharmau Prāt 488.7; (2) aniyata-gotra, or aniyatai- katara-g°, see s.v. gotra (1). See also s.v. rāśi. [024,2]
