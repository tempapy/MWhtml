• adhigama, m., also (even after consonants) dhigama (q.v.), spiritual realization; attainment of religious goal; used in senses very close to this in Skt. (BR and pw), as well as in Pali; seems nearly equivalent to abhisamaya, q.v.: (bhagavantaṃ…) adhigamabuddhir yadyogināṃ yogābhisamayakāle samādhisukhe (so with Tib. for °mukhe, note in ed., and Suzuki) samāptānām adhigamo bhavati, tasya cādhigamād yogināṃ yogaśabdo nipātyate adhigamaneneti Laṅk 11.17--12.3, of whose (viz. Buddha's) yogins the will to spiritual-realization becomes spiritual- realization when they have attained perfection (samāpta) in the bliss of trance at the time of spiritual-realization (at- tainment, abhisamaya) of yoga; and thru realization (attainment) of him (tasya = bhagavataḥ) on the part of (his) yogins the word yoga is (rightly) applied, by reason of (this) realization (attainment, that comes thru it); yasyāṃ ca rātryāṃ dhigamo, Laṅk 144.10 = 324.5, referring to the night of Buddha's attainment of enlight- enment, cf. 142.17; Bbh 81.24; Mvy 134; punar bodhisat- tvaḥ adhigamajñāne sāradarśī bhavati, na śruta-cintā- dharmārtha-vijñāna-mātrake Bbh 257.16, referring to Mvy 1548 jñāna-pratisaraṇena bhavitavyam, na vijñāna- pratisaraṇena.
