• praṇamati (?), praṇamayati (cf. Skt. id.), (1) ger. praṇamayya, saluting (in non-caus. mg.): Bhagavantaṃ Divy 463.22; but MSV i.71.16, same passage, añjaliṃ praṇamya; (2) añjaliṃ praṇamya, making an añjali: SP 60.2 (prose; so all mss., ed. em. praṇāmya); and so all Nep. mss. and WT, SP 100.5 (prose), KN with Kashgar rec. praṇāmayitvā; añjaliṃ praṇamayya Av i.347.4; ii.3.9. Is praṇamya ger. of praṇamati, or of praṇamayati? See praṇāmayati, which like Pali paṇāmeti is so used with añjaliṃ; is the literal mg. bends, or rather extends, holds out? [359,2]
