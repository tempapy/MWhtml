• vipāka, m. (Skt. and Pali id.), maturation, coming to fruition (of action): °ka-stha, of Buddhas, = vaipākika, q.v.; °ka-maheśākhya, see this; °ka-phalam Mvy 2276, one of the 5 phala, q.v. (2), expl. by iṣṭahetutvena in Sūtrāl. xvii.31 comm., in Bbh 102.19--20 by akuśalānāṃ dharmāṇām apāyeṣu vipāko vipacyate, kuśala-sāsravāṇāṃ sugatau; -karma-samādāna-hetuso-vipākaso-(for °śo; according to causes and results)-jñāna- LV 433.6; dattvā ca vipākāpratikāṅkṣī LV 181.2, not looking for ‘maturation’ i.e. recompense, reward (for the gift.)
