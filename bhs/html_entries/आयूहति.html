• āyūhati (= Pali id.), cf. (an)āyūha, exerts oneself, strives (for, acc.): Gv 69.24 (bodhisattvavimokṣam; similarly in the rest); 80.24, 25; 83.12; 199.24 (in all these followed by corresp. form of niryūhati, q.v.); 222.15 °hantī, followed by viyūhantī; passive, Laṅk 115.13 āyūhyamānaṃ nāyūhyate, niryūhyamānaṃ na niryūhyate, ata etasmāt kāraṇān…sarvadharmā āyūhaniryūhavi- [Page102-b+ 71] gatāḥ, being striven after it is not attained (or [successfully] striven after), being renounced it is not got rid of; that is why all states of existence are without either acquisition or riddance (Suzuki, neither taking birth nor…going out).
