• Saṃjaya, (1) (= Pali id.), n. of a king of the Śibis, father of Viśvaṃtara: Jm 52.3 ff.; (2) n. of a minister (amātya), former incarnation of the Buddha: Mv i.93.4 (gave his heart to a piśāca in exchange for recitation of a pious gāthā); (3) n. of a son of Kubera: Māy 230.21 (note that Saṃjaya is read by many mss. for Saṃjñāya, q.v., the yakṣa leader; so Burnouf, Introd. 532, citing from Suv).
