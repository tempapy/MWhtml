• pṛṣṭhībhavati (cf. parā-, vi-p°, and avapṛṣṭhī- kṛta; Pali vipiṭṭhi-katvā(na) clearly means turning one's back on worldly things, evils, Sn 67, 362, substantially [Page354-a+ 71] abandoning; and Pali piṭṭhito karoti is used in the lit. sense, turns one's back on, Jāt. i.71.23), (1) in Divy 326.9 pṛṣṭhibhūtaḥ, and in 11 avapṛṣṭhīkṛtaḥ, both seem to have the mg. suggested by Pali vipiṭṭhikatvā(na), above: (made) averse, turned away (from worldly things); see the passage cited s.v. Maitrīya. In Mvy 2590, also, pṛṣṭhī- bhavati may have this mg., becomes averse (followed by kelāyita, q.v., in a chapter headed ‘synonyms of nisṛjā’); but the two Tib. renderings are not clear; pw 7.359 under- stands this as belonging to (2); of course pw's assumption that the ‘correct reading’ is piṣṭī° is wrong; (2) becomes depressed (= vipṛṣṭhībhavati, q.v.), orig. doubtless turns one's back as a sign of unhappiness: cittaṃ nāvalīyate na saṃlīyate na pṛṣṭhībhavati AsP 320.17 (prose).
