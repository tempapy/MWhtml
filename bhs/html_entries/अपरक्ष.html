• ? aparakṣa, adj., (cf. AMg. aparaccha; for Skt. a-parokṣa; ending influenced by pratyakṣa; [stealing] in the presence of the owner or others when inattentive, Ratnach.; cf. also Sheth s.v. aparaccha), manifest, ep. of dhana, wealth: Gv 407.2 (vs) mā te (')parakṣeṣu dhaneṣv abhidhyā, have no covetousness for property in the presence of the owner. But perhaps better parakṣeṣu, when (the owner) is absent, tho this is not recorded. In any case a MIndic form, for (a-)parokṣa, q.v. [044,1]
