• saṃpracalati (cf. Skt. pra-cal-), (1) ppp., in (-saṃ- gīti)-saṃpracalite (sc. mahāvimāne), stirred, i.e. made resonant with song: LV 10.22 (Tib. glu dbyaṅs, = saṃgīti, len pa, received, taken); (2) otherwise noted only after corresp. forms of calati and pracalati, in same or similar passages to those of saṃpragarjati, q.v. (subject loka- dhātu or pṛthivī), shakes, quakes: °lati Divy 158.8; °litaḥ, °litā, ppp., Mvy 3006; Samādh 19.6; Divy 250.22. In Mmk 74.6 saṃpracalitāḥ, ep. of certain mantras, is corrupt; read samaya- with some form of calita, as proved by Tib., cited Lalou Iconographie, 96.2, as dam chig (read dam tshig) gyos pa rnams kyi las (cf. samayaḥ Mvy 1438 = Tib. sṅags kyi skabs su dam tshig, perh. conventional regulation in regard to the occasion of a mantra). The prec. epithet in Mmk, printed samayagrastāḥ, is prob. to be read samayāgra-(sthāḥ ?); Tib., l.c., dam chig (read tshig; = samaya) ñams pa (height). [577,1]
