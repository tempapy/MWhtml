• jalpa, m., or better jalpā (= Pali jappā, desire), desire: in Mv iii.284.19 (vs) mss. sarvāṃ prahāya bhavalo- bhajalpaṃ, not to be emended; same vs in Pali SN i.123.8 chetvāna (v.l. hitvāna) sabbaṃ bhavalobhajappaṃ. It is tempting to interpret in the same way such passages as Laṅk 186.8, 9 (vss) jalpaprapañcābhiratā hi bālās… jalpo hi traidhātukaduḥkhayonis, fools delight in jalpa and idle fancies (? see prapañca)…for jalpa is the source of the misery of the universe. This seems more naturally to mean desire than (idle) talk; but acc. to Suzuki's Index Tib. (smra ba) supports the latter; this might however only mean that Tib. knew the regular Skt. jalpa and had lost the tradition of the old word represented by Pali jappā. [239,2]
