• saṃghāvaśeṣa, adj., m. with or sc. dharma, or f. with āpatti (= Pali saṃghādisesa, which Lévi, see below believes Pktic for *saṃghāti°), one of a group of 13 (so also Pali) monkish offenses requiring suspension, but not expulsion, from the order (contrast pārājika, pātayantika), perh. permitting a remainder of (association with) the order (Lévi, JA Nov.-Dec. 1912, 504); but acc. to SBE 20.35 n. 2, (offences) which can be done away, but only by the saṃgha (see niravaśeṣa): trayodaśa °ṣāḥ (sc. dharmāḥ) Mvy 8359 (the 13 items listed 8369--81); trayodaśa °ṣā dharmāḥ Prāt 479.3; listed and described in the following, each item called °ṣaḥ; °ṣām āpattim MSV iii.11.16 etc. [549,2]
