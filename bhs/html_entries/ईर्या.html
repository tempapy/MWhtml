• īryā or iryā (chiefly the latter, semi-MIndic, has been noted; = Pali and AMg. iriyā) = the much com- moner īryā-patha (or iryā°), deportment, behavior, par- ticularly good, dignified, proper deportment: Mv i.302.10 iryaṃ (mss., Senart īryāṃ) paśyitvā (of a Pratyekabuddha); iii.60.9 (kalyāṇā) punar iyaṃ pravrajitasya iryā (Senart īryā); 92.10 iryā (Senart īryā); LV 115.2 (vs) teṣa (gods) yathā ca iryā; 116.7 (vs) yatha irya netra vimalāprabha, since he possesses proper deportment and an eye of pure splendor (so better than taking irya-netra as cpd. with Foucaux); 330.12 (vs) īryāṃ (no v.l. in mss.) caryāṃ ca prekṣate, he (Bodhisattva) regards (considers duly) proper deportment and conduct; MSV ii.186.10 (prose) tayā īryayā caryayā.
