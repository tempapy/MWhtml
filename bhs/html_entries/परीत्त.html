• 1 parītta, adj. (= paritta, q.v.; cf. also parīttaka, parīttaśubha, parīttābha), small, limited, restricted, minor; very common: Mvy 1918; SP 211.10; LV 402.3, 4; 438.10; Mv i.316.2; ii.49.17; Av i.329.10; Suv 6.5; 9.12; Divy 498.12; 504.12; Śikṣ 54.3; Dbh 26.21; Bbh 125.6; Sukh 25.4; mā parīttamanām (! for °manas) utpā- daya Gv 528.19, don't be faint-hearted (‘small-minded’); a-parītta, not small, Mv ii.44.8. [333,1]
  • 2 parītta (nt.; = Pali 2 paritta, see PTSD, or °ttā), protection, safeguard, refuge (from Skt. pari-trā-): suparīt- taṃ (v.l. °ītaṃ) bhāvayiṣyati Mv ii.145.8, he (the Bodhi- sattva) will produce, effect, a good safeguard (refuge, against the ills of life); otherwise, but very implausibly, Senart. The ī may be due to influence of the very common homo- nym, 1 parītta; but, of course, the ī of the preverb pari- is otherwise capable of lengthening in Skt. [333,1]
