• sāntarottara, nt. (= Pali santaruttara), the two inner garments (antarvāsas and uttarāsaṅga, both Skt.) worn by monks, to accept more than which is a naiḥsargika pāyattika offense under the circumstances of the 7th nai° pāy° (cf. Pali Vin. iii.214.20): Mvy 8391; MSV ii.48.15 (Corr.). As adj., possibly read sāntarottara(paṭikā-) for sāntarocchada° Śikṣ 208.11; see s.v. ucchada (2). A monk is forbidden to wear only these two (and discard the saṃghāṭī): MSV ii.94.8 (cf. Vin. i.298.5). [591,1]
