• bhaikṣāka (to Skt. and BHS bhikṣāka plus -a), as adj., relating to a mendicant: Buddhacarita xii.46 °kaṃ liṅgam āśritaḥ, and as n. mendicancy, ib. x.23; xiii.10; in BHS bhaikṣāka-kula, nt., a family that (regularly) gives almsfood (to a monk), is to be read for bhaikṣākulaṃ Divy 263.2 (yad apy asmākam ekaṃ °kulaṃ, tad api śramaṇo Gautamo 'nvāvartayati, said by a non-Buddhist), for bhaikṣyāka-kula MSV iv.226.17, and for bhekṣāka-kula KP 114.2, 5, where a monk is warned not to cultivate such relations.
