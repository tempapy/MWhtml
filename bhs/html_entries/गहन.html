• gahana (nt., or adj.), is sometimes, apparently, in- volved in word-plays with grahaṇa, q.v. (on the basis of MIndic pronunciation of the latter). In Śikṣ 286.1 (aśaṭhatā śaṭheṣu,) amāyāvitā gahanacariteṣu (this is the clear intention of the ms., see ed. note, and must be read), kṛtajñatā akṛtajñeṣu, etc., the Tib. is said to render guilelessness among the guileful, which is supported by the parallel phrases surrounding this. The transl. of Bendall and Rouse renders gahana-carita overreaching; it seems more likely to mean of obscure, hidden (or perhaps tangled) conduct, and so tricky.
