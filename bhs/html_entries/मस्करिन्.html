• Maskarin (once Mās°) Gośālīputra (°liputra, °likāputra, Gośalīp°), also Gośālin (= Pali Makkhali Gosāla, said to be founder of the Ājīvakas), n. of one of the six heretical teachers of Buddha's day (see s.v. Pūraṇa Kāśyapa); named with the others: Mvy 3546 (M° Gośalīputra, so also Mironov); Divy 143.11; Av i.231.4 (Māskarī, n. sg., Gośā°); Mv i.253.11 Maskarisya (ms. °ṣya) Gośāliputrasya; 256.20 °rī Gośālī (n. sg.); iii.383.15 °rī Gosālikāputro (mss. °līka°; v.l. Gośā°). [420,2]
