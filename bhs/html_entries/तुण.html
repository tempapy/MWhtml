• tuṇa (m. or nt.; cf. tūṇa, tuṇava; AMg. tuṇā [tuṇa?], an unidentified mus. instrument), some musical instru- ment: in lists, tuṇa-paṇava-mṛdaṅgāṃ LV 80.5; tuṇa- veṇu-(text vaiṇu-)-ravaiḥ 173.9; tuṇa-vīṇā-sughoṣakādi- bhiḥ 177.14. Tib. confused; it may intend this word by rgyud gcig (pa) in the first two (see under tuṇava), but if so the order varies from our text; on 177.14 this Tib. word does not occur, and tuṇa may be gliṅ bu (otherwise = veṇu or vaṃśa), altho the order suggests sgra sñan, but this elsewhere = sughoṣaka. In spite of Tib., which suggests a stringed (or in 177.14 a wind?) instrument, perhaps really a kind of drum. [255,1]
