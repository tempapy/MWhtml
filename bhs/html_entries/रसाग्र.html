• rasāgra (only noted Mv ii.478.18, perhaps hapl. for rasarasāgra), rasarasāgra, subst. and adj. Bhvr., also adj. rasarasāgrin, and subst. °sāgratā, (state of, posses- sing) excellent taste or flavor, lit…best taste of tastes (the Pali equiv. is rasagga-saggi, adj., or °ggi-tā, not °ta as stated PTSD; the meaning of the -s-in -saggi is obscure; Skt. sa-?); applied both to food and to persons: of food, āhāro siddho…na me kadācid edṛśo rasāgro…svādita- pūrvo Mv ii.478.18 (rasāgro could be subst. or Bhvr. adj.), I have never before tasted (food containing?) such excellent flavor; (divya-, or divyai) rasarasāgropeta, adj. with āhāra, or once with piṣṭaka, Kv 30.18 (misprinted rasārasā°); 44.2--3, 11; 48.2; 54.4 (misprinted rasarāsogrope°); 56.3; 60.23 (in all these rasarasāgra must be subst.); adj. Bhvr., said of the Bodhisattva by Gopā, hā mama rasārasāgrā LV 234.21 (vs; mss. rasa°, Lefm. em. m.c.); rasarasāgriṇaḥ, n. pl., said of Buddhas, among the 32 lakṣaṇa, Mv ii.306.4 [Page454-a+ 71] (by em. but sound); °sāgra-tā, abstr., Mvy 245 etc., 21st of the 32 lakṣaṇa, q.v.
