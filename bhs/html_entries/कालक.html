• kālaka, (1) adj. (= Pali kāḷaka; Skt. very rarely for kāla, see BR s.v.), black: Mvy 8397 śuddhaka-kāla- kānām (Kyōtō text °nam; Mironov °nām), sc. eḍakalom- nām (same passage in Pali suddha-kāḷakānaṃ, Vin. iii.225.28); varṇena te kālaka tatra bhonti SP 94.7 (vs; here ka could be pejorative, or m.c.); kālako vata bhoḥ śramaṇo gautamaḥ LV 255.4; 256.7 (prose; could be pejorative ka); (2) black spot on a garment, and also moral defilement (= Pali kāḷaka, id.), see s.vv. apagata- kālaka and sarvakālaka; (3) (m.?) blackhead, pimple on skin (= Skt. Lex., perh. once lit., id.; Pali kāḷaka; = BHS 1 kāla 1, q.v., rare): vyapagata-tila-kālaka-gātra, one of the anuvyañjana, Mironov's Mvy for Kyōtō °kāla° 309, and similarly LV 107.5; Mv ii.43.13; (4) (cf. 1 Kāla 3 and Kālika) n. of a nāga: Mvy 3327 (here definitely distinguished as a ‘commoner’ of the nāgas from Kāla, who is a ‘nāga-king’); Māy 221.24; 247.3; however, in Māy 247.13 dvau Kālakau nāgarājānau.
