Śilukṣa, n. of a place (adhiṣṭhāna, nigama) where Trapuṣa and Bhallika built a stūpa for the stones used by Buddha in washing a robe: Mv iii.310.15 (adhiṣṭhāna; here mss. śilujvaṃ, śilahvaṃ); 313.2 (śilukṣa-nāma- nigama); but in the next line, adyāpi gandhārarājye adhiṣṭhānaṃ śilā-nāmena jñāyati; cf. śilahvaṃ of one ms. above; I suspect that Śilāhvaya should be read, having the name of Stone(s).
