• ulkāmukha, (1) (nt., = Pali ukkāmukha) a (gold- smith's) smelting-pot or furnace: LV 405.14 -niṣka ulkāmu- khaprakṛṣṭo; Sukh 3.5 (-niṣko…) ulkāmukhe (so v.l., better than °khena text) saṃpraveśya supariṇiṣṭhitaḥ; Śikṣ 182.3 °khaṃ vā hriyamāṇasya (as a form of torture); (2) nt., in Mvy 6900, following dīpaḥ, ulkā, followed by pradīpaḥ, = Tib. skar mda-ḥi gdoṅ ṅam snaṅ (read prob. sna with Das), which acc. to Das = one having either his face or his nose (sna; snaṅ = light) glowing as a meteor; a demon (so Skt.); a meteor-mouthed arrow; a kind of fire- arm; (3) m. (= Pali Okkāmukha), n. of a son of King Sujāta Ikṣvāku: Mv i.348.12; 352.9; and of a later descen- dant, 352.12. [148,2]
