• vyantī-karoti, rarely vyanti° (cf. rare Vedic vyanta, remote; = Pali vyanti-k°; cf. next), puts an end to: °roti Mvy 7044; AsP 343.19, see s.v. chorayati (8); °kṛta Mvy 2550; 7043 (a-vya°); yāva sānaṃ taṃ pāpakaṃ karma °taṃ na bhavati Mv i.18.14; 20.(4--)5 (most mss. here vyanti°); 21.11 (here na om. in mss., Senart transp. before taṃ), until that evil action of theirs is ended (i.e. its effect exhausted); avidyāye prahīṇatvāt tṛṣṇāye °kṛtatvāt Mv iii.66.2, because ignorance is got rid of and thirst ended. Tib. usually renders by forms containing byaṅ ba(r), purify, which would be a possible rendering in Mv i.18.14, but surely cannot be the lit. mg. [514,2]
