• vi-paḍumaka, or vipaṭumaka, nt., a corpse destroyed by worms (so Tib. on Mvy 1158): Gv 157.16 vipaṭ°; -saṃjñā, contemplation of the notion of such a corpse, one of the aśubha-bhāvanā (q.v.) or -saṃjñā: Mvy 1158 vipaḍ°; Bhīk 27a.2 vipaṭ°. Also vipaṭumuka, MSV iii.54.11; vipaṭmaka, Śikṣ 211.1; and (corruptly) vipadāka- ŚsP 59.4; niruttamaka-(!) ŚsP 1258.6. See s.v. paḍuma(ka). Cf. Pali puḷuvakaṃ, Vism i.110.31 (worm-foul, Pe Maung Tin). [491,1]
