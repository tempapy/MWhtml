• saṃvṛta, ppp. (of Skt. saṃ-vṛ-), (1) of a door, narrow (so Burnouf, Chin., and Tib. dog; Kern, shut): idaṃ khalu niveśanam ekapraveśaṃ saṃvṛta-dvāram eva SP 73.3; (2) (= Pali saṃvuta) restrained, controlled: °ta āyatanaiḥ Mvy 399 = Tib. skye mched rnams bsdams pa (to sdom pa, used for saṃvara, q.v.), controlled as to the senses (āyatana 5); not by the senses; we might expect loc., but the instr. may be influenced by the surrounding phrases (visaṃyukto dhātubhiḥ, pracchinno granthaiḥ, vimuktaḥ paridāghaiḥ, etc.) where it seems more appropriate.
