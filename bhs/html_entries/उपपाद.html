• upapāda (m.; in this sense not in Skt.; cf. upapāduka, aupa°; corresp. to Pali upapāta, in cutūpapāta or cutup° Vin. iii.4.38; SN iv.59.13; and AMg. uvavāa, °vāya, birth, derived by Ratnach. from upapāta, but could as well represent upapāda; cf. also upapatti, same mg., from upa-pad-yate), birth, incarnation: SP 24.2 (vs) darśeti sattvāna cyutopapādam (= cyuty-upa°, Pali cutūpapāta, above; dvandva, fall from one existence and rebirth in another); Mv ii.359.12 (vs) cyutopapādaṃ jānāti sarva- sattvāna nāyako. Knowledge of this constitutes one of the three vidyā (see traividya) and is a product of divya- cakṣus as one of the abhijñā, AbhidhK. LaV-P. vii.106. [139,1]
