• katira, adj. (= katara, q.v., but like it used in sense of katama; in form based on atima, q.v. by analogy [Page166-b+ 71] of tara: tama forms), which (of many)?: Suv 60.4 (vs; read with mss., after 3 paśyāhi…eta dharmān), katiro 'tra sattvas tatha pudgalo vā, which among these (dharmas) is the creature, or the person too? Nobel em. katir atra; kati is, however, not used as sg., and all mss. read -ro. [166,1]
