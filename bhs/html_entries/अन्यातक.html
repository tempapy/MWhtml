• anyātaka, adj. (§ 2.15; = Pali aññāta, °taka; AMg. aṇṇāya; from Skt. ajñāta, in MIndic stranger), belonging to another or others; the word was unhistorically Sktized by association with anya, other: Mv i.346.11 ff. (repeatedly), e.g. adinnam anyātakaṃ śālim, rice belonging to others and not given (by them). In MSV iv. 107.11 spelled anyā- dakam (ed. em. ajñātakaṃ); Tib. (Dutt, 108 n. 1) gzan gi, of another (anya!). [042,1]
