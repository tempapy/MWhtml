• anyonya, adj. (generally used as in Skt.; but also) various, different (= anyamanya, 2, and Pali aññamañña); replaces anyamanya in prose of most texts: SP 132.3 (prose) na santi…trīṇi yānāni, kevalam anyonya- [Page042-b+ 71] caritāḥ sattvāḥ, there are not three vehicles, only creatures with different courses of action; 134.6 (prose) anyonya- dravyasaṃyuktāṃ, mixed with various substances; 137.13 (prose); 221.7 (prose) anyonyāsu lokadhātuṣu; 242.8 (prose) °nyeṣu buddhakṣetreṣu…°nya-nāmadheyās (Bhvr., of various names); Mv i.62.8 (vs) sugatānāṃ anyonya- nāmadheyānāṃ (as in SP 242.8); in the prose corresp. to this, i.58.16, we should surely read nānā-nāma° (mss. corruptly nănāṃ-nāma°; one ms. by haplogr. nā-nāma°; Senart em. anyonya-nāma°).
