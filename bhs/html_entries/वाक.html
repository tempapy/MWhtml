• vāka (= Pali id., AMg. vāga; for *vakka = Skt. valka; § 3.2), bark: certainly contained in reading of mss. (see Senart i n. 558) Mv i.236.1 cirā-vākodbhava, in what was orig. a vs, but is so corrupt in tradition that it cannot be restored in default of a parallel; if cirā-was orig. read, it stands for cīrā or cīra-, m.c.; ep. of garments worn by Dīpaṃkara; originating from (made of) bast and bark.
