• pratijñottaraka, adj. (pratijñā-ut°?), Mvy 1796, perh. surpassing one's promise; exceeding expectations; context indicates something like zealous, energetic. Tib. kha ḥog tu lus ḥkhrus pa, bathing the body with face down- ward (!); but Chin. bathing the body with lower part covered, that is, observing propriety even when alone (a famous Confucian precept).
