• ojohāra, adj. and subst. (f. °rī), lit. strength-robber; ep. of rākṣasas: Divy 295.6 rākṣasa eva ojohāra ihāgac- chati; of yakṣas, Jm 41.14 ojohārāḥ pañca yakṣāḥ; subst. m. and f., in a list of evil supernatural beings, ojohāro vaujohārī vā Laṅk 261.11; 262.13. Cf. next two. See also s.v. 2 -hāra; in both Divy and Jm the mg. to rob the strength (of people) would be possible. [159,1]
