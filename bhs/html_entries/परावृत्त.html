• parāvṛtta, ppp., and parāvṛtti, n. act. (both Skt.), reversed, and reversion, revulsion, of the basic mentality (esp. called āśraya, q.v., in Suzuki, Studies, 390 with refs.); technically of the fundamental change in mental attitude which is taught as necessary to knowledge of the true doctrine: parāvṛttāśraya (text °vṛtā°) Laṅk 9.11; cf. 10.14; parāvṛtty-āśraya, having the basis (of conscious- ness) characterized by (this) revulsion 93.3 (or read °vṛttāśra° ?); parāvṛtta, e.g. 284.11; °tti 10.14; for others see Suzuki's Index and Glossary; also in AbhidhK, see LaV-P's Index; Sūtrāl. ix.12--17 (Lévi *20, *24).
