• sarita (ppp. to Skt. sarati?), (1) adj., fluent, f. saritā, in a list of epithets of Buddha's voice or speech: Mvy 496; Sūtrāl. xii.9, comm.; Chin. in constant flow (Lévi, note to text, p. 80); Tib. on Mvy rgyun chags pa, made continuous; (2) adj., perhaps passing constantly or rapidly away, of worldly joys: Ud iii.5 saritāni vai snehitāni vai saumanasyāni bhavanti jantunaḥ (same word in Pali same vs, Dhp. 341; PTSD gone, set in motion; comm. anusaṭa, payāta; the former is doubtfully interpreted as diffuse, extravagant, CPD); (3) subst., m. or nt., drinking- cup: saritena (or śar°) Bhīk 29a.1, 5; see s.v. kāyaban- dhana; Tib. cited as phor bu, drinking cup, which renders BHS (and Skt.) sarakam Mvy 8956.
