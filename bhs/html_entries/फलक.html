• phalaka, (1) m., °kaḥ Mvy 9192 = Tib. sgrog guḥi rten ma, holder for a strap; perhaps a fastener, something like a button, to be affixed to a monk's robe, and to which a strap is fastened; I believe phalaka has this mg. in Pali gaṇṭhika-pha° pāsaka-pha° Vin. ii.136.38; 137.1, 3 (not a kind of cloth, perhaps made of leaves, as assumed SBE 17.246); so Chin. on Mvy, leather bag or pocket with button(s); (2) nt., in ŚsP 1430.9, cited approximately in Śikṣ 210.5 as: gāṃ hatvā tīkṣṇena śastreṇa catvāri phalakāni kṛtvā, acc. to Bendall and Rouse four quarters (of the animal), which seems implausible; possibly four leather bags (of the hide)? cf. Chin. cited above; or belts? (this mg. given for AMg. phalaga in Ratnach.); (3) nt., grain (of sand): vālikā- phalakāni Gv 134.20 f.; (4) see s.v. Halaka; (5) see also phalaha. (In Divy 316.26 phalaka may mean bark, as in Skt., used as material for garments.) [396,1]
