• abhiramya (Pali allegedly abhiramma, Maung Tin, see CPD; Ap. ahiramma, Jacobi, Bhav.; see an-, nir- abhi°), pleasing: LV 187.10--11 sarvamanāpāni copa- saṃhartavyāni viṣayābhiramyāṇi; Tib. yul ñams dgaḥ ba thams cad ni sbyor cig, apparently making viṣaya (yul) subject of upasaṃhartavyāni, and not translating abhi°. The sense must apparently be all-mind-charming and pleasing objects of sense are to be collected (prepared, for the prince to enjoy); yathābhiramyam (= Pali yathā- bhirantam), adv., as long as is agreeable: MSV ii.87.19.
