• nairmita-nairmāṇika (-vyapetaṃ, sc. Tathāgatam), (cf. prec., and s.v. nirmita), (who transcends) the (forms) connected with the magically transformed and with magic transformation: Laṅk 11.17 (prose). Such seems to be the lit. mg., but I do not understand what distinction may have been felt between the two, and Suzuki gives no real help. [312,2]
