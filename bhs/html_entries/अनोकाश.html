• anokāśa, adj. Bhvr. (= Pali anokāsa; for an-ava- kāśa), having no sufficient space, crowded: Mv i.175.12 °śā kṛtā svargā, the heavenly worlds are made crowded (by Buddha's appearance, which leads many to heaven). Most mss. read anākāśā, but there can be little doubt that Senart's text is correct. [037,1]
