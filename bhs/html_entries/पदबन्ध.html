• pada-bandha, m. (= pāda-b°, q.v.), a particular technique of holding or wielding (the bow), viz. apparently [Page317-b+ 71] by using the foot in some way; Tib. on LV renders gom stabs, step-manner (making a step?); in a list of arts to be mastered by a prince, and associated with muṣṭi- bandha, q.v.: LV 156.12; Divy 100.12; 442.7. On the Tib. see s.v. pāda-b°. [317,1]
