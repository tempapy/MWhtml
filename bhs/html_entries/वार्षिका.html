• vārṣikā (1) Mv i.19.11, taken by Senart as subst., garment for the rainy season; he reads (eraka) vārṣikā vā kārāpitā cīrakavārṣikā kārāpitā vā; but see eraka; it seems more likely that eraka and cīraka (perhaps read °kā in both) are separate words, substantives, and vārṣikā adj., grass-coverlets and bark- (or cloth-)strip-garments for the rainy season; (2) (= Pali vassikā; cf. prec., also var- ṣaka, °kī, varṣikā, and next), a kind of jasmine: Mv i.249.13 (prose, in cpd., list of flowers); ii.116.10 (text °kāṃ, v.l. °ka-).
