• paricaryā, lit. service (of the body), with special reference to (sexual) enjoyment: Prāt 480.2 (kāya-pa°); °ryā-saṃvarṇana Mvy 8372 commending of ‘service’ (Tib. bsñyen bkur), i.e. of sexual pleasure (in speaking to a woman; one of the saṃghāvaśeṣa sins); paricaryā (alone, in this sense) MSV iii.87.18. The Pali term is attakāma- pāricariyā, Vin. iii.133.13, ministration to one's lusts (CPD). See also pāricaryā. [322,1]
