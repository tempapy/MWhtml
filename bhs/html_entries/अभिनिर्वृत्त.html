• (abhinirvṛtta, ppp. Skt., also Pali °nibbatta; cf. °vartaka, °vartana, an-abhi°; come into being: to be read with ed. Divy 71.6 (mahānyagrodhavṛkṣo) 'bhi° (most mss. °vṛtaḥ), and with Senart's note on Mv i.168.7 °vṛttaṃ dharmaṃ deśenti nāyakāḥ. Senart does not indicate that this is an em., altho both his text and the crit. app. read °vṛtaṃ; this however seems uninterpretable in the context. Senart understands attained to (repeated) existence (with different Buddhas). Cf. under abhinivar- tate.) [052,1]
