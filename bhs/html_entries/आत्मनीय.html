• ātmanīya, adj., also subst. nt. (= Pali attaniya), own, one's own (= sva, adj., or ātmanaḥ, gen.): LV 301.21 (prose) °yāś ca duhitrīr (acc. pl.; § 13.15), his own daughters; usually quasi-subst., what pertains to the self, what is of the nature of the self (the existence of which is denied): [Page092-b+ 71] LV 205.8 (see under -grāha); 340.1 (vs) cakṣur na istri puruṣo na pi cātmanīyaṃ; RP 14.6 (vs) strī neha nāsti ca pumān na ca ātmanīyaṃ; KP 137.3 (vs) yasyeha ātmā (read nātmā? Tib. and Chin. render the neg. twice) na ca ātmanīyam (so read, text °yām); Mv iii.66.8 and 14--15 naivātmā naivātmanīyā śūnyā ātmena vā ātmanīyena vā (so Senart, prob. rightly; mss. corrupt, esp. in 8); iii.447.12-- 13 śūnyā an-ātmanīyā (Bhvr., having nothing that pertains to the self) ātmena vā ātmanīyena vā.
