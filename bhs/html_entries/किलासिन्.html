• kilāsin, weary, indolent, faint-hearted. Certainly = Pali kilāsu, which Geiger 39.1 derives with Trenckner from Skt. glāsnu; perhaps rightly; change of g to k may be due to confusion with forms of klam- (Pali kilam-), or even with Skt. kilāsin, leprous; in this sense in Mv ii.383.16--17 na jātu gilāno (note this form of glā!) bhave pāṇḍurāgo, na [Page184-a+ 71] cāpi kuṣṭhī nāpi ca kilāsī (clearly leprous). Defined āla- syopeta, vīryarahita, in Abhisamayālaṃkārāloka (GOS 62) 320.15: °sī, n. sg. m., Śikṣ 49.16; AsP 243.10. See also kilāsitā, akilāsin, °sitā; and kilāsa. [183,2]
