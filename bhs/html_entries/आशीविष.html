• āśīviṣa, m. (in Skt., and Pali-Pkt. āsīvisa, only serpent; so also here, even in LV, e.g. 317.9; 339.2), serpent-venom, repeatedly in LV: °ṣān vamanti sma 305.13, °ṣān…bhakṣayanti sma 14; °ṣān niścārayanti sma 306.13,…vamanto 21. In 306.18 āśīviṣapariveṣṭitaśarīrāḥ is taken by Foucaux in this sense, le corps enduit de venin de serpent; but surely it is rather having bodies entwined by serpents, despite neighboring passages showing the other mg. See sumbhalikā. [109,2]
