• pratisaṃvedin (to prec.; = Pali paṭi°; recorded from Skt., rarely, in pw, see °vedanā), (1) experiencing; having experience (usually of something which precedes in comp.): sukha-°dī LV 369.11; sukhaduḥkha-°dībhir (masc.!) Gv 377.12, 19; evaṃsukhaduḥkha-°dī (same cpd. in Pali, PTSD) Mvy 230; Mv i.229.4; ii.133.3-4; 284.13; Bbh 253.27; and read so in LV 345.14 for °prativedī, both edd., no v.l.; sarvakāya-pratisaṃvedy āśvasan (so read with Mironov; same cpd. in Pali, e.g. MN i.425.13; Vism. i.267.4 ff.) Mvy 1177, and modulations of this 1178, 1181-4, 1187-8; vimuktiprītisukha-°dī Divy 181.1; 567.18; (aparimāṇaduḥkha-)°dī Dbh 29.14; neg. a-prati°, svamativikalpasukhaduḥkhāpratisaṃvedināṃ Laṅk 221.8; (2) perceiving, recognizing, knowing: pūrvaṃ cāham al- paprajño 'lpapratisaṃvedy andhabhūto 'smy āsīt SP 135.12, see § 41.1.
