• vālikā-nagara (cf. AMg. vālua-ppabhā, °ppahā, JM. vāluā-puḍhavī, n. of a particular hell), sand-city; possibly, like the Pkt. words, n. of an infernal locality; but possibly rather a mirage-city, seen in the sands of a desert: dhig bhavāṃ sarada-abhra-saṃnibhā, vālikānagara-rūpa-saṃni- bhā Mv i.74.(8--)9, fie on the states of existence which resem- ble an autumn cloud or a ‘sand-city’. A third possibility might be a city built of or on sand, hence not substantial. [478,2]
