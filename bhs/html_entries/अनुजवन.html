• anujavana, (nt. ? cf. anujava, anujavati), pursuit, chasing after, in śvaloṣṭv-anujavana-sadṛśa, like a dog's chasing a clod (thrown at him, instead of the thrower), KP 105.2 (text °loṣṭvānu°, by error), 3 (here text °loṣṭānu°, which could be kept, with Skt. loṣṭa for loṣṭu); 106.8; 107.1, 5; the simile is clearly explained in KP 105; also (sarvadiksamudra)-prasarānujavanena Gv 242.19.
