• abhināmayati, (1) inclines (trans.), makes (the heart) incline (to something, dat.); cittam abhināmayati LV 394.6, 12; cittam abhināmayitum (nivāsāya, towards remaining) Jm 132.11; (2) turns away (trans.), distorts: dharmabhāṇakasyārthāny akathām abhināmayati Śikṣ 96.10, distorts the preacher's meaning into a wrong statement; (3) prepares (food): khādyabhojyam abhināmayet Mv i.325.8; (4) causes to pass, passes, spends (time), = atinā- mayati, q.v., for which this may be only a graphic corrup- tion (t and bh often confused): LV 369.11 (a week, saptarā- traṃ); kausīdenābhināmitam Divy 464.20, 25, (time, or life: subject unexpressed) was spent in idleness, cf. the absolute use of atināmayati, q.v.; MSV i.18.16; but i.72.18 and 73.3 (same passage as Divy above) °tināmitam.
