• vyupaśama, m. (= Pali vūpasama; also rarely Skt. id., see BR; see next two; at least in some of the appli- cations here listed, only Buddh.), (1) calming, tranquil- lization of the mind (citta): (parameṇa citta-) °mena Divy 516.13; citta-dama-°mena samanvāgataṃ Av i.101.3 (of a Buddha; see vyupaśamana); (2) bringing to rest, of the saṃskāra: teṣāṃ °maḥ sukhaṃ Av ii.198.10 = Mmk 579.12 (vs, = Pali DN ii.157.9); stilling, of desires: nirodho °mo 'staṃgamaḥ Bhīk 24a.4; of misery (duḥkha), Divy 587.7; Bbh 27.1; sarvasattvakleśaśaṃtāpa-°mena Gv 386.14; of disease, vyādhi-°mārthaṃ Divy 109.26; vyā- dhīnāṃ °māya Bbh 209.21; glānya-°māya Śikṣ 37.5; (3) tranquillity, presumably of mind (as in 1): (a-vyupa- śāntaḥ) a-vyupaśamārāmaḥ Bbh 169.4.
