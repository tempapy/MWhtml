• kāśikā (see prec.), (1) a piece or garment of Benares cloth: Divy 576.29--30 putra vātāyanena kāśikāṃ niṣkā- sayeti.tena vātāyanena kāśikā niṣkāsitā; 579.7 kāśikā dattā; (2) n. of a courtesan in Benares: Mv iii.375.16 ff.; the story told here (see 375.18) is that she got her name not from the city, but because she was worth a fee of a thousand (see kāśi 2, bhūmi 2, and kṣamati); her sister was called Uparddhakāśikā, q.v.
