• srota-āpatti, srotāpatti, often written śrot°, so regularly in Mv, Divy, and mss. of Av (= Pali sotāpatti), ‘entrance into the stream’, conversion to Buddhism, the state of the srota-āpanna, the first of the four stages of Hīnayāna religious development, the others being the states of the sakṛd-āgāmin, anāgāmin, and arhant; esp. often in comp. with -phala, the fruit of this attainment; con- tracted form °tāpatti regular in Mv, prose as well as vss, in other texts rare in prose (Divy 17.21), used in vss where favored by meter (so, I believe, regularly in Laṅk, e.g. srota-āpatti-gati- 116.13, prose, srotāpatti-phalaṃ, mss. śro°, 65.9, vs); śrota-āpatti-pratipannaḥ Mvy 5131; śrotāpatti-phala Mv i.175.1 (vs); 312.13 (prose); iii.254.11 (prose); 346.13 (vs); śrota-āpatti-phala Divy 46.26; 50.8, etc.; Av i.65.1 (ms., Speyer em. sro°) etc., common.
