• unnāma (AMg. uṇṇāma, unnāma, elation, pride; in Skt. one doubtful occurrence where, if correct, it means [physical] elevation; not in Pali), elevation (of spirits), elation; usually in comp. with its opposite avanāma or °mana, once with nāma, also of opposite mg.: Gv 244.3 an-unnāmāvanāma-tāṃ, state of not being elated or depressed; Śikṣ 105.5 lābhālābhatayā unnāmāvanāma-karo, causing elation or depression by getting or not getting; Śikṣ 108.12 (vs) unnāma-nāmāni bahūni gacchatī, goes to many fits of elation and depression, see s.v. nāma = avanāma; Śikṣ 150.6 nonnāmajāto bhavati; LV 33.7, text an-unnāmāvanāmana-tāyai, unto being not subject to elation or depression (mss. vary greatly).
