• pradūṣayati (Skt., corrupts, injures; Pali padūseti, also padoseti, used as in BHS with object citta, also manas, manaṃ), (1) with obj. citta, lit. corrupts (one's own) mind, = (as in Pali) becomes ill-disposed, irritated, angry, malicious towards someone (usually loc., or gen. with antike, some- times gen. alone): (devānām…antike) cittāni pradūṣay- itvā Mv i.30.9--10; arhato 'ntike Av i.287.7; dakṣiṇīyeṣu ii.148.2; dagdhasthūṇāyā (prose; gen.? MIndic loc.? or corruption for °yām?) api cittaṃ na °ṣayiṣyāmaḥ prāg eva savijñānake kāye (note loc.!) Divy 197.25; mamāntike cittaṃ pradūṣitam Karmav 26.15, he got angry at me; no dependent case-form, Divy 286.5; Av i.248.4; 289.10 etc.; ii.130.4; (2) without cittam, but app. in same mg.: mā bhavanto bhagavato kāśyapasyāntike bhikṣusaṃghasya bādhituṃ pradūṣetha Mv i.314.8-9, do not be malicious towards the Lord K., or (his) order of monks, to injure them.
