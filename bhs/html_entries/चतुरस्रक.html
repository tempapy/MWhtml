• caturasraka, nt. (cf. Skt. caturaśrikā, Kauṭ., see Schmidt, Nachträge, and J.J. Meyer, Index; acc. to Meyer a square shawl [or a blanket] used as protection against cold), prob. a square blanket or shawl, one of the appointments of monks' quarters: vṛṣi-kocava (so read, see s.v.)-bimbopadhāna-caturasraka-śatāni (things fur- nished to monks) Divy 40.11; 550.16; 553.3, 10; catu- rasrakam Mvy 8992, rendered in Tib., Chin., Jap. literally, square; bimbopadhāna and vṛsikā (or vṛṣ°, = Divy vṛṣi) occur in the same vicinity, and kocavaka not far away (8982).
