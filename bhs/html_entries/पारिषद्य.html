• pāriṣadya, m. (also pārṣadya; = Pali pārisajja; pāri° Skt. Lex. and once Rājat., for regular pāriṣada; also Skt. Lex. pārṣadya for regular pārṣada; see also par(i)ṣadya, and s.v. brahmapār(i)ṣadya), member of an assembly or group; pl. retinue; esp., and almost always, said of members of the retinue of a king (or god, or Māra, or the like), and often associated or cpd. esp. with amātya (so e.g. LV 26.10; Mv iii.160.19, and many others in the foll.), or with this and other royal retainers such as dau- vārika (e.g. LV 118.11); usually pl.: LV 26.10 (prose); 43.15; 50.9; 78.17; 118.11 (prose); 219.19; Mv ii.443.2 (prose, v.l. pari°), 12, 17, 18; iii.160.19; Divy 291.27; Suv 227.11, read °dyāḥ (all mss. contain y, and meter is better so) for Nobel °dāḥ. See also pārṣada which is used absolutely, like Skt. gaṇa. [343,1]
