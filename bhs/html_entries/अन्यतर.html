• anyatara, adj., like Pali aññatara (and cf. katara, q.v.) is very commonly used without its Skt. limitation to one of two; rather, as equivalent of Skt. anyatama (which is also used in BHS in the same way, notably in Divy and Av), and chiefly (1) in the mg. a certain; (an unspecified) one (of many): Mv i.36.10 (here could be interpreted in mg. 2); 343.4; ii.31.19; 65.1; 96.15; 145.4; 171.9; 461.14; iii.15.7; 53.13; Divy 102.8; 226.19; 227.26; 254.6; Av i.137.11; 208.8; 235.6; 244.3; Suv 214.4; RP 39.16; Śikṣ 39.1 (wrongly rendered another by Bendall and Rouse); Gv 84.17; Karmav 32.12; 35.16; Laṅk 176.8; (2) much more rarely, another, any other (of an unlimited number); so possibly (but not probably) Mv i.36.10, above; and RP 56.19 sarvathānapekṣo 'bhūt kāye jīvite ca, prāg evānyatarasmin bāhyavastuni,…how much less (not to speak of) in regard to any other external matter; (3) some (one or other), one or another, substantially = anyatarānyatara, q.v.: Mmk 304.16 anyatareṇa śucinā celakhaṇḍena.
