• parikathā (= Pali id., both senses; Skt. Lex. only), [Page320-b+ 71] (1) roundabout talk; indirect allusion (see s.v. avabhāsa): Divy 92.18 ff.; (2) more generally, talk, perhaps lengthy speech: LV 242.22 (vs) parikatha bhikṣu yadī na bhā- rasaṃjñā, if a (long) tale does not seem to you a burden; but Foucaux takes parikatha as 2 sg. impv. to °kathayati; Tib. dge sloṅ (= bhikṣu) khur gyi ḥdu śes (notion of burden) med (not) na (= yadi) gsuṅs (speak; verb or noun ?); more especially of a religious talk, sermon or dialogue, Divy 225.26; 235.25 (bhikṣavo…) °thāṃ kurvanti.
