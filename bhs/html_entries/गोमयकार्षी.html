• gomaya-kārṣī (or -kāsī), (possibly cf. kasso = paṅkaḥ Deśīn. 2.2?) perhaps a coating of cowdung, possibly mixed with something else, seemingly applied as purifica- tion: (gṛhaṃ saṃmārjitaṃ) sukumārī °kārṣī dattā Divy 306.23; MSV ii.100.13; 129.16; in Divy 338.24 text (sā kuṭikā siktā saṃmṛṣṭā saṃmārjitā) sukumārī (note same adj.!) gomayakāsiñcānupradattā, but mss. °kāsīñ cānu°, read °kāsi cānu° or °kārṣī cānu°; (gomayena miśrayitvā) yatra caṅkrame tathāgataḥ caṅkramyate tatra gomaya- kārṣīṃ prayacchati Divy 369.5, and, tatra gomayakārṣī dattā 7. If -kāṣī may be accepted as the true reading, perh. connected with root kaṣ and meaning something like a rubbing with cowdung. [217,1]
