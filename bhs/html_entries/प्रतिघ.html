• pratigha, generally m., in LV 329.22 nt. (Pali paṭigha, acc. to PTSD m. and nt.; Skt. pratigha, m., resistance; [Lex. and a few doubtful literary occurrences] anger), aversion, repugnance, loathing, hostility (hardly anger, at least I find no clear proof for this mg.; but in Mvy 1945, where it follows and seems to contrast with rāgaḥ, Tib. renders khon khro ba, anger). It is often bracketed and contrasted with anunaya, q.v. for citations; both (= rāga and dveṣa) are evil and must be shunned. Etymolog. gloss Śikṣ 149.5 it is called pratigha because it destroys, pratihanti, roots of merit. One of six kleśa, Dharmas 67 (follows rāgaḥ). Other occurrences (without anunaya): LV 11.5; Dbh 25.4; Bbh 7.16; 161.13; Śikṣ 6.17; 52.9; 251.15; 271.12. See also next. [362,1]
