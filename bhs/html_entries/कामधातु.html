• kāma-dhātu, m. (= Pali id.), the world (region, sphere) of desire, including all states of existence up to and including that of the paranirmitavaśavartin gods; the gods who live in this region are called kāmāvacara (see deva): very common, e.g. Mv ii.314.12; LV 45.15; 48.17; 299.20; Jm 192.11; esp. contrasting with rūpa-dhātu and ārūpya- (dhātu), qq.v., LV 428.19; Mvy 3072; KP 94.4.
