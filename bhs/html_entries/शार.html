• śāra, m. or nt. (= AMg. sāla; perh. read śāla ?), either ‘the 77th planet, named śāla’, or (more probably) ‘a celestial abode named śāla of the 8th Devaloka’ (Ratna- chandra, s.v. sāla, 7 and 8). In Mv i.231.4 (vs) read: ādityo (? next word uncertain; mss. vatavallo, which is metr. correct, or vadbalo) śāraṃ (or śālaṃ; mss. śāram) abhyud- gato (mss. atyudgataṃ; em. Senart) yathākāśe, like the sun when it has arisen in the sky up to the śāra (śāla). (On *śāra, a kind of bird, see sāra.) [526,1]
