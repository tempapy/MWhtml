• abhibhakṣaṇa, nt., feeding upon (to Pali abhibhak- khayitvā); so prob. read with 1 ms. in Mv i.361.9 ekaṃ mṛgaṃ visarjayiṣyāmaḥ, mahārājasya ca mṛgamānsena abhibhakṣaṇaṃ (v.l. avikṣaṇam; Senart em. avibhakṣa- ṇaṃ, supposed to mean non-cessation de nourriture; im- plausible) bhaviṣyati, ime ca mṛgā evaṃ anayavyasanaṃ nopapadyiṣyanti, we will let go one deer (every day), and the king will feed on deer-meat, and (at the same time) thus these deer will not get into serious trouble. Less likely is the v.l. avikṣaṇaṃ = AMg. avikkhaṇa (by false Sktization) = Pali (an-)apekkhana, Skt. (rare) apekṣaṇa = apekṣā desire (at the same time the king will crave deer-meat, and these deer…).
