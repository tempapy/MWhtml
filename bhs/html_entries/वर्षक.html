• varṣaka, (1) m. (Pali only vassika as adj.), house, hut for the rainy season, for monks or nuns: °kaḥ Mvy 9154 = Tib. dbyar khaṅ, which means not summer-house (BR) but the above; bhikṣuṇī-varṣakaḥ Av i.269.6; var- ṣake 11; (2) prob. for varṣika- or varṣikā, q.v., a kind of jasmine: varṣaka-dhānuṣkāri- (so read, see s.v. dhā- nuṣkārin) Mv iii.80.4 (vs); cf. next.
