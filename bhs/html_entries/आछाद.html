• āchāda, m. (cf. the following items), gift, present (not necessarily a garment!): Mv ii.98.3 lubdhakena vipulo āchādo (a rich reward) labdho; iii.128.10 vipulo āchādo dinno; 162.16 teṣāṃ āchādaṃ dāsyāmi (here gold minted and unminted, and garments, lines 14, 15); food, bhaktā- chādena (Speyer em. °chādanena; not food and dress but a gift of food) paripālanaṃ kariṣyati Av ii.112.7; dharmā- chāda, a pious or religious gift, SP 445.12 (dāsyāmo… avalokiteśvarāya…) dharmaprābhṛtaṃ dharmāchādam (note āchāda as synonym of prābhṛta! the gift given was a necklace, muktāhāra, 446.1, again called dharmāchāda 446.2); LV 352.5 f. dharmāchādāṃś ca saṃpreṣayanti sma (by which, yair dharmāchādair, the universe became covered with many jewelled parasols).
