• Pūraṇa Kāśyapa, or (in Mv always) Kāśyapa Pūraṇa (= Pali Pūraṇa, v.l. Purāṇa, Kassapa), n. of a celebrated heretical teacher of Buddha's time one (usually the first in listing) of a group of six (śāstāraḥ, tīrthyāḥ): in Divy 154.4 nirgranthāḥ; P. himself called a nirgrantha, Divy 165.14; the others are Maskarin Gośālīputra, Saṃjayin Vairaṭīputra, Ajita Keśakambala, Kakuda Kātyāyana, and Nirgrantha Jñātiputra (with variants), qq.v.; P.K. named with the rest, Mvy 3545; Divy 143.10; Av i.231.3; in the order K.P., MV 1.253. o and (as cpd., Kāśyapa-pūr°) 9; 256.20; iii.383.15; P.K. without the others, Av i.281.1; K.P. without the others, Mv ii.200.10; 207.2; Pūraṇa alone (without Kāśyapa, and without the names of the others, but often as one of the ‘six teachers’ or the like) Divy 143.9, 13; 154.4 ff.; 164.17, 26; 165.14 ff.; Av i.16.3; 47.7; 48.6; ii.24.11; 134.10.
