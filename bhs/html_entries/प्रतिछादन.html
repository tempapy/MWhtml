• pratichādana, nt., °na-tā, °nā? (in mg. 1, conceal- ment, once in Skt., Schmidt, Nachträge), (1) covering, for protection: citta-nagara-prati° Śikṣ 123.6; concealment, gambhīrārtha-dharma-°na-tā Bbh 82.17; esp. concealment of one's own faults, duṣṭhula-°nam Mvy 8473; svadoṣa- (text °dauṣa-)-°na-tā KP 116.2; kukṛta-°na-tā (v.l. °nā) Bbh 403.24; (2) (= Pali kaṇḍu-paṭicchādī, f., Vin. iv. 172.11, 15, glossed paṭicchādanatthāya) a cloth covering or bandage, in kaṇḍu-°nam, itch-covering, allowed to monks afflicted with a skin disease: Prāt 524.3; Mvy 8516; spelled kaṇḍū-pratichadanam Mvy 8943 (Index °chād°, but Mironov °chad°).
