• ālokita-vilokita (nt., dvandva; on Pali see below), look and gaze; in a cliché, (prāsādikena…) °kitena Mv i.301.6; iii.60.6; 182.12; in other forms of the same cliché avalokita and vyavalokita are substituted; see these on the Tib. interpretation of the difference of mg. between them, to which I do not attach much importance (it sounds etymologizing). However, acc. to Pali DN comm. i.193.17 these two words mean looking ahead and looking all around, which substantially = Tib. [106,2]
