• āsevaka, or °kā, some kind of garment: SP 283.9 (vs), cited Śikṣ 352.13, āsevaka (unmetr.! one ms. and Śikṣ °kaṃ; WT with most mss. °kāṃ; Kashgar rec. °kā) kṛsna tathādaditvā. Tib. acc. to Bendall rdul gzan, dust garment (Jä. cloak, against dust on a journey), but acc. to WT, and my own copy of Tib. SP, rṅul gzan, which = saṃkakṣikā, q.v.; Burnouf and Kern woolen garment; note that Tib. rdul and rṅul are very easily confused in writing; in MSV ii.52.5 āsevaka (ms., ed. em. āsīvaka) seems to mean patch (so Tib., lhan thabs kyis klan pa); in the next line, 6, sevakaṃ (ms., ed. em. sīv°) dattvā dhāraya, seems to show the same mg., and in 11 below, text āsīvakāṃs (by em.? ms. āsev° as before?) tu dattvā dhārayitvayam, confirms this mg. (reference is to materials unsuitable for making robes). [111,1]
