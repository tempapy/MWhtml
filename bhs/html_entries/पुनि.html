• puni, in LV 151.8 (vs) seemingly m.c. for *pune, certainly represents punar, but; both edd. read so, no v.l. No *pune seems to be recorded, even in a MIndic equiva- lent (as *puṇe, *uṇe). Possibly it may be assumed as loc. to the ‘stem’ puna- (Pkt. puṇa-, uṇa-) recorded by Pischel (see under punā). Less likely, in my opinion, *pune as ‘Māgadhism’ for MIndic puno. [347,2]
