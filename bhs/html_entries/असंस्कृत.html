• asaṃskṛta, adj. and subst. nt. (= Pali asaṃkhata, ep. of nibbāna, perh. as uncreated by a combination of factors, but see CPD), unconditioned; as nt., one of three unconditioned things. The adj. occurs e.g. Laṅk 189.14. The three asaṃskṛtāni are ākāśa, pratisaṃkhyā-nirodha (which = nirvāṇa), aprati° (see pratisaṃkhyā and aprati°), Dharmas 32; in Mvy 2184 °tam, but 2185--6 name only the 2d and 3d, not ākāśa; without mention of the term asaṃskṛta, and with substitution of nirvāṇa- (-dhātu) for pratisaṃkhyānirodha, the three are named Laṅk 177.3; 197.12; see also AbhidhK. La V-P. i.7.8. [082,2]
