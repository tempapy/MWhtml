• parigṛhīta, ppp. (in mg. comprehended, °taṃ bodhi- sattvaiḥ LV 423.14, may be regarded as standard Skt., where at least close relatives of this word are so used), ungenerous, close-fisted, stingy, in neg. a-pari°: amātsaryo 'haṃ (Buddha speaks) kulaputrā aparigṛhītacitto… buddhajñānasya dātā SP 485.1, I am, gentlemen, one who gives out the Buddha-knowledge without selfishness, with heart not niggardly (restricted); Tib. ḥdzin pa (= parigṛhīta; note that zin pa, a form of the same Tib. verb, renders parigṛddha, q.v.) sems (= citta) med do (I am not). The word of course is not connected with parigṛddha; Skt. parigṛhīta has mgs. like limited, restricted, of which this is a specialization. [321,2]
