• sadāmatta, (1) m. pl. (= Pali id., but rare and usually not in corresponding lists), n. of a class of godlings (yakṣas, Mv i.30.8), associated with and functioning like karoṭapāṇi and mālādhāra, qq.v.; also °taka and sadāmāda, qq.v.: Mv i.30.8; Divy 218.9, 30 ff.; Mmk 19.13; 43.18; 232.10; Mahāsamāj. Waldschmidt Kl. Skt. Texte 4, 187.5; (2) nt.sg., n. of a mythical city (= Divy °mattaka, q.v.): Av i.201.6; 203.1; = Pali id., Jāt. i.363.11 = iii.207.2, in same vs as Av i.203.1; in Pali seems to be misinterpreted as a palace (pāsāda) by the comm. and transl. (the comm. in fact alleges that the word means lit. silver), but the full story as told in both Av and Divy (not found in the Jātakas which are truncated) shows that a city is meant. [554,2]
