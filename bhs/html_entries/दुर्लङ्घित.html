• durlaṅghita, nt., some form of hostile magic or its result (see s.v. durbhukta): Mvy 4381; Māy 220.19; 245.18 (here dull°). Acc. to Tib. on Mvy, bgo ñe(s), app. bad clothes; or sgyuṅ (which I cannot find) ñes. On the basis of Skt., laṅghita could mean violation, hostile attack; or fasting, hunger, starving. [267,1]
