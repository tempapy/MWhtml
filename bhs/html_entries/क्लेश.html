• kleśa (also semi-MIndic kileśa), m. (= Pali kilesa), impurity, depravity; on relation to anuśaya, q.v., see esp. LaVallée Poussin, AbhidhK. v.1 note 4; Dharmas 67 six kleśa: rāga, pratigha, māna, avidyā, kudṛṣṭi, vicikitsā; AbhidhK. v.2, six anuśaya, same list (for the last two, the synonyms dṛṣṭi, vimati); Kv 80.5, read, katimāḥ ṣaṭ kleśāḥ (no list given); the word is extremely common, but usually vague and undefined; Mvy 862; LV 8.18; 11.5; 12.12, etc. etc.; kileśa, Mv i.299.16 (vs; mss. kilena, em. Senart), and others, § 3.109. A discussion in Burnouf, Lotus, 443 ff. [198,1]
