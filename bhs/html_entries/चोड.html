• coḍa, m. or nt. (Skt. cola, defined as jacket or blouse, Lex. = kañcuka, prāvaraṇa; BHS seems to use coḍa in a wider sense; see next), garments(s), clothing; regularly associated with bhakta or bhojana, together = food and raiment: paryeṣate bhakta tathāpi coḍaṃ SP 112.4; bhaktaṃ ca coḍaṃ ca gaveṣamāṇo 8; kim adya coḍena ca (so read with most mss. for text tha) bhojanena vā 113.8; (ananyacitto) bhakte ca coḍe ca bhavaty abhīkṣṇaṃ Śikṣ 113.8, and he always has his heart set on nothing but food and clothing. (All verses.) [234,1]
