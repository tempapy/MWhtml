• pradadhāti (= Pali padahati, with cittaṃ, DN iii.221.14), exerts (the mind, cittaṃ): evaṃ cittaṃ prada- dhyās tvaṃ Śikṣ 101.10 (vs, from Samādh). From this verb, as used in Pali, are derived pradhāna and prahāṇa (1), also prahita, qq.v.; and in Mv ii.208.1 (prahāṇaṃ) pradadhāti, exerts himself in exertion, should prob. be read (for corrupt mss.) instead of Senart's em. pratidadhāti; cf. both pradhāna and prahāṇa dependent on prahita, q.v. [In pw s.v. dhā with pra cited from LV Calc. as mg. sich einer Sache (acc.) hingeben, but Lefm. 385.12 (vs) reads with some mss. pradadhyau (to dhyā), which is proved right by meter and Tib. dgoṅs.] [379,2]
