• mārga, m. (Skt. id.; special uses, the first and third as in Pali magga), way; (1) aṣṭāṅga-m° (= Pali aṭṭhaṅga- magga), the (noble, ārya) 8-fold path: Mvy 996 etc.; the 8 steps (as in Pali) listed e.g. Mvy 997--1004, samyag- dṛṣṭi, -saṃkalpa, -vāc, -karmānta, -ājīva, -vyāyāma, smṛti, -samādhi; (2) daśāryagotra-mārgaṃ pratilabhate Laṅk 222.4, acc. to Suzuki the ten paths of discipline which belong to the noble family (of the Tathāgatas); what does this mean? the ten kuśala-karmapatha? Tib. renders literally, ḥphags paḥi rigs kyi lam; (3) four kinds of śramaṇa (as in Pali, Sn 83--89, where this is made clear, and the magga-jina, -desaka or -desin, -jīvin, and -dūsin are defined), Mvy 5127--30, mārga-jina, conqueror of the way (of religion), -deśika, teacher of the way, -jīvin, living in the way, -dūṣin, defiling the way (by hypocrisy and wickedness).
