• sāhasra-cūḍika, m. (cf. Pali sahassī cūḷanikā loka- dhātu), n. of a lokadhātu: Mvy 3042. Cf. repeatedly in AsP sāhasre cūḍike (or, cūḍikāyāṃ) lokadhātau, loc., the cpd. apparently divided into two words, sāhasre, always m., but cūḍikāyāṃ, f., in AsP 65.3; 66.2, and so v.l., one ms. out of six, 105.13, 16; 108.3, 5; but cūḍike text with 5 mss. in these four places, and elsewhere, e.g. 117.18, without v.l.; no reading with cūḍikāyāṃ has been noted except in the passages cited; (sahasrika)ś cyūḍiko (read cū°) lokadhātuḥ MPS 31.46. The form cūḍ° evidently means small, minor, and is related to Pali cūḷa, culla. Contrasted with dvisāhasra… and trisāhasramahā- sāhasra…, qq.v. In ŚsP 26.8 and elsewhere replaced by sāhasre lokadhātau, see sāhasra. [594,2]
