• pratipanna, ppp. (to °padyati; Pali paṭipanna simi- larly used), practised (sc. in religiously proper behavior): impersonally (passive), (sārthavāhenāpi) kiṃcit pūrvaṃ pratipannaṃ Mv iii.354.4, the merchant, for his part, had to some extent engaged in previous practice (of a religious course); so Senart in 19 (vāṇijakehi) pūrvaṃ (read pūrveṇa, mss. pūrvāṇa) pratipannaṃ, but mss. pratipannās, n. pl., as if with active mg., having practised; cf. Pali Vv.34 vs 23 cattāro ca paṭipannā (comm. 154.31 paṭipajjamānā, maggaṭṭhā ti attho) cattāro ca phale ṭhitā; also, as in Pali, behaving, acting (for the purpose of, dat.), ātmahitāya pratipanno…parahitāya etc. Bbh 4.3.
