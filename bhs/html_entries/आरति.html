• Ārati, f., n. of one of the ‘armies’ (senā) of Māra: Mv ii.240.3 (vs) kāmā te prathamā senā dvitīyā ārati vuccati.The meter is indifferent as to a- or ā- initially, and LV in the same vs has Arati, q.v. It is hard to be sure what meaning was attributed to the word. Foucaux (mécontentement) and Tib. (mi dgaḥ ba) understand LV as a-(neg.)-rati. [102,2]
