• pratilayana, (app. to Skt. prati-lī-, Pali paṭilīyati, withdraws, turns back, and derivs.), should mean with- drawal (from), non-adherence (to): only in Gv 494.18, said of bodhicittam, (sārathibhūtaṃ) mahāyāna-pratilayana- pūrvaṃgamana-tayā; it seems probable that the true text was mahāyānāprati°, with neg., it acts like a charioteer because of the fact that it is accompanied (conditioned, see pūrvaṃgamana) by not withdrawing (holding back) from the Great Vehicle.
