• aiṣikā (= Pali esikā; proverbially ‘stable, firm’, see refs. in PTSD), pillar (as that which makes firm, stead- fast): Śikṣ 3.7 (vs) niśraya (separate!) kleśa-adharṣika- tāyai aiṣika (m.c. for °kā; possibly read eṣika?) śraddha svayaṃbhuguṇānām, faith is a support, so as not to be subject to attack by the depravities, a pillar for the excellent qualities of the Self-existent. (Bendall and Rouse wrongly derive from eṣaka, to eṣati.) [158,2]
