• śrāvaka (so in Skt., and Pali sāvaka, in Skt. also of Jains and other sects), a (Buddhist) disciple, in Mahāyāna texts regularly used of followers of the Hīnayāna, passim: technical description, sarveṣām arhatāṃ kṣīṇāśravāṇām uṣitavratānāṃ samyagājñāsuvimuktacittānāṃ parikṣīṇa- bhavasaṃyojanānām anuprāptasvakārthānām Mv i.248.10 f., repeated below; a similar formula in Pali, Vin. i.183.24 etc.; stages and types of śr° Mvy 1008--1028; names of well-known śr° ib. 1029--1073; their qualities 1075--1126. PTSD says a sāvaka is ‘never an Arahant’, but see CPD s.v. araha(t) 2. [535,1]
