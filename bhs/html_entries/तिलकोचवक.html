• tilakocavaka, nt., a kind of arrowhead: Mvy 6099 (in a list of weapons); acc. to Tib. (mde ḥu zur bzhi pa) and Jap., an arrowhead with four edges or blades; Chin. arrowhead with four layers (?). I see no etymology for the word; Skt. tila plus BHS kocavaka, q.v., seems to make no sense. [254,1]
