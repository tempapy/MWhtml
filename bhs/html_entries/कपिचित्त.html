• kapi-citta, adj. (= Pali id.), ‘monkey-minded’, fickle: in LV 178.20 (vs) read, kapicittā 'ryapathe sthapeṣya haṃ, I shall establish the fickle ones in the noble path. Not only Tib. but a BHS ms. reported by Foucaux (Notes p. 139 l. 2) supports kapi-, and the Pali form confirms it. [167,2]
