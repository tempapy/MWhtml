• kācākṣa, adj., Mvy 8836, lit. glass-eyed; Tib. śel mig, glass-eye, acc. to Das used for spectacles. Does it orig. mean having a glass eye, literally? Or glassy-eyed, perh. = staring-eyed (so Chin.)? Occurs in a list of monstrous or deformed characteristics; followed by skandhākṣa, q.v. [175,2]
