• Tapoda, m. or nt., °dā (= Pali Tapodā, n. of a lake and its outlet river at Rājagaha), n. of one or more bodies of water (lake, spring[s], river ?) at Rājagṛha: tapoda-dvāreṇa Rājagṛhaṃ…prāvikṣat LV 240.1 (prose), entered R. by the gate of that-which-has-warm-water, so Tib., chu dron can gyi (of that which has warm water) sgo nas (by gate); the gate was named for warm springs or for the river outlet from them; (rājagṛhe samasamaṃ giriyagra- [v.l. giri-m-agra-]-samājaṃ) nāma parvaṃ vartati pañ- cānāṃ tapoda-śatānāṃ (so read for ed. tapo-śa°; mss. tapa-śa°, capośata-śa°). tatra dāni pañcahi tapoda-(so read, ed. tapo-, mss. tapa-, tapota-)-śatehi pañca udyāna- śatāni Mv iii.57.7, at R. at the same time there took place the festival called Mountain-top-gathering, of 500 Warm Springs. Now at these 500 Warm Springs there were 500 pleasure-parks; Tapodā, as in Pali n. of a river at Rājagṛha, [Page250-a+ 71] Pischel, SBBA 1904 p. 813 fol. 158a; n. of a river Māy 253.7 (doubtless the same).
