• dvipādaka, adj. (= Skt. dvipāda; cf. Pali dipādaka, said of the human body, Sn 205), ‘two-legged’, human, only with puṇyakṣetra, a human (personified) field of merit (virtue): of a Buddha, Divy 48.6; 63.24, 28; of other pious person, Mv i.329.11 (not quite rightly Senart). [274,2]
