• vairambha, m. (1) (= Pali ver°; see also next; appears to be derived from *virambha, but no form or deriv. of vi-rabh- is recorded), n. or epithet of certain very violent winds: vairambha-vāyu-vikṣipta (so, as one word) iva pakṣī LV 333.12 (vs); °bha-vātena yathaiva pakṣī kṣipyanti…RP 36.4 (vs); °bha-vātābhihata- śakuntavat Śikṣ 246.11 (prose); in Mv i.168.18 (vs) read, nearly with mss., vairambhā pi (mss. ṣi) ca vāta naṃ (see s.v. nam) vikopenti (q.v.) na dehakaṃ (q.v.; Senart em. wrongly); (Vairambhe mahāsamudre) vairambhā nāma vāyavo vānti Divy 105.26; (2) n. of an ocean: Divy 102.29 (read °bhas for text °bhā); 105.25 (see above), 29; (3) = Vairambhya, or as pl. its people: °bheṣu MSV i.216.4; 217.4.
