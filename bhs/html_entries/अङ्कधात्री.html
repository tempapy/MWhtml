• aṅka-dhātrī, nurse who carries a baby on her hip: Mvy 9478; Divy 475.12, defined 13--14: aṅkadhātrīty ucyate yā dārakam aṅkena parikarṣayaty aṅgapratyaṅgāni ca saṃsthāpayati.Tib. on Mvy paṅ na = aṅka, which it seems must be the orig. form, since in India babies are carried on the hip. Yet in all other cases the word is written aṃsa-dh° (or aṃśa-, in mss. also atsa-), and this seems to have had real currency, as shown s.v. For the cliché see s.v. kṣīra-dhātrī. [005,2]
