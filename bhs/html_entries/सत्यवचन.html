• satya-vacana and satya-vākya, nt., (see also abhi- [Page554-b+ 71] yācanā; = Pali sacca-vacana, more often sacca-kiriyā), solemn statement of truth as a means of magic control of events (cf. Burlingame, JRAS 1917.429 ff.): °vākyena Mv ii.97.9, 17, 20, °vacanena 19 (by this a kiṃnarī is bound); °vākya ii.218.4; 229.15; °vacana 218.15 ff., 229.17 ff. (by this an ascetic boy killed by a poisoned arrow is revived); °vacana Divy 473.19 ff. (by this a woman changes her sex); 571.5 ff.; SP 413.8, see satyādhiṣṭhāna. The ‘Skt.’ satyakriyā is cited in BR and pw only from a review of Hardy's Eastern Monachism in Ind. Stud. 3.119; if it actually occurs, even in BHS, I have failed to note it; is it perhaps a modern Sktization of the Pali saccakiriyā ? See Burlingame, l. c. 433 f. [554,1]
