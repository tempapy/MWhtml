• pūryasyām (loc. sg. of Skt. purvā, f.), or pūrvikāyam (loc. sg. of f. of Skt. pūrvaka; in both sc. jātau or jātyām), in a former birth: kiṃ punar Bhagavān…Śaṅkhakuñ- jarasyāsmākaṃ pūrvasyāṃ jātaṃ jānīte Karmav 27.15--16, but does the Lord know what, in relation to me, happened to (jātaṃ) Ś. (we should expect acc., °kuñjaram, as what in relation to me was Ś. born) in his former birth?; kiṃ punar Bhagavān…asmākaṃ Śaṅkhakuñjaraṃ pūrvikāyāṃ jātaṃ saṃjānīte 27.19--20, …as what in relation to me Ś. was born in a previous birth?; saced bhavāñ Chaṅkhakuñ- jaro 'smākaṃ pūrvikāyāṃ jātaḥ pitābhūt 28.2--3, if you, Ś., were born as my father in a previous birth (Ś. in his present birth was a dog).
