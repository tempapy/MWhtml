• dharaṇī, (1) acc. to Tib. on Mvy 5578 = phyam, defined by Jä. support (of rafters), the resting point of a beam; by Das, ‘the resting beam of a staircase or ladder. Also: prop, bracket, mortice’: dharaṇīyo (v.l. °ya), n. pl., Mv iii.228.5 (prose), as parts of a city gate; (2) a small weight (cf. Skt. dharaṇa, a considerably larger weight): in eka-suvarṇa-dharaṇī LV 63.19 (prose, no v.l.), acc. to Tib. = phye ma zho gcig, one grain (a very small weight, which is clearly intended in the context of LV; Jä. defines zho as dram, a small weight = (1/10) ounce) of dust. There is no word for gold in Tib.; it may be noted that suvarṇa is also used in Skt. as n. of a rather small weight (a karṣa) of gold, but acc. to BR, pw, not of other substances; did suvarṇa-dharaṇī mean a gold-grain in some such sense as a small weight commonly used in weighing gold? (3) n. of a rākṣasī: Māy 243.12. [276,1]
