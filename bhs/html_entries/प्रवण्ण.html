• pravaṇṇa, adj.-ppp. (= AMg. pavaṇṇa; semi-MIndic for Skt. prapanna), resorted to: raudrair narair aśubha- karma-m-ati-pravaṇṇaiḥ Mv i.84.3 (vs), by violent men who resort too much to evil deeds. So one ms., the rest °pravarṇaiḥ, which makes no sense; Senart em. °vaṇaiḥ, but his note recognizes that the penult is required by meter to be long; he suggests that doubling of ṇ of pravaṇa took place m.c. But the AMg. form fits without change. [384,2]
