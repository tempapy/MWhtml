• Ukkala, nt. (presumably = Skt. Utkala, Orissa; cf. Pali Ukkalā), n. of a locality (adhiṣṭhāna), where Trapuṣa and Bhallika originated; when they visited Buddha they were journeying from the south (Mv iii.303.6), presumably homeward bound (so also in LV 381.4--6, where they are described as uttarāpathakau): Mv iii.303.4 uttarāpathe ukkalaṃ nāmādhiṣṭhānaṃ. tato ukkalāto…trapuṣo ca bhalliko ca… [117,1]
