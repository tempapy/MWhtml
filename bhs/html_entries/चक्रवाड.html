• cakravāḍa, regularly m. (nt. forms rare; = Skt. °vāla, Pali cakkavāḷa), n. of a mountain or rather mountain- range, supposed to surround the earth: sometimes sg., Mvy 4149 °ḍaḥ; SP 363.5; Dbh 96.4; Mv ii.332.2 śailo… °ḍo; more often pl.; often followed by mahācakravāḍa, esp. in cpd. (dual dvandva Kv 91.11--12; Dharmas 124), oftener pl., or in longer cpds. where both have stem form, or both sg. (Mvy 4149, 4150), LV 150.1; 277.9; Mv i.6.1; ii.300.17; SP 244.11; Śikṣ 246.2; Suv 86.7; Sukh 36.14--15; 63.3; without mahācak°, SP 355.5 (pl.); LV 316.13 (pl.); Mv ii.341.10 (stem in cpd.); Śamādh 19.19 (vs, text cakravāḍa, meter requires cakravăḍo or °ḍā); in fig. use, (puṇya-)cakravāḍaṃ loke parisaṃsthāpayiṣyati Gv 112.22.
