• krīḍanikā, rarely °nakā, with or without dhātrī (cf. AMg. kīlaṇa-dhāī, and also krīḍāpanikā, s.v. °naka), a nurse who amuses (plays with) an infant, regularly as one of four kinds of nurses: with dhātrī, Mvy 9481; in a cliché, Divy 99.26; 271.20; 441.23; Av i.15.12 (very often); MSV i.132.21 and (°nakā) iii.134.8; without dhātrī, in same cliché, Divy 3.13; 58.13; Av i.219.10; 346.5; 360.1, and in some other places (but less often). See s.v. kṣīra-dhātrī. [197,1]
