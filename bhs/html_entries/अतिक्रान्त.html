• atikrānta, adj. (ppp.), surpassing, wonderful, (speci- fically of appearance) beautiful: atikrāntā (so mss., Senart abhi°) ca mānuṣāḥ Mv iii.96.19; 100.20; °ta-varṇa, of beautiful appearance, LV 396.17; Mv iii.316.13; 317.11; also Mv ii.257.9; 258.7; iii.197.15; 302.10 (in these four Senart em. abhikrānta°). The equivalent Pali is regularly abhikkanta (-vaṇṇa), but atikkanta also occurs in the same sense (CPD s.v.). In BHS, in this sense, abhikrānta occurs, if at all, only in LV 398.11 °ta varṇa, see abhi- krānta 2. Since t and bh are easily confused, it may be that graphic confusion is here concerned. On the other hand, atikrānta is erroneously read, e.g. in Divy 462.13, for abhikrānta (1), which is correctly read in the same passage in MSV i.70.3, and elsewhere in that text. [009,1]
