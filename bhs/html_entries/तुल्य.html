• tulya, adj., used in a peculiar sense in SP, and as I think misunderstood by Burnouf and Kern: equal in the sense of equally available, open to choice (said of different forms of dharma, religion, and specifically thinking of the three yānas): tulye (so with Kashgar rec., text tulya-) nāma dharmadhātupraveśe SP 60.8 (prose), when entrance into the sphere of religion is, after all (nāma), alike (all the same, open to free choice); the sequel complains that the speaker has been given only the hīna yāna by the Buddha; tulyeṣu dharmeṣu SP 61.12; 62.2 (both vss). Chinese versions confirm this interpretation. [255,2]
