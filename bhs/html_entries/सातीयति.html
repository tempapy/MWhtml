• sātīyati, text, or sādīyati, Kashgar rec. (see below, and cf. svādīyati), in SP 277.8 (prose), interests oneself in, shows attachment to, takes pleasure in: (na ca śrāmaṇeraṃ) na ca śrāmaṇerīṃ na bhikṣuṃ na bhikṣuṇīṃ na kumā- rakaṃ na kumārikāṃ °yati, na ca taiḥ sārdhaṃ saṃstavaṃ karoti; Tib. mi sten, not adhere, be attached, to. Text and [Page590-b+ 71] etym. uncertain; sātīyati could be denom. from sāta = śāta, q.v.; sādīyati prob. = Pali sādiyati, derived by Childers and PTSD from root svād, cf. svādīyati; both these forms could perhaps be caus. pass. of svād- as sug- gested PTSD, or svād° may (as I am inclined to guess) be only an unhistorical Sktization, the orig. being denom. sātīyati, with sādī° showing Prakritic d for t.
