• akālaka, (1) nt. (from akāla), food (delicacies) suitable for eating at other than meal times (= akālakhādyaka, q.v.): śālīnām odanavidhim akālakam (so Senart em., mss. odanaṃ viditakālakaṃ, or °kārakaṃ) anekavyañjana-m- upetaṃ Mv i.306.13 (vs; otherwise Senart); tair °kāni sajjīkṛtāni Divy 130.22; (2) adj. (cf. Pali akāla-cīvara), of monks' robes, provided at extraordinary times: (akā)la- kaiś cīvaraiḥ MPS 40.54. [002,1]
