• sahita, adj. (1) (= Pali id.) of speech, connected, coherent, sensible: Mvy 474 (°tā, of Buddha's speech); sahitaṃ ca bhūtaṃ (true) ca sadā prabhāṣate SP 373.4; sahitaṃ…sumadhuraṃ…abravīt Mv i.145.7 = 201.7 = ii.5.2 (Senart i.490 wrongly kindly, agreeable); MSV ii.174.2 (read) tavāsahitam, mama sahitam; (2) of hair and eyebrows, in sahita-bhrū LV 107.8 and sahita-keśa 12, both in list of anuvyañjana of the Buddha (Senart also reads sahita-keśa in Mv ii.44.10, but read with mss. ahasita-keśa, q.v.); Tib. LV both times mñam pa = even, which usually renders Skt. sama and has both mgs., alike, and level, flat, smooth; prob. therefore with smooth (orig. connected, so close-growing; perhaps thick?) eyebrows and hair. These terms seem to be substitutions for, and roughly equivalent to, ślakṣṇa-bhrū and -keśa of other texts (Nos. 65 and 76 of my list of anuvyañjana), both of which are lacking in LV. In Pali, Therīg. 254, sahita is used of a young woman's hair compared to a grove: kānanaṃ va sahitaṃ (comm. 210.7 ghanasaṃnivesaṃ uddham eva uṭṭhita-uddhadīghasākhaṃ upavanaṃ viya; in accordance with this Mrs. Rhys Davids renders dense).
