• unmiñja, m. (to ud-miñj, cf. under miñj-; and next), apparently lit. opening, and so starting, initial development, initiation of something: Dbh 18.17 sattveṣu mahākaruṇon- miñjaḥ saṃbhavati, and 19 mahāmaitryunmiñjaḥ, of Bodhisattvas; Sukh 4.4 (udāraḥ khalu ta) unmiñjo bhadrikā mīmāṃsā kalyāṇaṃ pratibhānaṃ. Müller (SBE 49 Pt. 2, p. 4) translates question, stating that ‘all the Chinese translators’ translate so. [133,1]
