• jānaka, adj. (Pali Gr. id., Childers; AMg. jāṇaga; = prec., but prob. formed on the pres. jānati plus -aka), knowing, wise: jānako jānakavihārehi Mv i.34.12 (prose); jānakāḥ pṛcchakā Buddhā bhagavantaḥ Divy 184.26; 299.15; MSV i.i.12 (prose), Buddhas, (tho) knowing (the answers), are (in the habit of) asking (questions; other- wise Divy Index); a-j°, foolish, ignorant: SP 10.1 (so read with Kashgar rec. and WT for ajñā°, unmetr.); 37.5; 47.14; 93.12; LV 323.12 (all vss). With objective gen., acquainted with: jānako duḥkhasya Av ii.119.11; 120.3, 11. [241,1]
