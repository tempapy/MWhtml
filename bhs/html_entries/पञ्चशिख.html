• Pañcaśikha (1) m. (= Pali °sikha), n. of a celebrated gandharva: Mv iii.197.15 ff.; 215.5 ff.; Av i.95.8--9 ff.; 113.5; Samādh 19.11 ff., 37; king of gandharvas, Mmk 46.1; as in Pali (DPPN) sometimes regarded (like Śakra) as an office rather than an individual, so that a person may be reborn as the gandharva P., Mv ii.49.3; (2) f., or adj., °khā mahāmudrā (q.v.), a mudrā belonging to Mañjuśrī (cf. Lalou's theory cited s.v. Pañcacīra), Mmk [Page315-b+ 71] 26.15; printed °kha-mahāmudrā Mmk 37.8, but in 37.26--27 and 58.24 mahāmudrā(ṃ) pañcaśikhāṃ baddhvā, and so regularly (fem.). [315,1]
