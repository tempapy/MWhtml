• anupāna-paṭṭaka, nt. (v.l. anupāra°, so Mironov; v.l. in both edd. antupāra°), °kam Mvy 9030, acc. to Chin. a dish, bowl or saucer, that has a standard; Tib. phul baḥi btuṅ phor (or gtaṅ phor); this cpd., reading btuṅ phor, seems to mean drinking-cup for presentation; it is recorded in Das, s.v. phul ba, transl. by the BHS word here treated and by Eng. a drinking plate.
