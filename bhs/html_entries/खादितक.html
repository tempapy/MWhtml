• khāditaka (Skt. khādita plus specifying ka, § 22.39), in ardha-khāditako, (a corpse) that has been half eaten: Mv ii.78.11 (prose) udakahrade ardhakhāditako plavanto …dṛṣṭo, was seen, floating, a half-eaten thing (corpse), in the pool of water; 11--12 tena…ārocitaṃ, Dharmapālo udakarākṣasena khāyito ti, he reported, Dh. has been eaten (khāyito, no -ka! simple fact) by a water-ogre; but again 13--14 paśyati ca taṃ…udakarākṣasena ardhakhādita- kaṃ plavantaṃ, he saw him floating, in the state of having been half-eaten by the water-ogre. One ms. in this last passage has khāyitaṃ instead of ardhakhāditakaṃ; then simply eaten. [204,2]
