• vātātapa, m. pl. (ordinarily wind and sunshine as in Skt., e.g. Mv iii.350.10; but also) wind and heat as bodily humors (dhātu): Mv iii.143.16 abhiṣyaṇṇā (q.v.) vātātapā, resumed 144.6 by abhiṣyaṇṇehi dhātūhi, which, as well as the general context, appears to prove that ātapa is here one of the bodily humors (as well as vāta, which is normal in this sense), viz. = pitta (q.v., BR).
