• ātapta-kārin, adj., acting zealously: °rī Mvy 1806 = Tib. brtun ciṅ byed pa; °riṇa(s), n. pl., Mahāsamāj. 195.1; Waldschmidt, Kl. Skt. Texte 4 p. 5, regards ātapta as hyper-Skt. for Pali ātappa, nt., zeal, which PTSD derives from Skt. *ātāpya, to (Pali, not Skt.!) ātāpa, a much rarer form than ātappa in Pali; but cf. ātāpin, which is common also in Pali.
