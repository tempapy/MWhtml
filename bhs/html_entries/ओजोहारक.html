• ojo-hāraka, adj. or subst. m. (cf. prec., but see also s.v. 2 -hāra, hāraka), strength-robbing, -robber; or, as expression of purpose, to rob the strength (of people): Mv i.253.3, said of the 1000 sons of a yakṣiṇī: te 'pi vaiśāliṃ ojohārakā preṣitā, vaiśālim gatvā manuṣyāṇām ojaṃ haranti (so that they all became sick).
