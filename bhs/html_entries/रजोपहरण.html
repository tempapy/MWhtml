• rajopaharaṇa (i.e. rajo'pa°), gender unknown, sweeper, either personal, or (= rajoharaṇa) instrument of sweeping, broom: °ṇa-samacittena, mānātimānavivarja- natayā Gv 463.26; cited Śikṣ 35.8, where ed. rajoharaṇa-, but v.l. rajopa°; note cites Tib. as phyag dar ba, translated the act of sweeping, but this interpretation is not clear, and Bendall and Rouse render like a mere sweeper (personal); Tib. Dictt. only phyag dar pa (Das, sweeper, duster; perh. intending broom, mop?). [449,2]
