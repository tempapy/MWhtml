• niścikīrṣā, Mvy 2456; La Vallée Poussin, AbhidhK. iv.189, note 3; or °ṣu-tā, Bbh 168.22 (= Pali nijigiṃ- sanatā, or °gīs°), in lābhena lābha-(Bbh ed. lābhaṃ) niś° (also -niṣpādanā, q.v.), extraction of a profitable gift (from a layman, by a monk) by (referring to) a gift received (from others); one of the 5 mithyājīva for a monk; see references s.v. kuhana. Wogihara, Lex. 26, believes the original form was nirjigīṣā or °ṣutā, relying on the Pali; I believe the contrary. [306,2]
