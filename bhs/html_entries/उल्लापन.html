• ullāpana (to ullapati, q.v.; see also next two), (1) nt., shouting or yelling derisively, abusive derision (this may possibly be the mg. of Skt. ullāpa in khalollāpāḥ Ind. Spr. 2047, for which Boehtl. harte Worte von schlechten Menschen): Mvy 5227 °nam (follows uccagghanam) = Tib. (b)stiṅ pa, abuse, also rñiṅ pa (? for rñi, snare, trap? then belonging to mg. 2 below?); Chin. disdain, Jap. talking in a loud voice, abusing; Śikṣ 271.6 nāsty uccag- ghanollāpana-dānam, see uccagghana; acc. to note, Tib. here brid, which means deceit (ful), (not wheedling, coaxing, with Bendall,) but it seems clear from uccagghana that this is an error; (2) adj., deceitful, deceptive: LV 176.4 (vs) (saṃskāra…) bāla-ullāpana riktamuṣṭivat (so read, see this); 212.14 (kāmāḥ…) ullāpanā riktamuṣṭivat (q.v.); Śikṣ 77.3 bāllollāpanāḥ, deceiving fools, said of lusts; 261.8 °nam; RP 39.13 bāllollāpanaṃ, of worldly life, esp. royal pleasures; read so in Dbh 43.6 for bālālāpanaṃ.
