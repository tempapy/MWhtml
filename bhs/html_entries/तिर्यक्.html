• tiryak, adv. (used in the sense of Pali tiro, BHS tiras, tiraskṛta, q.v.; the converse use of Skt. tiras in the sense of tiryak, crosswise, is recorded by BR s.v. 2a from lexicons, and once in Mārk. Pur.; but in this one passage it seems to me that tiras may have its Pali mg. of outside, away, afar), outside, away, afar, in contrast with iha; neha na tiryak nobhayam antarā Śikṣ 252.15, not here, not afar, not between the two. (Bendall and Rouse, Transl. 234 line 2, across; but this seems manifest nonsense in the context.) [254,1]
