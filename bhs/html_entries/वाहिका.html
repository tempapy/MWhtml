• vāhikā, (manner of) carrying: (tā dāni antaḥpurikā tena sārdhaṃ viśvastaṃ krīḍanti keliṃ) kurvanti, pṛṣṭhi- maṃ (? see this) āruhitvā nānāvāhikāye vāhenti Mv ii.479.19, the harem-women…carried him, when he had mounted on their backs (in amorous sport), in various ways-of-carrying; or possibly, in the manner of various vehicles. Acc. to Cappeller, HOS 15, Glossar, vāhikā means Reihenfolge, das Nacheinander, Kir. 15.25; nānā- makes it difficult to understand it so here (but perhaps not impos- sible; variously taking turns in the sport?).
