• anāpatti, f. (noun, = Pali id.; neg. of āpatti, q.v.) no offense; unpunishableness: Divy 330.1 (read with mss. anāpattis, or °ttiḥ, followed by punctuation: there is no offense); similarly 544.17, 19; Śikṣ 11.8; anāpattiḥ kṣiptace- tasaḥ, °ttiḥ śuddhāśayabhūmipraviṣṭasya Bbh 160.27; ŚsP 56.5 (see s.v. anadhyāpatti). The Index to Divy renders guiltless, as if Bhvr., but wrongly; as in Pali, the word is always a Karmadh. [022,2]
