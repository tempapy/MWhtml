• cāraṇa (nt.; = Pali id., see below; = Skt. caraṇa), (1) conduct, practice; in Pali, Sn 162, comm. takes ā as m.c., which it could be in Mv i.177.2, but elsewhere in prose: Mv i.177.2 (vs) anupakruṣṭa-cāraṇā; Gv 333.2 dharmāṇāṃ grahaṇa-cāraṇa-pratibodhiṣu (dvandva); (2) trained, skilled behavior; practice of an acquired art (Tib. cited as bslab pa, regularly = śikṣā): MSV i.34.11 (hasti- damako…) hastiratnam ekāhnā sarva-cāraṇebhir upa- saṃkrāmati, and in sequel; likewise with aśvaratnam (both of a cakravartin) i.35.11 ff.; both prose; (3) (rare in Skt.: pasturing, tending of animals: MSV iv.227.18.)
