• pratipraśrambha(ya)ti, (or °sram°; see praśrabh- yate; = Pali paṭippassambheti; PTSD records also °mbhati as pass. or intrans., but only °śrabhyate or °srabhyate, °ti, is so used in BHS), quiets, allays, puts an end to, regularly said of evils, but rarely also, apparently, of good things: °mbhayati, °yanti, duḥkhāni Bbh 63.10; Megh 296.13; Dbh 83.26; opt. °śrambheyuḥ, for °bhayeyuḥ, Megh 292.19 (duḥkhāni); inf. °śrambhayituṃ Mv i.254.2 (vyādhiṃ); ger. °śrabhya or °srabhya, duḥkhāni Mmk 182.3; Dbh 8.17; ṛddhyabhisaṃskārān (once written °rām, 163.3) Divy 161.11; 163.3; 190.22; 494.22; sarvakar- māntān Divy 549.10; in Gv 53.23 (Mañjuśrīḥ) sattvānāṃ dharmadeśanādhiṣṭhānaṃ pratipraśrabhya prakrāntaḥ, having brought to an end (by fulfilment), completed (?); pass. °srabhyati, subject disease (vyādhi), Mv i.253.9(?); 10 ff.; °bhyate or °bhyante, kāraṇā-viśeṣāḥ Divy 68.3; 138.10; 367.1; 568.15; Av i.4.11; 10.11, etc.; duḥkhāni Mmk 183.6.
