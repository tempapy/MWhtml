• ullapati, or ullāpayati (see s.v. ullāpana; in Skt. ullapati recorded only in mg. wheedle, flatter, coax; in Pali app. only boast, brag (falsely); noun derivs. show mgs. pointing to deceive, but no verb forms with this mg. have been noted), shouts or yells derisively at (acc.); associated with uccagghati, q.v.: SP 382.12 (prose) ye te taṃ bodhisattvaṃ…ullāpitavanta (but most mss. ullap°) ucca(g)ghitavantaḥ; 482.6 sūtrāntalekhakānām uccag- ghanaṃ kariṣyanty ullapiṣyanti; AsP 385.13 and 388.19 ullāpayiṣyati (see uccagghati).
