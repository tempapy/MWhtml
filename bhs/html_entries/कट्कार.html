• kaṭkāra, m. (so also Mironov; vv.ll. in the two edd. kadgara, kaṭkara, kaṅkara), some kind of bird: Mvy 4894, acc. to Tib. (one rendering) and Chin. = the prec. item, krakara, 4893, which in Skt. means a kind of part- ridge; cf. also Pali kakkara, said to mean jungle-cock; Tib. also bya baṅ, which acc. to Jä. means night-hawk, goat- sucker; or bat.
