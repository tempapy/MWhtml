• Namuci (= Pali id.), a name for Māra, used chiefly in vss (but also in prose, Dbh 28.17; 90.5); LV 261.6; 302.21 ff.; 311.15; 328.3; 357.14; Mv i.264.9; ii.238.6; 413.2; [Page291-a+ 71] iii.254.6; 381.10; Divy 393.11; Mmk 171.18; Samādh 19.36; RP 58.15; Namucibalanudaṃ, not n. pr. but ep. of the Bodhisattva, Mv i.208.12 = ii.10.20; in same cpd. with Māra, nihata-namuci-mārā(ḥ) Dbh.g. 52(78).4, Bhvr.; pl., perh. used of Māra and his hosts, or like the pl. of Māra, q.v.: tāṃ namucināṃ (but v.l. °cino, gen. sg.) mahatīm avasthām LV 356.9.
