• 1 yaṣṭi, f. (= Skt. iṣṭi, cf. prec.), sacrifice: yaṣṭi- (stem in comp.) Mv iii.145.20 (prose; so both mss. intend, Senart em. iṣṭi-); yaṣṭyā RP 54.18 (vs). [445,2]
  • 2 yaṣṭi, f. (Skt. id.), in Mv used of a marvelous kind of flagpole which is said to have adorned the capital cities of five former Buddhas; that of the city of Dīpavatī was named Valguyā, of the other four Valayā, qq.v.; it is described in Mv i.196.15 ff. as citrā darśanīyā, of seven colors (gold, silver, and jewels), 12 yojanas high and four in diameter; so iii.229.12 ff.; 232.9 ff.; the cliché is abbre- viated iii.234.11 f.; 238.14.
