• Nanda, (1) (= Pali id., DPPN Nanda Thera 1; also called Sundarananda, q.v.) n. of a monk, disciple of the Buddha, and his half-brother: Mv iii.132.20 (mama, sc. Buddha's, pitriyaputro); prob. also meant by Mvy 1041; 3604; LV 2.2; Sukh 2.11; 92.7; is he also the Nanda of Mvy 9471? (one of the ṣaḍvārgika monks, cf. Upananda (1) and Nandopananda); cf. also Nandana (1); (2) n. of a nāga-king, always associated and almost always compounded (regularly as dvandva, Nandopanandau or °nandakau) with Upananda(-ka), q.v.: SP 4.11 (here not a cpd.); LV 83.21; 204.10; Mvy 3278; Divy 162.9; 395.11; Suv 162.9; Kv 2.13; Mmk 62.2; 437.2; Gv 119.11; Karmav 72.18; Māy 221.18; 246.17; 247.33; (3) n. of a monk in a Jātaka story: Mv i.36.6 ff.; (4) n. of a devaputra: Mv ii.257.7, 12 etc.; LV 4.12; 6.12; 7.5; same (?) LV 438.16; (cf. DPPN Nanda 8?); (5) n. of a teacher: Mvy 3501; (6) n. of a Śākya youth (same as 1 above?): LV 152.12, and perhaps Av i.148.9; (7) n. of a king, said to be grand- father of Aśoka: Divy 369.12; (8) n. of a son of a śreṣṭhin of Śrāvastī, called ‘the lazy’: Av i.15.10; (9) n. of a yakṣa: Māy 17; 235.19; (10) n. of a cowherd, converted by Buddha: MSV i.51.1 ff.
