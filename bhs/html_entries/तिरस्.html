• tiras-, tiraskṛta- (cf. Pali tiro, outside, esp. as prior member of cpds.), in comp. with -prātiveśya, taken by Divy Index (and pw) as meaning near (neighbor), but rather outside (neighbor), (neighbor) living outside (one's own house): tiraḥprātiveśya-suhṛt-svajanādibhyo Divy 234.24; tena tiraḥprātiveśyāḥ pṛṣṭāḥ 272.4; tiraskṛta- prātiveśya-sajana-(read -svajana-?)-yuvatyaś 235.19. See also s.v. tiryak, where it is suggested that even Skt. tiras may have this mg. in Mārk. Pur. 17.3 (BR s.v. 2a). [253,2]
