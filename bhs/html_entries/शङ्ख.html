• śaṅkha (also written saṃkha, Mv), (1) m. (Pali saṅkha), n. of one of the four ‘great treasures’, (saṃkha) Mv iii.383.18, and of the ‘king’ who presides over it, Divy 61.4 (see s.v. elapatra); as a nāga, known in Skt.; Divy 61.4 mentions this Ś. in juxtaposition with (2) but the relation between them is not made clear; in Pali the two have no connexion, (1) being only a ‘treasure’ DN comm. i.284.8 f.; (2) (= Pali id., DPPN Saṅkha 3), n. of a future emperor: Divy 60.14 ff.; (3) n. of a rākṣasa, = Śaṅkhanābha: Divy 104.8.
