• karaṇīya (nt.; = Pali id.; rare in Skt., but cf. pw 2.297), affair, business, duty (thing to be done, fundamentally gdve.): deva-karaṇīyeṣu Mv i.32.12 (gods occupied) in affairs, matters of business, of the gods; see sa-karaṇīya; prob. here also cakṣu-karaṇīyā Mv iii.331.8, 10, 15, jñāna- kar° 11 (of the madhyamā pratipadā), having insight (knowledge) as its business, i.e. cause of insight (knowledge); in Pali parallels cakkhu-karaṇī, ñāna-karaṇī.
