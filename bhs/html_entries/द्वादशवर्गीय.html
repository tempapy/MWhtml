• dvādaśavargīya, f. °yā, pl., belonging to a group of twelve (nuns; clearly not heretics as stated in Index): °yābhiḥ śrutam Divy 493.12; °yābhir 495.23. No such group has been discovered in Pali; the context in Divy is my only source of knowledge of the meaning. It can hardly mean members of the dvādaśavarga, q.v., in the sense of quorum for ordination. Apparently these nuns were given to cavilling and trouble-making; they object to functions assigned to (Cūḍa-)Panthaka. [273,2]
