• adhyāśayati (rare; doubtless denom. from adhyā- śaya, q.v.; Pali only ppp. ajjhāsita intent upon, rare and only in cpds.), is intent upon, with loc.: bodhisattvo 'dhyāśayaty anuttarāyāṃ samyaksaṃbodhau Śikṣ 17.21. In LV 18.8 the good ms. A reads adhyāśayati for text °sayati (mahāpṛthivīṃ…abhinirjityādhyāsayati), is firm- ly fixed upon, occupies, with acc.; but probably text is right; Skt. adhyāste is used in this sense, and the LV form is only an aya extension of this; the meaning and construction do not fit adhyāśayati.
