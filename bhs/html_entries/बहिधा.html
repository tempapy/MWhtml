• bahidhā, prob. m.c. for (Skt.) bahirdhā or (= Pali) bahiddhā, in the sense (also in Pali) of outside of the Buddhist faith: tīrthikā ca bahidhānugatāś ca Mv i.69.17 (vs), heretics and followers of outside (teachers); so Senart, plausibly; mss. mostly vahidānu°, one vahir-anu°; the 2d syllable must be short, the 3d long, metrically.
