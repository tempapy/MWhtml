• anutsanna, neg. ppp. (= Pali anussanna; cf. prec., utsadana 2, and Skt. utsanna, stärker als normal, pw s.v. ud-sad), not excessive (in extent, number, or the like; cf. anutsada): Mv iii.343.4 °naś ca, of the Buddha's voice, not excessive(ly loud), not too full; Mv ii.43.12, read an- utsanna-gātrā (mss. quoted anusanta; Senart em. an- usandhi), limbs not excessive (too many), one (29th in my list) of the anuvyañjana, q.v.; corresponding to Pali anussannāsannasabbagattatā (CPD), condition of having all his limbs not too many nor deficient.
