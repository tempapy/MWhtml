• sa-pratīśa or °sa, adj. (see s.v. a-pratīśa; = Pali sappaṭissa, °tissa), respectful: spelled with ś, Mvy 1776; Mv iii.372.5; Divy 333.16, 26; Śikṣ 189.10; KP 10.6; °śa-tā, state of being…, Bhīk 31b.5; with s, Mv i.174.3; ii.287.6; LV 102.21, text su-pra°, read with v.l. sa-pra°; Divy 484.15, 20 (mss. s, ed. em. ś); Mmk 491.15; mss. vary, ś or s, Mv ii.258.9; iii.345.18; Divy 484.25; in Mv ii.200.19 Senart by em. sapatissa-, but with not even remote or partial support in mss., and resulting in incor- rect meter; in LV 160.10 Lefm. sa-pratīkṣāḥ (which would accord with Senart's conjecture as to the etymology, see s.v. apratīśa), but no ms. supports the reading; they point rather to sapratīśāḥ, tho most are confused. Usually preceded by sa-gaurava (°gārava). [559,2]
