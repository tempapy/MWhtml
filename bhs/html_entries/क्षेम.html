• kṣema, (1, as in Skt., adj. causing peace and comfort, or n. comfort: Mv i.302.1 kiṃ kṣemaṃ, what (can we do that) will cause you peace and comfort ? In the verse form 303.21 replaced by kiṃ karoma. Response to a cry of distress; foll. by kiṃ avidhāvidhaṃ ti krandasi.Senart fails to understand.) (2) n. of a king of old (= Pali Khema, oftener Khemaṃkara, q.v. DPPN): Divy 242.5 (his capi- tal was Kṣemāvatī, q.v.); (3) n. of a Buddha: Mmk 365.17 (vs, but submetrical; one syllable short).
