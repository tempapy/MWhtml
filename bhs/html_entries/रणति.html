• raṇati (unrecorded in this mg.), acc. to Bendall and Rouse makes contact with (loc.), perhaps rather delights in (so Vedic), or better yet is attached to, is (wrongly) involved with, contaminated by (in the sense of the Pali-BHS noun raṇa, q.v.): Śikṣ 263.9--11 cakṣū rūpeṣu na raṇati, śrotraṃ śabdeṣu (so punctuate), yāvan mano dharmeṣu na raṇati …saṃsargābhāvāt, na hi cakṣū rūpeṇa saṃsṛjyate… yan na saṃsṛjyate, tan na raṇati; advitīyasya…dharma- sya raṇaṃ nāsti.Note the noun raṇaṃ in the last sentence: …for the eye does not come in contact with form…what does not come in contact is not contaminated; of an isolated state-of-being there is no contamination.
