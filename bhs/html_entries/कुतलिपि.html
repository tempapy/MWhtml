• kuta-lipi (or, with v.l., kutana-lipi), some kind of script: Mv i.135.6. Senart thinks of reading kuṭa, referring to Kuṭaka as n. of a people, but this is recorded only once from the BhāgP., and seems implausible here. The word seems to correspond to kirāta-lipi of LV 125.21. [186,1]
