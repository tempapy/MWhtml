• mātāpitṛ, sg. or pl. (not otherwise noted in this sense), family, relatives, ‘home-folks’: (a king has granted a wish to a courtesan, his concubine; she replies) Mv i.348.16--17 mahārāja yāvatā khu mātāpitaraṃ āpṛcchāmi tato devasya sakāśāto varaṃ yācayiṣyāmi. tāye mātāpitṝ- ṇāṃ ārocitam. (18--349.1) tehi dāni yaṃ mataṃ so taṃ jalpati…tahiṃ aparā parivrājikā (2) paṇḍitā…sā āha: Jenti, tvaṃ vailāsikāye dhītā…It is obvious that a plurality of persons is meant here, and certainly not the ‘mother and father’ of Jentī. On this basis I think it likely that in LV 279.11 mātāpitṛsaṃjñino means regarding (each other) as members of the same family: (sarvasattvāś ca tasmin samaye maitracittā hitacittāḥ) parasparaṃ mātā° ‘bhūvan.
