• pratyuddhāra, m. (to praty-ud-dharati, q.v., but in somewhat different mg.; cf. next), the act of taking back (approval, or a gift, once given): chanda-°raḥ Mvy 8477 = Tib. dad pa phyir bsgyur ba (approving, then later changing); so Jap., which understands the monkish rules of discipline as the object; Chin. first showing respect for another (person, or possibly thing) then going back on it; changing one's mind; so interpret pratyuddhāra-tām avabhāsa-tāṃ (q.v.) ca pratilabdhukāmena Śikṣ 140.6, by one who (wrong- fully) seeks to take back (prob. a gift he has promised, see next)… [378,1]
