• Keśin, n. of the supernatural horse (the Bodhisattva) who saves shipwrecked persons from the island of ogresses (story of Jāt. 196, Valāhassa-J.): Mv iii.72.18; 75.11, 17 f.; 77.1. This name occurs only in Mv and only in the prose version; in the verse he is called Valāha or Vālāha, qq.v., as in other BHS and Pali versions. [193,1]
