• saṃghaṭita, ppp. (of saṃghaṭ(ay)ati, in unrecorded mg.), in SP 249.5 (prose) prob. shrunken, shrivelled (or possibly drawn in, contracted): (Prabhūtaratnas…) pari- śuṣkagātraḥ saṃghaṭitakāyo yathāsamādhisamāpannas tathā saṃdṛśyate sma; Tib. skum, drawn in, contracted, also paralyzed (Das); prob. the cpd. (°kāyo) is near-synonym of pariśuṣkagātraḥ, the correctness of which is supported by Tib. kun bskams, very dry; but possibly referring to temporary contraction of limbs in samādhi-posture; perh. same mg., contracted, limited or the like, with neg. a-, substantially free, in LV 272.13 (prose), in series of epithets of Bodhisattva,…askhalitagatir a-saṃghaṭita-gatir alīna- gatir…; Tib. ḥdrud pa med paḥi, not rubbed or dragged, perh. based on a-saṃghaṭṭita which is read in Calc. but in none of Lefm.'s mss.
