• sāntika, in adv. forms °ke and °kāt (= Pali santike, °kā; see santika, °ke, of which this may well be a secondary Sktization; but it reveals the true origin of the MIndic form), in or from the presence (of, gen.): buddhasya °ke Karmav 161.2, 4; °ke, °kāt, acc. to Kern, Preface ix, Kashgar rec. of SP for Nep. antike, °kāt; so sāntikātu, v.l. of Kashgar rec. 119.3; for other such cases see santike.
