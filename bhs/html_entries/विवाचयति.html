• vivācayati, (?) vipācayati (= Pali vipāceti), grum- bles, makes disapproving remarks: °yanti Mvy 2643 (= Tib. kha zer ba, acc. to Das = mukhara, loquacious, but Lex. also abusive); Divy 492.17; MSV i.44.16; 236.13; °yati Mvy 9360, but here Mironov vipācayati (= Pali), and Tib. rnam par smod pa, speaks abusively; always the third of a series of near-synonyms, avadhyāyati (Skt.) and kṣipati (Skt.; in Divy dhriyati, in Mv perhaps kṣīyati, q.v., in a similar context) being the others; in Pali, ujjhāy- ati khīyati vipāceti. Orig. form not clear; if it was vipāc°, what etym.? Yet vivāc° looks like a lect. fac., and is not really a natural form (caus. to vi-vac-?); the occurrence of vipāc- in Mironov also makes one doubtful. Perhaps vipācayati was a bit of ancient slang: gets (one) cooked = reviles. [500,1]
