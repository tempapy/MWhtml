• antarā-parinirvāyin, adj. (= Pali °nibbāyī), acc. to Tib. on Mvy 1015 (bar ma dor, for antarā) attaining nirvāṇa in the intermediate state (between death and re- birth; i.e. before the soul can be reborn in a new existence); Dharmas 103; Mv i.33.6. Acc. to CPD, rather entering nirvāṇa before the term, i.e. before having passed the first half of life (in the middle of life). This seems to be the orthodox Pali interpretation, but the Tib. interpretation was also known (cf. CPD s.v. antarābhava); it is refuted in Kathāvatthu viii.2 (Transl. pp. 212--3). [039,1]
