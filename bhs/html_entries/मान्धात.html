• Māndhāta, °tṛ (and other forms, see below; = Pali Mandhātā, n. sg., stem °tu-; there seems to be nothing in Buddhist legend suggesting identity with Skt. Mān- dhātṛ, except the name), n. of an ancient cakravartin king, sometimes (e.g. in Laṅk, also in Pali) regarded as a pre- vious incarnation of Śākyamuni: °taḥ, °to, n. sg., Mvy 3558; Mv i.348.9; Divy 210.20; 214.20; °tasya Mv i.348.9; Divy 210.21; 213.23; MSV i.67.14 ff.; 93.6 ff.; Mmk 609.1; °tṛ- (stem in comp.) Karmav 37.3; °tā, n. sg., Mv i.154.1; Divy 210.23; 214.21; Karmav 36.11; Laṅk 141.5; Mmk 608.22; °tuḥ, gen. sg., Divy 278.13; 576.10, etc.; Mādhātaḥ, cited as used by ‘some’, Divy 210.21; Mūrdhātaḥ (q.v.), as n. sg. Mvy 3557 (so v.l. of Mironov, who reads Mūr- dhagataḥ, Kyoto ed. Mūrdhataḥ), also Divy 210.19, and forms of this stem are much used in this Divy and MSV story, interchangeably with stems Māndhāta and °tṛ; e.g. Mūrdhātasya Divy 212.9, 18; °tena 212.19, 22. [430,1]
