• aneya, adj., hyper-Skt. (or Pkt. dial., Pischel 236) for MIndic (Pali) aneja (Pkt. aṇeja), immovable, or free from desire; ep. of Buddha, Mv ii.35.6; iii.93.7; 121.5; (not of Buddha; text fragmentary; app. firm, hard to move) Mahāsamājasūtra, Waldschmidt, Kl. Skt. Texte 4 p. 159, last line. Interpretation correct in Waldschmidt; Senart thinks = ananyaneya, q.v., but there is no support for this in Pali or elsewhere. The Pali parallel to the Mahāsa- māj. verse, DN ii.254.18, actually reads anejā, proving our interpretation. [037,1]
