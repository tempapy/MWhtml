• āvarjanā = āvarjana, winning to oneself, the making kindly disposed: in LV 245.14--15--read: bodhisattvo rudrakasya rāmaputrasya saśiṣyasyāvarjanāṃ (so 2 mss. incl. the best; ed. °janī-) kṛtvā…prakrāmad. (The gen. requires noun āvarjanāṃ.) Tib. ḥdun par byas nas, which is wrongly rendered by Foucaux; it appears to mean lit. having made reconciled or desirous, i.e. having made to be of good will (towards himself, the Bodhisattva).
