• piṅgala, m. (Skt. Lex. id., as Jain term; AMg. piṃgalaya), n. of one of the four ‘great treasures’, Mv iii.383.19, or of the ‘king’ who guards it, Divy 61.3; see s.v. elapatra; doubtless this guardian is the same as the nāga so named Mvy 3315; Māy 247.2; as n. of a yakṣa Suv 161.13 (?), see Kapila.
