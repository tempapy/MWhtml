• bhūta-koṭi (°ṭī?), f. (cf. koṭi, end, goal; and Pali koṭi-gata, having reached the end = perfection, salvation), the true goal: listed Mvy 1708 among ‘synonyms for para- mārtha, the supreme truth’ (note 1707 tattvam, 1709 tathātā, etc., all intellectual terms; not nirvāṇa!) = Tib. yaṅ dag paḥi mthaḥ, real end; Mvy 407 sthito bhūtako- ṭyām, of a Tathāgata, abiding in… (presumably absolute truth); LV 351.8 jñātā dharmatathatā avabuddhā bhūta- koṭiḥ; 423.1 bhūtakoṭy-avikopana-cakraṃ, the wheel which does not disturb (move, upset, confuse) the true end (per- fect knowledge or enlightenment); 428.11 bhūtakoṭy-avi- kopita-jñāna-viṣayatvād avitathānanyathāvādī; 437.1 bhūtakoṭīm (v.l. °ṭim) akoṭīṃ (v.l. °ṭiṃ) ca tathatāyāṃ tathatvataḥ…dharmacakraṃ nirucyate, the wheel of the law is declared to be the true end (goal, conclusion), and that which has no end (or beginning, in time).
