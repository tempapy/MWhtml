ET-CAETERA¦. Expressed by ādiḥ -di in the m. or n. gender and in the plur. or sing. numb.; as, ‘parrots, starlings, &c.,’ śukasārikādayaḥ; ‘Indra, Kuvera, &c.,’ indrakuverādayaḥ; ‘Of Agni, Pavana, &c.,’ agnipavanādīnāṃ; ‘the nose, eyes, &c.,’ nāsacakṣurādīni; ‘peace, war, &c.’ sandhivigrahādi; ‘by liberality, &c.,’ dātnādinā; ‘from liberality, &c.,’ dānādeḥ (for dānādinaḥ). Sometimes iti is prefixed to ādi; as, ‘the word chitralig, &c.,’ citraligityādi. Sometimes ādikaḥ -kā -kaṃ, ādyaḥ -dyā -dyaṃ and prabhṛti are used for ādi; as, ‘gifts, &c.,’ dānādikaṃ; ‘Indra, &c.,’ indrādyāḥ, or indraprabhṛtayaḥ pl.
