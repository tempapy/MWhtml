BY, prep. (By the side of) expressed by upaupa prefixed; as, ‘he sits by me,’ māmmām upāsteupāste; ‘a tank by a well,’ upakūpajalāśayaḥupakūpajalāśaya. — (Beside, denoting passage) expressed by atiati prefixed; as, ‘he passes by me,’ māmmām atyetiatyeti. — (Denoting the agent, instrument, cause, means or manner) expressed by the instr. case; as, ‘by him,’ ‘by that,’ ‘on that account,’ ‘by that means,’ ‘in that manner,’ may all be expressed by tenatena. In modern Sanskrit the instrument and agent are often expressed by dvārādvārā or dvāreṇadvāreṇa and karttṛkakarttṛkakartṛka; as, ‘to hit with a stick,’ kāṣṭhadvārākāṣṭhadvārā or kāṣṭhadvāreṇakāṣṭhadvāreṇa taḍtaḍ; ‘sacrifices by sages,’ ṛṣikarttṛkayajñāḥṛṣikarttṛkayajñāṛṣikartṛkayajñā. ‘By,’ in some of these senses, may often be expressed by the indec. part.; ‘a man may be happy by doing penance,’ tapastapas taptvātaptvā sukhīsukhī bhavetbhavet. — (According to) anurūpaṃanurūpaanurūpam, anusāreṇaanusāreṇa; ‘by rule,’ vidhivatvidhivat, vidhitasvidhitas, yathāvidhiyathāvidhi. — (As soon as, not later than) madhyemadhye; as, ‘by next year,’ āgāmivatsaramadhyeāgāmivatsaramadhye. — (The quantity at one time) expressed by the affix śasśas; as, ‘by thousands,’ sahasraśassahasraśas; ‘by twos and threes,’ dviśasdviśas triśastriśas; ‘by companies,’ paṃktibhiḥpaṃktibhi. — (Successive action) expressed by pratiprati or anuanu, or by repeating the word; as, ‘man by man,’ pratipuruṣaṃpratipuruṣapratipuruṣam; ‘day by day,’ pratidivaṇaṃpratidivaṇapratidivaṇam or anudivasaṃanudivasaanudivasam or divasedivase divasedivase. — (By himself, alone) ekāntatasekāntatas, nibhṛtaṃnibhṛtanibhṛtam, rahasirahasi, rahībhūtaḥrahībhūta -tātātā -taṃtatamtaṃ. — (In swearing or adjuring) expressed by the instr. c.; as, ‘swear not at all, neither by heaven, nor by earth,’ kamapikamapi śapathaṃśapathaśapatham māmā 'kārṣṭakārṣṭa svargeṇasvargeṇa nana pṛthivyāpṛthivyā nana; ‘by name,’ nāmnānāmnā, nāmatasnāmatas, nāmadheyatasnāmadheyatas; ‘by force,’ balātbalāt, balātkāreṇabalātkāreṇa, prasahmaprasahma; ‘by chance,’ daivātdaivāt; ‘by day,’ divādivā.
