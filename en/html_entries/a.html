A. As an indefinite article before nouns singular, for the most part not expressed in Sanskrit; as, ‘a lion,’ siṃhaḥsiṃha; (One) ekaḥeka -kākā -kaṃkakamkaṃ. — (Some, a certain) kaścit; as, ‘a certain man,’ kaścit puruṣaḥpuruṣa. — (Each, every) expressed by prati or anu, before the acc. neut.; as, ‘a day,’ ‘each day,’ pratidinaṃpratidinapratidinam or anudinaṃanudinaanudinam. When ‘a’ comes before a participle, after a verb of motion, it is expressed by the dat. c.; as, ‘he went a hunting,’ mṛgayāyai gataḥgata; or by arthaṃarthaartham preceded by the crude form of a noun; as, mṛgayārthaṃmṛgayārthamṛgayārtham gataḥgata; or by the infinitive; as, mṛgayāṃmṛgayām karttuṃkarttumkartum gataḥgata.
