SHĀSTRA, SHĀSTER, s. A term applied to scientific or philo- sophical treatises and books of law. There are properly only six Ṣāstras, comprising the five philosophical sys- tems (see SCHOOL), and the code of legal institutes; viz. 1. mīmāṃsāśāstraṃmīmāṃsāśāstramīmāṃsāśāstram or pūrvvamīmāṃsāśāstraṃpūrvvamīmāṃsāśāstrapūrvvamīmāṃsāśāstrampūrvamīmāṃsāśāstrapūrvamīmāṃsāśāstram, 2. sāṃkhyaśāstraṃsāṃkhyaśāstrasāṃkhyaśāstram, 3. vedāntaśāstraṃvedāntaśāstravedāntaśāstram, 4. nyāyaśāstraṃnyāyaśāstranyāyaśāstram, including under it the vaiśeṣikaśāstraṃvaiśeṣikaśāstravaiśeṣikaśāstram, 5. yogaśāstraṃyogaśāstrayogaśāstram, 6. dharmmaśāstraṃdharmmaśāstradharmmaśāstramdharmaśāstradharmaśāstram. The last includes the celebrated code of Manu or mānavadharmmaśāstraṃmānavadharmmaśāstramānavadharmmaśāstrammānavadharmaśāstramānavadharmaśāstram. The term Ṣāstra is also applied to less important branches of literature, and when used in the singular number denotes the body of all that has been written on each subject; as, ‘Treatises on rhetoric,’ alaṅkāraśāstraṃalaṅkāraśāstraalaṅkāraśāstram; ‘poetical works,’ kāvyaśāstraṃkāvyaśāstrakāvyaśāstram; ‘Treatises on the mechanical arts,’ śilpiśāstraṃśilpiśāstraśilpiśāstram.
