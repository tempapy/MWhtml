To INDUCE¦, v. a. anunī (c. 1. -nayati -netuṃ), ānī, nī. — (Incite to any action by argument, &c.) suhetuvādena or cāṭūktyā kasmiṃścit karmmaṇi pravṛt (c. 10. -varttayati -yituṃ) or niyuj (c. 10. -yojayati -yituṃ) or prayuj, protsah (c. 10. -sāhayati -yituṃ), utsah, samutsah, ākṛṣ (c. 1. -karṣati -kraṣṭuṃ), pralubh (c. 10. -lobhayati -yituṃ). Sometimes the sense of ‘induce’ may be expressed by the causal form; as, ‘to induce to return,’ nivṛt in caus. (-varttayati -yituṃ); ‘he induces her to go home,’ tāṃ gṛhaṃ gamayati. — (Bring on, produce) āvah (c. 1. -vahati -voḍhuṃ), utpad (c. 10. -pādayati -yituṃ). jan (c. 10. janayati -yituṃ).
