PREFERABLE, a. (Worthy to be chosen before, more desirable or excellent) varaṃvaravaram indec., varīyānvarīyān -yasīyasīyasī -yaḥyayaḥ (s)s, variṣṭhaḥvariṣṭha -ṣṭhāṣṭhāṣṭhā -ṣṭhaṃṣṭhaṣṭhamṣṭhaṃ, varaṇīyaḥvaraṇīya -yāyāyā -yaṃyayamyaṃ, pūrvvavaraṇīyaḥpūrvvavaraṇīyapūrvavaraṇīya &c., adhikavaraṇīyaḥadhikavaraṇīya &c., pūrvvagrāhyaḥpūrvvagrāhyapūrvagrāhya -hyāhyāhyā hyaM, praTamagrAhyaH#} &c., agragrāhyaḥagragrāhya &c., śreyānśreyān &c., śreṣṭhaḥśreṣṭha &c., garīyāngarīyān &c., adhikaṃadhikaadhikam prārthanīyaḥprārthanīya -yāyāyā -yaṃyayamyaṃ. The adverb varaṃvaravaram is usually followed by nana caca or nana punarpunar; as, ‘death is preferable to poverty,’ varaṃvaravaram mṛtyūrmṛtyūr nana caca dāridraṃdāridradāridram; ‘death is preferable to intercourse with the base,’ varaṃvaravaram prāṇatyāgoprāṇatyāgo nana punarpunar adhamānāmadhamānām upagamaḥupagama.
