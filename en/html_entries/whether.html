WHETHER, adv. kiṃkim, kiṃvākiṃvā, vāvā, utauta. The particle vāvā, either with or without kiṃkim, usually expresses the sense of ‘whether,’ as in the following examples; ‘whether you kill me or whether you release me,’ māṃmām mārayatamārayata vāvā muñcatamuñcata vāvā; ‘do you know whether he is virtuous or not?’ vetsivetsi kiṃkim dhārmmikodhārmmikodhārmiko nana vāvā; ‘whether or not he does that,’ tattat karotukarotu nana karotukarotu vāvā. — (Whether of twain, which of two) kataraḥkatara -rārārā -ratratrat.
