SACRAMENT, s. saṃskāraḥsaṃskāra, mahāyajñaḥmahāyajña. For the twelve sanskāras or sacred symbolical rites, see RITE. The following are the five mahā-yajnas, or great sacraments of the Hindūs (as explained in Manu III. 70. et. seq.), appointed to be per- formed every day by the Grihastha, or house-keeper, for the sake of expiating the sin incurred by the involuntary destruction of minute living creatures. They refer re- spectively to what is due to five objects, viz. himself, the gods, his fore-fathers, spirits or ghosts, and men or guests. 1. Study of Scripture, with prayer and medita- tion, called brahmayajñaḥbrahmayajña or ahutaḥahuta. 2. Offering oblations to the gods in fire, called devayajñaḥdevayajña or hutaḥhuta. 3. Offering libations of water and cakes to the manes of deceased ancestors, called pitṛyajñaḥpitṛyajña or prāśitaṃprāśitaprāśitam or śrāddhaṃśrāddhaśrāddham. 4. Casting the residue of oblations on the ground or in water, as an offering to spirits or goblins, called bhūtayajñaḥbhūtayajña or baliyajñaḥbaliyajña or prahutaḥprahuta or āhutaḥāhuta or baliḥbali m. 5. Receiving guests, and especially Brahmans, with hospitality, called nṛyajñaḥnṛyajña or manuppayajñaḥmanuppayajña or brāhmyahutaṃbrāhmyahutabrāhmyahutam or brāhmyaṃbrāhmyabrāhmyam or brahmahutaṃbrahmahutabrahmahutam. The Viṣnu-Purāna adds two other sacraments, viz. ‘propagation of offspring,’ prajāpatiyajñaḥprajāpatiyajña, and ‘observance of truth,’ satyayajñaḥsatyayajña, but these are com- paratively modern additions. The Christian idea of a sa- crament may be expressed by saṃskāraḥsaṃskāra or khrīṣṭaprakalpitasaṃskāraḥkhrīṣṭaprakalpitasaṃskāra; as, ‘the sacrament of baptism,’ jalasaṃskāraḥjalasaṃskāra or jalasecanasaṃskāraḥjalasecanasaṃskāra or majjanasaṃskāraḥmajjanasaṃskāra; ‘of the Lord's Supper,’ khrīṣṭabhojanasaṃskāraḥkhrīṣṭabhojanasaṃskāra or khrīṣṭamṛnyuramaraṇārthakasaṃskāraḥkhrīṣṭamṛnyuramaraṇārthakasaṃskāra.
