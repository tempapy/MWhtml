The sacred writings of the Hindūs may be arranged under seven heads, viz. 1. Vedas, 2. Upa- nishads, 3. Vedāngas, 4. Upavedas, 5. Ṣāstras, 6. Purānas and 7. Upapurānas. The Student is referred to each separate head for a fuller explanation than is here given of the subdivisions comprised under the several titles. 1. The Vedas are four in number, viz. ṛgvedaḥṛgveda, yajurvedaḥyajurveda, sāmavedaḥsāmaveda, atharvavedaḥatharvaveda. 2. The Upanishads, are the theological and philosophical chapters of the Vedas, either detached from, or comprehended in, the principal work, and collectively called vedāntaḥvedānta. 3. The Vedāngas are six sacred sciences subordinate to the Vedas, viz. Pronunciation, śikṣā, De- scription of religious ceremonies, kalpaḥkalpa, Grammar, vyākaraṇaṃvyākaraṇavyākaraṇam, Prosody, chandas, Astromony, jyotiṣaṃjyotiṣajyotiṣam, Explanation of difficult and obscure phrases, niruktaṃniruktaniruktam -ktiḥktiktiḥ. 4. The Upavedas, or Mi- nor vedas, are four, viz. Medicine, āyurvedaḥāyurveda, Archery and military art, dhanurvedaḥdhanurveda, Music, gāndharvavedaḥgāndharvaveda or gandharvavedaḥgandharvaveda, Mechanics and the use of tools, &c., śilpiśāstraṃśilpiśāstraśilpiśāstram or śastraśāstraṃśastraśāstraśastraśāstram. 5. Ṣāstra is a title of very general application, but it is properly re- stricted to six sacred and philosophical treatises, viz. mīmāṃ-mīmāmmīmāṃ sāśāstraṃsāśāstrasāśāstram, sāṃkhyaśāstraṃsāṃkhyaśāstrasāṃkhyaśāstram, vedāntaśāstraṃvedāntaśāstravedāntaśāstram, nyāyaśāstraṃnyāyaśāstranyāyaśāstram containing the branch called vaiśeṣikaṃvaiśeṣikavaiśeṣikam, yogaśāstraṃyogaśāstrayogaśāstram and dharmmaśāstraṃdharmmaśāstradharmmaśāstramdharmaśāstradharmaśāstram. The last contains the body of Hindū law; the others are the text- books of the six Hindū schools of philosophy, see SCHOOL. 6. The Purānas are eighteen mythological poems, see PURĀNA. 7.
