MILLION, s. niyutaḥniyuta -taṃtatamtaṃ, prayutaḥprayuta -taṃtatamtaṃ, daśalakṣaṃdaśalakṣadaśalakṣam; ‘ten million,’ koṭiḥkoṭi f.; ‘one hundred million,’ arbudaḥarbuda; ‘one thousand million,’ mahārbudaḥmahārbuda, mahāśaṅkhaḥmahāśaṅkha; ‘ten thousand million,’ padmaṃpadmapadmam; ‘one hundred thousand million,’ mahāpadmaṃmahāpadmamahāpadmam m.; ‘million million,’ abjaṃabjaabjam, svarbaṃsvarbasvarbam, nisvarbaṃnisvarbanisvarbam; ‘ten million million,’ mahāsvarbaṃmahāsvarbamahāsvarbam. According to Wil- son's Dictionary, svarbaṃsvarbasvarbam is equivalent to ten million million, according to the Mahrattī Dictionary to ten thousand million, and nikharbaṃnikharbanikharbam to a hundred thousand million: niyutaḥniyuta is also used for one hundred thousand, and there seems to be much uncertainty in the use of some others of the above Sanskrit terms. The word koṭiḥkoṭi is used generally for a large number, and hence the expression ‘by millions’ koṭiśas
