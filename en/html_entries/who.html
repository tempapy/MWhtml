WHO, pron. (Relat.) yaḥya yā yad, yau m. du., ye f. n. du., ye m. pl., yaḥya f. pl., yāni n. pl. The relative pronoun is not much used in Sanskrit excepting at the beginning of a sentence; as, ‘he who desires great riches is not wise,’ yo bahudhanam ākāṃkṣati sa buddhihīnaḥbuddhihīna. In other cases it is more usually expressed by sa ca or is inherent in a compound word or indecl. part.; thus, ‘in that city there dwelt a king who was beloved by his subjects,’ tasmin nagare vasati rājā sa ca prajāvallabhaḥprajāvallabha; ‘a saint who has subdued his organs of sense,’ jitendriyo muniḥmuni; ‘by all the birds who had assembled,’ sarvvaiḥsarvvaisarvai pakṣibhir militvā. — (Interrog.) kaḥka kā kiṃkim, kau m. du., ke f. n. du., ke m. pl., kāḥkā f. pl., kāni n. pl.; ‘who are you?’ kas tvaṃtvatvam; ‘who's there,’ ko'tra.
