NEGATION, s. nihnavaḥnihnava, apahnavaḥapahnava, nihnutiḥnihnuti f., apahnutiḥapahnuti f., apalāpaḥapalāpa, pratyā-pratyāpratyā khyānaṃkhyānakhyānam, apavādaḥapavāda, asvīkāraḥasvīkāra, niṣedhaḥniṣedha, pratyādeśaḥpratyādeśa. — (In logic) abhāvaḥabhāva according to one division it is of two kinds, ‘universal negation,’ saṃsargābhāvaḥsaṃsargābhāva; ‘relative or mutual negation,’ anyonyābhāvaḥanyonyābhāva; according to another division it is of four kinds, viz. ‘extinction,’ pradhvaṃsābhāvaḥpradhvaṃsābhāva; ‘antecedent non- existence,’ prāgabhāvaḥprāgabhāva; ‘reciprocal negation,’ anyonyābhāvaḥanyonyābhāva; ‘simple non-existence,’ atyantābhāvaḥatyantābhāva.
