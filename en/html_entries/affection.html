(Fondness) prītiḥprīti f.; ‘object of affection,’ prīti-prīti pātraṃpātrapātram; ‘paternal affection,’ sutasnehaḥsutasneha, putrasnehaḥputrasneha. — (Of the mind) manodharmmaḥmanodharmmamanodharma, manovikāraḥmanovikāra, manovikṛtiḥmanovikṛti f., manovṛttiḥmanovṛtti f., vṛttiḥvṛtti f., vikāraḥvikāra, vikṛtiḥvikṛti f.; ‘the affection of love,’ kāmavikāraḥkāmavikāra; ‘of anger,’ krodhavikāraḥkrodhavikāra. — (Of the body) vikāraḥvikāra, bhāvaḥbhāva, vṛttiḥvṛtti f., ākāraḥākāra, anubhāvaḥanubhāva; ‘of the eyes,’ netravikāraḥnetravikāra. — (Of the mind or body) bhāvaḥbhāva. Of these there are three classes: 1. Fixed or regular passions, sthāyibhāvāḥsthāyibhāvā m. pl., eight in number, viz. ratiḥrati f., hāsaḥhāsa, śokaḥśoka, krodhaḥkrodha, utsāhaḥutsāha, bhayaṃbhayabhayam, jugupsā, vismayaḥvismaya. 2.
