METAL, s. dhātuḥdhātu m., lohaḥloha, taijasaṃtaijasataijasam, suvarṇarūpyādi n.; ‘base metal,’ kupyaṃkupyakupyam; ‘factitious metal,’ kṛtrimadhātuḥkṛtrimadhātu m.; ‘fused,’ rasadhātuḥrasadhātu m., rasaḥrasa; ‘inferior,’ upadhātuḥupadhātu m.; ‘melting metals together,’ āvarttana; ‘the metals collectively,’ dhātujātaṃdhātujātadhātujātam, aṣṭadhātu n. The eight metals are, ‘gold,’ suvarṇaṃsuvarṇasuvarṇam; ‘silver,’ rūpyaṃrūpyarūpyam; ‘copper,’ tāmraṃtāmratāmram; ‘brass,’ pittalaṃpittalapittalam; ‘bell-metal, or a kind of pewter,’ kāṃsyaṃkāṃsyakāṃsyam; ‘tin,’ trapu; ‘lead,’ sīsakaṃsīsakasīsakam; ‘iron,’ lohaḥloha. Some make ‘steel,’ tīkṣṇāyasaṃtīkṣṇāyasatīkṣṇāyasam, one of the eight metals, instead of kāṃsyaṃkāṃsyakāṃsyam.
