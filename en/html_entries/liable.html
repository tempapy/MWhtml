LIABLE, a. (Subject to) adhīnaḥadhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ, yogyaḥyogya -gyāgyāgyā -gyaṃgyagyamgyaṃ, arhaḥarha -rhārhārhā rhaM, vaSaH -SA -SaM, SIlaH -lA -laM#} in comp.; ‘liable to be punished,’ daṇḍayogyaḥdaṇḍayogya &c. ‘liable to death,’ maraṇādhīnaḥmaraṇādhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ; ‘liable to disease,’ rogādhīnaḥrogādhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ; ‘not liable,’ anadhīnaḥanadhīna nA -naM#}. — (Responsible, amenable) āhvānādhīnaḥāhvānādhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ, abhi-abhiabhi yoktavyaḥyoktavya -vyāvyāvyā -vyaṃvyavyamvyaṃ, abhiyojyaḥabhiyojya -jyājyājyā -jyaṃjyajyamjyaṃ, abhiyokturāhvānādhīnaḥabhiyokturāhvānādhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ, vyavahāryyaḥvyavahāryyavyavahārya -ryyāryyāryāryyā -ryyaṃryyaryyamryaryamryyaṃ, vyavaharttavyaḥvyavaharttavyavyavahartavya -vyāvyāvyā -vyaṃvyavyamvyaṃ; ‘liable for the debt,’ uttamaṇāhvānādhīnaḥuttamaṇāhvānādhīna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ, dhanikāhvānādhīnaḥdhanikāhvānādhīna &c. ‘he is liable to pay,’ dātumdātum arhatiarhati. Sometimes ‘liable’ may be ex- pressed by the affix. āluālu; as, ‘liable to fall,’ patayāluḥpatayālu -luḥluluḥ -lululu.
