SENTIMENT, s. (Thought, opinion, notion) matamata, matiḥmati f., buddhiḥbuddhi f., kalpanākalpanā, manaḥkalpanāmanaḥkalpanā, bodhaḥbodha, saṅkalpaḥsaṅkalpasaṃkalpa. — (Sense contained in words) arthaḥartha abhiprāyaḥabhiprāya, see SENSE. — (Feeling, passion) bhāvaḥbhāva, rasaḥrasa, rāgaḥrāga, ṣāḍavaḥṣāḍava, ruciḥruci f., see PASSION. Eight sentiments or rasas are usually enumerated: 1. Love, śṛṅgāraḥśṛṅgāra. 2. Mirth, hāsyaḥhāsya syaM#}. 3. Tenderness, karuṇākaruṇā or kāruṇyaṃkāruṇyakāruṇyam. 4. Anger or rage, raudraḥraudra -draṃdradramdraṃ or krodhaḥkrodha. 5. Heroism, vīraḥvīra -raṃraramraṃ. 6. Terror, bhayānakaḥbhayānaka kaM#} or bhayaṃbhayabhayam. 7. Disgust, bībhatsaḥbībhatsa -tsaṃtsatsamtsaṃ. 8. Surprise, adbhutaḥadbhuta -taṃtatamtaṃ or vismayaḥvismaya. Sometimes a ninth is added, ‘tranquillity,’ śāntaḥśānta, or even a tenth, ‘paternal tenderness.’ vātsalyaṃvātsalyavātsalyam.
