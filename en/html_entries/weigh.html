To WEIGH, v. a. (In a balance) tultul (c. 10. tulayatitulayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), tūltūl (c. 10. tūlayatitūlayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), tolanaṃtolanatolanam kṛkṛ, tulanāṃtulanām kṛkṛ. — (Raise, as an anchor, &c.) uttuluttul. — (Ponder) vigaṇvigaṇ (c. 10. -gaṇayatigaṇayatigaṇayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), manasāmanasā vicarvicar, see To PONDER; ‘to weigh alternatives,’ vikḷpvikḷp (c. 10. -kalpayatikalpayatikalpayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ). ‘Weighs upon,’ viḍambayativiḍambayati; ‘weighs down,’ see To OUTWEIGH. To WEIGH, v. n. (Have weight) bhārībhārī -riṇīriṇīriṇī -ririri bhūbhū or asas, bhāra-bhārabhāra vānvān -vatīvatīvatī -vadvadvad asas or bhūbhū, bhārānvitaḥbhārānvita -tātātā -tamtamtam asas or bhūbhū; ‘what does this weigh?’ etatetat kimbhāramkimbhāram astiasti or etasyaetasya kiṃkim bhāraparimāṇaṃbhāraparimāṇabhāraparimāṇam. — (Be important) guruḥguru -rvīrvīrvī -rururu bhūbhū.
