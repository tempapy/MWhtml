RATHER, adv. (Preferably) varaṃvaravaram usually followed by nana caca or nana punarpunar as ‘rather death than poverty,’ varaṃvaravaram mṛtyurmṛtyur nana caca dāridraṃdāridradāridram. The root śiṣśiṣ with vivi may be used to express the sense of ‘rather,’ as ‘rather death than dishonour,’ apamānātapamānāt prāṇatyāgoprāṇatyāgo viśiṣyateviśiṣyate or the sense may be expressed by some other construction, as ‘I would rather that the sons of Dhṛtarāṣtra should kill me in the battle,’ yadiyadi māṃmām dhārtta-dhārttadhārtadhārtta rāṣṭrārāṣṭrā raṇeraṇe hanyushanyus tantan meme kṣemataraṃkṣematarakṣemataram bhavetbhavet. See PREFERABLE, PRE- FERABLY. — (Somewhat, a little) īṣatīṣat, kiñcitkiñcit, kimapikimapi; ‘rather less,’ īṣadūnaḥīṣadūna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ, kiñcinnyūnaḥkiñcinnyūna -nānānā -naṃnanamnaṃ.
