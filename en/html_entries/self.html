SELF, a. or pron. (As united to a pronoun, &c. reciprocally, or in composition with an adverb or noun) ātmā m. (n), ātma in comp.; ‘I will kill myself,’ ātmānaṃātmānaātmānam haniṣyāmi; ‘blame thyself,’ ātmānaṃātmānaātmānam ninda; ‘he shows himself,’ ātmānaṃātmānaātmānam darśayati; ‘for one's self,’ ātmārthaṃātmārthaātmārtham; ‘like one's self,’ ātmavat; ‘comparison with one's self,’ ātsaupamyaṃātsaupamyaātsaupamyam. — (As united to a pronoun, &c. emphatically) svayaṃsvayasvayam, svatas; ‘I myself will decide,’ svayaṃsvayasvayam niṣpattiṃniṣpattim kariṣyāmi. The pronoun saḥsa is some- times joined with the personal pronouns emphatically; as, ‘I myself,’ so'haṃsohasoham. — (As the first member of a compound word) ātma or sva or svayaṃsvayasvayam or svataḥsvata prefixed; ‘self done,’ ātmakṛtaḥātmakṛta -tātā -taṃtatamtaṃ, svayaṅkṛtaḥsvayaṅkṛta &c. ‘self-given,’ svayandattaḥsvayandatta -ttāttā -ttaṃttattamttaṃ, ātmadattaḥātmadatta &c. ‘self-sufficient,’ svasthaḥsvastha -sthāsthā -sthaṃsthasthamsthaṃ; ‘self-actuating,’ svayaṃvahaḥsvayaṃvaha -hāhā -haṃhahamhaṃ. — (Of one's self, of one's own accord) svayaṃsvayasvayam, svatas; ‘decayed of itself,’ svayaṃśīrṇaḥsvayaṃśīrṇa -rṇārṇā -rṇaṃrṇarṇamrṇaṃ.
