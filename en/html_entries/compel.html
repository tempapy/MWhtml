To COMPEL, v. a. Expressed by the causal form, usually in conjunction with the adverb balātbalāt or balenabalena; as, ‘the king should compel him to pay,’ taṃtatam rājārājā balādbalād dāpayetdāpayet; ‘he com- pels the Brahmans to perform the sacrifice,’ brāhmaṇānbrāhmaṇān yajñaṃyajñayajñam kārayatikārayati. The root arharh (c. 1. arhatiarhati -rhituṃrhitumrhituṃ) has sometimes the sense of ‘being compelled’; as, ‘he is compelled to pay,’ dātumdātum arhatiarhati. In modern Sanskrit such expressions as the following are found; ‘They compelled him to bear a burden, bhāraṃbhārabhāram voḍhuṃvoḍhum taṃtatam balādbalād dadhruḥdadhru (root dhṛdhṛ); ‘Compel the people to come,’ lokānlokān āgantuṃāgantum pravarttayapravarttayapravartaya.
