ARRAY, s. (Of battle) vyūhaḥvyūha, or -hanaṃhanahanamhanaṃ, vyūḍhiḥvyūḍhi f., balavinyāsaḥbalavinyāsa; ‘to draw up in battle array,’ vyūhvyūh (c. 1. -ūhateūhateūhate -ūhituṃūhitumūhituṃ). — (Order) kramaḥkrama, śreṇīśreṇī, paṅktiḥpaṅkti f., racanāracanā. — (Dress) ābharaṇaṃābharaṇaābharaṇam, bhūpaṇaṃbhūpaṇabhūpaṇam, paricchadaḥparicchada. To ARRAY, v. a. (To put in order) krameṇakrameṇa or vyūhenavyūhena racrac (c. 10. racayatiracayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), viracvirac or sthāsthā in caus. (sthāpayatisthāpayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), or vyavasthāvyavasthā; or vidhāvidhā (c. 3. -dadhātidadhātidadhāti -dhātuṃdhātumdhātuṃ), or sajjīkṛsajjīkṛ. — (To dress) vastramvastram āchadāchad (c. 10. -chādayatichādayatichādayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ), saṃvyesaṃvye (c. 1. -vyayativyayativyayati -vyātuṃvyātumvyātuṃ), bhūṣbhūṣ (c. 10. bhūṣayatibhūṣayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ); alaṅkṛalaṅkṛ, pariṣkṛpariṣkṛ. ARRAY, s. (Of an army) senāracanāsenāracanā. The following are dif- ferent forms of array; ‘In a circle,’ maṇḍalavyūhaḥmaṇḍalavyūha, cakravyūhaḥcakravyūha; ‘in a square,’ sarvatobhadraḥsarvatobhadra; ‘in line,’ daṇḍavyūhaḥdaṇḍavyūha; ‘in column,’ bhogavyūhaḥbhogavyūha; ‘in mixed order,’ asaṃhatavyūhaḥasaṃhatavyūha; ‘like a wain,’ śakaṭavyūhaḥśakaṭavyūha; ‘like a shark or crocodile,’ makaravyūhaḥmakaravyūha; ‘like a banner,’ patākāvyūhaḥpatākāvyūha; ‘like a shoulder,’ skandhaḥskandha. To ARRAY, v. a. vyūhvyūh (c. 1. -ūhateūhateūhate -hituṃhitumhituṃ), pratividhānaṃpratividhānapratividhānam kṛkṛ.
