LUNAR, LUNARY, a. cāndraḥcāndra -ndrīndrī -ndraṃndrandramndraṃ, cāndrikaḥcāndrika -kīkī -kaṃkakamkaṃ, cāndramasaḥcāndramasa sI -saM, candrasambanDI -nDinI -nDi (n), sOmyaH -myA -myaM, sOmikaH#} kI -kaM, EndavaH -vI -vaM;#} ‘lunar month,’ cāndramāsaḥcāndramāsa; ‘lunar asterism or mansion,’ nakṣatraṃnakṣatranakṣatram, dākṣāyiṇī; ‘the twenty-seven lunar asterisms collectively,’ dākṣāyiṇyaḥdākṣāyiṇya f. pl., nakṣatracakraṃnakṣatracakranakṣatracakram; ‘lunar day,’ tithiḥtithi m. f. Of these lunar days there are fourteen for the waxing, and fourteen for the waning moon; the first lunar day is called pratipad f., pakṣatiḥpakṣati -tītī f.; the day of new moon is called amāvasī, amāvasyā, amā; ‘the day of full moon,’ pūrṇimā, paurṇimā, paurṇamāsī, pūrṇenduḥpūrṇendu m. The eleventh tithi of each śuklapakṣaḥśuklapakṣa or waxing, and kṛṣṇapakṣaḥkṛṣṇapakṣa or waning moon, is regarded as sacred to Vishnu; and the following are the names given by Major Candy for the two ekādaśī of each month: caitraḥcaitra, 1. kāmadā, 2. pāpamocanī; vaiśākhaḥvaiśākha, 1. mohinī, 2. varūthinī; jyeṣṭhaḥjyeṣṭha, 1. nirjalā, 2. aparā; āṣāḍhaḥāṣāḍha, 1. śayanī, 2. yoginī; śrāvaṇaḥśrāvaṇa, 1. putradā, 2. kāmikā; bhādraḥbhādra, 1. parivarttinī, 2. ajā; āśvinaḥāśvina, 1. pāśāṅkuśā, 2. indirā; kārttikaḥkārttikakārtika, 1. bodhinī, 2. ramā; mārgaśīrṣaḥmārgaśīrṣa, 1. mokṣadā, 2. phaladā; pauṣaḥpauṣa, 1. prajāva-prajāva rdhinī, 2. saphalā; māghaḥmāgha, 1. jayadā, 2. ṣaṭtilā; phālgunaḥphālguna, 1. āmalakī, 2. vijayā. The ekādaśī of the śuklapakṣaḥśuklapakṣa of the months ā-ā ṣāḍhaḥṣāḍha and kārttikaḥkārttikakārtika are both particularly sacred. On the first, or śayanaikādaśī, Vishnu goes to sleep, and on the second awakes. (Difference of solar and lunar days) kṣayatithiḥkṣayatithi f.; ‘inauspicious lunar day,’ ghātatithiḥghātatithi f., dagdhati-dagdhati thiḥthi f.; ‘observance of the fasts and rites of the several lunar days,’ tithipālanaṃtithipālanatithipālanam; ‘lunar eclipse,’ candragrahaṇaṃcandragrahaṇacandragrahaṇam; ‘lunar measurement,’ cāndramānaṃcāndramānacāndramānam; ‘the lunar dynasty,’ somavaṃśaḥsomavaṃśa. — (Resembling the moon).
