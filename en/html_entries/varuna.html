VARUNA¦, s. (God of the waters, the Hindū Neptune, Regent of the West. He is also Lord of punishment, in which capacity he resembles Yama and Śiva) varuṇaḥ, pracetāḥ m. (s), vāruṇīvallabhaḥ. — (As lord of the waters) apāmpatiḥ m., appatiḥ m., jalapatiḥ m., keśaḥ, keśagarbhaḥ. — (As lord of aquatic animals) yādasāmpatiḥ m., yādaḥpatiḥ m., yādasānnāthaḥ. — (As holding the cord, with which he binds incorrigible offenders under the water) pāśī m. (n), pāśabhṛt m., pāśapāṇiḥ m. His consort is called varuṇānī, varuṇī. His son agastiḥ m., agastyaḥ, vāruṇiḥ m. His vāhana or vehicle is a fish.
