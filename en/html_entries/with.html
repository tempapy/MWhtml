WITH, prep. (Denoting cause, means, instrument) usually expressed by the instrumental case; as, ‘with a sword,’ khaṅgena; ‘with grief,’ śokena. — (In opposition to, against) instr. c., with or without saha; as, ‘to fight with one's enemies,’ śatrubhiḥśatrubhi or śatrubhiḥśatrubhi saha yudh or expressed by upari or by the dat. or gen. c., in such phrases as, ‘he is angry with his son,’ putrasyopari or putrāya or putrasya krudhyati. — (In com- pany, together with, along with) saha, sārddhaṃsārddhasārddham, samaṃsamasamam, sākaṃsākasākam, sahitaṃsahitasahitam, satraṃsatrasatram -trātrā, amā all with the instrum. c.: ‘along with me,’ mayā saha; ‘along with him,’ tena saha. The preposition sam, prefixed to roots, gives this sense of ‘with;’ as, ‘to live in company with,’ saṃvas. — (Associated, attended, connected with) sahitaḥsahita -tātā -taṃtatamtaṃ in comp. or expressed by the instr. c., with or without saha, or by pūrvva or ṣuraḥsara in comp.; as, ‘associated with enemies,’ śatrusahitaḥśatrusahita -tātā -taṃtatamtaṃ or śatrubhiḥśatrubhi sahitaḥsahita &c. — (Denoting manner) expressed by instr. c., or by sa in comp. with the accus. n., or by pūrvvaṃpūrvvapūrvvampūrvapūrvam or pūrvvakaṃpūrvvakapūrvvakampūrvakapūrvakam or puraḥsaraṃpuraḥsarapuraḥsaram affixed; as, ‘with anger,’ kopena, sakopaṃsakopasakopam, kopapūrvvaṃkopapūrvvakopapūrvvamkopapūrvakopapūrvam, kopapūrvvakaṃkopapūrvvakakopapūrvvakamkopapūrvakakopapūrvakam, kopapuraḥsaraṃkopapuraḥsarakopapuraḥsaram; ‘with respect,’ sādaraṃsādarasādaram; ‘with affection,’ prītipūrvvaṃprītipūrvvaprītipūrvvamprītipūrvaprītipūrvam. — (Among) madhye. To express a person with any thing in his possession, or a thing in connection with some- thing else, a compound adjective may often be used; thus, ‘a man with a stick in his hand,’ daṇḍahastaḥdaṇḍahasta puruṣaḥpuruṣa; ‘a man with riches,’ sadhanaḥsadhana or dhanavān puruṣaḥpuruṣa; a ship with two masts,’ dvikūpakā nauḥnau or kūpakadvayayuktā nauḥnau.
