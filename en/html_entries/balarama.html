BALARĀMA. This god is not often regarded as an avatār of Vishnu, but rather as an incarnation of the great ser- pent Ananta; hence he is sometimes called śeṣaḥśeṣa or anantaḥananta. He was the son of Vasudeva by Devakī, but was trans- ferred from the latter to the womb of Rohiṇī, the other wife of Vasudeva, and hence his name, saṅkarṣaṇaḥsaṅkarṣaṇasaṃkarṣaṇa. Other names for him are — (Fond of wine) priyamadhuḥpriyamadhu m. — (Killer of the boxer Muṣṭika) muṣṭikāntakaḥmuṣṭikāntaka. — (Secret in motion) guptacaraḥguptacara. — (Strong and good) balabhadraḥbalabhadra.
