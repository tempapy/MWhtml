NOT, adv. In absolute negation, denial, or refusal, ‘not’ is rendered by nana, nono, nānā, nahinahi. When prohibitive, by māmā, māsmamāsma, or sometimes by alaṃalaalam or kṛtaṃkṛtakṛtam with instr. c. When privative, it is expressed by aa, anan, nirnir, vivi prefixed; as, ‘there is not,’ nāstināsti; ‘he will not go,’ nana gamiṣyatigamiṣyati; ‘do not go,’ māmā gacchagaccha; ‘do not grieve,’ alaṃalaalam śokenaśokena; ‘not regardful,’ anapekṣaḥanapekṣa -kṣākṣākṣā -kṣaṃkṣakṣamkṣaṃ, nirapekṣaḥnirapekṣa &c. ‘not at all,’ aleśaṃaleśaaleśam, nana kiñcidapikiñcidapi; ‘not so,’ maivaṃmaivamaivam. In prohibitive phrases māmā may be used with the 3d pret., the augment being cut off; as, ‘do not grieve’ mā'śocīḥmāśocī; ‘do not fear,’ mā'bhaiṣīḥmābhaiṣī. The interrogative ‘is not?’ is rendered by nanunanu.
