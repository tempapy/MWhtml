NEITHER, pron. (Not either, not the one or the other) expressed by anyataraḥanyatara -rārārā -raṃraramraṃ or ekataraḥekatara -rārārā -raṃraramraṃ or ubhauubhau, or simply by the dual in conjunction with the negative nana; as, ‘neither of them can break the bow,’ dhanurdhanur bhaṃktumbhaṃktum ekataroekataro nana śaknotiśaknoti or ubhauubhau nana śaknutaḥśaknuta or simply nana śaknutaḥśaknuta. NEITHER, conj. expressed by the negative nana or nana caca, or by the prohibitive māmā. Sometimes in conjunction with vāvā; as, ‘he took neither food nor clothes for his journey,’ yātrārthaṃyātrārthayātrārtham bhakṣyaṃbhakṣyabhakṣyam vastraṃvastravastram vāvā nana jagrāhajagrāha; ‘take neither food nor clothes,’ bhakṣyaṃbhakṣyabhakṣyam vastraṃvastravastram vāvā māmā gṛhāṇagṛhāṇa; ‘do not eat the fruit neither touch it,’ phalaṃphalaphalam māmā khādatakhādata māmā spṛśataspṛśata; ‘he did not touch, neither did he smell,’ nāspṛśadnāspṛśad nana cājighratcājighrat.
