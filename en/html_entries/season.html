The year is di- vided into six seasons, according to the Hindūs, viz. 1. Spring, vasantaḥvasantavasaṃta, vasantasamayaḥvasantasamayavasaṃtasamaya, comprising, according to the reckoning which refers to the Northern parts of India, the two months of caitraḥcaitra and vaiśākhaḥvaiśākha, i. e. from the middle of March to the middle of May, see SPRING. 2. The hot season or Summer, grīṣmaḥgrīṣma, grīṣmakālaḥgrīṣmakāla, nidāghakālaḥnidāghakāla, compris- ing the two months of jyeṣṭaḥjyeṣṭa and āṣāḍhaḥāṣāḍha, i. e. from the middle of May to the middle of July, see SUMMER. 3. The rainy season, the rains, varṣāḥvarṣā m. pl., varṣākālaḥvarṣākāla, vṛṣṭikālaḥvṛṣṭikāla, com- prising the two months śrāvaṇaḥśrāvaṇa and bhādraḥbhādra, i. e. from the middle of July to the middle of September, see under RAIN, RAINY. 4. The autumnal season, śarad, śaratkālaḥśaratkāla, śaradṛtuḥśaradṛtu m., comprising the two months āśvinaḥāśvina and kārttikaḥkārttikakārtika, i. e. from the middle of September to the middle of No- vember, see AUTUMN. 5. The cold season, hemantaḥhemanta, comprising the two months mārgaśīrṣaḥmārgaśīrṣa and pauṣaḥpauṣa, i. e. from the middle of November to the middle of January. 6. The dewy season or season of hoar frost, śiśiraḥśiśira, śiśirakālaḥśiśirakāla, comprising the two months māghaḥmāgha and phālgunaḥphālguna, i. e. from the middle of January to the middle of March. According to more modern authorities the season of Spring begins with the month Phālguna, or the middle of February. This reckoning will place each of the succeeding seasons a month earlier. Sometimes the word āgamaḥāgama is used in composition for ‘the coming on of a season,’ or for the season itself, as ‘the rainy season,’ varṣāgamaḥvarṣāgama, meghāgamaḥmeghāgama; ‘the hot season,’ grīṣmāgamaḥgrīṣmāgama, uṣmāgamaḥuṣmāgama.
