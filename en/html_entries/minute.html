MINUTE, s. (Sixtieth part of an hour) paṣṭiprāṇapādāḥpaṣṭiprāṇapādā m. pl., kṣaṇapādaḥkṣaṇapāda, kṣaṇacaturthāṃśaḥkṣaṇacaturthāṃśa, sārddhadvipalaṃsārddhadvipalasārddhadvipalam; ‘half a minute,’ triṃśatprā-triṃśatprā ṇapādā. According to the Hindu reckoning a daṇḍaḥdaṇḍa or hour contains 24 minutes, and a palaṃpalapalam is equal to the 60th part of a daṇḍaḥdaṇḍa, therefore 2(1/2) palas are equal to a minute. A kṣaṇaḥkṣaṇa is equal to 30 kalā or 4 minutes. When, however, the word minute is used generally for a small portion of time it may be expressed by kṣaṇaḥkṣaṇa, palaṃpalapalam, vipalaṃvipalavipalam, truṭiḥtruṭi f., nimiṣaḥnimiṣa, nimeṣaḥnimeṣa; as, ‘a minute's delay,’ kṣaṇakṣepaḥkṣaṇakṣepa; ‘for a minute,’ kṣaṇaṃkṣaṇakṣaṇam -ṇātṇāt -ṇenaṇena; ‘in a minute,’ nimeṣamātreṇa; ‘a minute afterwards,’ kṣaṇāntaraṃkṣaṇāntarakṣaṇāntaram -rere. — (In geometry, the sixtieth part of a degree) kalā. — (Note, memorandum) smaraṇalekhaḥsmaraṇalekha, smaraṇārthalikhitaṃsmaraṇārthalikhitasmaraṇārthalikhitam, lekhaḥlekha, likhitaṃlikhitalikhitam, smaraṇacihnaṃsmaraṇacihnasmaraṇacihnam.
