FOR, conj. (Because) yatas, yat, yena, yasmāt, hi, iti hetoḥheto, tadarthaṃtadarthatadartham, anena hetunā. See BECAUSE. — (For as much as) yatas, yat, or expressed by the abl. c. of the abst. noun; as, ‘for as much as he was a good man,’ sajjanatvāt. FOR, prep. (In exchange for) prati with abl. c., vinimayena; ‘he exchanges beans for sesamum seed,’ tilebhyaḥtilebhya prati māṣān dadāti; or expressed by the inst. c., as, tilair māṣān pariva-pariva rttayati;#} ‘in exchange for one's life,’ svaprāṇavinimayena. — (Instead of, in place of) sthāne, prati. — (Toward) prati, uddiśya with acc. c. — (On account of, by reason of) arthaṃarthaartham, arthe, hetoḥheto, hetau, kṛte, nimitte, kāraṇāt all affixed to the crude; as, ‘for this cause,’ tadarthaṃtadarthatadartham, or expressed by uddiśya with acc. c., or by the abl. c.; as, ‘for anger he did it,’ kopāt tat kṛtavān, ‘for want of money,’ dhanābhāvāt. — (Conducive to) arthaṃarthaartham, arthe, or expressed by the dat. c., as, ‘for the good of the world,’ bhūtaye bhuvaḥbhuva; ‘for one's own advantage,’ ātmavivṛddhaye — (For the sake of) arthaṃarthaartham, arthe, hetoḥheto, kṛte, or by dat. c., as, ‘for the satisfying of hunger,’ kṣudhāśāntaye; ‘for thy enjoyment,’ tava bhogāya. — (In order to obtain) arthaṃarthaartham, arthe or by dat. c., as, ‘for fame,’ pratipattaye, or even by inst. c., as, ‘for hire,’ bhāṭakena. — (In return for) prati, or more usually expressed by the indec. part.; as, ‘for so doing,’ tat kṛtvā. — (During a certain time) yāvat, paryyantaṃparyyantaparyyantamparyantaparyantam; ‘for life,’ puruṣasyāyur yāvat, yāvajjīvaṃyāvajjīvayāvajjīvam, jīvanaparyyantaṃjīvanaparyyantajīvanaparyyantamjīvanaparyantajīvanaparyantam, or by prefixing ā with abl. c., as, āmaraṇāt, ājīvanāntāt (i. e. until death); ‘lasting for life,’ āmaraṇāntikaḥāmaraṇāntika -kākā -kaṃkakamkaṃ; ‘for three days,’ dinatrayaparyyantaṃdinatrayaparyyantadinatrayaparyyantamdinatrayaparyantadinatrayaparyantam; ‘for a hundred years,’ varṣaśataṃvarṣaśatavarṣaśatam yāvat. Sometimes expressed by the acc. c., as, ‘for one month,’ ekaṃekaekam māsaṃmāsamāsam; ‘for two months,’ dvau māsau; ‘for a hundred years,’ varṣaśataṃvarṣaśatavarṣaśatam, or even by the instr. c., as, ‘for twelve years,’ dvādaśabhir varṣair; ‘for a few days,’ katipayadivasaiḥkatipayadivasai; ‘for a long time,’ cirakālaṃcirakālacirakālam, anekakālaṃanekakālaanekakālam, cirāt, cireṇa, cirāya; ‘food for a year,’ varṣopa-varṣopa bhogyam annaṃannaannam. — (In proportion to, considering) apekṣya, uddiśya, pratīkṣya, avekṣya, all with acc. c. — (On the part of) apekṣya, uddiśya, kṛte; ‘as for me,’ māmapekṣya, māmuddiśya, matkṛte. — (On the side of) sapakṣaḥsapakṣa -kṣākṣā -kṣaṃkṣakṣamkṣaṃ; ‘he is for us,’ asmākaṃasmākaasmākam sapakṣo bhavati — (Notwithstanding, for all that) tathāpi. — (As if) vat affixed, iva; ‘he shewed himself for dead,’ mṛtavat or mṛtam iva ātmānam adarśayat. — (According to) yathā, anusāreṇa, anurūpeṇa; ‘for one's ability,’ yathāśakti. ‘For,’ may be expressed by the acc. c. in such phrases as ‘looking for,’ ‘waiting for,’ &c. as, ‘he looks for me,’ mām apekṣate, or by other cases; as, ‘we depend on God for success,’ īśvarādhīnā siddhiḥsiddhi; ‘I have great affection for her,’ tasyām anurāgavān asmi or tayā saha mahān snehaḥsneha, or by gata, as, ‘through love for him,’ tadgatena anurāgeṇa, or by the crude form, as, ‘search for money,’ dhanānveṣaṇaṃdhanānveṣaṇadhanānveṣaṇam, or even by the affix sāt, as, ‘he built a house for the Brahman,’ gṛhaṃgṛhagṛham cakāra dvijātisāt. — (For the most part) prāyeṇa, prāyas, prāyaśas, bhūyiṣṭhaṃbhūyiṣṭhabhūyiṣṭham.
