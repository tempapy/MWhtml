LOGIC, s. tarkaḥtarka, tarkavidyātarkavidyā, tarkaśāstraṃtarkaśāstratarkaśāstram, nyāyaḥnyāya, nyāyavidyānyāyavidyā, nyāyaśāstraṃnyāyaśāstranyāyaśāstram, vicāravidyāvicāravidyā, vyūhaḥvyūha, prohaḥproha, abhyūhaḥabhyūha, nirūhaḥnirūha, ūhaḥūha, vaiśeṣikaṃvaiśeṣikavaiśeṣikam, ānvī-ānvīānvī kṣikīkṣikī, anumānoktiḥanumānokti f., ghruvaḥghruva. The nyāyanyāya school was instituted by the sage Gautama, the branch of it called vaiśeṣikaṃvaiśeṣikavaiśeṣikam was founded by Kaṇāda. The following are some of the terms of logic; ‘universal pervadence or extension,’ vyāptiḥvyāpti f., abhivyāptiḥabhivyāpti; ‘extreme extension or conclusion unwarranted by the premises,’ ativyāptiḥativyāpti; ‘individual or particular extension,’ avyāptiḥavyāpti f., avyāpyavṛttiḥavyāpyavṛtti f.; sign of the existence of an inherent property, vyāptilakṣaṇaṃvyāptilakṣaṇavyāptilakṣaṇam; ‘subject of an inference,’ vyāpyaṃvyāpyavyāpyam. Other terms are upalaprakṣepaḥupalaprakṣepa, utthāpyākāṃkṣāutthāpyākāṃkṣā, pravṛttinimittaṃpravṛttinimittapravṛttinimittam, kaimarthyaṃkaimarthyakaimarthyam, vyāpyakoṭiḥvyāpyakoṭi f., siddhasādhanadoṣaḥsiddhasādhanadoṣa.
