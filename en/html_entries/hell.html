HELL, s. (Region under the earth, or residence of the Nāgas, Asuras, Daityas, &c., and not to be confounded with Naraka or the place of punishment) pātālaṃpātālapātālam, adhobhuvanaṃadhobhuvanaadhobhuvanam, nāga-nāga lokaḥloka. There are seven of these Pātālas or lower regions, saptapātālaṃsaptapātālasaptapātālam. These are, 1. atalaṃatalaatalam, 2. vitalaṃvitalavitalam, 3. pātālaṃpātālapātālam, 4. rasā-rasā talaṃtalatalam, 5. mahātalaṃmahātalamahātalam, 6. sutalaṃsutalasutalam, 7. talātalaṃtalātalatalātalam. — (Place of punishment after death) narakaḥnaraka, nirayaḥniraya, nārakaḥnāraka. The Naraka, or Tar- tarus of the Hindūs, is divided into numerous different places of torture; such as, ‘the place paved with swords,’ asipatraṃasipatraasipatram; ‘the place where the leaves of the trees are swords,’ asipatravanaṃasipatravanaasipatravanam; ‘the place of great darkness,’ andhatā-andhatā misraṃmisramisram; ‘a place in which the wicked are baked like pots,’ taptakumbhaḥtaptakumbha, kumbhīpākaḥkumbhīpāka; ‘the place of burning sand,’ taptabālukaḥtaptabāluka &c., see under HELL, p. 340; ‘abiding in h.,’ narakavāsaḥnarakavāsa; ‘descending to h.,’ adhaḥpatanaṃadhaḥpatanaadhaḥpatanam, adhogatiḥadhogati f., narakapatanaṃnarakapatananarakapatanam; ‘judge of h.,’ yamaḥyama; ‘his messenger,’ yamadūtaḥyamadūta; ‘road to h.,’ naraka-naraka sārgaḥsārga, adhaḥpathaḥadhaḥpatha, dhūmramārgaḥdhūmramārga; ‘punishment of h.,’ yamadaṇḍaḥyamadaṇḍa; ‘pit of h.,’ narakakuṇḍaṃnarakakuṇḍanarakakuṇḍam. Eighty-six of these pits are enumerated.
