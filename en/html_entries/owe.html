To OWE, v. a. (Owe money) dhṛdhṛ (c. 10. dhārayatidhārayati -yituṃyitumyituṃ) with acc. and dat.; as, ‘he owes a hundred pieces of money to Devadatta,’ devadattāyadevadattāya śataṃśataśatam mudrāmudrā dhārayatidhārayati; ‘he repaid me the money which he owed me,’ dhārayandhārayan mahyaṃmahyamahyam vasūnivasūni pratyapadyatapratyapadyata. — (Be indebted) ṛṇīṛṇī -ṇinīṇinīṇinī -ṇiṇiṇi bhūbhū, ṛṇavānṛṇavān -vatīvatīvatī -vadvadvad bhūbhū, ṛṇabaddhaḥṛṇabaddha dDA -dDaM BU, fRaM kf#}. — (Be obliged) anugṛhītaḥanugṛhīta -tātātā -taṃtatamtaṃ bhūbhū; ‘I owe you many thanks for your advice,’ anugṛhīto'smianugṛhītosmi tavopadeśenatavopadeśena. The sense of ‘owe’ may be expressed by the fut. pass. part.; as, ‘I owe him money,’ mayāmayā tasmaitasmai dhanaṃdhanadhanam pratideyaṃpratideyapratideyam or prati-pratiprati dātavyaṃdātavyadātavyam, or by changing the construction of the sentence thus, ‘I owe my deliverance to you,’ bhavānbhavān mamamama rakṣākāraṇamrakṣākāraṇam itiiti jñātavyaṃjñātavyajñātavyam.
