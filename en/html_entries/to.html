TO, prep. Many of the meanings of ‘to’ are expressed by prepositions or affixes, or by the infin. mood, or are in- herent in the inflections of the Sanskrit noun; thus, (Mo- tion to or toward any place) expressed by the acc. case alone, or with pratiprati &c. added; as, ‘he went to the city,’ nagaraṃnagaranagaram or nagaraṃnagaranagaram pratiprati jagāmajagāma, or by the gen. c., with antikaṃantikaantikam &c. as, ‘he sent me to you,’ māṃmām tavāntikaṃtavāntikatavāntikam or tavatava sakāśamsakāśam apraibītapraibīt. — (Motion to any state) by the acc. c.; as, ‘he attains to fame,’ khyātiṃkhyātim yātiyāti. — (Adaptation) by the dat. c. or crude form; as, ‘that husband was not to her taste,’ sasa patispatis tasyāḥtasyā santoṣāyasantoṣāyasaṃtoṣāya nābhavatnābhavat; ‘suited to royalty,’ rājayogyaḥrājayogya -gyāgyāgyā gyaM, rAjArhaH#} &c. ‘that is pleasing to me,’ tantan mahyaṃmahyamahyam rocaterocate. — (Address, declaration, compellation) by the acc. c. or dat. c.; as, ‘he addressed a speech to the king,’ rājānaṃrājānarājānam vacanamvacanam abravītabravīt; ‘speak to me,’ māṃmām brūhibrūhi; ‘I will declare this to my pupils,’ śiṣyebhyaḥśiṣyebhya pravakṣyāmipravakṣyāmi tattat; ‘he made it known to the king,’ tadtad rājñerājñe vijñāpayativijñāpayati. — (Giving) by the dat., gen., or loc. c.; ‘he gives sweetmeats to his son,’ putrāyaputrāya or putrasyaputrasya modakānmodakān dadātidadāti. — (Up to, as far as, denoting extent, degree, &c.) āā prefixed to the abl. or acc. c., paryyantaparyyantaparyanta or antaanta in comp.; as, ‘to the ocean,’ āsamudrātāsamudrāt or samudraparyyantaṃsamudraparyyantasamudraparyyantamsamudraparyantasamudraparyantam; ‘to the throat,’ ākaṇṭhātākaṇṭhāt; ‘to the knee,’ ājānuājānu; ‘the numbers from one up to ten thousand,’ ekādyayutāntasaṃkhyāḥekādyayutāntasaṃkhyā. — (Posses- sion, appertainment) by the gen. c.; as, ‘money belongs to me,’ mamamama dhanaṃdhanadhanam bhavatibhavati. — (Purpose, intention, object) by the dat. c. or infin. mood, or by arthaṃarthaartham &c. affixed; as, ‘the sat down to consult,’ mantraṇāyamantraṇāya or mantrayituṃmantrayitum or mantraṇārthammantraṇārtham upaviṣṭāḥupaviṣṭā; ‘the barber came to shave his master,’ bharttṛkṣaurakaraṇāyabharttṛkṣaurakaraṇāyabhartṛkṣaurakaraṇāya nāpitanāpita āgacchatāgacchat; ‘he came to make peace,’ sandhiṃsandhimsaṃdhim karttumkarttumkartum āgacchatāgacchat; ‘give me something to eat,’ āhārārthaṃāhārārthaāhārārtham kiñcinkiñcin mamamama dehidehi. — (As a sign of the infinitive) expressed by the infinitive, or by yathāyathā &c. as, ‘unable to move,’ calitumcalitum asamarthaḥasamartha; ‘I desire to hear,’ śrotumśrotum icchāmiicchāmi; ‘I did not expect to see you again,’ nana mama āsīdāsīd āśaṃsāāśaṃsā yathāyathā punarapipunarapi tvāṃtvām prekṣipyeprekṣipye. There may be other ways of expressing ‘to’ in Sanskrit, as in the following examples: ‘the peacock dances to the songs of the bees,’ śikhīśikhī nṛtyatinṛtyati madhukaragītaiḥmadhukaragītai; ‘he performed penance to the deity,’ devamdevam uddiśyauddiśya tapastapas tepetepe; ‘the town was speedily reduced to ashes,’ nagarīnagarī sahasāsahasā bhasmasādbhasmasād akriyataakriyata; ‘compared to that,’ tadapekṣyatadapekṣya or tadapekṣayātadapekṣayā; ‘to the east of the village,’ prāgprāg grāmātgrāmāt; ‘he goes to and fro,’ gamanāgamanaṃgamanāgamanagamanāgamanam karotikaroti, see FRO; ‘to one's face,’ pratyakṣaṃpratyakṣapratyakṣam -kṣataskṣataskṣatas, samakṣaṃsamakṣasamakṣam; ‘to a man,’ aśe-aśeaśe ṣatasṣatas, aśeṣeṇaaśeṣeṇa; ‘I have a debt to pay,’ mayāmayā ṛṇaṃṛṇaṛṇam dātavyaṃdātavyadātavyam; ‘ready to go,’ gantumgantum udyataḥudyata, gamanonmukhaḥgamanonmukha; ‘he causes her to enter the house,’ tāṃtām gṛhaṃgṛhagṛham praveśayatipraveśayati.
