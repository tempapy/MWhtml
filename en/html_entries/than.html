THAN, adv. or conj. Usually expressed in Sanskrit by the abl. c.; as, ‘a store of grain is better than all stores,’ dhānya-dhānyadhānya saṃgrahaḥsaṃgraha śreyānśreyān sarvvasaṃgrahātsarvvasaṃgrahātsarvasaṃgrahāt; ‘worthier than I,’ asmattoasmatto varīyānvarīyān. Or by nana caca; as, ‘silence is better than speaking un- truth,’ varaṃvaravaram maunaṃmaunamaunam nana caca vacanamvacanam anṛtaṃanṛtaanṛtam [i. e. better silence and not speaking untruth]. In modern Sanskrit ‘than’ is often expressed by apekṣayāapekṣayā, or apekṣyaapekṣya; as, ‘some of the organs of animals are stronger than those of men,’ jantūnāṃjantūnām kānicidkānicid indriyāṇiindriyāṇi manupyāpekṣayāmanupyāpekṣayā pravalānipravalāni; ‘acid has a stronger attraction for iron than copper,’ amlarasaṃamlarasaamlarasam tāsramtāsram apekṣyaapekṣya lohamloham adhikamadhikam ākarṣatiākarṣati; ‘placing the cup on some place higher than one's head,’ svamastakamsvamastakam apekṣyaapekṣya uccatareuccatare sthānesthāne pātraṃpātrapātram vinyasyavinyasya; ‘than him,’ tadapekṣayātadapekṣayā; ‘than me,’ madapekṣayāmadapekṣayā.
