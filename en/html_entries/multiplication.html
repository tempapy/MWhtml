MULTIPLICATION, s. (In Arithmetic, &c.) guṇanaṃguṇanaguṇanam, hananaṃhananahananam, ghātaḥghāta, badhaḥbadha, pūraṇaṃpūraṇapūraṇam, guṇatvaṃguṇatvaguṇatvam -tātā, aṅkapūraṇaṃaṅkapūraṇaaṅkapūraṇam, hataṃhatahatam, abhyāsaḥabhyāsa, aṅkavṛddhiḥaṅkavṛddhi f., saṃkhyāvṛddhiḥsaṃkhyāvṛddhi f., pratyutpannaṃpratyutpannapratyutpannam, guṇākāraḥguṇākāra -raṃraramraṃ. The Hindus have different methods of multiplication; as, ‘cross multiplication,’ vajrābhyāsaḥvajrābhyāsa, vajrabadhaḥvajrabadha, vajraghātaḥvajraghāta; ‘duodecimal multiplication,’ gomūtrikā; ‘multiplication by a figure divided into squares,’ kapāṭa-kapāṭa sandhiḥsandhisaṃdhi m.; ‘multiplication according to the places,’ sthāna-sthāna guṇanaṃguṇanaguṇanam; ‘by sub-multiples of the multiplier,’ vibhāgaguṇanaṃvibhāgaguṇanavibhāgaguṇanam; ‘by dividing the multiplier into parts, and taking the sums of the products of the multiplier.’ khaṇḍaguṇanaṃkhaṇḍaguṇanakhaṇḍaguṇanam; ‘by the whole multiplier at once,’ svarūpaguṇanaṃsvarūpaguṇanasvarūpaguṇanam. — (Act of increasing) bahulīkaraṇaṃbahulīkaraṇabahulīkaraṇam, pracurīkaraṇaṃpracurīkaraṇapracurīkaraṇam, vardhanaṃvardhanavardhanam, vṛddhiḥvṛddhi f.
