UNITY, s. ekatāekatā -tvaṃtvatvamtvaṃ, aikyaṃaikyaaikyam, advaitaṃadvaitaadvaitam, advayaṃadvayaadvayam; ‘the doctrine of unity,’ advaitamataṃadvaitamataadvaitamatam, advaitavādaḥadvaitavāda, abhedaḥabheda, abhedamataṃabhedamataabhedamatam, abhedavādaḥabhedavāda, aikyaṃaikyaaikyam, nirdvandvaṃnirdvandvanirdvandvam; ‘hold- ing it,’ advaitavādaḥadvaitavāda, advaitadṛṣṭiḥadvaitadṛṣṭi f., abhedabuddhiḥabhedabuddhi f.; ‘holder of it,’ advaitavādīadvaitavādī m., aikyavādīaikyavādī m., abhedavādīabhedavādī m., nirdvandvaḥnirdvandva, nirvikalpaḥnirvikalpa. As opposed to this is the doctrine of duality, or the as- sertion of two principles, viz. God and matter. It is termed dvaitaṃdvaitadvaitam, dvaitamataṃdvaitamatadvaitamatam, dvaitavādaḥdvaitavāda, bhedamataṃbhedamatabhedamatam, &c.
