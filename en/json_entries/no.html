No¦, adv. na, no, nahi; ‘or no,’ na vā. No¦, a. (None) usually expressible by na; as, ‘no one,’ na kaścit m., na kācit f., na ko'pi m., na kāpi f., na kaścana m., na kācana f.; ‘no thing,’ na kiñcit, na kimapi, na kiñcana; ‘no other,’ nānyaḥ -nyā -nyat; ‘no where,’ na kvacit, na kvāpi, na kutracit, na kutrāpi; ‘no more, not again,’ na punar; ‘no great distance,’ nātidūraṃ. When ‘no’ is privative, it is expressed by nir, a, vi &c. prefixed, or by hīna, śūnya, varjita, rahita &c. affixed; as, ‘having no fruit,’ niṣphalaḥ -lā -laṃ, aphalaḥ &c., viphalaḥ &c., phalahīnaḥ -nā -naṃ, &c.
