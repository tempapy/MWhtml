    I Wealthy:
    (1) dhanin (f. nī), if we are r. with wealth undistributed and unenjoyed: dānopabhogahīnena dhanena dhanino yadi, H. i. 156.;
    (2) dhanavat (f. tī);
    (3) dhanaśālin (f. nī);
    (4) mahādhana (f. nā), dhanāḍhya (f. ḍhyā), and sim. comp.s. (= very r.);
    (5) vittaśālin (f. nī);
    (6) vibhavasampanna (f. nnā);
    (7) by subs., he has friends, who is r.: yasyārthāstasya mitrāṇi, H. i. 124.
    II Costly, splendid; q. v.: mahādhana (f. nā) and sim. comp. s, r. couch: mahādhanaṃ śayanam, Ki. i. 38.
    III Abundant: q. v.: pracura (f. rā).
    IV Abounding in:
    (1) āḍhya (f. ḍhyā), a fort r. in weapons and machines: durgamāyudhayantrāḍhyam, Ka. iv. 60.;
    (2) by words given under abundant, the first became r. in gems: prathamā bahuratnasūrabhūt. R. viii. 28.;
    (3) samanvita (f. tā = endowed with: q. v.), r. in ore: khanidravyasamanvitā, Ka. iv. 51.: v. Also having. V. Of soil etc.:
    (1) sāravat or guṇavat (f. tī), Ka. iv. 50. and ix. 18.;
    (2) praśasta (f. stā): v. Excellent.
    VI Of colours: ujjvala (f. lā = bright).
    VII Highly sea- -soned:
    (1) ghṛtapluta (f. tā) and sim. comp.s. (= full of ghee);
    (2) sumadhura (f. rā = very sweet), Bha.
