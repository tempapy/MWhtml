    II Journey:
    (1) yātrāyātrā (also fig.), obstructor of the m. of the world: lokayātrāvighātakaḥ, Mah.;
    (2) yānamyāna, pra-, broke off m. ing. for three nights: trirātraṃ prayāṇa- bhaṅgamakarot, P. ;
    (3) prasthānamprasthāna (= setting out on a m.), just ascertain a day proper for our m.: nirūpyatāṃ tāvadasmākaṃ prasthānayogyadivasaḥ, Mu. iv.
    II As measure of distance:
    (1) prayāṇamprayāṇa, -kam, followed a few m. es: katicitprayāṇakānyanuvavrāja, C. v.;
    (2) adhvanadhvan (m (= way), after three m. es: adhvasu triṣu, R. xi. 57.
  • MARCH [435] (v.): yātiyātiyāt, pra-, abhi-, abhipra-, (yāyāyaiī, c. 2.), let the whole army m.: prayātu vāhinī sarvā, A. r.;
