    (1) nagnanagna (f. gnāgnā), what are you looking, n. woman: kiṃ nirīkṣase nagnike, P. iv. 62.;

    (4) digambaraḥdigambara (rārāra, raṃ) (poet), made n.: cakre digambaraḥ, K. s. xiii. 147.
    II Fig.: open, plain: anā- vṛtaḥvṛtavṛt (tātāta, taṃ).
    III Mere, simple: kevalaḥkevala (lālāla, laṃ).
