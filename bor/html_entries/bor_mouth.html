• MOUTH [460] (subs.):
    I Lit.:
    (1) mukhammukha, from the m. of Vasantaka: vasantakasya mukhāt, K.s.; of unchecked m.: ayantritamukhaḥ (khākhākha, khaṃ), D. viii.; honey in m., (but) poison in heart: mukhamadhurhṛ daya- viṣaḥ, Sa. v.;
    (2) vaktramvaktra;
    (3) āsyamāsya;
    (4) ānanamānana;
    (5) vadanamvadana. N.B. These words also mean face and when the meaning is doubtful, ranghra should be added, as vaktrarandhram, R. ix. 63.
    II Of things:
    (1) mukhammukha, pots with closed m.s.: apinaddhamukhā ghaṭāḥ, Ve. iii.; rising from the m.s of caves: darīmukhotthaḥ, Ku. i. 8.;
    (2) vaktramvaktra, the m. of a quiver: niṣaṅgavaktram, Ki. xvii. 36.; hundred-m.ed hole: śatamukhaṃ bilam, Mah.: v. Also orifice.
    III Of a river: mukhammukha, waters at the m. of a river: nadī- mukhāmbhaḥ, R. xiii. 10.
    IV Fig.: mukhammukha, grammar, the m. of all learning: sarvavidyānāṃ mukhaṃ vyākaraṇam, K. s. vi. 144.
  • MOUTH [460] (v.):
    I To devour: q. v.: grasate (grasgrasgra, c. l.).
    II To speak out, rant q. v.: śabdāyate (nomi.).
