    I As a particle of comparison:
    (1) vatvatvadvadh affixed, (he who looks on) other's wives as mothers, on other's things as clods mātṛvat paradāreṣu paradravyeṣu loṣṭravat, H. i.; as before pūrvavat;
    (2) yathāyathāyatha (either in comp. to expr. sim. comparison, or with tathātathā or evaṃ to expr. as......so...... also to introduce illustrations): as ordered yathānideśaṃ; as prescribed yathāvidhi; as before yathāpurā, Sa. vi.; as eating is inferred from nourishment or country from its language, so is knowable inferred from knowledge, yathā puṣṭayā bhojanamanumīyate yathā ca bhāṣayā deśaḥ tathā jñānākāreṇa jñeyamanumeyam. D. s. (see also R. iii. 23 and notice the repetition of yathāyathāyatha in such cases); as animals attempt to fly away when they see men with sticks, so.... yathā paśvādayaḥ daṇḍodyatakaraṃ purūṣamabhimukhamupalabhya palāyitumārabhante evaṃ.. S. i. p.; as for instance, fire is inferred from smoke yathā dhūmenāgniriti, N. d. As for instance: sometimes expr. by tathāhiḥ he, as a rule, enjoyed the pleasures of youth as for instance, sometimes roamed over artificial mountains, sometimes walked through summer houses sa prāyaśo yauvanasukhamanubabhūva, tathāhi kadācit krīḍāparvateṣu vicacāra kadācillatāgṛheṣu babhrāma, K.
    II. While: expr. by the pres. part: as he went, he thought vrajaṃśca samarthayāmāsa, K.
    III. Since, in as much as: q. v.:
    (1) by the abl. and when the subs. ends in tātāta or ā by the ins.: as the motion of the vessels (at sea) depends on wind vātādhīnapotagamanatvāt, K. b.; as there was much political work rājakāryasya bahulatayā Sa. vi.;
    (2) yataḥyatasyatayat...tataḥtatastatatat.. Miscell: as long as one lives yāvajjīvaṃ; as far as the ocean ā samudrāt; as high as the knee jānudaghnaḥ (ghnāghna, ghnaṃ); as yet adyāpi. N. B. as, when it connects two subs. s in apposition is expr. by bhūtvā or kṛtvā: I will remain as your queen tava mahiṣī bhūtvā tiṣṭhāmi; sent me to you as his agent māṃ pratinidhiṃ kṛtvā tvatsakāśaṃ prerayāmāsa.
