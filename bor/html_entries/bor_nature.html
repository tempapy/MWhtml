• NATURE: [467]
    I The creation, the universe: q. v.: sṛṣṭiḥsṛṣṭi.
    II The original cause: prakṛtiḥprakṛti.
    III Kind, sort: q. v.: prakāraḥprakāra.
    IV Subs- -tance, purport: q. v.: bhāvaḥbhāva. V. Natural constitution:
    (1) svabhāvaḥsvabhāva, for n. is at the head above all qualities: atītya hi guṇān sarvān svamāvo mūrdhni vartate, H.;
    (2) prakṛtiḥprakṛti, it is the n. of the great: prakṛtiḥ khalu sā mahīyasaḥ, Ki.;
    (3) nisargaḥnisarga (mostly as =adv.), this grove is beautiful by n.: nisargaramaṇīyo'yamārāmaḥ, Pra.;
    (4) bhāvaḥbhāva in comp., of impure n.: aśuddhabhāvaḥ (vāvāvauū, vaṃ) Mah.;
    (5) śīlamśīla (mostly in comp.), for it is the n. of inebriety and insanity: śīlaṃ hi madonmādayoḥ, D. ii.; bad n.d: duḥśīlaḥ (lālāla, laṃ).
    VI Essential n.: svarūpamsvarūpa, n. of the soul: ātmasvarūpam, S. vii. Ph.: to attend to, satisfy a call of n.: maitraṃ karoti, V. p. iii. 13. 8.; śaucaṃ karoti, Mah. s. 193. 4.
