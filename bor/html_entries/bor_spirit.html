• SPIRIT: [667]
    I Breath, life: q. v.: prāṇaḥprāṇa.
    II Animating principle: prāṇaḥprāṇa, s. alone is death- less: prāṇa evaiko mṛtyunā'nāptaḥ, S.: v. Soul. Ph.: the supreme s.: brahman (n.) or paraṃ brahma, Vi.
    III Mind: q. v.: manasmanasmana (n.), loftiness of s.: manasaḥ samunnatiḥ, Ku. vi. 66. Ph.: high s. s (=joy): praharṣaḥpraharṣa; good s. s: praphullatā.
    IV Energy, vigour:
    (1) sattvamsattva, loses s.: sattvāt paribhraśyate, H. 133.; evincing high s.: kṛtasattvavegaḥ, Ki. xvii. 15.;
    (2) tejastejastejatej (mfn.), lacking s.: nistejas, Mu. i. 13.; again with the s. hightened from a determination (to fight): bhūyaḥ samādhānavivṛddhatejāḥ, Ki. xvii. 7.
    V Of style: ojasojasojaoj (n.), Kav. viii. 4.
    VI Temper, disposition: q. v.: prakṛtiḥprakṛti.
    VII Prevailing disposition (as “s. of the age”): rītiḥrīti.
    VIII Real meaning: bhāvaḥbhāva or bhāvārthaḥ: v. Intention.
    IX A person: vyaktiḥvyakti.
    X A ghost:
    (1) bhūtaḥbhūta. Ph.: evil s.: pretaḥpreta, Wilson.
    XI Disembodied s.:
    (1) (= dead);
    (2) not expr. (as corporeality is always supposed), and the s. of the king Daśaratha: sa ca rājā daśarathaḥ, Ram. vi. 102. 4.
    XII A spirituous liquor: surāsurāsura.
