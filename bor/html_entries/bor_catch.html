• CATCH [107] (v. t.):
    I To seize:
    (1) dhārayatidhārayat or dharati (dhṛdhṛ, c. 10. and 1.), for which you were caught dhṛto'si yadartham, N. i. 143; for c. ing fish matsya- dhāraṇārtham, Tārānāth;
    (2) dadhāti, dhatte (dhādhādha, c. 3), the king caught the bird with his hand nṛpaḥ pataṅgaṃ tamadhatta pāṇinā, N. i. 124;
    (3) gṛhṇāti (grahgrah, c. 9), caught the descending (ball) in the air avapatantamākāśa evāgrahīt, D. vi.; I was caught stealing corayitvā grāhitaḥ, D. iv.;
    (4) badhnāti (bandhbandh, c. 9.) (=c. in a trap, etc.), c. ing of elephants kuñjarabandhanam, Nil.
    II Of fire: upa- gacchatigacchat (gamgamga, c. l.) or upaiti (iiīin, c. 2.), all of them caught fire and died sarva eva jvalanamupagatā uparatāśca, Mu. ii.
    III Of disease: to c. a disease vyādhimadhigacchati (gamgamga, c. l.), avāpnoti (āpāp, c. 5.), etc., Śr.
    IV To ensnare: Perh. dharati. V. To charm: q. v.
    VI To overtake: q. v.
  • CATCH [107] (v. i.): lagati (laglag, c. l): my garland has caught in the branch viṭape vaijayantikā me lagnā, Vi.
  • CATCH [107] (subs.):
    I Seizure: q. v. Ph. on c. dhartumdhartu.
    II Of a gate or lock: tālakamtālaka (?).
    III Profit, gain: q. v.
    IV The whole quantity caught at one time: expr. by circumlo.
