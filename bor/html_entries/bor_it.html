• IT: [395] expr. by the “n., m., or f. form” of pron. s given under he according as the noun for which it stands is in Sanskrit n., m., or f.: I have to say much about it: mama bahvākhyeyamatra, Mr.; what is it (you) are thinking of: idaṃ kiṃ cintayate, Mr.; it is she: saiveyam, Na.; whether it (bhāṇḍakaḥbhāṇḍaka) is the same or not: sa evaiṣā na veti, Mr.; therefore it (pṛthvīpṛthvī) looks even: ataḥ sā samā pratibhāti, Go.; its (mañjaryāḥmañjarimañjarī) smell: asyā gandhaḥ, K.; is it true: api nāma satyam, Ma.; it is said in the Purana that the earth is even: purāṇe bhūḥ sabhā kathayate, ṅo.; it has been established in the first part that the Lord of all is the creative cause of the world: prathame'dhyāye sarveśvaro jagata utpatti- kāraṇam pratipāditam, S.; if you think it not very tiresome to go: yadi nātikhedakaraṃ gamanaṃ kalayasi, K. N. B. Note how it is expr. in such cases.
