• SEAT [622] (subs.):
    I For sitting:
    (1) āsanamāsana, take this s.: idamāsanamāsyatām, Mu. i.; āsana- parigrahaṃ karotu, U. iii.; from the s. of justice: dharmāsanātdharmāsana, U. i. 7.;
    (2) pīṭhapīṭha (mn.: dimi- -nutive pīṭhikāpīṭhikā = a bench, stool), on a golden- legged s.: hemapādāṅkitāyāṃ pīṭhikāyām, K. ;
    (3) paryaṅkaḥparyaṅka (a sort of sofa), on a golden s. covered with Ranku's skin: sauvarṇe paryaṅke rāṅkavāstṛte, Ram. ii. 13. 6.;
    (4) āsandīāsandī (= a bamboo chair), when Vaiśampāyana was s. ed: āsandyāmupaviṣṭe vaiśampāyane, K. ;
    (5) śayanamśayana or śayanīyam (for lying down): v. Bed;
    (6) viṣṭaraḥviṣṭara (rare), s. ed: viṣṭarabhājviṣṭarabhāj (mfn.), R. v. 3.
    II Station, abode:
    (1) sthānamsthāna, the s. of our ancestors: sthānaṃ naḥ pūrvajānām, B. xxii. 28.;
    (2) dhāmandhāman (n.), the s. of fortitude: dhāma dhairyasya, K. ;
    (3) āyatanamāyatana, s. of all misconduct: sarvāvinayānāmāyatanam, K. v. Abode.
    III Regular place of sitting: perh. sthānamsthāna.
    IV That part on which a person sits: āsanamāsana, Si. v. 7.
    V In riding: expr. by verb.
  • SEAT [623] (v.):
    I To s. oneself: āsane upaviśati, sam-, (viśviś, c. 6.), niṣīdati (sadsad, c. 1.), etc.
    II To s. another: āsane upaveśayati (c. of viśviś) or abhiniveśayati, Si. i. 15.
