• MAN [433] (subs.):
    I A human being:
    (1) manuṣyaḥmanuṣyamanuṣī, rise and fall of men: pātotpātā manuṣyāṇām, H. ;
    (2) mānuṣaḥmānuṣa, always inaccessible to men: agamyo mānuṣaiḥ sadā, Mah.;
    (3) naraḥnara, but m. endowed with intellect: narāstu buddhisampannāḥ, Mah.; m-stealer: narahartā, Mit.;
    (4) mānavaḥmānava, m.-god: mānavadevaḥmānavadeva, R. xiv. 32.;
    (5) nṛnṛ (m.), m.- eater: nṛbhuj (mfn.), B. N.B. (a) of m.: mānuṣaḥ (ṣī, ṣaṃ): v. Human.; (b) old m., young m, etc. gen. expr. by adj., consisting chiefly of young men: yuvaprāyaḥ (yāyāyaiī, yaṃ), C. ; (c) a m. of straw: akiñcanaḥ, Ku.: (d) he is his own m.: ayaṃ svādhīnaḥ, K.
    II. The human species: expr. by above words, gen. in. pl.
    III As opposed to child: mānuṣaḥmānuṣa, when he became a m.: yadā sa mānuṣo'bhavat (comp. Beng.).
    IV One of manly qualities:
    (1) puruṣaḥpuruṣa, how can the appellation m. (be applied) to him: puruṣoktiḥ kathaṃ tasmin, Ki. xi. 71.; great m.: mahāpuruṣaḥ, U. ;
    (2) pumān, he who has money is a m. in this world: yasyārthāḥ sa pumān loke, H.
    V. As opposed to woman, wife:
    (1) puruṣaḥpuruṣa, men are treacherous hard-hearted: puruṣāḥ kṛtavnāḥ kaṭhinahṛdayāḥ, Vet. 3.; did she desire other men: kimanayā para- puruṣo'bhilaṣitaḥ, P. i. 4.;
    (2) pumān (v. Male), better impotence in men: varaṃ klaivyaṃ puṃsām, H.
    VI. An attendant, servant:
    (1) mānuṣaḥmānuṣa,
    (2) puruṣaḥpuruṣa, king's men: rājapuruṣāḥ, P.
    VII. In chess:
    (1) śāraḥśāra;
    (2) naraḥnara. Ph.: m.-of-war: yuddhapotaḥ.
  • MAN [433] (v.):
    I Lit.: puruṣādhiṣṭhitaṃ (f. tāṃ) karoti; manuṣyān, etc. sthāpayati (c. of sthāsthāstha) (with loc.).
    II Fig.: v. To strengthen.
