• RING [600] (subs.):
    I A circle: q. v.: maṇḍalammaṇḍala, dancing in a r.: maṇḍalanṛtyam.
    II For fingers: aṅgulīaṅgulīaṅguli (rīrīri) yamyamya, -kam, interchange of r. s: aṅgulīyaka- vinimayaḥ, D. ii.; r. -finger: anāmikā (but madhyamāmadhyamāmadhyama, i. e. the middle finger is the Hindoo r. -f. Hence madhyamopalaḥ, Ki. ix. 2.); bezel of a r.: maṇibandhaḥ., -nam, Sa. v.
    III A circular area: maṇḍalammaṇḍala, vīthiḥ (?).
  • RING [601] (v. t.):
    I Of bells: cālayati (c. of calcal) (= to move). N.B. vādayati (c. of vadvad) is of striking bells.
    II To fit with r. s: expr. by circumlo.
    III To encircle: pariveṣṭayati (c. of veṣṭveṣṭ).
  • RING [601] (v. i.):
    (1) kvaṇati (kvaṇkvaṇ, c. 1.);
    (2) raṇatiraṇat (raṇraṇ, c. 1.): v. To sound.
