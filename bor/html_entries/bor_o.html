• O, OH: [479]
    I In addressing:
    (1) hehehahāhi;
    (2) bho bhoḥ, or bho bho (gen. to men), o, courtiers: bho bho kulacāriṇaḥ, Vi.;
    (3) ayiayiayin or aye (expr. of tenderness), o, you unbearing: ayi asahane, Ra.;
    (4) rererarāri or are (to inferiors or in contempt), o, you fool: are mūrkha, Mr.;
    (5) hāhāha (in sorrow), o, my father, o, my mothers, o, my dear friend Sugriva: hā tāta, hā mātaraḥ, hā priyasakha sugrīva, U;
    (6) by voc.
    II Of joy and surprise:
    (1) ahoahoahu, oh, how grand and beautiful is earth: aho udāraramaṇīyā pṛthivī, Sa.;
    (2) ayiayiayin or aye, oh, you also don't know: ayi tvamapi na vetsi, Mah.
    III Of surprise, anger, contempt: āḥāsaāasār, oh, I understand, very well: āḥ jñātaṃ, tiṣṭha, Mu.
    IV Of grief: hā: v. Alas.
