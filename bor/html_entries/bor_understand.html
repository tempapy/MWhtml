    I In gen.:
    (1) budhyate (budhbudh, c. 4.), did (he) u. it to be a golden lotus: abuddha taṃ kāñcanamambujanma kim, N. i. 122.; then made him u. (i. e. informed): athainaṃ pratyabodhayat, R. i. 74.; u. (i. e. learn) this from me: matsakāśānni- bodhata, M. i. 119.;
    (2) avagacchati (gamgamga, c. 1.), foolish men u. the loss of friend as thorns put in the heart: avagacchati mūḍhacetanaḥ priyanāśaṃ hṛdi śalya- marpitam, R. viii. 38.;
    (3) avaiti (iiīin, c. 2.): v. To know;
    (4) gṛhnāti (grahgrah, c. 9.): v. To comprehend;
    (5) upalabhate (labhlabh, c. 1.): v. To perceive.
    II To imply; here the nominative is understood: atra kartohyaḥ; open or understood: prakāśaḥ sūcito vā.
