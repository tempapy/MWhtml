• FINE [278] (adj.):
    I Not coarse or heavy: sūkṣmasūkṣma (f. kṣmākṣmā), coarse and f.: sthūlaḥ sūkṣmaḥ, Ku. ii. 11.; f. thread: sūkṣmatantuḥ V. d.; of f. intellect: sūkṣmabuddhi (mfn.) M. n.
    II Excellent, good: q. v.
    (1) utkṛṣṭaḥutkṛṣṭa (ṣṭā, ṣṭaṃ);
    (2) su- in comp., having f. teeth: sudat (f. tīti), N.
    III Beautiful, ele- -gant: śobhanaḥśobhana (nānāna, naṃ), f. language: śobhanoktiḥ; oh. f. lady: śobhane, Mah.
    IV Artful: q. v.
  • FINE [278] (subs.): arthadaṇḍaḥ or sim. daṇḍaḥdaṇḍa (when the sense is clear), unable to pay the f.: daṇḍaṃ dātuma- śaknu van, M.; is liable to a fine of 100 (panas): śataṃ daṇḍamarhati, M.; is to be punished with a f. of fifty panas: pañcāśat paṇān daṇḍayaḥ, K. b.
  • FINE [278] (v.t.):
    I To refine: q.v.
    II To decorate: q.v.
    III To impose a f.:
    (1) arthadaṇḍaṃ karoti;
    (2) daṇḍayati (daṇḍ, c. 10.) (= to punish when the sense is clear);
    (3) daṇḍaṃ or damaṃ dāpayati (c. of dādāda=to cause to give), should f. him double: taṃ dāpayed dviguṇaṃ damam, Y. iii. 309.
