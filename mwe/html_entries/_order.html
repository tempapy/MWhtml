There are four Āśramas, corresponding to the four stages of the Brahman's life. Every Brahman who has passed through these four stages is supposed to have passed through the four orders in succession. When, in the first, he is called brahmacArI m.; in the 2d, gfhasTaH; in the 3d, vAnaprasTaH or vEKAnasaH or yatI; in the 4th, BikzuH m., sannyAsI m. The first order, or that of the religious student, is also called liNgaM. Other terms, expressive of religious orders, are parAyaRaM, mArgaH, sampradAyaH, panTAH m.
