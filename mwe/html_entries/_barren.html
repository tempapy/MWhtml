BARREN [039-a], a. (Not prolific) banDyaH -nDyA -nDyaM, asutI -tinI -ti (n), aprasavI -vinI -vi (n), prajArahitaH -tA -taM. — Unfruitful) nizPalaH -lA -laM, aPalaH -lA -laM, avakeSI -SinI -Si (n), SuzkaH -zkA -zkaM, — (Useless) vyarTaH -rTA -rTaM, anarTakaH -kA -kaM, moGaH -GA -GaM. — (Empty) SUnyaH -nyA -nyaM, riktaH -ktA -ktaM; ‘a barren woman,’ banDyA, apasUtA; ‘a barren soil,’ marusTalaM or -lI. BARREN [853-a], a. (As a woman) anapatyA, anapatyakA, ajAtApatyA, apra- jAtA. The Hindūs divide barren women into three classes, viz. 1. ‘She who has no offspring,’ banDyA; 2. ‘She who bears only one child,’ kAkabanDyA; 3. ‘She whose off- spring die,’ mftabanDyA.
