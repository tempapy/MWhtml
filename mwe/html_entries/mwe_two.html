TWO [815-b], a. dvO m. f. du., dve n. du. (dvi), dvayaM affixed, dvi prefixed; ‘two men,’ dvO puruzO or puruzadvayaM or expressed simply by the dual, puruzO; ‘of two men,’ dvayoH puruzayoH, or simply puruzayoH; ‘two months,’ dvimAsaM, mAsadvayaM; ‘two hundred,’ dve Sate n. du., SatadvayaM, or simply Sate; ‘two thousand,’ dve sahasre, sahasradvayaM, or simply sahasre. The crude form dvi is chiefly used in Bahuvrīhi compounds; as, ‘two-armed,’ dviBujaH -jA -jaM; ‘two-footed,’ dvipAdaH -dA -daM, dvipadaH &c. ‘two- handed,’ dvihastaH -stA -staM; ‘two-headed,’ dvimUrdDaH -rdDA -rdDaM; ‘two- formed,’ dvirUpaH -pA -paM; ‘two-edged,’ dviDAraH -rA -raM, uBayata- stIkzRaH -kzRA -kzRaM; ‘consisting of two,’ dvimayaH -yI -yaM; ‘two- year-old,’ dvivArzikaH -kI -kaM; ‘in two ways,’ dviDA, dvEDaM; ‘two or three,’ dvitrAH -trAH -trARi (tra).
