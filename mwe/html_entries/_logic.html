LOGIC [463-a], s. tarkaH, tarkavidyA, tarkaSAstraM, nyAyaH, nyAyavidyA, nyAyaSAstraM, vicAravidyA, vyUhaH, prohaH, aByUhaH, nirUhaH, UhaH, vESezikaM, AnvI- kzikI, anumAnoktiH f., GruvaH. The nyAya school was instituted by the sage Gautama, the branch of it called vESezikaM was founded by Kaṇāda. The following are some of the terms of logic; ‘universal pervadence or extension,’ vyAptiH f., aBivyAptiH; ‘extreme extension or conclusion unwarranted by the premises,’ ativyAptiH; ‘individual or particular extension,’ avyAptiH f., avyApyavfttiH f.; sign of the existence of an inherent property, vyAptilakzaRaM; ‘subject of an inference,’ vyApyaM.
