FOUR [289-a], a. catvAraH m. pl. catasraH f. pl. catvAri n. pl. (catur), catuzwayaH -yI -yaM. The neut. of this last word expresses ‘the aggregate of four,’ and sometimes ‘four’ itself; as, ‘four hours,’ daRqacatuzwayaM; ‘in four ways,’ caturviDaH -DA -DaM, caturDA; ‘four times,’ catuH ind., catuHkftvas ind.
