FOUR [289-a], a. catvāraḥ m. pl. catasraḥ f. pl. catvāri n. pl. (catur), catuṣṭayaḥ -yī -yaṃ. The neut. of this last word expresses ‘the aggregate of four,’ and sometimes ‘four’ itself; as, ‘four hours,’ daṇḍacatuṣṭayaṃ; ‘in four ways,’ caturvidhaḥ -dhā -dhaṃ, caturdhā; ‘four times,’ catuḥ ind., catuḥkṛtvas ind.
