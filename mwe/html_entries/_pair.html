PAIR [567-b], s. miTunaM, dvayaM, dvandvaM, yugaM, yugmaM, yugalaM, yamalaM, yAmalaM, yamaH, yamakaM, yutakaM; ‘a pair of doves,’ pArAvatamiTunaM; ‘a pair of oxen,’ gomiTunaM, goyugaM; ‘a pair of birds,’ pakziRor miTunaM, pakzimiTunaM, pakzidvayaM; ‘a married pair,’ dampatI m. du., BAryyApatI m. du., strIpuruzO. The word dvandvaH is used to denote that form of grammatical combination which unites two or more words of similar or opposite signification; thus, ‘hand and foot,’ pARipAdaM; ‘affection and hatred,’ rAgadvezO m. du.
