NEITHER [530-b], pron. (Not either, not the one or the other) expressed by anyataraḥ -rā -raṃ or ekataraḥ -rā -raṃ or ubhau, or simply by the dual in conjunction with the negative na; as, ‘neither of them can break the bow,’ dhanur bhaṃktum ekataro na śaknoti or ubhau na śaknutaḥ or simply na śaknutaḥ. NEITHER [530-b], conj. expressed by the negative na or na ca, or by the prohibitive mā. Sometimes in conjunction with vā; as, ‘he took neither food nor clothes for his journey,’ yātrārthaṃ bhakṣyaṃ vastraṃ vā na jagrāha; ‘take neither food nor clothes,’ bhakṣyaṃ vastraṃ vā mā gṛhāṇa; ‘do not eat the fruit neither touch it,’ phalaṃ mā khādata mā spṛśata; ‘he did not touch, neither did he smell,’ nāspṛśad na cājighrat.
