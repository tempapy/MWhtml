YOKE [848-b], s. (For the necks of oxen) yugaḥ -gaṃ, prāsaṅgaḥ, ābaddhaḥ; ‘of a plough,’ kuśaḥ; ‘pin of the yoke,’ yugakīlaḥ -lakaḥ; ‘tie of it,’ yoktraṃ, yotraṃ, ābandhaḥ. — (Yoke or pair of oxen) goyugaṃ, gomithunaṃ. — (Fit for the yoke) yugyaḥ -gyā -gyaṃ, prāsaṅgyaḥ &c. To YOKE [848-b], v. a. yuj (c. 7. yunakti, c. 10. yojayati -yituṃ), niyuj.
