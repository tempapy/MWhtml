SORRY [760-a], a. (Grieved) duHKI -KinI -Ki (n), duHKitaH -tA -taM, see GRIEVED. — (Sorry for one's own conduct, pained) anutApI &c., paScAttApI &c., anutaptaH -ptA -ptaM, anuSocakaH -kA -kaM, anuSayI &c., sAnuSayaH -yA -yaM, jAtAnuSokaH -kA -kaM, jAtAnutApaH -pA -paM, jAtAnuSayaH -yA -yaM, jAtaKedaH -dA -daM, KedayuktaH -ktA -ktaM, anutApayuktaH &c. ‘to be sorry,’ see To REPENT. — (Poor, wretched) tucCaH -cCA -cCaM, kfpaRaH -RA -RaM, kutsitaH -tA -taM or expressed by the affixes tara, ra, ka, kalpa, pASa, deSIya, or by the prefixes dur, ku, kad &c. as, ‘a sorry horse,’ aSvataraH, aSvakaH; ‘a sorry ox,’ fzaBataraH; ‘a sorry poet,’ kavikalpaH, kukaviH; ‘sorry fare,’ kadannaM; ‘a sorry wench,’ pApIyasI.
