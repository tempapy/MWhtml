PLANET [597-b], s. grahaH, Braman grahaH, tArA, tArakA -kaM, jyotis n., jyotizI, jyotizkaH, KecaraH, KagaH, gagaRecaraH, gagaRADvagaH, vihagaH, KolkaH, nakzatraM, fkzaH, dyuzad m., dyusad m., BaM; ‘minor planet,’ upagrahaH; ‘the planets collectively,’ grahagaRaH, jyotirgaRaH, grahamaRqalaM, Bramanto grahAH m. pl.; ‘the nine planets,’ navagrahAH m. pl. These, according to the Hindūs, are 1. the sun, sUryyaH; 2. the moon, candraH; 3. Mercury, buDaH; 4. Venus, SukraH; 5. Mars, maNgalaH; 6. Jupiter, guruH m.; 7. Saturn, SaniH m.; 8. the ascending node, rAhuH m.; 9. the descending node, ketuH m. The two last are considered to be minor planets. The other names of the planets will be found under each separate head of Mercury, &c. — (Auspicious planet) SuBa- grahaH, sugrahaH, sOmyagrahaH; of these there are four, viz. Mer- cury, Venus, Jupiter, and the Moon. — (Inauspicious pla- net) kugrahaH, aSuBagrahaH, pApagrahaH; of these there are five, viz. Mars, Saturn, the Sun, and the two nodes. ‘Planet at its apex,’ tuNgI m. (n); ‘motion of the planets,’ grahacAraH; ‘motion in their orbits,’ gatiH f.; ‘passage of a planet,’ saNkramaH, saNkrAntiH f.; ‘conjunction,’ saNgamaH; ‘aspect,’ grahadaSA; ‘influence,’ grahayogaH, grahAveSaH; ‘unpropitious influence,’ grahapIqA, grahabADA; ‘propitious influence,’ grahAnukUlyaM, grahabalaM; ‘worship of the planets,’ grahapUjA; ‘propitiation of them,’ grahaSAntiH f., grahasADanaM; ‘sacrifice in propitiation of them,’ grahayAgaH, grahayajYaH, grahamaKaH; ‘presents made to Brāhmans for propitiating them,’ grahadAnaM; ‘incantations to render them propitious,’ grahajapaH; ‘ascertainment of their aspect,’ graha- sADanaM; ‘calculations or tables for determining their posi- tion,’ grahasAraRI; ‘diagram representing their aspect,’ graha- kuRqalI; ‘meeting of the seven planets under one sign,’ saptagrahasaNgamaH.
