MONTH [513-a], s. mAsaH, mAs m., pakzadvayaM, pakzayugaM, nAkzatraM, yavyaH, varzAMSaH, varzANgaH. The following are the twelve Hindu months, 1. cEtraH, corresponding to the last half of March and the first half of April, 2. vESAKaH, April, May, 3. jyezwaH, May, June, 4. AzAQaH, June, July, 5. SrAvaRaH, July, August, 6. BAdraH or BAdrapadaH, August, September, 7. ASvinaH or ASvayujaH, September, October, 8. kArttikaH, October, November, 9. mArga- SIrzaH, November, December, 10. pOzaH, December, January, 11. mAGaH, January, February, 12. PAlgunaH, February, March. — (Lunar month) cAndramAsaH; ‘half of a lunar month,’ pakzaH; ‘light or first half of the month,’ SuklapakzaH, SudDapakzaH, sitapakzaH, pUrvapakzaH, praTamapakzaH, devapakzaH; ‘dark or second half of the month,’ kfzRapakzaH, tamisraH, tamisrapakzaH, uttarapakzaH, aparapakzaH, bahulaH; ‘last day of the half-month,’ pakzAntaH, pakzAvasaraH. — (Solar month) sOramAsaH, sOraH; ‘a month old,’ mAsajAtaH -tA -taM, mAsInaH -nA -naM.
