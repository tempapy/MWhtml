FOR [282-b], conj. (Because) yatas, yat, yena, yasmAt, hi, iti hetoH, tadarTaM, anena hetunA. See BECAUSE. — (For as much as) yatas, yat, or expressed by the abl. c. of the abst. noun; as, ‘for as much as he was a good man,’ sajjanatvAt. FOR [282-b], prep. (In exchange for) prati with abl. c., vinimayena; ‘he exchanges beans for sesamum seed,’ tileByaH prati mAzAn dadAti; or expressed by the inst. c., as, tilEr mAzAn pariva- -rttayati; ‘in exchange for one's life,’ svaprARavinimayena. — (Instead of, in place of) sTAne, prati. — (Toward) prati, uddiSya with acc. c. — (On account of, by reason of) arTaM, arTe, hetoH, hetO, kfte, nimitte, kAraRAt all affixed to the crude; as, ‘for this cause,’ tadarTaM, or expressed by uddiSya with acc. c., or by the abl. c.; as, ‘for anger he did it,’ kopAt tat kftavAn, ‘for want of money,’ DanABAvAt. — (Conducive to) arTaM, arTe, or expressed by the dat. c., as, ‘for the good of the world,’ BUtaye BuvaH; ‘for one's own advantage,’ AtmavivfdDaye — (For the sake of) arTaM, arTe, hetoH, kfte, or by dat. c., as, ‘for the satisfying of hunger,’ kzuDASAntaye; ‘for thy enjoyment,’ tava BogAya. — (In order to obtain) arTaM, arTe or by dat. c., as, ‘for fame,’ pratipattaye, or even by inst. c., as, ‘for hire,’ BAwakena. — (In return for) prati, or more usually expressed by the indec. part.; as, ‘for so doing,’ tat kftvA. — (During a certain time) yAvat, paryyantaM; ‘for life,’ puruzasyAyur yAvat, yAvajjIvaM, jIvanaparyyantaM, or by prefixing A with abl. c., as, AmaraRAt, AjIvanAntAt (i. e. until death); ‘lasting for life,’ AmaraRAntikaH -kA -kaM; ‘for three days,’ dinatrayaparyyantaM; ‘for a hundred years,’ varzaSataM yAvat. Sometimes expressed by the acc. c., as, ‘for one month,’ ekaM mAsaM; ‘for two months,’ dvO mAsO; ‘for a hundred years,’ varzaSataM, or even by the instr. c., as, ‘for twelve years,’ dvAdaSaBir varzEr; ‘for a few days,’ katipayadivasEH; ‘for a long time,’ cirakAlaM, anekakAlaM, cirAt, cireRa, cirAya; ‘food for a year,’ varzopa- Bogyam annaM. — (In proportion to, considering) apekzya, uddiSya, pratIkzya, avekzya, all with acc. c. — (On the part of) apekzya, uddiSya, kfte; ‘as for me,’ mAmapekzya, mAmuddiSya, matkfte. — (On the side of) sapakzaH -kzA -kzaM; ‘he is for us,’ asmAkaM sapakzo Bavati — (Notwithstanding, for all that) taTApi. — (As if) vat affixed, iva; ‘he shewed himself for dead,’ mftavat or mftam iva AtmAnam adarSayat. — (According to) yaTA, anusAreRa, anurUpeRa; ‘for one's ability,’ yaTASakti. ‘For,’ may be expressed by the acc. c. in such phrases as ‘looking for,’ ‘waiting for,’ &c. as, ‘he looks for me,’ mAm apekzate, or by other cases; as, ‘we depend on God for success,’ ISvarADInA sidDiH; ‘I have great affection for her,’ tasyAm anurAgavAn asmi or tayA saha mahAn snehaH, or by gata, as, ‘through love for him,’ tadgatena anurAgeRa, or by the crude form, as, ‘search for money,’ DanAnvezaRaM, or even by the affix sAt, as, ‘he built a house for the Brahman,’ gfhaM cakAra dvijAtisAt. — (For the most part) prAyeRa, prAyas, prAyaSas, BUyizWaM.
