To PRETEND [622-b], v. a. or n. (Sham, feign) chadma kṛ, kūṭaṃ kṛ, vyājaṃ kṛ, chalaṃ kṛ, kapaṭaṃ kṛ, dhvajīkṛ, chal (c. 10. chalayati -yituṃ), veśaṃ dhṛ (c. 10. dhārayati -yituṃ) or bhṛ, chadmaveśaṃ dhṛ. Often expressed by the root dṛś in caus., or by mithyā in comp.; as, ‘he pretends to be asleep,’ ātmānaṃ prasuptam iva or prasuptavad darśayati, mithyāsupto or mithyāprasupto bhavati; ‘he pretends to be dead,’ ātmānaṃ mṛtavat sandarśayati; ‘to pretend to be just,’ sādhuveśaṃ dhṛ, āryyaveśaṃ dhṛ, dharmmadhvajī -jinī -ji bhū, āryyarūpaḥ -pā -paṃ bhū. — (Allege a false plea) mithyāhetuṃ vad, mithyānimittaṃ vad, mithyā vad. — (Lay claim, pretend to) abhiman (c. 4. -manyate -mantuṃ), mithyā abhiman, mithyā- bhimānaṃ kṛ, mithyā grah, anadhikārapūrvvaṃ grah, abhipretaḥ -tā -taṃ bhū; ‘to pretend to learning,’ vidyābhimānaṃ kṛ, vidyābhipretaḥ -tā -taṃ bhū, vidyābhimānī -ninī -ni bhū.
