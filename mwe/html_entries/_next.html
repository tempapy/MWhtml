NEXT [532-a], a. (In time, &c.) anantaraH -rA -raM, nirantaraH -rA -raM. — (Next in place, having nothing intervening) avyavaDAnaH -nA -naM, avyavahitaH -tA -taM, anantarAlaH -lA -laM, nirantarAlaH -lA -laM, apa- dAntaraH -rA -raM, sannihitaH -tA -taM, pArSvasTaH -sTA -sTaM, antikaH -kA -kaM, antimaH -mA -maM. Next in time may also be expressed by paraH -rA -raM or antaraM; as, ‘the next day,’ paradivasaH, parAhaH; ‘the next world,’ paralokaH -kaM, lokAntaraM; ‘in the next world,’ paratra, pretya, amutra; ‘relating to the next world,’ pAratrikaH -kI -kaM, amutratyaH -tyA -tyaM, AmuzmikaH -kI -kaM; ‘the next moment,’ kzaRAntaraM; ‘next born,’ anantarajaH -jA -jaM; ‘next of kin,’ anantaraH -rA -raM; ‘next but one,’ ekAntaraH -rA -raM; ‘next but two,’ dvyantaraH -rA -raM, dvyekAntaraH -rA -raM. NEXT [532-a], adv. anantaraM, tadanantaraM, pratyanantaraM, paraM -reRa, paratas, parastAt, tataH paraM, ataH paraM, ata UrdDvaM; ‘next day,’ paradivase, paredyus; ‘next moment,’ kzaRAntare.
