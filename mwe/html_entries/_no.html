No [533-b], adv. na, no, nahi; ‘or no,’ na vA. No [534-a], a. (None) usually expressible by na; as, ‘no one,’ na kaScit m., na kAcit f., na ko'pi m., na kApi f., na kaScana m., na kAcana f.; ‘no thing,’ na kiYcit, na kimapi, na kiYcana; ‘no other,’ nAnyaH -nyA -nyat; ‘no where,’ na kvacit, na kvApi, na kutracit, na kutrApi; ‘no more, not again,’ na punar; ‘no great distance,’ nAtidUraM. When ‘no’ is privative, it is expressed by nir, a, vi &c. prefixed, or by hIna, SUnya, varjita, rahita &c. affixed; as, ‘having no fruit,’ nizPalaH -lA -laM, aPalaH &c., viPalaH &c., PalahInaH -nA -naM, &c.
