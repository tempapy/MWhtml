FROM [293-b], prep. In phrases where it denotes procession, deriva- -tion, deliverance, extraction, separation, distance, emis- -sion, removal, the reason or motive of any act, and the ground or cause of any thing, it is usually expressed by the abl. case. It is also expressed in some phrases by such words as sakASAt, maDyAt, praBfti, AraBya, or even by the prep. A with the abl. c. The following are examples; ‘coming out from his house,’ niHmftya gfhAt; ‘from avarice anger arises,’ loBAt kroDaH praBavati; ‘even from poison nectar may be taken,’ vizAd apy amftaM grAhyaM; ‘he delivers me from danger,’ trAyate mAM BayAt; ‘Krishna is a hundred kos from Somanāth,’ kfzRaH SataM kroSAH somanATAt; ‘he separates the good from the bad,’ sADUn duzweByaH pfTakkaroti; ‘having discharged an arrow from the bow,’ vARaM DanuzaH prakzipya; ‘he rises from his seat,’ AsanAd uttizWati; ‘from fear of punishment,’ daRqaBayAt; ‘from unsteadiness of mind,’ anavasTitacittatvAt; ‘from being founded on observation,’ pratyakzamUlatvAt. The abl. c. is also used to express ‘from,’ in the sense of since; as, ‘from childhood,’ bAlyAt or bAlyataH; ‘from the earliest period of the world,’ jagadAramBa- -kAlAt, but in these cases praBfti or AraBya or avaDi are often added, or A may sometimes, but very rarely, be prefixed; as, ‘from childhood,’ bAlyAt praBfti, AbAlyAt, AbAlyakAlAt; ‘from birth,’ janmataH praBfti, janmata AraBya, AjanmataH, janmAvaDi; ‘from the very first glance,’ praTamadarSanakzaRAt praBfti. The word sakASAt is used in such phrases as, ‘from the presence of the king,’ rAjasakASAt; ‘an order from the council,’ saBAsakASAd AjYA. The word maDyAt in such phrases as, ‘from among the soldiers,’ sEnyamaDyAt. Other examples of the modes of expressing ‘from’ are, ‘from the top to the bottom,’ UrdDvAd aDaHparyyantaM; ‘from the hole of the weasel to the hole of the serpent,’ nakulavivarAd AraBya sarpavivaraM yAvat; ‘from birth to death,’ janmAvaDi mftyuparyyantaM; ‘from that time forth,’ tadApraBfti, tatkAlAt praBftiH; ‘from this day,’ adyAraBya, adyapraBfti, adyAvaDi; ‘from city to city,’ purAt puraM; ‘from the beginning to the end,’ praTamAtpraBfti or ApraTamAd antaM, yAvat, anupUrvvaSas; ‘Let them be told from me,’ ucyantAM mama vacanAt; ‘from any one soever,’ yataHkutaScit; ‘from above,’ UrdDvAt, uparizwAt; ‘from below,’ aDastAt, aDaH- -sTAnAt; ‘from behind,’ paScimaBAgAt; ‘from far,’ dUrAt, dUratas; ‘from hence,’ itas, atas; ‘from without,’ vahirdeSAt; ‘from within,’ aByantarAt.
