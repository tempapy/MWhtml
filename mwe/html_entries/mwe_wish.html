To WISH [843-a], v. n. iṣ (c. 6. icchati, eṣituṃ), abhilaṣ (c. 1. -laṣati -ṣituṃ), vāñch (c. 1. vāñchati -ñchituṃ), kāṃkṣ (c. 1. kāṃkṣati -kṣituṃ), ākāṃkṣ, see To DESIRE. Often expressed by the desid. form of a root; as, ‘to wish to obtain,’ āp in des. (īpsati -psituṃ), labh in des. (lipsate -psituṃ); ‘to wish to go,’ gam in des. (jigami- ṣati -ṣituṃ). To WISH [843-a], v. a. (Well to one) iṣṭaṃ or maṅgalam or kalyāṇam āśaṃs (c. 1. -śaṃsate -situṃ) or āśās (c. 2. śāste -situṃ) or prārth. — (Wish one ill) aniṣṭaṃ or amaṅgalam āśaṃs or prārth. WISH [843-a], s. icchā, abhilāṣaḥ, ākāṃkṣā, iṣṭaṃ, spṛhā, manorathaḥ, īpsitaṃ, see DESIRE; ‘according to one's wish,’ yathābhimataṃ; ‘earnest wish,’ kautūhalaṃ, kutūhalaṃ, see LONGING.
