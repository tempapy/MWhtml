To WISH [843-a], v. n. iz (c. 6. icCati, ezituM), aBilaz (c. 1. -lazati -zituM), vAYC (c. 1. vAYCati -YCituM), kAMkz (c. 1. kAMkzati -kzituM), AkAMkz, see To DESIRE. Often expressed by the desid. form of a root; as, ‘to wish to obtain,’ Ap in des. (Ipsati -psituM), laB in des. (lipsate -psituM); ‘to wish to go,’ gam in des. (jigami- zati -zituM). To WISH [843-a], v. a. (Well to one) izwaM or maNgalam or kalyARam ASaMs (c. 1. -SaMsate -situM) or ASAs (c. 2. SAste -situM) or prArT. — (Wish one ill) anizwaM or amaNgalam ASaMs or prArT. WISH [843-a], s. icCA, aBilAzaH, AkAMkzA, izwaM, spfhA, manoraTaH, IpsitaM, see DESIRE; ‘according to one's wish,’ yaTABimataM; ‘earnest wish,’ kOtUhalaM, kutUhalaM, see LONGING.
