A. [001-a] As an indefinite article before nouns singular, for the most part not expressed in Sanskrit; as, ‘a lion,’ siMhaH; (One) ekaH -kA -kaM. — (Some, a certain) kaScit; as, ‘a certain man,’ kaScit puruzaH. — (Each, every) expressed by prati or anu, before the acc. neut.; as, ‘a day,’ ‘each day,’ pratidinaM or anudinaM. When ‘a’ comes before a participle, after a verb of motion, it is expressed by the dat. c.; as, ‘he went a hunting,’ mfgayAyE gataH; or by arTaM preceded by the crude form of a noun; as, mfgayArTaM gataH; or by the infinitive; as, mfgayAM karttuM gataH.
