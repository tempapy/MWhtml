To MUTTER [523-a], v. a. and n. (Utter words with a low indistinct voice) aspaṣṭaṃ vad (c. 1. vadati -dituṃ), aspaṣṭavācā vad, aspaṣṭasvareṇa vad, aspaṣṭoccāraṇena vad, avyaktaṃ vad, or uccar (c. 10. -cārayati -yituṃ), nīcavācā or nīcasvareṇa vad, nīcair vad, jap (c. 1. japati -pituṃ), prajap; this last verb is applied especially to the muttering of prayers, though it may be used in other senses; as, ‘to mutter into the ear,’ karṇe jap, karṇam upajap. — (Sound with a low rumbling voice) garj (c. 1. garjati -rjituṃ), stana (c. 1. stanati -nituṃ), gambhīraśabdaṃ kṛ. MUTTER [523-a], MUTTERING, s. (Uttering with a low voice) aspaṣṭoccāraṇaṃ, avyaktoccāraṇaṃ, nīcabhāṣaṇaṃ, nīcavākyaṃ, nīcasvareṇa bhāṣaṇaṃ, upāṃśu vādaḥ. — (Low rumbling sound) garjanaṃ, garjitaṃ, āmandraśabdaḥ, gambhī- raśabdaḥ; ‘of clouds,’ meghanirghoṣaḥ. — (Inaudible repetition of prayers, &c.) japaḥ, jāpaḥ, japanaṃ, svādhyāyaḥ, jāpyaṃ, japajāpyaṃ, upāṃśuḥ m.
