MODE [509-b], s. (Method, manner) rItiH f., mArgaH, viDiH m., viDAnaM, prakAraH, niyamaH, vfttiH f., ftiH f., kramaH, saMviDAnaM, paryyAyaH, maryyAdA, DArA, viDaH in comp.; ‘mode of living,’ vfttiH f.; ‘in this mode,’ anena viDAnena, eMvaviDaM -Dena, anena prakAreRa; ‘mode of speaking,’ vAgvyApAraH, vAgvfttiH f. — (Form) rUpaM, AkAraH, AkftiH f., saMskAraH. — (Custom) AcAraH, vyavahAraH, pracAraH, rItiH f., niyamaH, aByAsaH, sTitiH f., AvfttiH f., sampradAyaH. — (Fashionable custom) saByalokavyavahAraH, saByarItiH f., SizwavyavahAraH, SizwamArgaH, saByasampradAyaH, lokAcAraH. — (In metaphysics) viSezavasturUpaM, viSezadravyarUpaM, viSe- zadravyaprakAraH. — (In grammar) vAcyaM. — (In music) rAgaH m., rAgiRI f., varRaH. The musical modes are personified as either male or female. The male are called Rāgas; the female are Rāginīs, and are considered to be the wives of the Rāgas. The following are some of the names for the male: BEravaH, mAlavaH, sAraNgaH, hindolaH, vasantaH, vasantadUtaH, paYcamaH, dIpakaH, meGaH, nawaH, nawanArAyaRaH, kalpARaH, kAmaboDaH, trowakaH, viBAsaH. The following are some of the Rāginīs, or fe- male personifications: dIpikA, nawI, trowakI, deSakArI, gOqI, jaNgalA, DaneSvarI, BEravI, rAmakarI or rAmakelI or rAmakalI, sEnDavI, pUrvvI, BUpAlI, maDumADavI.
