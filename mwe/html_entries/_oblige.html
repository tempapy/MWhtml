To OBLIGE [542-a], v. a. (Constrain, compel). Expressed by the causal form usually in conjunction with balAt or balena or balAtkAreRa; as, ‘to oblige a man to do any thing,’ balena puruzaM kiYcit kf in caus. (kArayati -yituM) or pravft in caus. (-varttayati -yituM); ‘to oblige to pay,’ dA in caus. (dApayati -yituM); ‘to oblige to go,’ balena gam (c. 10. gamayati -yituM); ‘he obliged me to take the book,’ SAstraM mAM grAhayAmAsa. The root arh is sometimes used in this sense. See To COMPEL. (Bind) banD (c. 9. baDnAti, bandDuM), nibanD. — (By a compact or agree- ment) niyamena or niyamapUrvvaM or pratijYApUrvvaM banD. — (By a favor or kindness) upakAreRa banD or badDaM -dDAM kf, upakArabadDaM -dDAM kf. — (Favor, do a favor to) anugrah (c. 9. -gfhlAti -grahItuM), anugrahaM kf, anuruD (c. 4. -ruDyati -te -rodDuM), upakf, upakAraM kf, sAhAyyaM kf, hitaM kf, priyaM kf; ‘to be obliged.’ See the next.
