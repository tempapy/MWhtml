SHIP [737-a], s. vṛhannaukā, vṛhannauḥ f., vṛhattaraṇī, nauḥ f., naukā, vṛhatpotaḥ, potaḥ, taraṇiḥ -ṇī f., arṇavayānaṃ, jalayānaṃ, arṇavapotaḥ, vohitthaṃ, madguḥ m., plavaḥ; ‘to take ship,’ naukām āruh (c. 1. -rohati -roḍhuṃ); ‘ship of war,’ yuddhanauḥ f., yuddhanaukā. SHIP [737-a], an affix denoting abstract state or office is rendered by tā or tvaṃ; as, ‘a judge,’ dharmmādhyakṣaḥ; ‘judgeship,’ dharmmā- dhyakṣatā -tvaṃ; ‘a son,’ putraḥ; ‘sonship,’ putratā -tvaṃ. To SHIP [737-a], v. a. (Put on board ship) naukām āruh in caus. (-ropa- yati -yituṃ) or praviś (c. 10. -veśayati -yituṃ), naukāyāṃ niviś or samṛ in caus. — (Fix in its place) svasthāne dhā or ruh in caus., svasthānīkṛ.
