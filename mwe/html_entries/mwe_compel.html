To COMPEL [113-b], v. a. Expressed by the causal form, usually in conjunction with the adverb balāt or balena; as, ‘the king should compel him to pay,’ taṃ rājā balād dāpayet; ‘he com- pels the Brahmans to perform the sacrifice,’ brāhmaṇān yajñaṃ kārayati. The root arh (c. 1. arhati -rhituṃ) has sometimes the sense of ‘being compelled’; as, ‘he is compelled to pay,’ dātum arhati. In modern Sanskrit such expressions as the following are found; ‘They compelled him to bear a burden, bhāraṃ voḍhuṃ taṃ balād dadhruḥ (root dhṛ); ‘Compel the people to come,’ lokān āgantuṃ pravarttaya.
