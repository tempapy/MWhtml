LUNAR [471-b], LUNARY, a. cAndraH -ndrI -ndraM, cAndrikaH -kI -kaM, cAndramasaH -sI -saM, candrasambanDI -nDinI -nDi (n), sOmyaH -myA -myaM, sOmikaH -kI -kaM, EndavaH -vI -vaM; ‘lunar month,’ cAndramAsaH; ‘lunar asterism or mansion,’ nakzatraM, dAkzAyiRI; ‘the twenty-seven lunar asterisms collectively,’ dAkzAyiRyaH f. pl., nakzatracakraM; ‘lunar day,’ tiTiH m. f. Of these lunar days there are fourteen for the waxing, and fourteen for the waning moon; the first lunar day is called pratipad f., pakzatiH -tI f.; the day of new moon is called amAvasI, amAvasyA, amA; ‘the day of full moon,’ pUrRimA, pOrRimA, pOrRamAsI, pUrRenduH m. The eleventh tiTi of each SuklapakzaH or waxing, and kfzRapakzaH or waning moon, is regarded as sacred to Vishnu; and the following are the names given by Major Candy for the two ekAdaSI of each month: cEtraH, 1. kAmadA, 2. pApamocanI; vESAKaH, 1. mohinI, 2. varUTinI; jyezWaH, 1. nirjalA, 2. aparA; AzAQaH, 1. SayanI, 2. yoginI; SrAvaRaH, 1. putradA, 2. kAmikA; BAdraH, 1. parivarttinI, 2. ajA; ASvinaH, 1. pASANkuSA, 2. indirA; kArttikaH, 1. boDinI, 2. ramA; mArgaSIrzaH, 1. mokzadA, 2. PaladA; pOzaH, 1. prajAva- rDinI, 2. saPalA; mAGaH, 1. jayadA, 2. zawtilA; PAlgunaH, 1. AmalakI, 2. vijayA. The ekAdaSI of the SuklapakzaH of the months A- zAQaH and kArttikaH are both particularly sacred. On the first, or SayanEkAdaSI, Vishnu goes to sleep, and on the second awakes. (Difference of solar and lunar days) kzayatiTiH f.; ‘inauspicious lunar day,’ GAtatiTiH f., dagDati- TiH f.; ‘observance of the fasts and rites of the several lunar days,’ tiTipAlanaM; ‘lunar eclipse,’ candragrahaRaM; ‘lunar measurement,’ cAndramAnaM; ‘the lunar dynasty,’ somavaMSaH. — (Resembling the moon).
