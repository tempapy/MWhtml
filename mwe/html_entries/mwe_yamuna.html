YAMUNĀ [848-a], s. (A river, commonly called Jumna, which rises on the south side of the Himālayas, and after a course of 378 miles, falls into the Ganges at Prayaga or Allaha- bad. The personified river is considered to be the daughter of Surya or the Sun, and sister of Yama) yamunā, sūryyatanayā, sūryyaputrī, arkatanayā, yamasvasā f. (sṛ), śamanasvasā f., kālindī.
