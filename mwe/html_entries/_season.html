SEASON [719-b], s. (Fit time or opportunity) avasaraH, avakASaH, kAlaH, samayaH, prastAvaH, prasaNgaH, kAlayogaH, kAlasanDiH m., sanDiH m., suyogaH; ‘season for action,’ kAryyakAlaH, kAryyAvasaraH, kAryyasamayaH; ‘sea- son for ablution,’ snAnAvasaraH; ‘wrong season,’ akAlaH, anavasaraH; ‘out of season,’ akAlena, anavasareRa; ‘born out of season,’ akAlajaH -jA -jaM. — (Any time) kAlaH, samayaH, velA, see TIME. — (Auspicious moment) muhUrttaH -rttaM, lagnaH -gnaM, SuBa- lagnaH -gnaM, SuBamuhUrttaH -rttaM. — (One of the divisions of the year) ftuH m., kAlaH, samayaH; ‘revolution of the seasons,’ ftuparyyAyaH, see REVOLUTION; ‘the interval or junction between two seasons,’ ftusanDiH m.; ‘the seasons collectively,’ ftugaRaH, ftuvargaH; ‘the six seasons,’ zaqftavaH m. pl. The year is di- vided into six seasons, according to the Hindūs, viz. 1. Spring, vasantaH, vasantasamayaH, comprising, according to the reckoning which refers to the Northern parts of India, the two months of cEtraH and vESAKaH, i. e. from the middle of March to the middle of May, see SPRING. 2. The hot season or Summer, grIzmaH, grIzmakAlaH, nidAGakAlaH, compris- ing the two months of jyezwaH and AzAQaH, i. e. from the middle of May to the middle of July, see SUMMER. 3. The rainy season, the rains, varzAH m. pl., varzAkAlaH, vfzwikAlaH, com- prising the two months SrAvaRaH and BAdraH, i. e. from the middle of July to the middle of September, see under RAIN, RAINY. 4. The autumnal season, Sarad, SaratkAlaH, SaradftuH m., comprising the two months ASvinaH and kArttikaH, i. e. from the middle of September to the middle of No- vember, see AUTUMN. 5. The cold season, hemantaH, comprising the two months mArgaSIrzaH and pOzaH, i. e. from the middle of November to the middle of January. 6. The dewy season or season of hoar frost, SiSiraH, SiSirakAlaH, comprising the two months mAGaH and PAlgunaH, i. e. from the middle of January to the middle of March. According to more modern authorities the season of Spring begins with the month Phālguna, or the middle of February. This reckoning will place each of the succeeding seasons a month earlier. Sometimes the word AgamaH is used in composition for ‘the coming on of a season,’ or for the season itself, as ‘the rainy season,’ varzAgamaH, meGAgamaH; ‘the hot season,’ grIzmAgamaH, uzmAgamaH.
