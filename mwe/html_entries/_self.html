SELF [724-b], a. or pron. (As united to a pronoun, &c. reciprocally, or in composition with an adverb or noun) AtmA m. (n), Atma in comp.; ‘I will kill myself,’ AtmAnaM hanizyAmi; ‘blame thyself,’ AtmAnaM ninda; ‘he shows himself,’ AtmAnaM darSayati; ‘for one's self,’ AtmArTaM; ‘like one's self,’ Atmavat; ‘comparison with one's self,’ AtsOpamyaM. — (As united to a pronoun, &c. emphatically) svayaM, svatas; ‘I myself will decide,’ svayaM nizpattiM karizyAmi. The pronoun saH is some- times joined with the personal pronouns emphatically; as, ‘I myself,’ so'haM. — (As the first member of a compound word) Atma or sva or svayaM or svataH prefixed; ‘self done,’ AtmakftaH -tA -taM, svayaNkftaH &c. ‘self-given,’ svayandattaH -ttA -ttaM, AtmadattaH &c. ‘self-sufficient,’ svasTaH -sTA -sTaM; ‘self-actuating,’ svayaMvahaH -hA -haM. — (Of one's self, of one's own accord) svayaM, svatas; ‘decayed of itself,’ svayaMSIrRaH -rRA -rRaM.
