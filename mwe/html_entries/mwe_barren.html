BARREN [039-a], a. (Not prolific) bandhyaḥ -ndhyā -ndhyaṃ, asutī -tinī -ti (n), aprasavī -vinī -vi (n), prajārahitaḥ -tā -taṃ. — Unfruitful) niṣphalaḥ -lā -laṃ, aphalaḥ -lā -laṃ, avakeśī -śinī -śi (n), śuṣkaḥ -ṣkā -ṣkaṃ, — (Useless) vyarthaḥ -rthā -rthaṃ, anarthakaḥ -kā -kaṃ, moghaḥ -ghā -ghaṃ. — (Empty) śūnyaḥ -nyā -nyaṃ, riktaḥ -ktā -ktaṃ; ‘a barren woman,’ bandhyā, apasūtā; ‘a barren soil,’ marusthalaṃ or -lī. BARREN [853-a], a. (As a woman) anapatyā, anapatyakā, ajātāpatyā, apra- jātā. The Hindūs divide barren women into three classes, viz. 1. ‘She who has no offspring,’ bandhyā; 2. ‘She who bears only one child,’ kākabandhyā; 3. ‘She whose off- spring die,’ mṛtabandhyā.
