BALARĀMA [852-b]. This god is not often regarded as an avatār of Vishnu, but rather as an incarnation of the great ser- pent Ananta; hence he is sometimes called SezaH or anantaH. He was the son of Vasudeva by Devakī, but was trans- ferred from the latter to the womb of Rohiṇī, the other wife of Vasudeva, and hence his name, saNkarzaRaH. Other names for him are — (Fond of wine) priyamaDuH m. — (Killer of the boxer Muṣṭika) muzwikAntakaH. — (Secret in motion) guptacaraH. — (Strong and good) balaBadraH.
